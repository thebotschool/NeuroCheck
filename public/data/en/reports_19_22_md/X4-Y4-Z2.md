## 2. Success Guide

Understanding an individual learning style is the basis of effective development.

### Individual characteristics

*   **Pace:** Prefers a measured pace that requires a pause. The speed of tasks reduces accuracy.
*   **Perception:** Optimally assimilates the information visually. Visual support is critical for understanding.
*   **Focus:** Attention is sensitive to external stimuli. Effective concentration persists for 15–20 minutes.

### Supporting factors

*   **Step -by -step instructions:** Check lists, algorithms, schemes "If ... then ..." increase clarity.
*   **Visual supports:** Mind cards, infographics, color release improve the structure of perception.
*   **Intervals:** Short work sessions (15–20 minutes) with interruptions optimize the concentration.
*   **Working space:** The environment without distracting factors contributes to focusing.
*   **Speaking:** Discussion of the action plan reduces impulsiveness and structures the process.

### Calls

*   **Multitasking:** The simultaneous performance of several tasks causes cognitive overload.
*   **Oral instructions:** Long, oral instructions are perceived incomplete or forgotten.
*   **Distracting factors:** Noise, disorder, digital distraction reduce concentration.
*   **Time pressure:** Hurry provokes impulsive actions and superficial understanding.

## 3. Recommendations

### Home environment

*   **Planning:**
    *   Separate large tasks into small steps. Make a joint plan.
    *   Use a timer (15–20 minutes) for each work unit. This helps to manage time and see progress.
    *   Provide a minimalist workspace. Remove distracting objects and gadgets.
*   **Promotion:**
    *   Mark perseverance, following the plan and accuracy, and not just speed.
    *   Before starting the task, discuss the first steps. This develops planning skills.
    *   Explain that errors are part of the process. Offer to return and double -check.

### School environment

*   **Instructions:**
    *   Formulate the tasks step by step, algorithmically.
    *   Duplicate the key points of the tasks on the board.
*   **Environment:**
    *   A place is recommended at the board, away from distracting factors (windows, doors).
    *   Use presentations, schemes, tables. Visualize oral information.
*   **Feedback:**
    *   Sports short, involving questions to maintain attention.
    *   Give enough time to think about it, avoiding immediate requirements.
*   **Individual route:**
    *   It is possible to increase the time (15–20%) to the tests.
    *   It is permissible to use ready -made plans or algorithms for volumetric tasks.

## 4. Recommended training

These exercises and games develop key cognitive skills. Integrate them into everyday life.

### For attention

*   **Graphic dictations:** Develop auditory attention and ability to follow instructions.
*   **Schultte tables:** Improve concentration and peripheral vision through the search for numbers.
*   **Search for differences:** Games like "Find 10 differences" or "Wimmelbuch" train detailed attention.

### For self -control and coordination

*   **The game "Sea is worried once":** Forms the skill of rapid braking and control of movements.
*   **Board games:** Develop adherence to the rules and the sequence of actions (checkers, strategies).
*   **Rhythm tapping:** Coordination of hearing, attention and movement through a repeat of rhythm.

### For memory

*   **The game "Memory":** Strengthens visual working memory.
*   **Removing lists:** Training of sequences memorizing, starting with short lists.
*   **Retelling:** The retelling of the text with support to keywords develops semantic memory.
