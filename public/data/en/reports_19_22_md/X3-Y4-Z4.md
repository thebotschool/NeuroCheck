### **2. Profile: individual characteristics**

#### **Key features**

*   **Pace:** Requires a calm, measured pace. Hurry increases the risk of errors and loss of concentration.
*   **Perception:** Optimally assimilates visual and step -by -step information. Long oral or textual explanations are tiring.
*   **Attention:** Unstable, quickly exhausted. Productive concentration-up to 15-20 minutes, then a short break is needed.

#### **Support**

*   Step-by-step instructions: Tasks broken into small, understandable steps (for example, checklists).
*   Visual elements: diagrams, mental cards, color selection, short videos.
*   Clear rhythm: predictable schedule of work and rest (for example, 20 minutes of work, 5 minutes of break).
*   Similarity: Focus on one task at a time. After completion - the transition to the next.

#### **Obstacles**

*   Multivativity: for example, simultaneous listening and abstract.
*   A sharp change in activity: switching without preliminary warning.
*   Information noise: distracting objects, background noise.
*   Abstract instructions: long oral explanations without visual support.

<!-- page-break -->

### **3. Strategies: Growth support**

#### **Home environment**

*   **Homework:**
    *   Break the tasks: on blocks for 15-20 minutes. Between them-a 5-minute break.
    *   Use the timer: visual timer (sand clock, phone) to control time.
    *   Plan: make a simple list of cases for the evening.
*   **Support:**
    *   Direct the process: help with the structure, not with the solution. “Where will we start? What is the next step? "
    *   Organize the space: remove excess from the table before starting work.
*   **Promotion:**
    *   Praise the effort: focus on the process and diligence, and not just on the result. "I see how you tried not to be distracted!"

#### **School environment**

*   **The wording of tasks:**
    *   Step -by -step instructions: a clear algorithm for completing the task. Duplicate on the board.
*   **Lesson structure:**
    *   Lesson plan: a brief plan (2-3 points) on the board at the beginning of the lesson.
    *   Visibility: actively use schemes, tables, drawings as support.
*   **Location:**
    *   The optimal place: the first-second desk, away from distracting factors (window, door).
*   **Style style:**
    *   Alternate formats: short explanations (5-7 min) → Practice (10 min) → Warm-up/discussion.
    *   Individualization: Provide cards with algorithms or checklists.

### **4. Development: practical exercises**

Games and exercises that will help to develop key skills in a fascinating form.

*   **Attention and reaction:**
    *   Board games: “Volnet”, “Drum”, “Find a couple” for quick visual search and concentration.
*   **Memory and sequence:**
    *   Compilation of comics: presentation of a short story in 3-4 key shots.
    *   "Rest of Steps": Joint cooking according to strict instructions.
*   **Self -regulation:**
    *   Games: “The sea is worried once”, “Delay”, “edible, unauthorized”.
    *   Rhythmic exercises: tapping/laying out of a given rhythm, change in speed.
*   **Coordination and interhemispheric interaction:**
    *   Balance: walking along the line, standing on one leg with eyes closed.
    *   Juggling: Start with two light items or scarves.
    *   Graphic dictations: drawing patterns on dictation.
