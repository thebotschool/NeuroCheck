### Cognitive Options Report: CPT + Go/No-Go + TCP (19–22 years)

## Assessment of abilities

*   **Sustainability of attention and memory (CPT):** Deep attention to complex tasks. High working memory and strategic thinking.
*   **Impulsive control (Go/No-Go):** Severe impulsiveness. The tendency to hasty actions and answers.
*   **General condition of attention (TCP):** There are difficulties with concentration and reaction speed. Perhaps a consequence of overwork, stress or reduced motivation.

**Key conclusion:** High cognitive potential. Current overload. The main difficulties: impulsiveness and depletion of resources that impede the implementation of strengths.

<!-- page-break -->

## Student profile

### Individual characteristics

*   **Pace:** Dual. Effective in deep work on complex tasks. Prone to haste and impulsiveness in simple or stressful situations. It takes time for "warming up" and entering a productive state.
*   **Perception format:** Better assimilates structured, logical information. Visual schemes, plans, lists, Mayind cards support strategic thinking.
*   **Focus of attention:** "Spotlight", but unstable. Capable of deep immersion. It is easily distracted by external stimuli, prone to hasty actions due to impulsiveness.

### Efficiency support

*   Clear step-by-step instructions with checklists.
*   The use of visual timers (for example, the technique of ironing: 25/5 minutes).
*   The practice of the "internal pause" before action/answer (short inhale-output, account up to three).
*   Work in a quiet, predictable environment.

### Risk factors

*   Multivativity: depletes resources, provokes errors.
*   Oral instructions without visual support: loss of parts is possible.
*   Compressive terms: enhance impulsiveness and rash decisions.
*   Noisy, chaotic environment: distracts, reduces resources for the task.

## Individual approach

### Home environment

*   **Organization of work:**
    *   To break major tasks into small, understandable stages.
    *   Apply the technique "First complete - then check." After performing-a short break (10-15 minutes), then a fresh check to reduce errors.
*   **Support and motivation:**
    *   Discuss the level of fatigue, anxiety, stress, and not just results. Example: "How do you feel?" Instead of "Have you already done lessons?"
    *   Configure a balanced daily routine: study, rest, hobbies, sports. This strengthens cognitive resources.
*   **Mercing of attention:**
    *   Productive work: 25-40 minutes per task.
    *   Break: 5-10 minutes, without gadgets (for example, look out the window, walk, drink water).

### Educational environment

*   **The wording of tasks:**
    *   Provide tasks in writing with clear criteria.
    *   Give time to think about oral issues. Avoid blitz-questioning.
*   **Lesson structure/lectures:**
    *   Sound the plan at the beginning, summarize at the end.
    *   Material submission: logical, with a visual support (presentation, diagrams, infographics).
*   **Accommodation:** Optimal in the quiet part of the audience minimizing distraction.
*   **Individual educational route (IOM):**
    *   Inclusion in the development programs of Soft Skills: time management, stress resistance, awareness.
    *   Work with a mentor/coach: tracking progress, setting realistic goals. Increases success, reduces burnout.

## Practical exercises

These exercises are aimed at developing self -control and improving concentration.

### Development of attention and braking

*   Board games: chess, go, "jenga". Develop strategic thinking and restraint of impulses.
*   The game "Yes and no, do not say." He trains conscious speech control.

### Strengthening working memory and planning

*   Exercise "Repeat in the reverse order." Reproduction of series of numbers, words, movements in the reverse order.
*   Planning of the day/week. Daily drawing up lists of cases with priorities.

### Development of bodily control and rhythm

*   Balance exercises: a stand on one leg (with eyes open/closed), a balance. Improve physical and mental control.
*   Juggling or rhythmic throwing of the ball. It requires concentration, coordination and deterrent of unnecessary movements.
