# Cognitive profile: attention, self-control, memory (19-22 years)

## 1. Key conclusions

*   **Working memory (CPT):** Difficulties with the retention of volumetric information and complex instructions have been noted. Especially pronounced with fatigue or high data supply speed. This indicates overloading processing systems, and not a deficit of memory.
*   **Self-control (Go/No-Go):** Interhemistered asymmetry has been identified. It can be manifested in difficulties with new tasks requiring coordinated actions or integration of heterogeneous information (for example, listen and write down).
*   **Attention (TCP):** There is a tendency to impulsive solutions and errors of haste. It is more often manifested in conditions of a shortage of time or stress, as in control work.

## 2. Success Guide

### Individual profile

*   **Pace:** It shows extremes - haste under pressure or deceleration during overload. A stable pace requires an external organization.
*   **Perception:** Optimally assimilates the information visually and structurally: schemes, algorithms, infographics. Audial information without visual support is absorbed worse.
*   **Focus of attention:** Unstable. Easy to be distracted during overload. In stress, attention may narrow, leading to ignoring the context.

### Supporting factors

*   **Visualization:** Mind cards, schemes, highlighting keywords, short videos.
*   **Step -by -step algorithms:** Clear and simple instructions for complex tasks.
*   **Predictability:** A pre -well -known lesson or homework plan.
*   **Conscious pauses:** Time for thinking and self -testing.
*   **Rhythm:** The alternation of activity and rest (for example, 25/5 minutes).

### Interviewing factors

*   **Multitasking:** Simultaneous perception, recording and analysis of information.
*   **Fast change of tasks:** Switching between activities without a logical pause.
*   **Information noise:** Excess oral information without visual support, distracting sounds.
*   **Time pressure:** The tasks limited in time provoke impulsiveness and error.
*   **Abstract instructions:** Fuzzy, nonspecific formulations of tasks.

## 3. Recommendations

### For the house

*   **Homework performance:**
    *   **Decomposition:** Separate large tasks into small, understandable steps. Create joint checks.
    *   **Time management:** Use the “Prydor” technique (25/5 minutes) to maintain the focus.
    *   **Preliminary planning:** Discuss or write down the work plan before starting.
    *   **Organization of space:** Remove distracting items from the table, leave only the necessary.
*   **Support and motivation:**
    *   **Focus on the process:** Mark the thoughtfulness and attempts of self -testing, not speed.
    *   **Error analysis:** Instead of criticism, offer the analysis: "Let's figure out where the error has occurred in the logic." This develops introspection.
    *   **Attention cycles:** Keep in mind that productive attention lasts 20–25 minutes. Plan activities, respectively.

### For school

*   **The wording of tasks:**
    *   Tasks should be clear and step -by -step. For example: “Read paragraph 5, write down 3 definitions, make a diagram ...”.
    *   Use visual accents: **half -fat font**, lists, icons for key actions.
*   **Lesson structure:**
    *   Start a lesson with a brief plan on a board/slide.
    *   Alternate formats: short lectures (5-7 minutes), viewing schemes/videos, practical tasks, discussions.
    *   Duplicate important oral information visually (board, handout).
*   **Accommodation and feed:**
    *   Optimal place: the first or second desk to minimize distraction.
    *   Presentation style: energetic, structured, with an emphasis on visualization.
*   **Individual educational route (IOM):**
    *   Additional time (15-20%) on control/independent to reduce stress.
    *   Permission to use a supporting plan or scheme for oral answers or essays.

## 4. Developing practices

These exercises will help strengthen the identified growth zones. Priority is regularity, not duration.

*   **Attention and reduction of impulsiveness:**
    *   **Correct test:** Crossing the specified letters or words in the text. Develops concentration on details.
    *   **Strategic board games:** For example, “set”, “corridor”, “Katamin”, chess. Stimulate planning and thinking.
*   **Coordination and interhemispheric interaction:**
    *   **Juggling:** Start with two items. Synchronizes the work of the eyes, hands, hemispheres.
    *   **Neurohymnastics:** Exercises "Kulak-Robe-Ladon", "Upo-Shno".
    *   **Balancing exercises:** Using a balance board or slate. Improves bodily control and concentration.
*   **Working memory:**
    *   **Memory game:** Use sets with a large number of cards.
    *   **Creating Mind Cart:** After reading or viewing, create intelligence cards with key ideas. This helps to visualize and structure information.
*   **Rhythm and self -control:**
    *   **Game on a musical instrument:** Especially on tools requiring coordination of both hands (drums, piano).
    *   **RIGHTS RIGHTS:** Repeating the specified rhythms. He trains auditory attention and motor control.
