# Cognitive abilities report
### Attention, self -control, memory (19–22 years old)

This report offers an understanding of the strengths and zones of the development of the student. Its goal is to create an optimal environment for training and harmonious development both at home and at school.

## 1. Assessment of tests

*   **Attention (TCP):** High concentration for understandable and stable tasks. The reaction is fast, the errors are minimal.
*   **Self-control (Go/No-Go):** Difficulties with the suppression of impulsive reactions and maintaining concentration, especially with fatigue or the need for constant switching.
*   **Visual memory (CPT):** Effective memorization of individual elements. Difficulties arise if it is necessary to build a sequence or execution of multi -step instructions.

## 2. The profile of the student

### Features of training
*   **Pace:** The measured pace with short breaks is optimal. Excessive speed tires, a slow rate reduces the focus.
*   **Perception:** Preference of visual and step -by -step information. Schemes, algorithms, intelligence cards and lists significantly improve understanding. The continuous text makes perception difficult.
*   **Attention:** High concentration on one task. It is easily distracted if it is necessary to withhold several rules, switching between actions or suppress impulsive answers.

### Support factors
*   **Visualization:** Lesson plan, highlighting keywords, cards with rules.
*   **Step -by -step approach:** Decomposition of large tasks on 3-4 understandable steps.
*   **Rhythm and structure:** A clear working rhythm (20-25 minutes of work / 5 minutes of a break) and the predictable structure of the lesson contribute to confidence.
*   **Motor activity:** Short physical pauses help restore attention.

### Distraction factors
*   **Multitasking:** Simultaneous oral instructions.
*   **Information redundancy:** Long, unstructured text without pauses or visual support.
*   **Frequent change of tasks:** Sharp switching between different types of tasks without a short pause.
*   **External incentives:** Noise, conversations, disorder in the workplace.

<!-- page-break -->

## 3. Recommendations

### For the house
*   **Performing tasks:**
    *   Small work into small, controlled blocks.
    *   Make simple action plans: use stickers or checks.
    *   Organize the workspace: on the table only necessary for the current task.
*   **Support and motivation:**
    *   Consider the optimal concentration time (20–25 minutes) and plan short breaks.
    *   Mark follow the plan and self -control, not just speed. Encourage questions.
    *   In the evening, focus on the process and efforts, and not just on assessments.

### For school
*   **Instructions:**
    *   Formulate clearly, briefly, step by step. Provide one instruction at a time.
    *   Duplicate oral instructions in writing (on a board or card).
*   **Lesson structure:**
    *   Sound the lesson plan at the beginning: "Today we will study X, then Y, we will finish Z."
    *   Use visual supports: schemes, tables, presentations, color accents.
    *   Recommended place: Closer to the teacher and board, away from distracting factors.
*   **Individualization (IOM):**
    *   Allow the drafts for planning answers.
    *   Allow the use of a ruler or finger to track the line when reading.
    *   Offer control tasks on several cards to reduce the load on memory.

## 4. Practical training

These exercises are easily integrated into everyday life, contributing to the development of key skills.

### Attention and self -control
*   **Impulsion braking games:** "Edible-universal", "forbidden movement", "sea is worried once", "you go quieter-you will continue."
*   **Board games:** "Jenga", "Uno", chess, checkers. Develop strokes, compliance with rules, strategic thinking.

### Memory and organization of information
*   **Creation of comics:** Visualization of 3-4 key points from read or viewed material for the development of a logical sequence.
*   **Retelling according to the plan:** Drawing up 3-4 points of the plan before retelling for structuring information.
*   **Cooking:** Preparation of dishes by step -by -step recipe for training the sequence of actions.

### Coordination and bodily control
*   **Balance exercises:** Standing on one leg, walking along the line.
*   **Games with the ball:** Juggling, targets at the target, beat off the wall with additional tasks. Improve visual-motor coordination and reaction rate.
