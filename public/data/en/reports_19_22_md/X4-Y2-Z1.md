## Profile: attention and control. 19–22 years

Your profile of attention and control. This practical guide is based on the results of three cognitive tests. Not a diagnosis, but tools for success.

### 1. Test results

A brief overview of indicators.

*   **Test for attention (CPT):** The tendency to rapid fatigue in monotonous tasks. This affects concentration and accuracy.
*   **Impulsive test (Go/No-Go):** Fast, sometimes rash reactions. Difficulty in the delay of the first impulse.
*   **Working memory test:** Difficulties with maintaining several elements of information at the same time. Overload on an excess of data.

### 2. The guide for the success of your student

Understanding how the schoolboy perceives training determines his success.

#### Individual characteristics

*   **Pace:** Often uneven. Quick inclusion in interesting tasks, but the risk of focus loss for difficulties.
*   **Perception:** Visual and step -by -step information works best. Schemes, checklists, short instructions, selected text. Long oral explanations are perceived worse.
*   **Focus of attention:** Unstable. Distracted by external incentives. The resource of attention is depleted on complex or monotonous tasks.

#### Which helps

*   **Step -by -step algorithms:** A clear sequence of actions for any task.
*   **Visual supports:** Schemes, Mayind cards, reminders, color coding. All that unloads memory through vision.
*   **Short sessions:** Method "Prydor". For example, 20 minutes of work, 5 minutes of active break.
*   **Rule "Stop-think-make":** Conscious pause before action. A special sign or word for reminder.
*   **Predictability:** The stable daily routine reduces anxiety and cognitive load.

#### What interferes

*   **Multitasking:** Simultaneous requests lead to poor -quality execution.
*   **Long instructions:** Only the beginning or end is remembered. The middle is lost.
*   **Rush and pressure:** Phrases like "Faster!" enhance impulsiveness and increase errors.
*   **Noise and chaos:** Distracting factors directly reduce the resource of attention.

### 3. Recommendations

Practical steps for support.

#### For the house

*   **Organization of work:**
    *   Miss large tasks into 3-4 small subtasibles.
    *   Use a timer for work intervals (15–25 minutes).
    *   Together, make a simple plan on the sheet: read, solve, check.
*   **Support and motivation:**
    *   Praise the process and effort, not only the result. “I see how you tried to concentrate” is more important than the assessment.
    *   Allow to choose the procedure for completing tasks. This gives control.
    *   Realistic time to keep attention on a difficult task - **15–20 minutes**. Do not demand anymore without a break.
*   **Organization of space:**
    *   On the desktop - only necessary. Eliminate distracting factors.

#### For school

*   **The wording of tasks:**
    *   The instructions should be clumsy, brief, unambiguous. It is better to duplicate oral information in writing.
    *   Miss large tasks into stages, voicing every step after the previous one.
*   **Lesson structure:**
    *   Organize the lesson with blocks for 15–20 minutes, changing the types of activity.
    *   Place the student closer to the board, away from distracting factors.
*   **Style style:**
    *   Use more clarity: presentations, diagrams, video.
    *   Enjoy active activity: ask for something to bring, write, distribute. This "restarts" attention.
*   **Individual route:**
    *   In case of severe difficulties, discuss with a school psychologist and class teacher a Tutor support or adapted materials.

### 4. Recommended training

Games and exercises for the development of cognitive functions.

*   **For attention:**
    *   **"Corrective test":** Crossing or bypassing certain letters in the text.
    *   **"Find 10 differences":** Classic for training concentration.
    *   **Board games:** “Dobble”, “Drum”. They train the reaction speed and concentration.
*   **For self -regulation:**
    *   **"FROM ATOMISE":** The ability to quickly stop the movement by signal.
    *   **Balance exercises:** Standing on one leg, walking along the border. Develops the connection of the brain and body.
    *   **Rhythmic exercises:** Tapping or cooling the specified rhythm.
*   **For working memory:**
    *   **"Memory":** Search for paired cards.
    *   **"I put in a bag ...":** A game of memorizing the sequence of objects.
    *   **"Chef":** Removing and bringing several ingredients at a time.
