### **Cognitive profile: attention, control, memory (19–22 years)**

This report is based on the results of complex cognitive testing. Its goal is to provide a clear understanding of the individual’s individual abilities and offer specific strategies for his development.

---

#### **1. Key indicators**

*   **Attention (Continous Performance Test):** Demonstrates a high level of concentration and a quick, unmistakable reaction.
*   **Self-control (Go/No-Go Test):** It has effective self -regulation. It is able to maintain the accuracy of actions even in conditions requiring a quick reaction.
*   **Visual working memory:** Issued as a key area for development. Difficulties are noted with the retention of a significant amount of visual information, especially with fatigue or emotional stress.

#### **2. The guide for the success of your student**

The student has a unique cognitive profile: high attentiveness and discipline are combined with a limited volume of working memory. It is motivated for high -quality work, but requires a competent organization of the educational process.

**Personal profile**

*   **The pace of work:** Effective with sequential submission of information. Volumetric data presented at once reduce productivity.
*   **Perception format:** Optimally assimilates structured and logical information (lists, algorithms). He experiences difficulties with chaotic or overloaded visual materials.
*   **Focus of attention:** Sustainable and high. The main difficulty is the retention of many elements in memory, not concentration.

**Optimal support**

*   Step -by -step instructions and algorithms.
*   Using external supports: checks, intelligence cards, recordings.
*   Separation of major tasks into controlled stages.
*   The predictable working rhythm without a sudden change of context.

**Factors that reduce efficiency**

*   Multitasking.
*   Information overload.
*   Tasks requiring oral or mental memorization of long sequences.
*   Emotional stress and fatigue, critically reducing working memory.

<!-- page-break -->

#### **3. Recommendations**

##### **Home environment**

*   **Organization of work:**
    *   Miss volumetric tasks into 20–25-minute blocks. Provide short 5-minute breaks for "rebooting" memory.
    *   Together, make a plan or checklist before starting work. Focus on the sequence of actions: "What needs to be done first? What then? Let's write down."
    *   Apply the "unloading on paper" method: after reading the paragraph, fix the key thoughts or draw a simple scheme. This transfers the cognitive load to the external medium.

*   **Support and encouragement:**
    *   Mark not the final result, but the organization of the process: “It is excellent that you planned the work!”, “I see, you structured everything - it helps a lot.”
    *   Create a calm and predictable atmosphere. Stress and fatigue negatively affect working memory.

##### **School environment**

*   **The wording of tasks:**
    *   Use step -by -step instructions. For example, instead of “Analyze the paragraph” offer: “1. Read paragraph 2. Write 3 key dates. 3. Formulate the main idea.”
    *   Duplicate oral instructions in written: on the board, in chat or on cards.

*   **The structure of the material:**
    *   Present the information in a structured form: lists, diagrams, algorithms. Avoid continuous text or overloaded slides.
    *   Place key data (formulas, rules, terms) in a prominent place (board, handout) so that the student can turn to them without the need for memorization.

*   **Learning style and IOOM:**
    *   The most effective style of the "mentor", which helps in developing strategies for solving problems.
    *   Within the framework of the individual educational route (IOM), you can focus on project activities where the strengths (attention, control) will be applied, and the working memory is compensated by the planning and use of external supports.

#### **4. Recommended training**

The proposed exercises are aimed at developing working memory and the use of strengths (attention, control) for its support.

*   **Development of working memory:**
    *   **Memory game:** Classical exercise to remember the location of elements.
    *   **Reproduction of rhythms:** Repetition of the proposed rhythmic sequences. Start with simple, gradually complicating. It is possible to use a metronome or specialized applications.
    *   **Board games:** Choose games where you need to remember the location of cards or resources (for example, "Karcasson", "Colonialists").

*   **Strengthening attention and planning skills:**
    *   **Strategic Games:** Chess, checkers, go. These games contribute to the development of planning skills and reducing the load on RAM.
    *   **Assembly according to the instructions:** LEGO Technic models, furniture assembly, radio electronic designers. Develop the ability to follow algorithms and work with external plans.

*   **Bodily control and voltage removal:**
    *   **Juggling:** Start with two goals. Improves visual-motor coordination and concentration, while removing mental stress.
    *   **Respiratory practices:** For example, "square breathing" (inhale, delay, exhale, delay - 4 accounts). Effectively reduces stress levels and helps focus.
