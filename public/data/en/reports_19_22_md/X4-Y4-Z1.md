# Cognitive assessment report: 19–22 years old

This report will help parents and teachers better understand the strengths of the student and development zones. Recommendations are aimed at creating an optimal environment for successful training and harmonious development.

---

## 1.

**Attention and memory (CPT):** Holding concentration in prolonged tasks requires effort. Cognitive fatigue occurs quickly, especially with complex or extensive information.

**Self-control (Go/No-Go):** Difficulties with braking impulsive reactions were noted. This can be manifested in hasty actions and errors of inattention, especially with fatigue or stress.

**Visual working memory:** In conditions of short, clearly defined tasks, the student demonstrates high speed, accuracy and composure. This is his strong side - the ability to quickly mobilize.

**Bottom line:** The profile of the "sprinter", not the "marathoner". Great results in short, intensive tasks. The rapid depletion of resources with a long, monotonous load. This can lead to errors of attention and impulsiveness.

## 2. The guide for the success of the student

### Features: pace, perception, focus

*   **Pace.** It works impulse. It gives a quick and accurate result at a short distance. Quickly depleted with prolonged load. It requires alternating intensive work and short rest.
*   **Perception.** It better assimilates visual, structured, step-by-step information (diagrams, algorithms, checklists). Abstract or long oral instructions can cause overload.
*   **Focus of attention.** Deep dive to 15-25 minutes. Then a short “reboot” is needed to maintain efficiency.

### Support: what works

*   **Crushing of tasks.** Separation of large tasks into small, understandable steps with a clear result.
*   **Visual supports.** Schemes, tables, Mayind cards, highlighting keywords. Reduces cognitive load.
*   **Clear algorithms.** Step -by -step instructions.
*   **Technique "Prydor".** Interval work with a timer (for example, 25 minutes of work, 5 minutes of rest).
*   **Predictable environment.** A clear schedule, clear rules and expectations.

### Calls: what is bothering

*   **Multitasking.** Simultaneous listening, recording and analysis. Focus on one action is needed.
*   **Prolonged monotony.** Long lectures without interactive, large texts without structure.
*   **Faring instructions.** Blurry formulations of tasks. Call frustration and procrastination.
*   **Distracting factors.** Noise, visual mess. Greatly reduce concentration.

## 3. Recommendations

### For the house

1.  **Organization of work.**
    *   Break homework into blocks for 20-30 minutes. Between the blocks-a mandatory 5-minute break (water, stretching, gaze out the window).
    *   Make a plan for execution together. Example: mathematics (20 min), rest (5 min), essay (25 min). Visualize the plan as a checklist.
    *   Start with difficult tasks while the energy is high.

2.  **Support.**
    *   Focus on the process and efforts, and not just on the assessment. Praise for the allocated time, a compiled plan, an attempt to solve a difficult problem.
    *   Replace criticism with support. Instead: “Again, inattentive!”, Say: “I see, it’s difficult here. Let's look at it together. ”
    *   Provide a calm working atmosphere. Take the TV and extra gadgets.

### For school

1.  **Tasks: wording and structure.**
    *   Give specific tasks. Instead: “Study the paragraph”, say: “Read paragraph 5, write down 3 definitions, answer questions 1 and 4”.
    *   Present the material with schemes, tables, algorithms. This is the optimal format.
    *   In case of oral answer, allow me to use a short plan or scheme.

2.  **Work in the lesson: Organization.**
    *   Place the student closer to the teacher, further from distracting factors (window, door).
    *   Alternate activities: explanation (5-7 min), practice (10 min), work in pairs (5 min), check (3 min).
    *   Before the control: 1-2 minutes to pronounce the plan. Example: "First, read all the tasks, do the lungs, then return to the difficult."

3.  **Individual route.**
    *   Additional time for control (10-15%).
    *   The ability to take projects in parts.
    *   Regular, brief feedback on the process, not only by the result.

## 4. Training for development

Exercises for the development of skills and reference to load. Regularity is important: 5-10 minutes daily.

*   **Attention and self -control.**
    *   **The game "Corrective test".** In 5 minutes, cross out everything “A” and emphasize all “O” in the text. Gradually complicate, adding the rules.

*   **Working memory and planning.**
    *   **The game "Repeat the route".** On a 4x4 grid, show the route 3-4 cells. The student repeats. Gradually lengthen the route.

*   **Body control and rhythm.**
    *   **Exercise "Delay-Otomory".** Movement to the music. Freeze when it stops it. He trains quick braking and body control.

*   **Coordination and interaction of the hemispheres.**
    *   Juggling with two or three balls or scarves. Balance exercises (standing on one leg, with open/closed eyes). Develops the functions of self -regulation.
