**Cognitive profile: 19–22 years old**

The account reveals the individual characteristics of the student, offering ways for more effective and comfortable learning.

### 1. Individual profile

*   **Attention and memory (CPT).** The difficulty in holding several information elements at the same time. Long -term tasks require additional support, indicating a high cognitive load.
*   **Braking and control (Go/No-Go).** Impulsing control is perfectly developed. Accuracy and effectiveness in solving clear, short -term problems.
*   **Sustainability of attention (TCP).** Long -term monotonous work causes rapid fatigue, reducing concentration and increasing the likelihood of errors.

<!-- page-break -->

### 2. Features of training

#### Peculiarities

*   **Pace.** Short, intensive periods of activity. The need for frequent, short breaks for recovery.
*   **Perception.** The preference for visual and structural data. A visual plan of action facilitates the work, reducing the cognitive load.
*   **Focus.** Deep, but short -term concentration. High sensitivity to external and internal distracting factors.

#### Which enhances training

*   **Visual supports.** Check lists, diagrams, memory card, discharge. Visibility reduces the mental stress.
*   **Step -by -step instructions.** Decomposition of complex tasks on simple, consistent steps. Example: “1. Read §5. 2. Write out the formulas. 3. Solve problems. "
*   **Rhythm and timers.** Imodor technique (20–25 min work, 5 min rest). The timer structures concentration and provides the necessary breaks.
*   **Structured environment.** A quiet, organized space minimizing distraction.

#### Which prevents training

*   **Multitasking.** Quickly exhausts the resource of attention.
*   **Long monotonous tasks.** They lead to a focus loss and an increase in errors.
*   **Faring instructions.** The lack of a clear plan is confused.
*   **Distracting factors.** High sensitivity to noise and external irritants.

<!-- page-break -->

### 3. Practical recommendations

#### At home

*   **Homework.** Plan: break the tasks into steps, write down. Use the timer: 25 minutes of work, 5-7 minutes of rest (without gadgets). Start with a difficult task.
*   **Support.** Praise the process, not the result: "You followed the plan." Organize the workplace. Discuss fatigue to teach self -regulation.

#### At school

*   **The formulation of tasks.** Use a clear algorithm, numbered lists. Make sure the first step is understood.
*   **The structure of the lesson.** Alternate activity every 15-20 minutes. Duplicate key information visually (board, handout).
*   **Placement.** Optimal: 1-2 desks, the center of the class. The minimum number of distracting factors.
*   **Iom.** Allow checks and plan for control. Give additional time for volumetric tasks, reducing the alarm.

<!-- page-break -->

### 4. Developing training

Regular, short -term training (10 minutes daily) are more effective for the development of key skills.

*   **Attention.**
    *   Corrective samples (reproach letters/words in the text).
    *   Games "Find Differences".
*   **Working memory.**
    *   The game "Memory".
    *   Memorization and reproduction of sequences (numbers, words), gradually complicating.
*   **Coordination.**
    *   Juggling (2-3 balls).
    *   Balancing exercises (standing on one leg, with eyes closed, on a balance sheet).
*   **Rhythm.**
    *   RIGHT RITMES.
    *   Game on simple instruments (percussion) or dancing with learning movements.
