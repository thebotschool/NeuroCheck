## Cognitive abilities report

This report is designed to reveal unique cognitive features and propose personalized strategies for development. Our recommendations are aimed at creating a favorable environment for study and everyday life.

### 1. Test results: Brief review

*   **CPT: attention test**
    A tendency to impulsive answers is noted, especially in pressure conditions. This can lead to hasty errors.
*   **Go/No-Go: Reactions Control Test**
    There were difficulties with suppressing instant reactions. This may be due to fatigue, stress or overstrain.
*   **Visual Working Memorial Test**
    High level of development of working memory. The ability to process and maintain significant volumes of complex information, even in the presence of distracting factors.

### 2. The guide for the success of your student

This section describes the process of thinking and training.

#### Individual profile

*   **The pace of training**
    Prone to a quick, but impulsive pace of work. The principle “first does, then thinks” can manifest itself under pressure. Intellectual abilities allow solving problems, but rush provokes errors.
*   **Perception of information**
    Effectively assimilates complex, structured information (lectures, diagrams, large texts) thanks to developed memory. However, disorientation and chaotic actions are possible if the data is presented without clear logic.
*   **Focus and concentration**
    Attention is dual: deep concentration on memorization and analysis is a strong side. But it is easily distracted if the task requires constant self -control and suppression of impulsive reactions.

#### Support: success factors

*   **Step -by -step instructions**
    The breakdown of complex tasks into small, understandable steps reduces the impulsiveness and feeling of chaos.
*   **A pause before the answer**
    The practice of a short pause (inhale-output) before making a decision or an answer to the question, especially in the exam.
*   **Visual timers**
    A clear understanding of the temporary framework helps to optimally distribute efforts, avoiding haste.
*   **Clear evaluation criteria**
    Accurate knowledge of evaluation criteria allows you to control the quality of work, instead of the desire for speed.

#### Calls: obstacles on the way

*   **Multitasking**
    The requirement of rapid switching between tasks depletes self -control resources and provokes errors.
*   **Harsh deadlines**
    The situations “do now” is the main stress factor. In such conditions, there is a high probability of incorrect solution due to haste.
*   **Faring instructions**
    Unknown expectations lead to actions based on the first impulse.
*   **Noise and distracting factors**
    Additional load on the braking system that exacerbates complexity with control.

### 3. Recommendations

#### Home environment

*   **Preparation for classes: structure**
    *   Miss large tasks (coursework, session) into small subtasks with specific terms. Example: do not “prepare for the exam”, but “1. Study the notes on the topic A; 2. Solve 10 problems on the topic b; 3. Draw up a plan of the answer on the topic in ”.
    *   Use the equipment technique: 25 minutes of focused work, 5 minutes of rest. This develops attention and self -control.
    *   Before starting work, remove distracting objects, turn off the notifications.
*   **Motivation: approach to promotion**
    *   Praise for thoughtfulness and self -testing, and not just for speed. (“It is great that you found time to double -check everything!”).
    *   Discuss not only the result, but also the process: “What was the most difficult? Where did you want to hurry? How did you manage to concentrate? "
    *   The concentration on a complex task is stable for about 30–40 minutes, after which a short break is needed.

#### Educational environment

*   **Tasks: clearness and clarity**
    *   Formulate tasks in writing, with clockwork and examples. Oral instructions can be perceived inaccurately.
    *   At the beginning of the assignment, clearly prescribe success criteria and points for each part.
*   **Assessment and control: adaptation**
    *   Provide additional time on control and exams to reduce stress and demonstrate a real level of knowledge.
    *   Allow the use of drafts and encourage self -test as an obligatory part of the work.
*   **Material feed: Style**
    *   Use a calm, structured presentation style. Avoid rhetorical questions requiring an instant answer “from a place”.
    *   Give the time to think after the question before demanding an answer.
*   **IOOM: A personalized approach**
    *   With a high stress load (session), consider a consultation with a psychologist for the development of self -regulation techniques.
    *   The possibility of passing exams in a separate, quiet audience will help minimize distracting factors.

### 4. Recommended training

Development of skills: practical exercises.

*   **Attention and self -control**
    *   **Exercise "Stop-signal":** Before making a quick decision (reply to the message, follow the link), mentally say “stop” and count up to three.
    *   **The game "Delay":** During the routine task (reading, cleaning) at the timer signal (every 5-10 minutes), completely slow down for 15 seconds. Analyze your pose, thoughts, breathing. He trains the skill of braking.
*   **Coordination and rhythm**
    *   **Juggling:** Start with two balls. The lesson requires concentration and control over impulsive movements.
    *   **Rhythmic exercises:** Creteckle the complex rhythmic drawings with your palms or pencil, trying not to go astray and not accelerate. You can use a metronome.
*   **Memory and planning**
    *   **Game "Find a couple" (Memory):** With an additional rule: turning two cards turning over, pause and name them aloud before the continuation. It connects memory with a conscious action.
*   **Bodily control**
    *   **Respiratory practice "Square":** Before the stressful task (test, important conversation), perform several cycles: inhalation at 4 scores → Breathing holding at 4 accounts → Exhale to 4 accounts → delay of 4 accounts. Helps to "ground" and reduce impulsiveness.
