### Assessment of cognitive abilities
**Test: CPT+Go/No-Go+TCP (19–22 years old)**

The report provides an analysis of the cognitive characteristics of the student. Practical recommendations are included for optimizing training and support in the home environment.

### 1. Test results

*   **Visual memory (CPT):** It remembers general information, but can miss the details when distracting. The development of selectivity of attention is required.

*   **Self-control (Go/No-Go):** High reaction and control when using the leading hand. Attraction of the second hand reveals difficulties indicating the asymmetry of interhemispheric interaction.

*   **Sustainability of attention (TCP):** The concentration is unstable, especially with monotonous tasks or external distraction. Fast fatigue from the information flow.

<!-- page-break -->

### 2. The guide for the success of your student

#### Features: individual profile

*   **Optimal pace:** Measured, with interval pauses. Sprint regime leads to exhaustion; Interval work - to productivity.
*   **Dominative perception format:** Visual. It better assimilates information through diagrams, images, lists, selected text. Long oral instructions without visual support are less effective.
*   **Attention:** Unrestrained, prone to switch. He experiences difficulties with filtering external and internal stimuli, which leads to distraction.

#### Support: success factors

*   **Visual anchors:** Check lists, mental cards, keywords. Elements structuring information.
*   **Step -by -step instructions:** Decomposition of large tasks for 2-3 small steps. Promotes to reduce anxiety and maintain a focus.
*   **Time management:** Techniques, such as the tomato method (15–20 minutes of work / 5 minutes of rest), support concentration and provide mental rest.
*   **Motor activity:** Short physical warm -ups, especially with the intersection of the midline of the body, contribute to the activation of both hemispheres and an increase in concentration.

#### Obstacles: risk factors

*   **Multitasking:** The simultaneous implementation of several tasks (for example, study and background noise) reduces productivity and causes frustration.
*   **Verbal overload:** Long monologues or oral instructions without visual support lead to a loss of attention.
*   **Distracting incentives:** Inorganized working space, background conversations or noise reduce the focus.
*   **Unreasonable rush:** Requirements of quick execution or a sharp change in tasks without preparation worsen quality and cause concern.

<!-- page-break -->

### 3. Recommendations

#### At home: organization and support

*   **Working space:**
    *   Divide the tasks into blocks: 15–20 minutes. Use a timer for clarity.
    *   In breaks (2-5 minutes): short physical activity (jumping, stretching, crossed steps).
    *   Planning: together make a plan of 2-3 steps (on a sticker). Example: `1. Прочитать параграф. 2. Выписать 3 слова. 3. Решить 5 примеров.`
    *   Table organization: only the necessary materials for the current task.
*   **Promotion: Focus on the process:**
    *   Recognize efforts, and not just the result: “You have perfectly concentrated these 15 minutes”, “What a thoughtful plan!”
    *   In case of difficulties: instead of criticism, ask questions. “What causes difficulties? We will study this step together. "

#### At school: Methods and adaptation

*   **Instructions:**
    *   Use short, specific instructions with verbs of action: `Подчеркни`, `Составь`, `Соедини`.
    *   Break large tasks (project, essay) into stages with intermediate deadlines.
*   **Material feed:**
    *   Maximum visibility: schemes, images, video, supporting notes.
    *   The optimal place: closer to the board and the teacher, away from distracting factors (window, door).
    *   Interactivity: questions, requests to retell instructions, game elements.
*   **Adaptation: Individual route:**
    *   Control work: printed tasks with the allocation of keywords, the use of a draft for planning, additional time (if necessary).
