# Cognitive profile: teenager

This report reveals the unique cognitive features of your student. He is designed to help parents and teachers create an optimal environment for training and comprehensive development.

## 1. Key indicators

-   **Working memory and strategic thinking:** High abilities to remember and analyze complex information. The student effectively holds the data and builds logical connections.
-   **Reactions control:** A tendency to impulsive actions is noted. The development of the ability to meaningful slowdown before making decisions is required.
-   **Sustainability of attention:** The concentration decreases in a monotonous environment. There is sensitivity to external distracting factors.

## 2. The guide for the success of your student

### Individual characteristics

-   **Pace:** The natural pace is fast, sometimes gusty. This can affect quality. The goal is to master a conscious slowdown.
-   **Perception:** Optimally assimilates visual and structural information. Long, monotonous texts or oral explanations quickly reduce attention. Important *see* information.
-   **Focus of attention:** Attention "jumping" is capable of deep, but short -term concentration. Easily distracted. In case of concentration, powerful working memory allows you to quickly absorb the material.

### Effective approaches

-   **Visual materials:** Use presentations, intelligence cards, diagrams, graphs, color selection.
-   **Step -by -step instructions:** Clear checks and action plans reduce impulsiveness and help to keep the focus on the details.
-   **Interval work:** Use the Tomator method (25 minutes of work / 5 minutes of rest) to maintain concentration.
-   **Working space:** Provide silence and order. Minimize distracting factors such as a phone or extra objects.
-   **Preliminary planning:** Discuss the work plan before the start. This structures thoughts and prevents chaotic actions.

### Barriers in training

-   **Multitasking:** Attempts to fulfill several cases simultaneously lead to errors and loss of attention.
-   **Long oral explanations:** Monotonous lectures without visual support are the least effective format.
-   **Lack of structure:** The fuzzy formulations of the tasks (“Study the topic”, “Write the essay”) cause confusion.
-   **Time pressure:** Sudden questions "to speed" provoke impulsive, rash answers.

## 3. Recommendations

### For the home environment

-   **Organization of work:**
    -   Miss large tasks into small steps. Make an evening plan (for example, 20 min. Mathematics, 5 minutes break, 25 min. English).
    -   Use a timer for intervals. This forms external control and helps to maintain attention.
    -   The workplace should be minimalistic: only the necessary items. The phone is outside the access zone.
-   **Support and encouragement:**
    -   Praise for efforts and the process: "It's great that you have double -checked" or "I see how you tried to concentrate."
    -   Instead of "made lessons?" Ask: "What is your plan for today? How can I help?"
-   **Concentration:** High -quality concentration lasts **20-30 minutes**. Provide short breaks (get up, walk, drink water).

### For the school process

-   **The wording of tasks:**
    -   The instructions should be clumsy, consistent, use numbered or marked lists.
    -   Offer tasks that involve strong memory and the ability to structure (creating schemes, comparison of concepts in tables).
-   **Lesson structure and material:**
    -   The optimal place in the classroom: the first-second desk, away from the sources of distraction (window, door).
    -   Structure the lesson: voice the plan, summarize. Use presentations with diagrams, keywords, images.
    -   Enter the dialogue: ask direct, but not sudden questions. For example: "Ivan, in a minute I will ask you to name three reasons. Think." This gives time to prepare and reduces impulsiveness.
-   **Individual route:**
    -   The development of "flexible skills" is recommended: self-regulation, time management.
    -   Consider the adaptation of certification (for example, replacing an oral exam in writing) to reduce the impact of impulsiveness.

## 4. Recommended training

These exercises will help to develop self -control and attention in a playful way.

### Development of attention

-   **Schultte tables:** The search for numbers for a while improves concentration and peripheral vision.
-   **Correct samples:** Search and dispensing symbols in the text.
-   **Games:** "Find 10 differences" or "Find a couple" (Memory) train focus and working memory.

### Control and reduction of impulsiveness

-   **Games with the ball:** Table tennis, badminton develops the coordination of the "eye-hand" and the ability to inhibiting inhibition.
-   **Juggling:** It perfectly trains concentration, coordination and control of movements.
-   **Balanceboard:** Balance exercises require constant concentration and bodily control.

### Rhythm and pace development

-   **Musical instruments:** The game (especially on drums) develops a sense of rhythm, transferred to other types of activity (for example, the pace of speech or action).
-   **Dancing:** Learning sequences requires memorization and accurate reproduction of movements in a given rhythm.
