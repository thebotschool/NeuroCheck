# Cognitive profile: analysis and recommendations

This report is developed for parents and teachers. Its goal is to propose an understanding of the individual cognitive characteristics of the student, to identify the strengths and areas for development. Recommendations are designed to create a supporting environment for training and growth.

## 1. The main conclusions

*   **Working memory (CPT):** The student effectively remembers individual facts, but experiences difficulties with their logical sequence.
*   **Impulse control (Go/No-Go):** A tendency to quick, impulsive actions or answers without a complete comprehension of the situation was noted.
*   **Attention and accuracy (TCP):** In tasks requiring concentration, errors can occur due to haste, and not due to lack of knowledge. This is especially noticeable in conditions of stress or time restrictions.

<!-- page-break -->

## 2. Nature of training

### Key features

*   **Pace:** Dynamic and impulsive. A tendency to immediate action. This contributes to a quick start, but can affect the quality of the result.
*   **Perception:** The preference for visual and structured information. Abstract or long oral instructions can be less effective.
*   **Focus of attention:** Scanning type. Quick switching between tasks, which sometimes leads to loss of concentration and passage of details.

### Effective methods

*   **Visualization.** Schemes, mental cards, algorithms, infographics - everything that provides a holistic perception of information.
*   **Clear steps.** Step-by-step instructions and checklists for structuring actions and monitoring progress.
*   **A pause before action.** Learning an internal stop to understand before the answer or action.
*   **The structure of the time.** The use of rhythm in work (for example, timer, a technique of helping) for an effective organization.

### Obstacles in learning

*   **Multitasking.** Attempts to perform several cases simultaneously lead to errors and disorganization.
*   **Long oral instructions.** Information may not be completely learned, the middle parts are lost.
*   **Stress and manner.** In such conditions, impulsiveness intensifies, and accuracy decreases.
*   **Lack of plan.** Unstructured tasks cause difficulties with the organization of thoughts.

<!-- page-break -->

## 3. Way to growth

### Home environment

*   **Planning tasks.** Divide big tasks into stages. Make a plan before starting. Use checklist checks to track progress.
*   **Regular breaks.** The optimal duration of work without a break is 15–20 minutes. After that, a short physical break is needed.
*   **Involved encouragement.** Instead of the question "I did lessons?" Ask "What is your plan?" Or "where to start?" Mark not speed, but attentiveness and zeal.

### School environment

*   **Clear instructions.** Formulate tasks in the form of algorithms or numbered lists. Select key steps.
*   **Review of the lesson.** At the beginning of the lesson, voice a brief plan to help the student prepare.
*   **Optimal place.** Placing at the first or second desk minimizes distracting factors.
*   **Time for an answer.** Pause the question. Recall the need to read the task to the end and check the work.
*   **Individual approach.** Allow the use of pre -drawn up plans. Provide additional time (5-10 minutes) for self -testing in control work.

## 4. Practical exercises

These exercises contribute to the development of attention, control of impulses, memory and organization of information.

### Focus and control

*   **Reaction games:** "Edible-universal", "forbidden movement"-training of a timely stop of the reaction.
*   **Board games:** "Dobbl", "set", "drum" - develop the accuracy of the reaction.
*   **Correct samples:** The task is not speed, but the lack of passes and errors when crossing certain characters.

### Memory and structure

*   **Creation of comics:** The transfer of key events in the correct sequence after reading.
*   **Game "Tell me by steps":** Teaching a sequential presentation of rules, routes or recipes.

### Bodily control

*   **The game "Sea is worried once":** The development of the ability to freeze by signal and control the body.
*   **Rhythmic exercises:** Repetition of a given rhythm with claps.
*   **Balance exercises:** Walking along the line, a stand on one leg - a connection between body control and self -control.
