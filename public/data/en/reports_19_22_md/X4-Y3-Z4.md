### Cognitive profile report

This report offers a detailed view of the individual cognitive profile of the student. Its goal is to provide parents and teachers with practical recommendations for creating an optimal educational environment and maximum disclosure of potential.

### 1. Test results

Key conclusions for each test, revealing the features of cognitive functions.

*   **Working memory and attention (CPT):** Difficulties with the retention of several elements of the task in the memory, especially with a given sequence, are noted. This indicates quick mental fatigue and difficulties with self -control when performing complex instructions.
*   **Braking and control (Go/No-Go):** The test revealed insufficient coherence in the work of the right and left side of the body, which can affect coordination and adaptation to new or unusual tasks.
*   **Speed ​​and stability of attention (TCP):** There are difficulties with prolonged concentration, quick reaction and maintenance of mental stress. This is probably due to general overwork, cognitive overload or anxiety.

### 2. Profile of a student: Features of learning

This is not a list of problems, but a description of the unique style of thinking of a student. Understanding these aspects contributes to the creation of the most effective conditions for learning.

#### Individual features

*   **Pace:** Thoughtful, leisurely. It takes time to immerse in the task and processing of information. Hurry or high pace can lead to errors.
*   **Perception:** Visual-structural. Most effectively assimilates information presented clearly, logically and step -by -step. Abstract or voluminous oral explanations are perceived worse.
*   **Focus of attention:** Unstable, prone to rapid exhaustion. Productive concentration on one educational task is possible within 15–20 minutes. After that, a short break is needed.

#### Which contributes to success

*   **Visual funds:** Check lists, diagrams, intelligence cards, short written instructions.
*   **Step -by -step approach:** Savior of complex tasks into 2-3 small, clear steps.
*   **Predictability:** The clear daily routine, structured lessons, repeated algorithms of action.
*   **Silence:** The minimum number of distracting factors. It is possible to use headphones with instrumental music to block noise.
*   **One information channel:** Exclusion of oral comments during writing or reading.

#### What complicates training

*   **Multitasking:** Simultaneous performance of several actions (for example, listen and write).
*   **Long oral instructions:** More than two consistent teams in a row.
*   **Temporary pressure:** Phrases that focus at speed or terms (“faster”, “time”).
*   **Unstructured material:** Continuous text without paragraphs, diagrams, discharge.
*   **Sharp change in activity:** The need for a smooth transition between tasks with a small pause.

### 3. Practical recommendations

Target strategies to support the student at home and in an educational institution.

#### For the house

*   **Homework organization:**
    *   **Movement before the lessons:** A 5-minute warm-up (jumping, dancing, cross movements) activates the brain.
    *   **Task planning:** Drawing up a plan for doing homework together with a schoolboy. Refusing the executed points increases the sense of control.
    *   **Time management:** Use of the method of ironing (20 minutes of work, 5 minutes break). The breaks are required.
    *   **Decomposition of tasks:** Separation of large tasks into smaller, controlled stages.

*   **Support and motivation:**
    *   **Focus on efforts:** Praise for efforts and process, and not just for the result (“you worked a lot on it”, “It is great that you did not give up”).
    *   **Accepting errors:** Perceive errors as an opportunity for training, not failure. Analyze the causes and look for alternative approaches.
    *   **Energy management:** Recognize signs of fatigue or irritation. If necessary, pause or transfer part of the tasks.

#### For school

*   **The wording of tasks:**
    *   **Step -by -step instructions:** Give the command to sequentially, waiting for the execution of the previous step.
    *   **Visualization:** Duplicate oral tasks in writing (on the board, on the cards).
    *   **Clear verbs:** Use the imperative mood (“emphasize”, “write down”, “connect”).

*   **Lesson structure and material presentation:**
    *   **Visual support:** Use schemes, tables, diagrams, lesson plans on the board as much as possible.
    *   **Optimal place:** The first desk in the center minimizes distracting factors and facilitates contact.
    *   **Alternation of activity:** After 15–20 minutes of mental work, turn on motor activity (physical education, answers at the board).

*   **Elements of individualization:**
    *   Provision of additional time (15–20%) for control and independent work.
    *   Permission to use submarine cards with algorithms, formulas.
    *   The ability to take volumetric tasks (projects) in parts.

### 4. Recommended training

Games and exercises for the development of cognitive skills in a slight, exciting form. Daily classes for 10-15 minutes are sufficient.

*   **For attention and control (CPT, TCP):**
    *   **"Find 10 differences":** Develops concentration and attention to details.
    *   **Correct samples:** Ask to rewind certain letters or circle words in the text.
    *   **Board games:** “Dobble”, “set”, “drum” - improve the reaction rate and concentration.

*   **For coordination and interhemispheric interaction (Go/No-Go):**
    *   **Cross -steps:** Alternate touching with the right hand of the left knee, the left - the right. Perform slowly and consciously.
    *   **Juggling:** Start with two light objects. The goal is to synchronize the work of hands and eyes.
    *   **Drawing with two hands:** Simultaneous drawing of symmetrical figures (circles, squares) with both hands on the sheet.

*   **For working memory (CPT):**
    *   **"Memory" (find a couple):** A classic game to remember the location of cards.
    *   **"I am going on a hike":** A game of consistent memorization of objects added by each player.
    *   **Repeat the pattern:** Removing and reproducing short sequences of figures shown for a limited time.
