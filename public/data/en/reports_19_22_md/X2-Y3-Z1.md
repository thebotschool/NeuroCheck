# Cognitive profile report: attention, inhibition, concentration

This report offers a detailed view of the cognitive features of your student. Our recommendations are designed to help in revealing its potential, making the learning process effective and comfortable.

---

### **1. Analysis of key indicators**

*   **Perception accuracy test (CPT):** The tendency to inaccuracies during distraction was revealed. It is important to develop focus on details for a more complete “reading” information.
*   **Action control test (Go/No-Go):** Confident control in the usual situations is shown. Difficulties arise when activating an unknown hand, which indicates the need for the development of interhemispheric interaction.
*   **Concentration and reaction rate (TCP):** It demonstrates mature skills: quick concentration, long -term retention of attention and a decrease in impulsive errors. This is a key resource for academic success.

<!-- page-break -->

### **2. The guide for the success of your student**

#### **Individual profile**

*   **Pace:** Effective in fast rhythm. Control of parts is important. The optimal energy pace with mandatory self -testing is optimal.
*   **Perception:** Priority - structured visual information. Avoid visual disorder.
*   **Focus:** High concentration on one task. Support is required when switching between diverse tasks.

#### **What is strengthening**

*   Clear instructions.
*   Visual support (diagrams, discharge).
*   The predictability of the environment.
*   Complex, analytical projects.

#### **Which weakens**

*   Multitasking.
*   Excess visual information.
*   A sharp change in the rules.
*   Improvisation without preparation.

<!-- page-break -->

### **3. Personal recommendations**

#### **Home environment**

*   **Organization of tasks:**
    *   Ritual: plan, work, check. Speak stages before starting.
    *   Careful self-test: on the checklist, not fluently.
    *   Tomato method: 40-45 minutes of work, 5-10 minutes of active break.
*   **Support and motivation:**
    *   Encourage attentiveness, not speed.
    *   Focus on strategies: analyze the approaches, not just errors.

#### **School environment**

*   **The wording of tasks:**
    *   Clear instructions: lists, numbering, specific verbs.
    *   Visual allocation of key information.
*   **Lesson structure:**
    *   Structured feed: schemes, tables for new topics.
    *   Optimal place: far from distracting factors.
*   **Style style:**
    *   Use the strengths: offer complex individual projects.
    *   Individual route: Suitable for in -depth programs and olympiads. The role of the "editor" in group projects is possible.

<!-- page-break -->

### **4. Recommended training**

These exercises will help strengthen key skills in an interactive format.

*   **For attention and accuracy of perception:**
    *   Games "Find Differences".
    *   Copying complex patterns: accurate reproduction.
    *   Board games for attentiveness ("Volga", "set").
*   **For coordination and interhemispheric interaction:**
    *   Juggling.
    *   Game on the instrument (piano, guitar).
    *   Symmetric drawing (with both hands).
    *   Sports with coordination (tennis, swimming).
*   **For memory and rhythm:**
    *   Mnemics: associations and stories.
    *   Rhythmic games: repetition and creation of rhythms.
