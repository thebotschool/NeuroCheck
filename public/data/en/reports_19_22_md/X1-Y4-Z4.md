**The cognitive profile of the student**

This report reveals the cognitive features of the student. It is designed to help parents and teachers create optimal conditions for learning and development.

---

### 1. Key skills

*   **Working memory.** High level. The ability to effective and structuring information. Resistance to distraction when performing complex tasks.
*   **Brake control.** Requires development. Tender to impulsive actions. The need to train the skill of "braking".
*   **Attention and self -control.** Growth zone. Difficulties in managing attention and quick adaptation to changes. Perhaps due to fatigue or overload.

<!-- page-break -->

### 2. The guide for the success of your student

#### Key features

*   **Pace.** Uneven. High productivity with interest. Decrease in efficiency with fatigue, stress, rush; The risk of impulsive solutions.
*   **Perception.** The preference for structured, visual information. Schemes, plans, lists work effectively. Abstract, unstructured texts can cause difficulties.
*   **Focus of attention.** The ability to deep concentration. Rapid exhaustion in multitasking or monotonous work. Energy is aimed at one goal, but requires frequent reloading.

#### Supporting environment

*   **Visual supports.** Check lists, glider, stickers. Reduce mental load, organize tasks.
*   **Step -by -step instructions.** Decomposition of large tasks for small, specific stages.
*   **Clear time frame.** The use of timers (for example, prying) to increase the concentration and prevent fatigue.
*   **Predictable environment.** The clarity of the sequence of actions optimizes the distribution of resources.

#### Obstacles

*   **Multitasking.** A significant decrease in productivity and an increase in errors.
*   **Frenzy tasks.** The lack of structure causes frustration and confusion.
*   **Hurry and pressure.** They provoke impulsive errors, do not accelerate the work.
*   **Sensory overload.** Noise, disorder, distracting factors exhaust the resource of attention.

<!-- page-break -->

### 3. Recommendations

#### Home environment

*   **Optimization of work.** Short intervals (25-30 min) with breaks (5-10 minutes). Turn on physical activity or pleasant switching.
*   **Planning.** Make a simple plan before work: "Mathematics (2 tasks) → Break (5 min) → History (1 paragraph)". Mark the executed points for control and motivation.
*   **Workplace.** Cleanliness and lack of distracting objects. The phone is outside the access zone.
*   **Promotion.** Focus on the process and efforts: "Great work according to the plan", "resistance to distraction", "Self -correction of error."
*   **Pauses.** Offer rest with fatigue or irritation. Return to the task later, avoiding pressure.
*   **Concentration.** Effective holding of attention on a difficult task is up to 30 minutes. Further, effectiveness is reduced.

#### School environment

*   **The clarity of tasks.** Step -by -step instructions, numbering stages. Give 2 minutes before the control to draw up a personal plan.
*   **Visual support.** Keywords, ready -made plans, schemes. Divide complex topics into blocks. After each block, check understanding.
*   **Optimal place.** The first-second desk, away from distracting factors (windows, doors).
*   **Transition signals.** Warn in advance about the change of activity: "After 5 minutes we proceed to the discussion."
*   **Individual approach.** For volumetric work: Provide templates, checklists with criteria.
*   **Control.** Allow the use of a pre -drawn up plan for detailed answers.

### 4. Recommended training

Developing activity. Games and exercises for training self -control, attention, coordination.

*   **Attention and reaction.** Board games: “Vybl”, “Drum”, “Seth”. Training the search for coincidences.
*   **Rhythm and braking.** The game "Delay-Otomory" is a training of impulse control.
*   **Rhythmic drawings.** Reproduction of a given rhythm (popping, knocking). Gradual complication.
*   **Coordination.** Juggling (starting with two balls). Synchronizes the brain and body, develops concentration.
*   **Balance.** Exercises on a balancing board, walking along the line.
*   **Reducing stress and focus.** “Square breathing” before complex tasks: inhalation 4C - delay 4C - exhale 4C - delay 4C. 2-3 minutes to calm and concentration.
