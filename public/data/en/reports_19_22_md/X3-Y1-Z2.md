### Cognitive profile report

This report provides key data on the cognitive features of the student, his strengths and potential for development. Recommendations are designed to create an optimal environment for training and growth.

---

### 1. Evaluation of cognitive functions

*   **Memory and sequence (CPT):** Good memorization of facts. Calls in the reproduction of information in a clear sequence. This indicates the need for structuring.
*   **Self-control (Go/No-Go):** High level of self -control. The ability to fast, balanced decisions. Efficiency in stress conditions.
*   **Attention (TCP):** The tendency to distracting in monotonous tasks. Attention is unstable without external stimulation. Active involvement is required.

<!-- page-break -->

### 2. Profile of learning

#### Individual characteristics

*   **Pace:** Efficiency at a fast pace and short distances. The concentration decreases with prolonged monotony.
*   **Perception:** The preference for structured, visual information. Schemes, infographics, checklists are better absorbed than a continuous text or lecture.
*   **Focus of attention:** Attention of a “sprint” type: high concentration with a quick reaction. Reducing involvement in passive presentation of information.

#### Optimal conditions

*   **Visual funds:** Schemes, step -by -step instructions. Help structure the material.
*   **Interval work:** For example, the technique of tomorrow (25 minutes of work, 5 minutes of rest). Increases focus, prevents fatigue.
*   **Clear goals and plan:** A clear understanding of the task and sequence of actions. Significantly increases productivity.
*   **Element of "Call":** Tasks requiring a quick reaction or competition. Activate self -control and speed.

#### Factors that reduce efficiency

*   **Multitasking:** Simultaneous implementation of several tasks. Leads to the loss of information.
*   **Monotone:** Long, monotonous tasks without intermediate purposes. Cause cognitive fatigue and distraction.
*   **Information noise:** Open tabs, notifications, background conversations. Reduce productivity.

<!-- page-break -->

### 3. Recommendations

#### Home environment

*   **Organization of work:**
    *   Miss tasks into small, controlled steps.
    *   Apply timer: 25-30 minutes of work, 5 minutes of break.
    *   Consider the optimal concentration time: about 25-30 minutes for a difficult task.
*   **Support and encouragement:**
    *   Focus on the organization, not on a direct solution to problems.
    *   Encourage the process and efforts, not just the result.

#### Educational process

*   **The wording of tasks:**
    *   Tasks formulate as clear algorithms or checklists.
    *   The emphasis on step -by -step instructions to overcome difficulties with the sequence.
*   **Lesson structure and material presentation:**
    *   The lesson plan should be visualized (for example, on the board).
    *   Actively use visual materials: presentations, diagrams, short videos.
    *   Maintain attention through quick, interactive issues.
*   **Individual educational route (IOM):**
    *   In design work: use strong self -control, helping with the structure of the stages.
    *   In exams: consider adaptations, for example, a draft for a response plan or a written form of an oral exam to reduce stress during structuring.

### 4. Developing training

The following exercises contribute to the development of key skills in an interactive and applied form.

#### Attention and focus

*   **Schultte tables:** Training of peripheral vision and concentration.
*   **Game "Find the differences":** Develops focused and methodological attention.
*   **Application of the technique of "tomato":** Conscious use for any matter requiring concentration.

#### Memory on the sequence

*   **Creation of rebukes or comics:** Retelling the plot in the form of consecutive pictures.
*   **The game "Snow Com":** Training of memorization and reproduction of words chains.
*   **Mastering dance ligaments:** Removing and reproducing sequences of movements.

#### Coordination and bodily control

*   **Juggling:** Develops the coordination of the "Hands-Glaze" and a sense of rhythm.
*   **Table tennis, badminton:** Improve the reaction, accuracy and control of movements.
*   **Tapping rhythms:** Repetition and creation of rhythmic drawings. Associated with the perception and creation of sequences.
