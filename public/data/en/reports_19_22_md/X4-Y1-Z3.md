# Your cognitive profile
### Assessment for schoolchildren 19-22 years old

This report offers a detailed view of the strengths and development of your student. Recommendations are based on cognitive tests and aimed at the formation of an optimal environment for training and growth.

---

## 1. Review of testing

*   **Attention.** The test revealed difficulties with long -term attention and memorization of a large amount of information, indicating quick cognitive fatigue.
*   **Control.** A high level of self -control and suppression of impulses in clearly structured tasks has been demonstrated.
*   **Reaction.** In conditions of cognitive load and prolonged concentration, haste is observed. Mistakes are due to the desire to reduce overload, and not inattention.

**Key understanding:** Effective self -control decreases when the working memory is overflow. Priority is a decrease in cognitive load, and not the suppression of impulsiveness.

## 2. Your unique learning style

### Features of the process

*   **Pace.** Thoughtful approach. Hurry is a sign of overload and the cause of errors.
*   **Perception.** Preference is given to structured visual data: schemes, tables, step -by -step instructions. Long text and oral explanations are quickly tiring.
*   **Focus.** Attention is unstable with significant volumes of new information or monotonous work. However, it is as effective as possible on short, clear tasks.

### Supporting factors

*   **Visual supports:** Schemes, memory maps, text highlighting, short videos.
*   **Algorithms:** Step-by-step instructions and checks are more effective than general tasks.
*   **Short intervals:** The Imodor method (20-25 minutes of work, 5 minutes of rest) optimizes concentration.
*   **One task:** A clear, only goal focuses on.

### Distracting factors

*   **Multitasking:** Background distraction (music, messengers, conversations).
*   **Low structure:** Long, unstructured texts without discharge.
*   **Oral instructions:** Lack of visual support in oral tasks.
*   **Time pressure:** Stress from the need for quick solutions.

## 3. Practical recommendations

### For the home environment

1.  **Organization of work:**
    *   **Decomposition:** Missing large tasks into mini-shags. For example, instead of “Make mathematics” - “Solving 3 examples, a break, then the next 3”.
    *   **Timing:** Use the timer (20-25 minutes of work). This structures the process and reduces anxiety.
    *   **Planning:** Together, create a simple plan-scheme of work.
2.  **Support and encouragement:**
    *   **Priority:** Encourage attentiveness and self -test, not speed. Rule: "Make it, put off for 5 minutes, check."
    *   **Structuring:** Teach to highlight the key, make schemes, use stickers.
    *   **Duration:** The optimal concentration time on a difficult task is 15-20 minutes. Then a short physical break is needed.

### For the school environment

1.  **Making tasks:**
    *   **Definition:** Tasks should be as clear and step -by -step as possible, perfectly in written or printed form.
    *   **Visibility:** Actively use visual materials: presentations, schemes, infographics, handout.
    *   **Subsequence:** Imagine tasks one at a time. For example: "Open p. 5, ex. 2. Perform only paragraph A."
2.  **Organization of work in the lesson:**
    *   **Location:** The recommended place is the first-second desk, away from distracting factors.
    *   **Time:** The provision of 15-20% of additional time in control work significantly improves the result, relieving stress.
    *   **IOM:** Consider the elements of an individual educational route, such as the use of drafts or printed tasks with selected conditions.

## 4. A set of training

These simple exercises are aimed at developing key skills. Priority is regularity, not duration.

*   **Attention & Control:**
    *   **Games:** "Find the differences", "Dobbl". Develop detailed attention and speed of visual search.
    *   **Correct samples:** In the text (newspaper/magazine) to disrupt 'a', to circle 'o'. Start with a paragraph.

*   **Working memory:**
    *   **The game 'Memory':** Effective training of visual memory.
    *   **Remember and draw:** Show the figure (4-5 elements) for 10 seconds, then ask for reproduce from memory.

*   **Rhythm & body control:** (Strengthens the connection between the brain-body, self-regulation)
    *   **Tapping rhythms:** Back the simple rhythm (two short, one long), the student repeats. Gradually complicate.
    *   **Balance:** Standing on one leg (with open, then closed eyes). Start with 15 seconds.
