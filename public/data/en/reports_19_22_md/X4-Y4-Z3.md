# Cognitive profile: Assessment of abilities
### CPT + Go/No-GO + TCP (19–22 years old)

This report offers a deep understanding of the cognitive functions of your child. The goal is to identify key features and provide practical strategies for its development and support in training.

# 1. Testing insights

*   **Maintenance of information (CPT):** The difficulty in holding several details or tasks of the task, especially when distracting. Memory is effective, but subject to overload with an excess of data.
*   **Braking (Go/No-Go):** Difficulties with timely stop and impulse control. Fast fatigue from tasks requiring self -control.
*   **Attention and reaction speed (TCP):** The tendency to hasty answers without sufficient analysis. The actions “on the machine” lead to errors in carelessness, especially in conditions of stress or time restrictions.

# 2. The student profile

### Key features

*   **Pace:** Optimal, a calm, measured rhythm. Hurry provokes errors. It takes more time to process information.
*   **Perception:** Preference for visual-structured formats. Long oral explanations overload attention.
*   **Attention:** Unrestrained, quickly exhausted. The effective concentration lasts 15–20 minutes, after which you need a short break or a change in activity.

### Effective approaches

*   **Step -by -step instructions:** The principle of "one action at a time."
*   **Visual support:** Check lists, diagrams, mental cards, highlighting keywords.
*   **Clear rhythm:** Application of techniques like "Prydor" (20 minutes work, 5 minutes rest).
*   **Predictability:** The organized environment reduces the cognitive load.
*   **Pause before action:** Self -regulation training: read, pause, comprehend, start.

### Obstacles in learning

*   **Multitasking:** Simultaneous actions lead to errors or failure.
*   **Long oral instructions:** More than 2-3 teams are difficult to hold.
*   **Hurry and time Tale:** Enhance impulsiveness, reduce the quality of work.
*   **Sensory overload:** Noise, disorder, accumulation of people exhaust attention.

# 3. Practical strategies

### Home environment

*   **Organization of work:**
    *   Miss large tasks into small steps. Use checklists for planning.
    *   Start with the most complex items while the concentration is maximum.
    *   Apply the "one item on the table" to minimize abstracts.
    *   Plan short 5-minute physical breaks every 15-20 minutes.
*   **Support and motivation:**
    *   Mark efforts and process, and not just the result. Example: "It is great that you followed the plan!"
    *   Instead of reproaches, offer a break: "I see, you are tired. Let's rest and return to the task."
    *   Support the calm, predictable routine of the day.

### School environment

*   **The wording of tasks:**
    *   The instructions should be short, toned, given one at a time. Duplicate oral tasks in writing.
    *   Make sure of understanding: ask the child to retell the task.
*   **Structure and space:**
    *   Recommended place: 1-2 desks, in the center, far from distracting factors (window, door).
    *   Use visual timers to control the tasks.
    *   Provide drafts and extra time for self -testing in control work.
*   **Individual approach (IOM):**
    *   Allow the use of simple organizers or checklists.
    *   Let me take large projects in parts.
    *   Encourage the use of self -control strategies (for example, raising a hand before the answer).

# 4. Exercises for development

The proposed games and exercises are aimed at developing key cognitive skills. Adhere to regular classes in a playful way, avoiding coercion.

*   **Attention and braking:**
    *   **Correct samples:** Removing/bypassing the specified characters in the text.
    *   **Board games:** "High -speed caps", "Dobbl", "drum" - develop a reaction and analysis.
    *   **The game "Yes and no, do not say":** He trains control of automatic reactions.

*   **Working memory:**
    *   **The game "Memory":** Classic exercise for visual memory.
    *   **Repeat of sequences:** Removing and reproducing a number of objects with a gradual increase in complexity.
    *   **Graphic dictations:** Drawing on cells for dictation - develops the retention of instructions and attention.

*   **Body control and rhythm:**
    *   **Games "Sea is worried once", "Frozen":** Teach a quick stop at a signal.
    *   **Balance exercises:** Walking along the line, stand on one leg (with open/closed eyes).
    *   **Tapping rhythms:** Repetition of rhythmic patterns - develops a sense of time and internal control.
