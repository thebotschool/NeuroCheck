**Cognitive profile: **

This report reveals the cognitive characteristics of the student, offering strategies for effective learning and development. It is addressed to parents and teachers.

### **1. Overview of cognitive tests**

*   **Memory and organization (CPT):** The ability to memorize information is high. The development of data structuring and sequence of actions is required.
*   **Self-control (Go/No-Go):** Fast, sometimes impulsive reactions were noted. It is recommended to develop a deliberate approach to tasks.
*   **Sustainability of attention (TCP):** There is a decrease in the focus with monotonous, audio-oriented presentation of information. Attention requires active maintenance.

<!-- page-break -->

### **2. The guide for the success of your student**

#### Individual characteristics

*   **Pace:** Fast, gusty. The tendency to the beginning of actions without complete perception of instructions.
*   **Perception:** Priority of a visual organization. Effectively assimilates information through diagrams, tables, lists, intelligence cards.
*   **Focus of attention:** Pulsating. Productive intervals for 20-25 minutes, then a pause is required. Sensitivity to external and internal distracting factors.

#### Supporting strategies

*   Visual anchors: checklists, diagrams, highlighting keywords.
*   Clear algorithms: step -by -step instructions.
*   Work intervals: approach 25/5 minutes (Tomato method).
*   Pause for thinking: the formation of a habit of thinking before action.
*   Rhythm and structure: predictable schedule, sequence of actions.

#### Factors that reduce efficiency

*   Multivativity: simultaneously performing several tasks or quick switching between them.
*   Long oral lectures: monotonous presentation without visual support.
*   The pressure of time and rush: situations requiring a quick, rash answer.
*   Information noise: distracting external sounds, conversations, crowded spaces.

<!-- page-break -->

### **3. Practical recommendations**

#### Home environment

*   **Performing tasks:**
    *   Decomposition: Separate large tasks into small stages.
    *   Timing: use a timer (20-25 minutes of work, 5 minutes of rest).
    *   Planning: Form a short work plan before the start.
*   **Parental support:**
    *   Focus on the process: Discuss steps and strategies, and not just the result.
    *   Promotion: appreciate attentiveness, accuracy, deliberation of actions.
*   **The optimal interval of attention:** 20-25 minutes for one task.

#### School environment

*   **Design of instructions:**
    *   Step -by -step: Use numb and marking lists.
    *   Lesson plan: voice the structure of the lesson.
*   **Structuring the material:**
    *   Visual schemes: use intelligence cards, structural plans for projects.
    *   Presentations: a minimum of text, a maximum of visual schemes.
*   **Optimal place:** The front desk, far from distracting zones (windows, doors).
*   **Style style:** Interactive. Use questions, ask to retell to maintain attention.
*   **Adaptation (IOOM):**
    *   An additional time to check the work.
    *   The use of drafts for planning.
    *   The preference for written exams is oral.

<!-- page-break -->

### **4. Developing exercises**

The following exercises contribute to the development of key cognitive skills. Integrate them into everyday practice.

*   **Attention and self -control:**
    *   **Correct test:** Search and highlighting certain letters in the text (start from 5 minutes, gradually complicate).
    *   **Board games:** Chess, checkers, “set”, “vibble” (train strategic thinking, impulse control).
*   **Memory and sequence:**
    *   **Drawing up instructions:** Creation of step -by -step guides for simple actions.
    *   **Pictures retelling:** Visualization of the plot through key personnel after reading or viewing.
*   **Rhythm and bodily control:**
    *   **The game "Delay-Otomory":** Instant stopping of movements at the signal (develops a reaction, inhibition).
    *   **Juggling, picking the ball:** Exercises for concentration, coordination, a sense of rhythm.
