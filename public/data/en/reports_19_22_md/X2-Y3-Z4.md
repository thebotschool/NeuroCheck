### Cognitive assessment report: CPT+Go/No-Go+TCP (19–22 years old)

This report is designed to help understand the features of the cognitive development of the student. It contains practical recommendations for the use of a house and at school to make the learning process comfortable and productive.

---

### 1. Test results

*   **Test of visual working memory (CPT):** The general idea is well remembered. Visual details can be lost or distorted, especially when distracting. It is important to develop visual accuracy.
*   **Braking Test (Go/No-Go):** The usual actions of the leading hand are easily performed. Difficulties arise when using both sides of the body or in unusual situations. The development of coordination and interhemispheric ties is required.
*   **Attention test (TCP):** Concentration and self -control are reduced. This can be a sign of mental fatigue or overload. Support is necessary to prevent burnout.

### 2. The guide for the success of your student

#### Individual characteristics

*   **The pace of learning:** Mastering information takes time. Hurry reduces accuracy and concentration.
*   **Information perception:** Preference - clear visual information (diagrams, infographics). Details in a dense text can be missed.
*   **Duration of concentration:** Attention is unstable, quickly exhausted. The optimal focus on the task: 15–20 minutes, especially in the presence of distracting factors.

#### Effective approaches

*   **Step -by -step instructions:** Separation of large tasks into 2-3 small steps.
*   **Visual supports:** Check lists, schemes, marker allocation, short supporting notes.
*   **Rhythm and predictability:** A clear daily routine, predictable structure of classes.
*   **Clear short goals:** Focusing at small, achievable stages of the task.
*   **Tactile and motor breaks:** Short pauses for physical activity.

#### Barriers in training

*   **Multitasking:** The simultaneous performance of several actions causes overload.
*   **Long oral instructions:** Only the beginning of information is remembered.
*   **Noise and visual mess:** Reduce productivity.
*   **Rush and pressure:** They cause anxiety, block mental processes.
*   **Complex, overloaded with details Materials:** Long texts without clarity are read superficially.

### 3. Recommendations

#### For the house

*   **Homework:**
    *   Divide into blocks for 15–20 minutes with 5-minute breaks.
    *   Use a timer to visualize the time of work and rest.
    *   Make a simple plan before starting: "First mathematics, then a break."
    *   Organize the workplace by removing the excess.
*   **Promotion and support:**
    *   Praise for the effort: "I see how you tried to concentrate!"
    *   Do not scold for inattention. Instead: "Let's find a mistake together. Are you distracted?"
*   **Optimum duration of work:**
    *   Effective concentration on a difficult task: **15–20 minutes**. Regular breaks are important.

#### For school

*   **Instructions:**
    *   Formulate briefly, clearly, one at a time.
    *   Duplicate oral tasks in writing (board, card).
    *   Install visual contact before the task, contact by name.
*   **Organization of training:**
    *   Place the student closer to the teacher, away from distracting factors (window, door).
    *   Actively use visual materials: schemes, tables, intelligence cards. Select the main thing in color.
    *   Divide big topics into small logical blocks.
*   **Individual approach:**
    *   Provide extra time for tests.
    *   Allow the use of a draft, a reading line.
    *   Divide large tasks into parts with an intermediate check.

### 4. Recommended training

Developing training. Support and development of key skills. Regularity is recommended in a playful way.

*   **For attention and visual accuracy (CPT):**
    *   **"Find 10 differences":** Develops attentiveness to details.
    *   **Schultte tables:** Search for numbers for a while. Start with 3x3 tables.
    *   **Correct samples:** Refusing certain letters in the text.
    *   **Graphic dictations:** Drawing on cells under dictation.

*   **For coordination and interhemispheric interaction (Go/No-Go):**
    *   **Juggling:** Start with two balls.
    *   **Balance exercises:** Walking along the line, stand on one leg (with open/closed eyes).
    *   **"Lazy eights":** Drawing an infinity sign with both hands at the same time.
    *   **Finger gymnastics:** Exercises such as "ring" or "fist-rib-ladon".

*   **For memory and rhythm (TCP):**
    *   **Memory game:** Search for paired cards.
    *   **"Snowball":** Repetition and addition of the chain of words.
    *   **Tapping rhythms:** Repetition of a given rhythm.
