# Cognitive assessment report

This report provides key data on the student’s cognitive characteristics, highlighting his strengths and areas for development. The goal is to offer practical recommendations to optimize the educational process and everyday life.

# 1. Summary of test results

## Working memory and strategy (CPT)
A high concentration, the ability to memorize complex information and strategic planning, even if there are distracting factors, have been noted. This indicates developed self -control and strong analytical abilities.

## Braking and self-control (Go/No-Go)
Difficulties in containing impulsive reactions and control of actions, especially with fatigue or stress, have been identified. This can signal overwork or difficulties with priorities.

## Sustainability of attention (TCP)
There is increased distractiveness. Maintaining a long focus on one task is difficult, especially with monotonous presentation of information.

## General profile
The student has high intellectual potential and pronounced analytical abilities. However, productivity can be reduced due to difficulties with self-control and mild distraction. The brain functions as a powerful system sensitive to external interference.

# 2. The guide for the success of your student

## Individual profile
*   **The pace of work.** Optimal productivity is achieved at a measured, personal pace. Hurrying or pressure lead to errors and quality reduction. It takes time for deep immersion in the task.
*   **Perception of information.** Visually structured information is most effectively absorbed. Long audio materials or unstructured text quickly reduce concentration.
*   **Focus of attention.** Attention works like a "spotlight", allowing you to deeply analyze the details. However, it is easy to remove it with external stimuli or fatigue.

## Support factors
*   **Visualization.** Check lists, mental cards, diagrams, laconic presentations.
*   **A step -by -step approach.** Decomposition of large tasks for small, specific steps.
*   **Structured environment.** A quiet workplace with a minimum of distracting factors, order.
*   **Interval mode.** The use of techniques like "tomato" (25/5 minutes) to maintain concentration.

## Interviewing factors
*   **Multitasking.** Attempts to perform several cases simultaneously lead to errors and frustration.
*   **Monotone.** Long, non -interactive lectures or volumetric unstructured text.
*   **Uncertainty.** Fuzzy or vague tasks cause a stupor.
*   **Noise and chaos.** External conversations, notifications, open tabs are significant distracting factors.

# 3. Recommendations

## Home environment
*   **Organization of work.**
    *   Make a short plan of 2-3 steps before the start of the task.
    *   Use the timer: 30 minutes of work, 10 minutes of rest (change of activity, not a screen).
    *   Remove the phone from the visibility zone.
*   **Support and motivation.**
    *   Help in planning tasks, and not in their implementation. Example: "What is the terminal plan for this week? What is the first step?"
    *   Encourage the process and perseverance, and not just the result. To note a deep immersion in the topic in concentration.
*   **Holding attention.**
    *   Deep concentration lasts 25-40 minutes, after which a short break is required for the "reboot".

## Academic environment
*   **The formulation of tasks.**
    *   Provide tasks in writing, with clear criteria and deadlines.
    *   Use the verbs of action: “compare”, “make up”, “describe”.
*   **The structure of the feed.**
    *   Lectures to accompany presentations (schemes, theses). Provide materials in advance.
    *   Periodically ask targeted questions to maintain a focus.
*   **Choosing a place.**
    *   It is recommended to sit closer to the teacher, away from distracting factors (windows, doors).
    *   The ability to work in a quiet room for important control or exams.
*   **Individualization of training.**
    *   The emphasis on design and research activities for the implementation of strengths (strategic thinking, analysis).
    *   Consider the adaptation of exam formats (for example, written instead of oral) to reduce stress and impulsivity.

# 4. Recommended training

These exercises are aimed at developing key skills, strengthening strengths and compensation for growth zones.

## Attention and self -control
*   **Consciousness practices (MindFulness).** Short daily meditation (5-10 minutes) using applications (Headspace, Calm). Develop the ability to notice distraction and return the focus.
*   **Digital hygiene.** Installation of locks by distracting sites and applications (Freedom, Cold Turkey) for the duration of studies.

## Coordination and rhythm
*   **Juggling.** Develops concentration, visual-motor coordination and resistance to errors.
*   **Rhythmic games.** Games that require pressing music (OSU!, Guitar Hero) or exercises with a metronome.

## Bodily control
*   **Yoga or tai chi.** They contribute to the development of the connection of the mind and body, improve control and breathing control, and reduce overall impulsiveness.

## Memory
*   **Mnemonics.** Study and application of techniques of memorization (for example, "palaces of memory"). It turns the process of learning into a strategic game, using the strengths.
