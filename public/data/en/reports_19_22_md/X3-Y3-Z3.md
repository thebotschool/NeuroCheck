# Cognitive profile: attention, control, memory

### Assessment results report

This report is designed to provide clarity in the cognitive features of the student, based on test results. Information is aimed at improving the effectiveness of training and daily activities.

#### 1. Key conclusions

*   **Attention:** The tendency to a quick reaction can lead to impulsive errors. A pause is important before making decisions, especially in responsible situations.
*   **Control:** The usual tasks are easily performed. Difficulties arise when using both hands or in non -standard conditions. This indicates the need to strengthen interhemispheric coordination.
*   **Memory:** Separate facts and images are well remembered. However, difficulties arise with building them in a sequence or holding a step -by -step plan.

#### 2. The guide for the success of your student

This section describes the unique style of thinking and teaching a student.

##### Individual profile

*   **Pace:** Fast, gusty. Included to act until comprehension, especially under pressure.
*   **Perception:** Fragmented. It captures the details, but with difficulty sees the overall structure and interconnection. A visual "map" is needed to combine information.
*   **Attention:** High focus at short distances. It is depleted by stress or large volume of unstructured information. Hurry provokes errors.

##### Support factors

*   **Visual supports:** Schemes, intelligence cards, infographics. Everything that helps *see* structure.
*   **Step -by -step instructions:** Clear algorithms (step 1, step 2) reduce the cognitive load, preventing confusion in the sequence.
*   **The habit of "pause-verification":** Stop for a few seconds before the answer or delivery of work for self -testing.
*   **Rhythm and structure:** A clear schedule, predictable change of activities, rhythmic exercises for the "tuning" of the brain.

##### Containing factors

*   **Multitasking:** An attempt to carry out several cases simultaneously at the same time is almost guaranteed to lead to errors.
*   **Time deficiency:** Control work and tests for a while are a strong stress factor provoking impulsive answers.
*   **Long oral instructions:** A large amount of information by ear without visual support overloads the memory.
*   **Unstructured material:** A solid text or lecture without a clear plan makes it possible to make perception.

#### 3. Recommendations

##### Home environment

*   **Performing tasks:**
    *   Miss large tasks into small, understandable steps. For example, for an essay: find sources, make a plan, write a draft.
    *   Use gliders and checklists. Drawing up a work plan helps a student to feel control and see a sequence.
    *   The optimal duration of continuous work: 25-30 minutes. After that, a short (5 minutes) physical break is needed.
*   **Support:**
    *   Encourage methodology and accuracy, not speed. Mark the preparation of plans or self -test.
    *   Analyze errors as opportunities for growth. Discuss how to avoid rush in the future.

##### School environment

*   **Setting tasks:** Provide tasks in writing, divided into points. Use markers, numbering, highlighting keywords.
*   **Lesson structure:** Surrender and record a lesson plan on the board. This creates a predictable and comfortable environment.
*   **Accommodation:** If possible, minimize distracting factors (for example, not at the window). The proximity to the teacher contributes to better visual contact.
*   **Material feed:** Actively use visual materials: schemes, tables, presentations. Algorithms and rules to paint in steps.
*   **Individual support:**
    *   Provide extra time (15-20%) on control and test work for self-testing.
    *   Allow the use of drafts for response planning (especially for essays and statements).

#### 4. Recommended training

These exercises are aimed at developing key cognitive functions. It is important that they are perceived as a game.

*   **Attention and control:**
    *   **Strategic Games:** Chess, checkers, jackal. They teach to plan moves, wait and restrain the impulse.
    *   **Correct test:** Find and cross out certain letters in the text. Start from 5 minutes, gradually increasing time.
*   **Coordination and interhemispheric interaction:**
    *   **Juggling:** Start with two items. Perfectly synchronizes the work of the hemispheres.
    *   **Neurohymnastics:** Simple coordination exercises performed daily for 3-5 minutes. Example: "Fist-Robric Ladon."
    *   **Musical instrument:** Playing piano, guitar or drums requires coordinated work of both hands.
*   **Memory and sequence:**
    *   **Assembly according to the instructions:** Lego Technic designers, prefabricated models, origami. The task is to definitely follow a step -by -step scheme.
    *   **Creation of rebukes:** After watching the film or reading the head of the book, draw 4-6 key personnel in the correct order.
    *   **Recipe retelling:** Read a simple culinary recipe and ask to retell it by steps without missing the sequence.
