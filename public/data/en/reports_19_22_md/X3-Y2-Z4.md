### Cognitive assessment report
CPT+Go/No-GO+TCP. Age group: 19–22 years old

This report offers a detailed view of the cognitive abilities of your student, identifying key strengths and areas for development. It contains practical recommendations to support learning and growth.

---

### 1. A brief report

*   **Working memory (CPT):** It remembers the facts well. He experiences difficulties with building them in a sequence.
*   **Self-control (Go/No-Go):** Prone to impulsive actions and quick decisions without complete reflection.
*   **Sustainability of attention (TCP):** Attention is absent -minded, it is difficult to quickly respond to tasks. Mental overload or fatigue are possible.

<!-- page-break -->

### 2. The guide for the success of your student

### Features of perception

*   **Pace:** Fast but unstable. The schoolboy strives for a quick start, but loses concentration on complex or long -term tasks.
*   **Perception format:** Prefers visual and step -by -step information. He experiences difficulties with long texts and oral instructions without visual support.
*   **Focus of attention:** Unstable attention. It is difficult to maintain a concentration of more than 15–20 minutes, especially in the presence of distracting factors.

### Supporting factors

*   **Visualization:** Schemes, tables, mental cards, infographics. Everything that structures information.
*   **Step -by -step instructions:** Check lines with clear, short stages that can be marked as they perform.
*   **Pause before action:** The technique "Stop, think, do it." Helps to form an internal pause.
*   **Interval mode:** Work on the timer (for example, 20 minutes of activity, 5 minutes of rest) to maintain energy.
*   **Focus on one task:** A clear definition of one goal, avoiding multitasking.

### Distracting factors

*   **Multitasking:** The simultaneous performance of several actions leads to errors and frustration.
*   **Time pressure:** Phrases like “faster” or “5 minutes left” enhance impulsiveness.
*   **Long oral instructions:** The student quickly loses the essence after the first or second minute.
*   **External interference:** Noise, notifications, mess on the table easily distract attention.
*   **Unstructured material:** Continuous text without highlighting key thoughts or visual elements.

<!-- page-break -->

### 3. Recommendations

### Home environment

*   **Divide the tasks:** Break large homework into small, controlled parts. For example: "We will solve the first three examples."
*   **Use the timer:** Organize the work with short “sprints” (20 minutes) with mandatory breaks for physical activity.
*   **Visual plan:** Create a schedule or checklist of tasks for the evening with a schoolboy.
*   **Focus for attentiveness:** Praise for thoroughness and deliberation, and not for speed. "You have double -checked yourself - it's great!"
*   **Active participation:** Instead of the question "Have you done lessons?" Ask: “What is in the plan today? Why do you start? Need help with the organization? "
*   **Minimalism on the table:** In the workplace, only the objects necessary for the current task should be.

### School environment

*   **Step -by -step instructions:** Give the tasks one step at a time.
*   **Visual reinforcement:** Duplicate oral instructions in writing on a board or in the form of a printout. Use numb lists.
*   **Checking understanding:** Ask the student in your own words to repeat what you need to do.
*   **Lesson plan:** Announced the structure of the lesson at the beginning: "Today we will repeat ... we will study ... we will fulfill ...".
*   **Visual supports:** Use schemes, presentations, short videos for clarity.
*   **Optimal place:** Put at the first-second desk, away from distracting factors (window, door).
*   **Individual approach:**
    *   Give additional time in the test work.
    *   Allow me to use drafts to plan answers.
    *   Prefer tasks that require thoughtful, step -by -step execution (for example, small projects).

### 4. Developing practices

Develop skills through the game. These practices are easily integrated into everyday life.

### Attention and self -control

*   **Correct test:** Find and cross out certain characters on the page (for example, all letters “a”).
*   **Board games:** Dobble, drum, jenga. Develop a rapid reaction and concentration.
*   **Edible-universal:** Classical game of switching attention and braking of impulses.

### Memory and sequence

*   **Pictures story:** Lay out 3-5 plot pictures in random order. Ask to restore the sequence and make a story.
*   **Lego/Models:** Assembly according to the instructions. He trains algorithmic thinking and following steps.
*   **Memory (find a couple):** Training of visual memory and attention.

### Bodily control

*   **The sea is worried:** Exercise for body control and the ability to instantly "freeze" on command.
*   **Rhythm:** Tackle the simple rhythm, let the student repeat it for sure. Gradually complicate.
*   **Juggling:** The coordination of the “hands-eye” and concentration (even with two balls).
