### **2. Profile of a student: Instruction for a teacher**

This leadership will help to understand how the student perceives and processes information.

#### Key features

*   **Pace:** Dual. Quickly and accurately copes with understandable tasks. In complex or requiring planning situations, haste is possible.
*   **Perception:** Optimally assimilates visually and structurally presented information: schemes, maps, algorithms. Oral explanations require visual support.
*   **Focus:** Deep concentration is possible with clarity of the task and plan. The lack of structure can lead to a loss of focus and impulsiveness. Self -control intensifies when understanding the goal and method.

#### Success factors

*   **Visualization:** Schemes, tables, mental cards, infographics. Tools that reveal the overall structure.
*   **Algorithms:** Clear, step -by -step instructions. Decomposition of tasks reduces impulsiveness and increases efficiency.
*   **The principle of "Think - do - check":** Stimulating a conscious pause before action.
*   **Structure and predictability:** A calm, predetermined environment with an understandable sequence of actions.

#### Obstacles

*   **Multitasking:** Simultaneous performance of several tasks or frequent switching.
*   **Abstract tasks:** The lack of clear criteria and plan provokes disorganization and rush.
*   **Time limitation:** Enhances the tendency to impulsive decisions.
*   **Information overload:** A large amount of oral information without visual or structural support.

### **3. Development: practical recommendations**

#### Home environment

*   **Homework approach:**
    *   **Planning:** Before you start, make a simple plan: goals, steps, terms, testing method.
    *   **Decomposition of tasks:** Separate large projects into small, controlled stages.
    *   **Working intervals:** Effective concentration persists for 25-30 minutes. Use the “Prydor” technique (25 minutes of work, 5 minutes of break). For complex tasks, intervals can be reduced to 15–20 minutes.
*   **Support and encouragement:**
    *   **Accent on the process:** Mark thoughtfulness, following the plan, self -test, and not just speed.
    *   **Questions that stimulate reflection:** Instead of "Have you finished?" Ask: “What is your plan? Let's check if all the steps are completed? ”

#### Educational process

*   **The wording of tasks:**
    *   **Algorithms and checklists:** Provide a clear structure for analyzing or completing tasks. Example: “1. Highlight the heroes. 2. Describe the conflict. 3. Find 3 examples. 4. Make a conclusion. "
    *   **Templates:** For design work, offer ready -made structures templates.
*   **Lesson structure:**
    *   **Visual plan:** At the beginning of the lesson, voice and fix the plan on the board.
    *   **Visual supports:** Determines, formulas, dates draw up visually (diagrams, lists). Use colors to select logical blocks.
*   **Learning strategies:**
    *   **Project activity:** High self -control and the ability to work in certain conditions make the student suitable for the role of the coordinator or performer of specific design blocks.
    *   **Teaching information processing methods:** Mastering mental maps, techniques for working with text (emphasizing, planning), self -test skills.

    *   **Musical instruments:** Drums, piano. Stimulate the coordinated work of hands and rhythm.
    *   **Dancing:** Learning choreographic ligaments. He trains the memory of movements and sequence.
