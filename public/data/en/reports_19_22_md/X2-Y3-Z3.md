## Cognitive profile. Analysis.

Age: 19–22 years.

This report reveals the cognitive profile of the student, focusing on attention, control and memory. The goal is to identify strengths and growth zones, offering recommendations for the optimal environment of training and development.

---

## 1. Test results.

*   **Attention (CPT/TCP).** The answers are often impulsive, especially with time limiting. This leads to errors avoiding with a thoughtful approach.
*   **Self-control (Go/No-Go).** The control of the leading hand is much easier. Tasks requiring coordination of both hands require great efforts and concentration.
*   **Visual working memory.** Memory is effective. However, there is a tendency to surface perception of information. Perhaps distortion of parts or confusion of similar objects during distraction.

<!-- page-break -->

## 2. Potential. Guide for the student.

### Individual profile.

*   **Pace.** Fast, gusty. The tendency to action without preliminary understanding. The measured rhythm with time for self -testing is optimal.
*   **Perception.** Better assimilates structured data. Perceives the whole, but can miss the details without their explicit discharge.
*   **Focus.** Attention can be changeable. A good concentration decreases with fatigue or stress, leading to impulsive reactions.

### Which helps.

*   **A pause before action.** A conscious stop prevents impulsive errors.
*   **Step -by -step instructions.** Separation of tasks into stages with an intermediate check.
*   **Visual "anchors".** Selection of key information: markers, emphasizing, brief schemes. Helps to fix the details.
*   **Rhythm and activity.** Rhythmic movements (for example, tapping, walking) synchronize brain activity, improving concentration.

### What interferes.

*   **Time pressure.** High -speed tests or control increase stress and number of errors.
*   **Multitasking.** Simultaneous perception, recording and analysis without a clear structure cause fatigue and reduce the quality of work.
*   **Visual noise.** Overloaded texts, the mess at the workplace disperses attention.
*   **A sharp change in tasks.** A quick transition between activities without a pause.

<!-- page-break -->

## 3. Recommendations. Development support.

### Home environment.

*   **Homework.**
    *   **The technique of a prying.** 25 minutes of work, 5 minutes of break. Supports concentration.
    *   Start with the most difficult tasks.
    *   Install a clear plan before starting work.
*   **Support.**
    *   Focus on the process: "Take a hurry, double -check."
    *   Encourage independent correction of errors and planning.
*   **Organization of space.**
    *   The desktop is free from distracting items. The phone is outside the access zone.

### School environment.

*   **Instructions.**
    *   A clear, step -by -step feed. Keywords are highlighted.
    *   Example: "Read the paragraph (1). Select three key terms (2). Give an example to each (3)."
*   **The structure of classes.**
    *   An additional 5-10 minutes on tests for self -test. This is critical.
    *   Placing at a desk with a minimum of distracting factors.
*   **Material feed.**
    *   Visual supports: diagrams, intelligence cards, timline. Help to structure and remember.
    *   Encouragement of the use of drafts.
*   **Individual route (IOM).**
    *   Consolidation of the right to extra time for verification.
    *   Access to supporting abstracts or the use of personal schemes.

## 4. Development. Practical exercises.

These exercises contribute to the development of identified skills. Turn them in everyday life in a playful way.

*   **Attention and reduction of impulsiveness.**
    *   **Strategic Games:** Chess, checkers, go, "set". Develop planning and thought out in advance.
    *   **Correct samples:** Crossing or bypassing symbols in the text. Emphasis on quality, not speed.
*   **Intergribute coordination and motor skills.**
    *   **Juggling:** Synchronizes the brain and body. Start with two items.
    *   **Drawing with two hands:** Simultaneous creation of symmetrical patterns with both hands.
    *   **Musical instrument:** For example, piano or guitar requires well -coordinated hand.
*   **Visual memory and accuracy.**
    *   **Game "Find a couple" (Memory):** Develops memory and attention to details.
    *   **Exercise "Photographer":** Remembering the details of the room or image in 30 seconds.
    *   **Creation of intelligence cards:** Generalization of information from the text in the form of a scheme with key concepts. He trains the allocation of the main and memorization of the structure.
