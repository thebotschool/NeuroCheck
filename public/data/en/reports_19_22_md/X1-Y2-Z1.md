# Cognitive profile report

### Age: 19–22 years

This report offers a personal view of your cognitive abilities, highlighting the strengths and areas for growth. Recommendations are designed to help in independent training and interaction with teachers.

---

#### 1. Key discoveries

*   **Working memory and attention (CPT):** You demonstrate an excellent concentration ability. Effectively remember and hold complex information, even in the presence of distracting factors. This is your significant advantage in studying.

*   **Brake control (Go/No-Go):** A tendency to quick, sometimes hasty reactions was noted. Sometimes it is difficult to restrain the primary impulse, which can lead to hasty decisions.

*   **Sustainability of attention (TCP):** You are capable of prolonged and deep concentration. Your work remains accurate even with a long performance of one task, minimizing errors in carelessness.

*   **General profile:** You combine an outstanding ability to deep, prolonged concentration with a pronounced tendency to impulsive reactions. Your power is in the focus, and the call is in managing the decision speed.

<!-- page-break -->

#### 2. The guide for the success of your student

##### Your features

*   **Pace:** High. The rapid assimilation of information can lead to a passage of details or hasty conclusions.
*   **Perception:** Effectively assimilate structured, logically organized information. You work well with volumetric data with sufficient time for processing.
*   **Focus:** Deeply stable. Plunging into the task, you become less susceptible to external signals, which can interfere with timely change of tactics.

##### Supporting practices

*   **Pause before the answer:** A short "breath" before the reaction helps to avoid haste, especially in responsible situations.
*   **Planning:** Drawing up step-by-step plans and checklists for volumetric tasks. This slows down the process and structures thinking.
*   **Visual timers:** The Pomodoro technique (25/5 minutes) helps effectively control time and introduce regular pauses for self -control.
*   **Speaking:** Slasping the task (aloud or to yourself) slows down the thought process, preventing impulsive actions.

##### Calls

*   **Fast solutions without preparation:** Situations requiring an immediate response (blitz-surveys, unexpected questions) can provoke haste.
*   **Multitasking:** Frequent switching between tasks exhausts self -control, increasing the number of impulsive errors.
*   **Dedlines:** The approach of the deadlines can lead to haste and reducing the quality of work instead of mobilization.

<!-- page-break -->

#### 3. Development recommendations

##### Independent training

*   **Performing tasks:** Miss large tasks into stages. After each stage, pause for self-testing: "Is everything taken into account? Are there any mistakes because of rush?"
*   **Maintaining control:** Introduce "conscious pauses". For example, postpone the sending of important materials for 15-20 minutes for re-check.
*   **Promotion:** Praise yourself for accuracy and thoughtfulness, and not just for speed. The goal is speed management, not its suppression.
*   **Mercing of attention:** Effective concentration lasts 45-60 minutes. Use this, but be sure to plan breaks for "restarting" attention.

##### Interaction with teachers

*   **The wording of tasks:** Give preference to written work, projects, essays. They allow you to plan and edit. In oral exams, ask for time to think about an answer.
*   **Structuring information:** Assume clear evaluation criteria and lecture/project plans. This will help focus your deep concentration.
*   **Style style:** Ideal environment: calm, structured, with the ability to ask questions and without requirements for instant answers.
*   **Individual route:** Consider the courses on Soft Skills: "time management", "decision-making", "emotional regulation" will be especially useful.

<!-- page-break -->

#### 4. Practical training

*   **Attention and control:**
    *   **Board games:** "Uno", "Dobble", "Drum". They develop a quick reaction and the ability to restrain impulses.
    *   **Computer games:** Puzzles and strategies requiring deliberate solutions.

*   **Rhythm and coordination:**
    *   **Musical instruments:** Shocks, guitar. Develop a sense of rhythm, timeliness of the beginning and completion of actions.
    *   **Dancing:** Teach coordination of movements and following a given rhythm.

*   **Awareness and self -regulation:**
    *   **Meditation:** Applications (Calm, Headspace, Meditopia) will help to observe thoughts and impulses without an immediate reaction. Start with 5 minutes daily.
    *   **Chess, GO:** Great simulators to combat impulsivity. Develop strategic thinking and the ability to anticipate the consequences.
