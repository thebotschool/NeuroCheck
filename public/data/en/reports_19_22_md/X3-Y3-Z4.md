### 2. The guide for the success of your student

#### Individual features:

*   **Pace:** Uneven. High speed with familiar tasks, slowdown in a new or creative material requiring planning.
*   **Perception:** Visual-structural. Effectively assimilates the information presented clearly: schemes, step -by -step plans, tables, infographics. Continuous text or oral monologues are more complicated.
*   **Attention:** Unstable, exhausted. The concentration is reduced with a large stream of information, noise or multitasking. Requires external organization and support.

#### Which helps:

*   **Step -by -step instructions.** Clear, short algorithms of action.
*   **Visual supports.** Mental cards, schemes, timline, stickers.
*   **Predictable rhythm.** Work of intervals (for example, 25/5 minutes) to maintain concentration.
*   **Organized environment.** The order at the workplace, the lack of background noise.

#### What interferes:

*   **Multitasking.** Parallel execution of several cases or quick switching.
*   **Oral instructions.** Information without clarity is difficult to structured and is quickly forgotten.
*   **Hurry and pressure.** Sudden checks, tasks “for speed” cause stress.
*   **Large volumes without structure.** Long texts or tasks without breaking into parts.

<!-- page-break -->

---

### 3. Recommendations

#### At home:

1.  **Homework organization:**
    *   Miss large tasks into small steps. Make a plan (for example, on the sticker: "Read, write out dates, answer orally").
    *   The optimal concentration time: 20–25 minutes. Then - a short break (5-7 minutes) for physical activity or rest.
    *   Use visual means: markers, colored stickers, online services for mental cards.

2.  **Support and motivation:**
    *   Praise for the process: for the plan, for concentration, for efforts. This reduces anxiety and develops internal motivation.
    *   Before complex tasks, perform short coordination exercises (see section 4).

#### At school:

1.  **The wording of tasks:**
    *   Duplicate oral instructions in writing (on the board, in the planner).
    *   Use clear verbs: “emphasize”, “write down”, “compare”, “draw up a diagram”.
    *   Structure the material: lists, paragraphs, color, scheme.

2.  **Organization of the process:**
    *   Optimal place: the first-second desk in the center, a minimum of distracting factors.
    *   Give the time to "include" in the task. Do not require an instant answer.
    *   For design work: Provide a template or step -by -step plan. Construct you regularly to control the stages.

### 4. Recommended training

These exercises contribute to the development of the necessary skills. Perform them regularly, in a playful way, for 5-10 minutes daily.

*   **For attention and self -control:**
    *   **Corrective test.** In the newspaper, delete all the letters “A”, circle “O” for 5 minutes. Gradually complicate.
    *   **Board games.** "Jenga", "set", chess. Develop concentration and control.

*   **For coordination and interhemispheric interaction:**
    *   **Juggling.** Start with two balls. The goal is to improve the coordination of the hands and eyes.
    *   **Asynchronous gymnastics for the fingers.** For example: one hand "class", the other "Okay". Change the position by cotton.
    *   **Exercises on the balance sheet** Or walking along the line.

*   **For memory for the sequence:**
    *   **Compilation of comics.** After reading/viewing: 4-6 key personnel reflecting the sequence.
    *   **The game "Snow Com".** Repetition of a chain of words with the addition of a new.

*   **For the development of a sense of rhythm:**
    *   **Tapping rhythms.** Set a rhythmic pattern. Ask to repeat. Gradually complicate. You can use a metronome.
