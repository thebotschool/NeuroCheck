# Cognitive profile: Assessment and potential

This report reveals the cognitive characteristics of the student. The recommendations presented are designed to create optimal conditions for learning and growth.

---

### 1. Evaluation. Key conclusions

*   **Visual memory (CPT).** The perception is fast, but sometimes superficial. The replacements of the original images are similar.
*   **Self-control (Go/No-Go).** There are difficulties with the suppression of impulsive reactions, especially with fatigue or stress.
*   **Sustainability of attention (TCP).** High distraction. The monotonous presentation of information or the lack of visual support causes rapid fatigue.

<!-- page-break -->

### 2. The guide for the success of your student

#### Personal features

*   **Pace.** A slow pace with short, planned pauses is preferred. Hurry increases the number of errors and impulsiveness.
*   **Perception format.** Visual format. Information is better absorbed through the schemes, tables, infographics, color release. Oral speech or simple text is less effective.
*   **Attention.** Unrestrained, "scanning." Easily switches to external stimuli. Holding concentration on one task requires an external structure.

#### Efficiency support

*   Step -by -step instructions. Check lists. Separation of tasks into small steps.
*   Visual anchors. Schemes, Mayland cards, stickers, highlighting key phrases.
*   Work on the timer (for example, the "tomato" method). Work intervals (20-25 min) with breaks (5 min).
*   A clear, predictable routine. Alternation of activity and rest.

#### Factors that reduce efficiency

*   Multitasking (music, TV, messengers).
*   Monotonous, long explanations without interactive.
*   Information overload. Large volume of unstructured material.
*   Tasks with open wording. Abstract instructions.

<!-- page-break -->

### 3. Recommendations

#### Home environment

*   **Organization of work**
    *   Make a plan of 2-4 specific steps.
    *   Use a timer for work and breaks (25/5 minutes).
    *   Remove distracting factors: unnecessary items, notifications on the phone.
*   **Support and motivation**
    *   Note not the result, but the efforts and process.
    *   Help structure the tasks, not solve for the student.
*   **Duration of concentration**
    *   Effective concentration lasts 20-25 minutes. Then a short break for rebooting is needed.

#### School environment

*   **Instructions**
    *   Use clear, verb instructions.
*   **The structure of the lesson**
    *   Start a lesson with the voicing of the plan.
    *   Present the information with blocks, supplementing with visual supports.
*   **Визуальные Якоря**
    *   Place key visual materials (formulas, rules) on the board/slide in a permanent place.
*   **Material feed**
    *   Interactive style is preferred: questions, examples, mini-surveys.
*   **Individual approach (IOM)**
    *   To reduce the load in control work and exams, consider the adaptations:
        *   Extra time.
        *   Written form of response.
        *   Using the support sheet (formula, plan).

<!-- page-break -->

### 4. Development. Practice

These exercises will help to develop key skills. Practice them in a playful way, without coercion.

*   **Attention. Accuracy**
    *   "Find the differences." Classical exercise for attentiveness.
    *   Schultte tables. Develop peripheral vision, concentration rate.
*   **Coordination. Control**
    *   Juggling (start with two goals). Synchronizes the hemispheres, develops rhythm and impulse control.
    *   Games with the ball (beaten, catching with different hands). Develops rhythm and control.
*   **Memory. Structure**
    *   Mnemonics. Method "Palace of Memory", associations for memorizing information.
*   **Rhythm. Self -control**
    *   Tapping rhythms. Repetition of a given rhythm (clap, pencil). Gradually complicate.
    *   Strategic board games (chess, corridor, go). Develop ponds for actions, prevent impulsiveness.
