# Cognitive assessment: working memory, control, attention
### Age: 19–22 years

This report is designed to help understand the strengths of the student, his style of training and create optimal conditions for development and academic success.

---

### 1. Review of tests

*   **Working memory and sequence (CPT):** High ability to memorize and maintain complex information even with distracting factors. Effective use of reproduction strategies.
*   **Impulsive control (Go/No-Go):** Outstanding level of self -control. The ability to quickly make accurate, balanced decisions. Excellent coordination between processes of excitement and inhibition.
*   **Sustainability of attention (TCP):** The ability to long and productive concentration on the task. The minimum of errors caused by inattention or rush.

<!-- page-break -->

### 2. Learning style

#### Peculiarities

*   **Pace:** Measured and effective. Orientation on the quality of the process, avoiding impulsive errors.
*   **Perception:** The optimal assimilation of structured, logically built information. It is important to understand the overall picture and connections.
*   **Focus:** Deep and stable attention. The ability to long immersion in the task, minimizing external stimuli.

#### Which contributes to effectiveness

*   Visual schemes and intelligence cards.
*   Step -by -step plans for large projects.
*   The possibility of long -term work on one task.
*   A quiet, structured environment.

#### Which reduces productivity

*   Multitasking.
*   Chaotic presentation of information.
*   Too frequent change of tasks.
*   Strong background noise and constant interruptions.

<!-- page-break -->

### 3. Recommendations

#### For independent work

*   **Organization:** The “tomato” method (45-60 minutes of concentration, 10-15 minutes of a break) is ideal for long sessions.
*   **Support:** Create optimal conditions: quiet, comfortable place, access to resources. Trust his ability to independently plan.
*   **Promotion:** Mark the initiative, participation in complex projects, independence and completion of the begun.
*   **Attention:** It is able to hold the focus on a difficult task for up to 60–90 minutes. After that, a short break is needed.

#### For the educational environment

*   **The wording of tasks:** Research or design tasks with a clear goal are preferred, but freedom in the methods of its achievement.
*   **The structure of the material:** Submit training material systematic: with a plan, theses, schemes and key conclusions.
*   **Role in the team:** Effective as an analyst, strategist, editor. Maybe a mentor.
*   **Communication style:** The partnership approach (“Let's think together”) best stimulates its analytical abilities.
*   **Individual route:** Does not require special support. Participation in the programs of in -depth study, scientific conferences and internships is recommended.

<!-- page-break -->

### 4. Maintaining the form

The cognitive functions of the student are at a high level. These exercises are aimed at their further development, maintaining the flexibility of thinking and prophylaxis of burnout.

#### Attention and strategy

*   **Strategic board games:** Chess, Go, Economic Strategies. Develop long -term planning and concentration.
*   **Debate:** Teach a quick analysis of arguments and building a logical line.

#### Memory and flexibility

*   **Learning foreign languages:** Especially using interval systems.
*   **Methodology "Palace of Memory":** To remember large volumes of unrelated information.

#### Coordination and control

*   **Juggling:** Develops interhemispheric ties, the coordination of the “eye-hand” and concentration.
*   **Yoga or martial arts:** Teach control over the body, breathing and focus of attention.
