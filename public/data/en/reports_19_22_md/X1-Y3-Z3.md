### Cognitive assessment: individual report

This report was created to help you and the teachers to better understand the cognitive features of the student, his strengths and potential for development. The recommendations presented are aimed at creating a comfortable and effective environment for training and everyday life.

---

### 1. Evaluation results

*   **Visual working memory and strategy (CPT):** Outstanding ability to remember complex information and strategic planning. Demonstrates a high level of self -control and maturity of cognitive functions even in the presence of distracting factors.

*   **Braking and interhemispheric interaction (Go/No-Go):** Confident completion of tasks with a dominant hand. Difficulties with the other hand are noted, indicating the potential to enhance the coherence between the hemispheres of the brain. This can increase flexibility when performing non -standard tasks.

*   **Attention and impulsiveness (TCP):** The tendency to hasty, impulsive errors. In conditions of stress or time deficiency, the priority of speed over quality is possible.

### 2. The guide for the success of your student

#### Individual characteristics

*   **The pace of work:** Capable of deep, thoughtful work on complex tasks. In stressful situations (exams, deadlines) can switch to a hasty, impulsive regime, which leads to errors.
*   **Information perception:** Optimally assimilates logically structured information (schemes, plans, algorithms). It may experience difficulties while processing various types of data (for example, listen to a lecture and analyze the diagram).
*   **Focus of attention:** Capable of prolonged and deep concentration. However, concentration can be fragile: impulsiveness can lead to quick, but incorrect answers, especially at the end of the task.

#### Optimal conditions

*   **Visual supports:** The use of Mind Carts, diagrams and graphic gliders helps to structure information, corresponding to strategic thinking.
*   **Clear instructions:** Step-by-step guides and checks reduce the likelihood of impulsive errors, ensuring the sequence of tasks.
*   **Conscious pause:** Practice of the 2-3-second delay before making a decision or response. This method switches from the impulsive mode to a thoughtful one.
*   **Calm atmosphere:** The predictable environment without haste and external pressure allows you to fully use strategic thinking and deep analysis.

#### Factors that reduce efficiency

*   **Multitasking:** The simultaneous performance of several tasks quickly depleted self -control and provokes impulsiveness.
*   **Time limitation:** Tough time frame are the main trigger for hasty and less high -quality solutions.
*   **Unstructured information:** Chaotic lectures or tasks without a clear plan are poorly absorbed, since they do not offer supports for logical abilities.
*   **Fast change of activity:** Sharp switching between different types of tasks (for example, from the analysis of the text to mathematics) can cause difficulties due to insufficient flexibility of interhemispheric interaction.

### 3. Recommendations

#### Home environment

*   **Effective preparation:**
    *   Start large -scale tasks with drawing up a detailed plan, breaking them into small, understandable steps.
    *   Use the “Prydor” technique: 25 minutes of focused work, 5 minutes a break to maintain concentration and reduce fatigue.
    *   After completing the task, be sure to allocate time for a self -check for training a conscious pause and identifying impulsive errors.
*   **Support and motivation:**
    *   Encourage not for speed, but for quality and thoughtfulness. Create an atmosphere where deliberate solutions are valued.
    *   Long-term concentration (40-50 minutes) is possible with an interesting task. Short breaks are necessary to prevent exhaustion.

#### Educational environment

*   **Setting tasks:**
    *   Provide tasks with clockwork instructions and clear evaluation criteria.
    *   In testing and exams, it can be useful to provide a little more time to reduce stress and allow you to apply analytical abilities.
*   **Organization of training:**
    *   The student will be as effective as possible in design and research activities that require deep immersion, planning and analysis.
    *   At lectures, oral information is recommended to duplicate with visual schemes or presentations.
*   **Personal path of development:**
    *   Correctional support is not required. On the contrary, it is recommended to participate in scientific projects, olympiads and programs with increased academic load, where strategic skills will be in demand.
    *   It is possible to cooperate with the psychologist of the campus or participation in trainings on self -regulation and stress management for the development of control over impulsivity.

### 4. Recommended training

These exercises will strengthen the growth areas (self -control, interhemispheric interaction) and create a solid basis for further development.

*   **Attention and self -control:**
    *   **Board games:** Chess, go, checkers - games where success depends on strategy and patience.
    *   **Exercise "Find 10 differences":** Develops attentiveness to details, teaches not to rush to answer.
    *   **Conscious reading:** Reading the text with the subsequent retelling according to a pre-drawn up plan.

*   **Coordination and interhemispheric interaction:**
    *   **Juggling:** Effectively develops the coordination of the “hands-eye” and synchronizes the work of the hemispheres. You can start with two balls.
    *   **Game on a musical instrument:** Especially the piano or guitar, requiring the coordinated work of both hands.
    *   **Symmetric drawing:** Simultaneous drawing with two hands of the same figures, moving from the center to the periphery or vice versa.

*   **Body control and rhythm:**
    *   **Balance exercises:** Standing on one leg (you can with eyes closed), walking along the line. Helps to improve body sense and control impulses.
    *   **Dancing or martial arts:** Develop coordination, a sense of rhythm and the ability to perform complex sequences of movements.
