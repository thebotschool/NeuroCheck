# Report: Cognitive profile

This report is an analysis of the cognitive characteristics of the student. It is developed for parents and teachers, offering structured recommendations for revealing potential and creating an optimal education environment.

---

## 1. Analysis: Key indicators

### Visual Working Memory (CPT)

Fast processing of general visual data. Attention is required to the details; The tendency to inaccuracies or additions of images.

### Reactions inhibition (Go/No-Go)

Impulsiveness is present. A conscious pause is required before action to minimize errors.

### Sustainability of attention (TCP)

Difficulties with prolonged concentration, especially on monotonous tasks. Discovery and loss of the essence of information transmitted by ear are possible.

## 2. Student profile

### Key features

*   **Pace:** Fast, gusty. The tendency to immediate answers. Intentional slowdown is required.
*   **Perception:** Optimally - visual and structured information. Long monologues and continuous text are ineffective. The schemes, lists, dedicated theses are preferred.
*   **Focus of attention:** It works "sprint." Deep concentration is possible for short intervals (15–25 minutes). Long monotonous loads cause exhaustion.

### Supporting factors

*   **Step -by -step instructions:** Separation of large tasks into 3-4 clear stages.
*   **Visual supports:** Check lists, schemes, intelligence cards, timline, infographics. Visual perception of information is more effective.
*   **Short intervals:** Tomato method (25/5 minutes) is an ideal work format.
*   **"Rule of three seconds":** Conscious pause (account up to three) before the answer or action.
*   **Clear rhythm:** Using a timer or metronome for a stable pace.

### Interviewing factors

*   **Multitasking:** The simultaneous performance of several actions (listen, write, analyze) leads to a loss of information.
*   **Sharp change in tasks:** Switching without a pause causes fatigue and confusion.
*   **Long oral explanations:** Monologists for more than 5-7 minutes without visual support lead to a loss of attention.
*   **Time pressure:** Hurry and acceleration requirements provoke impulsiveness and increase the number of errors.

## 3. Recommendations

### Home environment

#### Planning

*   Use the timer: 20–25 minutes of work, 5 minutes of activity (Tomato method).
*   Make a brief plan, a list (2-4 points) for a working interval. Mark it to feel progress.
*   Organize a minimalist workplace. Eliminate distracting factors (for example, gadgets).

#### Motivation

*   Encourage the process and efforts, and not just the result.
*   Formulate questions about homework, focusing on planning and decomposition of tasks.

### Educational environment

#### Setting tasks

*   Present the tasks in writing, according to the items. Duplicate oral instructions on the board or in handouts.
*   Use active verbs: “emphasize”, “make”, “write down”.

#### Material

*   Place key information (rules, formulas) in a permanent place for visual fixation.
*   Accompany lectures with structured presentations: one slide is one idea, a minimum of text, maximum schemes and images.
*   Use logical pauses and questions to maintain attention.

#### Individual approach

*   Provide extra time for checking the tests.
*   Allow the use of drafts to plan answers.
*   With an oral answer, give 5-10 seconds to think.

## 4. Training

Simple exercises aimed at developing key cognitive functions. Easily integrated into everyday life.

### Attention and accuracy

*   **"Corrective test":** Refusing certain letters in the text (with complication).
*   **"Find 10 differences":** Develops detailed visual scanning.
*   **Puzzles:** They stimulate concentration and visual memory, especially with small elements.

### Control and inhibition

*   **Juggling:** Develops attention, coordination, control of impulses.
*   **Balansboard/Slacklain:** He trains the balance and control of movements.
*   **Rhythmic exercises:** Taping under the metronome. It forms a sense of pace and the ability to slow down.

### Working memory

*   **Memory game:** Classic exercise for memorizing steam.
*   **Exercise "Photographer":** Removing parts after a short visual scan of the environment.
