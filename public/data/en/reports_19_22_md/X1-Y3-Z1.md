# Cognitive profile
---

## 1. Overview: Key results

*   **Attention (CPT):** High concentration. The ability to long, productive work without abstracts and errors.
*   **Control (Go/No-Go):** Interhemistered asymmetry has been identified. Tasks requiring the synchronous work of the hemispheres (for example, both hands) may require additional control.
*   **Visual working memory:** Excellent. The ability to hold and effectively use a large amount of complex visual information.

<!-- page-break -->

## 2. Individual profile

### Peculiarities

*   **Pace:** Prefers a deep immersion in the task. It works measuredly, without haste, using a strategic approach.
*   **Perception:** Better assimilates structured, logically built information. Strong memory allows you to work with large amounts of data. Difficulties are possible with the rapid combination of various information (for example, oral instruction and a complex scheme).
*   **Focus:** High and stable concentration. Effective work on complex projects without the influence of external stimuli.

### Support

*   **Clarity:** Clear tasks, goals, criteria.
*   **Visualization:** Graphs, maps, schemes for organizing information and compensation of asymmetry.
*   **Independence:** The ability to plan work and choose strategies.
*   **Calm:** A quiet, ordered environment for maximum concentration.

### Obstacles

*   **Multitasking:** Reduces effectiveness.
*   **Chaos:** Unstracting information requires unnecessary effort.
*   **Pressure:** A quick search for non -standard solutions without support on the usual strategies can cause uncertainty.
*   **Hand coordination:** Tasks that require quick and coordinated work of both hands can be more complicated.

<!-- page-break -->

## 3. Recommendations for growth

### Home environment

*   **Organization:**
    *   Provide a permanent, quiet, well -lit training place.
    *   Use gliders (digital/paper) to structured tasks. Separate large tasks into small steps.
    *   It is recommended 60-90 minutes of focus on the task, then a short break (10-15 minutes).
*   **Support:**
    *   Encourage hobbies that develop the coordination of both hands (see section 4) to harmonize the work of the hemispheres.
    *   Stimulate participation in design and research activities, where his attention and memory are most in demand.

### Educational environment

*   **Tasks:** Formulate clearly, with a clear result. Ideal design, research, analytical work.
*   **Materials:** Provide in a structured form: presentations, abstracts, annotated lists.
*   **Individual route:**
    *   Correctional support is not required.
    *   Attract to scientific projects, conferences, olympiads to realize potential.
    *   When mastering new practical skills (for example, in the laboratory), use visual step-by-step instructions (checks, schemes).

<!-- page-break -->

## 4. Development: practical exercises

Exercises for harmonizing the hemispheres, developing coordination and maintaining cognitive functions.

*   **Coordination and interhemispheric interaction:**
    *   **Juggling:** Start with two balls, gradually increase. Develops the coordination of the "eye-hand".
    *   **Musical instruments:** For example, piano, guitar, drums - require independent work of the hands.
    *   **Symmetric drawing:** Simultaneous drawing of the same figures with both hands.
*   **Attention and strategic thinking:**
    *   **Board games:** Chess, GO, strategic games that require planning.
*   **Body control and rhythm:**
    *   **Dancing:** Any types where coordination of arms and legs is important (salsa, ballroom dancing).
    *   **Swimming:** Various styles (Brass, butterfly) for symmetrical work of the body.
*   **Memory:**
    *   **"Palace of Memory":** Lokuses method to memorize large volumes of information (lists, dates, names).
