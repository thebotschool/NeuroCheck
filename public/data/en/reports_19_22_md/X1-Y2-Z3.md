# Cognitive profile report
## Memory. Control. Attention. (19–22 years)

This report provides an analysis of the cognitive characteristics of the student. It was created for parents and teachers, offering an understanding of the strengths and areas for development. Recommendations are designed to create an optimal environment for training and growth.

# 1. Analysis of key skills
## Test results

*   **Visual working memory (CPT):** An outstanding ability to assimilate and maintain large amounts of information, even in conditions of distraction. This is a fundamental force for training.
*   **Inhibitory control (Go/No-Go Test):** There is a tendency to impulsive solutions. The development of the ability to think about action is required.
*   **Sustainability of attention:** The tendency to errors was confirmed due to haste, especially under the pressure of time or in stressful situations.

# 2. The guide for the success of your student
## Personal learning profile

This section describes the unique style of the student’s work, offering ways to effective interaction.

### Features of perception
*   **Pace:** Fast, but requires control. High intellectual performance may be accompanied by haste.
*   **Information perception:** Optimally assimilates structured and visual data. Effectively works with large arrays of information if there is time to comprehend.
*   **Focus of attention:** Capable of deep concentration. However, impulsiveness can interrupt this process, leading to quick, but ill -conceived answers.

### Support factors
*   Step-by-step instructions and checklists.
*   Visual materials: diagrams, mental cards, timline.
*   The practice of "internal pause" before making decisions.
*   Time management: Using the Pomodoro method for focused work.

### Factors that impede success
*   Multitasking, reducing the quality of work.
*   Stress situations without preliminary preparation.
*   Unstracting tasks requiring independent planning.
*   Lack of time for self -test.

# 3. Recommendations
## Support and Development Strategies

### For the house
*   **Work on tasks:** Separate large tasks into small steps. Plan time to "cool down" and re -check important work.
*   **Support and encouragement:** Mark thoughtfulness and accuracy, not just speed. Ask about plans and specific help.
*   **Maintaining concentration:** After 45-50 minutes of deep work, a short break without gadgets is needed.

### For an educational institution
*   **The wording of tasks:** Offer clear tasks with clear criteria. Project work with phased deadlines is preferable.
*   **The structure of the material:** Submit information systematically: lectures with a plan, presentations with schemes, tables. This corresponds to its data style.
*   **Style style:** Calm, logical, structured. Surrender the lesson or assignment plan.
*   **Individual approach:** It is recommended to provide additional time (15-20%) in exams. This will reveal its analytical abilities, minimizing the influence of impulsiveness.

# 4. Recommended training
## Exercises for the development of skills

These practices contribute to the development of self -control and planning.

### Attention and self -control
*   **Strategic board games:** Chess, Go, Economic Strategies. Develop long -term planning and restraint of impulsive actions.
*   **Exercise "Double task":** Reading with the simultaneous calculation of certain words. He trains a high concentration and suppression of abstracts.

### Memory and planning
*   **Planer maintenance:** Daily planning and priority of tasks. Promotes the structure of thinking.

### Rhythm and bodily control
*   **Juggling:** Improves the coordination of the “eye-hand” and focus, suppressing chaotic movements.
*   **Musical instruments (drums, guitar):** Develop a sense of rhythm and self -control, requiring accuracy and endurance.
