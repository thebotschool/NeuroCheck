# The cognitive profile of the student
ID #A7B3 | Age: 19–22 years

The report will help to understand the unique features and potential of your student. Recommendations are aimed at creating a favorable environment for development and effective training.

---

# 1. Analysis of the profile

### Test results

*   **Sustainability of attention (TCP):** Tender to hasty answers. Errors are related to the processing of information, not with distraction.
*   **Impulsive control (Go/No-Go):** Difficulties with suppression of the first reaction. It is difficult for a student to refrain from immediate action, even if it is incorrect.
*   **Visual working memory (CPT):** Good perception of the common essence. A rush leads to a missing details and their replacement with similar, but inaccurate images.

**Key conclusion:** Present **Impulsive cognitive style**. Thinking and actions are fast, but can lead to surface processing and hasty solutions, especially in stressful conditions.

# 2. The guide for the success of your student

### Individual characteristics

*   **Pace:** Fast, reactive. It works with jerks with high energy, but can quickly get tired, losing quality.
*   **Perception:** Better assimilates visual and structured information. Solid text or long oral instructions are less effective.
*   **Focus of attention:** Attention is more likely "galloping" than stable. It easily switches, but hardly holds concentration on the details, especially with monotonous tasks.

### Which helps

*   **Clear instructions:** Breaking large tasks into 3-4 small steps.
*   **Visual supports:** Schemes, intelligence cards, text highlighting, short checks.
*   **Conscious pauses:** The habit of "stop-think" before the answer or action.
*   **Rhythm and timers:** Interval work (for example, 25 minutes of focus, 5 minutes of break).
*   **Feedback:** Promotion for attentiveness and self -test, and not for speed.

### What interferes

*   **Multitasking:** Simultaneous actions (listen, write, be distracted) reduce effectiveness.
*   **Time pressure:** The phrases "Faster!", "Little time!" enhance impulsiveness and errors.
*   **Monotone:** Long, monotonous tasks quickly cause a loss of interest and attention.
*   **Only oral instructions:** Important information transmitted exclusively orally can be perceived inaccurately or forgotten.

# 3. Recommendations

### Home environment

*   **Organization of work:**
    *   Plan the evening: "Mathematics, 10 minutes break, English."
    *   Break major tasks: "Search info today, plan tomorrow, slides of the day after tomorrow."
    *   Use a timer (20-25 minutes of work), then a short break for activity.
*   **Support and encouragement:**
    *   Provide a calm workplace without distracting devices.
    *   Focus on the self -test: "Have you checked the work? Let's see together."
    *   Praise the manifested control: "It is excellent that you are in no hurry. It reduces errors."
*   **Realistic expectations:**
    *   Effective attention on one task is about 20 minutes. Then we need a change of activity.

### School environment

*   **The wording of tasks:**
    *   The instructions should be chatting, with duplication (board, chat).
    *   Select keywords: *"Analyze"*, *"Compare"*, *"Exclude excess"*.
*   **Work structure:**
    *   Encourage drafts on control.
    *   Select 3-5 minutes for self-test at the end of the work.
*   **Class placement:**
    *   Optimally closer to the teacher and the board, away from distracting factors.
*   **Style style:**
    *   Interactive format is effective: short lectures (5-7 min) → Discussion (3 min) → Work at the board (5 min) → Test (3 min).
    *   Use visual supports: presentations, schemes, notes.
*   **Individual route:**
    *   The development of self-regulation and time management is recommended.
    *   In stressful situations (exams), up to 25% of the additional time to reduce anxiety and thoughtful verification can be useful.

# 4. Practical training

### Attention and control of impulsivity

*   **The game "Speak / Silent":** The host shows cards (for example, animals/plants). On the "animal" - to name, on "plant" - to be silent. Purpose: an accurate reaction without errors.
*   **Correct samples:** In the printed text in 2 minutes, cross out all the "o" and circle "a. The main thing is accuracy, not speed.

### Visual memory and accuracy

*   **The game "What has changed?":** 7-10 items on the table. The student looks 30 seconds. You change 1-2 subjects. Task: Find changes.
*   **Copying on the model:** To redraw complex symmetrical patterns or hieroglyphs, striving for the maximum similarity.

### Rhythm and coordination

*   **Rhythmic dictations:** The presenter taps the rhythm (two short, one long). The student exactly repeats with claps. Gradually complicate.
*   **Balance exercises:** Standing on one leg with closed eyes. Walking "Pyatka-K-Nosk" along the line. Improves conscious control over the body.
