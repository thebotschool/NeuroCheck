# Cognitive assessment report

This report is designed to identify the strengths of the student, determine the growth areas and offer effective support strategies.

---

### 1. Evaluation. Results.

*   **Attention and impulsiveness (CPT).** The tendency to fast, hasty actions. The speed sometimes prevails over accuracy, especially under pressure.
*   **Self-control (Go/No-Go).** High ability to conscious control and inhibition. Significant potential for self -regulation.
*   **Visual memory.** When memorizing visual information, loss of parts or substitution is possible. The general essence is perceived well, but the accuracy can be reduced.

<!-- page-break -->

### 2. Learning. Profile.

#### Individual characteristics.

*   **Pace.** Fast mind. High pace of thinking and actions. This can lead to haste. Style: “To do, then check” is not always optimal.
*   **Perception.** It grabs the overall picture and the essence well, but it can miss important details. Visual information is processed holistically, without a focus on accuracy.
*   **Focus.** A great ability to concentrate. In conditions of monotony or timewater, an “autopilot” provokes impulsive errors is possible. This is a feature of the distribution of attention, not a shortage.

#### Support. Which helps.

*   **"Inhale-out of action."** Conscious micropausa before the answer or action. Activates a strong skill of self -control.
*   **Step -by -step instructions. Check lists.** A clear structure where we check each step. Prevents the omission of parts.
*   **Visual anchors.** Selection of key information (marker, emphasizing, frame). Focusing attention on the details, not only in the general sense.
*   **Project work.** Tasks requiring strategy, planning and accuracy. Open the strengths: resistance to work, deep analysis.

#### Obstacles. What interferes.

*   **Multitasking without structure.** Simultaneous implementation of cases without a plan. Leads to bustle, superficiality.
*   **Tasks "at speed".** Tests with a rigid time limit. They provoke impulsiveness, increase errors in carelessness.
*   **Monotonous work.** Long -term completion of the same type of tasks. Reduces vigilance, activates hasty actions.
*   **Oral instructions.** Without visual support, important information is perceived inaccurately.

<!-- page-break -->

### 3. Recommendations. Actions.

#### House. Independent work.

*   **Homework.**
    *   Miss large projects into managed parts. After each part: short break, self -test.
    *   Use the "Prydor": 25 minutes of focused work, 5 minutes of rest. Supports concentration, avoids autopilot.
    *   Before delivery of any work: postpone for 30-60 minutes. Then re -read a fresh look. The best way to find a hurry mistake.
*   **Support.**
    *   Praise not speed, but thoughtfulness and accuracy. For example: “I see, you carefully checked everything, excellent work!”
    *   Discuss strategies, not just the result. Ask: “How do you plan to approach this task?”, “What step will be the first?”

#### School. Teachers.

*   **The formulation of tasks.** Give tasks with clockwork, measurable criteria. Example: "Write an essay that includes 3 arguments, introduction, conclusion and list of literature."
*   **The structure of the material.** Use visual supports: schemes, intelligence cards, presentations with the release of key theses. Duplicate important information in writing.
*   **Organization of control.** If possible, provide extra time on tests and exams. Focus on the need to check the work before delivery.
*   **Interaction.** Encourage participation in research projects, debate, mentor programs. These formats require strategic thinking, use self -control and the ability to solve complex problems.

### 4. Training. Development.

Exercises for the balance of the speed of thinking, accuracy and control.

*   **Attention and self -control.**
    *   **"Find the differences."** He trains visual attentiveness to details.
    *   **Assembly of puzzles, models.** Develops patience, strategic planning, focus on elements.
    *   **Chess, go.** They teach to calculate the moves, weigh solutions, restrain the impulse.

*   **Memory and accuracy.**
    *   **Mnemonics.** The study of techniques for accurate memorization (association, "Palace of Memory"). The emphasis on accuracy, not to volume.
    *   **Exercise "Copper".** Reproduction of a complex pattern or short text after 10-second viewing. Requires accuracy.

*   **Bodily control and rhythm.**
    *   **Juggling.** Develops the coordination of the "eye-hand", synchronizes the hemisphere of the brain.
    *   **Balance exercises (balance badge, yoga).** They teach body control, which is associated with the development of mental self -control.
    *   **Game on a musical instrument (by notes).** It requires a clear rhythm and sequence. Prevents ahead.
