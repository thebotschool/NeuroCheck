### Report: Cognitive assessment (7–9 years)

This report will help to understand the features of your child. It contains practical recommendations for creating an optimal education environment, at home and at school.

---

### 1. The main results

*   **Attention and working memory (CPT).** There is a tendency to impulsive answers. There are difficulties with holding visual details, which manifests itself in the confusion of similar images.
*   **Self-control (Go/No-Go).** It is difficult for a child to restrain the first impulses and avoid unnecessary actions. This is especially pronounced with fatigue.
*   **Sustainability of attention (TCP).** Long -term concentration and fast reaction is difficult. There is a rapid fatigue leading to an increase in errors.

<!-- page-break -->

### 2. The guide for the success of your student

#### Individual profile

*   **Pace.** The work begins quickly, sometimes impulsively, but productivity decreases after 15-20 minutes of intensive load.
*   **Perception.** Information is better absorbed by ear and through actions. Visual information (for example, long text) requires more attention.
*   **Focus of attention.** Unstable and short -term. Attention is easily distracted by external stimuli (sounds, movements). It is difficult to filter unnecessary information, which leads to quick overload.

#### Supporting factors

*   **Step -by -step instructions.** One task is one clear instruction.
*   **Visual supports.** Simple schemes, checklists with illustrations, highlighting key points.
*   **Rhythm and predictability.** A clear routine, the use of timers, the installed rituals before studying.
*   **Motor activity.** Short breaks for warm -up, using movements during the score.

#### Distracting factors

*   **Multitasking.** The performance of several actions at the same time (listen and write) reduces the quality.
*   **Noise and visual disorder.** Distract and deplete the resource of attention.
*   **Long oral instructions.** Only the beginning or end of a complex phrase is remembered.
*   **Hurry and temporary restrictions.** They provoke impulsiveness and anxiety.

<!-- page-break -->

### 3. Practical recommendations

#### Home environment

*   **Organization of work.**
    *   Divide the tasks into blocks for 15–20 minutes. Between them-5-minute active breaks.
    *   Spend the work plan before the start: "First mathematics, then a break, then reading."
    *   Provide a minimalist workplace: only the necessary items.
*   **Support and promotion.**
    *   Praise the effort, not just the result: "You are perfectly concentrated!", "I see your efforts."
    *   Use the guide phrases: "Let's get back to the assignment", "What did we stop at?"
*   **Concentration.** Arbitrary attention is effectively held for about 15 minutes. Plan breaks.

#### Educational process

*   **The formulation of tasks.**
    *   Give the instructions step by step. Write down, say, ask to repeat.
    *   Use clear verbs: "Emphasize", "Cover", "Count."
*   **The structure of the lesson.**
    *   The optimal place: the first-second desk, in the center, far from distracting factors.
    *   Use visibility: support schemes, cards, step -by -step algorithms.
    *   Alternate the activities every 15–20 minutes: a letter, an oral answer, work with visual materials, a short warm -up.
*   **Individual approach.**
    *   Provide extra time for tests.
    *   Allow the use of a line for reading and drafts.
    *   Use signal cards (readiness for a response, help request) to reduce impulsiveness.

<!-- page-break -->

### 4. Developing exercises

These exercises will help strengthen key skills. Do 10-15 minutes daily in a playful way.

*   **For attention.**
    *   **Corrective test.** Use a newspaper or magazine. Task: cross out all the letters "A". Complication: delete "a", circle "O".
    *   **Find the differences.** A classic game for the development of visual accuracy and concentration.
    *   **Desktop walkers.** Training the tracking of progress and compliance with the rules.
*   **For coordination and self -regulation.**
    *   **Robot.** Let the commands ("Two steps forward", "turn right", "freeze"), which the child performs accurately, without unnecessary movements.
    *   **Games with the ball.** Fight the ball with different hands, throw it, calling the words on the topic.
    *   **Graphic dictations.** "One cage to the right, two up ..." develop a connection "I hear-yield-I will."
*   **For working memory and braking.**
    *   **Memory.** Search for paired cards.
    *   **Edible-universal.** He trains reaction speed and inhibition.
    *   **Remember and repeat.** Repeat the sequences: pops, words, numbers, movements (start with 2-3 elements).
