**The cognitive profile of the student**

This report reveals the individual cognitive profile of the student. It was created for parents and teachers to help create an optimal environment for training and development. The information is clearly set out, the recommendations are applicable in practice.

### 1. Overview of the results

*   **Working memory.** Holding volumetric information is difficult. Fatual fatigue during cognitive overload is possible.
*   **Self -control.** High self -regulation. The ability to accurate and quick reactions to clear tasks.
*   **Sustainability of attention.** Teenage to distracting. Concentration on monotonous tasks requires visual support.

### 2. The guide for the success of your student

This leadership reveals a unique way of perception and processing of information.

**Individual profile**

*   **Pace.** Prefers a measured, systematic approach for analysis and memorization. Demonstrates a quick and accurate reaction to clear signals.
*   **Perception.** The most effective through visual materials: schemes, lists, intelligence cards, videos. Oral lectures and long texts without visual support are complex.
*   **Attention.** Prone to distracting external stimuli. Strong inhibitory control is noted. Productivity is high on short, interesting tasks; The routine quickly causes fatigue.

**Which contributes to success**

*   **Step -by -step instructions.** Decomposition of complex tasks for controlled stages.
*   **Visual supports.** The use of checklists, diagrams, discharge.
*   **Work on the timer (Tomator method).** Short productivity intervals with mandatory breaks.
*   **Focus.** One task at a time.
*   **Feedback.** Regular confirmation of adult progress.

**What makes the process difficult**

*   **Multitasking.** Causes cognitive overload and errors.
*   **The volume of information.** Large blocks, especially orally, without pauses.
*   **Faring instructions.** Blurry formulations.
*   **Distracting factors.** Noise, conversations, open tabs in the browser.

### 3. Practical recommendations

**For the house**

*   **Organization of work.**
    *   Divide homework into blocks for 20–25 minutes. After each block - a mandatory break.
    *   Provide a quiet, organized workplace. Eliminate distracting factors: notifications, extra objects.
    *   Make a joint work plan for the evening.
*   **Support and motivation.**
    *   Mark success in self -control and completing short tasks. Extract the accuracy and speed of the reaction.
    *   Use supportive questions, for example: “What is according to the plan? Where to start? " Instead of "did you do lessons?"
*   **Attention.** Expect a productive concentration for 15–20 minutes. Then a change of activity is required.

**For school**

*   **The wording of the tasks.**
    *   Formulate the tasks as specifically as possible, use the verbs of action.
    *   Duplicate oral instructions in writing (on a board or in a chat).
*   **The structure of the lesson.**
    *   Present the material structured: theses, intelligence cards, checklists.
    *   Apply interactive style: ask questions, ask for repeat instructions, involve in discussion. Avoid monotonous lectures.
*   **Personalization of training.**
    *   The optimal place in the class is the first or second desk to minimize distracting factors and easy contact with the teacher.
    *   Consider the elements of the individual educational route (IOOM): additional time on the control, written form of delivery of volumetric topics (diagrams, tables, essays).

### 4. Recommended training

These exercises develop skills using strengths.

**Attention and working memory**

*   **Board games:** “Dobble”, “set”, “corridor”, “drum”. Develop concentration, reaction rate, multi -layered thinking.
*   **Exercise "Memory".** Develops visual memory and concentration.
*   **Mnemonics.** Studying techniques for memorization: associations, history, "palaces of memory."

**Coordination and bodily control**

*   **Neurohymnastics.** Simultaneous multidirectional movements to improve interhemispheric interaction.
*   **Juggling.** He trains concentration, peripheral vision, coordination.

**Rhythm and self -regulation**

*   **Tapping rhythms.** Repetition of a rhythmic pattern according to the model (metronome).
*   **The game "Delay".** Develops rapid inhibitory control and bodily regulation.
