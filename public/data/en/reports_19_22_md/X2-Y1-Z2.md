# Cognitive profile: analysis and development

The report is an individual cognitive profile. He offers practical strategies for effective training and self -organization. Recommendations are addressed to both the student and teachers.

### 1. A brief report for each test

*   **CPT test: accuracy of visual memory.** Difficulties with distinction of similar details are noted. This indicates a quick processing or insufficient depth of perception.
*   **Test Go/No-Go: Control of reactions.** Outstanding self -control. The ability to fast, balanced decisions has been shown. Impulsive actions are effectively suppressed.
*   **TCP Test: Sustainability of attention.** The instability of concentration is noted. Long monotonous tasks can cause difficulties.

### 2. The guide for the success of your student

Your profile combines high self -control with dynamic attention. You effectively suppress unwanted actions, but holding the focus on one task requires effort.

#### Individual profile

*   **Pace.** The optimal rhythm is an interval sprint. Fast, accurate jerks are characteristic. Maintaining a uniform pace at a distance causes difficulties. Monotonous work leads to fatigue, errors.
*   **Perception.** Structural, visual information is effectively absorbed. Long texts or oral lectures without visual supports can reduce concentration, leading to omissions and errors.
*   **Focus.** Attention easily captures new, but equally easily switches to distracting factors, external or internal.

#### Effective strategies

*   Visual anchors. Schemes, intelligence cards, checklists. Highlighting keywords. Everything that structures information.
*   Step -by -step instructions. Division of tasks into small, understandable steps. This provides rapid progress, supports motivation.
*   Interval work. Technique "Tomato" (25/5 minutes). Supports concentration, prevents exhaustion.
*   Conscious start. Planning to the task: what to do, the first step, the completion criteria.

#### Barriers for concentration

*   Multitasking. Listening to the lecture and the simultaneous use of the phone leads to loss of information. Attention is not distributed effectively.
*   Monotone. Long, non -interactive lectures. Large volumes of text. Uniform tasks quickly deplete attention.
*   Information noise. Open tabs, notifications, extraneous conversations. Distracted focus.
*   Lack of a clear goal. Blurred formulations of tasks (“Study the topic”) can lead to loss of direction.

### 3. Recommendations

#### Independent training

*   **Working process.**
    *   Use the tomato method. Optimal interval: 20-30 minutes.
    *   Make a plan of 2-4 steps before starting. Mark the executed. This gives control and satisfaction.
    *   Organize a zone without abstracts. Disconnect notifications, close unnecessary tabs.
*   **Work with the material.**
    *   Do not read the text in a continuous stream. Transform information into diagrams, tables, supporting abstracts with theses.
    *   After studying the material, try to draw an intelligent card from memory. This reveals gaps, consolidates knowledge.
*   **Support and motivation.**
    *   Use short breaks (5-10 minutes) for physical activity. Avoid scrolling social networks that overloads attention.

#### Approach for teachers

*   **The wording of the tasks.**
    *   Formulate the tasks with cheer, measurable blocks. Example: instead of “write an essay” - “1. Formulate three theses. 2. Choose two arguments for each. 3. Write an introduction and conclusion. ”
*   **The structure of the feed.**
    *   Structure lectures by presentations. One slide is one thought. Use lists, diagrams, images.
    *   Sound the plan at the beginning of the lesson, summarize at the end. This helps to keep attention.
*   **The style of interaction.**
    *   Interactive: questions, short assignments in pairs, mini-surveys. This updates attention.
    *   A place is recommended in the audience closer to the teacher. Avoid location in distracting factors (windows, doors).
*   **Individual approach.**
    *   In exams or control, adapt the conditions. Provide a quiet room or allow the use of a preliminary plan-bank to structuring response.

### 4. Recommended training

These exercises develop attention and memory, supporting self -control and coordination.

*   **Development of attention.**
    *   **Schultte tables.** Exercise for concentration and peripheral vision. Purpose: quickly find the numbers 1-25. Daily practice (3-5 minutes) improves attention stability.
    *   **Corrective tests.** Search and deletion of letters in the text. He trains the focus on monotonous tasks.
*   **The development of visual memory.**
    *   **The game "Memory".** Develops accurate memorization of visual images and their location.
    *   **Mnemics "Palace of Memory".** Creation of bright visual associations for memorization. Increases the depth and meaningfulness of the process.
*   **Coordination and control.**
    *   **Juggling.** Develops interhemispheric interaction, the coordination of the “eye-hand” and dynamic concentration.
    *   **Balansboard.** He trains bodily control, balance. Associated with general regulation and concentration.
*   **Feeling of rhythm.**
    *   **Shock or bitbox.** Develops a sense of rhythm. Helps to structure activities, including a working pace.
