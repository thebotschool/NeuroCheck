## Report. Cognitive assessment

This report reveals the cognitive features of the student, his strengths and growth zones. The goal is to provide practical recommendations for effective training and development.

---

### 1. Review of tests

*   **CPT (working memory and attention):** High stability of attention. Effective memorization of information, even in the presence of distracting factors. The ability to strategic planning and its implementation.
*   **Go/No-Go (braking and control):** Expressed self -control. Accurate and timely reaction, effective suppression of impulsive actions.
*   **TCP (concentration and distractiveness):** Potential difficulties with prolonged concentration on monotonous, audio tasks without visual support.

---

### 2. Training profile

#### Features of training

*   **Pace:** Productivity is high in clear and structured tasks. Reducing the pace is possible with a monotonous or passive perception of information.
*   **Perception:** Optimal assimilation occurs through visual materials: schemes, graphs, presentations. Audial information without visualization is perceived with difficulty.
*   **Focus of attention:** Dual nature. High concentration on strategic, complex projects. The tendency to distract from routine or monotonous tasks (operational attention).

#### Effective approaches

*   Visual materials: presentations, intelligence cards, diagrams, checklists.
*   Clear structure: step -by -step instructions, clear plans, understandable criteria.
*   Active participation: discussions, practical tasks, dialogue.
*   Interval mode: Work with short breaks (for example, a method of sores).

#### Restraining factors

*   Monotony: long lectures without visual support.
*   Abstractness: Faring Tasks without a plan and criteria.
*   Distracting factors: extraneous noise, notifications, conversations.
*   Sosystem presentation: frequent change of topics without deepening.

<!-- page-break -->

---

### 3. Recommendations

#### For independent work

*   **Decomposition of tasks:** Separate large tasks into small steps. Use plans and checklists.
*   **Optimization of the workplace:** Minimize distracting factors. Use social networks locks, remove the phone.
*   **Time management:** Use the “Prydor” technique (25/5 minutes). This maximizes the concentration on monotonous tasks.
*   **Fatigue management:** Avoid work "through force." A short break and change of activity will increase productivity.

#### In the educational process

*   **The clarity of tasks:** Formulate the tasks as specifically as possible, with clear criteria. For complex projects - provide the structure.
*   **Visual feed:** Use presentations, infographics, demonstrations. Enter the dialogue, ask questions.
*   **Team role:** Thanks to strong planning and self -control, a student can be an effective project leader, a mentor, or a participant in research groups.
*   **IOM (individual educational route):** For exams with a long concentration (especially oral), consider a written form or short breaks to reduce fatigue.

### 4. Practical exercises

The presented exercises are aimed at developing operational attention and strengthening strategic thinking, as well as self -control.

*   **Awareness (MindFulness):** Daily short meditation (5-10 minutes) train the return of attention and reduction of abstracts. Applications: Headspace, Calm.
*   **Juggling:** Develops concentration on several objects, visual-motor coordination, reduces susceptibility to external stimuli. Start with two goals.
*   **Mental cards (Mind Maps):** Apply for lecture and planning to be noted. This technique transforms a passive perception into an active, structured and visual process.
*   **Rhythmic classes:** Playing musical instruments or dancing. They contribute to the formation of an internal pace, improve concentration and coordination.
*   **Yoga / Pilates:** Practices that combine physical activity with concentration on breathing and body. Strengthen the connection of “reason-gathe” and contribute to self-regulation.
