# Cognitive test report

### Details: CPT+, Go/No-Go, TCP. Age: 19–22 years.

This report reveals the individual cognitive features of your student. The recommendations presented will help optimize training and development, creating a supporting environment at home and at school.

---

## 1. Key conclusions

*   **Attention and memory (CPT):** Difficulties are observed with the retention of consistent information, especially with fatigue or distraction. This indicates the need for support for self -regulation and concentration.
*   **Braking and control (Go/No-Go):** Interhemistered asymmetry has been identified. This can affect the performance of tasks requiring coordinated work of both hands or hemispheres.
*   **Sustainability of attention (TCP):** Easy distraction is noted. Audial information without visual support is perceived less effectively.

## 2. The guide for the success of your student

### Features of perception

*   **Pace:** Prefers a calm, measured pace. Hurry and frequent switching reduce efficiency. It takes time to adapt to the task.
*   **Format:** The most effectively perceives visual information: schemes, lists, algorithms. Long oral explanations without clarity quickly cause fatigue.
*   **Focus:** Attention is unstable, exhausted during overload or multitasking. Help is required to maintain the focus and minimize external stimuli.

### What supports

*   **Visual tips:** Check lists, Mayind cards, color selection, short schemes.
*   **Step -by -step instructions:** Skipping complex tasks into simple, consistent stages.
*   **Rhythm and predictability:** A clear routine, the use of timers, the usual rituals.
*   **Regular feedback:** Short, positive confirmations help to keep the focus.

### What interferes

*   **Multitasking:** The simultaneous performance of several actions (for example, listen and record) reduces productivity.
*   **Fast change of tasks:** Sharp transitions between topics without a short pause.
*   **Information noise:** Distracting sounds, disorder at the workplace, a background TV.
*   **Long oral instructions:** Monologists without visual support are the least effective way to transmit information.

## 3. Recommendations

### For the house

*   **Organization of work:**
    *   **Tomato technique:** 20 minutes of work, 5 minutes of break.
    *   **Planning:** Make a task plan for the evening, mark the completed.
    *   **Order:** The workplace should be free from excess.
*   **Support and motivation:**
    *   **Partnership:** Instead of control - offer help and support.
    *   **Praise:** Mark efforts and process, and not just the result.
    *   **Realism:** Keep in mind that the focus of attention on a difficult task is 15-20 minutes.

### For school

*   **The wording of tasks:**
    *   **Check lists:** Miss tasks into simple, consistent steps. Provide them in writing.
    *   **Example:** Instead of “write an essay” - “1. Find 3 ideas. 2. Choose the best. 3. Compose a plan of 5 points. 4. Write a draft”.
*   **Lesson structure:**
    *   **Visibility:** Use presentations, schemes, keywords, infographics.
    *   **Location:** Place the student at the first or second desks, away from distracting factors (window, door).
*   **Style style:**
    *   **Interactive:** Ask questions, ask to retell the instructions, involve it.
    *   **Pause:** Take short breaks to change the focus between information blocks.
*   **Individual route (IOM):**
    *   **Reply format:** Allow me to take oral topics in writing or according to plan.
    *   **Control:** Provide extra time, let me use the pre -composed algorithm.

## 4. Recommended training

Exercises for the development and strengthening of neural connections.

### Attention and memory

*   "Find 10 differences", "Memory".
*   Schultte tables (search for numbers in order).
*   Removing the sequence of objects (5-7 items, remove one, name the absent).

### Coordination and interaction

*   Juggling (start with two items).
*   Cross -steps (right hand - left knee).
*   Graphic dictations.

### Rhythm and body control

*   Removing or cooling rhythms.
*   Exercises for balance (stand on one leg, walking along the line).
*   The game "Delay-Otomory".
