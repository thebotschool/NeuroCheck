# Cognitive assessment report: Profile Strategist-Sprinter (age: 19–22)

This report is designed for an in -depth understanding of your strengths and areas for development. The information presented is not a diagnosis, but a strategic guide to disclose your potential.

# 1. Analysis of key skills

*   **Working memory and attention (CPT):** You effectively hold and structure complex information, keeping the focus even with external distraction. This indicates developed memory and high concentration.
*   **Reactions control (Go/No-Go):** Demonstrate excellent self -control, maintaining accuracy and composure in conditions requiring a quick reaction.
*   **Impulsive control (TCP):** The tendency to quick decisions has been revealed. Sometimes the speed prevails over a deep analysis, which can lead to errors due to inattention, especially under pressure.

# 2. Success Guide

## Key features

*   **Pace:** Very high. You think and act quickly. The task is to learn to distinguish between effective speed and impulsiveness.
*   **Information perception:** You are strong in processing large volumes of structured information. Schemes, plans, logical chains and voluminous texts are perceived easily.
*   **Focus of attention:** Attention is stable and deep. You are able to concentrate for a long time. However, with a hurry or pressure, the focus can decrease, which leads to errors.

## Support strategies

*   **The principle of the final pause:** Always allocate time for rechecking. Before completing the task, make a short pause and look at work with a fresh look.
*   **Visual checklists:** For difficult tasks, use step -by -step plans. Noting the executed points, you control the process and avoid haste.
*   **Structuring:** Continue to use your strong side. Convert information to mental cards, diagrams and tables. This improves memorization and helps to identify inaccuracies.

## Factors that make it difficult to progress

*   **Time deficiency:** Situations requiring many quick answers without the possibility of correction (blitz-pins, tests with an automatic transition).
*   **Speed ​​priority over quality:** The internal or external installation for speed, and not on quality, often provokes impulsive errors.
*   **Monotone:** Boring or too simple tasks can cause a desire to hurry, which reduces the quality of the result.

# 3. Recommendations

## For home support

*   **Preparation for exams:** Divide the material into logical blocks. After each block, make a short gravity with a mandatory error check.
*   **Principles of praise:** Focus not at speed, but on thoughtfulness and quality. For example: "It is great that you double -check and found a mistake."
*   **Mercing of attention:** Effective work on a difficult task lasts 60–90 minutes. After this intensive segment, plan not only the rest, but also the 5-minute self-testing session.

## For teachers and mentors

*   **The wording of tasks:** Encourage tasks that require justification of answers ("Explain your choice"). This stimulates introspection and slows down the answer process.
*   **Control structure:** At the exams, allocate 10-15% of the time for the final check. This reduces stress and allows you to effectively use the speed of work: first perform, then calmly double -check.
*   **Optimal presentation style:** You are ideal for research and project activities, where deep study and analysis are valued. Put in projects where planning, data analysis and information structuring is required.
*   **Individual route:** Participation in scientific conferences, mentor programs (as a mentor) and internships is recommended. Your analytical skills and self -organization will be in demand.

# 4. Recommended training

These exercises will help harmonize speed and accuracy.

*   **Attention and strategy:**
    *   **Chess, Go, Sogi:** They develop the ability to calculate moves and suppress impulsive reactions, forming a thoughtful strategy.
*   **Rhythm and coordination:**
    *   **Playing musical instruments by notes:** Improves the exact and timely performance of visual actions, strengthening the connection "I see - I analyze - I do."
*   **Control of body and emotions:**
    *   **Mindfulness practices:** Short daily sessions (5-10 minutes) of meditation or body scanning. Develop the skill of observation of thoughts and impulses without an instant reaction.
*   **Strengthening memory:**
    *   **"Palace of Memory":** Mentally place important information (dates, formulas, plan points) in the rooms of a familiar building. This enhances your abilities to structuring and memorization.
