### Cognitive profile: 19–22 years old

This report is your guide for the development of the potential of the child. We will show the strengths and growth zones, offering practical steps to support the house and at school.

---

### 1. Test results

*   **Attention and memory (CPT).** He sees the whole, misses the details. It needs time for accurate memorization.
*   **Self-control (Go/No-Go).** It acts impulsively, especially with fatigue or stress. It is difficult to stop in time.
*   **Impulsiveness (TCP).** Prone to quick decisions. Haster leads to errors requiring thoughtfulness.

**Conclusion.** Significant potential. Key growth zones: self -control and attention to details. It acts impulsively, which reduces the demonstration of knowledge.

<!-- page-break -->

### 2. Features of training

#### Features of perception

*   **Pace.** Medium or slow. Pause for self -testing is necessary. Hurry increases errors.
*   **Perception.** It better assimilates clear visual information: schemes, checklists, step-by-step instructions. The overloaded text distracts.
*   **Focus.** Effective on one task. Distracted with multitasking or external noise.

#### Effective strategies

*   *The rule "Stop-think-make."* A short pause before action. You can use a reminder.
*   *Step -by -step instructions.* Divide big tasks into stages. For example: "Read, emphasize, calculate, write down."
*   *Visual supports.* Check lists, markers, schemes. Help to focus on the main one.
*   *A clear rhythm.* Work on the timer (20/5 minutes) maintains concentration and prevents fatigue.

#### Obstacles

*   *Multitasking.* The simultaneous performance of several actions leads to errors.
*   *Hurry and pressure.* Stress, impulsiveness, quality reduction.
*   *Complex oral instructions.* Long instructions are not completely perceived. Details can be missed.
*   *Noise.* Loud sounds, a mess, an excess of visual stimuli are distracted.

<!-- page-break -->

### 3. Support and development

#### Home environment

*   *Homework.* Break into blocks for 15-25 minutes with short breaks. Create a "clean" work area. Discuss the work plan in advance.
*   *Promotion.* Mark attentiveness and accuracy, not speed. With errors, teach a self -test: "How to find a mistake?"
*   *Attention.* Concentration on a difficult task: 15-25 minutes. Plan breaks.

#### School environment

*   *Instructions.* Short, clear, step -by -step. Write down long instructions. Provide samples.
*   *Information.* The minimum of distraction on the board and in the materials. Highlight the main thing. The place in the forefront is recommended.
*   *Feed and IOM.* Pause when handling, give more time to the answer. Consider the elements of IOM: additional. Time on tests, drafts for self -testing.

<!-- page-break -->

### 4. Recommended training

Game exercises for the development of the necessary skills.

*   *Attention and self -control.*
    *   “Delay!”: Fast reaction, body control.
    *   Board games (checkers, chess, uno, jenga): patience, strategy.
    *   Graphic dictations: auditory attention, step -by -step instructions.
*   *Visual accuracy and memory.*
    *   “Find the differences”: attention to the details.
    *   “Corrective test”: Search for characters in the text (for example, all “A” in a minute).
    *   Memory: Visual Working Memory.
*   *Coordination and bodily control.*
    *   Balance exercises: walking along the line, stand on one leg (with open/closed eyes).
    *   RIGHT RITMES: Repetition of a given rhythm for the connection of hearing and motor skills.
