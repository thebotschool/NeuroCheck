### **2. Success Guide**

#### Features of training

*   **Pace.** High. Effective work with a clear structure.
*   **Perception.** Preferred visually organized data. An unstructured text or a chaotic flow of information can cause difficulties.
*   **Attention.** Sustainable, deep. Long -term concentration on tasks with a clear structure.

#### Optimal support

*   **Visual supports.** Mental cards, diagrams, diagrams, timline, infographics.
*   **Step -by -step instructions.** Clear algorithms and plans for complex tasks.
*   **Structuring the material.** Separation of the text into short paragraphs, subtitles, lists.
*   **Preliminary planning.** Drawing up a plan before starting work.

#### Obstacles in learning

*   **Unstructured tasks.** Tasks with unclear requirements or lack of finished logic.
*   **Information overload.** Large amounts of data without dividing into semantic blocks.
*   **Oral instructions.** Complex multi -stage tasks explained without visual support.

### **3. Personal recommendations**

#### Homework and independence

*   **Organization of tasks.**
    *   Before starting volumetric work, always make a plan or mental card.
    *   System of new material in the form of schemes, tables or lists.
    *   The recommended duration of concentration: 60–90 minutes, then a short break (10-15 minutes).
*   **Motivation and promotion.**
    *   Support and encourage the organization’s process, not just the result. For example: "Great plan!", "Clear scheme!".
    *   Encourage participation in projects where attention and stress resistance are valued, and structuring is also trained.

#### Support in the educational process

*   **The formulation of tasks.** Provide tasks in the form of a clear algorithm or step -by -step plan.
    *   Example: Instead of “Analyze the topic” - “1. Study sources. 2. Select the arguments. 3. Give examples. 4. Formulate the conclusion.”
*   **Material feed.**
    *   Lectures and seminars are more effective with visual presentations: schemes, graphs, key theses.
    *   For coursework/projects, provide structures or support issues.
*   **Individual route.**
    *   The high potential of attention and self -control allows you to recommend participation in research projects, olympiads, internships.
    *   The main attention is the development of metacognitive skills: planning, systematization, time management.

### **4. Developing training**

These exercises contribute to the development of information organization skills and strengthening strengths.

#### The development of structural thinking

*   **"History on the chain."** Compilation of logical stories from 5-7 random pictures or words.
*   **Drawing up the rebukes.** Retelling the plot of the film, books or events in the form of a comic book/series of drawings for training linear thinking.
*   **Mental cards.** The use of tools (XMind, MIRO) or paper for a visual organization of thoughts: plans, notes, project structure.

#### Strengthening attention and control

*   **Puzzles.** Sudoku, Japanese crosswords develop concentration and logic.
*   **Juggling.** Develops interhemispheric integration, coordination and attention.
*   **Strategic games.** Chess, Go and other strategies requiring planning several moves ahead.
