## **Cognitive Profile Assessment: Schoolboy 19–22 years old**

This report reveals the cognitive profile of the student, identifying key strengths and areas for development. The proposed recommendations will help optimize the educational process and support the formation of the necessary skills.

---

### **1. Key test results**

*   **Memory and Organization of Information (CPT):** Details are easily remembered, but it can be difficult to build them into a logical sequence or develop a plan.

*   **Braking and coordination test (Go/No-Go):** The tasks that require synchronous work of the hands reveal asymmetry in the activity of the hemispheres. This can complicate the development of new, complex skills.

*   **Test for stability of attention (TCP):** Long -term holding of attention on one task, especially with a monotonous presentation or the presence of distracting factors, causes difficulties and cognitive fatigue.

<!-- page-break -->

### **2. The guide for the success of your student**

#### **Features of training**

*   **Pace:** Uneven. Separate ideas are seized quickly, but they need additional time to communicate them in the system, understanding and planning. A rush in difficult tasks leads to errors.
*   **Perception format:** The best perception is achieved through structured and visually organized information: schemes, intelligence cards, step-by-step algorithms, checklists. Audial information without visual support is absorbed less effectively.
*   **Focus of attention:** Attention is unstable, especially with a long load or in a noisy environment. The student may turn off the process if the information is submitted monotonously and does not require active participation.

#### **Efficiency support**

*   **Visual supports:** The use of intelligence cards, timelines, schemes for the abstract and understanding of the material.
*   **Step -by -step instructions:** Clear, brief steps to complete projects or solutions to problems.
*   **Interval work:** The Method "Prydor" (25 minutes of work, 5 minutes of rest) helps to avoid overwork and maintain concentration.
*   **Clear structure:** The clear beginning, the middle and end of the lesson or assignment.
*   **Practical involvement:** Tasks requiring active actions, drawing, assembly or discussion.

#### **Factors that reduce productivity**

*   **Multitasking:** Simultaneous requirements for listening, recording and analyzing new information.
*   **Long monologues:** Lectures without pauses, questions and visual support.
*   **Large volumes of unrelated information:** Tasks without a clear goal, requiring only retelling.
*   **Rush and fast change of activity:** The lack of time to switch and deep immersion in the task.
*   **Distracting factors:** Noise, background conversations, extra tabs on the computer.

<!-- page-break -->

### **3. Development strategies**

#### **Home environment**

*   **Educational process:**
    *   Separate large tasks (report, project) into small, understandable steps. Use stickers or digital gliders to track progress.
    *   The optimal duration of continuous work is 20–25 minutes, followed by a 5-minute break (get up, rushes).
*   **Motivation and growth:**
    *   Help with the organization of the process, and not with a direct solution: “Where do we start?”, “What is the next step?”, “Will we make a plan?”.
    *   Mark efforts and process (planning, compliance with intervals), not only the final result.
*   **Working space:**
    *   Maintain order on the desktop. Remove the phone and gadgets if they are not required to complete the task.

#### **School environment**

*   **Setting tasks:**
    *   Formulate tasks as clearly and step by step, especially for design work. Ideally-in the format of the checklist.
    *   At the beginning of the lesson, voice the plan: “Today we will consider three topics. First ..., then ..., at the end ... ".
*   **Material feed:**
    *   Use presentations with diagrams, key theses and infographics.
    *   An interactive style of presentation with involving questions and short practical tasks is preferred.
    *   In the classroom, it is better to place a student closer to the teacher, away from the window or door to minimize distracting factors.
*   **Individual approach:**
    *   For control and exams, adaptation is useful: providing additional time, the ability to clarify the wording, written delivery of work to reduce the cognitive load.

### **4. Developing activity**

These exercises will help strengthen the necessary skills. It is recommended to perform them regularly in a playful way.

*   **For attention and self -control:**
    *   **Concentration games:** Sudoku, searching for differences, labyrinths.
    *   **The Udodor app:** Use as a simulator to form an interval habit.
*   **For memory and organization:**
    *   **Creation of intelligence cards (Mind Maps):** Transformation of a textbook paragraph into a scheme with keywords and connections.
    *   **Retelling for the swording:** Watching a video or reading a story, then sketching or description of 3-5 key scenes in the correct order.
*   **For coordination and interhemispheric interaction:**
    *   **Juggling:** Starting from two items. He trains the connection of the “eye-hand” and synchronizes the brain.
    *   **Game on a musical instrument:** A guitar or piano requires the coordinated work of both hands.
    *   **Neurohymnastics:** Simple exercises, such as “Fist-Robric Ladon” or “Ear-Shop”.
*   **For rhythm and bodily control:**
    *   **Table tennis:** Develops a reaction, attention and coordination.
    *   **Rhythmic exercises:** Removing various rhythms alternately and at the same time with both hands, possibly to the music.
