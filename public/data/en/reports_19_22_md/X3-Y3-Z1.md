# Analysis of cognitive abilities

This personal report reveals the strengths of the student and the region for development. The goal is to create an optimal environment for training and effective growth.

---

## 1. The main conclusions

*   **Working memory:** Excellent memorization of facts. Difficulties arise if necessary to build information in the correct sequence. The student knows all the components, but can get confused through the process.
*   **Control:** Confident fulfillment of tasks using the usual strategies. The effectiveness decreases if it is necessary to act non -standard or to use both sides of the brain at the same time, which indicates the potential to improve interhemispheric interaction.
*   **Attention:** The exceptional ability to concentrate. The student can work for a long time and concentrate, avoiding abstracts and impulsive errors. This is his key strong side.

## 2. The guide for the success of the student

### Features of perception

*   **Pace:** High productivity when focusing on one specific task. The pace is reduced if it is necessary to plan or structure a large amount of information.
*   **Format:** Optimally assimilates clearly structured, visually organized information. A solid text or a long monologue is perceived as “informational noise”.
*   **Focus:** Deep and stable. Allows you to "plunge" in complex topics. The reverse side is the risk of “getting stuck” in details, missing the overall picture.

### Which contributes

*   **Visual structures:** Schemes, mental cards, timline, checklists. They help to see the overall structure before immersion in details.
*   **Step -by -step instructions:** Skipping of large tasks into small, consistent stages (for example, “Stage 1: Collection of information. Stage 2: Drawing up a plan.”).
*   **Rhythm and coordination:** Actions that require rhythm and symmetry improve the joint work of the hemispheres and increase cognitive flexibility.

### What makes it difficult

*   **Multitasking:** The simultaneous implementation of several tasks (for example, listen, notes, think) leads to overload. Sustainable focus poorly tolerates constant switching.
*   **Unstructured tasks:** Tasks without a clear plan, criteria and sub -questioning cause frustration and stupor.
*   **Hurry in planning:** It takes time for an internal organization of thoughts and structuring work before starting actions.

## 3. Recommendations

### Home environment

#### Organization of the work process:

*   Before starting work: 5 minutes to draw up a simple plan - what and in what order.
*   Divide the work into intervals (for example, 40 minutes of work, 10 minutes of a break) with one clear task for each interval.
*   Visualize long -term tasks: use glider, boards, stickers.

#### Support and motivation:

*   Help not with the solution, but with the structuring of tasks. Ask questions: “Where to start?”, “What is the next step?”, “How to imagine this?”
*   Mark not only the result, but also the process: a well -compiled plan, neat records, successful following the algorithms.

### School environment

#### Formulation of tasks:

*   Provide tasks with clockwork and criteria. Example: "The project includes: a title sheet, a plan of 3 chapters, 5 sources."
*   At the beginning of the topic or lesson, offer a plan. This will create a "frame" for new information.

#### Material feed:

*   Actively use diagrams, tables, lists, highlighting keywords with color. These are key "anchors" for perception.
*   There is no need to plant on the first desk to control attention. Provide the full visibility of demonstration materials (boards, screen).

#### Individual route:

*   A high concentration makes the student an excellent candidate for Olympiads and in -depth programs.
*   For project work, a brief consultation with a tutor or teacher may be required at the planning stage to structuring the plan.

## 4. Practical training

### For memory and planning:

*   **Board games:** Chess, go. Develop strategic thinking and planning in advance.
*   **Assembly according to the instructions:** Lego Technic designers, prefabricated models, origami according to complex schemes.
*   **Cooking:** Preparation of dishes according to new, multi -step recipes.

### For coordination and flexibility:

*   **Juggling:** Start with two items.
*   **Musical instruments:** Key, drums. Develop coordination of both hands.
*   **Finger gymnastics:** Exercises for the simultaneous performance of different figures with hands.

### For control and rhythm:

*   **Dancing:** Memorization and repetition of the sequence of movements (for example, hip-hop, salsa).
*   **Balance exercises:** Balansboard, walking along the line, a stand on one leg with eyes closed.
