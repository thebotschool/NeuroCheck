**Cognitive profile**
*Analysis of working memory, self-control and stability of attention (19-22 years).*

The report is designed to provide a deep understanding of the cognitive characteristics of the student. The recommendations presented are designed for maximum support for its potential.

---

**1. Key test results**

*   **Sustainability of attention and working memory (CPT):** An outstanding concentration and the ability to memorize large volumes of information, even in the presence of distracting factors. This is a significant force in training.
*   **Self-control (Go/No-Go):** Difficulties with the suppression of impulsive reactions and a timely stop of actions are noted. This can cause errors due to haste and lead to rapid fatigue.
*   **Reaction speed and attention (TCP):** A high ability to long -term, focused work without inattention errors, provided that the task is clarified and minimal switching.

**2. Individual training profile**

**Peculiarities**
*   **The pace of work:** Prefers a measured, deep pace. Capable of prolonged immersion in one task. A quick change in activity or haste is exhausted.
*   **Information perception:** It more efficiently assimilates structured and visually organized information (schemes, plans, Mayind cards). This simplifies logic and reduces the cognitive load.
*   **Focus of attention:** It is characterized by a "deep" focus. When immersed in a task, it is difficult to distract. However, this makes it difficult to quickly and smoothly switch between activities.

**Support factors**
*   Monosadality.
*   Clear, step -by -step instructions and plans.
*   Visual supports: schemes, graphs.
*   Predictable, calm atmosphere.
*   Short, independent pauses for "rebooting".

**Containing factors**
*   Multitasking.
*   Frequent switching between tasks.
*   Compressed, unrealistic terms.
*   Unstructured tasks.
*   Noisy or distracting media (for example, Open Space).
<!-- page-break -->

**3. Personalized recommendations**

**For the house**
*   **Organization of work:**
    *   Miss large tasks into small, step -by -step stages. Make a plan.
    *   Use the timer (for example, the method of prying: 30–40 minutes of work, 5-10 minutes of rest without gadgets). This will help to avoid overwork.
    *   Provide clean workspace: remove the excess, turn off the notifications.
*   **Support and encouragement:**
    *   Help in a weekly load planning.
    *   Discuss the causes of impulsiveness or fatigue, and not criticize.
    *   Praise for quality and completion, not speed.
*   **Maintaining attention:**
    *   Deep attention is possible up to 45-60 minutes. After that, a full -fledged break for "switching" is needed.

**For the educational environment**
*   **Setting tasks:**
    *   Provide written tasks with clockwork criteria and step -by -step plan. Avoid oral, vague instructions.
    *   For large projects, install intermediate deadlines.
*   **Material feed:**
    *   Use structured materials: presentations with logic, abstracts with subtitles, diagrams.
    *   Lectures with deep immersion in one topic are preferred, and not a frequent change in formats.
*   **Individual approach:**
    *   In exams/control, if possible, provide a place without distracting factors.
    *   Consider participation in long -term research projects. This will use the strengths: deep immersion, analytics, stable attention.

**4. Developing practices**

Purpose: to develop the skill of "braking", cognitive flexibility and the ability to switch.

*   **Coordination and control:**
    *   **Balance exercises:** Standing on one leg, walking along the line. Develop subtle control of movements and the ability to inhibition.
    *   **Juggling:** He trains the ligament of the "eye-hand" and the switching of attention between objects.
*   **Attention and reaction:**
    *   **Board games:** "Dobble", "drum", "uno". Develop a rapid reaction and suppression of impulsive answers.
    *   **Game "Delay":** Movement to music with an instant stop. Develops the skill of braking.
*   **Rhythm and memory:**
    *   **Musical instruments (drums) or dancing:** Develop the following rhythm, timely beginning and stop.
    *   **Exercise "Repeat and add":** He trains memory and ability to wait for his turn.
