# Cognitive profile: individual report

This report offers a deep understanding of the individual cognitive characteristics of the student. The results of CPT, Go/No-Go and TCP (age groups 19–22 years) formed the basis of recommendations aimed at improving the efficiency of learning and creating the optimal environment for development.

---

## 1. Test results

*   **Squeezing Test (CPT):** Effectively assimilates information, but experiences difficulties with its ordered reproduction. Facts are remembered, but their sequence requires additional attention.

*   **Self-control test (Go/No-Go):** Difficulties in the suppression of impulsive reactions were revealed, especially with fatigue. This indicates significant energy costs to maintain the focus and self -control.

*   **Test for stability of attention (TCP):** There is increased distractiveness to external factors. Paying attention in monotonous tasks, such as long lectures or reading volumetric text, requires additional support.

## 2. The guide for the success of your student

### Individual profile

*   **Working pace:** Optimal, a calm, measured rhythm. Hurry or temporary pressure increases errors and impulsiveness. Short pauses contribute to the restoration of concentration.
*   **Perception:** Visually structured information is most effectively absorbed. Audiomaterials without visual support are more complicated.
*   **Attention:** Demonstrates an intermittent focus. The brain actively responds to external stimuli, which distracts from the main task.

### Support: What helps

*   **Step -by -step instructions:** Separation of large tasks into small, understandable steps.
*   **Visual supports:** Schemes, mental cards, graphs, highlighting key elements. Short videos.
*   **Short intervals:** Method Methods (20-25 minutes of work, 5 minutes of rest) to maintain concentration and avoid overwork.
*   **Clear priorities:** Understanding the main task is minimized by a sense of overload.
*   **Organized environment:** Silence and lack of distracting factors at the workplace increase productivity.

### Obstacles: what is interfering

*   **Multitasking:** Attempts to fulfill several cases simultaneously lead to chaos and errors.
*   **Long monologues:** Oral explanations for more than 5-7 minutes without pauses or visual support reduce assimilation.
*   **Rush and pressure:** The requirement of the quick performance of tasks provokes impulsiveness and interferes with logical planning.
*   **Unstructured information:** Large volumes of unrelated text or lack of plan cause frustration.
*   **Background noise:** TV, conversations, open tabs in the browser critically reduce efficiency.

## 3. Recommendations

### For the house

*   **Performing tasks:**
    *   Divide large tasks into parts. Example: "First solve 3 problems in algebra, then a break of 5 minutes."
    *   Use planners or lists of affairs. Refusing the points executed enhances the sense of control.
    *   Install the timer for 20-25 minutes of work, followed by a short break.
*   **Support and motivation:**
    *   Praise the process, not just the result. Example: "You have planned the time perfectly."
    *   Help organize a workplace: remove the excess, agree on "digital hygiene" (for example, a phone outside the access zone).
*   **Mercing of attention:**
    *   Consider a limited effective concentration: about 15–25 minutes, after which the productivity is reduced.

### For school

*   **The wording of tasks:**
    *   Offer tasks in the form of a clear algorithm: "1. Read paragraph 2. Write out 5 key dates 3. Answer in writing to questions 2 and 4."
    *   For design work, provide ready -made templates with sections.
*   **Lesson structure:**
    *   Duplicate the key information of the lecture on the board or slides in the form of theses, diagrams, diagrams.
    *   Make short pauses and ask involving questions to maintain attention.
*   **Individual approach:**
    *   It is recommended to place a student closer to the teacher, away from distracting factors (window, door).
    *   If necessary (control, exams), consider the elements of an individual educational route: extra time, quiet room, structured tasks.

## 4. Practical training

These exercises will help to develop skills that showed potential to improve. Regularity and game format are important.

### Attention and self -control

*   **Search games:** “Find 10 differences”, “Search for objects” (Wimmelbuch) - develop concentration and selectivity of attention.
*   **Board games:** Requireing strategies and waiting for the move: chess, checkers, cacasson.
*   **Respiratory exercises:** The technique of "square breathing" (4 seconds inhale, 4 delay, 4 exhale, 4 delay) before the task helps to focus.

### Memory and sequence

*   **Creating instructions:** Ask to describe by steps the preparation of a simple dish, passing the level in the game or assembly of the model.
*   **Game "Memory":** Start with the classic, then complicate, demanding the memorization of the sequence of opening cards.
*   **Personal of the plot:** After watching a film or a series, ask to retell events, paying attention to their correct order.

### Bodily control and rhythm

*   **Games with the ball:** "Edible-universal"-develops the speed of reaction and inhibition.
*   **Juggling, balance sheet:** Improve coordination and the connection between the brain and the body, indirectly affecting cognitive control.
*   **RIGHTS RIGHTS:** Repetition of a simple rhythmic pattern (cotton) develops a sense of pace and promotes self -regulation.
