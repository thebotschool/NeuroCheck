### Cognitive assessment report

This report offers a deep understanding of individual cognitive features, identifying strengths and areas for support in training and everyday life.

---

### 1. Evaluation results

*   **Attention for stability (CPT):** Outstanding ability to prolonged concentration and work with large volumes of information. Formed strategies help to effectively solve complex problems.
*   **Control and braking test (Go/No-Go):** Asymmetry in the work of the hemispheres has been identified. This can affect the implementation of non -standard tasks requiring synchronous work of both sides.
*   **Reaction rate test (TCP):** The results indicate possible overwork, stress or anxiety. This temporarily reduces the ability to quickly reaction and manage attention in changing conditions.

<!-- page-break -->

### 2. Individual learning profile

#### Features of perception

*   **Pace:** Prefers a deep, measured immersion in the material. Hurrying and harsh deadlines cause stress.
*   **Format:** Optimally perceives structured and logically organized information. Visual schemes and plans significantly improve understanding.
*   **Focus:** Capable of prolonged concentration on one task. However, current exhaustion reduces the resistance of attention in conditions of switching or noise.

#### Effective approaches

*   **Step -by -step instructions:** Clear action plans reduce anxiety and contribute to concentration.
*   **Visual supports:** The use of schemes, planners, markers to highlight key points helps assimilation.
*   **Calm atmosphere:** The absence of distracting factors is critical of productive work.
*   **Rhythm and routine:** Regular daily routine and short, timely breaks support energy.
*   **Monosadality:** A deep immersion in one major project corresponds to the strengths.

#### Obstacles

*   **Multitasking:** Attempts to perform several cases simultaneously lead to rapid depletion.
*   **Fast change of tasks:** Frequent switching do not give an opportunity for a deep study and cause frustration.
*   **Oral instructions:** Without visual support, complex information is perceived with difficulty.
*   **Unexpected changes:** Sudden adaptation to new conditions increases stress.

<!-- page-break -->

### 3. Recommendations

#### Home environment

*   **Organization of work:**
    *   Use the “Plaud” method: 25-30 minutes of focused work, then 5-10 minutes of rest.
    *   Create a written plan for the day. Distribution of performed points enhances the feeling of control.
*   **Support and motivation:**
    *   Reduce pressure and expectations, focusing on well -being.
    *   Enjoy efforts, not just the final result.
    *   Provide a full sleep and time for rest without gadgets.

#### Educational institution

*   **The wording of tasks:**
    *   Provide tasks in written criteria and terms in writing.
    *   Report a lecture or project plan in advance.
*   **The structure of classes:**
    *   At the beginning of the lesson, voice and write down the plan.
    *   Serve the material with structured blocks, with pauses.
*   **Individual approach:**
    *   A temporary reduction in the training load may be useful for recovery.
    *   Consider the consultation with the curator to adapt the curriculum (for example, a shift of terms).

### 4. Development and support

These exercises will help remove the current overvoltage and improve coordination.

#### Stress decrease

*   **Respiratory practices:** “Squarey breathing” (inha than 4 scores for 4 scores) for quick reassurance.
*   **Schultte tables:** Training of concentration and peripheral vision. Available online and in applications.

#### Coordination

*   **Neurohymnastics:** Simple exercises with the simultaneous involvement of both hands (for example, “fist-rib-ladon” or drawing different figures in the air).
*   **Juggling:** It perfectly develops the coordination of the "eye-hand" and interhemispheric interaction.
*   **Musical instruments:** The game on keyboard or string instruments, requiring the coordinated work of both hands.

#### Development of strengths

*   **Strategic Games:** Chess, go or other board games that require planning allow you to use strong cognitive skills in a playful way.
