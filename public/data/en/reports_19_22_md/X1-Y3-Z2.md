### 2. The guide for the success of your student

#### Individual profile

*   **Pace:** Prefers to work at his own, measured pace. Needs independent planning. Short, regular breaks are required to preserve the focus and prevent mental fatigue.

*   **Perception:** The most effectively perceives structured and visually designed information: schemes, checklists, presentations. Solid text or monotonous speech is less effective. The audial channel without visual support is the least productive.

*   **Focus of attention:** Deep concentration is achieved on interesting and understandable tasks. Distraction occurs with a passive perception of information or in conditions of multitasking. Attention works "design".

#### Efficiency support

*   Clear structure: step-by-step instructions, checklists, clear criteria.
*   Visual supports: diagrams, intelligence cards, graphs, color selection.
*   The "One task at a time" mode: complete concentration in the current case.
*   Time management techniques: for example, the Tomator method (25 minutes of work, 5 minutes of rest).
*   Independent planning: the ability to divide the task into stages.

#### Obstacles

*   Monotony: long lectures or audio without visualization.
*   Multivativity: simultaneous listening, recording and analysis.
*   Information chaos: unstructured data stream, lack of plan.
*   A sharp change in activity: the need for quick switching to a new type of task.

### 3. Recommendations

#### For home: independent work

*   **Organization:**
    *   Make a plan or intelligent card before starting work.
    *   Break large tasks into small, perform step by step.
    *   Use a timer for interval work (for example, 30 minutes of work, 5-10 minutes of a break).
*   **Support:**
    *   Create a workplace without distracting factors (disconnect notifications).
    *   Focus on the process, encourage yourself for the completed stages.
*   **Expectations:**
    *   Effective concentration on a difficult task is 25–40 minutes. Plan activities based on these intervals.

#### For school: support for teachers

*   **Tasks:**
    *   Provide the tasks in writing with clockwork instructions.
    *   Offer the choice of execution format (abstract, presentation, project).
*   **Material feed:**
    *   Accompany lectures with presentations with key theses and schemes.
    *   Make short pauses for questions or discussions.
    *   Duplicate important information visually (board, handout).
*   **Individual route:**
    *   High potential for research and project activities. Attract to such formats.
    *   The written form of exams/tests to think about the answer is preferred.

### 4. Recommended training

These exercises are aimed at developing the stability of attention and interhemispheric interaction.

#### Attention and self -control

*   **Practice "Tomato":** Regular use for training focused work.
*   **Digital hygiene:** Conscious disconnection of notifications during study.

#### Coordination and interhemispheric interaction

*   **Juggling:** Start with two goals. Develops coordination, attention, connection of hemispheres.
*   **Tool game:** For example, piano or guitar. Requires synchronous work of both hands.
*   **Mirror drawing:** Simultaneous drawing of symmetrical figures with both hands.

#### Bodily control

*   **Balance exercises:** Standing on one leg (with closed eyes for complication), the use of a balance sheet. Improves self -awareness and concentration.
