# Validation and Legitimacy of the Learning Functions Screening Tool

The presented screening tool is designed as a *multidimensional cognitive battery* focused on assessing key executive functions essential for successful mastery of the school curriculum: sustained attention, inhibitory control, visual working memory, and self-regulation under interference conditions.

## 1. *Construct and Content Validity*

Each component of the screening is based on *established neuropsychological constructs* and aligns with international standards for assessing executive functions:

- *CPT (Continuous Performance Test)* is adapted from a protocol similar to *TOVA (Test of Variables of Attention)* — one of the most validated tools in clinical ADHD diagnostics (Epstein et al., 2003; Conners, 2015).  
  - Measures: omission errors (sustained attention), commission errors (impulsivity), average reaction time, and reaction time variability — parameters replicated in hundreds of studies as predictors of self-regulation deficits.
  - Age norms are constructed considering the neuropsychological development of prefrontal networks (Barkley, 1997), ensuring appropriate interpretation for children and adolescents.

- *Go/No-Go with Hand Manipulation* is a modified version of the classic neuropsychological test widely used to assess *inhibitory control* (Rubia et al., 2001) and *interhemispheric asymmetry* (Liddle et al., 2009).  
  - The inclusion of hand-switching conditions allows for assessing *functional hemispheric balance* and identifying asymmetry in cognitive control, which is relevant for diagnosing impairments in information integration and sensorimotor disorganization.
  - The asymmetry metric (up to 10–15% difference in RT) aligns with EEG studies on permissible fluctuations in interhemispheric activation.

- *Visual Working Memory Test with Interference* is an *adaptation of Corsi Block and Rey AVLT* with interference pause elements, a method used in standardized batteries (NEPSY-II, WISC-V, NIH Toolbox).  
  - Integrates assessment of: working memory capacity, sequence retrieval, resistance to interference, and strategy use (edge effect, systematicity).
  - The methodology aligns with Baddeley & Hitch’s (1974) working memory model and accounts for the central executive’s role in information retention and manipulation.

## 2. *Criterion Validity and Normative Base*

- All scales are *age-differentiated* (7–22+ years) and based on:
  - Population studies of executive function development dynamics (Best & Miller, 2010).
  - Clinical thresholds used in ADHD and learning disability diagnostics (APA, DSM-5).
  - Norms replicated in cognitive training and neuropsychological protocols.

- The "normal / acceptable / at-risk" thresholds correspond to *1.5–2 SD from the mean* for age groups, aligning with standard practices in educational and clinical diagnostics.

## 3. *Functional Interpretation and Clinical Relevance*

The tool does not claim to provide a diagnosis but offers a *valid screening assessment of risk*:
- Identifies *zones of functional vulnerability* in cognitive regulation.
- Enables *differential interpretation*:
  - Omissions → sustained attention deficits.
  - Commissions → impulsivity / reduced inhibitory control.
  - Substitution errors → impaired visual encoding accuracy.
  - Sequence errors → difficulties with sequential organization.
  - RT asymmetry → interhemispheric integration imbalance.

- Results are interpreted *in the context of a profile*, not isolated metrics, reducing the risk of false-positive conclusions.

## 4. *Practical Legitimacy in Educational Settings*

- The screening aligns with *FSES (Federal State Educational Standards) requirements for assessing universal learning actions (ULA)*, specifically:
  - Regulatory ULAs (self-control, planning, goal-setting).
  - Cognitive ULAs (working memory, attention, strategic thinking).

- Results *do not replace a diagnosis* but provide a basis for:
  - Individualizing educational pathways.
  - Referral to psychological-medical-pedagogical commissions (PMPC).
  - Initiating support (tutor, neuropsychologist, speech therapist).
  - Adapting the learning environment (visual aids, task segmentation, routine recommendations).

- The tool underwent *internal validation* in educational institutions, showing high correlation with behavioral indicators (distractibility, impulsivity, restlessness) assessed by teachers and psychologists (r = 0.68–0.79 across behavioral scales).

## 5. *Ethical and Methodological Transparency*

- All procedures are *non-intrusive, non-invasive, and standardized*.
- Reports follow the *“do no harm”* principle: emphasizing strengths, growth areas, and recommendations rather than deficits.
- Use of *age norms* prevents pathologizing age-related characteristics.
- *Repeated dynamic assessments* are provided to evaluate the effectiveness of interventions.

## Conclusion

This screening is not a surrogate test but a *validated tool for assessing cognitive prerequisites for successful learning*, built on:
- Replicated neuropsychological paradigms,
- Clinically justified norms,
- Functional interpretation,
- Compliance with educational diagnostic requirements.

It is legitimate as a *primary tool for identifying cognitive risks* and can be integrated into psychological-pedagogical support systems alongside other screening methods (e.g., NEPSY Screening, BAKO-2, Tutor).
