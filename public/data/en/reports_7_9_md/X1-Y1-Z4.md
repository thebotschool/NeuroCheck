# Cognitive development report

### **Features of the profile: attention, control, memory (7–9 years)**

This report provides a comprehensive view of the cognitive characteristics of the student, offering practical recommendations for support in learning and development.

## 1. Review of tests

*   **Attention (CPT):** The concentration decreases with prolonged or monotonous tasks, which can cause errors.
*   **Control (Go/No-Go):** A high level of self -control, the ability to follow the rules and effectively stop. Impulsiveness under control.
*   **Visual memory:** Great visual working memory. Easy memorization of visual sequences and keeping information.

## 2. The guide for the success of your student

The student has a potential to achieve goals, but he needs support in managing attention.

### Individual characteristics

*   **Pace:** The optimal, rhythmic pace is optimal. A fast pace leads to overload, slow - to a loss of interest.
*   **Perception:** The visual channel prevails. Information is effectively absorbed through diagrams, images, video and visual instructions.
*   **Attention:** The focus of attention can be changeable. It is held on interesting tasks, but weakens with uniformity or long explanations.

### Which contributes to success

*   **Visual supports:** Check lists, visual plans, timers, color isolation.
*   **Phased tasks:** Separation of large tasks into short, controlled stages (10-15 minutes).
*   **Clear instructions:** Simultaneous supply of one task.
*   **Movement and game:** Interactive elements, short breaks for physical activity.
*   **Predictability:** Visible plan of the day or lesson.

### Which prevents success

*   **Monotone:** Long, monotonous tasks.
*   **Multitasking:** Simultaneous performance of several actions.
*   **Long oral instructions:** The risk of loss of part of the information.
*   **Background noise:** Distracting sounds and chaos are quickly tiring.

<!-- page-break -->

## 3. Recommendations

### For the house

*   **Organization of work:**
    *   Divide homework into 15-minute blocks with short breaks (2-3 minutes). Use the timer.
    *   Provide the "clean" working area: only the necessary objects for the task.
    *   Use checklist checks for evening tasks.
*   **Support:**
    *   Praise for **effort and process**, not just for the result.
    *   Enough in planning: Discuss the procedure for completing tasks.
*   **Mercing of attention:**
    *   Effective attention remains for 10-15 minutes. After that, a change of activity or break is required.

### For school

*   **Instructions:**
    *   Give short, clear, step -by -step instructions. Duplicate them visually.
    *   Use color discharge for key information.
*   **Lesson structure:**
    *   Change the activities every 15-20 minutes.
    *   Place a schoolboy on the first or second desk to minimize abstracts.
*   **Material feed:**
    *   Use schemes, infographics, intellect cards, short videos. Visual perception is priority.
    *   Establish eye contact before the explanation.
*   **Individualization:**
    *   Provide the visual plan for the lesson.
    *   Allow the use of Algorithm cards.
    *   Offer a choice in tasks.

## 4. Recommended training

These exercises and games will help strengthen attention using strengths: memory and self -control.

*   **Development of attention:**
    *   **Graphic dictations:** Improve concentration and following instructions.
    *   **"Find the differences":** He trains attention to the details.
    *   **Correct samples:** Increase selectivity.
*   **Coordination and control:**
    *   **Games with the ball:** Develop motor skills and accuracy of movements.
    *   **Neurohymnastics:** Exercises "Ring", "Fist-Robric Ladon" to improve interhemispheric interaction.
*   **Memory support:**
    *   **"Memory":** He trains visual memory and concentration.
    *   **"Remember and repeat":** A gradual increase in the number of memorized objects.
*   **Rhythmic development:**
    *   **RIGHTS RIGHTS:** Helps to stabilize attention.
    *   **Exercises under the metronome:** Synchronization of movements with a given rhythm.
