### **Cognitive assessment: profile of attention, control and memory (7–9 years)**

Understanding the potential. This report reveals the strengths and areas of the growth of your student. The results are a landmark for effective development and training.

### **1. A brief report for each test**

*   **Test for consistent memory.**
    The child remembers facts well, but experiences difficulties with their ordered reproduction. This indicates a good memory potential requiring the development of information planning and structuring skills.

*   **Braking and control test (Go/No-Go).**
    A quick reaction and timely braking of actions turned out to be a difficult task. The functions of self -control and “internal brakes” are in the process of formation and need support, especially in situations that require quick solutions.

*   **Test for stability of attention.**
    In conditions of a clear and unchanging task, the child demonstrated a high ability to concentrate. Long -term, concentrated work is his strength.

**General conclusion.**
The child has high potential for deep concentration. Difficulties arise with multi -stage, quick tasks or the need for instant shift between actions.

### **2. Instructions for the child as a student**

#### **Individual characteristics.**

*   **Pace.**
    The schoolboy is a "marathon", not a "sprinter". The most productive at a calm, measured pace. A rush leads to confusion and errors.

*   **Perception format.**
    It is best assimilating information presented clearly, logical and step by step. Long oral instructions or multi -step tasks are difficult.

*   **Focus of attention.**
    Capable of deep and prolonged concentration on one understandable task. Attention is violated if it is necessary to quickly switch, self -restoration or tracking of several objects at the same time.

#### **Which helps.**

*   **Step -by -step instructions.**
    A clear plan of action, where each step ends before the start of the next. Ideal-in a checklist format or a numbered list.

*   **Visual supports.**
    Cards with algorithms, highlighting keywords, diagrams, mental cards.

*   **Predictability and rhythm.**
    A clear, repeated structure of the lesson or the completion of tasks. This reduces the cost of brain resources to control, directing it to the task itself.

*   **Pauses for "braking".**
    Give one instruction at a time, making a small pause for its processing before the next.

#### **What interferes.**

*   **Multitasking.**
    Requests like “Listen, look and write down at the same time” practically impossible and cause overload.

*   **Fast change of tasks.**
    A frequent transition between activities without a logical pause or completion.

*   **Oral multi -component instructions.**
    Tasks given by a voice of several steps (for example, “Take a red pencil, circle the second picture, and draw a cross under it”). Probably only the first or last action will be remembered.

*   **Noise and distracting factors.**
    The chaotic situation requires constant filtration of excess, which depleted the resource of self -control.

### **3. Recommendations**

#### **For the house.**

*   **Homework.**
    *   Miss tasks into short blocks (15–20 minutes) with interruptions.
    *   Use a timer to visualize the working hours. It helps to tune in.
    *   Make a simple plan before starting: "First mathematics, then rest, then reading." Cross out the executed points.

*   **Support and promotion.**
    *   Praise for attentiveness and completion of the task, and not for speed. ("I see how you tried and was not distracted, it's great!")
    *   If the schoolboy "hovered" or began to make mistakes, offer: "Let's pause, drink water and return in 5 minutes."

*   **Holding attention.**
    Effective concentration on one understandable task: 20–25 minutes. If necessary, frequent switching (for example, a difficult example with several actions): attention resource can be exhausted after 5-10 minutes.

#### **For school.**

*   **The formulation of tasks.**
    *   Crusting the instructions. Instead of one long - three short with pauses.
    *   Give the tasks one at a time. First distribute the material, make sure that everyone is ready, then voice the actions.

*   **Structure and organization.**
    *   Place the student at the first or second desk in the central row to minimize distracting factors and direct contact with the teacher.
    *   Provide a visual support on the desk: a card with a lesson plan, an algorithm for solving a problem or rule.

*   **IOOM elements (individual educational route).**
    *   It is recommended to include in the curriculum work on the development of regulatory functions (self -control, planning).
    *   It will be useful to work with a psychologist or tutor aimed at training the "internal brake" and the ability to act according to the algorithm.

### **4. Recommended training**

These games and exercises will help to develop the necessary skills.

*   **For attention and self -control (skill "slow down").**
    *   **"Edible-Sreedy":** Classic game for training quick reaction and braking.
    *   **"Forbidden movement":** The host shows movements, one of which is “forbidden”. It cannot be repeated.
    *   **Graphic dictations:** For example, "two cells to the right, one up, stop." Excellent teach to listen, act step by step and control the impulse.

*   **For consistent memory and planning.**
    *   **"Build in order":** Lay out 3-5 pictures illustrating history. Ask to reproduce order. Gradually increase the amount.
    *   **Compilation of stories on a series of pictures:** Develops the ability to see a logical sequence of events.
    *   **Board games for memorizing a sequence** (for example, with the repetition of combinations of moves or sounds).

*   **For coordination and bodily control.**
    *   **"Fist-Robric Ladon":** The exercise of neurohymnastics, where you need to alternately and in the rhythm change the position of the hands on the table.
    *   **Games with the ball:** Beat off the ball on the floor or wall in a given rhythm (for example, 2 times quickly, 1 time slowly).
    *   **Walking along the line or border:** Develops balance and sensorimotor control.
