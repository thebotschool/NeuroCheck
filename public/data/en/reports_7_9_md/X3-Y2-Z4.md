**Cognitive assessment report: CPT+Go/No-Go+TCP (7–9 years)**

This report is an analysis of the cognitive characteristics of the student. Its goal is to propose practical recommendations for the development of potential and creating an effective educational environment.

# 1. A brief report for each test

## Consistent memory (CPT)
The student confidently remembers the information. The main challenge is to build it in the correct sequence. He remembers the content, but can confuse order.

## Impulse control (Go/No-Go)
Difficulties with the suppression of impulsive reactions are noted. This can be manifested in a hurry, interrupting and errors on inattention.

## Attention and control (TCP)
Tasks that require simultaneously retaining rules, monitoring and quick action cause a significant cognitive load and are difficult to give.

# 2. The guide for the success of your student

## Individual profile

### The pace of work
The tendency to a quick, impulsive pace, which often leads to errors. External slowdowns and organized pauses for understanding are needed.

### Perception format
Information is better absorbed when it is presented clearly, briefly and step -by -step. Long verbal instructions on ear can be lost and not processed.

### Focus of attention
Attention is stable on short, understandable, monosyllabic tasks. It is easily distracted by multicomponent or long -term tasks, as well as on a background noise.

## Supporting strategies

*   **Visual supports:** The use of cards with a plan of action, schemes, highlighting keywords with color.
*   **Step -by -step instructions:** The principle of "one task is one instruction." The next step is voiced only after the previous one.
*   **Rhythm and pronouncing:** Rhythm is tapping (for example, with a pencil) or pronouncing steps aloud for slowdown and concentration.
*   **Clear signals "Stop":** The use of a conditional sign (raised arm, card) to indicate the need to think before the answer.

## Barriers in training

*   **Multitasking:** Requests to perform several actions simultaneously reduce effectiveness.
*   **Hurry and oral instructions on the run:** They lead to information overload and loss of important information.
*   **Large amount of information:** Long, unstructured texts or tasks on one sheet without dividing into blocks cause difficulties.
*   **Distracting factors:** Noise and visual disorder reduce concentration.

# 3. Recommendations

## For the house

### Working with homework
Miss homework into short blocks (10-15 minutes). Include mandatory physical warmings between blocks. Use a visual timer to control time.

### Support and planning
Draw up and say an action plan in advance. Discuss the plan the next day before going to bed so that the student is ready.

### The system of incentives
Focus praise on the process and applied effort, and not just on the result. Mark the manifestations of self -control and following the plan.

### The optimal duration of concentration
The student productively keeps attention on one type of activity for no more than 15 minutes. After this, a change in activity or a short break is needed.

## For school

### The formulation of tasks
Crusting complex tasks on simple, consistent steps. Example: "Read the condition. Emphasize the question. Write down the action."

### The structure of the lesson
Place the student closer to the teacher, away from distracting factors (window, door). Contact by name more often, establish visual contact before the instructions. Ask to briefly repeat the task to confirm understanding.

### Communication style
Use algorithmic cards for complex actions. Apply clear and short commands.

### Individual route
Consider the inclusion of elements of an individual educational route (IOM) for the development of regulatory universal educational actions (UUD): planning, control, evaluation. Tutor support can be useful for organizing educational activities.

# 4. Recommended training

These simple games and exercises contribute to the development of the necessary functions. Regularity and positive attitude are important.

## Attention and self -control

*   **Graphic dictations.**
*   **Correct samples:** Search and dispensing the specified characters in the text.
*   **The game "Find the differences".**

## Memory and organization

*   **The game "Repeat me":** Reproduction of the sequence of objects. Gradually increase the number of elements.
*   **Compilation of stories from the pictures:** Develops logical and temporary sequence.
*   **Retelling of the events of the day:** Restoration of chronology of actions.

## Rhythm and braking

*   **The game "Forbidden movement":** Control of impulsive reactions through the suppression of certain movements.
*   **"Edible-Sreedy":** Classical game to a quick reaction and control.
*   **RIGHTS RIGHTS:** The exact reproduction of the specified rhythms.

## Coordination and bodily control

*   **Games with the ball:** Catching and throws in a given rhythm, fighting off the wall.
*   **Balance exercises:** Standing on one leg, walking along the drawn line, “Swallow”.
