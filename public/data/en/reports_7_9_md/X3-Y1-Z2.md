### Assessment of cognitive skills

This report gives an understanding of the strengths and growth zones of your student. Recommendations are designed to increase the effectiveness of learning both at home and at school.

---

# 1. Cognitive profile

*   **Working memory:** The information is remembered well, but the reproduction sequence requires development.
*   **Self -control:** High level. An excellent ability to follow the rules, control impulses, quickly switch.
*   **Sustainability of attention:** Difficulty with prolonged concentration, especially on monotonous tasks. Fatigue, distractiveness is noted.

<!-- page-break -->

# 2. Individual learning approach

## Features of perception

*   **The pace of work:** Fast and accurate on short tasks. Slow down and makes errors on long or monotonous.
*   **Information perception:** Optimally - visually and step -by -step. Oral instructions are hard to remember without clarity.
*   **Duration of concentration:** Short, but intense focus (15–20 minutes). Requires a frequent change in activity or breaks.

## Which contributes to success

*   Step -by -step instructions.
*   Visual support: checklists, schemes, cards.
*   Short working intervals (for example, a method of siring).
*   Clear, predictable rules.

## What complicates training

*   Multitasking.
*   Long oral instructions.
*   Monotonous tasks.
*   Lack of clear structure.

<!-- page-break -->

# 3. Practical recommendations

## Home training

*   **Performing tasks:**
    *   Break large tasks into 2-3 small ones.
    *   Use the timer: intervals for 15–20 minutes.
    *   Make simple plans for the evening. Reviewing motivates.
    *   Make complex items in the beginning, when attention is as much as possible.
*   **Support and motivation:**
    *   Praise for efforts and following the plan, not only for the result.
    *   Apply a system of rules and remuneration (effective thanks to developed self -control). Example: Completion of tasks until 19:00 = board game.

## School training

*   **Submission of instructions:**
    *   Short, clear phrases. Avoid verbose instructions.
    *   Use visual algorithms (board, cards).
*   **Lesson structure:**
    *   Alternate activities every 15–20 minutes.
    *   Place the child at the board, away from abstracts. Provide visual contact.
*   **Individual route:**
    *   The emphasis on the development of regulatory UUD: planning, self -control, assessment.
    *   Consider the work with a tutor or psychologist for the development of planning strategies.

<!-- page-break -->

# 4. Games and exercises for development

Games and exercises that will support the development of the necessary skills.

*   **Development of attention and memory to the sequence:**
    *   **Graphic dictations:** Training of auditory attention and holding the sequence.
    *   **"Repeat the pattern":** Reproduction of a sequence of 3-5 items. Complicate gradually.
    *   **Board games:** Walking with the rules and the sequence of actions.
*   **Coordination and self -control (strengths):**
    *   **"Edible-Sreedy":** A quick reaction and braking game.
    *   **Rhythmic games:** Cooling/polling rhythm. Develops a sense of rhythm and sequence.
*   **General development of skills:**
    *   **Assembly of designers:** Training the follow -up of step -by -step instructions.
    *   **Cooking on recipes:** Transfer of training skills to household activities.
