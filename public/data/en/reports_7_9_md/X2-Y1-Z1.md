# Report: Potential and development
### Cognitive assessment (7-9 years)

This report offers a deep understanding of the strengths of your child and areas for growth. Recommendations are based on test results, aimed at creating an optimal environment for training and comprehensive development.

---

## 1. Key conclusions

*   **Attention (TCP):** High concentration ability. Sustainable work on a task without abstracts is a pronounced strength.
*   **Self-control (Go/No-Go):** Effective self -regulation. Demonstrates the ability to follow the rules and meaningfully approach actions.
*   **Visual memory (CPT):** Optimization of visual memory. It may experience difficulties with the simultaneous holding of many small visual details. Uses an effective strategy: memorizing the first and last elements of the series.

<!-- page-break -->

## 2. Understanding the potential

### Individual characteristics

*   **Pace:** Thoughtful and consistent. It may require more time to process a large volume of visual data.
*   **Perception:** Optimally assimilates clear, step -by -step instructions. Visual information is effective in moderate volume and clear structure.
*   **Focus of attention:** It is capable of deep and prolonged concentration, especially with clear tasks without an excess of visual details.

### Optimal conditions

*   **Step -by -step instructions:** Clear algorithms of action.
*   **Visual anchors:** Using color, stickers, simple schemes to highlight key information.
*   **Strategy "Start-Coon":** When mastering the list or rules: assimilation of the first, then the latter, after - medium elements.
*   **Clear rhythm:** Regular change in activity (work/rest) to maintain concentration.

### Calls

*   **Multitasking:** The simultaneous performance of several tasks is difficult. A consistent approach is required.
*   **Visual overload:** An excess of unstructured visual information (for example, on the pages of a textbook) can cause confusion.
*   **Oral instructions:** Long verbal explanations without visual support are more complicated.
*   **Rush:** The pressure of time, especially in the tasks of memorization, can lead to errors and uncertainty.

<!-- page-break -->

## 3. Practical solutions

### At home

*   **Organization of tasks:**
    *   Break work into parts (for example, 15 minutes of activity, 5 minutes break).
    *   Use a timer to visualize the remaining time.
    *   Apply cards for memorization (poems, rules: term - definition).
*   **Support and incentive:**
    *   Focus on the process and efforts, and not just on the result. Mark the use of strategies ("excellent, which has broken the task into parts!").
    *   Create "memory cards" or intelligence cards. This turns memorization into a creative process.
*   **Attention management:**
    *   Concentration up to 20 minutes. For the tasks for memorization, short breaks are recommended every 10-12 minutes.

### At school

*   **The wording of tasks:**
    *   Step -by -step instructions.
    *   Work plan on the board in the form of a numbered list.
    *   Duplication of important oral instructions with a brief record.
*   **Lesson structure:**
    *   Divide the material into semantic blocks.
    *   Use subtitles and highlighting keywords in texts.
    *   The dosed use of visual materials: one idea - one slide/poster.
*   **Accommodation and feed:**
    *   The place is recommended closer to the board to minimize visual abstracts.
    *   Subject style: "instructor-partner"-calm, clear, supportive. Avoid rhetoric and prolonged retreats.
    *   **Support elements:** A full -fledged individual route is not required, but visual supports (cards with a plan, algorithms) are useful, as well as permission to use a line for tracking and writing off.

## 4. Way to development

Games and exercises aimed at developing visual memory and strengthening existing strengths.

### For memory

*   **"Memory":** Classical game for visual memory. Start with 6-8 pairs, gradually increasing the complexity.
*   **"What has changed?":** Lay out 5-7 items. After memorization, ask the child to turn away, change one element. Purpose: to determine the change.
*   **Graphic dictations:** Drawing on cells under dictation. Develops memory, attention and spatial thinking.

### For attention

*   **Correct samples:** Crossing certain letters in the text.
*   **Board games:** "Find 10 differences", "Dobbl", labyrinths.

### For coordination and control

*   **"Lazy eights":** Drawing an infinity sign in the air or on paper with both hands, alternately and together.
*   **Cross -steps:** Following the right elbow of the left knee and vice versa, at a slow pace.
*   **Tapping rhythms:** Reproduction of rhythms. Develops auditory attention and a sense of rhythm, contributes to the structure of information.
