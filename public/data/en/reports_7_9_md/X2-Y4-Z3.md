### **Cognitive profile report**
**Study of potential**

This report offers the view of the individual characteristics of the perception and training of the student. Recommendations are designed to maintain development and create an optimal environment for revealing its potential.

---

### **1. Key indicators**

*   **Visual memory:** Excellent memorization of initial and final information. Details in the middle can be less clear. This is a strong strategy for learning.
*   **Impulse control (Go/No-Go):** The development of the ability to timely self -control and the suppression of impulsive reactions is required, especially when the tasks are rapidly switching.
*   **Attention and self -regulation:** The tendency to fast, impulsive action. It is important to comprehend the instructions before the start of execution.

<!-- page-break -->

### **2. The guide for the success of your student**

#### **Features of perception**

*   **Pace:** Fast, impulsive. Actions can be ahead of the complete understanding of the task.
*   **Format:** Effectively perceives the information in small, tall portions. It remembers perfectly **The first and last** In the sequence.
*   **Attention:** Labily, easily distracted. The concentration is difficult if you need self -control or change of activity.

#### **Effective strategies**

*   **Step -by -step instructions:** One task is one instruction.
*   **Visual support:** Check lists, schemes, color accents.
*   **Clear rhythm:** Predictable rituals of the beginning and completion.
*   **Short breaks:** 1-2 minutes of movement for restarting attention.
*   **Timer:** Visualization of time, control of the duration of work.

#### **Obstacles**

*   **Multitasking:** Reduces accuracy, leads to errors.
*   **Sharp change of tasks:** The absence of pauses causes overload.
*   **Large amount of information:** Long oral instructions, continuous text.
*   **Distracting factors:** Noise, conversations, visual mess.

<!-- page-break -->

### **3. Personal recommendations**

#### **For the house**

*   **Homework:**
    *   Separate tasks into blocks: 10-15 minutes of work, 3-5 minutes break.
    *   Apply the principle of “first and last”: key tasks at the beginning, memorized - at the end.
    *   Before work: jointly say the plan (2-3 steps). Example: Open a notebook. Solve examples. Check.
*   **Support and Wednesday:**
    *   Approve diligence and self -control, not only the result. Example: Mark the correction of errors.
    *   Provide a calm workspace: a minimum of abstracts, disconnecting gadgets.
*   **Focus of attention:**
    *   Effective concentration on the task: 10-15 minutes. Next is a brief rest.

#### **For school**

*   **Making tasks:**
    *   Instructions: One step at a time. Example: "Open the textbook." (Pause). "Find the rule." (Pause). "Perform the exercise."
    *   Clear verbs: “write”, “monitor”, “emphasize”.
*   **The structure of the material:**
    *   Key information: place at the beginning or end (board, presentation).
    *   Visual anchors: color, icons, frames for tasks.
*   **Optimal placement:**
    *   Perfect place: first desk, a class center. Away from distracting factors (window, door).
*   **Individual route:**
    *   In case of difficulties with self -regulation: consider the maintenance of the tutor or elements of an individual educational route (IOOM). This will help to adapt tasks and pace.

### **4. Practices for growth**

Practices for growth: 5-10 minutes daily, in a playful way. The development of key skills.

*   **Attention and control:**
    *   **"Frozen!" / "The sea is worried once":** Instant stop at a signal.
    *   **"You go quieter - you will continue":** Speed ​​control and timely stop.
    *   **Board games:** Compliance with the queue and rules.
*   **Visual memory:**
    *   **"Memory":** Search for paired cards (start with 4-6 pairs).
    *   **"What has changed?":** Memorization of 5-7 items, then identifying changes.
*   **Coordination and movement:**
    *   **Balance:** Walking along the line, a stand on one leg, the transfer of the ball with eyes closed.
    *   **Games with the ball:** Catching, throws on the target-training of the ligament of the "eye-hand" and self-control.
*   **Rhythm:**
    *   **"Echo":** Repetition of a torn or clamped rhythm.
