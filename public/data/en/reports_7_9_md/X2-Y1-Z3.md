### **Cognitive assessment report**

This report offers a detailed view of the cognitive features of your student. The goal is to provide clear recommendations for the development and creation of an optimal training environment.

***

#### **1. Analysis of cognitive tests**

*   **Visual memory:** Difficulties with complete memorization of extensive visual details were noted. At the same time, an effective strategy is present: fixing the initial and final elements.
*   **Braking (Go/No-Go):** High efficiency in tasks requiring a quick reaction and electoral control. Successful concentration in the conditions of clear rules.
*   **Sustainability of attention (CPT):** The tendency to impulsive reactions and hasty actions. Control weakens in monotonous tasks.

<!-- page-break -->

#### **2. The guide for the success of your student**

**Key features.**
*   **Pace:** A quick, gusty pace is characteristic. The priority of action on thinking, which can lead to errors by carelessness.
*   **Information perception:** The optimal assimilation of short, structured information. Long or visually overloaded texts cause fatigue and provoke impulsiveness.
*   **Focus of attention:** Capable of deep concentration in dynamic and understandable tasks. In monotonous or volumetric tasks, attention control is reduced.

**Development support.**
*   **Step -by -step instructions:** Miss large tasks into 2-3 small stages with clockwork. Example: "Emphasize the main thing, then write out the dates, check what was written."
*   **Visual assistants:** Use short checks or self-testing patterns. Example: "1. Read. 2. Understand. 3. Write down. 4. Check."
*   **Rhythm and rituals:** Enter repeated actions before and after completion of work for better adaptation and transition to the task.
*   **Verbalization:** Encourage pronouncing an action plan out loud. This contributes to slowdown and conscious planning.
*   **Using strategies:** Focus on key information at the beginning and end of the material, given the natural patterns of memorization.

**Factors of influence.**
*   **Multitasking:** The simultaneous performance of several tasks significantly increases the risk of errors.
*   **Long monotonous tasks:** Repeating, the same type of exercise quickly deplete the resource of self -control.
*   **Abstract calls:** General phrases like "Be careful!" Ineffective, as they do not give specific tools.
*   **Hurry and time Tale:** Time restriction enhances the innate tendency to haste.
*   **Visual noise:** The lack of a clear separation and excess elements on one page.

<!-- page-break -->

#### **3. Recommendations**

**Home environment.**
*   **Organization of work:**
    *   Use the technique of "tomato": 15–20 minutes of work, 5 minutes of active break.
    *   Divide large tasks into parts. Instead of "all mathematics" - "the first three tasks, then rest."
    *   Ask the student to voice his plan to complete the assignment before the start of work. This contributes to planning.
*   **Support and encouragement:**
    *   Praise for the process, and not just for the result: "You are in no hurry and checked - excellent!", "You withstood 15 minutes of concentration."
    *   Treat errors in carelessness as a consequence of haste. Offer a joint search for a solution.
*   **Mercing of attention:** An effective concentration interval on the educational task is 15–20 minutes. Plan activities based on these cycles.

**School context.**
*   **The wording of tasks:** The instructions should be consistent, short and clear. Use specific verbs: “emphasize”, “monitor”, “write down”.
*   **Structuring the material:**
    *   On the board or in handouts, use lists, color selection and clear separation of blocks.
    *   Provide checks for self-testing when performing control and independent work.
*   **Class placement:** The optimal place is in the teacher's visibility zone, with a minimum of external distracting factors (for example, not by the window).
*   **Style style:** A dynamic presentation is welcomed with the involvement and frequent "control points": "Have you found everything? Raise your hand."
*   **Individual approach:** It is recommended to use a simple timer to control working intervals and the provision of tasks in printed form with already highlighted keywords.

#### **4. Recommended training**

The proposed games and exercises contribute to the development of key cognitive skills.

*   **Attention and self -control:**
    *   **Board games:** Checkers, chess, UNO, Jenga. They teach to wait, calculate moves and control impulses.
    *   **Game "Delay":** Music plays, the child is dancing. When stopping music, you need to freeze instantly.
*   **Visual memory:**
    *   **Game "Find a couple" (memorial):** Start with 6-8 couples, gradually increasing the number.
    *   **"What has changed?":** Lay out 5-7 items. The child remembers, turns away. You change or remove the item. The task is to determine the change.
*   **Coordination and rhythm:**
    *   **Graphic dictations:** Drawing on cells under dictation. He perfectly trains the bunch of "I hear, I am making" and the skill of slowdown.
    *   **Fighting rhythm:** Repetition of adults simple and complex rhythmic patterns.
*   **Bodily control:**
    *   **Balance exercises:** Walking along the line, a stand on one leg (with open, then with eyes closed). Promotes to improve the awareness of the body and its control.
