# Cognitive development report
## Comprehensive assessment: memory and self -control (7–9 years)

This report provides key conclusions based on test results. It will help you determine the strengths and areas for development, and will also offer practical recommendations for home and school.

---

### 1. A brief report for each test

### Consistent memory
The test (analogue of CPT) revealed a good ability to memorize information (images, words). However, reproduction in the correct sequence is difficult. This indicates the need for the development of ordering and planning skills, and not the memory itself.

### Self -control
The test (analogue of Go/No-Go) showed a tendency to impulsive reactions. Actions often lead to understanding the instructions. Difficulties with inhibiting the response are noted, especially when performing simple or fascinating tasks.

### 2. The guide for the success of your student

### Features of perception

#### Pace
A fast pace of task is noted. This can lead to errors due to incomplete perception of instructions.

#### Perception
The best assimilation of information occurs with step -by -step submission and the presence of visual supports (schemes, illustrations, brief lists). Long -term oral explanations are quickly tiring.

#### Focus
Attention is stable on short, clearly formulated tasks. However, it is quickly exhausted when it is required to hold several rules or a complex sequence of actions.

### What supports

#### Clear instructions
Formulate tasks step by step. For example: "First read the task. Then emphasize the verbs. After checking your work."

#### Visual support
Use cards with algorithms, day plans, reminders in the form of stickers.

#### A pause before action
Teach the child to make a micropauza before the answer or action. You can use a signal gesture (for example, a finger to the lips).

#### Predictability and rituals
The consistent structure of the day or lesson reduces anxiety and promotes concentration. Example: "We always start with 5 minutes of reading, then 10 minutes of the letter."

### What makes it difficult

#### Multitasking
Requests, including several actions at the same time (“Listen to me, write down the family and remove from the table”) can lead to poor -quality execution or complete lack of result.

#### Volumetric instructions
The child can only remember the beginning or end of a long oral instruction, losing the main content.

#### A sharp change in tasks
A quick transition to a new task without a short pause can lead to its implementation according to the rules of the previous one.

#### Distracting incentives
Excessive noises and distracting factors overload the self -control system and provoke impulsiveness.

### 3. Recommendations

### For the house

#### Organization
*   Miss tasks into short blocks (10-15 minutes). Use the timer.
*   Between the blocks, take short breaks for physical activity.
*   Create a simple written plan for the evening: "1. Dinner. 2. Mathematics. 3. Reading." Mark the executed points.

#### Support
*   Praise for effort and process, and not just for the result: “Well done, that I was in no hurry and checked!”, “I noticed how you wanted to answer right away, but you listened. Great!”
*   In case of mistakes due to haste, calmly suggest: "You seem to hurry up. Let's remember the rule and try again together."

### For school

#### Instructions
*   Give only one instruction at a time.
*   Duplicate oral tasks with a brief record on the board or individual card.

#### Learning environment
*   Place the student closer to the teacher, away from distracting factors (window, door).
*   Use visual tips: cards with algorithms, posters with rules of conduct.
*   Before starting work, say the plan with the student: "What are we doing first? What then?"

#### Individual approach
*   Classes on the development of regulatory functions are recommended (attention, self -control, planning), individual or group.
*   Perhaps Tutor support to help in organizing educational activities in the lesson.

### 4. Recommended training (in a game form)

### Attention and control

*   **"Frozen!" or "the sea is worried once":** Teaches a quick reaction to a stop signal and body control.
*   **"Edible-Sreedy":** Develops the speed of reaction and the ability to inhibit.
*   **"Graphic dictation":** He trains the ability to listen and accurately follow a step -by -step instruction.

### Memory and order

*   **"Repeat me":** Repetition of sequences of movements, cotton, words or numbers. Start with 2-3 elements, gradually complicating.
*   **"Shop":** Give the child a list of 3-4 purchases and ask to bring them in the indicated order.
*   **Compilation of stories according to the pictures:** Lay out 3-4 plot pictures and ask to make a coherent story, observing the logic of events.

### Coordination and body

*   **Walking along the line:** Develops concentration and control over the body. You can complicate by adding an object to your hands (a glass of water) or on your head (a bag of cereal).
*   **Games with the ball:** Rhythmic picking the ball on the floor or wall under the expense. Promotes synchronization of movements and internal rhythm.
