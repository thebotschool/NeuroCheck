# Cognitive profile. Age 7–9 years.

This report reveals the cognitive features of your student. Recommendations are designed to support effective and comfortable training at home and at school.

---

## 1. Brief test results

*   **Visual memory (CPT):** Effective memorization of initial and final information. Details in the middle can be less clear. This indicates a developing useful strategy.
*   **Self-control (Go/No-Go):** Difficulties in quick switching and braking of actions are manifested. This affects the implementation of complex instructions.
*   **Sustainability of attention (TCP) ::** A high ability to prolonged concentration on one task in the absence of distracting factors and unchanging rules.

## 2. The guide for the success of your student

Your schoolboy is capable of deep concentration and zeal. Its success largely depends on the competent organization of the educational process.

### Features of training

*   **Pace:** Productivity is maximum at a calm, measured pace. Hurrying or pressure can provoke impulsive errors.
*   **Perception:** Optimally assimilates clear, structured and visually executed information. An important, located at the beginning or end of the instruction, is better remembered.
*   **Concentration:** Deeply focuses on one task. Many rules or frequent switching can disperse attention.

### Supporting factors

*   **Step -by -step instructions:** One action by every step.
*   **Visual supports:** Check lists, diagrams, algorithms, key allocation.
*   **Clear rules:** One simple rule for each task.
*   **Predictability:** The sequence of actions reduces the load on self -control.
*   **The emphasis on key:** Important information is more effective at the beginning or end.

### Interviewing factors

*   **Multitasking:** Simultaneous listening, memorization and recording reduce assimilation.
*   **Long -term oral instructions:** Especially with many conditions or prohibitions.
*   **Sharp change in activity:** The transition without a pause causes stress for self -regulation.
*   **Background noise and chaos:** Increase the load on attention, exhausting the resource of self -control.

## 3. Recommendations

### Home environment

#### The structure of training

*   **Separate the tasks:** Divide the big tasks into small steps: read, write off, emphasize, check.
*   **Use the timer:** Work in short intervals (15–20 minutes of work, 5 minutes of rest) to maintain concentration.
*   **Focus on one:** There should be only a current task on the table, without distracting objects.
*   **Discuss the plan:** Before starting work, pronounce the sequence of actions.

#### Motivation and support

*   Encourage **Attentiveness and following the rules**, not speed. "You did a great job according to the instructions!"
*   With mistakes, react calmly: "Let's find together where we retreated from the rule."

### School environment

#### The formulation of tasks

*   Use short, clear verb teams: "Write out. Emphasize. Close."
*   Avoid double instructions and negations. Use direct commands: "Color only apples."

#### Structuring the material

*   Select key rules and samples on the board or in cards (frame, color).
*   For complex topics, use visual algorithms (diagrams, memos).

#### Style style

*   Calm, structured presentation of material with visual examples is most effective.
*   Provide additional time for tasks requiring self -control (for example, a test dictation).

#### Personalization of training

*   It is recommended to use training cards with step-by-step instructions or checklists.
*   Planting at the first desk in the center minimizes distracting factors.

## 4. Recommended training

The proposed games and exercises are easy to integrate into everyday life. They contribute to the development of key skills in a playful way.

### Attention and self -control

*   **"Delay!":** Move to the music. Stop instantly when the music stops.
*   **“No, don't say”:** Classical game for the development of speech control.
*   **Graphic dictations:** They train to keep instructions and concentration (for example, "two cells to the right, one up ...").

### Visual memory

*   **"Memory" or "Find a couple":** Start with a small number of cards (4-6 pairs).
*   **"What has changed?":** Lay out objects, the child remembers. Then one item changes or removes.
*   **Removing lists:** Use short lists (3-4 points), emphasizing the beginning and end.

### Coordination and rhythm

*   **Games with the ball:** Fight the ball, catch, alternate your hands in a certain rhythm.
*   **Walking along the line:** Pass along the drawn line (straight, with your back forward) without stumbling.
*   **Cooling rhythm:** Repeat the set rhythm with claps, gradually complicating.
