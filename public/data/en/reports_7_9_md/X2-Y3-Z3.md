# Cognitive profile: Development report (age 7-9 years)

This report reveals the individual characteristics of the student, its strengths and areas for development. The goal is to optimize the educational process at home and at school.

---

### 1. Review of tests

*   **Visual working memory (CPT):** The desire to memorize information. Difficulties with holding parts in the middle of the sequence. Effective use of strategy: the best memorization of information at the beginning and end.

*   **Braking and control (Go/No-Go):** The preference of the leading hand. The need for additional control for the other hand, which leads to errors. Indicates the difficulties in interhemispheric interaction, especially when performing simultaneous tasks.

*   **Attention (TCP):** The tendency to quick, impulsive response. Frequent errors in carelessness due to premature actions.

### 2. The guide for the success of the student

#### Individual profile

*   **Pace:** Fast, impulsive pace of work. Reducing quality and accuracy due to haste.
*   **Perception format:** Optimal assimilation of visual and structured information. Key information is more efficiently remembered at the beginning or end of the instruction.
*   **Focus of attention:** Instability of attention, especially with monotonous or exact tasks. The need for external support to maintain involvement.

#### Supporting factors

*   **Step -by -step instructions:** Separation of the task into small stages. (For example: "Read. Emphasize. Write down.")
*   **Visual tips:** Plan on the board, checklists, highlighting keywords.
*   **The strategy "Territory":** Important information at the beginning and end of the instruction/lesson.
*   **Rhythm and pauses:** Speaking of the rules, taping the rhythm, short micropauses between tasks.
*   **Singlety:** A clear installation to perform one action at one point.

#### Interviewing factors

*   **Multitasking:** Simultaneous actions reduce efficiency and increase errors.
*   **Long oral instructions:** More than two consistent actions are difficult to keep in memory.
*   **High pace/Competition:** They provoke impulsiveness and increase the number of errors.
*   **Noise and distracting factors:** Easily violate concentration.

### 3. Recommendations

#### Home environment

*   **Organization of work:**
    *   Break the tasks into blocks for 15-20 minutes with short breaks for physical activity.
    *   Use a timer to visualize the time of work and rest.
    *   Start with the most difficult task while the energy level is high.
    *   Make a written plan of the evening: "1. Dinner 2. Mathematics (15 min). 3. Rest (5 min). 4. Reading (15 min)."

*   **Support and encouragement:**
    *   Praise for diligence and accuracy, and not for speed. (For example: "I see you tried very hard and checked yourself. Great work!")
    *   Provide a calm workplace without extra items.
    *   Before starting work, pronounce the tasks jointly, convinced of their understanding.

#### School environment

*   **The wording of tasks:**
    *   The instructions should be short, cherished, step -by -step. Duplicate oral tasks on the board.
    *   Attract the student's attention before the explanation: "Petya, look at me. Now an important task."

*   **Lesson structuring:**
    *   Place the student closer to the teacher, away from distracting factors (door, window).
    *   Apply visual supports: a lesson plan, diagrams, cards with rules.
    *   Support the calm, predictable and friendly style of information. Avoid sharp switching between activities.

*   **Elements of an individual route:**
    *   Provide extra time to perform written work and tests.
    *   Allow the use of a line for reading or a card to close the lattered examples.
    *   For difficult tasks, offer a checklist of self-testing: "Is the condition read? ✔️ The question is emphasized? ✔️ The answer is recorded? ✔️"

### 4. Developing practices

These exercises and games contribute to the development of key skills in interactive form.

*   **Attention:**
    *   **Graphic dictations:** Training of auditory attention and self -control.
    *   **Search for differences:** The development of concentration and attentiveness to details.
    *   **Board game "Cortex":** Training of a quick and accurate reaction.

*   **Coordination and interhemispheric interaction:**
    *   **Neurohymnastics (3-5 minutes daily):** Exercises "Fist-Robric Ladon", "Use-Shot" (with one hand by the nose, with the other by the opposite ear with the subsequent shift).
    *   **Drawing with two hands:** Simultaneous creation of symmetrical figures (circles, Christmas trees) with both hands.
    *   **Games with the ball:** Throwing the ball out of the hand to the hand, including movements through the middle line of the body.

*   **Memory (based on strengths):**
    *   **The game "Memory" (find a couple):** Great training of visual memory.
    *   **"Remember and repeat":** Removing and reproducing the sequence of objects with a gradual increase in complexity.

*   **Body control and braking:**
    *   **"You go quieter - you will continue":** Classical game for self -control.
    *   **"Stop-signal":** Furious by signal, the development of bodily control.
    *   **"Forbidden movement":** Repetition of all actions, except for one forbidden, for the development of braking.
