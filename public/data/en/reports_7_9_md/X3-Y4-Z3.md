### Cognitive assessment report 7-9 years

This report is developed for parents and teachers. It offers an understanding of the cognitive characteristics of your child and recommendations to create an optimal development environment.

### 1. A brief report for each test

*   **Working memory (CPT):** Information is absorbed well. Difficulties arise with the retention of data in a given sequence. This indicates the potential in memory and the need for the development of organization skills.
*   **Impuls control (Go/No-Go):** Difficulties with stopping the action and making a decision in speed are noted. This indicates the need for the development of self -control.
*   **Attention (TCP):** The tendency to impulsive reactions is confirmed. Actions are often ahead of the complete understanding of the task.

<!-- page-break -->

### 2. The guide for the success of your student

#### Individual profile

*   **Pace:** The natural speed of action is fast. Effective training requires assistance in mastering a slower, conscious pace.
*   **Perception:** Better assimilates the information **Visually and step by step**. Long oral instructions can lead to a loss of a sequence.
*   **Focus:** Attention is unstable, easily distracted. External supports and a clear structure to maintain concentration are required.

#### Supporting environment

*   **Step -by -step instructions:** Miss tasks into small, specific steps (for example, "collect toys in the box" instead of "take in the room").
*   **Visual supports:** Use cards, schemes, checklists with the order of action.
*   **Predictable rhythm:** A clear daily routine and repeated rituals create comfort.
*   **Short breaks:** 1-2 minutes of physical activity help restore self-control.

#### Factors that make the process difficult

*   **Multitasking:** The simultaneous performance of several tasks often leads to errors.
*   **Long oral instructions:** Sequences of several actions are difficult to hold (for example, "go, take, bring").
*   **Sharp change in activity:** An unexpected transition between classes makes it difficult to adapt.
*   **Distracting factors:** Noise and chaos quickly deplete attention and self -control.

<!-- page-break -->

### 3. Recommendations

#### Home environment

*   **Organization of classes:**
    *   Divide homework into blocks for 10-15 minutes with breaks. Use the timer.
    *   Make a joint work plan: "First mathematics (10 min), then rest (5 min), then dictation (15 min)."
    *   Provide a quiet, organized workplace without distracting items.
*   **Support and encouragement:**
    *   Praise for **effort and self -control**, not speed. For example: "You tried not to rush well!" Or "You could stop and think, well done!"
    *   Calmly disassemble impulsive mistakes, without criticism: "You hurried. Let's try more slowly."
*   **Concentration:** Realistic time to keep attention on a difficult task is 10-15 minutes. Do not demand more.

#### School environment

*   **The clearness of instructions:**
    *   The instructions should be short, clear and consistent. Perfect: **One sentence is one action**.
    *   Ask the child to repeat the instructions to confirm understanding.
*   **The structure of the material:**
    *   Use visual means: cards, algorithms, highlighting keywords with color.
    *   Place important information in one place (for example, page number in the upper left corner of the board).
*   **The style of interaction and IOOM:**
    *   Communicate in a calm voice. Use rituals (for example, "the lesson begins, hands on the desk, eyes at me").
    *   **Individual educational route (IOM):** It may take a tutor to develop planning. Consider the possibility of working in a quiet place (first desk, small group) and the provision of additional time for testing.

### 4. Developing activity

These activity is aimed at developing key skills. It is recommended to engage in regular sessions (5-10 minutes a day).

#### For attention and self -control

*   **"You go quieter - you will continue":** A game of the ability to stop on time.
*   **"The sea is worried once":** Training of bodily control through fading by signal.
*   **"Yes, and there is no talk":** Speech control exercise.

#### For memory and sequence

*   **"Repeat me":** Reproduction of the sequence of movements or sounds.
*   **Graphic dictations:** Fulfillment of step -by -step instructions ("Two cells to the right, one up").
*   **Pictures Stories:** Creating a coherent story, building pictures in a logical sequence.

#### For coordination and bodily control

*   **Games with the ball:** Catching and throwing the ball by signal (for example, only for the word "catch").
*   **Balance exercises:** Walking along the line, a stand on one leg, the use of a balancing board.
