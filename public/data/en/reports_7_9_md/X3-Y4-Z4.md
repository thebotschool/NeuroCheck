## Cognitive Profile Assessment: holding the sequence, self -control, visual memory (10–13 years)

This report is designed to provide parents and teachers with a deep understanding of the cognitive characteristics of the student. It serves as a guide to create an optimal environment of training and development.

---

## 1. Review of tests

*   **Holding the sequence:** Effective memorization of details, but difficulties with their logical ordering. This indicates the potential in planning and organization.
*   **Self -control:** The tendency to impulsive actions, especially with a quick change in instructions. The development of the "braking" mechanism is required to increase efficiency.
*   **Visual memory:** The retention of several rules or objects causes significant efforts and leads to rapid fatigue. Focus on the development of stability of attention.

## 2. Profile of the student

### Peculiarities

*   **The pace of work:** Uneven. Thoughtfulness at high concentration, but a tendency to impulsiveness with fatigue or pressure.
*   **Information perception:** The most effective through visual and step -by -step formats (schemes, cards, lists, images). Long text or oral instructions are more complicated.
*   **Focus of attention:** Unstable. Productive concentration on one task is 10-15 minutes. Then a short pause or a change of activity to prevent errors is required. Distribution of external stimuli.

### Effective approaches

*   **Step -by -step instructions:** One task is one step.
*   **Visual tips:** Check lists, graphic timers, cards with algorithms, highlighting keywords.
*   **Clear rhythm:** Predictability reduces anxiety, releasing resources for training.
*   **Short pauses:** Permission for a break (get up, stretch) every 15 minutes.
*   **The principle of "one task":** One subject or task in the focus of attention.

### Factors that reduce efficiency

*   **Multitasking:** Attempts to simultaneously perform several actions reduce the result.
*   **Long oral instructions:** More than two steps in one instructions make memorization.
*   **Sudden change of activity:** Requires significant effort to switch.
*   **Noise and visual mess:** Expand the resource of attention.
*   **Time pressure:** Enhances impulsiveness and increases the number of errors.

## 3. Recommendations

### For the house

*   **Organization of homework:**
    *   Separate tasks into 15-minute blocks with 5-minute breaks. Use a visual timer.
    *   Start with the most difficult tasks.
    *   Together, make an evening plan (for example: mathematics 15 minutes, break 5 minutes, Russian language 15 minutes, dinner).
*   **Promotion and support:**
    *   Praise efforts and process, and not just the result. Examples: "Great concentration!", "You followed the plan!"
    *   Give the instructions one step. After performing the first step, go the following.
*   **Working space:**
    *   The workplace should contain only the necessary objects for the current task. Eliminate distracting factors.

### For school

*   **The wording of tasks:**
    *   Tasks should be short, unambiguous, step -by -step (for example: "Read the paragraph. Find the main idea. Write it down").
    *   Provide written instructions (on a board, card) even with an oral explanation.
*   **Lesson structure and place:**
    *   Recommended place: the first/second desk of the central row to minimize abstracts.
    *   Use visual supports: a lesson plan, algorithms on the board.
*   **Style style:**
    *   Calm, measured tone. Install visual contact or light touch to attract attention before an important instruction.
    *   Give the time to comprehend, do not require an instant answer.
*   **Individual route:**
    *   The support of a tutor or school psychologist is recommended. Focus - on development **regulatory skills**: planning, self -control, reflection.
    *   It is possible to provide additional time for testing or using a draft plan.

## 4. Recommended training

Game exercises that support the development of cognitive functions. The key to success is regularity and a positive approach.

### Attention and self -control

*   **Graphic dictations:** Drawing on cells under dictation. Develops auditory attention and following instructions.
*   **Games for "freezing":** For example, "Zamry-Otomory". They train the ability to rapidly inhibition of impulsive reactions.

### Memory and sequence

*   **"Repeat me":** Repetition of sequences (clap, movement, words). Start with 3-4 elements, gradually complicate.
*   **The game "Memory":** Develops visual memory and concentration.
*   **Stories from the pictures:** Creating stories based on a series of images. Teaches logical and temporary sequence.

### Coordination and bodily control

*   **Games with the ball:** Catching and throwing the ball, hitting a target. Improves the connection of the "eye-bar" and sensorimotor control.
*   **Walking along the line:** Increases balance and control over the body.

### Rhythm development

*   **Tapping rhythms:** Repetition of a given rhythm. Promotes the organization and ordering of activities.
