# Cognitive assessment report
### CPT+Go/No-GO+TCP (7–9 years old)

This report illuminates the individual cognitive features of your student. It is designed to help parents and teachers identify strengths and areas for development, creating optimal conditions for learning and growth.

# 1. Cognitive profile
### Review of test results.

**Working memory (CPT)**
The child effectively assimilates information, but may experience difficulties with reproducing data in an exact sequence. Support for organizing and structuring the memorized material is important.

**Self-control (Go/No-Go)**
High level of self -control and regulation of actions. The student demonstrates the ability to quickly reaction and timely stop, which is a key element of successful training.

**Sustainability of attention (TCP)**
Sustainable and focused attention is a strong side. The child is able to concentrate on the task for a long time without distracting and maintaining productivity.

# 2. The guide for the success of your student

### Individual characteristics
*   **Pace.** Stable, moderately fast pace of work. Effective with clearly certain tasks without a complex sequence of actions. Self -control contributes to thoughtful implementation.
*   **Perception.** Prefers visual and step -by -step information. Visual materials (schemes, illustrations, lists) are more effective than voluminous oral explanations.
*   **Focus of attention.** Highly resistant attention. The schoolboy is able to immerse himself deeply into the task, minimizing distraction.

### Optimal conditions
*   **Step -by -step instructions.** Separation of complex tasks into simple, consistent stages.
*   **Visual supports.** Check lists, numbered lists, graphic schemes to support the completion of tasks.
*   **Rhythm and structure.** The predictable course of the lesson or homework. Propuring stages helps to organize internally.
*   **The "anchor" of the sequence.** Stimulation of the beginning from the first or last element when working with consistent tasks.

### What can make difficult
*   **Multitasking.** The parallel performance of several actions (listen, record, track) can overload the planning system and lead to errors.
*   **Long oral instructions.** Extensive verbal instructions containing many steps can cause confusion in the order of action.
*   **Fast change of activity.** Unpredictable and frequent switching between tasks interfere with the internal organization.

<!-- page-break -->

# 3. Recommendations

### For the house
*   **Homework.** Divide tasks into productive 15-20-minute blocks. Use a simple visual plan (list, board) with the ability to mark the executed points.
*   **Support and promotion.** Mark not only the result, but also compliance with the plan. In case of violation of the sequence, gently direct back to the intended steps.

### For school
*   **The formulation of tasks.** Instructions should be given step by step, one action at a time. Additionally duplicate the full sequence on the board in the form of a numbered list.
*   **The structure of the lesson.** Provide the student with visual access to the board. Use algorithmic cards for standard tasks to reduce the cognitive load for memory and enhance the focus on execution.
*   **Individualization.** Although special conditions are not binding, a personalized approach, including visual supports, contributes to the development of regulatory universal educational actions (UUD) and the maximum disclosure of potential.

# 4. Recommended training
These activity contribute to the development of cognitive skills in an interactive form.

### For memory and sequence
*   **"Repeat me."** Reproduction of the sequence of objects, sounds or movements with gradual complication.
*   **Graphic dictations.** The development of auditory perception and transfer of consecutive instructions into action.
*   **Assembly according to the instructions.** Classes with designers (for example, LEGO) or puzzles, requiring strict following plan.

### For attention and coordination
*   **Games with the ball.** Exercises that require rhythm, coordination and parallel performance of intellectual tasks.
*   **“Find a couple” (memori).** A game training and work memory.

### For rhythm and bodily control
*   **Rhythmic dictation.** The exact repetition of the proposed rhythm.
*   **Dance games.** Repetition of dance movements according to the model, synchronizing auditory, visual and motor information.
