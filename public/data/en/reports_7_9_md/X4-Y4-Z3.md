### **Cognitive development report**

This report provides key data on the cognitive functions of the student. Its goal is to help you create the most effective and supportive environment for training and growth.

---

### **1. Key indicators**

*   **Preservation and memory (CPT):** Difficulties with consistent memorization and retention of information. Fatual fatigue is noted when performing such tasks.
*   **Self-control (Go/No-Go):** Difficulties with the regulation of impulsive reactions have been identified. A tendency to hasty actions in situations requiring a deliberate choice.
*   **Attention and reaction speed (TCP):** The trend towards impulsiveness is confirmed. He begins to carry out tasks without fully acquaintance with the instructions, which leads to errors.

<!-- page-break -->

### **2. The guide for the success of your student**

#### **Individual characteristics**

*   **Pace:** Fast but impulsive. Inclined to act until complete understanding.
*   **Perception:** It better assimilates short, clear, visual information. Long oral instructions are ineffective.
*   **Focus of attention:** Unstable, easily distracted. Effective concentration lasts **10-15 minutes**, then a break is required.

#### **Supporting factors**

*   **Visual supports:** Cards, schemes, stickers.
*   **Step -by -step instructions:** Separation of tasks into small, specific steps.
*   **Clear rhythm:** Predictability in schedule and rituals.
*   **Physical activity:** Short, energetic breaks for "rebooting".
*   **Tactile contact:** Calm attracting attention, approval.

#### **Factors interfering with progress**

*   **Multitasking:** Simultaneous requests or instructions.
*   **Verbal overload:** Long oral explanations.
*   **Rush:** Strengthening impulsiveness under pressure.
*   **Background noise:** Any distracting sounds.

<!-- page-break -->

### **3. Recommendations**

#### **For the house**

*   **Organization of work:**
    *   Savior tasks into 15-minute blocks. Use the timer.
    *   Turn on 5-minute active breaks between blocks.
    *   Create checklists for complex tasks.
    *   Start with the most complex objects when the energy level is high.
*   **Support and encouragement:**
    *   Mark specific efforts: "You focused well for 10 minutes!"
    *   Avoid criticism for slowness or inattention. Offer to double -check.
*   **Wednesday:**
    *   Minimize distracting factors at the workplace.
    *   Exclude background noise during class.

#### **For school**

*   **The wording of tasks:**
    *   Provide the instructions briefly, clearly, step by step.
    *   Example: instead of "solving the problem ...", use:
        1.  "Read the condition. Emphasize the main thing."
        2.  "Write down a short condition."
        3.  "Start with the first action."
*   **Lesson structure:**
    *   Visualize the lesson plan on the board.
    *   Establish eye contact before the instructions.
    *   Use the cards-strokes with the rules on the desk.
*   **Accommodation and feed:**
    *   Optimal place: the first desk in the center, far from distracting factors.
    *   Pack: calm, friendly, without haste.
*   **Individual route:**
    *   Adapt the training materials: reduce volume, increase time for tests.
    *   Consider the maintenance of the tutor to help the organization of activities.

### **4. Practical classes**

These exercises in a playful form develop the necessary cognitive functions. It is recommended to practice 10-15 minutes daily.

*   **For attention:**
    *   **Correct samples:** Crossing out a given letter on the text page.
    *   **"Find the differences":** Games with two similar pictures.
    *   **Board games-"Brodylings":** Demand the rules and calculation.

*   **For memory:**
    *   **"Memory":** Classical game for the search for paired cards.
    *   **"What has changed?":** Removing the location of objects, then search for changes.
    *   **"Snowball":** Adding a new element to a repeating phrase.

*   **For self -control and braking:**
    *   **"You go quieter - you will continue":** Furious by signal.
    *   **"Edible-Sreedy":** A game with a ball requiring quick differentiation.
    *   **Graphic dictations:** Drawing on step -by -step instructions.

*   **For coordination and bodily control:**
    *   **Walking along the line:** Movement on the drawn line.
    *   **Games with the ball:** Throws, fishing, beating.
    *   **Balance exercises:** Standing on one leg, "Swallow".
