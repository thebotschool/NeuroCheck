# Cognitive assessment: attention, self -control, working memory
## A report on the development of a student (10–13 years)

This report is developed for parents and teachers. He offers a clear understanding of the unique abilities of the student and determines the directions for development. Our recommendations will help create a supporting environment that contributes to success in training and comprehensive growth.

### 1. Overview of key competencies

#### Visual Working Memory (CPT)
The schoolboy shows the strengths in memorizing the beginning and end of the information. The retention of all parts in the middle of the row can be more difficult.

#### Self-control (Go/No-Go)
There is a tendency to impulsive reactions, which affects the ability to immediate inhibition and follow the rules.

#### Distribution of attention (TCP)
Tasks that require the simultaneous retention of several information flows (listen, watch, act) can cause overload.

### 2. The guide for the success of your student

#### Individual characteristics

*   **Pace.** Prone to impulsiveness, which can lead to hasty actions and errors in carelessness.
*   **Perception of information.** It better assimilates the information presented at the beginning and end (for example, in lists or algorithms). The middle part can “fall out” of the focus.
*   **Focus of attention.** Attention can be unstable, especially if it is necessary to simultaneously perform several actions. Distracted by external incentives.

#### Effective strategies

*   **Step -by -step instructions.** Provide short, clear instructions (2-3 steps at a time).
*   **Visual supports.** Use schemes, algorithms, checklists, color release. Visualization reduces the load for memory.
*   **Accent on key points.** Place the most important information at the beginning or end of instructions and lists.
*   **Rhythm and predictability.** A clear daily routine and lessons creates a comfortable and predictable environment.
*   **Short breaks.** Enter pauses (1-3 minutes) between tasks to restore concentration.

#### Potential challenges

*   **Multitasking.** The simultaneous performance of several actions (for example, listening and writing) is a significant difficulty.
*   **Long oral instructions.** Oral instructions consisting of more than two sentences can be lost.
*   **A sharp change in activity.** Fast transitions between tasks without warning can lead to a loss of concentration.
*   **Distracting environment.** Noise and disorder reduce the resource of attention.
*   **Urgent decision -making.** The need for quick choice can cause difficulties or impulsive answers.

### 3. Practical steps

#### For the house

##### Homework
*   Miss tasks into short blocks (15–20 minutes). This corresponds to the optimal period of concentration.
*   Enter short physical warm -ups (5 minutes) between the blocks.
*   Provide the instructions for one task at a time. Make sure the instruction is understood by asking the student to repeat it in your own words.

##### Support and promotion
*   Focus praise on efforts and process, and not just on the result: "You tried very hard!" Or "I see how you followed the plan."
*   Create a joint visual plan for a day or evening (for example, on a board) to structured tasks.

##### Working space
*   Provide a quiet place for classes without unnecessary distracting items. Remove gadgets and toys from the field of view.

#### For school

##### Instructions
*   Use the most simple and unambiguous formulations of tasks.
*   Divide complex tasks into clear stages, writing them in the form of numbered lists on the board.

##### Organization of the lesson
*   Duplicate oral instructions with visual: on the board, cards or screen.
*   Use the memory feature: repeat key rules at the beginning and at the end of the lesson.

##### Position and communication
*   It is recommended to place a schoolboy at the first desk in the center in order to minimize distracting factors and provide direct contact with the teacher.
*   Establish eye contact or light touch before giving a personal task to make sure of attention.

##### Individual route
*   Consider the support of a specialist (neuropsychologist, tutor) for the development of self -regulation and organization of the educational process.
*   It is recommended to provide additional time for testing and reduce the volume of the same type of tasks.

### 4. Development of potential

The presented games and exercises are aimed at developing key cognitive functions. The regularity (10-15 minutes daily), and not the duration, is of key importance.

#### For attention and working memory

*   **"Memory" / "Find a couple".** Classical game for training visual memory.
*   **Schultte tables.** Contribute to the expansion of the field of view and improve concentration.
*   **"I put in a bag ..."** Oral game developing consistent memory.

#### For self -control and braking

*   **Games for a stop.** For example, "dead-oomory" or "sea is worried once", requiring an instant reaction to a signal.
*   **"Edible-universal."** A game with the ball, training the reaction rate and the ability to selective action.
*   **Graphic dictations.** Develop accurate adherence to instructions and control over motor skills.

#### For coordination and balance

*   **Balance exercises.** Standing on one leg, walking along the line.
*   **Games with the ball.** The alternation of the hands when repulsing the ball from the floor.
*   **Rhythmic exercises.** Repetition of simple rhythmic patterns.
