# Cognitive profile: 7-9 years

This report is designed to reveal the strengths and development of your child. It serves as a practical guide to optimize training and everyday life.

## 1. Test results
*   **Sustainability of attention (CPT):** Fast fatigue and loss of concentration were noted, especially after intense incentives.
*   **Self-control (Go/No-Go):** The tendency to impulsive actions has been revealed. The reaction is ahead of the understanding of the instructions.
*   **Visual working memory:** Difficulties with memorizing several elements and preserving their sequence.

## 2. Profile of learning

Understanding individual characteristics will help to form an optimal approach to learning.

### Individual characteristics

*   **Pace:** Fast, gusty. The tendency to proceed to the task until the instructions are completely comprehended.
*   **Perception:** More effective through short, visual instructions. Long oral explanations reduce concentration.
*   **Focus of attention:** Unstable. The concentration on monotonous tasks is limited to 10-15 minutes, requires switching.

### Optimization of the process

*   **Visual tips:** Check lists, diagrams, algorithms (for example, “read, emphasize, write down”), color selection.
*   **Step -by -step instructions:** Decomposition of complex tasks on 3-4 numbered steps.
*   **Predictability:** Clear rules and rituals, for example, “Stop. Think. Make "before the start.
*   **Short pauses:** The possibility of physical activity (1-2 minutes) between tasks.

### Factors that reduce efficiency

*   **Multitasking:** Simultaneous task or sharp switching between them without a pause.
*   **Time pressure:** The indications of the rush ("Come on faster") enhance impulsiveness and error.
*   **Distracting factors:** Excess noise, visual disorder at the workplace.
*   **Long oral explanations:** Holding attention is reduced after several sentences.

## 3. Recommendations

### For the home environment

#### Homework

*   **Sessions:** Work with blocks of 15 minutes, with 3-5 minute breaks. Use a time to visualize time.
*   **Priority:** Start with the most difficult tasks while the energy level is high.
*   **Decomposition:** Separate tasks into small parts. For example: "Decide the first two examples, then check."

#### Support and motivation

*   **Promotion:** Mark the process and effort, not just the result. Recognize the moments of self -control: “Excellent, you thought before answering!”
*   **Correction:** Avoid criticism of impulsive errors. Return to the rule calmly: “It looks like you hurried. Let's read the task again. ”

#### Organization of space

*   **Workplace:** Minimize distracting items on the table (only the necessary textbooks, notebooks, pens).

### For the school environment

#### Instructions

*   **Step -by -step feed:** Give the instructions one at a time, with pauses. For example: “Open notebooks. Write down the number. Open page 5. "
*   **Verbs of action:** Use clear verbs: “Emphasize”, “Cover”, “Count”.

#### The structure of the lesson

*   **Plan:** Visualize a short lesson or assignment plan (for example, on the board, or subscription cards).
*   **Place in the class:** Place the child in the center, at 1-2 desks, away from distracting factors (windows, doors).

#### Material feed

*   **Visual support:** Accompany oral instructions with visual materials (diagrams, recordings on the board).
*   **Individual approach:** Give additional time on control or allow the use of algorithmic cards.

## 4. Developing exercises

These simple exercises and games integrated into everyday life contribute to the development of key skills.

### Attention

*   **Graphic dictations:** "One cage to the right, two up ...".
*   **Correct samples:** Refusing certain letters in the text.
*   **"Find 10 differences":** Classic tasks for comparing images.

### Memory

*   **"Memory":** Search for paired pictures.
*   **"What has changed?":** Removing the location of objects with the subsequent definition of changes.
*   **Learning:** Short poems or tongue twisters, starting with the duplicate.

### Self -control and coordination

*   **"You go quieter - you will continue":** Game for training stop by signal.
*   **"FROM ATOMISE":** Dancing or running with a sudden stop to the music.
*   **Games with the ball:** Bowing the ball in a given rhythm or throwing with a naming of words on the topic.
*   **Rhythmic pops:** Repetition of a given rhythm.
