# Cognitive profile report: 7–9 years old

This report presents key data on the cognitive features of your student. The goal is to provide effective strategies to support home and at school.

## 1. Evaluation results

*   **Visual working memory:** High level. The ability to keep information even during distraction is a strong side in training.
*   **Self -control and coordination:** It easily copes with the leading hand, but the coordination of both hands requires development. This affects tasks that require simultaneous listening and recording.
*   **Sustainability of attention:** Low. Long -term concentration on monotonous tasks causes fatigue and distraction.

<!-- page-break -->

## 2. Training profile

### Key features

*   **The pace of work:** A confident beginning, a decrease in the pace with a long monotonous load. Effective in short tasks.
*   **Information perception:** Optimal - through **Visual channel**. Visual materials (diagrams, pictures, selected text) contribute to understanding. The auditory perception of long explanations is difficult.
*   **Focus of attention:** Unstable. It is easily distracted by external stimuli, especially with the loss of novelty or the need for perseverance.

### Supporting factors

*   **Visual supports:** Check lists, diagrams, cards with rules, text highlighting.
*   **Step -by -step instructions:** One task, a clear and brief formulation.
*   **Rhythm and movement:** Pronouncing, taping rhythm, short physical pauses to change activity and coordination.
*   **Predictability:** A pre -well -known class or homework plan.

### Interviewing factors

*   **Multitasking:** Simultaneous actions (listen and write, read and rewrite) cause difficulties.
*   **Long oral instructions:** The risk of loss of meaning.
*   **Monotone:** The same type, long tasks lead to fatigue and reduction of concentration.
*   **Unstructured environment:** Distracting objects, noise, sharp change of tasks.

## 3. Recommendations

### Home environment

*   **Homework performance:**
    *   Divide the tasks into blocks for 15–20 minutes. Between the blocks-a 5-minute break (water, movement).
    *   Use a timer for visual time control.
    *   Assignments and record (dictation, presentation) perform in stages: to say, make sure of understanding, then write down.
*   **Support and motivation:**
    *   Create a "clean zone" on the desktop: only the necessary items.
    *   Mark **effort**, not just the result: “You tried 15 minutes without abstracts!”, “He noticed and corrected the mistake - excellent!”
*   **Optimization of the daily routine:**
    *   After school - a mandatory rest (1–1.5 hours) without gadgets; A walk or a calm game is preferred.
    *   The maximum concentration on the educational task is 15–20 minutes. Do not require a greater duration.

### School environment

*   **The wording of tasks:**
    *   Give the instructions **Step -by -step and clearly**. Example: “Open p. 25. Find the task 3. Read the condition. What is known? What to find? ”
    *   Duplicate oral instructions visually (plan, board number).
*   **Lesson structure:**
    *   Alternate activities every 15-20 minutes: auditory, written, practical, oral.
    *   For coordination tasks (writing, drawing), use rhythmic exercises: tapping, finger gymnastics.
*   **Placing and feeding of the material:**
    *   The optimal place is 1-2 desks, the center, far from the windows/doors to minimize distracts.
    *   Use visibility: schemes, intelligence cards, video. The visual memory compensates for the instability of attention.
*   **Individual educational route:**
    *   Consultation of a school psychologist/neuropsychologist is recommended for a program for the development of interhemispheric interaction and attention.
    *   It is temporarily possible to reduce the amount of written work. Replace them with oral answers or test tasks with the choice of answer.

## 4. Developing training

The proposed games and exercises strengthen attention and coordination. It is recommended to engage in 10-15 minutes daily.

### Attention and memory

*   **Graphic dictations:** Drawing on cells for dictation (for example, “one cage to the right, two up”).
*   **Find the differences:** Training of concentration and visual scanning.
*   **Remember and draw:** Show a simple figure for 10 seconds, then ask to draw it from memory.

### Coordination and bodily control

*   **Cross -end movements (neurohimnastics):** Simultaneous touching the left knee with the right hand, then with the left hand - the right knee. Perform at a different pace.
*   **Constitutional?** Clinkles according to the schemes, including crossroads.
*   **Games with the ball:** Throwing, fighting off the wall with an additional task (for example, naming a word).

### Rhythm development

*   **Echo:** The tapping of the rhythm (palms, pencil) followed by repetition of the child. Gradual complication.
*   **Synchronous drawing:** Drawing symmetrical patterns (circles, waves) with both hands at the same time.
