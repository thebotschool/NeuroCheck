# Cognitive assessment report: memory, inhibition, attention

This report is an analysis of the cognitive functions of your student, based on testing results. It is designed to highlight the strengths and designate areas for development, offering practical recommendations to support training and everyday life.

<!-- page-break -->

## 1. Key results

### Assessment of cognitive functions

*   **Visual working memory:** The ability to effectively memorize and maintain visual information (images, diagrams, sequences). This is a strong side that forms a reliable support in the learning process.
*   **Impulse control (Go/No-Go):** The test revealed difficulties with timely inhibition and avoiding hasty actions.
*   **Attention and reaction speed:** The results confirm the tendency to haste. The student seeks to react quickly, sometimes ahead of the complete understanding of the instructions.

### Resume

Excellent visual memory is combined with a certain tendency to impulsive actions. The main goal is to use strong visual abilities to develop self -control and improve thinking before action.

<!-- page-break -->

## 2. Individual learning profile

### Peculiarities

*   **Pace:** The natural pace of work is fast, gusty. There is a tendency to start completing the assignment without fully familiarizing themselves with the instructions.
*   **Perception:** Information is best absorbed through **Visual channels**: Pictures, schemes, color highlighted text, short videos.
*   **Focus of attention:** Attention is stable with understandable and visually structured tasks. Impulsiveness can lead to errors when the action is ahead of the thought.

### Support

*   **Visual supports:** Check lists, visual schemes ("Make once, make two"), highlighting keywords, examples.
*   **Step -by -step instructions:** Separation of large tasks into small, clear stages. One instruction is one action.
*   **Rule "Stop. Think. Make":** Introduction of a short pause before starting the task. Perhaps pronouncing aloud.
*   **Rhythm:** Rhythmic exercises, pronouncing or taping steps that contribute to the "grounding" and a slowdown in reactions.

### Obstacles

*   **Multitasking:** Situations that require simultaneous listening, observation and records can overload the self -control system.
*   **Quick pace and oral instructions:** Long explanations without visual support are perceived with difficulty.
*   **Hurry and pressure of time:** Phrases of the type “faster”, “left time left” provoke impulsiveness and increase the number of errors.
*   **Noise and distracting factors:** Extraneous activity easily stimulates impulsive reactions.

<!-- page-break -->

## 3. Recommendations

### Home environment

*   **Performing tasks:**
    *   Separate large tasks into 2-3 stages with short breaks (for example, 15 minutes of work, 5 minutes of rest).
    *   Together pronounce the work plan before the start: "First, then then." Write down the plan on the sticker.
    *   Use a visual timer to track time.
    *   The optimal time to keep attention on one difficult task is 15–20 minutes.
*   **Support:**
    *   Praise for accuracy and following the instructions: "Excellent, you did everything exactly according to plan!"
    *   With errors due to haste: calmly offer to return back and double-check the rule.

### School environment

*   **The wording of tasks:**
    *   The instructions should be clumsy, short, step -by -step. It is advisable to duplicate oral instructions on the board or card.
    *   Select the key points of the task with color or emphasizing (for example, "*attentively* Write down, *Emphasize* vowels ").
*   **Lesson structure and workplace:**
    *   The optimal place is the first or second desk in the center of the class, away from windows and doors to minimize distracting factors.
    *   Before the test, it is useful to give a minute to pronounce or mental planning of actions.
*   **Material feed style:**
    *   Actively use schemes, tables, mental cards and other visual agents. The strong visual memory of the student contributes to the assimilation of the material.
    *   **Individual educational route:** It is recommended to provide tasks in the form of a checklist. Allow me to use a line for tracking a line when reading or writing for a better concentration.

## 4. Developing exercises

These games and exercises will help to develop self -control and attention in interactive form.

### For attention and self -control

*   **"Edible-universal":** A classic game that trains the reaction and restraint.
*   **"Yes, and there is no talk ...":** Exercise to keep several rules in memory.
*   **"Stop game":** The movement, which instantly stops on the command "Stop!".

### For memory

*   **"Memory" or "Find a couple":** Effective training of visual memory and concentration.
*   **"What has changed?":** Lay out 5-7 items. The schoolboy remembers them. After removing or moving one item, it must determine the change.

### For coordination, rhythm and bodily control

*   **Graphic dictations:** Develop coordination "Hearing-Enjoy-Station" and teach to follow the instructions.
*   **RIGHT RIGHTS:** Repetition of rhythmic patterns (clap, tapping). Gradual complication.
*   **Balance exercises:** Walking along the line, stand on one leg. Contribute to a better sensation and body control.
