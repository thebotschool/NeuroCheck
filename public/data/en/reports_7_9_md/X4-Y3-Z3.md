### Cognitive capacity report
**Attention. Control. Memory.** (7–9 years)

This report provides key conclusions on cognitive assessment. It is designed to identify the strengths of your child and designate directions for growth. Recommendations are designed to support its development and increase comfort in training and everyday life.

---

## 1. Overview of the results

### Test for stability of attention and memory (CPT)
Difficulties with prolonged attention and consistent memorization are noted. Fatigue increases when performing volumetric tasks.

### Self-control and coordination test (Go/No-Go)
There is insufficient synchronization of the work of the cerebral hemispheres. This can be manifested in difficulties while performing tasks requiring auditory perception and writing, or coordination of both hands.

### Impulsive test (TCP)
The tendency to the rapid start of the implementation of tasks was revealed without preliminary fully comprehending the instructions.

## 2. Approach to learning

### Student profile
This guide will help optimize the learning process of your child.

*   **Pace:** Quick start with the subsequent decrease in energy. It is difficult to maintain a uniform pace at a long distance.
*   **Perception:** The preference for visual, clear and step -by -step information. The simultaneous processing of information from different channels is difficult (for example, hearing and a letter).
*   **Attention:** Short -term and unstable. It is easily distracted, inclined to switch, especially with boring or voluminous tasks.

### Effective strategies

*   **Visual supports:** Using cards-instructions, diagrams, algorithms, color coding.
*   **Skipping tasks:** The division of large tasks into 2-3 small, controlled steps.
*   **Structure and rhythm:** A clear schedule of activity. Examples: rhythmic exercises before writing, short warm -ups, the use of timers.
*   **Singlety:** Focus on one business. Elimination of distracting items from the workplace.

### Factors interfering with training

*   **Multitasking:** The requirement of a simultaneous hearing, writing or working with several sources of information.
*   **Long oral instructions:** Risk of loss of the middle of the message.
*   **High pace and rush:** A quick change in activities without a pause leads to errors and fatigue.
*   **Noise and disorder:** Distracting factors reduce concentration.

## 3. Recommendations

### Home environment

#### Homework
*   Use short work sessions: **15–20 minutes**, with mandatory breaks to physical activity (jumping, stretching, drinking water).
*   Create a visual plan: for example, `1. Математика (2 примера), 2. Перерыв (10 прыжков), 3. Чтение (1 страница)`. Mark the executed points.
*   Start with the most difficult tasks while the concentration level is maximum.

#### Support and promotion
*   Focus on efforts and efforts, and not just on the result: "Excellent, you could concentrate for 15 minutes!" Or "I see how you tried to write carefully."
*   Help deceive: "Let's first read the task first, and then proceed to execution."

#### Organization of space
*   Before starting work, remove all unnecessary items from the table. The order on the table contributes to the clarity of thought.

### School environment

#### The formulation of tasks
*   Let's short, clear and single -step instructions. Apply the principle of "look-make."
*   Duplicate oral instructions visually: brief records on the board, tips on the desk.

#### The structure of the lesson
*   Recommended place: Closer to the teacher and the board, away from windows and doors to minimize abstracts.
*   Use individual checks or cards with a lesson/task plan.

#### Material feed
*   Integrate the elements of motion: physical fitting, hand exercises that require bilateral coordination (for example, drawing symmetrical figures in the air).
*   Provide extra time and support for tasks that require simultaneous listening and recording.

#### Individual educational route
*   Consider the possibility of providing additional time for testing.
*   Officially allow the use of visual supports (multiplication tables, words analysis schemes).
*   Miss volumetric tasks into parts for phased change.

## 4. Recommended training

These exercises and games are aimed at developing key skills. The key to success is regularity and positive mood.

### For attention
*   **Graphic dictations:** Exercises "One cage to the right, two up ..." develop auditory attention and following instructions.
*   **Correct samples:** Search and deletion of certain letters in the text (for example, all "A" or "O"). Start with 1-2 paragraphs.
*   **"Find 10 differences":** A classic game for the development of concentration and visual attention.

### For coordination and interhemispheric interaction
*   **"Lazy eights":** Simultaneous drawing of the sign of infinity with both hands in the air or on paper.
*   **Cross movements:** Alternally touching the left knee with the right hand and the left hand of the right. Can be performed to the music.
*   **Games with the ball:** Throwing the ball out of the hand to the hand, beaten from the wall alternately with two hands.

### For memory and planning
*   **"What has changed?":** Lay out 5-7 items, ask to remember. After the child turns away, change something (remove, add, change places). The task is to determine the change.
*   **"Build according to the instructions":** Give short oral commands for creating Lego structures or cubes. Gradually complicate the sequence.

### For rhythm and self -control
*   **Games "Delay!" Or "the sea is worried once":** They require a quick reaction to the signal and fixation of the posture.
*   **Tapping rhythms:** You set a simple rhythm with your hand or pencil, the child exactly repeats it. Develops hearing and control of movements.
