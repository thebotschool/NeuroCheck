# Development Profile: 7–9 years

This report offers an understanding of the individual characteristics of the student. Recommendations are aimed at creating an optimal environment for training and harmonious development.

## 1. Overview of the results

### Test for attention and memory (CPT)
The ability to hold several steps or elements in memory is limited, especially with fatigue or emotional excitement. There is a rapid fatigue and risk of confusion in the sequence of actions.

### Self-control test (Go/No-Go)
There is a tendency to impulsive reactions. Actions or answers often ahead of the analysis, leading to errors.

### Attention test (TCP)
With clear and predictable tasks, high concentration and productivity are demonstrated. This ability is a significant support for development.

## 2. Individual profile

### Key features

*   **Pace.** Prefers a calm, measured pace. Hurry or pressure increase impulsive errors and reduce attention.
*   **Perception.** He perceives information more effectively visually and step by step. Long oral instructions overload work memory.
*   **Focus of attention.** Attention works as a "spotlight". In a quiet, structured environment, the focus is strong and stable. In conditions of chaos, noise or multitasking, the focus dissipates.

### Supporting environment

*   **Visual supports.** Check lists, cards with a plan, scheme, highlighting keywords.
*   **Crushing of tasks.** Separation of large tasks into 2-3 small, understandable steps.
*   **Clear rules.** Maximum efficiency is achieved when knowing the exact sequence of actions.
*   **A single channel of information.** When performing the task, distracting sounds or objects on the table reduce concentration.
*   **Rhythm.** Using a timer, rhythmic signals (pops, score) help maintain pace and attention.

### Obstacles

*   **Multitasking.** Several simultaneous instructions lead to non -fulfillment of tasks.
*   **Fast change of activity.** It takes time to transition between tasks.
*   **Noise and distracting factors.** Open doors, windows, conversations reduce the resource of attention.
*   **Long oral instructions.** The perception of long monologues is low.

## 3. Practical steps

### In the home environment

*   **Organization of the workplace.** Create a constant, quiet space without unnecessary items.
*   **Timer method.** Use cycles: 15 minutes of work, 5 minutes of break.
*   **Visual plan.** Make a visual plan of action for the evening (for example, "1. Dinner. 2. Mathematics. 3. Reading").
*   **Planning development.** Before the task, ask: "Where do we start? What is the first step?"

*   **Support and promotion.**
    *   **Focus on efforts.** Praise for the process and diligence ("I see how you tried to concentrate!"), And not just for the result.
    *   **Realistic expectations.** Active attention is limited to 10-15 minutes. Avoid overloads.

### In the educational process

*   **The formulation of tasks.**
    *   **Short instructions.** Give the instructions step by step: "1. Read the condition. 2. Emphasize the question. 3. Write down a short condition."
    *   **Checking understanding.** Spend: "Repeat what needs to be done?"
*   **Structure and space.**
    *   **Choosing a place.** Recommended place: 1-2 desks, closer to the teacher, away from distracting factors (window, door).
    *   **The visual plan of the lesson.** Duplicate the plan or stages of the task on the board or card.
*   **The style of interaction.**
    *   **Calm tone.** A friendly and supportive atmosphere. Avoid criticism for inattention.
    *   **Individual approach.** Use visual supports, the possibility of dividing the tests, additional time for verification.

## 4. Developing exercises

### Attention and control

*   **"Find the differences."** Develops concentration and visual scanning.
*   **Graphic dictations.** Improve auditory attention and follow the instructions.
*   **Board games.** For example, "Dobble", "high -speed caps", "drum". They require a quick, but targeted reaction.

### Memory

*   **"What has changed?"** Lay out objects, the child remembers. Remove or change one item, the task is to find a change.
*   **"Snowball".** The game of repetition and adding elements to the chain ("I put an apple in a bag ...", "I put an apple and a ball in a bag ...").

### Rhythm and coordination

*   **"FROME-OOMI."** Dancing with sudden stops for braking.
*   **RIGHT RITMES.** Repeating the specified rhythmic patterns.
*   **Games with the ball.** Throwing at the target, beating, fishing. Develops coordination and self -control.
