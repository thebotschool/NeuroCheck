# Cognitive development report
## Attention. Braking. Memory. 7-9 years.

This report provides an understanding of the strengths and zones of the development of the student. Recommendations are based on the results of cognitive testing and are designed to create optimal conditions for successful training and well -being.

### 1. Test indicators

*   **Visual memory:** High ability to memorize and retain visual information, even when distracting. Strong side, the basis of training.
*   **Action control (Go/No-Go):** Difficulties with self -control and timely inhibition of undesirable actions.
*   **Sustainability of attention (CPT):** The tendency to impulsive answers, errors in carelessness due to haste.

### 2. Profile of learning

The student demonstrates a quick pace of thinking with difficulties with behavioral control. The information is absorbed quickly, but its use requires external regulation.

#### Features of perception

*   **Pace:** Thinking is quick. Impulsiveness leads to the passage of details. An external rhythm is required for the sequence of actions.
*   **Perception format:** Preference of visual information: diagrams, pictures, brief written instructions, color coding. Auditory information without visual support is perceived worse.
*   **Focus of attention:** Attention is easily switched, it is difficult to hold on one task, especially in the absence of interest. High sensitivity to distracting factors (noise, inner thoughts).

#### Supporting factors

*   **Visual supports:** Check lists, step-by-step schemes, highlighting key elements.
*   **Step -by -step instructions:** One action at a time. Detailing of each step.
*   **Rhythm and timers:** The use of visual timers (sand clocks) for short, focused work intervals (10-15 minutes).
*   **Predictability:** Clear rules and rituals. The clear structure of actions increases confidence.
*   **Physical activity:** Short breaks for physical exercises for "rebooting" control.

#### Obstacles

*   **Multitasking:** Long, complex oral instructions.
*   **High pace:** A quick change in tasks without clear transitions.
*   **Sensory overload:** Noise, disorder, a large number of people.
*   **Rush and pressure:** Situations requiring an immediate response provoke impulsiveness.

### 3. Recommendations

#### In the home environment

*   **Organization of training:**
    *   Savior homework into blocks (15 minutes of work, 5 minutes of active break).
    *   Use checks or boards for marking the tasks performed. This gives visibility and a sense of control.
    *   Speak a clear plan of action before starting work.
*   **Support and motivation:**
    *   Praise for efforts and process, and not just for the result. Example: "Great that you checked yourself!"
    *   Avoid criticism for inattention errors. Calmly offer a joint repeated analysis.
*   **Focus of attention:** Realistic time of active keeping attention on one task is 10-15 minutes. The requirement is more counterproductive.

#### In the educational process

*   **Instructions:**
    *   Formulate the instructions as clearly as possible, briefly, step by step.
    *   Duplicate oral instructions with visual: on the board, on the cards.
    *   Miss complex instructions into simple teams.
*   **Organization of space:**
    *   Place the schoolchild on the first desk, closer to the center. This reduces distracting factors, promotes contact with the teacher.
    *   Use repeated rituals to start and end the lesson.
    *   Actively use visual materials: schemes, intelligence cards, videos.
*   **Adaptation of the process:**
    *   Consider the provision of additional time for testing.
    *   Allow the use of a reading line or drafts for calculations.
    *   Agree on the signal (for example, the raised hand) for the request of the Time-Auta in case of loss of control.

### 4. Recommended training

The proposed games and exercises contribute to the development of the necessary skills.

#### Development of attention and self -control

*   **"Fast!" (analogue "Sea is worried once"):** Instant traffic stop by signal. He trains quick braking of actions.
*   **"Yes, and there is no talk":** Development of monitoring of speech reactions and attentiveness.
*   **Board games-detectors:** Games with the rules requiring the passage of the stroke or return are developing self -control.

#### Development of coordination and bodily control

*   **"Robot":** Performing clear commands of an adult on movement. Synchronization of auditory instructions and bodily action.
*   **Games with the ball:** Throwing the ball with the condition (for example, catch only after cotton).

#### The development of rhythm

*   **"Rhythmic echo":** Repetition of a given rhythm (tapping, cotton) with gradual complication. Helps organize pace.

#### Development of attention to details (based on visual memory)

*   **Graphic dictations:** Accurate follow -up instructions for moving in cells.
*   **Games to search for differences, labyrinths, proofing samples:** They train the focus and prevention of haste.
