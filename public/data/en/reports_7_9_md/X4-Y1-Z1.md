### Cognitive assessment report

CPT+GNG+TCP rating. Age: 7–9 years.

This report provides a comprehensive overview of the cognitive characteristics of the student. It is designed to identify strengths and development zones, offering practical approaches for effective support in training and everyday life.

---

### **1. Brief test overview**

*   **Work memory test (CPT):** Difficulties with maintaining sequences and planning multi -stage actions have been identified.
*   **Self-control test (Go/No-Go):** Demonstrates developed self -control and attentiveness to clear rules, the ability to quickly reaction and its deterrence.
*   **Test for stability of attention (TCP):** The ability to work is confirmed, provided that there are clear rules and structured tasks.

### **2. The guide for the success of your student**

Successful education of the student depends on the understanding of his unique cognitive profile.

#### Individual characteristics

*   **Pace:** Comfortable, leisurely. It takes time to process information and planning. A rush can lead to inaccuracies.
*   **Perception:** Optimally assimilates information presented by short, understandable steps and reinforced **visual supports** (schemes, images, lists). Long oral instructions are less effective.
*   **Focus of attention:** It shows a high concentration on simple, clear tasks with understandable rules. However, with complex, multi -stage tasks or the need to retain a large amount of information, attention may decrease.

#### What supports

*   **Step -by -step instructions:** Savior of complex tasks into small, consistent stages.
*   **Visual tips:** The use of checklists, algorithms, diagrams, highlighting key elements.
*   **Clear rhythm:** The use of timers for short working intervals (for example, 15 minutes of work, 5 minutes of break). Predictability reduces fatigue.
*   **The principle of "one task at a time":** Organization of a workplace with a minimum number of distracting items.

#### What makes it difficult

*   **Multitasking:** Simultaneous perception, memorization and performance of several actions reduces productivity.
*   **Long oral monologues:** Paying attention to long -term oral instructions is limited.
*   **Information overload:** A large amount of new material or many tasks on one page cause fatigue and refusal of work.
*   **Unstructured environment:** Noise, chaotic change of activity, disorder in the workplace.

### **3. Recommendations**

The application of these recommendations will help optimize the educational process and support the development of the student.

#### For the house

*   **Homework organization:**
    *   Divide homework into blocks for 15–20 minutes. Between the blocks there are short breaks (physical activity, rest).
    *   Use the “Task Board” (visual plan of the day/week) to track the completed tasks.
    *   Recommend the use of drafts for planning and creating schemes before the final execution.
*   **Support and encouragement:**
    *   Focus on praise for **efforts and following the plan**, not just for the result.
    *   Instead of criticism of errors, analyze together: “What step was the most difficult? How can I improve the next time? ”
*   **Realistic expectations:**
    *   Effective productive attention on complex mental task is about **15–20 minutes**. An increase in this time can be counterproductive.

#### For school

*   **The wording of tasks:**
    *   Provide maximum specificity and step -by -step in formulating tasks. Write down key steps on the board or on an individual card.
    *   Example: instead of “Write about the summer”, use the plan: 1. Describe the weather (2 sentences). 2. Tell us about the places where there were (3 offers). 3. Highlight the most remembered (1 sentence).
*   **The structure of the lesson and the workplace:**
    *   The optimal place: the first-second desk, close to the teacher and the board, away from distracting factors.
    *   Give the tasks one by one, especially when performing tests.
*   **Material feed:**
    *   Use a calm, flat tone. Reinforce oral instructions with visual materials (laconic presentations, patterns on the board, support cards).
*   **Individual educational route (IOM):**
    *   Consider the possibility of providing additional time for control, permission to use drafts or plans during answers, the use of visual checks for design work.

### **4. Recommended training**

These exercises are aimed at developing key cognitive skills.

*   **For attention and strategies:**
    *   **The game "Corrector":** Search and dispensing certain letters in the text (for example, all “a”). Gradually complicate, adding other actions (for example, “A” delete, “O” circle).
    *   **Graphic dictations:** Performing drawings according to vocal instructions (for example, “one cage to the right, two cells up”).
*   **For working memory:**
    *   **Memory game:** Search for paired cards. Start with a small amount (4-6 pairs), gradually increasing. Speak strategies.
    *   **Exercise "Buying in the store":** Memorization and repetition of a list of objects. Gradually increase the list, teach the grouping of subjects by categories.
*   **For rhythm and coordination (development of self -control):**
    *   **RIGHTS RIGHTS:** The exact repetition of rhythmic patterns set by adults.
    *   **Games with the ball:** Throwing the ball with beaten from the floor under the expense or in a given rhythm.
*   **For bodily control (connection of the body and brain):**
    *   **Games "Sea is worried once", "Frozen":** Training the ability to timely stop the movement at the signal.
    *   **Exercise "Copying Machine":** The exact reproduction of the type adopted by adults.
