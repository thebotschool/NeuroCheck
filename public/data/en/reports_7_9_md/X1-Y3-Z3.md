# Development Profile: Cognitive assessment

This report offers a detailed overview of the cognitive features of your student, identified during testing. The goal is to provide practical recommendations for creating the most effective environment of training and development.

---

### **1. Key aspects**

*   **Visual memory.** High level of development. Confidently remembers the sequences. Saves the focus even with external distraction. The basis for effective training.

*   **Control and coordination.** Noticeable asymmetry in the accuracy of movements. The leading hand dominates. Indicates the need for the development of interhemispheric interaction, especially for tasks that require accurate coordination.

*   **Attention and impulsiveness.** There is a tendency to impulsiveness. Actions are often ahead of thinking, leading to hasty mistakes. The development of self -control is required.

### **2. The guide for the success of your student**

### Peculiarities

*   **Pace.** Fast, gusty. Included to start the action until the completion of the instructions.
*   **Perception.** The visual channel prevails. Pictures, schemes, short lists, videos are more effective than oral explanations.
*   **Focus.** Deep concentration on interesting. Attention is easily disturbed due to impulsiveness. It is difficult to hold attention to monotonous tasks or with long expectation.

### Support

*   **Visual agents.** Check lists, schemes, cards with rules. Using markers to highlight the key.
*   **Step -by -step instructions.** Separation of large tasks into 2-3 small steps. Issuance to issue in series, not at a time.
*   **The rule "Stop - Think - do."** Introduce as a ritual before each task. Create a visual hint.
*   **Rhythm and movement.** The use of cotton, account, short physical mines. Activates the brain, improves coordination.
*   **Two -handed activity.** Classes that require the simultaneous work of both hands (drawing, modeling, music). Contribute to the coordinated work of the hemispheres.

### Obstacles

*   **Multitasking.** Simultaneous listening and accurate execution of writing/drawing causes significant stress.
*   **Fast change of tasks.** The absence of a short pause when switching leads to disorganization.
*   **Long oral instructions.** More than two consistent teams are perceived with difficulty.
*   **Hurry and pressure.** Phrases of the type “faster”, “hurry” enhance impulsiveness, provoking errors.

### **3. Development strategies**

### Home environment

*   **Homework.**
    *   Break into blocks for 10-15 minutes. Between them - 3-5 minutes of movement.
    *   Use a visual timer to control time.
    *   Create a visual plan of the evening: a sequence of actions indicating the time.
*   **Support and promotion.**
    *   Praise for attentiveness and following the plan, and not for speed. Example: "Great, what you checked before passing!"
    *   Analyze impulsive errors calmly. Example: "You hurried and did not notice. Next time we will pause."
*   **Organization of space.**
    *   The workplace should be free from distracting items. Only necessary for the current task.

### School environment

*   **The formulation of tasks.**
    *   The instructions should be short and clockwork. Example: "Open the textbook on page 5. Find exercise 3." Paise.
    *   Duplicate oral tasks in writing on the board (list).
*   **The structure of the lesson.**
    *   Turn on short physicaltings with cross -drawn movements.
    *   Use visual materials: schemes, tables, presentations.
    *   Place the student closer to the teacher, away from the sources of distraction.
*   **Style style.**
    *   Speak calmly and measuredly.
    *   Before contacting by name, pause to help concentrate.
*   **Elements of an individual educational route (IOM).**
    *   Allow me to use a reading line.
    *   Provide tasks in the form of printed checklists.
    *   For complex tasks that require simultaneous hearing and writing, simplify (for example, insert missed words instead of complete rewriting).

### **4. Growth tools**

The development of self -control and coordination through the game. Give 5-10 minutes daily.

### Attention and control

*   **"Yes, no say":** Develops control of speech reactions.
*   **"You go quieter - you will continue":** Teaches instant movement of movement.
*   **Graphic dictations:** Strengthen the ability to follow the instructions and concentrate.

### Coordination and integration

*   **Mirror drawing:** Simultaneous drawing with two hands of symmetrical figures.
*   **"Fist-Robric Ladon":** A consistent change in the positions of the hand. It is performed with one hand, then the other, then both.
*   **Games with the ball:** Fitting from the wall, throwing between the hands.

### Memory

*   **"Find a couple" (Memory):** Effectively trains visual memory.
*   **"What has changed?":** Memorization of 5-7 items, then detecting changes.
*   **"Remember and draw":** Reproduction from memory of a simple figure or pattern after a short show.
