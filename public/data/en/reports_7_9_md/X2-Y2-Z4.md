# Individual development profile
Cognitive assessment (7–9 years)

This report offers a detailed understanding of the cognitive profile of your student. It is designed to identify strengths and areas for development, providing practical recommendations to support training and growth.

<!-- page-break -->

## 1. Cognitive profile

*   **Visual memory:** Effective assimilation of information at the beginning and end of the sequence. Requires support in holding the central elements.
*   **Braking:** There is a tendency to impulsive actions. The development of self -control is required.
*   **Attention:** Difficulties with multitasking and simultaneous retention of the task, control of actions and quick reaction.

<!-- page-break -->

## 2. The guide for the success of your student

### Individual characteristics

*   **Working pace:** Uneven. Impulsiveness in simple tasks is manifested; Slow down when memorized due to uncertainty or overload.
*   **Information perception:** The most effectively is perceived visually and in parts. High memorization of the first and last element in the sequence.
*   **Focus of attention:** Unrestrained, subject to distraction. Difficulties with filtering external stimuli (noise, movement).

### Which helps

*   **Visual supports:** Short lists, schemes, color selection, cards with rules or algorithms.
*   **Step -by -step instructions:** One task at a time. Important information is posted at the beginning or end of the instruction.
*   **Rhythm and pauses:** Pronouncing actions aloud, using a timer for work intervals (for example, 15/5 minutes), clear start and completion signals.
*   **Predictability:** A clear daily routine, previously agreed rules.
*   **Tactile stimulation:** The ability to take hands (antistress ball) for concentration and relief.

### What prevents

*   **Multitasking:** Simultaneous listening, observation and recording.
*   **Long oral instructions:** Only the beginning and end are held, the middle is lost.
*   **Noise and chaos:** Loud sounds, active movement, visual mess.
*   **High pace of the lesson:** A quick change in tasks without time to switch and comprehension.
*   **Rush and pressure:** Phrases that cause anxiety and increase in errors.

## 3. Practical recommendations

### For the house

*   **Homework:**
    *   Break work into blocks for 15–20 minutes with short breaks. Use the timer.
    *   Perform one item at a time, removing all the excess from the table.
    *   Spend the work plan before the start. Place the most difficult thing at the beginning.
*   **Support and encouragement:**
    *   Praise for efforts and the use of strategies, and not just for the result: “Well done, that you are in no hurry!”, “It's great that you checked at first!”
    *   Instead of censure for errors, ask: “What do you think, why it happened? Let's try differently. "
*   **Organization of everyday life:**
    *   Give the instructions one at a time. Divide complex indications of simple steps.
    *   Use visual checks for morning and evening affairs (pictures or short words).

### For school

*   **Setting tasks:**
    *   Formulate the tasks clearly, briefly and step by step. Duplicate oral instructions on the board or card.
    *   Use the “effect of the edge”: highlight the beginning and end of the instructions for better memorization.
*   **Lesson structure:**
    *   Structure the material on the board and in handouts: lists, diagrams, paragraphs, highlighting keywords.
    *   Let's pause (5-10 seconds) before the answer for formulating thoughts.
*   **Accommodation and environment:**
    *   The preferred place is the first desk, closer to the center or against the wall (away from distracting factors).
*   **Elements of an individual route:**
    *   Additional time for tests.
    *   Permission to use submarine cards with algorithms or plans.
    *   Breaking volumetric tasks into several parts.

## 4. Development programs

These exercises contribute to the development of cognitive functions. Daily practice is recommended in a playful way (10-15 minutes).

*   **Attention and self -control:**
    *   **Graphic dictations:** They train the retention of instructions and attentiveness (“Two cells to the right, one cage up ...”).
    *   **Correct samples:** Search and dispensing certain letters on the page.
    *   **The game "Delay-Otomory":** Develops the ability to stop by signal in time.
*   **Visual memory:**
    *   **Memory game:** Search for paired cards. Start with 4-6 pairs, gradually increasing the amount.
    *   **"What has changed?":** Remember 5-7 items on the table, then determine what was removed or changed.
*   **Coordination and body control:**
    *   **Neurohymnastics:** Exercise "Fist-Robric Ladon" (separately with each hand, then two together).
    *   **Games with the ball:** Bowing the ball from the floor or wall in a given rhythm.
    *   **Balance exercises:** "Swallow", walking along the line, a stand on one leg.
*   **Rhythm and regulation:**
    *   **Tapping rhythms:** Repetition of a given rhythm.
    *   **Respiratory exercises:** Slow inhalation (4 accounts), delay (2 accounts), slow exhalation (6 accounts) for calming and concentration.
