**Cognitive development report**
*Age: 7–9 years*

This report provides an analysis of the cognitive functions of the student. The goal is to identify the strengths and areas for development by proposing practical recommendations for effective training at home and at school.

---

### 1. Cognitive tests: brief overview
*   **Sustainability of attention (CPT):** Difficulties with the retention of several consecutive tasks are noted. Tiredness and fragmented memorization are observed.
*   **Self-control (Go/No-Go):** Difficulties with quick switching between the instructions have been identified. Impulsiveness or slow reaction is possible.
*   **Complex attention:** Difficulties are observed when performing multi -component tasks that require simultaneous attention, action and compliance with the rules. External support is required.
<!-- page-break -->
### 2. Profile of a student

#### Individual characteristics
*   **Pace:** It works in a comfortable, unhurried rhythm. Hasterness and high load lead to rapid fatigue.
*   **Perception:** The preference for visual and tactile information. Abstract oral instructions are less effective.
*   **Attention:** Unstable, easily distracted. Holding the focus on one task - up to 10-15 minutes, especially in the absence of interest.

#### Which helps
*   **Instructions:** Clear, short, step -by -step.
*   **Visualization:** Schemes, cards, color coding, visual aids.
*   **Wednesday:** Predictable schedule and rituals.
*   **Tactility:** The use of objects (counting sticks, expander) and rhythm (tapping) during training.
*   **Time for processing:** A pause is needed to comprehend instructions before starting the action.

#### What prevents
*   **Multitasking:** Simultaneous listening and recording are difficult.
*   **Long instructions:** More than 2-3 steps lead to loss of information.
*   **Distracting factors:** Noise, movements, visual disorder reduce concentration.
*   **High pace:** Haster causes errors, frustration and refusal of activity.
<!-- page-break -->
### 3. Practical recommendations

#### For the house
*   **Organization of homework:**
    *   Break work into blocks for 10-15 minutes. Turn on short motor breaks.
    *   Use a time to visualize time.
    *   Start with the most difficult tasks.
    *   Make a visual work plan: "Mathematics (2nd example) -> break (5 min) -> reading (1 p.)".
*   **Support and encouragement:**
    *   Mark the effort and process, and not just the result. Example: "I see how you focused!"
    *   Create a "corner of assistance" with hints (rules, alphabet, plan).
*   **Realistic expectations:** Effective attention on a difficult task is up to 15 minutes, then you need a change of activity.

#### For school
*   **The wording of tasks:**
    *   Step -by -step instructions, waiting for each step. Example: "Open the textbook ... Find the exercise ... read ...".
    *   Duplicate oral instructions with a recording on a board or card.
*   **The structure of the material:**
    *   Planting at 1-2 desks to minimize distracts.
    *   The use of checklists for volumetric tasks.
    *   Visual organization of the workplace: only necessary on the table.
*   **Communication style:**
    *   Calm, non -pace of speech, eye contact.
    *   Using gestures and visual aids.
    *   **Individual educational route:** Consider the additional time for testing. It is possible to accompany the tutor or psychologist for the development of self -control.

### 4. Recommended exercises

The above games and exercises will support the development of the necessary skills. Do 10-15 minutes a day in a playful way.

#### Attention
*   **Correct test:** Crossing certain letters in the text (for example, "a"). Complication: deleting "A" and bypassing "O".
*   **"Find the differences":** Classical tasks for the development of concentration and visual attention.

#### Coordination and bodily control
*   **Graphic dictation:** Drawing on oral instructions. Develops the connection of rumor movements.
*   **Games with the ball:** Catching and picking the ball in a given rhythm. He trains the reaction and braking.

#### Memory
*   **"What has changed?":** Remember the location of 5-7 items, then find changes after their rearrangement or removal.
*   **Moving with movement:** Accompanying the memorization of poems with simple claps or steps.

#### Rhythm and self -regulation
*   **"Repeat me" (rhythm):** Repetition of a given rhythm (for example, tapping). Develops self -control through the rhythm.
