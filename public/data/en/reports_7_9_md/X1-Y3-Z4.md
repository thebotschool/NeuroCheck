## The cognitive profile of the student

Assessment: visual memory + regulation and control + stability of attention (age: 7–9 years)

This report was created to deeply understand the individual characteristics of your student. The data presented are based on the results of cognitive tests and are formulated as practical recommendations for the development of home and at school.

---

### 1. Overview of the results

The analysis of the tests reveals a unique cognitive profile.

*   **Visual working memory:** Great indicators. The student effectively remembers visual information, holding it even with distraction.
*   **Control and braking (Test Go/No-Go):** Difficulties in the uniform control of the movements of both hands were revealed. This indicates the need to harmonize the work of the cerebral hemispheres, especially in tasks that require coordination.
*   **Sustainability of attention (CPT test):** Difficulties with prolonged attention, control of impulses and a quick reaction with the complication of tasks were noted.

**General conclusion:** Strong visual memory provides reliable storage of information. However, the development of data processing mechanisms (attention) and their expression (motor control, letter) is required. The student understands and remembers the instructions, but experiences difficulties with their unmistakable and continuous implementation.

### 2. The profile of the student

Understanding individual characteristics is the key to effective training.

#### Key features

*   **The pace of work:** Uneven. It takes time to "turn on". A slow start is possible with subsequent acceleration. Hasting often leads to errors. A calm, predictable rhythm is important.
*   **Perception:** Expressed visual. The best understanding and memorization is achieved through schemes, images, brief algorithms and color highlighted. Long oral explanations are less effective.
*   **Focus of attention:** Sustainable in short intervals (10-15 minutes). Extremely sensitive to external distracting factors (noise, movement) and fatigue. It is difficult to filter out secondary information.

#### Effective approaches

*   **Visual supports:** Short instructions in pictures. Selection of important information with color. Visual execution samples.
*   **Step -by -step instructions:** Missing the task into separate, consistent steps. "One step is one task."
*   **Clear rhythm:** Using a timer for structuring work and rest (for example, 15 minutes work, 5 minutes break). This makes the load predictable and contributes to the "reboot" of the brain.
*   **Tactile interaction:** Tasks providing touch, movements, drawing with both hands. Promotes the development of interhemispheric interaction.

#### Factors of influence

*   **Multitasking:** Simultaneous requirements to listen, memorize and record reduce effectiveness.
*   **Long oral instructions:** Only the beginning or end of the message is remembered, the middle is lost.
*   **Noise and chaos:** Any distracting factors violate the concentration.
*   **Rush:** Speed ​​tasks cause stress and block access to internal resources.

### 3. Practical recommendations

The use of these strategies will help to reveal the student’s potential.

#### For the house

*   **Homework organization:**
    *   Miss tasks into short blocks (15–20 minutes). Between the blocks - a short break (water, movement, exercise).
    *   Start with the most difficult or written tasks, while the student is full of strength.
    *   Use the “visual schedule” (cards with images for “reading”, “letters”, “mathematics”). This allows the student to independently monitor progress.
    *   Allow me to use the draft. This reduces fear of error and focuses on the process.
*   **Support and encouragement:**
    *   Praise for efforts and process, and not just for the result. (“I see how you tried!”, “It's great that you noticed a mistake and corrected it”, “You could concentrate again after a pause - this is a super -voyage!”)
    *   Remember, the optimal concentration time on a complex educational task is about 15 minutes. Plan breaks.

#### For school

*   **The wording of tasks:**
    *   Apply the principle of "one step at a time." The instructions should be short, clumsy and visually supported (on the board, on the card).
    *   The ideal format: "Look at the sample. Make the same." This activates a strong visual memory.
*   **Work structure and accommodation:**
    *   The optimal place in the class - in the center, closer to the board, remotely from the window and door, to minimize distracting factors.
    *   Before starting written work, offer 1-2 minutes of a simple hand exercise (clenching the fists, rhythmic pops).
    *   Use colored markers on the board to highlight the key parts of the task.
*   **Individual educational route (IOM):**
    *   It is recommended to provide additional time for control and written work.
    *   Permission to use visual templates or algorithms (for example, memos for solving problems).
    *   The possibility of an oral response in cases where a written expression causes the greatest difficulties.

### 4. Targeted exercises

These exercises and games are aimed at strengthening attention and interhemispheric interaction, based on strong visual memory.

*   **Attention and control:**
    *   **Graphic dictations:** "Two cells to the right, one up ...". Develops auditory attention, self -control, spatial thinking.
    *   **"Find 10 differences":** Improves concentration and ability to notice details.
    *   **The game "Sea is worried once":** He trains the ability to stop and control the body in time.

*   **Coordination and interhemispheric interaction:**
    *   **Mirror drawing:** Simultaneous drawing of symmetrical figures (circle, square, butterfly) with both hands.
    *   **"Fist-Robric Ladon":** A consistent change of hand positions. Start slowly, with one hand, then with the other, then two at the same time.
    *   **RIGHTS RIGHTS:** Build a simple rhythm, the student repeats - first with one hand, then with the other, then with two.

*   **Memory (strengthening the strength):**
    *   **The game "Memory" ("Find a couple"):** A classic game that uses and develops strong visual memory.
    *   **"Remember and draw":** Show a card with several simple figures for 5-10 seconds. The schoolboy must reproduce them from memory in the correct order.
