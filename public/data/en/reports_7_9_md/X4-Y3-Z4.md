# Cognitive assessment report

## Assessment of cognitive abilities: attention, self -control, memory (7–9 years)

This report is designed to help parents and teachers understand the strengths and growth zones of the student. This is not a diagnosis, but a guide for creating a supporting environment of training and development.

---

### 1. Brief test overview

*   **Sustainability of attention (CPT):** There are difficulties with a long concentration on one task. Fatual fatigue from the mental stress is noted.
*   **Self-control (Go/No-Go):** High control of movements by the leading hand. Coordination of both hands or actions by an unnecessary hand require additional effort.
*   **Visual working memory (TCP):** Difficulties with the simultaneous maintenance of attention, impulse control and a quick reaction to the changing conditions of the problem.

<!-- page-break -->

### 2. The guide for the success of your student

#### Individual characteristics

*   **Pace:** Uneven. It takes time to "include" in the task. A quick change in activity exhausts.
*   **Perception:** Visual-kinesthetic. It more efficiently assimilates information that he sees or can reproduce physically. Long oral instructions are ineffective.
*   **Focus of attention:** Short, unstable. Concentration up to 15–20 minutes, then a break is required. Sensitivity to external distracting factors.

#### Which helps

*   Clear, step -by -step instructions.
*   Visual supports: schemes, cards.
*   Activity with rhythm and movement.
*   The predictability and daily routine.

#### What interferes

*   Multitasking.
*   A sharp change in activities.
*   Noise, distracting factors.
*   Long -term oral explanations.

<!-- page-break -->

### 3. Recommendations

#### For the house

*   **Organization of work:**
    *   Intervals: 15–20 minutes of work / 5-7 minutes break.
    *   Organization of the workplace: only necessary for the current task.
    *   Check lists: joint planning and mark of the executed.
*   **Support and encouragement:**
    *   Encourage diligence, not just the result. The emphasis on efforts.
    *   Offer the choice: "Where do we start?"
    *   Turn on games involving both hands (ball, modeling, designer).

#### For school

*   **The wording of tasks:**
    *   Instructions: Briefly, clearly, step by step. Checking understanding.
    *   Duplication of oral tasks in writing.
*   **Lesson structuring:**
    *   Minimization of distracting factors: location in the class.
    *   Visual supports: diagrams, Mayind cards, timers.
    *   Miss large tasks into parts.
*   **Material feed:**
    *   Use gestures, movement, rhythm.
    *   Offer alternative response formats (cards, drawings).
*   **Individual educational route (IOM):**
    *   It may take more time for testing.
    *   Adaptation of tasks: reduction of volume while maintaining complexity.

<!-- page-break -->

### 4. Recommended training

Simple exercises and games for the development of key skills.

#### For attention and memory

*   **"Find the differences", "What is gone?"** (objects).
*   **Graphic dictations.**
*   **The game "Snow com"** (to memory of words).

#### For coordination, rhythm and bodily control

*   **Neurohymnastics** (for example, "Use-Shno").
*   **Games with the ball** (throwing, fighting off).
*   **Removing and repetition of rhythms.**

#### For self -control

*   **The game "Yes and not say."**
*   **Board games "Walking"** (with rules and order).
