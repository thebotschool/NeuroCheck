# Cognitive assessment report

This report offers a detailed analysis of the cognitive features of your student. Its goal is to identify strengths and designate zones for development, contributing to the creation of an optimal environment for training and disclosing potential.

---

## Overview of key indicators

### Test for attention (CPT)
Hold attention and control of actions, if necessary, a quick reaction requires effort.

### Self-control test (Go/No-Go)
Movement by the leading hand more precisely. The use of an unknown hand causes difficulties, pointing to the features of interhemispheric interaction.

### Visual Working Test
Removing the details of the images causes difficulties. The strategy for memorizing the initial and final elements is effective.

## Individual approach to training

### Key features

*   **Pace:** A stable, measured pace of work is preferred. Sharp changes or haste can lead to errors or stupor.
*   **Perception:** The most effective perception through a visual-kinesthetic channel: it is necessary to see, touch or actively interact with information (diagrams, rhythm, tactile elements).
*   **Focus of attention:** Attention is unstable, quickly depleted with multitasking. Effective for concentration on one task; Difficulties arise in parallel processing of information (listen, watch, write).

### Supporting factors

*   **Step -by -step instructions:** Missing volumetric tasks into short, sequential steps. Use lists or checklists.
*   **Visual tips:** Schemes, tables, color discharge, stickers. Place key information at the beginning and end of the material.
*   **Rhythm and movement:** The use of rhythm (score, clamping) helps maintain pace. Short motor breaks contribute to the "reboot" of the brain.
*   **Clear boundaries:** Visual timers help regulate the time of task.

### Distracting factors

*   **Multitasking:** The simultaneous performance of several actions (for example, listening and writing) causes overload.
*   **Noise:** Extraneous sounds, conversations, distracting views from the window easily disperse attention.
*   **Hurry and frequent change of tasks:** It takes time to transition between different activities.
*   **Overload of information:** Solid text, small font or page with many different tasks will disorient.

## Support strategies

### Home environment

*   **Homework:**
    *   Divide into blocks for 15–20 minutes, alternating with 5-minute motor breaks.
    *   Use the timer. Sound the work plan in advance: "First task No. 1, then No. 3. After the break - the rest."
    *   Extract the strengths: "Pay attention to the first and last rule, we will start with them."
*   **Support and encouragement:**
    *   Mark efforts and process, not only the result: "I see how you try, this is great!"
    *   Organize the workplace: delete all unnecessary, leaving only necessary for the current task.

### School environment

*   **The wording of tasks:**
    *   Give the instructions one step. Example: "Step 1. Read the text. Step 2. Find the verbs and emphasize. Step 3. Write them out."
*   **Structuring the material:**
    *   Use work sheets with a clear structure: a minimum of visual noise, a large font, 1-2 tasks on the page.
    *   Place the child in a place with a minimum number of distracting factors (for example, near the wall, not by the window).
*   **Style style:**
    *   Turn on the motor elements in the lesson: "Show with your hands how the letter is written", "Cut the rhythm."
    *   Allow the use of a line for tracking a string or color markers for allocation.
*   **Individual educational route (IOM):**
    *   A temporary reduction in the amount of written tasks is recommended, with an emphasis on oral or creative (drawing schemes, collages).
    *   The consultation of a school psychologist or tutor is desirable to develop supporting strategies.

## Developing practices

The proposed practices contribute to the development of attention, memory and coordination in an interactive form.

### Development of attention

*   **Correct samples:** Refusing certain letters in the text. Start from 1-2 minutes.
*   **Search for differences:** Classic exercise for concentration and visual attention.
*   **The game "Fly":** Mental movement of the chip around the 3x3 field in given areas. The task is to determine the moment of exiting the boundary of the field.

### Development of memory

*   **The game "Memory":** Training of visual memory through the search for paired images.
*   **"What has changed?":** Removing 5-7 items on the table. The child determines what has changed after removing or moving one object.

### Coordination and interaction of the hemispheres

*   **Drawing with both hands:** Simultaneous symmetrical drawing (circles, waves) with two hands.
*   **Gymnastics for the brain:** Exercises "Fist-Robric Ladon" or "Ring" (alternate connection of the thumb with the rest).
*   **Game "Mirror":** Repeating the movements of the partner, imitating mirror reflection.
