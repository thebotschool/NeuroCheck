### **Cognitive profile: 7–9 years**

### **1. The main results**

*   **Attention (CPT).** Sustainable concentration on the task. The child retains the pace and focus without distraction. This indicates a mature readiness for learning.
*   **Self-control (Go/No-Go).** High ability to follow the rules and restrain impulses. An important factor for successful assimilation of material and interaction in the classroom.
*   **Visual working memory.** Easy memorization of visual information (pictures, diagrams). The ability to keep data in memory, even with external distraction.

<!-- page-break -->

### **2. Success Guide**

#### **Key features**

*   **Pace.** A flat, confident pace of work. Indicates internal control and accurate self -esteem.
*   **Perception.** The most effective assimilation of information occurs visually: through diagrams, images, samples, clear algorithms.
*   **Focus.** The ability to long, stable concentration. Effective work on one task without loss of quality.

#### **Efficiency factors**

*   **Clear instructions.** Optimal work when understanding the sequence of actions.
*   **Visual supports.** Schemes, plans, intelligence cards, lists-all this helps to structure and remember the material.
*   **Gradual complication.** Readiness for challenges. The ability to offer complex tasks and projects.
*   **Rhythm and structure.** Stable schedule and predictability contribute to productive work.

#### **What should be taken into account**

*   **Multitasking.** Parallel performance of tasks reduces effectiveness. The preference for the sequence.
*   **External distraction.** Noise, disorder - create an extra load, despite the stability of attention.
*   **Faring instructions.** The absence of a specific action plan is confused. The child focuses on rules and algorithms.

<!-- page-break -->

### **3. Recommendations**

#### **Home environment**

*   **Organization of classes.**
    *   Provide the workplace without distracting factors.
    *   Effective work: 20–25 minutes of concentration, then a short pause (5 minutes).
    *   Use visual gliders and lists of cases to track progress.
*   **Support and motivation.**
    *   Discuss the implementation plan before the start of work.
    *   Mark not only the result, but also the process: zeal, concentration, following the plan.
    *   Develop independence in routine tasks, based on developed self -control.

#### **School environment**

*   **Making tasks.**
    *   Formulate the tasks clearly, step by step.
    *   Provide written or visual execution samples - this is a strong support for assimilation.
*   **Organization of the educational process.**
    *   High potential in project activities requiring planning and concentration. It is possible to appoint responsible for the plan in group work.
    *   Accommodation in the zone with minimal distraction (away from the door/window).
    *   The most effective style that combines clear instructions with creative freedom in the framework of the rules.
*   **Individual potential.**
    *   Adaptation of the program is not required.
    *   It is recommended to participate in olympiads, circles (logic, programming, chess) for the development of strengths.

### **4. Development of skills**

Game exercises to support and develop cognitive skills.

*   **Attention.**
    *   Board games: “Chess”, “Checkers”, “Seth”, “Corridor”.
    *   Graphic dictations (for example, “one cage to the right, two up”).
*   **Memory.**
    *   Games for visual memory: “Memory”, “Find 10 differences”, “What has changed in the room?”.
*   **Coordination and rhythm.**
    *   Rhythmic exercises: tapping, cooling of rhythmic drawings.
    *   Games with the ball, juggling (starting with two scarves or balls).
*   **Bodily control.**
    *   Games to control impulses: “The sea is worried once”, “Delay”, “edible-unauthorized” (at a fast pace).
*   **Complex development.**
    *   Designers (LEGO, complex sets according to the instructions).
    *   Puzzles with a lot of details, puzzles.
