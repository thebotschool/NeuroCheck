# Report. Cognitive profile.
### Memory. Control. Attention.
_Age: 7–9 years_

This report reveals the unique abilities of your child and determines the areas to support. Recommendations are created for parents and teachers to provide a comfortable and effective environment for development.
---

# 1. Evaluation. Key conclusions.

*   **Visual memory:** Information is absorbed effectively. However, maintaining the correct data sequence can cause difficulties. The content is remembered, but the reproduction procedure requires attention.
*   **Impulse control:** There is a tendency to rapid response. Impulsive actions can be preceded by complete perception or pondering instructions.
*   **Sustainability of attention:** The effectiveness of attention is optimal in short intervals. Long or monotonous tasks can lead to rapid fatigue and distraction.

# 2. Profile. Your student.

#### Individual characteristics.

*   **Pace:** The start of work is often impulsive. High efficiency requires a moderate pace with short pauses for recovery.
*   **Perception:** Visual and step -by -step information is most effectively absorbed: schemes, short lists, algorithms.
*   **Focus of attention:** Arbitrary attention to one task is preserved for up to 10-15 minutes. Disclosures occur with external irritants or monotony.

#### Support.

*   **Visual supports:** Plans, algorithms on the board or in the form of cards (1. Read, 2. Emphasize, 3. Resolve).
*   **The rule "Stop-think-make":** Formation of a pause before the answer or action. It is permissible to introduce a conditional signal (for example, a raised finger).
*   **Work/rest rhythm:** Structured intervals: 15 minutes of work, 3-5 minutes of outdoor activities (physical warm-up, visual pause). The timer makes the process clear.
*   **Speaking:** Propuring the steps of the assignment aloud contributes to the retention of the sequence and a decrease in impulsiveness.

#### Factors that reduce efficiency.

*   **Multitasking:** Parallel performance of several actions (for example, hearing, recording, viewing slides).
*   **Long oral instructions:** Volumetric oral instructions transferred once can lead to incomplete execution or violations of the sequence.
*   **Time pressure:** Situations requiring an immediate response without preparation (for example, express survey) can cause impulsive reactions.
*   **External distraction:** Excess items in the workplace, extraneous conversations, noise from the outside quickly deplete the resource of attention.

<!-- page-break -->

# 3. Recommendations.

#### For the house.

*   **Homework:** Separate tasks into 15-minute blocks. Provide short breaks. Start with the most difficult tasks when attention is as much as possible.
*   **Support:** Together develop simple plans. Instead of the question of performing lessons, ask: “Which is the next plan? Let's do it together. ”
*   **Promotion:** Evaluate the process, not just the result (speed, assessment). Mark compliance with the plan, stability of attention, initiative in asking questions.
*   **Attention limit:** An effective attention interval for educational tasks: 10-15 minutes. Exceeding the limit leads to errors and fatigue.

#### For school.

*   **The wording of tasks:** Formulate clearly, briefly, step by step. Duplicate oral instructions visually (board, card).
*   **Lesson structure:** Break the topics into subtasks. After each stage, make a pause to test the understanding (for example, “Did you manage everything?”, “Raise your hand, who understood”).
*   **Class placement:** Place the child closer to the teacher, in the central part of the class. Avoid places at windows or doors to minimize abstracts.
*   **Style style:** Use interactive elements: cards, readiness signals, the ability to think over the answer before voiceing.
*   **Regulatory UUD:** The development of regulatory UUD (universal educational actions) focusing on self -control, planning and following instructions is recommended.

# 4. Practice. Workout.

The exercises and games presented below are aimed at developing key skills in interactive form.

#### Attention.

*   **Correct samples:** Crossing out the target letter (for example, "a") in the text. Start with small volumes (1-2 paragraphs).
*   **"Find the differences":** Develop concentration and arbitrary attention.
*   **Board games:** Games requiring the tracking of progress and compliance with the rules (for example, UNO, Dobble, Walking).

#### Coordination and rhythm.

*   **"Edible-universal":** A game with a ball, where a quick solution is required: catch or beat off.
*   **Rhythmic exercises:** Repetition of a given rhythm with cotton or tap.

#### Consistent memory.

*   **"Repeat me":** Repetition of a series of 3-4 movements (for example, cotton-top-jump).
*   **"Shop":** Removing and reproduction of 3-4 items in a given sequence.
*   **Pictures story:** Drawing up a story on 3-4 plot pictures in the correct sequence.

#### Impuls control. Physical.

*   **"The sea is worried once" / "freeze":** Games requiring instant stopping and holding the posture by signal.
*   **Respiratory exercises:** Slow breath (4 scores), delay, slow exhalation. Promotes to calm and slow down the reaction to the task.
