# Cognitive assessment report

**Age:** 7–9 years

This report reveals the unique cognitive features of your student. It contains individual recommendations designed to optimize the process of training at home and at school.

---

## 1. Analysis of tests

*   **Visual working memory:** High ability to memorize and retain visual information. Resistance to distracting factors.
*   **Action control (Go/No-Go):** Effective control by the leading hand. Intergusaceous interaction requires development, especially in coordination tasks.
*   **Sustainability of attention:** Strong, prolonged concentration on one task. The key advantage in learning.

<!-- page-break -->

## 2. Way to the success of the student

### Individual profile

*   **The pace of work:** Stable, calm. Slowing on tasks with coordination of hands or “eye-hand” (for example, rewriting, drawing).
*   **Perception:** The visual channel prevails. Schemes, pictures, written instructions are most effective. Auditory information is enhanced by visual support.
*   **Focus of attention:** It is stable with clarity of the task and suitable format. Sensitive to a quick change in actions or multitasking.

### Support strategies

*   **Visual supports:** Cards, step -by -step illustrations, color coding. Visibility structures information.
*   **Step -by -step instructions:** One action at a time. Example: "Read the text. Take a pencil. Emphasize the main idea."
*   **Rhythm and movement:** Speaking aloud, taping, score. Promotes interhemispheric interaction.

### Factors that reduce efficiency

*   **Multitasking:** Simultaneous actions (listen and write) cause overload and errors.
*   **Only oral instructions:** Information without visual support is quickly forgotten.
*   **Rush and sharp transitions:** It takes time to switch between tasks. Fast shifts reduce productivity.

<!-- page-break -->

## 3. Practical recommendations

### For homework

*   **Organization:**
    *   Divide into blocks for 15–20 minutes with short breaks.
    *   Use checklists to visualize progress.
    *   For writing - first a draft for reducing stress and focus on the content.
*   **Promotion:**
    *   Mark care, accuracy and completion, and not just speed.
    *   With difficulties with a letter/drawing: the joint work "hand in hand" or "drawing in the air".
*   **Attention:** Effective concentration lasts 15–20 minutes. After that, a 3-5-minute active break is needed.

### For the educational process

*   **Instructions:**
    *   Clear, short, step -by -step instructions. Example: "Open the notebook. Write down the number. Look at the board."
    *   Duplicate oral instructions with a recording on a board or card.
*   **Materials:**
    *   Place key information (samples, rules) in sight.
    *   Use visual tips: schemes, algorithms, color markers.
    *   Give more time to rewrite with the board or the ability to photograph.
*   **Innings:**
    *   Maintain rhythm (for example, choral pronunciation).
    *   Temporarily shift the emphasis from a letter to oral answers, work with cards, schemes or work at the board.

## 4. Developing exercises

These exercises help strengthen neural connections and develop key skills in a playful way.

### For attention and memory (strengthening the strengths)

*   **Visual games:** "Find 10 differences", "Memory".
*   **Graphic dictations:** "One cage to the right, two up."
*   **Labyrinths:** Passing on paper.

### For coordination and interhemispheric interaction (growth zone)

*   **Bilateral drawing:** Simultaneous drawing with two hands (circles, waves, butterflies).
*   **Finger gymnastics:** Exercises where the hands perform different movements ("ring", "fist-rib-lady").
*   **Games with the ball:** Remarishing, fighting off the wall alternately. Juggling (starting with shawls).

### For rhythm and bodily control

*   **"Rhythmic echo":** Repetition of a given rhythm (palms, pencil).
*   **"Mirror":** Accurate and simultaneous repetition of movements.
*   **Cross -steps:** Walking in place with touching the elbow and the opposite knee.
