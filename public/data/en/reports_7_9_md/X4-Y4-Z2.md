# Cognitive assessment report: Development Profile: 7–9 years old

This report offers a detailed overview of the cognitive characteristics of the student. It is designed to help parents and teachers in creating an optimal environment for development, using strengths and developing growth zones.

## 1. Evaluation: Key aspects

*   **Working memory:** Difficulties with the retention of many parts or consistent steps are noted. Perhaps rapid fatigue leading to the loss of the start of the instruction.
*   **Self-control (Go/No-Go):** Difficulties with the suppression of impulsive reactions have been identified. It can act without completing the perception of the instructions, or to experience difficulties with a quick change in the rules.
*   **Sustainability of attention (TCP):** The reduced stability of attention to the same tasks is confirmed. The concentration is rapidly reduced in the absence of interest.

## 2. The guide for the success of the student

This guide will help to find the optimal learning approach.

### Individual characteristics

*   **Pace:** Thoughtful, measured. Pause of information processing is required. Hurry can cause stress and increase the number of errors.
*   **Perception:** Visual-kinesthetic. Preference is given to visual, tactile materials (cards, diagrams, objects) instead of exclusively auditory information.
*   **Attention:** It is held shortly (5-10 minutes). A change in activity or a short break is required. Sensitive to external distracting factors.

### Effective approaches

*   **Step -by -step instructions:** One task, one short instruction.
*   **Visual supports:** Cards with an action plan (for example: open a textbook, find a page, read the rule).
*   **Clear rhythm:** The predictable mode "Work-Remado" (for example, 10 minutes of work, 3 minutes of the break).
*   **One information channel:** Speak or show, avoiding simultaneous presentation.
*   **Tactile stimulation:** The use of small objects (eraser, smooth stone) for concentration of attention is allowed.

### Interviewing factors

*   **Multitasking:** Instructions requiring several actions at the same time.
*   **Long oral instructions:** The perception of long speech instructions is difficult.
*   **Visual and audial noise:** Distracting sounds, excess objects in the field of view.
*   **Time pressure:** Phrases creating a rush.
*   **Unexpected change of plans:** Sharp transitions between activities without warning.

## 3. Recommendations

### For the house

*   **Organization of homework:**
    *   **Short approaches:** 10-15 minutes of work, 5 minutes of a break with motor activity.
    *   **Priority:** Start difficult tasks at the beginning, when the energy level is maximum.
    *   **Workplace:** Take out all the extra. Leave only the necessary objects for the current task.
    *   **Joint planning:** Make a simple plan for the evening (for example: dinner, mathematics, reading).
*   **Support and encouragement:**
    *   **Promotion:** Mark the efforts and diligence, and not just the result. (For example: "I appreciate your work.")
    *   **Day of the day:** Provide the predictable, calm mode. Stability reduces anxiety, improves cognitive functions.
    *   **Readiness for classes:** Make sure the child rested and is not hungry. Avoid classes immediately after watching media content or active games.

### For school

*   **The wording of tasks:**
    *   **Step -by -step instructions:** Let's one step. Make sure of understanding by asking to repeat.
    *   **Visual algorithm:** Use cards for complex tasks (for example: read the condition, emphasize the main thing, write down the solution, check).
*   **Lesson structure and workplace:**
    *   **Place in the class:** The first-second desk in the central row to minimize distracting factors.
    *   **Alternation of activities:** Change the types of activity (letter, oral tasks, visual materials, physical education).
*   **Elements of an individual educational route (IOM):**
    *   **Adaptation of tasks:** Discuss with the school psychologist a reduction in the volume of the same type of exercises, extra time on control.
    *   **Tutor/Psychologist:** Consider the maintenance of the tutor or the help of a psychologist in lessons requiring an increased concentration.

## 4. Recommended training

The above exercises are integrated into everyday life, contributing to the development of key skills.

*   **For attention:**
    *   **"Corrective tests":** Crossing certain letters on the text page (1-2 minutes).
    *   **"Find the differences":** Exercises for comparing images.
    *   **Graphic dictations:** Performing drawings according to the auditory instructions. Develops auditory attention and spatial thinking.
*   **For memory:**
    *   **"Shop":** Remoning and “purchase” 3-4 items. The list is gradually increasing.
    *   **"What has changed?"**: Changing the situation or removing one of 5-7 items on the table, followed by the definition of changes.
    *   **Memory game:** Search for paired images.
*   **For coordination and bodily control:**
    *   **"Fist-Robric Ladon":** Slow, rhythmic change of hand positions. Improves self -control, interhemispheric interaction.
    *   **Games with the ball:** Throws, fishing, beating. Training the coordination of the "eye-hand" and the reaction rate.
    *   **Walking along the line:** Movement in a straight or curved line, preserving the balance.
*   **For rhythm:**
    *   **Cooling rhythm:** Repetition of simple rhythm with claps and stomp.
    *   **Poems and counts:** The study of short verses and counts. Rhythmic speech contributes to the organization of brain activity.
