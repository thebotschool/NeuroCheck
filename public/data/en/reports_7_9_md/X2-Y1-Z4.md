# Cognitive profile: attention, memory, control

**Age:** 7–9 years

This report offers a detailed overview of the cognitive abilities of your student. It was created for parents and teachers to help reveal the child’s potential and support him in learning.

---

## 1. Key results

*   **Visual working memory (CPT):** Removing a large volume of visual details may be complicated. The child effectively uses the strategy of memorization: he holds the information best at the beginning and end of the sequence.
*   **Braking (Go/No-Go):** High level of concentration and self -control. Able to follow clear rules. The potential of self -control is manifested in structured tasks.
*   **Sustainability of attention (TCP):** Maintaining the focus with prolonged or multitasking causes difficulties. Attention is quickly exhausted.

<!-- page-break -->

## 2. Features of training

### Individual characteristics

*   **Pace:** The most productive at a unhurried, thoughtful pace. A rush leads to errors caused by impulsiveness.
*   **Perception:** Best of all perceives structured visual information. He needs an “anchor” to fix the gaze.
*   **Focus of attention:** Short -term, but deep attention. Perfectly concentrates on one simple task. Difficulties arise if necessary to keep several conditions or monitor the process for a long time.

### Which helps

*   **Step -by -step instructions:** One task is one clear instruction.
*   **Visual supports:** Check lists, diagrams, discharge, simple icons. Everything that helps visualize the plan.
*   **Clear rhythm:** Predictable schedule for a change of activity. Using a timer for periods of work and rest.
*   **Rules and algorithms:** Successful when following understandable, pre -known rules.

### What interferes

*   **Multitasking:** Simultaneous tasks, such as “listen, remember and write down”, cause overload.
*   **Long oral instructions:** Active memory holds no more than one sentence at a time.
*   **Visual noise:** Overloaded pages, mess on the desktop.
*   **Sharp change in activity:** Transition without warning and preparation.

## 3. Recommendations

### Home environment

*   **Performing tasks:**
    *   Miss large tasks into 2-3 short approaches (10-15 minutes).
    *   Use a visual timer to control time.
    *   Make a simple written plan of 2-3 points before starting work.
*   **Support:**
    *   Praise the process, not just the result: "You followed the plan!"
    *   The workplace should be minimalistic: only the necessary items in the field of view.
*   **Duration of attention:** Realistic time to keep of arbitrary attention on a complex educational task - **10-15 minutes**. After this, a short (3-5 minutes) break with physical activity is required.

### School environment

*   **Formulation of tasks:**
    *   Give the instructions one step. Wait for the execution, then proceed to the next.
    *   Select the keywords on the board or in printouts.
    *   Use simple, specific formulations.
*   **Structuring information:**
    *   Place important information (rules, topics) in the upper left corner of the board/pages. This is a natural "entry point" for memorization.
    *   Use lists and numbering instead of continuous text.
*   **Style style and environment:**
    *   It is recommended to place a student closer to the teacher, away from distracting factors (window, door).
    *   Warn the change of activity: "After 3 minutes we proceed to the next task."
    *   Consider the elements of an individual educational route: personal checks, adapted cards with already structured information.

## 4. Recommended training

These exercises will help to develop skills that require support. Regularity is important (5-10 minutes daily).

### Development of attention

*   **Correct samples:** Ask to cross out all the letters “A” or circle all the letters “O” in the text. Start with short fragments.
*   **"Find 10 differences":** Classic exercise for training concentration and visual scanning.

### Development of memory

*   **"Memory" / "Find a couple":** He trains visual working memory perfectly. Start with 4-6 pairs, gradually increasing the amount.
*   **"Repeat the sequence":** Put 3-4 items. Let me see 10 seconds, then ask the child to repeat the sequence from his set.

### Development of control and rhythm

*   **"The sea is worried once":** Teaches to stop the movement by signal, developing braking.
*   **RIGHT RIGHTS:** You set a simple rhythm, the child repeats. Gradually complicate. Develops auditory attention and a sense of rhythm.
