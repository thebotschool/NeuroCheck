## 2. The guide for the success of the student
### The optimal approach. Guide for teachers and parents.

### Peculiarities
*   **Pace:** Unstable. A combination of concentration and impulsiveness. Support for stable rhythm is required.
*   **Perception:** Visually. Step by step. Oral instructions can be complicated.
*   **Focus:** Clear tasks keep attention. Consistent actions require effort.

### Support
*   Visual supports: diagrams, checklists, color selection.
*   Step -by -step instructions: dividing tasks into small steps.
*   Clear rituals: the formation of habits before starting actions.
*   Predictable environment: rhythm without haste and sharp changes.

### Obstacles
*   Multivativity: reduces effectiveness, leads to errors.
*   Oral multi -step instructions: call for working memory.
*   Haster: provokes impulsiveness and error.
*   Distracting factors: overload attention, enhance impulsiveness.

## 3. Recommendations

### Home environment
*   **Homework:** In parts. Short breaks (5-7 min). Use the plan: 2-3 points.
*   **Mercing of attention:** Efficiency is 15-20 minutes. A break is needed.
*   **Support:** Praise for following the plan, not for speed.
*   **Help:** Make a plan together. Learn self -organization.

### School environment
*   **Tasks:** Briefly. Step by step. Duplicate oral instructions.
*   **Material:** Divide into blocks. Use schemes, tables.
*   **Place:** Closer to the teacher. Further from abstracts.
*   **Innings:** Interactive. Clear rules. Speak algorithms.
*   **IOM:** Sopore cards with plans for testing.

## 4. Recommended training
### Purpose: The development of key skills.

### Attention and control
*   “You go quieter - you will continue”: Impulse control.
*   "Stop-game": Fast reaction. Braking training.
*   Graphic dictations: auditory attention. Follow the instructions.

### Memory and sequence
*   “Repeat me”: from 2-3 elements. Gradual complication.
*   Pictures: 3-4 pictures. Order. Story.
*   Board games: memorization of sequences.

### Coordination and rhythm
*   "Lokot-Chenone": interhemispheric interaction.
*   RIGHT RITMES: Development of a sense of pace. Reducing haste.
