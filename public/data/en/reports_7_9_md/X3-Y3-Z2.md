# Cognitive assessment report

The goal of the report is to help parents and teachers. He reveals the strengths and areas for growth, offering tools for the development of a student.

## 1. Test results

*   **CPT (Continous Performance Test):** Long -term concentration is difficult, especially on monotonous tasks. Fast fatigue and distraction are possible.
*   **Go/No-Go Test:** Great self -control leading hand. With the second hand, it is more difficult, which indicates the need to develop interhemispheric interaction.
*   **Visual working memory:** Good memorization of facts. Difficulties with the organization of information and compliance with the sequence.

## 2. The guide for the success of the student

### Features of perception

*   **Pace:** The average rate of work is optimal. Too fast leads to errors, slow - to the loss of attention.
*   **Perception:** More effective through visual supports (diagrams, images) and active actions. Simultaneous listening and recording are difficult.
*   **Attention:** Unstable on the same tasks. Effectively retained in short tasks (up to 10-15 minutes) with the subsequent change of activity or break.

### Efficiency support

*   Clear, step -by -step instructions.
*   Visual supports: cards, checklists.
*   Predictability: understandable routine.
*   Physical activity: change of posture, warm -up, use of both hands.

### Distraction factors

*   Multitasking (simultaneous listening and recording).
*   Long oral instructions.
*   Monotonous work (more than 10-15 minutes).
*   Unpredictable transitions between tasks.

## 3. Development recommendations

### Home environment

**How to do homework:**
*   Divide homework into blocks (10-15 minutes). Take short breaks (2-3 minutes).
*   Use a timer to visualize time and concentration.
*   Start with complex written tasks while the energy level is high. Leave oral for later.

**How to support and encourage:**
*   Encourage the process, not just the result. Celebrate diligence and focusing.
*   Create a visual schedule (weekly plan) for the development of planning skills.

**How much attention can be kept:**
*   Complex educational task: up to 15 minutes.
*   Interesting game or creativity: up to 25-30 minutes.

### School environment

**How to formulate tasks:**
*   Miss tasks into small steps. Give the instructions sequentially, as the previous one is fulfilled.

**How to structure information:**
*   Apply visual structures: numbered lists, schemes, intelligence cards. This helps to streamline information.
*   Recommended place: 1-2 desks, a class center, to minimize abstracts.

**Style style:**
*   Use visual materials.
*   Turn on the movement: clamping rhythm, gestures, two -handed drawing.

**IOOM elements (individual educational route):**
*   It is recommended to include regulatory functions in the curriculum (planning, self -control) and interhemispheric interaction. Consultation with a school psychologist or neuropsychologist may be useful.

## 4. Recommended training

These exercises develop key functions. Regularity is important: 5-10 minutes daily.

### For attention

*   "Find the differences."
*   "Corrective test": Refusing/conducting certain letters in the text.

### For coordination and bodily control

*   "Mill": alternate swings with hands with touching the opposite leg.
*   “Ear-Zno”: with the left hand, take up the tip of the nose, the right-by the left ear, then a change of position.
*   Drawing with two hands: simultaneous image of symmetrical figures.

### For memory and sequence

*   “Repeat me”: repetition of a series of movements (3-5).
*   "Shop": oral "purchase" of items with repetition in the same order.
*   Compilation of stories: according to a series of 3-4 plot pictures.

### For rhythm

*   Testing rhythms: repetition of simple rhythms with cotton or tools.
