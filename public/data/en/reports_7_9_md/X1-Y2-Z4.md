### **2. Profile of a student**

The student’s profile demonstrates a strong visual memory that allows you to remember complex rules, but also indicates a tendency to impulsiveness and distraction leading to errors. Efficiency is achieved by a support on visual memory and the creation of a structured environment to support attention and self -control.

#### Peculiarities
*   **Pace:** Variable. A quick start due to impulsiveness, but an imminent focus loss and slowing, if necessary, concentration.
*   **Perception:** Visual. It better assimilates information through vision (diagrams, images, lists) than by ear.
*   **Attention:** Unstable. Capable of short -term concentration (5-10 minutes), but then attention is depleted. Long work on one task causes difficulties.

#### Supporting factors
*   **Visual supports:** Short instructions-images, checklists, color release of key.
*   **Step -by -step instructions:** Missing tasks into simple, consistent actions.
*   **Clear rhythm:** Working with a timer (10-15 minutes), short physical breaks.
*   **The possibility of movement:** Permission to use a small item (for example, expander) to relieve voltage.

#### Interviewing factors
*   **Multitasking:** Instructions that include several tasks reduce efficiency.
*   **Long oral explanations:** Fast loss of attention with audio instructions.
*   **Distracting environment:** Noise, visual disorder instantly distract attention.
*   **Rush and pressure:** Phrases that stimulate speed provoke rash answers and errors.

### **3. Recommendations**

#### Home environment
*   **Organization of tasks:**
    *   Break work into blocks for 10-15 minutes. Provide 3-5-minute breaks for movement.
    *   Use a timer (kitchen, sand) to visualize time.
    *   Start with the most difficult tasks while the level of attention is high.
*   **Support and motivation:**
    *   Encourage effort and process, and not just the result. For example: "You tried not to be distracted!" or "excellent concentration for 10 minutes."
    *   Together draw up a visual work plan: "1. Mathematics (3 examples). 2. Break. 3. Reading (1 page)."
*   **Attention limit:** The optimal period of continuous concentration on the educational task is 10-15 minutes. Exceeding this limit is counterproductive.

#### School environment
*   **Design of instructions:**
    *   The instructions should be short, cherished, step -by -step. It is advisable to duplicate them visually (board, card).
    *   Example: instead of “Read the text, write out verbs and emphasize the endings in them”, use: “1. Read the first sentence. 2. Find the verb. 3. Write it. 4. Select the end.”
*   **Organization of space:**
    *   Place the student closer to the teacher, far from distracting factors (windows, doors).
    *   The desk should only have the material necessary for the current task.
    *   Turn on interactive elements: demonstration of the answer, work at the board, repeating instructions.
*   **Elements of individualization:**
    *   Visual schedule of a day or lesson on a desk.
    *   Self-control cards for self-control: “Raise your hand”, “Think first, then say”, “Look at the sample”.

### **4. Developing training**

The proposed games and exercises are integrated into everyday life for the development of skills.

*   **Attention:**
    *   **"Find differences"** and labyrinths.
    *   **Graphic dictations:** For example, "one cage to the right, two up."
    *   **Correct test:** Fraightening the set letters in the text for a certain time.
*   **Coordination and self -control (reduction of impulsiveness):**
    *   **"The sea is worried once":** A game of body control and fading by signal.
    *   **"FROMISE-BUGI":** Furious in a given position when stopping music.
    *   **Games with the ball:** Holding and returning the ball by signal (for example, cotton).
*   **Memory (support for a strong side):**
    *   **"Memory" ("Find a couple"):** The development of visual memory and concentration.
    *   **"Repeat the pattern":** Reproduction of a sequence of 3-4 items (cubes, beads).
*   **Rhythm (self -regulation):**
    *   **RIGHTS RIGHTS:** Repetition of a given rhythm with a hand; It is possible to use drums or tambourines.
