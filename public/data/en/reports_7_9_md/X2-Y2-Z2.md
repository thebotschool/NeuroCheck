# Cognitive profile. Age: 7–9 years

This report offers a deep view of the cognitive abilities of your student, based on the results of standard neuropsychological tests. The goal is to provide a clear understanding and practical recommendations to support its development both at home and at school.

## 1. Review of indicators

*   **Test of visual working memory (CPT):** The memorization of the first and last elements is successful, but the average elements cause difficulties. This indicates the difficulty of holding a large volume of visual information.
*   **Braking Test (Go/No-Go):** There are difficulties with a timely stop of action during the ban. This indicates a tendency to impulsive reactions.
*   **Test for stability of attention (TCP):** Attention is unstable, concentration on the same tasks is difficult. There is rapid fatigue and distraction, especially with monotony of the task.

## 2. The guide for the success of your student

This is a profile developed for a better understanding of how your schoolboy perceives information and studies.

### Key features

*   **Pace:** Uneven. There may be haste due to impulsiveness, replaced by slowdown in case of uncertainty or incomplete memorization of parts.
*   **Perception:** Visual-box. It is most effectively based on clear visual anchors (for example, the first word, a bright illustration). Excess parts overload.
*   **Focus of attention:** Throbbing. Attention is actively at the beginning of the task, but is quickly exhausted. It is required to "restart" through a change in activity or a short pause.

### Supporting factors

*   **Step -by -step instructions:** Savior of large tasks into small, understandable steps.
*   **Visual anchors:** Placing key information at the beginning or end of the page/Instruction.
*   **The principle of "stop - think - do":** The introduction of a short pause before the answer or action.
*   **Rhythm and timers:** Using a timer for short work sessions (10-15 minutes), rhythmic settings for configuration.
*   **Clear formulations:** One sentence is one thought.

### Interviewing factors

*   **Multitasking:** The requirement of the simultaneous performance of several actions or quick, unprepared switching.
*   **Long monotonous tasks:** For example, the same type of exercises or prolonged reading without interruptions.
*   **Information overload:** Excess text or images, complex explanations.
*   **Noise and chaos:** The difficulty of filtering external stimuli.
*   **Rush:** The requirement of an immediate response.

## 3. Recommendations

### For the house

*   **Homework organization:**
    *   Separate large tasks into 2-3 stages of 10-15 minutes. Provide short breaks (get up, drink water).
    *   Use the kitchen timer: "15 minutes for mathematics, then 5 minutes of rest."
    *   The workplace should be ordered: on the table only necessary for the current task.
*   **Support and encouragement:**
    *   Mark efforts and ability to think the action, not just the result.
    *   Instead of "do it faster!" Use: "Let's pause and check the plan."
    *   Help find a personal strategy of memorization: "Why is it more convenient to start - with the first or with the last example?"

### For school

*   **The wording of tasks:**
    *   Provide instructions on the principle of "one instruction at a time."
    *   Use checks or numbered lists for multi-stage tasks.
    *   Place the schoolchild closer to the board and the teacher, remotely from distracting factors (windows, doors).
*   **Lesson structuring:**
    *   At the beginning of the lesson, voice the short plan: "First we repeat, then write, we will play at the end."
    *   Alternate activities every 15–20 minutes (letter, oral answer, work with visual material, short warm -up).
    *   For rules and instructions, use the "effect of the edge": place the main idea by the first or last.
*   **Elements of an individual educational route (IOM):**
    *   It is recommended to provide the possibility of short "motor" pauses (for example, the distribution of notebooks).
    *   It may be necessary to adapt the volume of tasks (for example, 7 indicative examples instead of 10).

## 4. Recommended training

These exercises and games will help to develop the necessary skills. Do 10-15 minutes daily in a playful way.

### For attention and memory

*   **"Find a couple" (memorial):** Start with 4-5 pairs of cards, gradually increasing the number.
*   **"Graphic dictations":** Drawing according to oral instructions ("one cage to the right, two up").
*   **"Corrective test":** In a small text, cross out all the letters "A" or circle all "O".

### For self -regulation

*   **"The sea is worried once":** A classic game of the ability to freeze by signal.
*   **"Edible-universal":** A game with the ball, developing a quick reaction and braking of a wrong answer.
*   **"Repeat the rhythm":** The exact repetition of the torn rhythm. He teaches you to listen, concentrate and control movements.
*   **"You go quieter - you will continue":** The movement until the host is looking, and fading when turning.
