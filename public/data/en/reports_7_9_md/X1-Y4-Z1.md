# 2. The guide for the success of your student

## Features of training

*   **Pace:** Variable. Sustainable concentration on long -term tasks. The tendency to haste and errors with quick solutions or the need to restrain himself.
*   **Perception:** Priority of visual information. Effectively assimilates schemes, pictures, algorithms. Oral instructions require visual support.
*   **Focus of attention:** Stable, but prone to impulses. Distraction is not due to boredom, but because of the desire to act, ahead of the completion of the instructions.

## Support: What is effective

*   Visual clues: memorization cards, color discharge, diagrams, Mayind cards.
*   Step -by -step instructions: detailed algorithms of actions (for example, to solve problems).
*   Clear rhythm: predictability reduces anxiety, increases productivity.
*   Promotion of the plan: Joint discussion of the sequence of actions.
*   Stop signals: conditional gestures or words for a conscious pause before the reaction.

## Obstacles: What makes it difficult

*   Multivativity: Focus on one task at a time. Avoid simultaneously performing several actions.
*   Oral instructions without support: partial assimilation without visual reinforcement.
*   Fast change of rules: it takes time to adapt to new instructions.
*   High -speed competitions: stimulate impulsiveness, increase the risk of errors.

# 3. Practical recommendations

## Home environment

*   **How to do home:**
    *   Savior tasks: short blocks (15-20 minutes of work, 5 minutes break). Use the timer.
    *   Priority: Start with complicated when the concentration is maximum.
    *   Control: Use checklist checks to track the stages of execution.
*   **How to support:**
    *   Approval: focus on attentiveness and deliberation, and not at speed.
    *   Work on errors: Analyze calmly as a joint solution of the problem.
*   **Duration of concentration:** 15-20 minutes, then a short break (physical activity).

## School environment

*   **How to formulate tasks:**
    *   Creativity: clear formulations with visual supports (sample, algorithm).
    *   Step -by -step: instructions to give one step, waiting for execution.
*   **How to structure:**
    *   Location: Closer to the teacher, away from distracting factors.
    *   Structure: Algorithm cards for standard tasks.
*   **Style:** Calm, predictable. Avoid speed competition.
*   **Individual route:** Consider additional time, drafts, visual timers, reminders on the desk.
