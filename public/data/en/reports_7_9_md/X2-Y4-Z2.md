# Cognitive assessment report
### Age: 7–9 years
_Complex rating CPT, Go/No-Go, TCP_

The Neurocheck report determines the key cognitive features of your student. It reveals the strengths and areas for development, offering specific recommendations for effective and comfortable training.

## 1.

*   **Test of visual working memory (CPT):** The tasks of visual working memory are revealed by difficulties in keeping exact details. At the same time, the student remembers the initial and final elements well, which is his strength.

*   **Braking Test (Go/No-Go):** The test indicates the challenges in the control of impulses and the quick decision -making. This affects the compliance with the rules and the timely stop of actions.

*   **Test for stability of attention (TCP):** Sustainability of attention is reduced when performing monotonous tasks. Fast fatigue and distraction are noted, which is characteristic of the current stage of development, but requires support.

## 2. The guide for the success of your student

### Peculiarities

*   **Pace:** The pace of work is unstable. Impulsiveness is possible at the beginning of tasks or deceleration due to uncertainty/fatigue.
*   **Perception:** Preference is given to visual information: visual images and short, clear instructions. Long oral explanations are perceived worse.
*   **Focus of attention:** Attention is unstable. It is difficult to ignore external stimuli. The focus is easily lost with monotonous work.

### Support

*   **Consistent instructions:** One task, one instruction. Avoid many instructions at the same time.
*   **Visual supports:** Use schemes, stickers, color selection. Apply a visual schedule of a day or lesson.
*   **Rhythm and predictability:** A clear daily routine, alternation of activity and rest (for example, 15/5 minutes) stabilizes attention and reduces anxiety.
*   **The effect of the edge:** Extract the first and last items in the lists or instructions for better memorization.

### Obstacles

*   **Multitasking:** Complex, simultaneous requests overload the planning system.
*   **Long -term oral explanations:** Attention is quickly lost. Interactivity and visual support are required.
*   **Rush, lack of warning:** Sudden changes in activities or requirements cause confusion.
*   **Sensory overload:** Noise, disorder, accumulation of people exhaust the resource of attention.

## 3. Recommendations

### For the house

#### Homework
*   Missing homework into short blocks (15–20 minutes) is a peak of productive concentration.
*   Enter short physical breaks between blocks: jumping, stretching, water balance.
*   Use a visual timer (sand clock) to track time.
*   Start with the most difficult or least attractive tasks while the energy level is high.

#### Support
*   Approve the effort, not just the result. Example: "You tried to concentrate perfectly!"
*   Provide a calm, predictable environment. Before starting classes, remove all distracting items from the table.

#### Communication
*   Establish eye contact before the instructions. Make sure you are listening.
*   Ask the child to retell the task to test understanding.

### For school

#### Tasks
*   Formulate the tasks briefly, clearly, one at a time.
*   Record the main stages of the task on the student’s board or personal card.
*   Determine the clear criteria for the implementation: "Solve 5 examples from the first column."

#### Space
*   Plant the child closer to the teacher, away from distracting zones (window, door).
*   Allow short motor breaks (class help) if fatigue is noticeable.

#### Individual route
Consider the adaptation of the educational process. This may include:
*   Additional time for tests.
*   The use of algorithmic submarines.
*   Permission to use noise -disposable headphones during independent work.

## 4. Recommended training

The proposed games and exercises are integrated into everyday life, developing key skills in a fascinating form.

### Attention

*   **"Corrective tests":** Cross out the letter on the magazine page. Start with 1-2 minutes.
*   **"Find the differences":** Classic for the development of concentration and visual attention.
*   **Board games:** “Dobble”, “drum”, “high -speed caps” develop a rapid visual reaction.

### Coordination and control

*   **Games with the ball:** Casting with cotton, target throws, maintenance.
*   **"The sea is worried once":** He trains brake control.
*   **Obstacle course:** Creep, jumping, passage under objects for the development of coordination and body scheme.

### Memory

*   **"Memory" (find a couple):** Effectively develops visual working memory.
*   **"What has changed?":** Remember the items on the table, then determine the changes after removal/movement of one.
*   **Sequences:** The game "I go to the store and buy ..." for training consistent memorization.

### Rhythm

*   **Repetition of rhythm:** Cut the/set a simple rhythm. The child repeats. Gradually complicate.
*   **Poems and songs:** The rhythm contributes to the organization of speech and attention.
