### **Cognitive assessment report**
**Attention + Braking + Memory (7–9 years)**

This report reveals the cognitive profile of your student. It contains practical recommendations for effective and comfortable training at home and at school.

## 1. Cognitive profile

*   **Attention (CPT):** Sustainable concentration on the task is a key strong side. This is a reliable basis for learning.
*   **Braking (Go/No-Go):** A tendency to impulsive actions is noted. The reaction is sometimes ahead of thinking about what can lead to hasty mistakes.
*   **Visual memory:** Excellent visual working memory. Information is absorbed clearly (images, diagrams) and kept even when distracting.

## 2. The guide for the success of your student

### Individual characteristics

*   **Pace:** A combination of deep concentration and a tendency to hasty answers.
*   **Perception:** The optimal assimilation of information through visual channels: diagrams, images, algorithms. Oral instructions can be less effective.
*   **Focus:** Attention is stable, but sensitive to internal impulses. The ability to deep immersion is combined with the risk of distraction to sudden ideas.

### Which helps

*   **Pause principle:** Teaching micro-pause before action or response.
*   **Visual supports:** The use of checklists, algorithms and the release of key points.
*   **Step -by -step approach:** Decomposition of complex tasks for controlled stages.

### What interferes

*   **Competitive pace:** Situations requiring quick answers can provoke impulsive errors.
*   **Long oral instructions:** The risk of an incomplete listening and a premature start of the task.
*   **Multitasking:** The simultaneous performance of several actions overloads the control of attention. Focus on one task is preferable at a time.

## 3. Recommendations

### For the house

#### Organization of homework

*   Start with the most difficult tasks using the peak of concentration.
*   Use the timer: 15–20 minutes of work, then 3-5 minutes of active break. This helps to maintain focus.
*   Create a visual plan of the day with checkboxes for the mark of the tasks performed. This strengthens a sense of control and completeness.

#### Support and motivation

*   Praise for attentiveness and accuracy, and not for speed.
*   In case of mistakes due to haste: discuss the situation without condemnation, focusing on the strategy of thinking before action.

### For school

#### Formulation of tasks

*   Give the time to think before the answer. For example: "I will ask a question. Consider the answer within a minute, then I will ask ..."
*   Work out the rule of the raised hand and the expectation of the teacher’s permission to answer. This trains the necessary pause.

#### Structuring information

*   Use visual means: lists, schemes, color release on the board and in materials.
*   Place the student closer to the teacher and the board to minimize the abstracts.

#### Individual approach

*   A special educational route is not required. Sufficiently point pedagogical support.
*   It is recommended to use visual supports, a draft for fixing the first thought and sufficient time for thinking.

## 4. Recommended training

These exercises contribute to the development of self -control and strengthening cognitive strengths.

### For attention and memory

*   **Corrector:** The task is to delete the set letters on the text page. Gradual complication.
*   **Memory:** Classic game with paired cards for training visual memory.
*   **What has changed?:** Remember the location of 5-7 items. Then change one element (removal, addition, movement). The goal is to find a change.

### For braking and coordination

*   **Frozen!:** Move to the music. Freeze instantly when it stops it.
*   **Edible-universal:** A game with a ball requiring a quick reaction (catch) or its braking (beat off).
*   **Rhythmic exercises:** The exact repetition of the proposed rhythms with the palms. Develops a connection between hearing, control and action.
