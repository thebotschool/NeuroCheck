### Cognitive assessment report

Memorization. Control. Attention. (Age: 7–9 years)

This report reveals the potential of your child. He illuminates his strengths and areas that require support. Our recommendations will help to make training and everyday life more effective and comfortable.

---

### 1. Brief analysis

*   **Mercing of attention:** The child is quickly tired and distracted from monotonous tasks. Support is required in saving the focus.
*   **Movement control:** Some asymmetry in the work of the hemispheres is noted. This can affect the simultaneous performance of tasks requiring coordination (for example, listen and write).
*   **Remoning and planning:** Information is absorbed fragmented. Difficulties with reproducing multi -step instructions and holding an action plan.

<!-- page-break -->

### 2. The guide for the success of your student

#### Individual characteristics

*   **Pace:** Leisurely. It takes time to adapt and process information. Productivity is higher in a calm, measured environment.
*   **Perception:** Visual. Information is better absorbed through diagrams, images, color discharge, short videos. Oral instructions without a visual support are less effective.
*   **Attention:** Unstable. Productive focus is limited to 10-15 minutes, then a change of activity or a short break is needed.

#### Effective approaches

*   **Step -by -step instructions:** Clear algorithms of action.
*   **Visual supports:** Cards, schemes, plans.
*   **Activity:** Short physical pins, rhythmic exercises.
*   **Singlety:** Concentration in one case.
*   **Predictability:** The understandable structure of classes.

#### Factors that reduce productivity

*   **Multitasking:** Overloads attention.
*   **Long oral instructions:** It is difficult to remember.
*   **Distracting factors:** Noise, excess objects.
*   **Rush and pressure:** Block thinking.
*   **Monotone:** Causes quick fatigue.

<!-- page-break -->

### 3. Recommendations

#### Home environment

*   **Job:**
    *   Savior tasks into 15-minute blocks. Take 5-minute movement breaks. Use the timer.
    *   Create a visual class of classes: "1. Mathematics (15 min)", "2. Rest." Mark completed points.
    *   Start with more complex tasks while the energy level is high.
*   **Support:**
    *   Mark efforts, not just the result: "You tried very hard!"
    *   Develop strategies for memorization: grouping, visualization (icons).
*   **Attention:**
    *   Consider the restriction of productivity in 10-15 minutes.
    *   Before starting classes, perform coordination exercises (see section 4).

#### Educational process (for teachers)

*   **Instructions:**
    *   Crusting the tasks of specific steps. Example: "Read, emphasize, write down."
    *   Give the instructions one step.
*   **Materials:**
    *   Use visual supports: diagrams, color, drawings.
    *   Alternate types of activity every 15–20 minutes.
    *   Provide minimal distraction in the class (location of the desk).
*   **Individualization:**
    *   Provide extra time for control.
    *   Allow tips (cards with algorithms).
    *   Make sure the child understood the task before the start of it.

### 4. Recommended training

These exercises will strengthen attention, memory and interhemispheric interaction. Turn them in the daily routine for 5-10 minutes.

*   **Attention and control:**
    *   **Correct test:** Refusing a given letter in the text.
    *   **Graphic dictation:** Drawing on cells according to the instructions.
    *   **Find the differences:** Classic concentration exercise.

*   **Coordination and interhemispheric interaction:**
    *   **Kulak-Robe Ladon:** Alternate change of hand positions. Performed by one, then with both hands.
    *   **Mirror drawing:** Simultaneous symmetrical drawing with both hands.
    *   **Games with the ball:** The transfer of the ball, including crossing the hands or cotton.

*   **Memory and planning:**
    *   **The game "Memory":** Search for paired images.
    *   **Repeat me:** Reproduction of the sequence of movements.
    *   **What has changed?** Determination of changes in a number of objects.
