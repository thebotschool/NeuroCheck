# Cognitive profile: individual report

The report is developed for parents and teachers. The goal is to identify the strengths of the student and zones for development, creating an optimal environment for learning.

---

## 1. A review of abilities

*   **CPT: memory and sequence**
    Information is absorbed well. Removing order or sequence of elements causes difficulties.
*   **Go/No-Go: Reaction and Control**
    Impulsive actions are manifested. It is more difficult to keep attention in conditions of choice between action and its absence.
*   **TCP: Focus and endurance**
    Attention is unstable. Fast fatigue and distraction in monotonous tasks.

<!-- page-break -->

## 2. The guide for the success of the student

### Individual approach

*   **The pace of work**
    Uneven. The child can start quickly, but then tires and slows down, especially with monotonous tasks.
*   **Perception of information**
    Visual and step -by -step information is best absorbed. Long oral instructions are perceived with difficulty.
*   **Concentration of attention**
    The focus is unstable, easily distracted. The ability to concentrate is reduced after 5-10 minutes of intensive work.

### Supporting factors

*   **Step -by -step instructions**
    Miss tasks into specific, small steps.
*   **Visual supports**
    Use cards, color allocation, timers for clarity.
*   **Alternation of activity**
    Short work cycles (for example, 10 minutes of work, 5 minutes of activity).
*   **Predictable rhythm**
    A clear sequence of actions helps the student tune in and control himself.

### Calls

*   **Multitasking**
    Simultaneous listening and performing other actions is difficult.
*   **Long oral instructions**
    Overload memory and attention.
*   **Noise and distraction**
    External irritants reduce the level of attention.
*   **Monotone**
    Long -term performance of the same type of tasks leads to fatigue and an increase in errors.

## 3. Practical recommendations

### For the house

*   **Homework: Organization**
    *   Miss homework into short blocks (10-15 minutes) with active breaks.
    *   Use a timer to visualize the working hours.
    *   Start with the most difficult tasks while the concentration is maximum.
*   **Support and motivation**
    *   Organize a quiet, permanent workplace without distracting items.
    *   Praise not for the result, but for efforts and the process: “You tried very hard!”, “Great that you could concentrate for 10 minutes!”.
    *   Discuss the action plan before starting a difficult task: "What will you do first? What then?"

### For school

*   **Training material: feed**
    *   Give the instructions one at a time. Make sure the first step has begun before voicing the next.
    *   Miss large tasks into 2-3 small subtasks with clumsy criteria.
    *   Use visual schedules (a lesson plan on the board) and individual caps.
*   **Educational process: structure**
    *   Place the student at the first or second desk, in the center of the class, away from distracting factors (window, door).
    *   Turn on short physicalite or neurohymnastics.
    *   Use paired work where a partner can serve as an example.
*   **Individual route**
    *   It is recommended that Tutor support or consultations with a school psychologist for the development of self -control and the organization of study.
    *   Adapt the volume of tasks without reducing complexity. Better fewer examples, but performed consciously.

## 4. Developing games and exercises

### Attention and self -control

*   **The game "Delay!":** Move to the music, stopping when it is turned off.
*   **Correct samples:** Cross or circle certain letters in the text (start with 1-2 minutes).
*   **Board games:** Games requiring the expectation of the course and observation of others (for example, “Jenga”, “Uno”, simple “walkers”).

### Memory and sequence

*   **The game "Repeat me":** Reproduce the sequences of movements, cotton or sounds (3-4 elements).
*   **"Shop":** Remember and “buy” objects on the list, gradually increasing the number.
*   **Drawing up stories:** Restore the sequence of events in several plot pictures.

### Coordination and movement

*   **Balance exercises:** Walking along the line, stand on one leg (with open, then closed eyes).
*   **Games with the ball:** Throwing, picking the ball in a certain rhythm for coordination of movements and attention.
*   **Rhythmic exercises:** Tapping or polling a given rhythm. You can use a metronome.
