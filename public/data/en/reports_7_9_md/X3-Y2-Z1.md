# Cognitive development report
## Individual profile. Age: 7–9 years

Этот отчёт предлагает детальный взгляд на уникальные способности вашего школьника. Рекомендации, основанные на результатах тестирования, помогут создать оптимальную среду для обучения и развития.

---

## 1. Assessment of key skills

*   **Working memory (CPT):** The child absorbs information well, but its structuring in the correct sequence requires additional attention. It remembers "what", but not always "as in order."
*   **Self-control (Go/No-Go):** The tendency to impulsive actions or answers without sufficient reflection was noted. The development of the skill of an internal pause is required.
*   **Sustainability of attention (TCP):** A high level of concentration on one task with understandable rules and the absence of distracting factors. This is a strong side.

<!-- page-break -->

## 2. The guide for the success of your student

### Individual profile

*   **Pace:** Unevenness is manifested. A deep concentration is possible, but also impulsiveness, especially if it is necessary to inhibition or a quick change in the rules.
*   **Perception:** It more efficiently assimilates information through visual and step -by -step instructions. Abstract or long oral instructions can be difficult.
*   **Focus:** High ability to concentrate on one task. However, the focus can shift if simultaneous pulse control is required or the retention of several steps in the mind.

### Supporting factors

*   **Visual supports:** Check lists, schemes, cards with rules.
*   **Step -by -step instructions:** Issues of one stage of the assignment at a time.
*   **Rule "Stop-signal":** Introduction of a short pause before action or answer.
*   **Predictable rhythm:** A clear daily routine and lesson.
*   **Structured environment:** Minimization of distracting factors at the workplace.

### Obstacles

*   **Multitasking:** The simultaneous performance of several tasks reduces accuracy.
*   **Long oral instructions:** It is difficult to keep in memory and perform.
*   **Sharp change in activity:** Transitions without preliminary preparation.
*   **Noise and chaos:** An unstructured environment enhances impulsiveness.
*   **Time pressure:** Speed ​​tasks can provoke a rush and errors.

<!-- page-break -->

## 3. Practical recommendations

### At home

*   **Homework:**
    *   Miss large tasks into short blocks (15–20 minutes) with interruptions on physical activity.
    *   Use checklists: the child deleted the executed points.
    *   Remove distracting items from the table before starting work.
*   **Promotion:**
    *   Praise for accuracy and following the plan, and not for speed.
    *   With errors due to haste, calmly offer to repeat more slowly.
*   **Attention:** The child is productively concentrated up to 20 minutes. Then a short pause is needed.

### At school

*   **The wording of tasks:**
    *   Instructions should be step -by -step, oral or visual (on the board, card).
    *   Use short, clear formulations.
*   **Lesson structure:**
    *   Support or post a short lesson plan.
    *   Use numb lists for multi -stage tasks.
*   **Accommodation:** Optimally-the first-second desk, away from distracting zones (window, door).
*   **Style style:** Calm, consistent, predictable. It is important to give time for an internal pause before the answer.
*   **Individual route (IOM):** It is recommended that the development of regulatory skills: planning, control of impulses, following the rules. It can be work with a tutor or participation in specialized classes.

## 4. Recommended training

These exercises will help to develop the necessary cognitive skills in a playful way.

### Focus and sequence

*   **Graphic dictations:** Drawing on oral instructions (for example, "one cage to the right, two up").
*   **Repeat the pattern:** Reproduction of a sequence of objects (beads, cubes).
*   **Board games-"Walking":** Develop the ability to follow the rules, wait for the queue and observe order.

### Self -control and rhythm

*   **The game "Delay!" (or "the sea is worried once"):** Training control over the body by signal.
*   **The game "edible is inedible":** Develops a rapid braking reaction.
*   **Rhythmic exercises:** Removing or cooling of a given rhythm for organizing the nervous system.

### Movement and coordination

*   **Games with the ball:** Catch or throw the ball at a given signal (cotton, sound).
*   **Walking along the line:** Improves the concentration and control of movements.
*   **Balanciers and balancing boards:** Develop coordination and balance, which is associated with the functions of self -control.
