# The cognitive profile of the student

## 1. Overview of the results

*   **Visual working memory:** High ability to memorize and retain visual information. Saves the focus even with external distraction. The strength of the training.
*   **Impulse control (Go/No-Go):** Difficulties with the control of impulsive reactions were noted. The tendency to hasty actions and errors caused by inattention.
*   **Sustainability of attention (CPT):** The concentration decreases during monotonous work. The focus is held for up to 10-15 minutes, then attention can weaken.

<!-- page-break -->

## 2. Key to effective training

### Peculiarities

*   **Pace:** Fast, impulsive. A conscious effort is required, which improves the quality of tasks.
*   **Perception:** Mostly visual. Schemes, images, short written instructions are more effective than oral explanations.
*   **Attention:** Oscillating. Deep immersion in the task for up to 10-15 minutes, after which it is possible to distract to external stimuli or inner thoughts.

### Support

*   **Visual supports:** Check lines, color selection, step-by-step schemes.
*   **Step -by -step instructions:** Presenting the tasks in stages. For example: "First, then."
*   **Rhythm and predictability:** Installed routine. The use of timers (work/rest), clear rituals.
*   **Physical activity:** Short breaks for driving (get up, rushing) help to restore attention and reduce impulsiveness.

### Factors that reduce efficiency

*   **Multitasking:** Difficulties with the simultaneous performance of several actions (for example, listen and write). The brain does not hold two streams of information.
*   **Long monologues:** Reducing attention with prolonged oral explanations without visual or interactive support.
*   **Distracting environment:** Visual and audio mess (noise, scattered objects) significantly overloads the system of attention.
*   **Time pressure:** The requirement of the quick performance of tasks leads to impulsive solutions and errors.

<!-- page-break -->

## 3. Practical recommendations

### For the house

*   **Homework:** Divide into short blocks: 15 minutes of work, 5 minutes of active break (without gadgets). Use the timer.
*   **Organization:** Make a visual task plan. Release the finished points to feel control and progress.
*   **Promotion:** Celebrate diligence and attentiveness, not speed. "You tried very hard and checked yourself - that's great!"
*   **Workplace:** Minimize distracting objects. Leave only the necessary for the current task.

### For school

*   **Tasks:** The instructions should be short, even and consistent. Duplicate oral instructions in writing.
*   **Lesson:** Alternate activity every 15-20 minutes. Turn on short physical education.
*   **Accommodation:** Optimal: the first-second desk in the center. Remote from the window/door minimizes distraction.
*   **Innings:** Apply visual materials: schemes, tables, presentations. Make sure of visual contact before giving instructions.
*   **Individual approach:**
    *   Permission to use a personal timer.
    *   Tasks on separate cards.
    *   The possibility of short, quiet discharge in the class during fatigue (as agreed).

## 4. Developing classes

### Attention

*   **Graphic dictations:** Strengthen auditory attention and ability to follow instructions.
*   **"Find the differences":** Develops concentration and detailed perception.
*   **Correct samples:** Ringing or bypassing certain letters in the text. Start with short passages.

### Control and coordination

*   **Figus games:** "The sea is worried", "delay" - develop bodily control and reaction to the signal.
*   **"Edible-universal":** He trains a quick reaction and its inhibition.
*   **Balance:** Exercises on a balance sheet or balancing pillow. Improve the connection of the brain and body, contribute to self -control.

### Memory (fortification)

*   **"Memory":** Effective training of visual memory.
*   **"Repeat the pattern":** Reproduction of the sequence of objects after short viewing.

### Rhythm

*   **Tapping rhythms:** Reproduction of specified rhythms. Promotes synchronization of brain activity. Gradually complicate.
