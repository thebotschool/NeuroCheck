# Your schoolboy. Cognitive profile.

This report in detail represents the strengths and areas of the growth of your student. Recommendations are designed to increase the efficiency and comfort of learning.

---

## 1. Key results

*   **Visual memory:** The exceptional ability to retain and recall visual information, even in conditions of distraction. This is a strong side and a reliable basis for learning.
*   **Self -control:** There is a tendency to impulsive actions. It is difficult to quickly switch between the "action" and "braking" modes, which can lead to errors.
*   **Sustainability of attention:** Concentration on one task for a long time requires effort. Attention is easily distracted, especially with external stimuli or, if necessary, simultaneously monitoring several actions.

## 2. The guide for the success of your student

**Key profile:** The combination of strong visual memory with the need to support the development of self -control and attention. The child absorbs information well, but may experience difficulties with its purposeful use.

### Individual profile

*   **Pace:** Uneven. Impulsive actions or, on the contrary, delays in tasks that require increased self -control are possible.
*   **Perception:** It more effectively assimilates information through visual images, diagrams, algorithms. Visual memory is a key resource.
*   **Focus of attention:** Unstable. Holding the concentration on one task-up to 10-15 minutes. Easily distracted by external stimuli.

### Which helps

*   Visual supports: cards, diagrams, color selection, stickers.
*   Step -by -step instructions: one action at a time, details of steps.
*   Rhythm and predictability: daily routine, timers, pre -according to rituals.
*   Physical breaks: short activity between tasks for restarting attention.

### What interferes

*   Multivativity: simultaneously performing several actions reduces effectiveness.
*   Long oral instructions: reduce the assimilation of information.
*   Noise and chaos: external distracting factors, disorder in the workplace.
*   Pressure and rush: enhance the anxiety and the number of impulsive errors.

<!-- page-break -->

## 3. Recommendations

### Home environment

**Organization of educational space:**
*   Missing tasks into short blocks (10-15 minutes) with 5-minute breaks to physical activity.
*   Apply visual plans and checklists to track progress.
*   Start with the most difficult tasks using the peak of attention.

**Support and motivation:**
*   Mark the efforts, not only the final result.
*   Analyze errors together, focusing on the process, and not on criticism.

**Workplace:**
*   Provide a quiet, clean workplace. Take out extra objects.
*   The "screen detox" is recommended an hour before classes and sleep.

### School environment

**Task wording:**
*   The principle of "one step is one action."
*   Duplicate oral instructions with visual supports on the board.

**Lesson and space:**
*   Optimal place: the first desk in the center to minimize abstracts.
*   Use the visual schedule of the lesson.
*   Work in tandem with an organized student may be useful.

**Individual adaptation:**
*   Support for the development of self -control. Consultation of a school psychologist or neuropsychologist is recommended.
*   Possible adaptations: extra time on control, reduction in the volume of the same tasks, the use of algorithmic cards.

## 4. Recommended training

These exercises develop attention and self -control, using the stronger side - visual perception.

### For attention

*   **Graphic dictations:** Develop auditory attention and retention of the sequence.
*   **"Find the differences":** Teaches concentrations on details and stability of attention.

### For bodily control

*   **Games for "dead":** Direct training of braking movements by signal.
*   **Balance exercises:** Walking along the line, stand on one leg. Physical control contributes to cognitive.

### For memory and rhythm

*   **"Piggy bank of movements":** Repetition and extension of the sequence of movements. Develops motor memory.
*   **Rhythmic games:** Tapping or cooling the specified rhythm. Develops a sense of time and reduces impulsiveness.
