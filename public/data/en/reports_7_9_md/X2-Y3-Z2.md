### **Cognitive assessment report**
**Memory + inhibition + attention (age: 7–9 years)**

This report provides key data on the cognitive features of your student. This is not a diagnosis, but a tool for understanding an individual style of learning and optimizing the environment for its development.

---

### **1. Review of tests**

*   **Visual working memory (CPT):** The child demonstrates the ability to effectively remember the initial and final elements in a series of images. Holding a large volume of visual information remains a growth zone.
*   **Braking (Go/No-Go):** A high level of self -control when working with a leading hand. The development of coordination and interaction of the cerebral hemispheres requires attention.
*   **Sustainability of attention (TCP):** Maintaining concentration on the same tasks represents certain difficulties. Attention is reduced with prolonged monotonous activity.

<!-- page-break -->

### **2. The profile of the student**

#### **Individual approach**

*   **Pace:** Work can be fast and confident in the clarity of the task, or slow and careful in a collision with new or complex information.
*   **Perception:** The optimal assimilation of information is achieved through **Visual agents** (schemes, illustrations, color selection) and **Active interaction** (Practical actions, rhythmic exercises).
*   **Attention:** Periods of deep concentration are limited **10-15 minutes**. After that, a short pause or a change in activity is needed.

#### **Effective strategies**

*   Visual supports: diagrams, drawings, checklists.
*   Step -by -step instructions: dividing complex tasks into 2-3 simple steps.
*   Rhythm and movement: synchronized actions (pops, walking) during memorization. Exercises involving both hands.
*   The emphasis on the structure: a clear designation of the beginning and summing up at the end of the task.

#### **Factors requiring attention**

*   Multivativity: simultaneous listening and recording, or observing the board and notebook.
*   The same tasks: long exercises without interruptions.
*   Information overload: large volumes of visual data (slides, texts).
*   Oral instructions without visual or recorded support.

<!-- page-break -->

### **3. Recommendations**

#### **For the house**

*   **Homework organization:**
    *   Apply the principle of interval work: 15 minutes of focused activities, then 5 minutes of active break (physical exercises).
    *   Create a short plan (2-3 points) before the start of the task: “Solve 3 examples. Emphasize the letters. Rest. "
    *   Use strengths of memory: when memorized, pay special attention to the beginning and end of the material.
*   **Support and encouragement:**
    *   Avoid criticism for "inattention." Instead, offer strategies: “We will divide it into parts?”, “Remember how we remembered it?”
    *   Encourage classes developing two -handed coordination: modeling, designers, playing instruments, help in everyday life (cooking).
*   **Attention resource:** Keep in mind that the productive concentration is 10-15 minutes.

#### **For school**

*   **The wording of tasks:**
    *   Miss instructions into short, sequential steps.
    *   Duplicate oral tasks visually: recordings on the board, algorithms on the cards.
*   **Structuring information:**
    *   Place key information (rules, examples) at the beginning or end of the presentation/material.
    *   Use color, frames, schemes for visual organization of material.
*   **Effective presentation:**
    *   Alternate activity formats every 10-15 minutes: explanation, group work, written exercises.
    *   Turn on the motor elements: physical education, answers using cards.
*   **Individual educational route:**
    *   Permission to use a ruler or finger when reading.
    *   Providing cards with a response plan or a solution algorithm.
    *   The optimal placement of the student to minimize distracting factors (closer to the teacher, away from the window).

### **4. Developing exercises**

*   **Attention:**
    *   **"Corrective tests":** Refusing a given letter in the text (2-3 minutes).
    *   **"Find the differences":** Training of concentration and visual search.
*   **Coordination and interhemispheric interaction:**
    *   **"Crossroads":** Following the elbow of the opposite knee (1-2 minutes daily).
    *   **"Drawing with two hands":** Simultaneous drawing of symmetrical figures on the sheet.
*   **Memory:**
    *   **"Memory":** Search for paired images.
    *   **"What has changed?":** Removing 5-7 items, determination of changes after one movement/removal.
*   **Rhythm:**
    *   **"Rhythmic echo":** Repetition of a given rhythm with claps or steps.
    *   **Games with the ball:** Bowing the ball in a given rhythm.
