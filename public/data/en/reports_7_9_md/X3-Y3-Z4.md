# Neurocheck: report on cognitive development
## Assessment results: memory, control, attention (7–9 years)

This report describes the cognitive profile of your child, highlighting the strengths and areas for development. The proposed recommendations were created to support the optimal training environment.

### 1. Cognitive profile

*   **Working memory and sequence (CPT).** It assimilates the information well, but experiences difficulties in compliance with order. Support for structuring steps is required.
*   **Self-control and braking (Go/No-Go).** High accuracy of reactions by the leading hand. Synchronization of interhemispheric interaction is necessary.
*   **Distribution of attention (TCP).** It is difficult to hold the focus on several tasks. Fast fatigue with multi -factor attention.

<!-- page-break -->

### 2. Individual learning strategies

**Individual characteristics**
*   **Pace.** The optimal, a calm, measured pace. Hurry depletes self -control, increasing the number of errors.
*   **Perception.** Effectively assimilates visual and step -by -step information. Responds to training through movement and tactile sensations. Long oral instructions are ineffective.
*   **Focus of attention.** Single -channel. Effectively performs one task at a time. Multiple instructions simultaneously lead to overload.

**Support: what works**
*   **Step -by -step instructions.** Clear, short steps, preferably visually decorated (for example, cards).
*   **Visual supports.** Schemes, color coding, timers, short videos.
*   **Rhythm and predictability.** Clear daily routine. Rhythmic exercises for focusing attention.
*   **Bodily involvement.** The use of movement, tactile actions (drawing, modeling, speaking) to strengthen training.

**Obstacles: what is interfering**
*   **Multitasking.** Simultaneous requirements.
*   **Long oral instructions.** Lack of pauses and visual support.
*   **A sharp change in tasks.** Without warning or short pause.
*   **Noise and distracting factors.** Any extraneous irritants.

<!-- page-break -->

### 3. Practical recommendations

**For the house**
*   **Organization of homework**
    *   Divide the tasks into blocks for 15–20 minutes. Turn on short motor breaks.
    *   Use checks or visual plan for each stage. The mark on the completion increases the sense of control.
    *   Start with the first step: "Let's see what is the first step?"

*   **Support and promotion**
    *   Mark the effort and process, and not just the result. "I see how I tried to follow the plan."
    *   With errors due to inattention: "The brain is tired. We will pause, we will return later."

**For school**
*   **The formulation of tasks**
    *   Instruct one step at a time. Wait for the execution before the next step.
    *   Duplicate important oral instructions in writing (board, memo).

*   **Lesson structure and workplace**
    *   The optimal place is the front row, the center, to minimize abstracts.
    *   Use visual materials: intelligence cards, algorithms.

*   **Elements of an individual route**
    *   Cards-assistants with algorithms.
    *   Permission to use a line for reading (focusing).
    *   Additional time for testing, if necessary.

### 4. Developing practices
The following exercises are integrated into everyday life, contributing to the development of key skills in a playful way.

**Attention and memory**
*   **Repeat of sequences.** Build a number of 3-4 items, ask to remember and reproduce order. Gradually complicate.
*   **Graphic dictations.** Exercises such as "cell to the right, two up" develop algorithmic thinking and auditory perception.
*   **Board games-"walks".** They train compliance with the rules, the sequence of moves and concentration.

**Coordination and interaction**
*   **"Lazy eights."** Simultaneous drawing of the sign of infinity with both hands in the air or on paper.
*   **Cross -steps.** Walking in place, touching the opposite knee of the palm.
*   **Modeling and constructing.** Encabise both hands in working with plasticine, clay, designers.

**Rhythm and control**
*   **The game "Delay-Otomory".** Develops the ability to timely inhibition of the action.
*   **Repetition of rhythms.** Set the rhythm with claps, the child repeats.
*   **Balancing exercises.** Walking along the curb, stand on one leg.
