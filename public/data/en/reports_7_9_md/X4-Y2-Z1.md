## Cognitive test report
### Attention, self -control and working memory (7–9 years)

This report analyzes the cognitive features of your student. Recommendations are designed to support its development and make training more effective both at home and at school.

---

### 1. Test results

*   **Working memory (CPT):** Difficulties with the retention of consistent information and a long concentration are noted. Attention is quickly depleted with prolonged or complex tasks.
*   **Self-control (Go/No-Go):** There is a tendency to impulsive actions. Difficulties may arise with the expectation of your turn or restraining an immediate reaction.
*   **Visual attention (TCP):** High concentration in clearly structured tasks with clear rules. This is a strong side: attention is stable in the absence of distracting factors and a clear sense of the task.

## 2. Profile of learning
Understanding the conditions in which the student reaches maximum productivity.

### Individual characteristics

*   **Pace:** The natural pace is fast. Optimally - slow, measured rhythm with pauses for thinking.
*   **Perception:** Visual perception prevails: schemes, images, short lists, color selection. Long oral instructions are less effective.
*   **Attention:** The resource of attention is limited in monotonous or complex tasks. Effective in short, understandable and interesting tasks.

### Optimization of the process

*   **Visualization:** The use of cards with plans, schemes of the sequence of actions, reminders.
*   **Step -by -step approach:** Decomposition of complex tasks for simple, specific steps. For example: "Read the task", "Find the first sentence", "Emphasize the main words."
*   **Pause:** The introduction of a conscious pause before the answer or action. Examples: a short breath-out of the score, the score is up to three.
*   **Rhythm:** The use of a timer for short working intervals. The use of rhythmic actions (for example, tapping) to maintain concentration.

### Distraction factors

*   **Multitasking:** Ineffective. Tasks should be unidirectional.
*   **Hurry and frequent shift:** They can provoke impulsiveness and errors.
*   **External distracting factors:** Noise, disorder exhaust the resource of attention.

## 3. Practical recommendations

### For the home environment

*   **Working process:**
    *   **Intervals:** 15–20 minutes of work, then 5-7 minutes of active break. Use a visual timer.
    *   **Planning:** Make a simple plan of 2-3 points before starting work. Place it in a prominent place.
    *   **Order:** The desktop should be free from distracting items. Only the necessary materials.
*   **Support:**
    *   **Promotion:** Mark efforts, not just the result. For example: "I see how you tried to concentrate!"
    *   **Help:** In case of difficulties, offer a pause and a joint analysis of the problem, avoiding criticism.
*   **Communication:**
    *   For instructions: maintain visual contact, speak slowly, ask for repeat the task.

### For the school environment

*   **Instructions:**
    *   Formulate clearly, briefly, step by step. Duplicate oral tasks on the board or card.
    *   Location: the first-second desk, far from distracting factors (window, door).
*   **Lesson structure:**
    *   **Algorithms:** Use Algorithm cards for typical tasks.
    *   **Pause:** Make a short pause before you give time to think and restraint the impulse.
    *   **Dynamics:** Alternate activity, maintaining predictability and structure.
*   **Individual approach (IOM):**
    *   With stable difficulties, consider the elements of IOM for the development of self -regulation and attention, attracting a school psychologist or defectologist.

## 4. Recommended training
Games and exercises for the development of key skills in everyday life.

### For the development of attention

*   **"Corrective test":** Crossing a certain letter on the page in 2 minutes.
*   **"Find the differences":** Exercises for concentration and visual analysis.
*   **Labyrinths:** Passing with the eyes, then with a pencil.

### For self -control and coordination

*   **"The sea is worried once":** Training of freezing and bodily control.
*   **"Edible - inedible":** Development of reaction and braking ability.
*   **"Yes, no say":** Control of automatic speech reactions.

### For memory development

*   **"Memory":** Search for paired cards. Start with 4-6 pairs, gradually increasing the amount.
*   **"I put in a bag ...":** Game of auditory memory with the sequential addition of objects.

### For rhythm and organizing thinking

*   **"Echo":** Repetition of rhythmic patterns (pops, tapping).
*   **Movement to music:** Marching, jumping, simple movements to the beat of clear rhythmic music.
