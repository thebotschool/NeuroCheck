# Cognitive profile. Report

This report offers a detailed analysis of the cognitive features of your student (age group: 7–9 years), focused on attention, self -control and memory. It is designed to help reveal the potential of the child, offering personalized strategies to create an optimal environment of learning and development.

---

## 1. Review of tests

### Test for stability of attention and memory (CPT)
Difficulties with prolonged retention and memorization are noted. There is rapid fatigue and confusion in the sequence of actions, which affects the assimilation of the material.

### Self-control test (Go/No-Go)
The more confident work of the leading hand has been revealed. Tasks that require coordination of both hands or simultaneously performing several actions (for example, listen and write) are difficult.

### Concentration test in simple conditions (TCP)
In short, structured tasks, a high concentration is demonstrated. This indicates the effectiveness of attention in a predictable, calm atmosphere, however, with the complication of tasks, the concentration decreases.

## 2. Student profile

Understanding individual characteristics helps to create optimal conditions for learning and productivity.

### Key features

*   **Pace:** The pace of work is moderate. It decreases with complication of tasks or fatigue. It takes more time to include in the process and processing information.
*   **Perception format:** Information is absorbed more efficiently if it is presented visually and step by step. Audial data without visual support are perceived more complicated.
*   **Focus of attention:** Attention is focused on short, understandable tasks, but it is easily dissipated with multitasking, noise or the need to keep several instructions.

### Success factors

*   **Visual support:** Schemes, algorithms, color selection of key information, short video instructions.
*   **Step -by -step division:** Breaking large tasks into 2-3 simple, understandable steps.
*   **Rhythm and movement:** The use of rhythmic exercises and movements for synchronization of brain activity.
*   **Organized environment:** A quiet workplace without distracting factors.
*   **Predictability:** A clear, unchanging plan of action.

### Obstacles

*   **Multitasking:** Simultaneous oral instructions.
*   **Background noise:** Talk, TV or music significantly reduce concentration.
*   **High pace of the lesson:** Frequent change of topics without enough time for fixing.
*   **Verbal overload:** Long oral explanations without visual support.
*   **Time pressure:** The speed requirement causes anxiety and leads to errors.

## 3. Recommendations

### For the home environment

*   **Homework organization:**
    *   Divide homework into intervals: 15–20 minutes of work, then 5-7 minutes of active break (movement, rest).
    *   Start with the most difficult tasks while the energy level is high.
    *   Together, create a visual plan for completing tasks (for example, on a sticker: read, select, write down).
*   **Support and encouragement:**
    *   Encourage efforts, perseverance and following the plan, and not just the result.
    *   Instead of criticism of errors, analyze: "Where did the plan go down? What was difficult?"
*   **Mercing of attention:**
    *   Realistic concentration time on a difficult task is about 15 minutes. Provide breaks.

### For the educational process

*   **The wording of tasks:**
    *   The instructions should be short, toned, given one at a time (for example: "Open the notebook. Write down the number.").
    *   Duplicate oral instructions in writing (on a board or individual cards).
*   **Lesson structure:**
    *   Break the topics into logical blocks. After each block - a brief test of understanding or practical action (raise a card, draw a diagram).
    *   It is recommended to place a child in the first or second desk in the center to minimize distraction.
*   **Style style:**
    *   Actively use visual materials: schemes, intelligence cards, presentations, physical objects (score sticks).
    *   Involve both hands: bypassing with one hand, emphasizing the other; Symmetric drawing.
*   **Elements of an individual educational route (IOM):**
    *   Give additional time to perform tests.
    *   Allow me to use helping cards with algorithms or rules.

## 4. Training exercises

The proposed exercises are aimed at developing key skills. Daily practice is recommended in a playful way, 10-15 minutes.

### Attention and memory

*   **Graphic dictations:** "Two cells to the right, one up ...". Develop auditory attention and spatial thinking.
*   **The game "What has changed?":** Offer to remember 5-7 items. After the child is turned away, change the location or number of objects.
*   **Memorization of verses with pictures:** Create visual tips (symbols) for each line or word.

### Coordination and interhemispheric interaction

*   **Exercise "Fist-Robric Ladon":** Perform the movements sequentially with each hand, then both at the same time.
*   **Mirror drawing:** Simultaneous drawing of the same figures with both hands (circles, squares).
*   **Games with the ball:** Throwing the ball from hand to hand, adding cotton or other action.

### Bodily control and rhythm

*   **Rhythmic paths:** Removing or tinkling of a given rhythm.
*   **Walking with tasks:** Movement around the room, simultaneously performing intellectual tasks (for example, calling the words to the letter, counting in the reverse order).
