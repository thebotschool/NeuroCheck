# Assessment of cognitive abilities: Individual report

This report is designed to reveal the individual characteristics of the student. Practical recommendations for effective training and targeted support are presented here.

---

### 1. Key insights

*   **Working memory:** Information is absorbed. However, the organization of the sequence of actions requires attention.
*   **Impulsive control:** Differences in performing the tasks of the leading and unaware of a hand indicate the need to develop interhemispheric interaction, especially in visual-motor coordination.
*   **Attention:** There is a tendency to impulsive actions. This can lead to errors due to insufficient pondering, despite the knowledge of the rules.

### 2. The guide for the success of your student

#### Features of development

*   **Dynamic pace:** Quick inclusion in the work is possible, but with the risk of loss of concentration or haste. Maintaining a stable pace requires effort.
*   **The preference of the visual format:** The best assimilation of step -by -step visual instructions. Long oral instructions can be less effective.
*   **Short focus:** Attention is held on simple, brief tasks. Multicomponent tasks or distracting factors can reduce concentration.

#### Effective support

*   **Step -by -step algorithms:** Divide complex tasks into specific, consistent steps.
*   **Visual support:** Use checks, subscription cards, schemes. Select keywords with color.
*   **Structure and rituals:** Introduce clear, repeated actions for setting up to work.
*   **Self -control pauses:** Create moments to check the executed.
*   **Motor activity:** Turn on the tasks requiring hand manipulations.

#### Barriers in training

*   **Multitasking:** The simultaneous performance of several actions reduces productivity.
*   **Long oral instructions:** Oral instructions are perceived fragmented.
*   **Fast switching:** A sharp change in tasks causes confusion.
*   **Distracting environment:** Noise and visual disorder exhaust the resource of attention.
*   **Time restriction:** The pressure of time enhances impulsiveness and increases the number of errors.

### 3. Recommendations

#### Support at home

1.  **Organization of homework**
    *   **Separation of tasks:** Crusting large tasks for short stages with small breaks.
    *   **Focus timer:** Use a timer for concentration (for example, 15 minutes of work, 5 minutes of rest).
    *   **Visual plan:** Use a simple sheet to display 2-3 current steps. Mark completed points.
    *   **Checking understanding:** Before starting work, ask to retell the task in your own words.

2.  **Support and promotion**
    *   **Processing the process:** Mark efforts, self -control, following the plan. Example: "It is fine that you checked everything!"
    *   **Development of attention:** Explain that attention is being trained. The focus on a difficult task is limited (10-15 minutes), then you need a vacation.

#### Support at school

1.  **Formulation and presentation of tasks**
    *   **Visual duplication:** Fix key oral instructions on the board.
    *   **Algorithms:** Provide the assistant cards with a step-by-step plan to complete the tasks.
    *   **One task at a time:** Avoid multitasking in the instructions. Example: “We finish exercise 5. Close the notebooks. We open textbooks on page 20. "

2.  **Organization of the educational process**
    *   **Optimal place:** The first or second desk in the central row to minimize distracting factors.
    *   **Individual approach:**
        *   Additional time for control.
        *   Using a ruler when reading.
        *   Kinesiological pauses.
    *   **Non -verbal communication:** Agree on a conditional sign to return attention without attracting overall attention.

### 4. Recommended training

Targeted training. These exercises develop identified skills. The key is regularity and a positive approach.

*   **For attention and self -control:**
    *   **"FROM ATOMISE":** Develops body control by signal.
    *   **Graphic dictations:** Strengthen concentration and follow the instructions.
    *   **Board games:** Form compliance with the rules and waiting for the queue.

*   **For memory and sequence:**
    *   **"Repeat me":** He trains memorizing the sequence of movements.
    *   **Pictures Stories:** Develops the connectivity of thinking and the sequence of presentation.
    *   **"I put in a bag":** Classical game for holding verbal sequence.

*   **For coordination:**
    *   **Two -handed drawing:** Creating symmetrical patterns with both hands.
    *   **"Fist-Robric Ladon":** A consistent change in the positions of the hand. First separately, then at the same time.
    *   **Games with the ball:** Throwing, juggling, picking up from the wall alternately with the right and left hand.
