# The cognitive profile of the student. Age 7–9 years

This report contains an analysis of the cognitive characteristics of the student. It is intended for parents and teachers, providing information about the strengths and directions for development. The goal is to create an optimal environment for training and progress.

## 1. Analysis of tests

*   **Visual memory (CPT).** The difficulty in holding many details is noted. Strong side: intuitive memorization of the first and last element of the row.
*   **Impulse control (Go/No-Go).** Difficulties are observed with a timely stop. Hasty actions are possible before pondering the instructions.
*   **Focusing attention (TCP).** The tendency to impulsive reactions is confirmed. Actions are often ahead of a complete understanding of the task, which reduces accuracy.

## 2. The guide for the success of the student

### Key features

*   **Pace.** Fast, impulsive, with a tendency to lead. The principle "I do first, then I think."
*   **Perception.** Effectively assimilates clear visual information (diagrams, algorithms). It is overloaded from an excess of disordered visual details.
*   **Attention.** Unstable, subject to breakdowns due to the desire for a quick reaction. The focus shifts from the task of action.

### Support strategies

*   **Visual anchors.** The allocation of the first and last item in tasks or lists. Uses a natural strategy of memorization.
*   **Step -by -step instructions.** One action in one short step.
*   **Pause "Stop".** The ritual of a calm breath-loud before action. Creates a micropauza for understanding.
*   **Rhythm and rituals.** A clear start and completion of work (timer, plan). Helps to tune in.
*   **Short tasks.** It is more effective to perform several small tasks (3 to 5 minutes) instead of one voluminous.

### What interferes with progress

*   **Multitasking.** It is difficult for simultaneous perception and action (for example, listen and write down).
*   **Long oral instructions.** More than two steps can be missed. Only the beginning or end is remembered.
*   **A sharp change in activity.** Without a pause, the child can continue the previous action.
*   **Noise and distraction.** Provoke impulsive reactions, violate concentration.
*   **Long expectation.** It causes stress, can lead to impulsive actions or loss of interest.

## 3. Personal recommendations

### Home environment

*   **Tasks at home.**
    *   Break work into blocks by **15–20 minutes**. Between them take active breaks.
    *   Use a time to visualize time.
    *   Make and write down the work plan: `1. Математика (2 примера). 2. Чтение (1 страница).` Select the first and last item.
*   **Support and motivation.**
    *   Mark **Attentiveness and self -control**, not speed. For example: "How great it is that you thought at first, and then wrote!"
    *   Provide a calm workplace without distracting items and screens.
*   **Concentration of attention.**
    *   Productive attention on one task - no more **15–20 minutes**. Exceeding leads to errors and fatigue.

### School environment

*   **Instructions.**
    *   Let's step by step: “Open a notebook. Write down the number. Look at the assignment one. ”
    *   Use clear verbs of action: “Cover”, “Emphasize”, “Connect”.
*   **Organization of the lesson.**
    *   Duplicate oral instructions visually (board, algorithm on the desk: `1. Прочитай. 2. Подчеркни. 3. Запиши`).
    *   Place the student closer to the teacher, away from distracting factors (window, door).
*   **Communication.**
    *   A friendly, but clear and structured style is effective. Before starting difficult work, say the rules: “Now we are listening to 2 minutes. Hands on the desk. Then I will say what to do. ”
*   **Individual approach.**
    *   Provide assistant cards with typical tasks.
    *   Allow short motor breaks (prolong a pencil, take a test).
    *   On control work: check the understanding of the assignment before completing (“Repeat, what needs to be done?”).

## 4. Developing exercises

These exercises will help strengthen skills that require additional support.

*   **Focus and control.**
    *   **"Crushing-Sreedy."** A quick reaction and braking game.
    *   **"You go quieter - you will continue."** Develops bodily control and the ability to stop.
    *   **Graphic dictations.** They require an attentive hearing, holding the instructions and accurate execution.
*   **Visual memory.**
    *   **"Memory" (find a couple).** Classical game for training visual memory.
    *   **"What has changed?"** Remember 5-7 items. The child turns away, you change one item. The task is to find a change.
*   **Rhythm and movement.**
    *   **"Echo".** You set a simple rhythm (popping, tapping), the child exactly repeats. Complicate gradually.
    *   **Neurohymnastics.** Exercises for multidirectional movements (for example, one hand strokes the stomach, the other knocks on the head).
*   **Awareness of the body.**
    *   **Games for balance.** Stand on one leg for 10 seconds, go along the line.
    *   **Breathing "Ball".** Put your hand on your stomach. On inspiration, the stomach is inflated, on the exhale - is blown away. 3-4 breaths before the task will help concentrate.
