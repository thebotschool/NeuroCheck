### Personal report: Cognitive profile
**Age group: 7–9 years old**

This report is developed for parents and teachers. He will help to understand the strengths of your student and determine the growth zones. The proposed recommendations are aimed at creating an optimal environment for training and development.

---

### 1. Key skills: review

*   **Visual memory:** High level. Your student effectively remembers and reproduces visual information and sequences.
*   **Impulse control:** Develops. There is a tendency to impulsive actions requiring pondering before performing.
*   **Sustainability of attention:** Requires support. The concentration of attention is reduced with prolonged or monotonous tasks, which can lead to distraction.

<!-- page-break -->

### 2. Guide for training

It is important to consider these features when interacting with a student.

#### Key features

*   **Pace:** The natural pace is fast. For effectiveness, intentional slowdown and pauses are important.
*   **Channel for perception:** Visual. Information is better absorbed through diagrams, images, short records and checklists.
*   **Focus of attention:** Intensive, but short. Effective concentration persists for 15-20 minutes, after which an activity or break is required.

#### Learning support

*   **Visual supports:** Check lists, diagrams, drawings, recovery stickers.
*   **Step -by -step instructions:** Tasks are served sequentially, one step at a time.
*   **Structured rhythm:** Short working intervals (15 minutes) with mandatory breaks (5 minutes).
*   **Preliminary pronunciation:** Repetition of instructions by a student for a better understanding.

#### Obstacles

*   **Multitasking:** The performance of several actions simultaneously reduces effectiveness.
*   **Long oral instructions:** Lead to loss of attention.
*   **Rush:** Provokes impulsive reactions.
*   **Distracting factors:** Excess objects and loud sounds exhaust attention.

<!-- page-break -->

### 3. Practical recommendations

#### For the house

*   **Organization of workspace**
    *   Break tasks into blocks (15-20 minutes).
    *   Start with complex tasks using the peak of concentration.
    *   Use a visual timer to track time.
    *   Create a visual plan of the day with a child (for example, mathematics (15 minutes) - rest (5 min) - reading (15 minutes)).
*   **Motivation and environment**
    *   Encourage attentiveness, not speed (for example, “you checked everything - excellent!”).
    *   Provide a clean workplace free from extra items.
    *   Establish contact before the task (eye to eye), convince of attention.

#### For school

*   **Making tasks**
    *   The instructions should be short, cherished, step -by -step.
    *   Duplicate oral instructions visually (for example, page number on the board).
    *   Provide visual algorithms for complex tasks.
*   **Lesson and place organization**
    *   The optimal place is the first desks, the center of the class, minimization of abstracts.
    *   Turn on short breaks (physical education, “Minutes of silence”).
*   **Interaction**
    *   Pause before questions. Offer the rule "count up to three before the answer."
    *   Correctly guide if the student shouts (for example, “raise your hand, please”).
*   **Individual approach**
    *   Meter micro -transit to restore attention.
    *   Use printed checks for tasks.
    *   Encourage maintaining attention and compliance with the rules.

### 4. Developing classes

These games and exercises will help to develop key skills in an accessible form.

*   **For attention**
    *   “Find the differences” and “Labyrinths”: develop concentration.
    *   Graphic dictations: training auditory attention and following instructions.
*   **To control pulses**
    *   Board games: teach to wait their turn.
    *   The game "Delay!": Develops a quick, but controlled response.
    *   The game is "edible-universal": he trains the inhibition of the reaction.
*   **For memory and rhythm**
    *   Memory game: supports visual memory.
    *   Placing rhythms: improves internal organization and rhythm.
*   **For bodily control**
    *   Balance exercises: develop bodily control related to attention.
