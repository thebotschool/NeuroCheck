# Neurocheck: Cognitive profile
## Assessment of attention, memory and self -control (7–9 years)

This report is an analysis of the results of cognitive tests. It is designed to propose practical recommendations for the effective support of the student at home and in the educational environment.

### 1. Evaluation results

*   **CPT (memory and attention):** It is difficult for a student to hold large volumes of information and perform multi -stage instructions. Attention is quickly depleted, especially with fatigue or distraction.
*   **Go/No-Go (self-control):** Demonstrates a high level of self -control and the ability to follow clear rules with a clear task. This is a strong side - the possibility of concentration in suitable conditions.
*   **TCP (complex attention):** It experiences difficulties in situations requiring simultaneous tracking of several objects and a quick reaction. In conditions of multitasking, attention is scattered, leading to errors.

**General conclusion:** With strong potential for self -control, the student needs an external structure and support in the organization of activities. His working memory and attention are easily overloaded.

### 2. Profile of learning

Understanding the characteristics of the student is the key to effective learning.

#### Individual characteristics
*   **Pace:** Leisurely. It takes time to comprehend and include in the task. Haster leads to errors.
*   **Perception:** Visual-kinesthetic. It better assimilates information through visual and tactile channels (schemes, cards, algorithms) than through oral explanations.
*   **Focus:** Capable of deep concentration on one task. The focus easily loses when trying to multitasking or abundance of verbal information.

#### Efficiency support
*   **Step -by -step instructions:** The principle of "one step is one action."
*   **Visual supports:** Check lists, schemes, cards with rules, highlighting keywords.
*   **Predictability:** Clear rituals of the beginning/end of work, a pre -known plan.
*   **Singlety:** Separation of activity: "Now we write," "now we are listening."
*   **Brevity:** Capacious formulations instead of lengthy explanations.

#### Obstacles in learning
*   **Multitasking:** Complex instructions simultaneously create difficulties.
*   **Information noise:** Long explanations, an excess of the text, a mess in the workplace.
*   **High pace:** Sharp shifts of activities without pauses.
*   **Uncertainty:** Lack of a clear plan of action or criteria.

### 3. Recommendations

#### For the house
1.  **Organization of workspace:**
    *   Divide the tasks into blocks for 15–20 minutes. Provide short breaks (2-5 minutes) between blocks.
    *   Create a working area without distracting factors. On the table is only necessary for the current task.
    *   Use visual schedule and checklist. A schoolboy can mark the executed points.
2.  **Support and motivation:**
    *   Mark efforts and process, and not just the result. Example: "Your concentration is impressive!", "You follow the plan!"
    *   Before a difficult task, help you draw up an action plan. Fix it in writing or visually.

#### For school
1.  **Setting tasks:**
    *   Formulate the instructions sequentially: "One instruction at a time." Avoid multi -stage instructions.
    *   Use written instructions (on the board, card). Highlight the key verbs (`ПОДЧЕРКНИ`, `ВЫПИШИ`, `РЕШИ`).
2.  **Organization of the process:**
    *   **Seat:** A place in the 1-2 row reduces distracting factors.
    *   **Visualization:** Offer a schematic lesson plan. Use a timer to control time.
    *   **Individual approach:**
        *   Provide personal algorithms for performing typical tasks.
        *   Allow me to use a reading line.
        *   Stay time for testing by 15–20%.

### 4. Practical exercises

The presented games and exercises will support the development of the necessary skills. It is recommended to perform them regularly, for 5-10 minutes daily, in a playful way.

#### For attention and self -control
*   **Graphic dictations:** Drawing on cells under dictation. Develops auditory attention, following the instructions.
*   **Find the differences:** He trains concentration on the details.
*   **Forbidden movement:** Repetition of movements, except in advance. Develops brake control.

#### For memory and memorization
*   **Memory (find a couple):** A classic game to remember the location of cards.
*   **I put in a bag ...:** Consistent addition and repetition of objects in a circle.

#### For coordination and rhythm
*   **Neurohymnastics: "Fist-Robric Ladon"** Alternate rhythmic change of position of the hand on the table (one, then with both hands).
*   **Cooling rhythm:** The exact repetition of the given rhythm with claps. Gradual complication.
