# Cognitive assessment report

### Your path to understanding and developing the potential of a student.

---

### 1. Test results

Assessment of cognitive functions in areas of attention, self -control and memory.

*   **Sustainability of attention (CPT):** Difficulties in maintaining prolonged concentration and rapid fatigue have been revealed.
*   **Self-control (Go/No-Go):** The tendency to impulsive actions, difficulties with the inhibition of spontaneous reactions were noted.
*   **Visual working memory:** The limited volume of working memory, which complicates the retention of several elements of information and planning the sequence of actions.

### 2. The profile of the student

Key aspects of perception and interaction with the educational process.

#### Individual characteristics

*   **Pace:** Uneven, takes time for adaptation. Quick exhaustion leads to a decrease in efficiency.
*   **Perception:** Preferably visual and step -by -step presentation of information. The audial channel can be overloaded.
*   **Focus of attention:** Short -term, easily dissipates under the influence of external or internal stimuli. It is difficult to filter out secondary information.

#### Which contributes

*   **Visual supports:** Check lists, diagrams, intelligent cards, highlighting keywords. Work plan as a navigator.
*   **Step -by -step instructions:** Separation of large tasks into small, controlled stages.
*   **Timer:** Using a timer for short, focused working segments.
*   **Predictability:** A clear and understandable plan of a day or lesson reduces anxiety and promotes concentration.
*   **Optimal environment:** A quiet workplace with a minimum of distracting factors.

#### What makes it difficult

*   **Multitasking:** The simultaneous performance of several actions reduces the quality of each.
*   **Long oral instructions:** The risk of losing a significant part of the information.
*   **Distracting noise:** Visual or audio stimuli deplete the resource of attention.
*   **Rush and pressure:** They provoke anxiety and impulsiveness, negatively affecting the quality of work.

### 3. Recommendations

Practical steps to support the house and at school.

#### For the house

*   **Homework:**
    *   Divide the tasks into blocks of 15–20 minutes with breaks (5-10 minutes).
    *   Create checklists of tasks; Independent deletion of the executed develops a sense of control.
    *   Start with the most complex objects while the level of attention is maximum.
*   **Support:**
    *   Mark efforts and process, and not just the result.
    *   Analyze errors together, without conviction.
*   **Organization:**
    *   Provide a constant, quiet and ordered workplace.
    *   Use a visual timer to track time.

#### For school

*   **The wording of tasks:**
    *   Provide the instructions briefly, clearly, one point.
    *   Example: instead of "Open textbooks on page 35, read the rule and perform exercise 5, and then write down your homework" Use:
        1.  "Open the textbook on page 35." (Pause for execution).
        2.  "Read the rule in the frame." (Pause).
        3.  "Now let's complete the exercise 5. Let's start with the first sentence."
*   **Lesson structure:**
    *   Always fix the lesson plan (2-3 points) on the board.
    *   Duplicate oral instructions visually.
    *   Introduce short physical breaks every 20 minutes.
*   **Location and feed:**
    *   Optimal place: the first or second desk in the central row to minimize abstracts.
    *   Use a calm, even pace of speech with pauses.
*   **Individual route:**
    *   Consider the possibility of providing additional time in control work.
    *   It is permissible to use support materials (checks, algorithms for solving problems) in the performance of tasks.

### 4. Recommended training

Games and exercises for the development of cognitive functions. Regularity is more important than the duration (10 minutes daily more effective than an hour once a week).

*   **For attention:**
    *   **Correct samples:** Search and deletion of certain letters in the text. Gradually complicate the tasks.
    *   **Board games:** "Dobbl", "uno", "high -speed caps" - games that require a quick reaction and concentration.
*   **For coordination and bodily control:** (contribute to self -regulation)
    *   **Balance exercises:** Walking along the line, stand on one leg (with open, then closed eyes).
    *   **Juggling:** Start with two balls or bags. Develops the coordination of the "eye-hand" and attention.
*   **For working memory:**
    *   **The game "Memory":** Search for paired cards.
    *   **"Repeat me":** Repetition of sequences from words, numbers or movements. Gradually increase the length of the chain.
    *   **Retelling:** A brief retelling of the essence of the cartoon or paragraph of the text immediately after perception.
*   **For rhythm and self -control:**
    *   **Rhythmic games:** Repetition of a given rhythm with claps or steps.
    *   **"FROME-OUTOMI":** Exercise that develops the ability to quickly reaction and body control.
