### Report. Cognitive profile

This report reveals the cognitive profile of the student. It was created to support its development and assistance in studying.

#### 1. A brief report for each test

*   **Sustainability of attention (CPT):** A tendency to distract on monotonous tasks. Concentration depends on interest.
*   **Self-control (Go/No-Go):** Difficulties in containing impulsive reactions, especially with fatigue or high load.
*   **Visual working memory:** Perfectly developed. The ability to remember complex visual sequences using effective strategies.

> **Key conclusion:** Strong visual memory is an important resource. Its effective use requires the development of stability of attention and self -control. Successful in interesting, short tasks; It needs support for routine or long -term work.

#### 2. The guide for the success of your student

**Individual features:**

*   **Pace:** Uneven. High speed with enthusiasm, slowdown in monotony. Pauses are required.
*   **Perception:** The visual channel prevails. Effectively assimilates information through diagrams, images, checklists.
*   **Focus of attention:** Electoral, exhausted. Deep concentration with interest, but rapid exhaustion for boredom or overload.

**Which helps:**

*   **Visual supports:** Check lists, intelligence cards, markers are allocated.
*   **Step -by -step instructions:** The division of large tasks into specific, small stages.
*   **Timer:** The use of a timer for interval work (for example, 15–20 minutes of work / 5 minutes of a break) supports concentration.
*   **Fast connection:** The immediate result of work (mini-tests, short exercises) motivates.
*   **Work in pairs:** Cooperation with a partner helps to keep the focus.

**What interferes:**

*   **Multitasking:** The simultaneous performance of several tasks leads to errors and frustration.
*   **Long oral instructions:** The risk of loss of meaning, only part of the information is remembered.
*   **Monotone:** Long lectures, the same type of tasks, expectation deplete the resource of attention.
*   **Lack of structure:** Abstract tasks (“Think over the topic”) cause a stupor.
*   **Noise and distracting factors:** Extraneous incentives are easily distracted due to difficulties with braking.

#### 3. Recommendations

**For home:**

*   **Homework:**
    *   Apply “Rule 15-5”: 15 minutes of work, 5 minutes of active break.
    *   Start with complex objects while attention is as much as possible.
    *   Create a visual plan for the evening: 3-4 specific matters for crossing.
*   **Support:**
    *   Encourage the process, not just the result. Mark efforts (“You tried to concentrate!”).
    *   Help divide the tasks into steps. Your role is an external organizer, not a performer.
*   **Workplace:**
    *   The table should contain only necessary for the current task.

**For school:**

*   **The wording of tasks:**
    *   The instructions should be short, cherished, step -by -step. Duplicate orally/in writing.
    *   Use the verbs of action: “emphasize”, “highlight”.
*   **Lesson structure:**
    *   Sound the lesson plan at the beginning: "We repeat, study, fulfill."
    *   Alternate formats: lecture, work with a textbook, couples, independent tasks.
*   **Class placement:**
    *   Recommended place: the first-second desk, away from distracting factors (window, door).
*   **IOM elements:**
    *   Supporting abstract with key theses.
    *   Resolution for a timer or short micro -transverts (for example, go for water).
    *   Separation of testing into sequential parts.

#### 4. Recommended training

These games and exercises develop key skills in an exciting form.

*   **Attention and observation:**
    *   **"Find the differences":** Develops concentration and visual scanning.
    *   **Correct samples:** Refusing certain letters in the text during the time. Complication: crossing and emphasizing different letters.
    *   **Labyrinths:** Passing only with the eyes, without touching with a pencil.
*   **Body control and self -regulation:**
    *   **"The sea is worried":** Game for a timely stop at a signal.
    *   **"Edible-Sreedy":** He trains a quick reaction and braking.
    *   **Rhythmic games:** The exact repetition of the sequence of cotton or tapping. Develops rhythm and sequence of actions.
*   **Memory and attention:**
    *   **"Memory" ("Find a couple"):** He trains the memorization of location and an attentive search.
    *   **"Remember and draw":** Memory and playback from memory of simple figures/patterns after brief viewing.
