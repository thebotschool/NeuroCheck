# Cognitive assessment report
### Memory. Braking. Attention. (Age: 10-13 years)

This report is designed to help parents and teachers understand the potential of the child and the zone for its development. Recommendations are aimed at creating an optimal environment for training.

---

## 1. Cognitive profile

### Visual Working Memorial Test
The child demonstrates a strong visual working memory. It effectively remembers and builds sequences using strategic thinking. This is a key strong side.

### GO/No-go test (braking)
Impulsiveness is a zone for growth. It is difficult for a child to stop the action in time, which leads to hasty reactions and insufficient reflection.

### Attention for stability (CPT)
Holding attention is a challenge. The child quickly gets tired and is easily distracted, which complicates a long -term concentration on one task.

### General conclusion
The high potential of memory is combined with difficulties in self -control and attention. The child "knows what to do", but experiences difficulties with the implementation.

## 2. The guide for the success of your student

This profile describes a student capable of brilliant results in short, exciting tasks. Rutin or prolonged work can cause difficulties.

### Features of perception
*   **Pace.** Variable. He quickly grasps ideas, but implementation can be slow and erroneous due to abstracts and impulsiveness.
*   **Perception.** Optimally perceives visual, structured information: schemes, plans, infographics, video. Support on strong visual memory.
*   **Attention.** Unstable. Easily distracted by external stimuli. A soft and constant return to the task is required.

### What promotes progress
*   Visual supports: checklists, visual plans, marker allocation, stickers.
*   Skipping tasks: big tasks for short stages. The principle of "one step at a time."
*   Predictable rhythm: a clear schedule of work and breaks (for example, 15 minutes of work / 5 minutes of pause).
*   Motor activity: short breaks for movement (warm -up, assignment).
*   Immediate feedback: instant encouragement per focus.

### Which prevents concentration
*   Multivativity: simultaneous implementation of several unrelated tasks.
*   Long oral instructions: only part of the information is absorbed.
*   Monotonous work: long -term same -type tasks lead to fatigue and errors.
*   Distracting factors: noise, visual mess.
*   Negative criticism: phrases like "again distracted?" They cause frustration, not improvement.

## 3. Recommendations

### For the house

#### Organization of work
*   **Method "Prydor".** 15–20 minutes of work, 5 minutes of break. Use the timer.
*   **Priority.** Start with the most difficult tasks while there is enough energy.
*   **Checklist.** Visualize a list of tasks for tracking progress.

#### Support and promotion
*   **Focus on the process.** Mark efforts, not just the result. Example: "I see how you tried to concentrate!"
*   **Constructive dialogue.** Instead of "did you do lessons?" Ask: "What is the plan? Let's see the first point."

#### Working space
*   **Minimalism.** The desktop should be free from distracting items. Only necessary.

### For school

#### Instructions
*   **Brevity and clarity.** Tasks formulate step by step and specifically.
*   **Visual duplication.** It is advisable to duplicate oral instructions on a board or card.
    1.  Open the textbook: p. 45.
    2.  Read the rule: in the frame.
    3.  Perform exercise No. 5 (paragraphs a, b).

#### Organization of training
*   **Location.** Place at the board, far from distracting factors (windows, doors).
*   **Lesson plan.** Surrender the structure of the lesson at the beginning.

#### Individual approach
*   **Extra time.** Provision on control work.
*   **Visual timer.** Allow it to use it on the desk.
*   **Separation of tasks.** Offer tasks broken into separate parts.

## 4. Recommended training

These exercises and games are easy to integrate into everyday life. They contribute to the development of attention and self -control.

### For attention
*   **Schultte tables.** Search for numbers for a while. Improves concentration and expands the field of view.
*   **Graphic dictations.** Develop auditory attention and spatial thinking.
*   **"Find the differences."** Classical game for training attention for details.

### For self -control
*   **"Delay!".** Moving to the music, stop by signal. He trains the STOP skill.
*   **Rhythm tap.** Repetition of rhythmic drawings. Develops impulse control.

### For coordination and balance
*   **Balance.** Standing on one leg, walking along the line. Strengthens the connection of the body and brain.
*   **Juggling.** Start with two items. Powerful training, coordination and multitasking.
