# Neurocheck: Cognitive assessment

This report analyzes the cognitive abilities of your child based on complex testing. We present key data and practical recommendations to support its development both at home and at school. Our goal is to help create an optimal environment for training and growth.

### 1. Review of tests

*   **CPT (working memory and attention):** The test revealed difficulties with maintaining a large amount of information, especially in multi -component tasks or in the presence of distracting factors.
*   **Go/No-Go (self-control):** High self -control indicators. The child effectively suppresses impulsive reactions, demonstrating the ability to stop timely.
*   **TCP (stability of attention):** High stability of attention was noted in the conditions of clear tasks and the lack of abstracts. The child is able to work for a long time accurately and without errors.

**The main conclusion:** Key strengths - the ability to **focusing on a specific task** And **Effective self -control**. Difficulties are manifested if necessary to keep in memory several steps of action requiring the activation of working memory.

### 2. The guide for the success of your student

### Individual profile:

*   **Pace:** It requires a leisurely, measured pace for high -quality information processing. Hurry reduces effectiveness.
*   **Perception:** Optimally assimilates the information presented **Visually and step by step** (schemes, checklists, cards). Oral or voluminous text instructions are less effective.
*   **Focus:** Demonstrates a high concentration on brief, clearly formulated tasks. Attention weakens with multi -stage tasks, prolonged work without interruptions or in noise conditions.

### Effective strategies:

*   **Step -by -step instructions.** Divide large tasks into 2-3 small, understandable steps.
*   **Visual supports.** Use checklists, stickers, schemes for visualization of execution.
*   **Organization of the workplace.** Eliminate distracting objects, leaving only necessary for the current task.
*   **Timer.** Use a timer to structured work (for example, 20 minutes of work / 5 minutes of a break). This develops the stability of attention.
*   **Verbalization of actions.** Propuring the plan aloud ("First I will do ...", "then ...") supports working memory.

### Factors requiring attention:

*   **Multitasking.** Avoid the simultaneous purpose of several tasks. Focus on one goal at a time.
*   **Long oral instructions.** Information can be lost. The instructions should be brief or duplicated in writing/visually.
*   **Hurry and pressure.** Phrases that cause a feeling of lack of time can block strategic thinking.
*   **Distracting factors.** Background noise (TV, conversations) and visual disorder reduce productivity.

### 3. Recommendations

### Home environment:

*   **Organization of work.**
    *   Perform tasks by blocks by **20–25 minutes**, alternating with 5-minute breaks to physical activity.
    *   Create **Visual plan** tasks for the evening. Place it in a prominent place.
    *   After oral tasks, ask the child to retell the information. This contributes to comprehension and memorization.
*   **Support and promotion.**
    *   Mark **zeal and process**, not just the result. Example: "Your concentration is impressive!"
    *   Focus on its strengths, especially on self -control: "You did a great job without being distracted from the task."

### School environment:

*   **Setting tasks.**
    *   Formulate tasks **Specifically and step -by -step**. For example, when writing an essay, offer a clear structure.
    *   Duplicate oral instructions **in writing** (on the board, in the personal plan).
*   **The structure of the lesson.**
    *   It is recommended to place for **The first or second desk** To reduce abstracts.
    *   Before complex tasks, provide time to draw up a brief plan of action.
*   **Individual approach.**
    *   Consider the provision **additional time** for tests.
    *   Allow use **visual supports** (for example, cards with algorithms) during independent work.

### 4. Developing exercises

The following exercises and games will help strengthen working memory and develop strategic thinking.

### Development of working memory:

*   **"What has changed?"** Lay out 5-7 items. The child remembers, then turns away. Change one item (take/add/change places). The task is to find a change. Gradually increase the number of objects.
*   **"Repeat the sequence."** Start with 3 words or numbers. Ask to repeat. Gradually increase the length of the chain (up to 4-5 elements). Similarly with the sequence of movements.

### Development of attention and strategy:

*   **Board games.** Choose games that require planning: checkers, chess, "Carcasson", "Uno". They strengthen strategic thinking and following the rules.
*   **Labyrinths and correctional samples.** Offer to find and cross out a specific letter in the text (for example, "A" in a newspaper article). This trains the stability of attention.

### Development of coordination and rhythm (connection "brain-Telo"):

*   **Neurohymnastics.** Exercise "Fist-Robric Ladon": alternately change the position of the brush on the table. Perform each hand separately, then two at the same time.
*   **RIGHT RITMES.** Take a simple rhythm. The child’s task is to repeat it exactly. This develops auditory attention and the ability to consistent processing of information.
