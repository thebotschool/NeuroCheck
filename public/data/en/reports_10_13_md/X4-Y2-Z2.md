# Cognitive development report
### Understanding the potential of your student

This report is your guide. It will help to better understand the features of the development of the child, to determine the strengths and areas for growth. All recommendations are compiled for effective support in training and everyday life.

# The main results

**Working memory.**
Difficulties in holding several elements of information, especially in order. Lack of strategies for memorization.

**Impulse control (Go/No-Go).**
The tendency to quick, impulsive actions. The development of the ability to think over the reaction is required.

**Sustainability of attention (CPT).**
It is easily distracted, especially with monotonous tasks. External stimulation is necessary to maintain concentration.

# The guide for the success of your student

## Individual profile

*   **Pace.** The tendency to quick, impulsive actions. Hasting often leads to errors.
*   **Perception.** Prefers visual information. Better assimilates data broken into small steps. Long verbal explanations reduce attention.
*   **Attention.** Unstable, especially with the same tasks. Easily distracted. It requires a soft, constant return of the focus.

## Supporting environment

*   **Visual supports.** Cards with plans, text highlighting, diagrams, intelligence cards.
*   **Step -by -step instructions.** Tasks are divided into small, consistent actions. One task at a time.
*   **Rhythm and routine.** A clear daily routine, timers, pronouncing actions.
*   **Short intervals.** Effective work 10-15 minutes. Fast feedback and short breaks.

## Obstacles in learning

*   **Multitasking.** The inability to effectively follow several instructions at the same time.
*   **Long instructions.** Oral explanations without visual support.
*   **Monotone.** Monotonous, repeated work.
*   **Sensory overload.** Noise, disorder, background stimuli.

# Recommendations

## For the house

### Organization of training

*   **Savior tasks.** 15 minutes of work, 5 minutes break (active). Use the timer.
*   **Priorities.** Start with difficult tasks while there are strength.
*   **Pure space.** Desktop without extra items.
*   **Visual plan.** Make a simple list list. Cross out the completed points.

### Emotional support

*   **Focus on efforts.** Praise the process and effort, not just the result. For example: "Excellent, you held on 10 minutes!"
*   **Support for fatigue.** Instead of a reproach: "You're tired. Let's take a break."
*   **Realistic expectations.** The maximum concentration is 10-15 minutes. Reloading is necessary.

## At school

### Submission of instructions

*   **Briefly and clearly.** Step -by -step instructions. A pause after each step.
*   **Personal appeal.** Use the name of the child to attract attention.

### The structure of the lesson

*   **Crushing of tasks.** Divide large tasks into small parts.
*   **Algorithms.** Use assistant cards with an action plan.
*   **Change of activity.** Change the formats of work more often (letter, oral answer, physical education).

### Organization of the workplace

*   **Place.** The first desk in the center. Away from distracting factors.
*   **Visualization.** Use visual aids, game elements.

### Individual route

*   **Adaptation.** With significant difficulties, consider the development of an adapted program. This may include work with a psychologist, special work sheets.

# Recommended training

## Development of attention

*   **Corrective test.** In 2 minutes, cross out all the specified letters on the text page (magazine, newspaper).
*   **Find 10 differences.** Classical game for training concentration.

## Development of self -control (braking)

*   **The sea is worried once.** The ability to freeze and hold the pose by signal.
*   **Forbidden movement.** Repeat all the actions of the leader, except for one predetermined.

## Development of memory

*   **What has changed?** Remember the location of 5-7 items. The child turns away, you change one item. The task is to determine the change.
*   **Purchases.** A chain of words: "I go to the store and buy [subject 1]", the child repeats and adds "and [subject 2]."

## The development of rhythm

*   **Rhythmic echo.** Cut or wind a simple rhythm. The child repeats exactly. Gradually complicate.
