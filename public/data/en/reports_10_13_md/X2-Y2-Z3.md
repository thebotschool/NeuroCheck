### 2. The guide for the success of your student

This leadership describes the conditions under which the student is most productive.

#### Key features

*   **Pace:** Fast, impulsive pace. The desire for speed can lead to errors due to inattention.
*   **Perception:** The preference for visual, structured information. Long or abstract verbal instructions are absorbed worse.
*   **Focus:** Short periods of concentration (15–20 minutes), requiring subsequent switching or rest. High distraction to external stimuli.

#### Effective approaches

*   **Step -by -step instructions:** Consistent, step -by -step instructions. One team provides clarity at a time. Example: "Solve the problem." (pause) "Now emphasize the verbs."
*   **Visual supports:** Visual support tools: a plan on the board, algorithms, highlighting keywords. This helps to keep attention and understanding of the task.
*   **Clear rhythm:** The predictable daily routine and the sequence of actions. Stability reduces anxiety and helps to tune in to work.
*   **The rule "Stop. Think. Make ":** A short pause before action or answer develops self -control.
*   **Focus on one task:** The elimination of distracting objects from the field of view increases concentration.

#### Obstacles in learning

*   **Multitasking:** The simultaneous performance of several tasks reduces the quality and leads to passes.
*   **Accent on speed:** Speed ​​competition enhances impulsiveness and increase the number of errors. Value is exactly.
*   **Excess incentives:** Visual and audio disorder exhausts attention.
*   **A sharp change in activity:** The absence of clear pauses between tasks interferes with adaptation and involvement.

### 3. Practical recommendations

#### In the home environment

*   **Organization of work:**
    *   Break the tasks into blocks for 15–20 minutes. Turn on 5-minute physical breaks.
    *   Make a simple work plan: 1. Mathematics. 2. Rest. 3. Russian. This makes the process predictable and controlled.
*   **Support:**
    *   Focus on the process, and not just on the result: "I appreciate your efforts and attention."
    *   Gently return to the task when distracting.
*   **Communication style:**
    *   The instructions should be calm and single -assured.
    *   Make sure of understanding by asking to repeat the task.

#### In the educational environment

*   **The wording of tasks:**
    *   Instructions should be given sequentially, with pauses.
    *   Attach attention to key information.
*   **Lesson structure:**
    *   Use visual supports: lesson plan, assignment of tasks, algorithms.
    *   Place the student in the first desks, away from distracting factors (window, door).
*   **Style style:**
    *   Encourage raising your hand and waiting for an answer.
    *   Provide extra time (2-3 seconds) for thinking.
    *   Turn on short physical education, especially exercises to intersect the midline of the body.
*   **Elements of an individual route:**
    *   It is permissible to use a reading line.
    *   Provide extra time for testing.
    *   The possibility of processing work after 5-10 minutes after completion.

### 4. Developing exercises

The presented exercises develop key skills in a playful way, easily integrating into everyday life.

#### For attention and self -control

*   **Graphic dictations:** Develop auditory attention and accuracy of following instructions.
*   **The game "Delay-Otomory":** Effectively trains inhibitor control.
*   **The game "Yes and there is no talk":** Develops control of impulsive answers and speech awareness.

#### For visual memory and coordination

*   **Memory game:** Improves visual memory and concentration.
*   **Exercise "Photographer":** Place 4-5 items. Give 10 seconds to remember. Cover them and ask to name them. Gradually increase the amount.
*   **Games with the ball:** Remarishing the ball with a given rhythm or additional conditions (for example, fishing after cotton) improves coordination.

#### For rhythm and bodily control

*   **Cooling rhythms:** You set the rhythm, the child repeats. This develops inner rhythm and self -regulation.
*   **Walking to music with a different pace:** He trains the adaptation of the body to the external rhythm and control of movements.
