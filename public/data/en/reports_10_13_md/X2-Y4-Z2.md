### Cognitive assessment report

This report is designed to help parents and teachers understand the unique features of the development of the student. It focuses on the strengths of the strength and potential areas of growth, contributing to the creation of a comfortable and productive environment for training.

### 1. Test results

*   **Visual memory:** Difficulties with accurate memorization and reproduction of visual information were noted, especially after abstracts. Perhaps mixing similar details.
*   **Braking (Go/No-Go):** Difficulties with self -control and holding of attention have been identified. There is a tendency to impulsive actions, which may indicate the rapid fatigue of the nervous system.
*   **Sustainability of attention (TCP):** High distractiveness is observed. Attention is easily switched, especially when performing monotonous tasks.

### 2. The guide for the success of your student

#### Individual characteristics

*   **Pace:** The average or slow pace of work with regular pauses is optimal. Hurry reduces accuracy and motivation. A quick change in tasks can lead to overload.
*   **Perception format:** It more effectively assimilates the visually and structurally presented information. Perfect: **Visual support** (schemes, plans) in combination with **brief oral explanation**.
*   **Focus of attention:** Attention works "flashes". Deep immersion is possible for a short period (15–20 minutes). External stimuli (noise, movements) easily distract.

#### Factors contributing to success

*   **Step -by -step instructions:** Missing large tasks into 2-3 small, understandable steps.
*   **Visual tips:** Check lists, stickers, Mayind cards, highlighting keywords.
*   **Timer:** Using a timer (for example, 20 minutes of work / 5 minutes of rest) helps to structure time.
*   **Rhythm and rituals:** A clear daily routine and familiar actions before starting studies are set to work.
*   **Joint work:** The presence of a calmly working nearby person can help maintain concentration.

#### Factors that impede success

*   **Multitasking:** The simultaneous performance of lessons and other distracting actions (TV, music) reduce effectiveness.
*   **Oral instructions:** Long, monotonous explanations without visual support are perceived poorly.
*   **Lack of structure:** Fuzzy tasks without deadline lead to frustration and procrastination.
*   **Noise and disorder:** Inorganized space and extraneous sounds interfere with concentration.
*   **Criticism:** Comments like "do not be distracted" are ineffective and increase the level of stress.

<!-- page-break -->

### 3. Recommendations

#### For the house

*   **Homework organization:**
    *   Set a clear time for lessons. The optimal approach to the task: **20–25 minutes of work**, then 5-10 minutes a break.
    *   Use checks to track tasks.
    *   Provide a clean workplace; Remove the phone from the visibility zone.
*   **Support and encouragement:**
    *   Mark **Efforts and process**, not just the result.
    *   Help to divide the tasks into parts; Joint planning is effective.
    *   Admit the difficulties: "It's boring, I understand. Give a timer for 15 minutes, then a break?"

#### For school

*   **Clear tasks:**
    *   The instructions should be short, clear, and preferably visually duplicated (on the board, card).
    *   Use specific formulations: do not “prepare for the control”, but “solve problems No. 5, 8, 11, repeat the formulas from page 34”.
*   **Lesson structure:**
    *   Place the student closer to the teacher and the board, away from distracting factors (window, door).
    *   Use support notes, schemes and visual cards when explaining new material.
    *   Alternate the formats of activity: explanation, work with cards, discussion, independent work.
*   **Individual route:**
    *   Consider the possibility of providing additional time in control and independent work.
    *   If possible, provide materials for familiarization (for example, presentation) before the lesson.

### 4. Recommended training

Games and exercises to strengthen skills. Regularity (10-15 minutes daily) is more important than the duration.

*   **For attention and self -control:**
    *   **Correct samples:** In the text, delete a certain letter or circle prepositions. Start from 5 minutes.
    *   **The game "Delay!":** Dance, when stopping music, freeze instantly. He trains impulse control.
    *   **Board games:** "Dobble", "drum" develop the concentration and speed of the reaction through the search for coincidences.

*   **For visual memory:**
    *   **The game "Memory":** Removing and searching for paired cards.
    *   **What has changed?:** Place 7-10 items. After memorization, remove/move 1-2. The task is to determine the changes.
    *   **Drawing from memory:** Show the figure/pattern for 10-15 seconds, then reproduce from memory.

*   **For rhythm and bodily control:**
    *   **RIGHT RIGHTS:** Repetition of simple, then complicated rhythms.
    *   **Juggling:** Develops the coordination of the "eye-hand" and concentration.
    *   **Dance games:** Just Dance and similar ones develop coordination, rhythm and self -control.
