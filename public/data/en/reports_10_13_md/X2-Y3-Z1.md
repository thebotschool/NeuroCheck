# 1. A brief report for each test

*   **Visual memory:** Active desire for memorization. There is a tendency to confusion of parts during external distractions. Help is needed in the formation of effective strategies.
*   **Braking and self-control (Go/No-Go):** Confident execution with one hand, difficulties with the other. Indicates the need to strengthen interhemispheric interaction.
*   **Sustainability of attention:** Outstanding result. The ability to long, stable concentration without loss of accuracy. Key strong side.

# 2. The guide for the success of your student

## Individual characteristics

*   **Pace:** Medium or slow. Priority is the accuracy of execution, especially in tasks with fine motor skills or copying.
*   **Perception:** It more effectively assimilates visual, structured, step -by -step information. Visual supports (diagrams, short instructions) are critical.
*   **Focus of attention:** High resistance to task. Vulnerability is manifested when memorizing many small visual details. The disadvantage is not in attention, but in the processing of volumetric visual information.

## Which helps

*   **Visual tips:** Samples, diagrams, algorithms, color selection.
*   **Step -by -step instructions:** Savior of complex tasks.
*   **Rhythm and predictability:** A clear routine, rules, rituals.
*   **Support for the stronger side:** Calm environment for perseverance.
*   **Memorization:** Stories, associations for facts and images.

## What interferes

*   **Multitasking:** Simultaneous tasks or quick switching.
*   **Pace:** Hurrying, lack of time.
*   **Visual volume:** Unstractural, overloaded information.
*   **Demonstrations:** Noise, chaos.

# 3. Recommendations

## For the house

*   **Home work:**
    *   Miss tasks: divide into parts. Example: "2 examples, then a break, then the next 2."
    *   Start with complex: perform written or difficult tasks at the beginning.
    *   Accuracy control: check not only the fact of execution, but also the correct copy.
*   **Support and encouragement:**
    *   Encourage the process: mark perseverance and effort, and not just the result. Example: "You worked hard, well done!"
    *   Develop strategies for memorization: offer associations or stories.
*   **Working cycle:** Effective concentration-15-20 minutes. An active break is required for 3-5 minutes.

## For school

*   **The wording of tasks:**
    *   Speciality: tasks should be specific, with a visual sample. Example: instead of “draw autumn” - “draw an autumn tree with yellow and red leaves, as here”.
*   **Lesson structure:**
    *   Time: increase time for written and copying tasks.
    *   Visual supports: use numbered lists, diagrams, color selection.
    *   Accessibility: key information (rules, samples) should always be visible.
*   **Style style:**
    *   Temp: calm, measured. Duplicate oral instructions with a record/scheme.
*   **Adaptation:** Consider a change in the volume of written work and the provision of supporting materials.

# 4. Recommended training

These exercises will help strengthen the necessary skills in a playful way.

## Attention and memory

*   Games: “Find 10 differences”, “What has changed in the room?”
*   Board games: "Memory" (paired cards).
*   Practice: copying patterns by points or cells.

## Coordination and interaction

*   Symmetric drawing: simultaneous drawing with two hands.
*   Modeling: simultaneous plasticine skating with two hands.
*   Work with the ball: throwing from hand to hand.

## Rhythm and control

*   Rhythmic echo: repeating the rhythm with palms.
*   Balance: walking along the line, stand on one leg (with open/closed eyes).
*   Game: "The sea is worried once."
