# Individual cognitive profile

This report offers a detailed view of the unique cognitive profile of your child. The test results will help create an optimal environment for training and development.

## 1. Key results

*   **Visual memory (CPT).** The ability to memorize information is present. There is a tendency to confusion of parts or thought up when distracting. The development of strategies for memorization is required.
*   **Braking (Go/No-Go).** Impulsiveness is observed. The tendency to rapid reaction, ahead of thinking.
*   **Sustainability of attention (TCP).** A pronounced ability to long and stable concentration. High productivity with deep immersion in the task.

**Key conclusion.** High attentiveness and concentration were noted. The main growth zones: impulsivity and the need for strategies for working with visual information.

## 2. The guide for the success of your student

### Features of the profile

*   **Pace.** The measured pace of work is preferred. Hurry reduces quality. Speed ​​tasks are not recommended, as they provoke errors.
*   **Perception.** The best assimilation of information occurs with a clear, structured and consistent presentation. Chaotic data is remembered with difficulty.
*   **Focus.** The focus of attention is stable, but narrowly focused. The child is able to go deeply into one task. Quick switching or simultaneous control of several conditions causes difficulties.

### Optimal conditions

*   **Step -by -step instructions.** Divide big tasks into small, understandable steps.
*   **Visual signals.** Use the "anchors" or "stop"-signals to remind you of the need to think before action.
*   **Rhythmic exercises.** The tapping or cooling the rhythm before the task helps to tune in to a calm pace.
*   **Speaking.** Discussion of the plan of the assignment out loud contributes to planning and reduces impulsiveness.

### Factors that reduce efficiency

*   **Multitasking.** The simultaneous implementation of two or more tasks (for example, listen and write down) almost guaranteedly leads to errors.
*   **Fast change of tasks.** Sharp transitions between activities cause fatigue and disorganization. A small pause or ritual of the transition is needed.
*   **Unstructured information.** A large volume of text or images without a clear structure overloads the information processing system and memory.
*   **Only oral instructions.** Details of instructions can be lost without visual support due to the features of working memory.

## 3. Recommendations

### Home environment

*   **Organization of the work process.**
    *   **Workplace.** Minimize the number of extra items on the table.
    *   **Time management.** Use the timer: 15-20 minutes of work, then a 5-minute break. This supports high concentration.
    *   **Planning.** Together, make a simple plan before starting work: "What do we do first? What then?"
*   **Support and motivation.**
    *   **Focus on quality.** Praise not for speed, but for attentiveness and ability to stop, think about it. For example: "Great that you did not hurry and checked yourself!"
    *   **The development of self -control.** If you find an error, do not say "wrong." Ask: "Let's see again. Are you sure? What rule is applicable here?" This forms the skill of self -testing.

### Educational environment

*   **Instructions.**
    *   **Brace and clarity.** Let's briefly, clearly and one at a time. For example: "Open the notebooks. (Pause). Write down the number. (Pause). Open page 5."
    *   **Visual support.** Duplicate oral instructions (page number, exercises) on the board.
*   **Organization of space and materials.**
    *   **Location.** Place the child closer to the board, further from distracting factors (window, door).
    *   **Materials.** Use work sheets with clearly divided blocks for tasks.
    *   **Hipples.** In control work, highlight keywords in tasks.
*   **Pedagogical approach.**
    *   **The pace of speech.** The calm, measured pace of the teacher’s speech contributes to better perception.
    *   **Individual route.** Give additional time to complete the tasks requiring verification. Use assistant cards with an algorithm of action (for example, "1. Read. 2. Emphasize the main thing. 3. Resolve. 4. Check").

## 4. Recommended training

These exercises and games are aimed at developing key cognitive skills.

### Attention and self -control

*   **"Edible - inedible."** Classic game for training quick reaction and braking.
*   **"Delay!".** The development of instant stopping of movements at the signal of music.
*   **Graphic dictations.** Exercises for accurate adherence to instructions and maximum concentration without rush.

### Visual memory and strategies

*   **"Find a couple" (Memory).** Start with 6-8 cards, gradually increasing their number. Discuss strategies for memorization (for example, "by rows", "by color").
*   **"What has changed?"** Lay out 5-7 items, give 30 seconds to remember. Then ask the child to turn away and change something (take it, add, change places).
*   **Compilation of stories according to the pictures.** To memorize a number of unrelated images, invent the history that unites them.

### Rhythm and coordination

*   **"Rhythmic echo."** You clap or tap a simple rhythm, the child repeats it exactly. Gradually complicate.
*   **Walking along the line.** Simple walking along a drawn or imaginary line (straight, zigzag) helps synchronize the brain and body, developing control.
