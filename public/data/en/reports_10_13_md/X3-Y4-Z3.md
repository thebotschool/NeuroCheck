### **Cognitive assessment report: consistent memory + braking and control + impulsiveness (age: 10-13 years)**

This report helps to understand the strengths and development zones of the student. Recommendations are based on the results of cognitive tests and are aimed at creating an optimal environment for training and growth.

---

#### **1. Analysis of the results**

*   **Conservative memory test (CPT):** Information is absorbed well. However, holding the sequence of events requires effort. The student is actively looking for his own strategies for compensation.
*   **Braking and control test (Go/No-Go):** Difficulties with prolonged concentration and timely inhibition of reactions were noted. Fast fatigue during tasks requiring attention indicates reduced cognitive endurance.
*   **Impulsive and self -control test (TCP):** There is a tendency to impulsive actions: the beginning of the task without fully familiarization with the instructions, quick, rash answers.

<!-- page-break -->

#### **2. The guide for the success of your student**

##### **Individual characteristics**

*   **Pace:** The pace of work is uneven. The impulsiveness at the beginning of the task is replaced by slowdown and fatigue when performing complex tasks requiring concentration.
*   **Perception:** The most effectively assimilates visual, step-by-step information: schemes, algorithms, checklists. This compensates for the difficulties with the sequence.
*   **Focus of attention:** Attention is unstable. It is easily distracted, experiences difficulties with a long concentration, especially with monotonous or requiring self -control tasks.

##### **Which helps**

*   **Step -by -step instructions:** Skipping tasks into 3-4 short steps.
*   **Visual supports:** Schemes, memory maps, text highlighting, timers.
*   **Clear rhythm:** The alternation of work (15-20 min) and short pauses (3-5 minutes) for relaxation.
*   **Preliminary planning:** Joint discussion of the action plan before starting work.
*   **Stop rule:** Introduction of the signal (“pause”, “think”) to interrupt impulsive actions.

##### **What interferes**

*   **Multitasking:** The simultaneous implementation of several cases causes overload and error.
*   **Hurry and competition:** Enhance impulsiveness, reducing quality. Priority is accuracy.
*   **Long oral instructions:** Lead to focus loss.
*   **Lack of structure:** Chaos in tasks and at the workplace interferes with concentration and planning.

<!-- page-break -->

#### **3. Recommendations**

##### **For the house**

*   **Organization of work:**
    *   Separate homework into blocks for 15-20 minutes, alternating with interruptions.
    *   Use a visual timer to control time.
    *   Create a simple work plan: 1. Problem. 2. Break. 3. Task.
*   **Support and encouragement:**
    *   Encourage accuracy, following the instructions, deliberate actions.
    *   Instead of criticism, direct to the instructions: "What was the first step?"
*   **Mercing of attention:**
    *   Consider: the productive concentration lasts 15-20 minutes. Additional time provokes fatigue and error.

##### **For school**

*   **The wording of tasks:**
    *   The instructions should be brief and clockwork. Duplicate oral tasks in writing.
    *   After the instructions, pause, ask about the first step.
*   **Lesson structure:**
    *   Start a lesson with a plan of 2-3 key stages.
    *   Use algorithms and schemes to explain new material.
*   **Class placement:**
    *   Optimal place: 1-2 desks, center. Minimize distracting factors.
*   **Podia style and feedback:**
    *   Avoid quick polls provoking impulsive answers. Give the time to think or offer a written answer.
    *   Consider the development **individual educational route (IOM)**. Focus: development of regulatory functions (planning, self -control), load adaptation.

<!-- page-break -->

#### **4. Recommended training**

Practical games for the development of key skills.

*   **Attention and self -control:**
    *   **Board games:** “Cortex”, “Dobble”, “Drum” develop a reaction and following the rules.
    *   **The games are “edible-universal”, “and there is no no talk”:** They train "stop-reaction."
    *   **Graphic dictations:** Develop the ability to listen and step by step to follow the instructions.
*   **Consistent memory:**
    *   **The game "Snow Com":** Consistent adding words.
    *   **Retelling along the chain of pictures (3-5):** Drawing up a story in order.
    *   **Remoning and repetition of recipes:** Help in the kitchen, following the sequence.
*   **Body control and rhythm:**
    *   **Neurohymnastics:** Exercise "Kulak-Robe Ladon", multidirectional movements of the hands.
    *   **Games with the ball:** Bowing the ball in a given rhythm.
    *   **Cooling rhythms:** The exact repetition of a given rhythm. Develops concentration and sequence.
