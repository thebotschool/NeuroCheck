# 2. The guide for the success of your student

### Features of training

*   **Pace:** Productivity is high in the presence of interest. Monotonous tasks can reduce pace and effectiveness.
*   **Perception:** Visual and logical perception prevails. The clear structure, scheme and understanding of the ultimate goal is important.
*   **Focus of attention:** Maximum on non -standard and significant tasks. It decreases with dry or monotonous material feed.

### Which helps

*   **Visualization:** Schemes, infographics, mental cards, highlighting keywords. All that makes information visual and structured.
*   **Clear goals:** Understanding “why” and “what next” increases motivation.
*   **Element of the game:** Tasks requiring quick wits, design work, quests, team projects.
*   **Short intervals:** Alternation of 15–20 minutes of work on a routine task with a brief break or change of activity.

### What interferes

*   **Monotone:** Long lectures without interactive, the same type of exercise.
*   **Multitasking:** The need for simultaneous listening, recording and following a presentation without pauses.
*   **Lack of feedback:** The lack of confirmation of the correctness of actions can reduce interest.
*   **Passive role:** Situations where it is required only to listen and remember, without the possibility of active participation.

# 3. Recommendations

### At home

### Organization of homework

*   **Tomato method:** 20 minutes of work on one item, then a 5-minute break. Especially effective for less beloved items.
*   **Priority:** Start with more complex or interesting tasks. Perform routine tasks during the period of maximum concentration.
*   **Check lists:** Turn the routine into a list of tasks. Crossing out the points of the points gives a feeling of control and completeness.

### Support and motivation

*   **Discussion:** Talk not only about assessments, but also about the process of overcoming difficulties. Mark perseverance in boring tasks.
*   **Communication with reality:** Show how school knowledge is used in life or its hobbies (for example, physics in games, chemistry in cooking).
*   **Optimal time:** The concentration on a monotonous task lasts 15–20 minutes. Then a change in activity is needed.

### At school

### The formulation of tasks

*   **Choice:** Give the opportunity to choose, for example: "Solve any 8 out of 12 examples."
*   **Design approach:** Offer research tasks where he can apply logic and strategic thinking.
*   **Crushing:** Divide large tasks into small, understandable steps with intermediate results.

### Lesson structure and material presentation

*   **Visual anchors:** Use the patterns on the board, key terms, infographics.
*   **Change of formats:** Alternate short lectures (5-7 minutes) with discussions in pairs, practical tasks and polls.
*   **Location:** Place the student so that he is in the field of view of the teacher for timely return of attention.

### Individual educational route

*   **Headphones:** For the same type of written work, you can allow quiet background music without words, if it helps to concentrate.
*   **The role of the expert:** Offer the role of an “assistant” or “expert” on topics in which he is strong in order to use his potential.

# 4. Recommended training

These exercises help strengthen attention and self -control in a playful way.

### Attention and strategy

*   **Board games:** Chess, checkers, “Karcasson”, “jackal” - games requiring planning several moves ahead.
*   **Graphic dictations:** Tasks for careful listening to instructions and accurate following them.

### Coordination and bodily control

*   **Juggling:** The beginning with two balls develops the coordination of the “eye-hand” and concentration.
*   **Balance exercises:** Walking along the line, a stand on one leg, a balance border.

### Memory and rhythm

*   **Musical instruments:** The game on drums or guitar, as well as applications for creating rhythms, train a sense of rhythm and sequence of actions.
*   **The game "Remember and repeat":** Reproduction of sequences (pops, movements, words, pictures).
