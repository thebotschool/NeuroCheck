# Cognitive development report: potential analysis

Your schoolboy has a unique cognitive profile. This report will help to better understand its strengths and effectively use them for harmonious development and training.

## 1. Evaluation of cognitive functions

### Visual working memory
Effective memorization of complex visual information. Application of productive strategies to keep data. Excellent organization of thinking.

### Go/No-Go (self-control)
Excellent control of pulses and actions, even if necessary, quick reaction and inhibition. Synchronous work of both cerebral hemispheres.

### Continous Performance Test.
High level of concentration. The ability to prolonged and unmistakable implementation of monotonous or high -precision tasks without reducing productivity.

## 2. The profile of the student

### Individual characteristics
*   **Pace:** Stable, measured. The ability to long and high -quality work without exhaustion.
*   **Perception:** Optimally assimilates structured, logically constructed information. Effectively works with visual supports (diagrams, tables) and consistent verbal instructions.
*   **Focus of attention:** Deep, stable. Capable of complete immersion in the task, not being distracted by external stimuli.

### Conditions for development
*   **Definition:** Maximum efficiency is achieved in the presence of a clear structure and purpose of the task.
*   **Tasks:** He prefers tasks that require logic, analysis and search for patterns.
*   **Visualization:** Schemes, Mayind cards and step-by-step plans contribute to the quick organization of information.
*   **Independence:** Motivated by the opportunity to independently plan work on large projects.

### Factors that reduce efficiency
*   **Uncertainty:** The lack of clear criteria or goals causes frustration.
*   **Demonstrations:** Frequent, meaningless interruptions violate a deep concentration.
*   **Simplicity:** Monotonous tasks that do not require mental effort are quickly bored.

<!-- page-break -->

## 3. Recommendations

### For the house
*   **Homework:** The schoolboy is able to independently plan and fulfill the entire amount of tasks. The model is recommended: Discussion of the plan → Self -implementation → Checking the result. Microcontrol is excessed.
*   **Schedule:** It can hold attention to a difficult task for up to 30–45 minutes, after which a short break is required (5-10 minutes).
*   **Support:** Encourage not only the result, but also the process of the solution: “An excellent logical approach!”, “A thoughtful plan of action!”.
*   **Dialogue:** Discuss strategies for memorization and approaches to complex topics. This contributes to the conscious use of its strengths.

### For school
*   **Tasks:** Use multicomponent instructions that require analysis, comparison and classification. Example: "Read the paragraph, highlight three key ideas, make a scheme and prepare two questions for discussion."
*   **Material feed:** The schoolboy is ready for design and research activities, can be the leader of the group. The appeal to logic, the search for causal relationships is important.
*   **Location:** It works effectively in any part of the class, since its attention depends little on external factors.
*   **Individual route:** The standard program can be too simple. It is recommended to participate in olympiads, scientific conferences, circles with in -depth study of objects (mathematics, physics, programming, linguistics).

## 4. Recommended training

The purpose of the exercises is to offer a worthy challenge to the strengths of the student and contribute to the further development of his potential.

### Development of attention and strategic thinking
*   **Intellectual games:** Chess, Go, Sogi - for a deep miscalculation of moves and constructing a long -term strategy.
*   **Complex board games:** For example, "colonialists", "energy network" - for planning resources and adaptation to changing conditions.

### Development of memory and interhemispheric interaction
*   **Musical instruments:** Playing piano or guitar - develops coordination of both hands.
*   **Juggling:** Improves coordination, concentration and interaction of the cerebral hemispheres.

### Development of bodily control and rhythm
*   **Orienteering:** Combines physical activity with the skills of reading a card and making decisions.
*   **Dancing:** Choreography with complex sequences of movements develops memory and a sense of rhythm.
