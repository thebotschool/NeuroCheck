# Cognitive assessment report

This report provides key data on the cognitive profile of your student, identifying the strengths and areas for development. Recommendations are designed to support effective training and harmonious development.

---

## 1. A brief report for each test

*   **Test of visual working memory (CPT):** High ability to memorize information sequences and the use of effective strategies.
*   **Go/No-Go Test (self-control):** Excellent pulses control, quick switching between tasks without errors. Mature self -regulation.
*   **Test for stability of attention (TCP):** Calling the focus in long -term retention, especially on monotonous tasks. There is a quick fatigue and errors on inattention.

**Resume:** The student has strong cognitive resources: developed memory and self -control. The key area for development is stability of attention. Purpose: Optimization of the use of attention resource for the full realization of potential.

## 2. Instructions for the child as a student

### Individual characteristics

*   **Pace:** Productivity with clarity of the task, interest and a calm environment. Slow down with distraction or mental fatigue.
*   **Perception format:** Effectively assimilates clear, structured, visually executed information. Schemes, plans, tables, checklists serve as a support for attention.
*   **Focus of attention:** Unstable. Deep immersion in an interesting, but rapid "loss" of routine or long -term oral explanations.

### Which helps

*   **Visual funds:** The plan on the board, highlighted words, short checklists.
*   **Step -by -step instructions:** Skipping tasks into small, understandable steps.
*   **Rhythm and predictability:** A clear daily routine, the use of a timer, understandable rituals.
*   **Micro-shit:** Short pauses (2-3 minutes) for the movement or change of focus.

### What interferes

*   **Multitasking:** The simultaneous performance of several actions reduces the quality.
*   **Noise and disorder:** Distracting factors reduce the resource of attention.
*   **Long oral instructions:** Most of the information is lost.
*   **High pace without pauses:** Leads to cognitive overload, irritability.

## 3. Recommendations

### For the house

*   **Homework organization:**
    *   Break the tasks into blocks for 15–20 minutes with short breaks. Use the timer.
    *   Make a simple work plan together.
    *   Organize the workplace: only the necessary items on the table, eliminate distraction.
*   **Support and encouragement:**
    *   Praise effort and process, not only the result.
    *   Instead of "be more attentive", ask questions: "What is interfering?" Or "how to break the task?"

### For school

*   **The wording of tasks:**
    *   Let's clear, step -by -step tasks, one at a time. Use cards or board.
    *   Make sure of understanding: Ask to repeat the task.
*   **Lesson structure:**
    *   At the beginning of the lesson, voice and write down the plan on the board. This creates predictability.
*   **Class placement:**
    *   The optimal place is the first-second desk in the central row. Minimizes distraction, facilitates eye contact.
*   **IOOM elements (individual educational route):**
    *   **Check lists:** Use for complex tasks where you can mark the completed points.
    *   **Additional time:** Provide in control and independent work if necessary.
    *   **"Legal" breaks:** Allow short warm -ups if you see signs of fatigue.

## 4. Recommended training

The presented games and exercises in an exciting form will help to develop the necessary skills.

### For attention

*   **Correct test:** Search and deletion of characters in the text in a limited time. Gradually complicate the rules.
*   **Board games:** "Dobble", "drum", "memorials" - develop rapid concentration and visual search.

### For coordination and bodily control

*   **Exercise "Fly":** Curtain eyes behind an imaginary "fly" on the 3x3 field. He trains concentration and oculomotor muscles.
*   **Juggling:** Start with two balls. Develops the coordination of the "hands-eye" and interhemispheric interaction.

### For memory and rhythm

*   **Rhythmic echo:** Repetition of a given rhythm with claps. He trains auditory attention and consistent processing of information.
