# Cognitive assessment report
*(Age: 10-13 years)*

This report is an analysis of the results of cognitive tests (CPT, Go/No-Go, self-control test). He is designed to help in understanding the individual characteristics of the student and offer practical tools for his education and development.

## 1. Review of tests

*   **Attention and memory (CPT):** Difficulties with maintaining the sequence of information and attention stability are noted. There is a tendency to action without preliminary planning, which affects memorization.
*   **Self-control (Go/No-Go):** Difficulties in containing impulsive reactions have been identified. The resource of attention is depleted faster than that of peers, when performing tasks requiring a long concentration.
*   **Impulsive braking (TCP):** The tendency to action was confirmed by the first motivation, often until the instructions are fully obtained. Difficulties in understanding the answer before issuing it are noticeable.

## 2. Profile of learning

Understanding these features allows you to build an optimal environment for teaching and developing a student.

### Key features

*   **Pace:** The tendency to a quick, but not always consistent pace is manifested. Actions often ahead of planning, which can lead to errors.
*   **Perception:** The most effectively perceives short, clear information. Visual materials (schemes, illustrations) significantly improve understanding. Long oral explanations are more complicated.
*   **Focus of attention:** Attention is unstable and quickly exhausted. The optimal concentration time on one task: **10-15 minutes**, after which a short break is needed (sensory or motor).

### Effective approaches

*   **Visual tips:** The use of checklists, step-by-step schemes, visual reminders.
*   **Step -by -step instructions:** Presentation of tasks in the form of individual, sequential steps. The next step is given only after the previous one.
*   **Timer:** The use of sand clock or visual timer for better perception and time control.
*   **Clear rhythm:** Creation of predictable routine and rituals to reduce anxiety and set up on the educational process.

### Obstacles in learning

*   **Multitasking:** Simultaneous requests or tasks lead to scattering attention and forgetfulness.
*   **Long oral instructions:** The ability to process and hold oral information is limited by 2-3 semantic units.
*   **Noise and distracting factors:** Any external stimuli (sounds, disorder) significantly reduce concentration of attention.
*   **Speed ​​competition:** The emphasis on the speed of the tasks provokes impulsiveness and increases the number of errors. Priority is quality and accuracy.

## 3. Practical recommendations

### Home environment

*   **Homework:** Separate tasks into short 15-minute blocks with 5-minute breaks. During the break, physical activity or a change of focus, without the use of gadgets, is preferred.
*   **Organization of space:** The workplace should be minimalistic. Remove all unnecessary items, leaving only necessary for the current task.
*   **Support:** Focus on the promotion **efforts, accuracy and following the plan**, not just speeds. Mark conscious pauses for thinking.
*   **Communication:** Establish eye contact before handling. Use short, clear phrases, formulating one request at a time.

### School environment

*   **Making tasks:** Provide tasks one at a time. Duplicate oral instructions on the board or on an individual card.
*   **Lesson structure:** Apply visual supports: a lesson plan, numbered lists, highlighting keywords.
*   **Accommodation:** Recommended place in the class: the first or second desk, in the center, far from distracting areas (window, door).
*   **Teaching style:** Contact a student by name more often to maintain attention. Integrate short physical education or finger gymnastics.
*   **Individual route:** Consider the possibility of including additional breaks, use the timer and provide tasks in the checklist format.

## 4. Training of skills

The proposed games and exercises are easily integrated into everyday life, contributing to the development of the necessary cognitive skills in an interactive form.

*   **For attention:**
    *   **Graphic dictations:** Develop auditory attention and ability to follow instructions.
    *   **"Find the differences":** They train concentration and attentiveness to details.
    *   **Correct samples:** Exercise for searching and rejecting certain symbols in the text.

*   **For memory and sequence:**
    *   **The game "What has changed?":** He trains visual memory and attentiveness (memorizing the location of objects).
    *   **"Remember and repeat":** Exercises for playing short sequences (words, numbers, movements).

*   **For bodily control and self -regulation:**
    *   **Figus games:** For example, “Zamri-Otomory” develop the ability to quickly stop movement (“stop reaction”).
    *   **Balance exercises:** Walking along the line, stand on one leg. Contribute to improving coordination and bodily control.
    *   **Tapping rhythms:** Develops auditory attention, a sense of rhythm and control of impulsivity through the exact repetition of the specified patterns.
