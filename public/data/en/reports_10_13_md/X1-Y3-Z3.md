# Cognitive profile
### Age: 10-13 years

This report offers a deep understanding of the cognitive characteristics of the student. It is designed to highlight the strengths and determine areas for purposeful development, contributing to effective training and harmonious adaptation.

---

# 1. Key indicators

*   **Working memory (CPT):** High ability to keep and process complex information. Memorization skills are well developed. The stronger side, the basis for training.
*   **Braking control (Go/No-Go):** Difficulties in the coordination of hand movements were revealed. There may be difficulties with writing and concentration when performing multitask operations.
*   **Attention and self -control (TCP):** The tendency to impulsive reactions is noted. A rush in answers can interfere with a full -fledged demonstration of knowledge.

<!-- page-break -->

# 2. Features of training

### Individual characteristics

*   **Pace:** Variable. It is capable of methodological work, but shows a tendency to impulsiveness in conditions of quick response.
*   **Perception:** Optimally assimilates structured and visual information: schemes, plans, algorithms.
*   **Attention:** Capable of deep concentration on one task. Difficulties were noted with a quick switching or performing several actions at the same time.

### Optimization

*   **Visual supports:** Check lists, step-by-step instructions, visual schemes.
*   **Structure:** The predictable rhythm of the lesson and homework.
*   **Control:** Conscious pauses before action, for example, "first listen."
*   **Priority of accuracy:** The emphasis on correctness and accuracy, not speed.
*   **Samples:** The presence of ready -made examples for completing tasks.

### Barriers

*   **Multitasking:** The simultaneous performance of several actions causes exhaustion.
*   **Fast change of tasks:** It takes time to transition between activities.
*   **Verbal instructions:** Without visual support, it can be partially missed due to impulsiveness.
*   **Competition:** The element "who faster" provokes a rush and errors.
*   **Distracting factors:** Noise and external stimuli reduce self -control.

<!-- page-break -->

# 3. Recommendations

### Home environment

*   **Organization of work:**
    *   Crusting large tasks on step -by -step lists.
    *   Use the timer: 15–20 minutes of work, 5 minutes of a break.
    *   Discuss the action plan before starting: "What will you do first? Then? How to check?"
*   **Support:**
    *   Encourage the process, not just the result: "I see your efforts", "excellent that I listened."
    *   Softly guide when rushing: "Let's re -read the task together."
*   **Concentration:** The effective concentration lasts 15–25 minutes per task. Regular short breaks are necessary.

### Educational environment

*   **Setting tasks:**
    *   Instructions: short, clear. Duplicate oral instructions in writing.
    *   Use the algorithms: "1. Read. 2. Emphasize. 3. Plan 4. Answer."
*   **Learning environment:**
    *   Accommodation: closer to the teacher, away from distracting factors (window, door).
    *   Feedback: pause before the question, appeal by name to attract attention.
    *   Self -control: Encourage mandatory self -test.
*   **Individual route:**
    *   **Pace adaptation:** Additional time for written work, flexible deadlines.
    *   **Visual supports:** The use of submarine cards (rules, formulas, plans).

# 4. Developing practices

The proposed exercises contribute to the development of key cognitive functions through game activity.

### Attention and self -control

*   **Graphic dictations:** Develop a step -by -step following instructions.
*   **Board games:** They train the expectation, planning and compliance with the rules (chess, checkers, “monopoly”).
*   **"Yes, no say":** Exercise to control impulsive answers.

### Coordination and interhemispheric interaction

*   **Synchronous drawing:** Simultaneous creation of symmetrical patterns (circles, waves) in the air or on paper.
*   **"Crossroads":** Slow walking in place with touching the opposite knee with the hand.
*   **Juggling:** Start with two light objects (balls, scarves).

### Memory and rhythm

*   **Rhythmic patterns:** Repetition of specified rhythms with gradual complication.
*   **"Snowball":** Playing on auditory memory (consistent addition of words).
