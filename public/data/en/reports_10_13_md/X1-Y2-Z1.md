# Cognitive profile report
## Memory, inhibition, attention (10-13 years)

The presented report contains an analysis of the cognitive features of your student. The goal is to provide parents and teachers with information that contributes to the optimal development of the potential of the child.

## 1. Test results

*   **Working memory and attention (CPT):** The ability to retain and structure complex information. A strong side contributing to effective training.
*   **Braking (Go/No-Go):** The tendency to impulsive actions and hasty decisions. The development of self -control skills is required.
*   **Sustainability of attention (TCP):** High concentration on tasks that do not require constant restraint. The ability to long and accurate work.

## 2. The guide for the success of your student

Understanding the unique style of teaching a child is the key to his success.

### Individual characteristics

*   **Pace:** Fast, tendency to immediate actions. A conscious slowdown is required to improve the quality of work.
*   **Perception:** The preference for structured and visual information: diagrams, tables, step -by -step plans. Oral abstract instructions can be perceived partially.
*   **Focus of attention:** The ability to deep immersion in one task. Difficulties with the suppression of impulsive reactions requiring pause or reflection.

### Which contributes to training

*   **Step -by -step instructions:** Skipping of large tasks into small, controlled steps.
*   **Visual tips:** Reminders that stimulate thinking before action (for example, "stop" signs).
*   **Clear rhythm:** Regular rituals that help reduce impulsiveness (for example, short breathing exercises).
*   **Preliminary planning:** Discussion of the action plan before the start of work using strong memory and logic.

### What complicates training

*   **Multitasking:** A quick switch between the tasks depletes the self -control system, leading to errors.
*   **Oral instructions:** Without visual support, they may not be fully understood, provoking premature actions.
*   **Rush and pressure:** An increase in the likelihood of making hasty, non -optimal decisions.
*   **Long expectation:** Situations that require containing an answer cause fatigue and enhance impulsiveness.

## 3. Recommendations

### For the house

*   **Organization of work:**
    *   Separate homework for short intervals (20–25 minutes of work, 5 minutes of active break) to prevent fatigue.
    *   Plan actions: pre -pronounce the steps of completing each task.
*   **Support:**
    *   Mark attentiveness and self -test, and not just the speed of execution.
    *   Introduce the "rule of three seconds" before the answer to questions for training pause.
*   **Duration of concentration:** Focused attention remains up to 25-30 minutes, then the productivity is reduced.

### For school

*   **The wording of tasks:**
    *   Written or visual presentation of tasks is preferred.
    *   Select keywords (not, except, but first, then) to prevent errors.
*   **Lesson structure:**
    *   Work on clear algorithms and plans. Provide checks for testing.
    *   Accommodation on front desks minimizes distraction and provides visual contact with the teacher.
*   **Interaction style:** A friendly, but directive style with clear instructions is effective. Use leading questions to stimulate planning.
*   **Elements of individualization:** Consider the provision of additional time for verification or the possibility of using drafts for primary ideas.

## 4. Recommended training

These exercises develop self -control and cognitive skills.

*   **Attention and self -control:**
    *   **The game "Delay!"** Moving to music with an instant stop when turning it off. Complication: fading only on a specific sound.
*   **Memory and sequence:**
    *   **Strategic board games:** Chess, checkers, "set". Develop planning and foresight.
*   **Rhythm and coordination:**
    *   **RIGHTS RIGHTS:** Repetition of a given rhythm. Improves auditory attention and action on the model.
*   **Bodily control:**
    *   **"Square breath":** Slow inhale, delay, exhale, delay (4 counts each). Helps to calm down and control impulses.
