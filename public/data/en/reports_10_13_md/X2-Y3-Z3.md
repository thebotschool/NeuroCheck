### Cognitive development report
#### Age: 10-13 years

This report provides an individual profile of the cognitive features of your student. It contains practical recommendations to support training and development.

## 1. Key indicators

*   **Visual working memory (CPT):** The schoolboy shows the effort to memorize visual information. Difficulties with accurate retention of details and exposure to distractings are noted, which can lead to distortions.
*   **Impulsive control (Go/No-Go):** Fast and confident completion of tasks by the leading hand. The tasks requiring bilateral coordination cause difficulties, indicating the need to develop interhemispheric interaction.
*   **Sustainability of attention (TCP):** The tendency to impulsive reactions is manifested, expressed in hasty answers or actions. It is important to develop internal control and self -regulation.

## 2. Profile of learning

### Features of perception

*   **Pace:** Fast, impulsive. Haster often leads to errors associated with inattention, and not with a lack of knowledge.
*   **Perception:** Optimally assimilates visual information (images, diagrams). Support for accurate memorization and prevention of confusion in details is required.
*   **Attention:** Unstable, tendency to easy distraction. Fast fatigue during tasks requiring coordination of both hands (for example, a letter and holding a notebook).

### Effective approaches

*   **Step -by -step instructions.** Break big tasks into 2-3 sequential steps.
*   **Visual supports.** Use checklists, memos, highlighting keywords.
*   **Rhythm and rituals.** The predictable structure supports concentration. The use of rhythmic elements (pronouncing, clamping) is also effective.
*   **One task at a time.** Avoid multitasking (for example, "Listen and write"). Offer to perform actions sequentially.
*   **Pause for reconciliation.** Before starting work, ask the student to repeat the instructions in your own words.

### Factors that impede training

*   **Multitasking.** The simultaneous perception of several streams of information (listen, watch, write) leads to overload.
*   **Speed ​​competition.** It stimulates impulsiveness and increases the likelihood of errors.
*   **Volumetric oral instructions.** More than two consistent teams are difficult to hold by ear.
*   **Distracting incentives.** Excessive visual and sound factors exhaust the resource of attention.

## 3. Practical recommendations

### Home environment

*   **Organization of the workplace.** Eliminate distracting factors on the desktop.
*   **Using a timer.** Apply intervals: 15-20 minutes of work, then a 5-minute active pause (movement, stretching).
*   **Disruption of tasks.** Divide large tasks into smaller stages (for example, instead of “Solving 10 examples” - “Solve three, then check and rest”).
*   **Effects on the effort.** Encourage accuracy and diligence, not speed. For example: “I see, you carefully circled all the letters!”, Instead of “how quickly you coped!”
*   **Joint planning.** Develop a simple checklist of tasks for homework with marks about execution.
*   **Clear instructions.** Formulate the tasks on one sentence. Example: "Take the blue pen. Open the notebook. Write down the number."

### Educational environment

*   **Optimal place.** Placing at the first or second desk in the central row reduces distracting factors.
*   **Contact establishment.** Before starting the explanation of the assignment for the whole class, make sure of the visual contact with the student.
*   **Duplication of instructions.** Reinforcement of oral instructions with short notes or patterns on the board.
*   **Extra time.** Provide more time for written work due to increased requirements for coordination of movements.
*   **Tasks-cards.** Offer tasks in cards with step -by -step algorithms.
*   **Using the ruler.** Allow the use of the line to track the line when reading and writing.
*   **Savior of tests.** Divide the tasks in control work into smaller, controlled blocks.

## 4. Recommended exercises

The proposed exercises contribute to the development of key skills in interactive form.

### For attention and self -control

*   **Graphic dictations.** Drawing on cells under dictation. Develops the skill of complete perception of instructions.
*   **The game "Yes, and not say."** Forms control over impulsive answers.
*   **Corrective tests.** Search and deletion of a given letter in the text for a while.

### For coordination and interhemispheric interaction

*   **Synchronous drawing.** Simultaneous drawing with two hands. Start with simple symmetrical figures, gradually complicating.
*   **Exercise "Fist-Robric Ladon".** A consistent change in the position of the hand on the surface, first with one hand, then two at the same time.
*   **Kinesiological exercises.** For example, a cross -tied locomotive of the opposite knee.

### For the development of visual memory

*   **The game "Memory".** (Find a couple)
*   **"What has changed?"** Lay out 5-7 items. Ask the student to remember them, then change the location, remove or add the element. The schoolboy calls the changes.
*   **Stories from the pictures.** Make and retell a short story using 3-4 consecutive images.
