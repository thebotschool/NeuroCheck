### **Your cognitive profile**

This report offers a clear look at the strengths and growth zones of your student. This is a practical guide designed to help create an optimal environment for training and development.

---

#### **1. Key indicators**

*   **Self-control (Go/No-Go):** Excellent level. Demonstrates the maturity of the control functions of the brain and the ability to effectively control reactions.
*   **Sustainability of attention (CPT):** It decreases with monotonous or uninteresting tasks. The focus of attention requires dynamics.
*   **Visual working memory:** When memorizing visual information (diagrams, images), it can allow inaccuracies in the details. The general meaning is perceived well.

#### **2. The guide for the success of your student**

Understanding the unique style of learning your student.

**Individual features:**

*   **Pace:** Variable. High productivity in the presence of a call or a research task. A noticeable slowdown on routine tasks.
*   **Perception:** Optimally assimilates logically and structured information. Simple, clear visual materials serve as an effective support.
*   **Focus:** The targeted "spotlight" is on interesting material. It quickly weakens with boring tasks. Actively included in the discussion and solving complex problems.

**Which contributes to success:**

*   **Call:** Tasks formulated as a riddle, case or problem.
*   **Structure:** Clear checklists and step-by-step action plans.
*   **Intervals:** Short work sessions (for example, a method of prying).
*   **Practice:** The connection of knowledge with real life and personal interests.
*   **Projects:** Activities requiring self -regulation and analytical abilities.

**Which prevents progress:**

*   **Monotone:** Long lectures, the same type of exercises.
*   **Multitasking:** Simultaneous perception of different streams of information.
*   **Overload:** Too many small details, text or images.
*   **Uncertainty:** Lack of intermediate goals in big tasks.

<!-- page-break -->

#### **3. Recommendations**

##### **For home:**

*   **Organization of work:**
    *   Miss large tasks into small, controlled steps.
    *   Use a focus timer (20-25 minutes of work, a short break).
    *   Alternate activities every 30 minutes to maintain attention.
*   **Support and motivation:**
    *   Focus on the learning process, and not just on assessments. Mark perseverance and self -control.
    *   Help to find practical meaning in the material being studied.
    *   Provide a clean, not distracting workplace. Remove the phone during class.

##### **For school:**

*   **The wording of tasks:**
    *   Offer tasks that require analysis, comparison, search for errors or creative approach.
    *   Give a choice: "Solve any 5 problems out of 8."
    *   Introduce design and research activities.
*   **Lesson structure:**
    *   Start with a brief plan of the lesson, outling goals and content.
    *   Use supporting abstracts, intelligence cards and simple schemes for the visual structure.
    *   Alternate formats: short lectures, discussions, practical tasks, pair work.
*   **Individual approach:**
    *   Place the student closer to yourself to minimize distracting factors.
    *   Involve the olympiad, debate and school projects.

#### **4. Recommended training**

Practical exercises for the development of key skills in interactive form.

*   **Attention and concentration:**
    *   **Strategic Games:** Chess, go, complex board games develop attention and planning.
    *   **"Corrective test":** Exercise for crossing certain letters in the text to train attention stability.
*   **Visual memory and observation:**
    *   **"Find 10 differences":** Develops attentiveness to details.
    *   **"Remember and draw":** Reproduction from memory of simple figures after brief viewing.
*   **Coordination and bodily control:**
    *   **Juggling:** Improves the coordination of the "eyes-hand" and the interaction of the cerebral hemispheres.
    *   **Balance Bord:** He trains balance and concentration on bodily sensations.
*   **Rhythm and internal pace:**
    *   **Musical instruments:** The play on drums or taping rhythms under the metronome helps to “configure” the internal rhythm that affects the pace of any activity.
