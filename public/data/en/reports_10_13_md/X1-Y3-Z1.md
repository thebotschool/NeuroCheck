## Cognitive assessment report: strengths and growth zones (age: 10–13 years)

This report reveals the cognitive profile of the student: its strengths and development zones. Recommendations are aimed at increasing comfort and productivity in training and everyday life.

### 1. Cognitive profile

*   **Attention and self -control (TCP):** High concentration. The ability to long and stable work without abstracts and errors.
*   **Braking and control of pulses (Go/No-Go):** Asymmetry is manifested in the management of an unaware hand. Requires to strengthen interhemispheric interaction, which affects the letter and the implementation of complex instructions.
*   **Visual working memory (CPT):** Great ability to remember complex visual information. Effective assimilation strategies.

<!-- page-break -->

### 2. The guide for the success of your student

#### Individual characteristics

*   **Pace:** High and stable outside of written tasks. It decreases when working that requires thin motor skills.
*   **Perception:** The visual channel prevails. It more efficiently assimilates diagrams, images, diagrams, lists. Oral instructions require visual support.
*   **Focus of attention:** "Marathon" type. The ability to long -term concentration on one task. Call: switching between tasks and control of impulsive movements, especially when writing.

#### Which helps

*   **Visual tips:** Check lists, intelligence cards, color coding, diagrams, algorithms.
*   **Step -by -step instructions:** Decomposition of tasks on small, consistent steps (1, 2, 3 ...).
*   **Predictability and rhythm:** A clear schedule, understandable rules, stable rituals create confidence.
*   **Enough time for a letter:** Lack of haste reduces stress, improves quality.

#### What interferes

*   **Multitasking:** Difficulty with simultaneous listening and notes. Difficulties with quick switching between tasks.
*   **Rush and temporary pressure:** Exacerbates motor difficulties, leads to errors and inactivity, especially in the letter.
*   **Volumetric oral instructions:** Long monologues without visual support quickly overload.

<!-- page-break -->

### 3. Recommendations

#### For the house

*   **Homework organization:**
    *   **Priority.** Start with the most difficult written tasks while the energy level is maximum.
    *   **Intervals.** Use a timer (for example, 25/5 minutes) for effective work, taking into account its high concentration.
    *   **Planning.** Make a simple list of tasks with checkboxes.
*   **Support and encouragement:**
    *   **Praise.** Mark the effort and content, not only speed or handwriting. Example: "I see your diligence, the result is excellent!"
    *   **Progress.** Avoid comparisons. Focus on personal growth: "Today you wrote more carefully than before."
*   **Concentration.** Up to 30 minutes of continuous attention with interest.

#### For school

*   **The wording of tasks:**
    *   **Instructions.** Separate short, numb steps.
    *   **Duplication.** Correct the oral tasks on the board.
    *   **Samples.** Provide visual examples of design.
*   **Lesson structure:**
    *   **Place.** Optimal: a desk with a full view of the board.
    *   **Time.** Give additional time for written work. This creates equal conditions.
*   **Material feed:** Combine an oral explanation with the active use of boards, presentations, schemes, handout.
*   **IOOM elements (individual educational route):**
    *   **Flexibility.** Consider the permission to carry out part of the volumetric written tasks on the computer. This helps to reveal knowledge bypassing motor barriers.

### 4. Recommended training

Exercises for development. Strengthening interhemispheric ties, bodily control and coordination.

*   **Attention and control:**
    *   **"The sea is worried once."** Develops the reaction rate, trains braking.
    *   **Graphic dictations.** Drawing on cells under dictation.
*   **Coordination and bodily control:**
    *   **Cross -steps.** Walking in place: Right elbow to the left knee, and vice versa. Slowly, 1-2 minutes daily.
    *   **Juggling.** Start with two light objects (balls, scarves). Perfectly develops the coordination of the “hand-eye” and interhemispheric interaction.
    *   **Drawing with two hands.** Simultaneous creation of symmetrical figures (circles, squares) on a sheet of paper.
*   **Memory:**
    *   **“Find a couple” (memori).** Develops memory, attention, strategic thinking.
*   **Rhythm:**
    *   **Tapping rhythms.** Repeating the specified rhythms. Complicate. Use both hands. Corrections with the rhythm of the letter.
