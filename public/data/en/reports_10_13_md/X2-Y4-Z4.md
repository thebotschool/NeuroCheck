**Report on evaluating cognitive abilities**
 Age 10-13 years

This report contains key observations and recommendations based on the results of cognitive testing. It is designed to support the development of the potential of your student.

---

### 1. Test results

*   **Sustainability of attention.** Difficulties in holding the focus and quick processing of information are noticeable. This can manifest itself in absent -mindedness, especially when performing monotonous tasks.
*   **Self -control.** Difficulties with impulsiveness, especially with fatigue, are noted. The development of internal regulation skills is required.
*   **Visual working memory.** Difficulties are recorded with accurate reproduction of visual parts, especially when distracting. The development of effective strategies for memorization is recommended.

### 2. The guide for the success of your student

This information will help teachers and parents find the optimal approach to learning.

#### Features of perception

*   **Pace.** Work can begin quickly, but fatigue with difficult tasks leads to slowdown.
*   **Perception format.** The best absorption of information occurs with simultaneous visual and auditory presentation. Dependence on one channel (for example, a long lecture) quickly exhausts.
*   **Focus of attention.** Attention is unstable. The child is easily distracted. The productive concentration on one task lasts no more than 10-15 minutes, after which a short break is required.

#### Efficiency support

*   **Clear structure.** The presence of a lesson or assignment plan (1-2-3) simplifies perception.
*   **Step -by -step instructions.** The separation of large tasks into small steps (“1. Ideas. 2. Plan. 3. The first paragraph”) significantly increases efficiency.
*   **Visual supports.** Check lists, schemes, text highlighting, short memos.
*   **Timer.** Using a timer for short working intervals (for example, 15 minutes of work, 5 minutes of rest) helps to organize time.
*   **Work in pairs.** Interaction with a partner helps to keep attention.

#### Obstacles in learning

*   **Multitasking.** The performance of several actions at the same time (listen, write, watch) leads to a loss of information.
*   **Fast change of tasks.** The high pace of the lesson, which does not give time for switching, can cause "loss" from the process.
*   **Noise and distracting factors.** Extraneous sounds, mess on the desktop, the open door reduce productivity.
*   **Long oral instructions.** Without visual support, the information is quickly lost.

### 3. Recommendations

#### Home environment

*   **Organization of work.**
    *   Divide homework into blocks for 15–20 minutes. Provide short breaks (5 minutes).
    *   Use simple checklist checks to track the tasks performed.
    *   Provide a clean workplace. Remove distracting gadgets.
*   **Support and promotion.**
    *   Praise for diligence and process, not only for the result.
    *   Discuss strategies for memorizing and completing tasks.
    *   Keep calm with difficulties. Additional pressure is counterproductive.

#### Educational process

*   **The formulation of tasks.**
    *   The instructions should be clumsy, short and step -by -step.
    *   Duplicate oral tasks in writing (on the board or individually).
    *   Make sure of understanding the assignment by asking the child to repeat it in your own words.
*   **Structure and presentation of material.**
    *   Start a lesson with the voicing and recording of the plan.
    *   Place the child in the first or second desk to minimize distraction.
    *   Use visual materials: schemes, intelligence cards, videos.
*   **Individual approach.**
    *   Provide additional time for control and independent work (if possible).
    *   Allow the use of drafts for recording thoughts.
    *   Observation is recommended for a school psychologist or neuropsychologist for dynamic evaluation and correction.

### 4. Recommended training

These exercises contribute to the development of key functions. It is recommended to engage in 10-15 minutes daily, in a playful way.

*   **Development of attention.**
    *   **Graphic dictations.** Effectively train auditory attention and self -control.
    *   **Schultte tables.** Search for numbers in order by time. Start with 3x3 matrices.
    *   **"Find 10 differences."** Classic concentration and visual attention.
*   **Strengthening memory.**
    *   **The game "Memory".** Develops visual memory and concentration.
    *   **Exercise "Photographer".** Removing 5-7 items in 30 seconds followed by reproduction from memory. Gradually increase the number of objects.
*   **Coordination and control.**
    *   **Games with the ball.** Throwing the ball with the simultaneous completion of tasks (for example, the name of the days of the week).
    *   **Rhythmic exercises.** Cooling or tinkling of a given rhythm, complicated “okays”. Helps to establish a connection between planning and action.
    *   **Labyrinths on paper.** The passage of the labyrinth without tearing a pencil and touching the walls is an excellent exercise for self -control.
