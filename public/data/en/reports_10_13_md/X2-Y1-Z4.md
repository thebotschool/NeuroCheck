# Cognitive profile
## Analysis and recommendations for the age of 10-13 years

This report provides key data on the cognitive abilities of the student. The goal is to identify the strengths and areas for development by proposing practical recommendations for creating an optimal education environment.

---
## Key results

*   **Impulse control (Test Go/No-Go):** High level of self -control. The ability to quickly and accurate decision -making in certain conditions.
*   **Visual working memory:** Requires support. Removing visual parts is complicated in the presence of distracting factors or a large amount of data.
*   **Sustainability of attention (CPT test):** The area for development. Long -term holding of the focus is difficult, which can lead to fatigue and reduction in pace.

## The profile of the student

### Peculiarities
*   **Pace:** "Sprinter". The most productive in short, intense intervals. Quickly depleted from a long monotonous load.
*   **Perception:** It is best absorbed by clear, structured, visually presented information. Long oral explanations are less effective.
*   **Focus of attention:** Works "flashes". High concentration on short, interesting tasks. It is easily dissipated with routine or prolonged work.

### Effective approaches
*   **Step -by -step instructions:** Clear, brief algorithms of action.
*   **Visual supports:** Schemes, Mayind cards, color coding, checklists.
*   **Time management:** Short work cycles (15–20 minutes of work, 5 minutes of break).
*   **Predictability:** Knowing the lesson and rules plan contributes to self -control.
*   **Singlety:** Focusing on one specific action without switching.

### Factors that reduce productivity
*   **Multitasking:** The simultaneous performance of several actions is difficult.
*   **Long oral instructions:** Limited retention of more than two consistent teams.
*   **Monotone:** Long -term similar tasks lead to a loss of attention.
*   **Noise and disorder:** Visual and audio distracting factors exhaust the resource of attention.
*   **Sharp change in tasks:** It takes time for mental switching.

## Practical recommendations

### For the house
*   **Homework performance:**
    *   **Decomposition of tasks.** Miss large tasks into small, controlled steps.
    *   **Using a timer.** Use a timer for work intervals (for example, 20 minutes of work / 5 minutes of a break).
    *   **Prioritization.** Start with the most complex or unloved objects during the period of maximum productivity.
    *   **Organization of the workplace.** Eliminate extra distracting objects from the table.
*   **Support and encouragement:**
    *   **Focus on efforts.** Encourage efforts and efforts, and not just the result.
    *   **The development of visual memory.** Discuss images, look for associations, create pictures on pictures.
*   **Attention resource:**
    *   **Resource assessment.** Effective concentration lasts approximately 15–20 minutes.
    *   **Short breaks.** After this period, a short physical break is needed.

### For school
*   **The wording of tasks:**
    *   **The clarity of instructions.** Provide brief, clear, step -by -step instructions. Duplicate them in writing (board, card).
    *   **One task at a time.** Avoid multitasking.
    *   **Checking understanding.** Make sure that the schoolboy understood the task (ask to briefly repeat).
*   **Lesson structure and material presentation:**
    *   **Lesson plan.** Sound and write down the lesson plan on the board.
    *   **The optimal location.** Plant the student closer to the teacher/board, away from distracting factors.
    *   **Alternation of activities.** Change the activities every 15-20 minutes.
*   **Individualization of training:**
    *   **Additional time/drafts.** Consider the possibility of additional time in control work or the use of drafts.
    *   **Helpers cards.** Apply cards with algorithms or rules.

## Recommended training

The proposed exercises develop key skills. Perform them in a playful way, without coercion.

*   **For attention:**
    *   **Schultte tables:** Search for numbers in order. Develops concentration and expands the field of view.
    *   **"Find the differences" and "labyrinths":** Exercises for concentration and attention to details.
    *   **Graphic dictations:** Develop auditory attention and spatial thinking.
*   **For visual memory:**
    *   **The game "Memory" ("Find a couple"):** He trains short -term visual memory.
    *   **Exercise "Photographer":** Removing parts after a short review (15–20 seconds) of the room or picture.
    *   **Copying of patterns:** The sketching of complex patterns on the model, looking at it as little as possible.
*   **For coordination and bodily control:**
    *   **Neurohymnastics:** Exercises that involve both hands (for example, "fist-rib-lady", drawing with two hands). Improve interhemispheric interaction.
    *   **Games with the ball to the reaction:** Games to the reaction speed with the ball and additional tasks (for example, "edible-impregnated").
*   **For rhythm:**
    *   **Tapping rhythms:** Repeating the specified rhythms (cotton, on the table). Rhythm as an external organizer of attention.
