# Cognitive development report

The report reveals the cognitive features of your student based on testing. He offers practical recommendations to support and develop potential, focusing on effective assistance in study and at home.

---

# 1. Analysis of tests

*   **Attention and memory (CPT).** The retention of several parts is difficult. Support for the development of strategies for memorization is required, especially in the presence of distracting factors.
*   **Self-control (Go/No-Go).** He experiences difficulties with stopping and changing the action. Quickly tires from tasks requiring constant self -control and impulsion of pulses.
*   **Sustainability of attention (TCP).** Attention is easily dissipated, especially with monotony. External stimulation is required to preserve the focus.

# 2. The guide for the success of the student

## Peculiarities

*   **Pace.** It works with impulses. Quick start, but fatigue and loss of concentration without a visible result.
*   **Perception.** The best perception is through visual and practical channels. Visual supports and interactivity improve memorization.
*   **Focus of attention.** Unstable, easily switches. Difficulties with concentration on monotonous or long -term tasks.

## Support

*   Step-by-step instructions and checklists. Reduce anxiety, increase concentration.
*   Visual supports. Schemes, Mayind cards, color allocation. External support for memory and attention.
*   Short working intervals. Work on the timer (20/5). Saves cognitive endurance.
*   Clear and fast feedback. Supports motivation.
*   Rhythm and predictability. A clear schedule, understandable structure. Saves mental strength.

## Calls

*   Multitasking. Leads to errors and fatigue.
*   Long oral instructions. It is difficult to hold more than two or three steps.
*   Monotone. Long lectures or the same type of exercise quickly reduce attention.
*   Visual and audial noise. Powerful distracting factors.

# 3. Practical recommendations

## Home environment

### Organization of work

*   Miss tasks into blocks (15–20 minutes) with interruptions. Use the timer.
*   Make a simple work plan. Example: "3 examples in mathematics, 5 minutes rest, paragraph of history."
*   Create a permanent workplace. Eliminate distracting objects (phone, toys).
*   Discuss progress. Questions: "What happened the easiest? Where was it difficult?"

### Motivation and support

*   Praise the process, not only the result. "He worked well on the timer" more important "well done, five."
*   Consider the focus time (10-15 minutes on a difficult task). Provide breaks.
*   Help with leading questions. "What needs to be found?" Instead of ready -made solutions.

## School environment

### Tasks

*   Instructions: briefly, clearly, one step.
*   Duplicate oral tasks in writing (board, card).
*   Attach attention to the instructions: eye contact, appeal by name.

### Lesson and material

*   Place the student closer to the teacher, away from distracting factors.
*   Use the visual schedule of the lesson (3-4 points) on the board.
*   Turn on short warm -ups/change of activity every 15-20 minutes.
*   Apply interactive methods: cards, online quiz, steam work.

### Individual route

*   Provide extra time for tests.
*   Adapt the volume of tasks (for example, 6 examples instead of 10, with an emphasis on understanding).
*   Use supporting schemes and algorithms (“How to solve the problem”: 1. Read. 2. Emphasize. 3. Write “Given”).

# 4. Exercises for development

Practice for development. Regular exercises (5-10 minutes daily) will help strengthen key skills.

## For attention

*   **Corrective test.** Sure the letters "A" or circle the prepositions "B in the text.
*   **Schultte tables.** Search for numbers from 1 to 25 for a while. Available online and in applications.

## For memory and sequence

*   **The game "Memory".** Classical game for visual working memory.
*   **Graphic dictation.** Drawing on cells according to oral instructions.

## For self -control and coordination

*   **The game "Yes, and not say."** Training of impulsive answers.
*   **Exercises for the intersection of the midline of the body.** The right hand to the left ear, the left to the right knee. Develops interhemispheric ties.

## For rhythm and bodily

*   **Tapping rhythms.** Repetition of a given rhythm. Gradual complication.
*   **Games with the ball.** Relocation of the ball at a pace. Synchronization of the body and brain.
