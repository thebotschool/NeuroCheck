# Report. Cognitive assessment

Understanding the potential of your student. This report reveals individual characteristics and offers recommendations for development based on cognitive testing.

## 1. Key results

*   **Working memory:** Excellent retention of visual information. High ability to memorize and use strategies.
*   **Braking (Go/No-Go):** Impulsiveness is manifested in situations requiring prolonged concentration. Self -control is an area for development.
*   **Attention and self -regulation:** There is a rush in answers and interruptions. Confirms the tendency to impulsive behavior.

## 2. Profile of learning

Understanding the individual style of your student.

### Individual characteristics

*   **Pace:** Tall, often impulsive. Prone to fast, but not always thought -out answers.
*   **Perception:** Effectively assimilates visual information: diagrams, tables, infographics, video.
*   **Attention:** High on short, dynamic tasks. Quickly depleted with monotonous work. Effective focus holds up to 15–20 minutes.

### Optimal conditions

*   Visual hints: checks, algorithms, step-by-step instructions.
*   Alternation of work and rest: using a timer (20 minutes of work, 5 minutes of break).
*   Separation of tasks into small, understandable steps.
*   Pauses for self-control: the "stop" rule (slow inhale-output).
*   Predictability: clear rules and expectations.

### Factors that impede training

*   Multitasking.
*   Hurry and competition in speed.
*   Long oral instructions without visual support.
*   Distracting noise and chaos.

## 3. Applied recommendations

### At home

*   **Organization of work:**
    *   Lessons in short blocks: 20 minutes of work, 5 minutes of physical activity.
    *   Using checklists for day/week to track progress.
*   **Support and encouragement:**
    *   Praise for accuracy, following the instructions and the efforts.
    *   Calmly analyze errors, focusing on future actions.
*   **The structure of the day:**
    *   Create a predictable daily routine to reduce cognitive load.
    *   Organize workspace without distracting factors.

### At school

*   **The wording of tasks:**
    *   Decomposition of large tasks for small subtasks with a clear result.
    *   Visual duplication of oral instructions (on the board, in a printout).
*   **Lesson structure:**
    *   Placing a student closer to the teacher, away from distracting factors.
    *   Active use of visual materials (presentations, diagrams, video).
    *   Providing a small pause to think about an answer in an oral survey.
*   **Style style:**
    *   Structured material feed. Avoid monotony.
    *   Promotion for thoughtfulness and accuracy, and not for speed.
*   **Individual approach:**
    *   Consideration of the possibility of additional time in control work.
    *   Permission to pass large design work in parts.

## 4. Development programs

Practical exercises for the development of self -control and attention in a playful way.

### Attention and self -control

*   **The game "Delay!":** Motor impulse stop training.
*   **Board games:** Games requiring the expectation of the move, compliance with rules and planning (for example, chess, checkers, caurson, jenga).
*   **Graphic dictations:** Development of concentration and following instructions.

### Coordination and rhythm

*   **"Fist-Robric Ladon":** Sequential implementation of movements, development of coordination.
*   **RIGHT RIGHTS:** The exact repetition of the rhythm heard.

### Memory and attentiveness

*   **Memory game:** The development of visual memory and concentration.
*   **Exercise "Corrective test":** Training of stability of attention (for example, deleting certain letters in the text).
