### Cognitive assessment: review

This report is your guide for disclosing the potential of a student. It is based on the results of cognitive testing and offers practical recommendations for home and school, helping to better understand the strengths and growth zones.

---

### 1. Test results

*   **Visual memory (CPT):** Effective memorization using personal strategies. Support is required in distinguishing between similar parts.
*   **Braking function (Go/No-Go):** Difficulties with impulse control and fatigue are noted with tasks requiring constant self -control.
*   **Sustainability of attention (TCP):** High ability to long and stable concentration on one task. Key strong side.

### 2. Individual learning profile

This section describes the unique style of the student’s brain.

#### Individual characteristics

*   **Pace:** High endurance during prolonged work on one task. The tendency to impulsiveness and haste, if necessary, quickly switching.
*   **Perception:** Priority of visual and structured information. Hearing instructions without visual support are less effective.
*   **Focus of attention:** Deep concentration on one task. Difficulties with switching and filtering impulsive reactions are possible. The development of the "brake" function is required.

#### Which maintains effectiveness

*   Step -by -step instructions.
*   Visual supports.
*   Predictability.
*   Pause for self -testing.
*   Promotion of the plan.

#### What creates difficulties

*   Multitasking.
*   Completely oral instructions (especially long).
*   Tasks "at speed".
*   Hurry and pressure.
*   High noise level.

### 3. Practical recommendations

#### For the house

*   **Homework**
    *   Divide the tasks into intervals (20–25 minutes with breaks).
    *   Use checklist checks to track progress.
    *   Introduce the rule "10-minute pause" before the final check.
*   **Support**
    *   Mark attentiveness and accuracy, not speed.
    *   Analyze impulsive errors constructively, without censure.
*   **Attention resource:** Up to 40-50 minutes on monotonous tasks. On tasks with high self -control (for example, control work), the resource is exhausted in 15–20 minutes.

#### For school

*   **The formulation of tasks**
    *   Provide specific, step -by -step instructions (in writing).
    *   Select key elements in the formulations of tasks.
*   **Organization of the lesson**
    *   Provide the optimal place: the first desks, away from distracting factors.
    *   On tests: Allow the draft, allocate time for verification (5-7 minutes).
*   **Teaching style**
    *   Use a combined approach: visualization + audio feed.
    *   Avoid frequent change of activity.
*   **Individual adaptation**
    *   Consider the provision of additional time for control (for verification).
    *   Allow me to ask clarifying questions on the wording.

### 4. Targeted training

The following exercises develop identified cognitive functions.

*   **Attention and impulse control**
    *   Board games requiring expectations and strategies (“Chess”, “Carcasson”, “Jackal”).
    *   The game "Yes and there is no talk."
*   **Coordination and rhythm**
    *   Juggling.
    *   Tapping rhythms.
*   **Visual memory and accuracy**
    *   Copying complex patterns or patterns on cells.
    *   Games "Find 10 differences" or searching for items.
*   **Bodily and emotional control**
    *   Respiratory exercises (for example, "square breath").
