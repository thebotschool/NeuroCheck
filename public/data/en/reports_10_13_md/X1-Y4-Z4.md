### Cognitive profile: individual report (10–13 years)

This report is designed to reveal the strengths and areas for the development of your student. Recommendations will help create an optimal environment for its successful training and harmonious growth.

---

### 1. Key conclusions

*   **Visual working memory:** The ability to process and maintain visual information is a pronounced strength. Effectively applies strategies for memorization.
*   **Inhibitory control:** There are difficulties with the suppression of impulsive reactions and maintaining concentration with rapid switching. The development of self -control is required.
*   **Sustainability of attention:** The difficulty in long -term attention is noted, which can lead to fatigue and a decrease in productivity.

### 2. The guide for the success of your student

This section reveals the unique style of learning your student. Understanding these features contributes to effective interaction and optimal development.

#### Individual style

*   **Pace:** The pace of learning is uneven. Short breaks and time are needed to adapt to a new task. A high pace or frequent change of actions reduces concentration and increases the likelihood of errors.
*   **Perception format:** **Visual perception** - The main strong side. Information is more effective through schemes, graphs, video and structured text. Long -term oral explanations are perceived with difficulty.
*   **Focus of attention:** Attention is selective. Deep immersion is possible if there is interest (supported by strong memory). At the same time, it is easily distracted by external factors and quickly loses concentration on routine or monotonous tasks.

#### Optimal conditions

*   **Visual supports:** Check lists. Plans on the board. Color selection of key information. Mind cards.
*   **Step -by -step instructions:** Single, clear instructions. Example: “First, the first two examples decide. After completing, show me. "
*   **Predictability and rhythm:** The predictable structure of the day and lesson. Using a timer for interval work (for example, 20 minutes of work, 5 minutes of break).
*   **Physical activity:** Short breaks for physical activity. They contribute to the restoration of concentration.

#### Effective barriers

*   **Multitasking:** The need to perform several tasks at the same time. Simultaneous listening and recording can reduce performance.
*   **Long oral instructions:** Oral instructions containing more than 2-3 steps are difficult to absorb.
*   **Sensory overload:** Excessive noise surrounded. Visual mess. Distracting background stimuli.
*   **Rush and pressure:** Requirements for speed. Pressure. This increases the anxiety and number of errors.

### 3. Recommendations

#### Home environment

*   **Homework organization:**
    *   Divide the tasks into intervals for 15-20 minutes, alternating with 5-minute breaks. Use the timer.
    *   Start difficult tasks at the beginning, when attention is as much as possible.
    *   Together, create a visual task plan. The mark of the executed points will enhance the sensation of control.
*   **Support and encouragement:**
    *   Focus praise on **process and efforts**, not on the result. Example: "I see how you tried to concentrate, it was a success!" Or "You have made a great plan!"
    *   Provide a quiet workplace. Eliminate distracting objects from the field of view.
*   **Attention:**
    *   Productive attention is limited to 15-20 minutes. Do not expect a longer concentration without interruptions.

#### School environment

*   **The wording of tasks:**
    *   Provide the instructions clearly, briefly and step by step. Duplicate oral instructions visually (on the board, in the memo).
    *   Example: instead of “Solve number 156”, use: “Open page 45. Find number 156. Read the first task. Perform only it. ”
*   **Structure and feed:**
    *   Optimal placement: closer to the teacher, away from distracting factors.
    *   Actively use visual materials: presentations, diagrams, video. The lesson plan on the board serves as a visual support.
*   **Elements of an individual educational route:**
    *   Additional time for tests.
    *   The ability to divide large control into several stages.
    *   The use of cards with step -by -step algorithms for typical tasks.

### 4. Development of skills

Simple exercises integrated into everyday life in a playful way contribute to the development of key skills.

**Attention and self -control**
*   **Board games:** “Find 10 differences”, “Cortex”, “Dobble”. Passing the labyrinths.
*   **Schultte tables:** Search for numbers in order, for a while. Start with 3x3 format, gradually complicate.
*   **The game "Delay-Bugs":** Instant stop at a signal. He trains impulse control.

**Coordination and motor control**
*   **Balance exercises:** Walking along the line, stand on one leg (with eyes closed). Classes on a balancing board.
*   **Juggling:** Start with two items. Develops the coordination of the "eye-hand" and concentration.

**Memory (strengthening of strengths)**
*   **Memory game:** Search for paired cards.
*   **The game "What has changed?"**: Place 5-7 items. The child remembers, turns away. Change the location or composition of objects.

**Rhythm (organization of attention)**
*   **Tapping rhythms:** You set a simple rhythm, the child repeats. Gradually complicate.
*   **Metronome:** Use a quiet metronome to maintain a pace during monotonous written work.
