### 2. The guide for the success of your student

High potential requires targeted support. The self -control system is present, but needs synchronization with the pace of action.

#### Key features

*   **Pace:** Dual. Capable of deep immersion in complex tasks. In situations requiring a quick reaction or assimilation of oral instructions, a tendency to haste is manifested.
*   **Perception:** Optimally assimilates visually structured information (schemes, plans, algorithms). Long oral instructions are difficult.
*   **Focus of attention:** Stable in the presence of a clear goal and rules. It is scattered with a chaotic presentation of information or the need to hold long sequences.

#### Support factors

*   **Visual supports:** Check lists, schemes, step-by-step instructions, intelligence cards.
*   **Clear rules:** Simple algorithms of action. Example: "First listen, then answer."
*   **Pause for thinking:** Time for understanding. Encouraging accuracy over speed.
*   **Predictability:** A pre -known class of classes contributes to self -control.

#### Potential barriers

*   **Long oral instructions:** Multi -step verbal instructions lead to focus loss.
*   **Multitasking:** The simultaneous performance of several actions overloads the system.
*   **Speed ​​competitions:** Enhanced haste, provoking errors.
*   **Unstracted stream:** It makes it difficult to allocate key details.

<!-- page-break -->

### 3. Recommendations

#### For the house

*   **Homework performance:**
    *   Divide big tasks into small steps. Use checks or stickers with a sequence of actions.
    *   It is recommended 20–25 minutes of continuous operation, then a short break (5 minutes).
    *   Joint planning before starting work: Discuss the sequence of steps. This activates self -control.
*   **Support and encouragement:**
    *   Encourage the process, not just the result. Focus efforts and following the plan.
    *   In case of errors due to haste, calmly suggest returning to the instructions and check the missed steps.
    *   Discuss the events of the day, asking them to tell them in order. This trains a consistent memory.

#### For school

*   **The wording of tasks:**
    *   Instructions: short, clear, step -by -step. It is advisable to duplicate them on the board or in handouts.
    *   Example: instead of "retell the text", use a text plan of 3-5 points and retell it. "
*   **Lesson structure and workplace:**
    *   Accommodation: Provide the visibility of the board. Visual supports are a key tool.
    *   Lesson plan: voice a short plan at the beginning of the lesson.
*   **Style style:**
    *   Strengths: use the ability to deep concentration. Involve in design and research activities, where accuracy is important, not speed.
    *   Pause: make a small pause before the question. This gives time to process and helps to restrain the impulsive answer.
    *   Olympiad activity: Consider participation in olympiads requiring logic and analysis. Regulatory functions correspond to this.

### 4. Games for development

The following games and exercises contribute to the development of skills in an interactive form.

*   **Attention and self -control:**
    *   **Reading by roles:** Read the dialogs by transmitting intonations and pauses. You can synchronize with audiobook.
    *   **Board games:** "Chess", "checkers", "Karcasson", "Colonialists". Develop planning, waiting for the course and following the rules.
*   **Memory for the sequence:**
    *   **The story on the chain:** Players alternately add sentences, repeating the previous ones.
    *   **Compilation of comics:** Lay out 4-6 pictures in the correct order and make a story.
    *   **Culinary recipes:** Cooking on a step -by -step recipe is a practical training of the sequence.
*   **Body control and rhythm:**
    *   **Game "Mirror":** One player shows slow movements, the other repeats exactly.
    *   **Rhythmic exercises:** Rhythm reproduction with claps or topot. The rhythm organizes brain activity.
