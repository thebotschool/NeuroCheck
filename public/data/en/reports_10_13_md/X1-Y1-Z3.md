# Cognitive assessment report

This report is developed for parents and teachers. Its goal is to provide a clear understanding of the strengths of the student and areas for development. Recommendations are designed to create optimal conditions for successful training and harmonious growth.

## 1. Review of tests

*   **Working memory (CPT):** An excellent ability to hold and reproduce complex information, as well as plan actions. This is a strong side.
*   **Self-control (Go/No-Go):** A high level of reaction control, the ability to stop timely, if necessary. This indicates a mature self -regulation system.
*   **Reaction speed (TCP):** The tendency to impulsiveness in some situations, expressed in a hurry, interrupting or premature actions until the instructions are completely perceived.

**Key conclusion:** The student has the necessary cognitive resources for complex tasks (excellent memory, self -control). However, the use of these skills can be difficult due to a tendency to haste. The main task is the development of the conscious use of strengths through the development of a pause before action.

## 2. Profile of a student

### Individual characteristics

*   **Pace:** The tendency to a quick, impulsive start. The ability to long and thoughtful work, provided that the involvement is involved and a clear structure. A soft correction of the tendency to premature actions is required.
*   **Perception:** Effectively assimilates structured and visual-logical information (diagrams, algorithms, sequences). Less effective with quick oral instructions, as it can miss important details, starting to act prematurely.
*   **Focus of attention:** The ability to deep concentration. The start of work is a vulnerable moment requiring smooth inclusion without abstracts.

### Which contributes to success

*   Visual supports: checklists, diagrams, intelligence cards, highlighting keywords.
*   Step-by-step instructions: dividing tasks into 3-4 small stages.
*   A pause before starting: a clear instruction to repeat the task and determining the first step.
*   Praise for accuracy: Focus on the quality of execution, not at speed.
*   Rhythm and predictability: a clear daily routine, understandable rules and rituals.

### Which prevents progress

*   Multivativity: The performance of several tasks simultaneously leads to errors.
*   Oral instructions without visual support: especially in a quick or noisy environment.
*   Speed ​​competitions: provoke a rush, impulsiveness, reduce quality.
*   The pressure of time at the beginning of the task: phrases that stimulate a rush enhance the vanity.
*   Unstructured environment: chaos at the workplace, background noise.

## 3. Recommendations

### For the home environment

*   **Organization of tasks:**
    *   Miss large tasks into parts. Example: instead of “Make mathematics” - “First task 1, then 5 minutes break, then examples 2”.
    *   Use a timer to structuring time (20-25 minutes of work, 5 minutes a break with physical activity).
    *   Before starting work, pronounce the action plan together: "What needs to be done? Where to start? What next?"
*   **Support and motivation:**
    *   Praise for the process and accuracy: “I see, you tried and checked everything”, “The work was done without blots.”
    *   Learn to cope with an impulse: softly interrupt interruption, offering to wait for the end of thought.
*   **Attention management:** Effective concentration lasts up to 25 minutes. A short (5-10 minutes) break with movement is recommended.

### For the school environment

*   **The wording of tasks:**
    *   The instructions should be chat, brief, step -by -step. It is advisable to duplicate oral instructions in written (on the board, cards).
    *   At the beginning of the lesson, fix the plan on the board: 1. Checking DZ. 2. New theme. 3. Exercise.
*   **Lesson structure:**
    *   Provide a “readiness signal” and a pause between the instructions and the start of execution. Example: "You can start on my command" Start! "."
    *   Place the student closer to the teacher, away from distracting factors (window, door, rear desks).
*   **Style style and individual approach:**
    *   A calm, structured feed style, based on logic and algorithms, is effective.
    *   Development of regulatory skills (UUD): Tasks are recommended with a preliminary preparation of an action plan and subsequent self-testing on the checklist.

## 4. Practical exercises

The following exercises contribute to the development of self -control and awareness in a playful way.

*   **For attention and self -control:**
    *   Graphic dictations: development of auditory attention, following instructions.
    *   Games "Forbidden Movement" / "Yes and there is no say": the development of control of automatic reactions.
    *   Reading by roles: pause training, intonation, pace slowing down.

*   **For memory and sequence:**
    *   Board games: "Memory", "Find a couple."
    *   Stories by pictures (4-5): the development of logical ties.

*   **For rhythm and motor skills:**
    *   RIGHT RITMES: Repetition of rhythms (from simple to complex). Promotes streamlining the internal pace.
    *   Games with the ball: the development of coordination and reaction (for example, "edible-universal" at a fast pace).

*   **For awareness:**
    *   Exercise "Delay-Otomory": instant freezing at a signal for 10-15 seconds. Develops bodily control.
    *   Passage of labyrinths: drawing a line without touching the walls. Teaches smoothness and control of movements.
