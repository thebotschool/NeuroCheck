**Cognitive profile: report**

This report is an analysis of the cognitive functions of your student, identifying the strengths and areas for development. The goal is to provide practical recommendations to increase the efficiency and comfort of learning both at home and at school.

---

### **1. Test results**

*   **Working memory (sequence):** High ability to memorize information, but difficulties with maintaining its correct sequence.
*   **Self -control (inhibition):** Difficulties with the suppression of impulsive reactions were noted. Making decisions often occurs until complete comprehension.
*   **Attention (CPT):** The concentration of attention is unstable. There is a tendency to distract, especially with monotonous or long -term tasks. External stimulation is required to maintain the focus.

### **2. The guide for the success of the student**

This section offers an understanding of an individual style of teaching a student, describing the optimal conditions for his development and success.

#### Features of training

*   **The pace of work:** Variable. It is characterized by periods of quick, but impulsive activity, replaced by slowdown, if necessary, consistent processing of information.
*   **Perception format:** Visual-concrete. Preference is given to information presented in the form of diagrams, lists, images and step -by -step instructions. Oral explanations are absorbed less effectively.
*   **Focus of attention:** Selective. Attention is activated by novelty and interest, but quickly fades away with monotonous tasks. An effective concentration on one educational task is up to 10-15 minutes.

#### Success factors

*   **Visual materials:** Check lists, algorithms, schemes, highlighting key points.
*   **Crushing of tasks:** Savior of large tasks into small, controlled steps.
*   **Alternation of activity:** Regular short breaks (2-3 minutes) after 10-15 minutes of concentrated work.
*   **Pause before action:** The formation of a habit of a short stop and reflecting before the answer or action.
*   **Elimination of distracting factors:** Reducing background noise and providing instructions on one channel.

#### Obstacles in learning

*   **Multitasking:** Simultaneous attention requirements reduce productivity.
*   **Long oral instructions:** Multicomponent verbal instructions are difficult to keep in memory.
*   **Stress from rush:** The need for quick decision -making provokes impulsive answers.
*   **Monotone:** Uniform or prolonged tasks lead to rapid fatigue and loss of concentration.

### **3. Recommendations**

#### Home environment

*   **Organization of tasks:**
    *   Joint creation of visual plans for day/evening.
    *   The use of a timer for structuring working intervals.
    *   Preliminary pronouncing the algorithm for performing tasks.
*   **Support and motivation:**
    *   Focus on the promotion of efforts and strategy, and not just a result.
    *   Analyze errors associated with inattention in a constructive manner.
*   **Attention management:**
    *   Consider the limited duration of arbitrary attention (10-15 minutes) for uninteresting tasks. Avoid overloads.

#### School environment

*   **Instructions:**
    *   Provide short, clear, consistent instructions, preferably in writing.
    *   Offer one task at a time.
*   **Lesson structure:**
    *   Placement of a student in the zone of minimum abstracts (closer to the board).
    *   The use of visual supports (plans, diagrams, algorithms).
    *   Dynamic alternation of activities to maintain attention.
*   **Individualization:**
    *   Adaptation of the volume of educational material (for example, reducing the number of similar tasks).
    *   Resolution of auxiliary products for focusing (ruler, "window").
    *   Providing additional time to perform tasks requiring step -by -step processing.

### **4. Targeted development**

The proposed game activity contributes to the development of cognitive functions detected as growth zones.

*   **Attention and self -control:**
    *   **"Edible-Sreedy":** Develops the reaction rate and inhibit.
    *   **"Frozen!" / "The sea is worried once":** Strengthens bodily control and the ability to stop action.
    *   **Graphic dictations:** Develop auditory attention and following instructions.
    *   **Board games:** “Dobble”, “Drum”, “Find a couple” (focusing attention).

*   **Consistent memory:**
    *   **"Repeat me":** Reproduction of sequences of sounds, movements. Complicate the rhythm and length.
    *   **"Tell me in order":** Retelling of events, plots, algorithms. Stimulation of building logical chains.
    *   **Assembly according to the instructions:** Designing step by step according to the scheme (LEGO, models).

*   **Rhythm and coordination:**
    *   **Rhythmic exercises:** RIGHT RITMES, marching.
    *   **Coordination games:** Exercises on a balancing board, walking on limited surfaces. Improve bodily control.
