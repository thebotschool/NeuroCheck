**Cognitive potential report**

This report reveals the individual characteristics of the student. It is designed to help parents and teachers create an optimal environment for training and development, revealing strengths and offering strategies for growth.

### **1. Key indicators**

*   **Consistent memory:** The student demonstrates a strong memory for facts and images. The memorization of the information is excellent, however, building and preserving the sequence requires additional support.
*   **Self -control:** A high level of self -control and developed actions management skills. The ability to quickly make decisions and effective inhibition of impulsive reactions.
*   **Sustainability of attention:** Attention is stable. The schoolboy is able to concentrate on the task a long and productively, maintaining high accuracy and minimizing distraction.

### **2. Profile of learning**

Understanding the unique cognitive characteristics of the student contributes to the creation of the most effective and comfortable educational process.

#### **Personal style**

*   **Pace:** It works at a stable, focused pace. Tasks requiring sequence memorization (algorithms, plans) can be performed more slowly for deep assimilation. This is a manifestation of an information processing strategy, not unwillingness.
*   **Perception format:** The best assimilation of the material occurs with its structural and visual representation. Preference is given to schemes, plans, step -by -step algorithms instead of continuous text or oral presentation.
*   **Focus of attention:** High stability of concentration allows you to deeply dive into the task, maintaining productivity up to 40-50 minutes. The stronger side is the ability to work on integrated, homogeneous projects.

#### **Effective approaches**

*   **Visual supports:** Schemes, intelligence cards, timline, tables, infographics. Tools that help "see" structure.
*   **Step -by -step instructions:** Separation of large tasks into small, consistent stages. Check lines are an ideal solution.
*   **Logical ties:** Explanations through the format "Reason → Investigation". Understanding the logic of the sequence contributes to better memorization.
*   **Using strengths:** The support on high self -control and stable attention to solve complex, but fascinating problems.

#### **Possible obstacles**

*   **Unstructured information:** Large amounts of data that require accurately memorizing order without clear logic.
*   **Oral multi -step instructions:** Tasks involving several consistent actions without visual support can lead to confusion.
*   **Rush and multitasking:** The need for quick switching between tasks with different sequences of actions.

### **3. Way to success**

Individual recommendations for the support and development of the student.

#### **In the home environment**

*   **Preparation for tasks:** Before doing homework, offer to draw up a short plan: "What and in what order?" For volumetric work (essay, project), help determine 3-4 key points of the structure.
*   **Support:** Help not perform tasks *for* schoolchild, a *structure* their. Ask the leading questions: “Where will we start?”, “What is the next step?”, “Is everything taken into account according to plan?”.
*   **Promotion:** Mark not only the result, but also the process: a well -compiled plan, following the instructions, perseverance in difficult situations. This shifts the focus for efforts.
*   **Opening hours:** High concentration allows you to effectively work with blocks of 40-50 minutes. After that, a short break (5-10 minutes) for relaxation and a change in activities is recommended.

#### **In the school environment**

*   **Material feed:** Tasks, especially in the exact sciences, history and literature, are best formulated in the form of clear algorithms or step -by -step plans.
*   **Lesson structure:** Actively use visual tools: schemes, tables, handout with a dedicated structure. This is useful for the whole class and is critical for this student.
*   **Teaching style:** The most effective approach that connects the facts in logical chains. Explain *Why* Events follow in a certain order.
*   **Individual route:** Special IOM is not required, since cognitive functions are developed at a high level. Recommended:
    1.  **Individualization of support:** Providing supporting schemes or algorithms on complex topics requiring sequence to remember.
    2.  **Using strengths:** Active involvement in the design, olympiad and research activities, where the analytical abilities, attention and self -control of the student will be in demand and will be further developed.

### **4. Developing activity**

These exercises are aimed at strengthening consistent memory and maintaining strengths (attention, self -control).

*   **Memory and sequence:**
    *   **Games with the words:** “Snow lump” or “chain of words” - training of holding the order of elements.
    *   **Cooking:** Preparation of dishes according to new, step -by -step recipes - requires strict observance of the sequence.
    *   **Assembly according to the instructions:** Designers (Lego Technic), prefabricated models, origami according to complex schemes.
*   **Attention and self -control:**
    *   **Strategic board games:** Chess, GO, “Colonialists”, “Energy Set” - develop long -term planning and concentration.
    *   **Puzzles:** Sudoku, Japanese crosswords, logical tasks.
*   **Rhythm and coordination:**
    *   **Musical instruments:** Learning to play on notes (for example, piano, guitar) - the order of sounds and rhythm are important.
    *   **Juggling:** Develops coordination, a sense of rhythm, the ability to build a sequence of movements.
    *   **Dancing:** Learning dance ligaments, memorization and reproduction of the order of movements.
