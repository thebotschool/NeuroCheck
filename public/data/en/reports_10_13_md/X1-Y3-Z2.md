# Cognitive profile: Assessment of abilities
### Visual memory • Self -control • Attention

This report offers a focused view of the cognitive features of your student, providing key data and practical recommendations for development.

# 1. Review of tests

*   **Visual memory:** Outstanding ability to remember and retain complex visual information. Effective use of strategies.
*   **Self -control and switching:** There are difficulties in coordinating the movements of the left and right side of the body. This can affect the reaction rate, letter and multitasking.
*   **Sustainability of attention:** Difficulties with prolonged focus retention on monotonous or uninteresting tasks are noted. Attention is reduced in the absence of dynamics.

# 2. Successful training guide

Understanding individual characteristics helps optimize the educational process to achieve the best results.

## Individual characteristics

*   **Pace:** High efficiency in interest. Retain in the routine, especially in a letter due to the need for concentration and coordination.
*   **Perception:** The priority of visual materials (schemes, pictures, video) over the text and oral explanations.
*   **Attention:** Effectively on short, dynamic tasks. Dissipated with prolonged explanations or monotonous exercises.

## Learning support

*   **Visual supports:** Check lists, schemes, color release, short videos.
*   **Step -by -step instructions:** Decomposition of tasks for small, understandable stages.
*   **Rhythm and movement:** Short breaks, rhythmic exercises, a timer for interval work.
*   **Game elements:** Transformation of routine tasks into challenges.
*   **Clear structure:** Preliminary familiarization with the lesson plan or the algorithm of the task.

## Obstacles to concentration

*   **Multitasking:** The need to carry out several actions at the same time (listen, watch, adapt).
*   **Monotone:** Long, the same type of task.
*   **Abstract instructions:** Long oral explanations without visual support.
*   **Fast change of tasks:** The lack of time for mental switching between tasks.
*   **Distracting factors:** Noise, visual interference, conversations.

# 3. Practical recommendations

## For homework

1.  **Organization of work**
    *   **Short blocks:** Separate tasks for intervals (20 minutes of work, 5 minutes of break).
    *   **Visual plan:** Make a list of tasks. Mark the executed points.
    *   **Priority:** Start with complex or least favorite tasks.
2.  **Support and motivation**
    *   **Praise for efforts:** Mark the process and attached efforts, and not just the result.
    *   **Working environment:** Provide the workplace without distracting items.
3.  **Attention management**
    *   **Timer:** Use to visualize working intervals.
    *   **Soft return:** Return attention to the task when distracting correctly.

## For the school environment

1.  **Making tasks**
    *   **Clarity:** Formulate tasks step by step.
    *   **Visualization:** Use schemes, tables, coloring.
    *   **Personalization:** Offer checks, lessons, the possibility of choosing tasks.
2.  **Optimization of the process**
    *   **Location:** The first-second desks, the center of the class, far from distracting factors are recommended.
    *   **Dynamics:** The lesson should be interactive and dynamic (short surveys, interactives).
    *   **Written tasks:** Give more time. Consider the possibility of printing part of the tasks.

# 4. Developing classes

Recommended classes will help strengthen key skills. Do regularly for 10-15 minutes a day using the game format.

## Attention and self -control

*   **"Find 10 differences":** Classical game for training concentration.
*   **Board games:** “Set”, “vibble”, “corridor” (develop a reaction and retention of the rules).
*   **"Frozen!" (The sea is worried once):** Exercise for bodily control and self -regulation.

## Coordination and synchronization

*   **Mirror drawing:** Simultaneous drawing of symmetrical figures with both hands.
*   **Juggling:** Start with simple items (balls, scarves) for synchronization of the hemispheres.
*   **Finger gymnastics:** Rhythmic exercises and complex movements of the hands.

## Development of memory

*   **"Memory" (find a couple):** Training of visual memory in a game format.
*   **"Chain of words":** Repetition and adding new elements to the sequence.
*   **Removing sequences:** Reproduction of sequences of movements, sounds or images.
