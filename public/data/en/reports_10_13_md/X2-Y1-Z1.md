## The cognitive profile of the student

This report provides an individual view of the cognitive abilities of the student. He is designed to help parents and teachers understand the strengths and areas for development, forming an effective and comfortable educational environment.

### 1. Key results

*   **Attention (CPT):** Great concentration. The ability to long and stable work on the task, supporting the quality of the implementation.
*   **Self-control (Go/No-Go):** High level of self -control. Effective suppression of pulses and rapid switching between tasks, which indicates the flexibility of thinking and self -regulation.
*   **Visual working memory:** Certain difficulties with accurate memorization and reproduction of detailed visual information, especially when distracting or lack of a clear structure of the material.

<!-- page-break -->

### 2. Profile of a student

#### Features of training

*   **Pace:** Stable, confident pace of work. It is possible to slow down on tasks requiring accurate memorization of visual details, since it strives for unmistakable implementation.
*   **Perception:** He perceives structured, logically constructed information more effectively. The auditory and text presentation of the material is more reliable than the support on fragmented visual images without context (for example, icons without signatures).
*   **Focus of attention:** High and stable concentration. It is able to immerse himself deep into the task, minimizing the influence of external irritants.

#### Which helps

*   Clear, step -by -step instructions and algorithms.
*   Supporting abstracts, structured schemes with textual explanations.
*   Speaking of information aloud to activate auditory memory.
*   Mnemics, the creation of associations for memorization.

#### What interferes

*   Visual overload: a large amount of disordered visual information.
*   Multivature, requiring the rapid holding of visual details in memory.
*   Abstract presentations consisting only of images without oral or text comments.

<!-- page-break -->

### 3. Recommendations

#### Home environment

*   **Organization of work:**
    *   When working with saturated schemes or cards: encourage the creation of brief text plans or pronouncing content.
    *   Using color markers: helps logically group information in the notes, creating a structure for memorization.
*   **Support:**
    *   Effective working cycle: 40-50 minutes of focused work, then 5-10 minutes of a short break (without gadgets).
    *   Praise for perseverance and the desire to find your approach to complex material, and not just for the final assessment.
*   **Motivation:**
    *   Its strengths are self -control and attention. Encourage participation in project activities, debate, research.

#### Educational process

*   **The wording of tasks:**
    *   Tasks formulate clearly and sequentially. Duplicate oral instructions with text (on the board, in the handout).
    *   When explaining: use the bunch “I say and show”, reinforcing the visual series with oral comments.
*   **The structure of the material:**
    *   Presentations: Avoid overloaded slides. Use lists, numbering, headlines, brief signatures to images.
*   **Style and individualization:**
    *   The student is ready for complex intellectual challenges. Optimal style "Coach-mentor".
    *   Possible support: permission to use a voice recorder for lectures, the provision of supporting abstracts. Active involvement in the Olympiad and project activities, where its analytical abilities and concentration will be fully manifested.

### 4. Practical exercises

These simple exercises contribute to the development of skills in an accessible and game form.

#### Visual memory and attention

*   **"Memory" or "Find a couple":** Start with 8-10 pairs of cards, gradually increasing their number. Focus on strategic memorization of location.
*   **"Photographer":** Look at the picture or room 15-20 seconds. Close your eyes and list the maximum number of parts. Check yourself.
*   **Drawing from memory:** Look at a simple object (key, flower). Then draw it from memory, trying to convey the details as accurately as possible.

#### Coordination and bodily control

*   **Juggling:** Start with two items. Develops the coordination of the "eye-hand" and interhemispheric interaction.
*   **Rhythmic exercises:** The tapping or cooling of the given rhythm of one, then the other, then with both hands. It can be complicated by adding movements.
