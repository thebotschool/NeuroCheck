# Personal report: cognitive profile and recommendations

This report is an analysis of the cognitive profile of the student. It contains key conclusions and practical recommendations for revealing the potential and optimizing training.

### 1. Review of testing

*   **Working memory (CPT):** A high ability to memorize and retain complex visual information has been revealed. Strategic thinking is a strong side that supports training.
*   **Braking (Go/No-Go):** The difficulty in the coordinated work of the cerebral hemispheres is noted. This can affect the letter, coordination and multitasking (for example, listening and recording).
*   **Attention (TCP):** Significant difficulties with concentration and self -control have been identified. There are difficulties with holding the focus and independent regulation of activity.

### 2. The guide for the success of your student

This section describes the individual learning style.

#### Individual style of learning

*   **Pace:** Uneven. It takes time to adapt to the task and switching. Hashing causes stress and disorganization.
*   **Perception:** Visual. Visual, structured information is more effective: schemes, tables, checklists. Oral explanations are more difficult.
*   **Focus of attention:** Unstable. Attention is subject to the influence of external and internal factors. Monotonous or complex tasks can lead to loss of concentration.

#### Success factors

*   Phased instructions.
*   Visual materials.
*   Clear rhythm and predictability.
*   Using a timer.

#### Obstacles

*   Multitasking.
*   Long -term oral instructions.
*   Distracting environment: noise, visual mess.
*   A sharp change in activity.

### 3. Practical recommendations

#### Home environment

*   **Organization of work:**
    *   Divide the tasks of short intervals (15-20 minutes) with 5-minute breaks for physical activity.
    *   Together, form an evening plan. Mark the executed points.
    *   Organize workspace: a minimum of distracting items.
*   **Support and encouragement:**
    *   Encourage effort and process, and not just the result. Example: "I see how you tried to concentrate!"
    *   Avoid imperative phrases such as “Gather”. Instead, ask: “What interferes? How can I help? "
*   **Optimum duration:** The productive concentration is 15–20 minutes. Do not demand more.

#### School environment

*   **The wording of tasks:**
    *   Formulate the instructions briefly and sequentially. Avoid multi -component instructions.
*   **Lesson structure:**
    *   Designate the lesson plan at the beginning using the board. This increases predictability.
    *   Reinforce oral information with visual materials: terms, schemes, numbers.
    *   The optimal location: the first-second desk in the central row to minimize abstracts and visual contact.
*   **Style style:**
    *   Use a calm tone, reinforce the speech with gestures.
    *   Give the time to think about answers. Do not rush with switching between tasks.
*   **Individual approach:**
    *   Provide additional time for control and written work.
    *   Allow the use of printed templates to minimize rewriting and accent on the content.

### 4. Development program

The presented exercises are aimed at developing cognitive skills in a playful way. Recommended duration: 10-15 minutes daily.

*   **Focus and self -control:**
    *   **Correct samples:** Crossing out a given letter or symbol in the text.
    *   **Graphic dictations:** Following the drawing according to auditory instructions.
*   **Coordination and interhemispheric interaction:**
    *   **Drawing with two hands:** Simultaneous creation of symmetrical figures.
    *   **Exercise "Fist-Robric Ladon":** A consistent change in the position of the hand, gradually increasing the pace. Perform one, then the other, then with both hands.
    *   **Juggling:** Start with two light objects.
*   **Memory (support on the strengths):**
    *   **Memory game:** Search for paired cards.
    *   **"What has changed?":** Removing a number of objects with the subsequent detection of changes.
*   **Rhythm and bodily control:**
    *   **RIGHT RIGHTS:** Repeating the specified rhythmic drawings.
    *   **Games with the ball:** Throwing the ball with the simultaneous name of words on the topic (for example, "cities").
