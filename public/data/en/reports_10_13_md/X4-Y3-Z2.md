Cognitive profile. Individual report (10–13 years)

This report will help to understand the cognitive development of the student. It contains practical recommendations for effective training and support.

---

### 1. Brief test overview

*   **Test for attention and working memory (CPT):** The focus of attention is not always stable. Removing sequences requires effort. Distribution, difficulties with the beginning of the performance of tasks are possible.
*   **Self-control test (Go/No-Go):** The coordination of the left and right hands requires development. Susynchronization of the hemispheres is possible, which affects the letter and complex tasks.
*   **Test for visual memory (TCP):** Interest in monotonous tasks quickly fades away. Atimal attention with short, dynamic activities.

### 2. Your schoolboy. Understanding the training

#### Key features

*   **Pace:** Moderate. It takes time to comprehend and start. Hurry reduces productivity.
*   **Perception:** Mostly visual and kinesthetic. Pictures, diagrams, color selection are effective. Training through movement, tactile experience. Long oral instructions are ineffective.
*   **Focus of attention:** Unstable. Concentration: 5-10 minutes. A frequent change in activity or short breaks is required.

#### Support: what works

*   **Visual supports:** Check lists, step-by-step instructions in pictures, highlighting keywords.
*   **Predictability:** Timer for tasks. For example: "10 minutes of work, then 3 minutes of rest."
*   **Decomposition of tasks:** Separation of a large task into 2-3 small, understandable steps.
*   **Physical warm -up:** Short cross -drawn movements before complex tasks activate both hemispheres.

#### Obstacles: what to avoid

*   **Multitasking:** One team at a time. Tasks take turns.
*   **Long oral instructions:** Only brief, step -by -step instructions.
*   **Rush and pressure:** Phrases like “faster” cause stress and disorganize.
*   **Background noise:** A working TV, loud conversations reduce concentration.

### 3. Recommendations. The path to success

#### Home environment

*   **Homework:**
    *   Divide the tasks into blocks for 10-15 minutes. Turn on active breaks.
    *   Priority: complex objects at the beginning of work.
    *   Visual schedule: icons, plan for the evening. Mark the completed tasks.
*   **Support and encouragement:**
    *   Praise efforts, not only the result. The emphasis on the process: "You tried very hard!"
    *   Comparison: only with their own achievements. Avoid comparisons with others.
*   **Working space:**
    *   Minimalism on the table: only current materials. Distracting items remove.

#### School environment

*   **Instructions:**
    *   Step -by -step instructions. Wait for the current step, then let's go.
    *   Accommodation: Closer to the teacher, further from distracting factors (window, door).
*   **Learning structure:**
    *   Support cards with algorithms. For example: 1. Read. 2. Emphasize. 3. Write. 4. Resolve. 5. Check.
    *   Key rules: highlight the color on the board.
*   **Individual approach (IOM):**
    *   Most often call by name to keep attention.
    *   Short tasks with a quick visible result.
    *   IOOM elements: using a reading line, additional time for written work, short motor pauses at the desks.

### 4. Training. Development of skills

These exercises develop key skills in an exciting form.

*   **Attention:**
    *   **"Corrective test":** Cross/circle the specified letters in the text. Start with 2-3 minutes.
    *   **"Find 10 differences":** Classical concentration training.
    *   **Board games:** “Dobble”, “drum”, “memorials” - develop attention and speed of reaction.
*   **Coordination and bodily control (interhemispheric interaction):**
    *   **"Fist-Robric Ladon":** The sequence of movements on the table. Train with one hand, then with two.
    *   **Drawing with two hands:** Symmetric figures (circles, squares) simultaneously with both hands (in the air or on the sheet).
    *   **Juggling:** Start with two light balls.
*   **Memory and planning:**
    *   **"What has changed?":** Remember 5-7 items on the table, then determine the changes.
    *   **Repeat of sequences:** Repeat the rhythm (pops, stomping) or a sequence of movements (sit down, slap, stomp).
*   **Rhythm:**
    *   Exercises to the music, repeating movements for the host.
    *   Fixing rhythm with spoons or palms. The rhythm helps the brain to organize its activities.
