# Cognitive abilities report
### Attention. Control. Memory (10-13 years)
This report reveals the potential of your student, offering a personalized view of his strengths and areas for development. We provide practical recommendations to support home and at school.

# 1. Assessment of abilities
*   **Attention and working memory:** The focused retention of several elements and following the plan is difficult, especially in the conditions of distraction. There is a tendency to a chaotic approach without a pronounced strategy of memorization.
*   **Self-control (Go/No-Go):** Strong basic self -control has been identified. The student demonstrates a high ability to contain impulsive reactions in the presence of clear and unambiguous instructions.
*   **Auditory attention and impulsiveness:** In more complex speech tasks, there is a tendency to haste. The reaction until the completion of the question indicates potential difficulties with executive braking, requiring simultaneous listening, understanding and response.

# 2. The guide for the success of your student

## Individual characteristics
*   **Pace:** The tendency to a quick, impulsive pace, which can lead to errors. The most effective work is achieved in measured, calm rhythm, without haste.
*   **Information perception:** Optimally assimilates the data presented by the elemental, with clumsy visual supports (images, diagrams, brief records). Long oral instructions are perceived less effectively.
*   **Focus of attention:** It is well concentrated on short, specific tasks with clear rules. Attention is easily dissipated with multitasking, extraneous noise or with the perception of the task as voluminous.

## Supporting factors
*   **Step -by -step instructions:** Separation of large tasks into 2-3 small, specific steps. Example: “First open a notebook. Then find exercise number five. Read the first sentence. "
*   **Visual tips:** The use of cards, checklists (with visual elements), highlighting key points with color.
*   **Rhythm and predictability:** The use of a timer for short work sessions (for example, 15 minutes of work, 5 minutes of rest). Creation of predictable rituals for a sensation of control.
*   **"Stop-signals":** An agreement on a conditional sign (a gesture or word, for example, “think”) to help to pause before the answer and avoid impulsive actions.

## Interviewing factors
*   **Multitasking:** Simultaneous requirement to listen, watch and write down. This can overload the cognitive system.
*   **Hurry and competition:** Tasks focused on speed or competitive spirit can cause fussiness and errors.
*   **Long oral instructions:** Extensive monologues without visual support quickly lose their effectiveness.
*   **Visual and audial noise:** Railing, loud conversations, background sounds can significantly distract.

# 3. Recommendations

## Home environment
*   **Homework:**
    *   Work in short blocks (15–20 minutes) with mandatory 5-minute breaks (drinking water, light activity).
    *   Before starting work together, make a simple plan: “1. Mathematics (task). 2. Rest. 3. Russian (exercise). "
    *   Remove everything superfluous from the table, leaving only necessary for the current task.
*   **Support and encouragement:**
    *   Praise for attentiveness and completeness of work, and not for speed. Example: "You are in no hurry and checked everything, excellent!"
    *   Help master the strategies of memorization: “Let's come up with a story for these words” or “We will draw a rule scheme”.
*   **Mercing of attention:** An effective concentration on one educational task is no more than 15–20 minutes. Exceeding this limit can cause fatigue and increase the number of errors.

## Educational environment
*   **The wording of tasks:**
    *   The instructions should be short, toned, one action at a time.
    *   Duplicate important oral instructions on the board (briefly, keywords).
*   **Structuring the material:**
    *   On the board and in handouts, use numbered lists, diagrams, highlight the main color or frame.
    *   Provide clear algorithms for standard tasks.
*   **Organization of space and process:**
    *   The optimal place in the class: the first-second desk, in the center, to minimize distracting factors and ensure direct visual contact with the teacher.
    *   Before the question of the question, make sure of visual contact and make a short pause to think about the answer.
*   **Elements of an individual route:** It is useful to include self-regulation tools in the learning process: checklists of self-testing, visual schedules, algorithm cards for completing tasks.

# 4. Developing exercises
These exercises are aimed at supporting and strengthening the identified cognitive functions. The key factor is regularity (10-15 minutes daily), not duration.

## Attention and working memory
*   **"Memory" / "Find a couple":** Classical game for training spatial memory.
*   **"What has changed?":** Place 5-7 items, offer to remember them. Ask the student to turn away, change the location or remove one subject. The task is to determine the changes.
*   **"Graphic dictation":** Drawing on cells under the voice dictation (for example, “two cells to the right, one up”).

## Control and reaction
*   **"The sea is worried once":** An effective exercise for the development of the ability to instantly fix the position by signal.
*   **"Edible-Sreedy":** He trains a quick reaction and executive braking (not to catch “inedible”).
*   **RIGHT RIGHTS:** Repetition of the adults of a given rhythmic pattern (cotton, on the surface).

## Coordination and sequence
*   **Board games-"Walking":** Develop the ability to follow the rules, wait for the queue and act sequentially.
*   **Designers or puzzles according to the instructions:** They stimulate following the plan and correlation of actions with the scheme.
