### **Cognitive assessment report**

This report offers a detailed analysis of the cognitive abilities of your student. The recommendations presented are not a diagnosis, but practical tools designed to optimize training and everyday development.

---

#### **1. A brief report for each test**

*   **Working memory (CPT):** Information is absorbed effectively. However, building a sequence can cause difficulties. The memory is strong, but requires structural support.
*   **Reaction inhibition (Go/No-Go):** Performing the tasks with the usual hand is easy. Switching to the other hand causes difficulties. This indicates the need to strengthen interhemispheric interaction affecting attention and letter.
*   **Attention and self -control (TCP):** There is a tendency to impulsive answers. Perhaps the beginning of the task until the instruction is comprehended or the priority of speed over accuracy.

#### **2. The guide for the success of your student**

**Individual characteristics**

*   **Pace:** The work is characterized by a quick, sometimes impulsive rhythm. Slow down or “freezing” are possible in a collision with complex sequences or multicomponent tasks.
*   **Information perception:** Information is best absorbed in a structured, visual format: schemes, brief plans, step -by -step instructions. Long oral explanations are less effective.
*   **Attention:** Sustainable attention is observed on short, clear tasks. Discrimination with multicomponent instructions, prolonged self -control or background noise are possible.

**Support**

*   Visual supports: algorithms, step-by-step schemes, checklists.
*   Clear rhythm: exercises involving both arms or legs for synchronization and concentration.
*   Signal "Stop": short instructions, previously agreed pauses for thinking.
*   One task at a time: focus on one action without switching between types of tasks.

**Obstacles**

*   Multivativity: simultaneous requests ("do it, then then") lead to errors or failure.
*   Hurrying and competition: Speed ​​installation promotes impulsiveness, reducing quality.
*   Long oral instructions: the brain captures the beginning, but can miss the rest.
*   Tasks for fine motor skills with one (unusual) hand: these tasks require significant cognitive resources, which leads to depletion of attention.

#### **3. Recommendations**

##### **For the house**

*   **Homework**
    *   Divide homework into blocks for 15–20 minutes. Provide short physical breaks between blocks.
    *   Make a simple action plan: 1. Mathematics, 2. Reading. Cross out the completed points.
    *   Use the timer: 15 minutes of work, 5 minutes of rest.
*   **Support**
    *   Encourage accuracy and deliberate actions, not speed. Mark the moments when the schoolboy was in no hurry.
    *   With difficulties with the sequence, say steps: “What first? And then what? ”
*   **Concentration**
    *   The productive concentration on the same type of task is about 15–20 minutes. Plan activity, given this limit.

##### **For school**

*   **Instructions**
    *   Give the instructions in stages. Use markers: "First ... second ...".
    *   Duplicate oral instructions on the board in the form of brief records or diagrams.
*   **Learning environment**
    *   Place visual materials (lesson plan, algorithms) in a prominent place.
    *   Recommended place in the classroom: Closer to the teacher and a board to minimize abstracts.
*   **Individual approach**
    *   Include tasks for two -handed coordination (modeling, drawing with both hands).
    *   Focus on correctness and accuracy, and not on speed.
    *   Give additional time for written work without affecting the assessment due to the pace.

#### **4. Recommended training**

These exercises and games are aimed at developing key cognitive functions. Daily practice is recommended in a playful way for 5-10 minutes.

*   **Attention and self -control**
    *   “Edible, unauthorized” or “forbidden movement”: develop a reaction of inhibition.
    *   Graphic dictations: form the skill of step -by -step instructions.

*   **Memory and sequence**
    *   “Repeat me”: reproduction of chains of movements or words (3-5 elements).
    *   “Cook”: memorization and repetition of “recipes” (3-4 ingredients) in the correct order.
    *   Pictures: Creation of stories in the series (3-5) pictures, building them in a logical sequence.

*   **Coordination and interhemispheric interaction**
    *   “Cross -toed steps”: slow touches with the left hand of the right knee, the right - left.
    *   Drawing with two hands: simultaneous creation of symmetrical patterns (circles, waves).
    *   Games with the ball: alternating chopping off the ball with the right and left hand.

*   **Rhythm and bodily control**
    *   “Rhythmic dictation”: tapping or polling the given rhythm alternately with hands/legs, then alternating.
