# Report. Cognitive profile. Age: 10-13 years.

This report was created to understand the unique cognitive strengths and zones of the schoolchild. Recommendations are based on testing data and are aimed at creating an optimal environment for training and growth.

---

### 1. Overview. Individual profile.

*   **Working memory (CPT).** Maintenance of information and sequence require development. A tendency to randomness without a clear plan is noted.
*   **Self-control (Go/No-Go).** High level. The ability to quickly and accurate response or abstinence.
*   **Sustainability of attention (TCP).** It decreases with monotonous or uninteresting tasks. Requires support.

<!-- page-break -->

### 2. Guide for success. Features of training.

Key aspects for effective interaction and training.

#### Individual characteristics.

*   **Pace.** Intermittent. High involvement at the start, but interest can quickly fade away with long or monotonous tasks. Efficiency is achieved in short, intense intervals.
*   **Perception.** Optimal - information in visual, short blocks. Visual supports (diagrams, images, lists) are critical. Long -term oral explanations are absorbed less effectively.
*   **Focus of attention.** Contrast. High concentration on short, reactive tasks. Attention is dissipated with monotony or multi -step processes.

#### Supporting factors.

*   Step -by -step instructions. Decomposition of tasks.
*   Visual timers. Check lists.
*   Rhythm. Predictability. Clear structure.
*   Game elements. Competitive format.

#### Factors that impede training.

*   Multitasking.
*   Long oral instructions.
*   Monotone. Lack of feedback.
*   The obscure goal of the task.

<!-- page-break -->

### 3. Practical recommendations.

#### For the home environment.

##### Organization of homework.

*   Separation into intervals: 15–20 minutes of work, 2-5 minutes of active break.
*   Check lists: a list of tasks with marks about execution. Promotes a feeling of control and completeness.
*   Verification of understanding: Ask to retell the instructions before starting.

##### Support and promotion.

*   The emphasis is on the effort, not the result. Example: "You have perfectly concentrated these 15 minutes."
*   Extract the manifestation of self -control. Mark the moments of successful abstinence from abstracts.
*   Provide a calm workspace. Minimize visual distraction.

#### For the school environment.

##### Formulation of tasks.

*   Brevity and step -by -step. One instruction is one action (for example: “write down”, “emphasize”).
*   Visual duplication of oral instructions (board, cards).

##### The structure of the lesson.

*   Optimal placement: closer to the teacher, away from distracting sources (window, door).
*   Inclusion of interactive elements: cards, short tests, tasks to a quick reaction. Uses strengths.
*   Provision of supporting abstracts or visual cards in front of complex topics.

##### Elements of an individual educational route (IOM).

*   Consider the official consolidation of the use of visual plans, checklists and written instructions.
*   The observation of a psychologist or neuropsychologist is recommended for monitoring progress.

### 4. Developing training.

Exercises for the development of key skills.

#### Attention.

*   **Corrective test.** The development of concentration.
*   **Search for differences.** Stimulates visual attention.

#### Memory. Subsequence.

*   **Memory.** Training of visual working memory.
*   **Removing sequences.** Development of memory and order.

#### Coordination. Self -control.

*   **Игра «Съедобное-несъедобное».** Развитие реакции и торможения.
*   **The intersection of the midline of the body.** Strengthening interhemispheric ties.

#### Rhythm.

*   **Rhythm tap.** The development of auditory attention and a sense of pace.
