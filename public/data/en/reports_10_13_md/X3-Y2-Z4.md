# Cognitive development report
## Memory, inhibition, attention (10-13 years)

We present a report developed for a deep understanding of the cognitive characteristics of your child. Here you will find testing data and practical recommendations for its comprehensive support.

---

# 1. Cognitive profile

*   **Visual Working Memory (CPT)**
    The ability to remember the facts is high. However, reproducing the exact sequence of data requires additional support. The child compensates, restoring information from the beginning or end.
*   **Impulse control (Go/No-Go)**
    There is a tendency to impulsive reactions. The brain is quickly tired of inhibition of the action. The development of self -control is required.
*   **Sustainability of attention (TCP)**
    The concentration of attention is unstable. There is a difficulty in long -term focus retention and quick information processing. Support for the development of these skills is necessary.

# 2. The guide for the success of your student

Understanding the individual characteristics of your child is the key to its effective development. This guide will help to build optimal interaction and avoid difficulties.

### Individual characteristics

*   **Pace**
    The pace of work is uneven. Impulsiveness at the start of the task is manifested, but with prolonged or complex tasks, rapid fatigue and a decrease in the pace are observed.
*   **Perception format**
    He prefers visual and step -by -step information: schemes, algorithms, short instructions. Long oral explanations are less effective.
*   **Focus of attention**
    Attention is unstable, easily distracted. It is difficult to keep the focus on the task for more than 7-10 minutes without changing activity or support.

### Optimal conditions

*   **Step -by -step instructions**
    Skipping tasks into short, specific stages. One instruction at a time.
*   **Visual tips**
    The use of visual supports: timers, plans, color selection, memolation cards.
*   **Clear rhythm and predictability**
    The predictability and rhythm of the day. Clear rituals of the beginning and completion of activity.
*   **Signals for a pause**
    Introduction of signals for a pause: "stop", "think." This creates an opportunity for a conscious reaction before action.

### Factors that make the process difficult

*   **Multitasking**
    The brain processes only one team at a time.
*   **Fast change of tasks**
    Oral instructions on ear are often not absorbed, and the child does not have time to switch.
*   **Noise and visual chaos**
    Background noise or many distracting objects overload the attention system.
*   **Long explanations**
    Long, abstract explanations without clarity cause fatigue and are not absorbed.

# 3. Practical recommendations

### Home environment

*   **Homework**
    *   Break work into blocks for 10-15 minutes with active breaks.
    *   Start with complex or unloved items.
    *   Make a visual plan for the evening (for example, with pictures).
*   **Support**
    *   Focus on efforts, and not just on the result.
    *   Create a calm, predictable atmosphere. Security contributes to self -control.
*   **Attention**
    Realistic time to keep the focus on the task is 7-10 minutes. Avoid overloads.

### School environment

*   **The formulation of tasks**
    Clearly, briefly, definitely. Example: "Open page 25. Decide example No. 3."
*   **The structure of the lesson**
    A visual plan on the board (checklist). Accommodation: Closer to the teacher, away from distracting factors.
*   **Style style**
    Interactive, involving. Example: "Show ...", "Repeat ...", "What is the next step?"
*   **Individual route (IOM)**
    *   Additional time for tests.
    *   The use of visual supports (cards with rules).
    *   Crushing of volumetric tasks.

# 4. Developing exercises

These exercises, integrated into everyday life, contribute to the development of key skills in an easy and exciting form.

### Attention and self -control

*   **The game "edible - inedible"**
    Develops a quick reaction and inhibition.
*   **Games "Delay!", "The sea is worried once"**
    Develop bodily control and the ability to stop.

### Memory and sequence

*   **The game "Repeat me"**
    An adult shows the sequence of movements, the child repeats. Gradually complicate.
*   **Cooking by recipe**
    The step -by -step execution of visual instructions trains consistent memory.

### Coordination and rhythm

*   **Neurohymnastics**
    Exercises with the simultaneous work of both hands synchronize the brain and improve control.
*   **RIGHT RITMES**
    Reproduction of a given rhythm develops concentration and a sense of rhythm that affect self -regulation.
