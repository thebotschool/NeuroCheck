Cognitive profile
Understanding the potential of your student.

This report provides key data on the cognitive features of your student. Recommendations are designed to create an optimal environment of training and development.

---

## 1. Cognitive review

Brief results of evaluating cognitive functions.

*   **Consistent memory:** It assimilates the information well, but experiences difficulties with its ordered reproduction. Active development of compensation strategies has been noted.
*   **Brake control (Go/No-Go):** There are difficulties with the suppression of impulsive reactions, especially with fatigue or prolonged concentration. This indicates the need for the development of self -control and energy management.
*   **Attention and processing speed:** It takes more time to process information. Attention is quickly exhausted, which makes it difficult to hold the focus and control of actions.

## 2. Instructions for the child as a student

Principles of effective interaction and support.

### Individual characteristics

*   **Pace:** Own, measured. Hurrying or multitasking leads to errors and overload.
*   **Perception:** Optimally perceives visual and step -by -step information: schemes, plans, algorithms. Oral instructions are less effective.
*   **Focus of attention:** Attention is unstable. Effective concentration lasts **15–20 minutes**, after which a short break for recovery is needed.

### Success factors

*   Visual support: plans, keywords, schemes, infographics.
*   Step -by -step instructions: clear algorithms for complex tasks.
*   Structured rhythm: 15-20 minutes of work, 3-5 minutes of break.
*   Similarity: Focus on one goal.
*   Predictability: a pre -known plan of the day or assignment reduces anxiety.

### Obstacles

*   Multivativity: simultaneous performance of several actions.
*   Distracting factors: noise, visual mess, extra open tabs.
*   Time pressure: haste, criticism of the pace.
*   Unstructured tasks: voluminous tasks without breakdown into stages.

## 3. Recommendations

Practical steps to support the development of a student.

### For the house

*   **Organization of tasks:**
    *   Miss home work into 15-20-minute blocks. Use a timer to visualize intervals.
    *   Make short 3-5-minute breaks between blocks.
    *   Make a joint plan for the lessons. Mark the executed points for a sensation of control.
    *   Start with more difficult tasks while the resources of attention are maximum.
*   **Support:**
    *   Focus on the process: "What is the plan?", "Do you need help?" Instead of "made lessons?"
    *   Mark efforts: "I see how you try to figure it out."
    *   Provide a permanent, quiet workplace. Eliminate distracting factors (for example, telephone).

### For school

*   **Submitting information:**
    *   Let's clearly, step by step, one at a time.
    *   Duplicate oral instructions in writing: on the board, screen, or on the card.
    *   Before starting work, make sure that the sequence of actions is understandable. Ask to repeat the plan.
*   **Lesson structure:**
    *   Start a lesson with the voicing and recording of the plan. This increases predictability.
    *   Recommended place: first-second desk in the center. This minimizes distracting factors and provides visual contact.
*   **Individual approach:**
    *   Provide extra time for control and independent work.
    *   Allow the use of pre -prepared plans for oral answers or essays.
    *   Savior large tasks into small stages with intermediate control.

## 4. Recommended training

These exercises develop key cognitive functions. The regularity and gradual increase in the load are important.

*   ### attention and self -control
    *   **"Corrector":** Removing a particular letter in the text (for example, "A" or "O"). Start with 5 minutes, increasing the duration.
    *   **Labyrinths:** The passage of labyrinths improves concentration and goal -setting.
*   ## consistent memory
    *   **"Chain of words":** Repetition and adding a new word in the sequence.
    *   **"Retelling of the day":** Evening retelling of the events of the day with an emphasis on chronology.
*   ### Coordination and Balance
    *   **Juggling:** Start with two items. Develops the coordination of the "eye-hand" and focusing.
    *   **Balance exercises:** Standing on one leg (with open/closed eyes), walking along the line.
*   ### Development of the rhythm
    *   **Tapping rhythms:** Repetition of a given rhythm. Helps to form an internal "metronome" and improves self -regulation.
