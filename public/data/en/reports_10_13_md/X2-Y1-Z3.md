## Cognitive profile. Age 10-13 years.

Understanding abilities for effective learning.

<!-- page-break -->

## 1. Overview. Key areas

*   **Visual memory (CPT)**
    Effective assimilation of visual data. Difficulties with the retention of exact images, especially during distraction, are noted. The tendency to interpret or confusion of similar elements.
*   **Self-control (Go/No-Go)**
    High level of braking and self -control. The ability to effectively suppress impulsive reactions. Significant strong side.
*   **Attention and reaction (TCP)**
    There is a tendency to hasty actions. Mistakes are possible due to premature reactions to complete perception of instructions. This affects accuracy, not of understanding.

<!-- page-break -->

## 2. Profile of a student. Potential and features

Your schoolboy is a capable student. The key task is to synchronize the speed of thought with the accuracy of execution. The brain functions in a quick start mode, while maintaining effective control mechanisms.

### Individual characteristics

*   **Pace**
    Fast, gusty. The essence is grasped instantly, but the details and conditions can be missed.
*   **Perception**
    Optimally perceives clear, structured data. Visual information without context or system is more complicated.
*   **Focus of attention**
    Effective concentration on interesting. In routine or multi -stage tasks, rush can cause errors.

### Supporting factors

*   Step -by -step instructions. A clear sequence of actions.
*   Visual anchors: diagrams, checklists, selection. Help maintain important details.
*   A pause before the answer (for example, 'count up to three'). Activates control.
*   Calm rhythm: pronouncing the rules, taping. Synchronizes thought and action.

### Factors that reduce efficiency

*   Speed ​​competition. Provokes impulsive errors.
*   Multitasking. Reduces the assimilation of information.
*   Blurry instructions. Specifics are required.
*   Quick pace of speech. Perhaps the premature start of actions.

## 3. Recommendations. Maximization of potential

### For the house

*   **Organization of work**
    *   **Tomato method method**: 20–25 minutes of work, 5 minutes break. Supports concentration.
    *   **Check lines for self-testing**: Simple points: "Are the conditions read?", "Are the calculations checked?", "Is it accurately completed?"
*   **Support and promotion**
    *   **Recognition of the process, not speed**: Mark awareness: "Great detail!", "Thoughtful approach!"
    *   **The optimal duration**: 20–25 minutes of effective work on complex tasks. Plan accordingly.

### For school

*   **The formulation of tasks**
    *   **Power**: Instructions step by step, especially for complex tasks. Step by step, with confirmation of understanding.
    *   **Visual selection**: Select keywords and numbers (color, emphasis).
*   **Structure and feed**
    *   **Location**: A place with a minimum of abstracts, providing visual contact.
    *   **Communication style**: Calm, clear, with pauses. Warn the importance of full listening.
    *   **Learning support**: Check lists are recommended for verification and memo with rules. These are support tools, not concessions.

## 4. Practice. Development through the game

Games and exercises for the development of key skills. In a light and exciting form.

*   **Attention and control**
    *   **Strategic games**: Chess, go, corridor. Develop planning and restraint of impulses.
    *   **"Stop-signal"**: Rhythmic action with instant stop at a signal. He trains a brake reaction.
*   **Visual memory and accuracy**
    *   **"Photographer"**: 30-second observation, then a detailed description. Improves fixation of visual images.
    *   **Intelligence cards**: Visual structuring of information: key data, connections. Promotes memorization.
*   **Coordination and bodily control**
    *   **Juggling**: (Starting with two goals). Develops coordination and interhemispheric interaction.
    *   **Musical instruments**: Especially shock or percussion. Develop a sense of rhythm and motor control.
