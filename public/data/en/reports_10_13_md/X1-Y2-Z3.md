# The cognitive profile of the student
_Cognitive test results_

This report is designed to reveal the unique features of your student. It contains an analysis of the strengths and zones for development, as well as practical recommendations for creating an optimal education environment.

---

# 1. Overview of the results

Key data of the cognitive profile.

*   **CPT test (visual working memory):**
    High ability to memorize complex information and strategic planning.
*   **GO/No-go test (pulse control):**
    Difficulties with timely braking are manifested, which can lead to hasty actions.
*   **TCP test (attention):**

# 2. Training profile

Understanding a unique learning style for effective interaction.

### Key features

*   **Pace:**
    Tall, impulsive. The tendency to act until complete perception of the instructions, which leads to errors due to haste.
*   **Perception:**
    It perfectly assimilates logical and visual data. Able to work with complex, multi -step tasks with their clear structure.
*   **Focus:**
    Attention is stable. The main challenge is to restrain the first impulse, not a distraction. The tendency to Falstart.

### Supporting environment

*   **Visual anchors:**
    Short, clear checks (for example: "Read. Emphasize. Record"). Reminders (sticker "stop") to activate thoughtfulness.
*   **Step -by -step approach:**
    Separation of tasks into small, consistent stages. Confirmation of the execution of each step before the continuation.
*   **Rhythm and predictability:**
    Measured pace of work. Propuring an action plan out loud: "What first? What then?"
*   **Priority of accuracy:**
    Encouraging thoughtfulness and correctness, not speed. "It is great that you were in no hurry and checked everything!"

### Factors that reduce efficiency

*   **Multitasking:**
    The simultaneous performance of several tasks or quick switching causes fatigue and error.
*   **Speed ​​competitions:**
    They provoke impulsiveness, fixing an undesirable strategy.
*   **Oral, verbose instructions:**
    Long audio explanations without visual support are often ignored.
*   **Noise and chaos:**
    An unstructured, loud environment enhances impulsiveness, interfering self -control.

# 3. Practical recommendations

Specific actions to optimize the educational process.

### Home environment

*   **Organization of work:**
    *   Separate the tasks of short intervals (15–20 minutes) with mini-perepasses.
    *   Make a simple visual plan before starting classes (for example: "Mathematics: No. 5, No. 6. Break of 5 min. Russian: Ex. 12").
    *   Use a timer to structured working blocks, not to stimulate speed.
*   **Support and encouragement:**
    *   Provide a quiet, calm workspace.
    *   Change the wording: instead of "Did you do your homework?" Ask "What is in plan? Let's see together."
    *   Focus on the process: "I see your zeal", "well done, what I double -check."

### School environment

*   **The wording of tasks:**
    *   Provide written instructions, preferably in a numbered list.
    *   Select the keywords ("not", "besides", "in order") for clarity.
*   **Lesson structure:**
    *   Place a student in a class zone with a minimum of distracting factors.
    *   Before the general task of the class, individually clarify the understanding of the instructions.
*   **Style style:**
    *   Use a calm, measured pace of speech. Avoid sharp transitions.
    *   **Individual educational route (IOM):**
        Officially allow the use of visual checks and timer. Focus the assessment of accuracy, and not on the speed of completing tasks.

# 4. Developing exercises

Games and exercises for the development of self -control and consistency of actions.

### Attention and self -control

*   **Graphic dictations:**
    Develop the ability to follow the instructions sequentially.
*   **"Find 10 differences":**
    Promotes careful and methodical scanning.
*   **Board games-"Walking":**
    Training compliance with priority and rules.

### Rhythm and coordination

*   **RIGHT RIGHTS:**
    Repetition of simple rhythmic patterns (pops, knock). It is permissible to use the metronome.
*   **Games with the ball:**
    Transmission and catching of the ball in a given rhythm or with the condition (for example, after cotton).

### Memory (maintaining the strength)

*   **"Memory" ("Find a couple"):**
    Develops visual memory and focus.
*   **"Repeat the sequence":**
    Restoring the order of objects after changing their location.

### Bodily control

*   **"The sea is worried once":**
    A classic exercise for the ability to instantly freeze at a signal, training "stop-reaction".
*   **Respiratory practices:**
    For example, "breathing on the square" (inhale, delay, exhale, delay of 4 counts). Promotes to calm and control impulses.
