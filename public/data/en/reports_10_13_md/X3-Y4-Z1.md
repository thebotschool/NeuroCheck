# Cognitive assessment
## CPT-GNG-TCP (10–13 years)

The report is designed to understand the strengths and zones of the development of the student. Recommendations are based on test results. The goal is to create an effective environment for training and growth.

# 1. Test results

## Consistent memory (CPT)
The student remembers data (pictures, words) well, but can confuse their sequence. Support for building logical chains is necessary.

## Self-control (Go/No-Go)
Difficulties with the suppression of impulsive reactions and a quick switch between tasks. There is a quick fatigue from tasks requiring volitional control and concentration.

## Sustainability of attention (TCP)
In a stable and predictable environment, attention remains stable and long. This is a strong side that you can rely on.

# 2. The guide for the success of your student

## Individual characteristics
*   **Pace:** Needs a leisurely, measured approach. A quick pace can lead to errors.
*   **Perception:** It better assimilates visual, step -by -step information: schemes, algorithms, lists. Audial perception without visual support is less effective.
*   **Attention:** Stable on simple, monotonous tasks. It is depleted if necessary by quick switching, making decisions or suppressing the first pulse.

## Which helps
*   **Visual support:** Schemes, timline, cards for structuring information and sequence of actions.
*   **Clear instructions:** Tasks broken into understandable stages.
*   **Predictable rhythm:** Clear alternation of work and rest (for example, 20 minutes of work, 5 minutes of break).
*   **Timer:** Using a timer to visualize the allotted time.
*   **Focus on one task:** Work on one specific task, excluding multitasking.

## What interferes
*   **Multitasking:** The need to keep several instructions in the mind or quickly switch between different items.
*   **Rush:** A high pace of speech, a quick change in activities, a limited time for thinking.
*   **Distracting factors:** Extraneous sounds and movements, especially when the task requires self -control.
*   **Uncertainty:** Blurry instructions without a specific action plan.

# 3. Recommendations

## For the house

### Organization of work
*   Break homework into blocks for 15–20 minutes.
*   Between blocks is a mandatory break for movement or drink. Avoid the use of gadgets.
*   Make a visual plan of completing tasks. Mark the executed points for a sensation of control and completeness.
*   Start with the most difficult tasks while the student is full of strength.

### Support
*   Praise for the process and effort, and not just for the result: *"I see how you tried to focus"*, *"Great that followed the plan"*.
*   Consider the possibilities: productive holding of attention on a complex task is limited to 15-20 minutes.

### Communication
*   Long instructions break into short steps: *“First collect toys in the box. Ready? Now get the bed "*.

## For school

### The formulation of tasks
*   Use the most specific verbs of action: “emphasize”, “write”, “compose”.
*   Duplicate oral instructions with a brief record on the board or on the card.

### The structure of the lesson
*   Present the training material in the form of clear algorithms or circuits.
*   Visually highlight the key steps on the board or in the handout.
*   Before the control work, pronounce the action plan.

### Space and time
*   Recommended place: the first-second desk in the center, away from distracting factors (windows, doors).
*   **Individual route:** Consider the elements of an individual educational route: additional time for the performance of tests, permission to use a draft plan before writing a detailed response.

# 4. Recommended training

These exercises will help to support and develop cognitive functions in a playful way.

## For attention and self -control
*   **Correct samples:** Wip off or circle the specified letters in the text (start from 5 minutes).
*   **Board games:** “Balancing chairs”, “Jenga”, “Drum”. Develop concentration and control of impulses.
*   **Graphic dictations:** Drawing on cells under dictation.

## For consistent memory and planning
*   **The game "I put in a bag ...":** Repetition and addition of the chain of objects.
*   **Cooking by recipe:** Joint cooking with strict follow -up instructions.
*   **Pattern reproduction:** Removing and playing patterns from cubes or chips after brief viewing.

## For bodily control and rhythm
*   **Games "The Sea is worried once" or "Delay":** Braking training at the signal.
*   **Balance exercises:** Standing on one leg, walking along the line.
*   **Rhythmic exercises:** Closing or taping the specified rhythm (you can use palms or tools).
