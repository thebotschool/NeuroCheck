# Cognitive assessment report
## CPT-A / GNG-B / TCP-C (10–13 years)

---

## 1. Review of tests

*   **Sustainability of attention (CPT):** Long -term concentration is difficult. The schoolboy quickly gets tired and loses focus, especially in the presence of distracting factors.
*   **Braking (Go/No-Go):** High impulsiveness is noted. The student experiences difficulties in monitoring actions, which leads to errors due to rush.
*   **Visual working memory:** Difficulties with maintaining the sequence of actions or images. The student is inclined to the chaotic beginning of tasks without a preliminary strategy.

<!-- page-break -->

## 2. The guide for the success of the student

### Individual characteristics

*   **Pace:** The work begins quickly and impulsively, but then exhaustion and slowdown follows. Maintaining uniform rhythm is a task.
*   **Perception:** The most effectively assimilates visual, brief, step -by -step information. Long oral monologues cause cognitive overload.
*   **Focus:** Attention is unstable, easily dissipated by external (noise, movement) or internal impulses.

### Which helps

*   **Visual tips:** Check lists, timers (sand, visual), highlighting keywords.
*   **Step -by -step instructions:** Tasks divided into 2-3 simple steps.
*   **Predictability:** A clear and repeated daily routine and lesson.
*   **Pause:** The rule “stop-think-make” before action.

### What interferes

*   **Multitasking:** The simultaneous performance of several actions is difficult, causes frustration.
*   **Long instructions:** Information is lost. Only the beginning or end is remembered.
*   **Fast change of tasks:** It takes time to switch between activities.
*   **Distracting factors:** Noise, visual disorder reduce concentration.

<!-- page-break -->

## 3. Recommendations

### For the house

*   **Organization of work:**
    *   Separate tasks into short blocks (15-20 minutes). Short physical breaks are provided.
    *   Use checklists to visualize progress and control of control.
    *   Start with difficult tasks while the supply of forces is maximum.
*   **Support and encouragement:**
    *   Praise for efforts and process, and not just for the result. For example: "Great that you noticed a mistake!"
    *   Gently return to the task when distracting, avoid criticism.
*   **Wednesday:**
    *   Organize the workplace by minimizing distracting objects.

### For school

*   **The wording of tasks:**
    *   Let's briefly, clearly, one at a time. Duplicate in writing (on the board, card).
    *   Use the verbs of the action: “Emphasize”, “Circle”, “Count”.
*   **Lesson structure:**
    *   Support and write down the lesson plan. This creates predictability.
    *   Place the student in the first desks, away from distracting factors (windows, doors).
*   **Style style:**
    *   Use physical signals to attract attention to the instructions.
    *   Consider the additional support: tutor, school psychologist or neuropsychologist for individual correction of self -regulation skills.

<!-- page-break -->

## 4. Recommended training

### For attention

*   **Correct samples:** Refusing the specified characters in the text.
*   **Find the differences:** Classical game for the development of concentration.

### For memory and sequence

*   **Memory:** Search for paired images.
*   **"I am going on a hike ...":** Oral game to memorize the sequence of objects.

### For braking and bodily control

*   **The sea is worried:** A game that requires freezing in a given command on command.
*   **Edible-universal:** Training of reaction and ability to stop the action in time.
*   **Graphic dictations:** Following the instructions for performing patterns on cells, moving control.

### For rhythm

*   **Tapping rhythms:** The exact reproduction of the specified rhythms.
