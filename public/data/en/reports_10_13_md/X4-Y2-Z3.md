# Cognitive development report

This report provides a comprehensive analysis of cognitive abilities and offers practical recommendations to support the student.

---

### 1. Review of testing

*   **Test for attention and memory (CPT):** The difficulty of holding sequences, especially with external distraction, is noted. The development of strategies for memorization is required.
*   **Self-control test (Go/No-Go):** The tendency to impulsive reactions has been revealed. Difficulties with a stop of action, a predominance of speed over pondering.
*   **Regulation test (TCP):** The tendency to haste in educational tasks is confirmed. The desire to be the first to influence the accuracy of answers.

**General analysis:** Key areas for development: self -control, working memory, concentration. Natural speed and intelligence require support in the formation of internal regulation mechanisms.

### 2. Profile of a student

#### Individual characteristics

*   **Pace:** Fast, gusty, uneven. High start speed, possibly fatigue, mistakes from rush, not from ignorance.
*   **Perception:** The most effective thing is visual and step -by -step feed. Long oral instructions overload work memory. It is important to see, not only hear.
*   **Attention:** Unrestrained, sensitive to external stimuli. Productive concentration-up to 10-15 minutes, then a short break is required.

#### Effective approaches

*   **Step -by -step instructions:** Divide the tasks by 2-3 simple steps (for example: open, write down, then).
*   **Visual supports:** Use checklists, diagrams, color discharge, reminders.
*   **Timer:** Use a timer for working intervals (for example, 15 minutes work, 5 min. Rest).
*   **Clear rituals:** An understandable routine of the day and tasks reduces anxiety, promotes tuning.
*   **Pause before the answer:** The rule “count to three” before the answer helps to control the impulse.

#### Factors interfering with training

*   **Multitasking:** Let's get one instruction at a time (for example: “Take the toys”).
*   **Long oral explanations:** Only part of the phrase is remembered.
*   **Speed ​​competitions:** They provoke a rush, increase errors. Appreciate accuracy and accuracy.
*   **Noise and disorder:** Distract, reduce concentration.
*   **Fast change of tasks:** Micro-pauses are required for the transition between activities.

### 3. Application of knowledge

#### For the house

*   **Homework organization:**
    *   Divide large tasks into stages with breaks (example: 3 examples, 5 minutes rest, 3 more examples).
    *   Introduce a simple checklist: [] mathematics, [] reading, [] portfolio. The child will note the implementation himself.
    *   Workplace: clean, only necessary for the current task.
*   **Support and encouragement:**
    *   Praise the process (“How attentive you are!”), Not only the result.
    *   Work on errors: Analyze calmly (“Hurry, missed a sign. Next time we will check”).
*   **Attention:** On an uninteresting task, it is depleted in 10-15 minutes. This is the norm, not a lack of desire.

#### For school

*   **The wording of tasks:**
    *   Instructions: briefly, clearly, one at a time. Contact by name, use eye contact.
    *   Duplicate oral tasks with a recording on a board or card.
*   **Lesson structuring:**
    *   Place: the first desk in the center, to minimize abstracts.
    *   Warn the change of activity in advance (for example: “After 2 minutes we move to the textbook”).
    *   Apply simple solution algorithms: 1. Read. 2. Emphasize. 3. Resolve. 4. Check.
*   **Style style:**
    *   Question style: Avoid rhetorical. Question, pause, then select the responsible.
    *   IOOM (individual educational route): Consider the additional time on control, help card with algorithms.

### 4. Developing games and exercises

Simple games and exercises integrated into everyday life for the development of key functions.

*   **Attention and working memory:**
    *   “Find a pair” (Memory): trains memory, concentration.
    *   “What has changed?”: Remember 5-7 items, then find a change.
    *   "Corrective test": delete the letter "A" on the page in 5 minutes.

*   **Self -control and reduction of impulsiveness:**
    *   “The sea is worried once”, “Delay!”: They develop brake function, a quick reaction.
    *   “Edible-universal”: trains a quick assessment and decision-making.
    *   Rhythm is tapped: repetition of rhythm for adults with gradual complication.

*   **Body control and coordination:**
    *   Graphic dictations: coordination of hearing and motor skills.
    *   Games with the ball: throws with the name of words on the topic require the concentration of movement and thinking.
    *   Balance exercises: walking along the line, stand on one leg.
