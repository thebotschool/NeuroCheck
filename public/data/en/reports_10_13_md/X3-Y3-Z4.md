# Cognitive development report

### Age: 10-13 years

This report is your personal guide for the disclosure of the student’s potential. Our recommendations were created to support effective and comfortable education both at home and at school.

---

### 1. Comprehensive assessment

*   **Attention and self -control:** Difficulties with maintaining attention, reaction management and operational switching between tasks are observed. Manifestations of distraction or impulsiveness are possible.
*   **Inter -home interaction:** Difficulties are noted in synchronous coordination of movements between the left and right hand. This indicates the need for the development of connections between the hemispheres of the brain, which is important for writing and performing complex actions.
*   **Visual working memory:** Effective memorization of visual images and facts. The sequence of elements or events can be a growth zone.

### 2. The guide for the success of your student

#### Features of perception

*   **Pace:** A leisurely, thoughtful pace of work is required. Haster often leads to errors. Thoughtful is the basis of success.
*   **Format:** Priority for the visual and step -by -step presentation of information. Schemes, plans, algorithms help assimilation. Long oral instructions are less effective.
*   **Focus:** Attention is unstable, easily exhausted. Short concentration intervals require regular pauses. Multivature causes stress and reduces productivity.

#### Support strategies

*   **Visual supports:** Use lessons, checklists, algorithms in the form of cards.
    *   Example: `1. Прочитай. 2. Выдели главное. 3. Запиши решение.`
*   **Step -by -step instructions:** Miss tasks into 2-3 simple steps.
    *   Example: `Открой тетрадь, запиши число, приступай к упражнению.`
*   **Rhythm and predictability:** A clear daily routine and predictable structure of the lesson (greeting, checking, new topic, fixing, result) create stability and reduce fatigue.
*   **Short working intervals:** Apply the method `Помодоро`: 15 minutes of work, 3-5 minutes of active break.
*   **Tactile stimulation:** Tactile items (anti-stress cords), fingers or short physical activity contribute to the restart of attention.

#### Factors that reduce efficiency

*   **Multitasking:** Simultaneous performance of several tasks (`слушай, смотри, пиши`) causes cognitive overload.
*   **Fast change of tasks:** The lack of time to complete the previous process disorients and prevents inclusion in new activity.
*   **Noise and distracting factors:** External irritants (noise, location at the window/door, active neighbors) significantly reduce productivity.
*   **Abstract instructions:** Long, abstract oral instructions without visual support make it difficult to understand and memorize.
*   **Time pressure:** Phrases creating a rush (`быстрее`, `осталась минута`), cause anxiety and block thinking.

### 3. Recommendations

#### Home environment

*   **Homework organization:**
    *   Make a simple plan for 2-3 points.
        *   Example: `1. Русский. 2. Перерыв. 3. Математика.`
    *   Use the timer: 15 minutes of work, 5 minutes of outdoor activities (jumping, water, look out the window).
    *   Focus on one task. Remove from the table all unnecessary items.
*   **Support and encouragement:**
    *   Encourage the process and effort, not only the result.
        *   Example: `Я вижу твою сосредоточенность`, `Ты сам нашёл ошибку`.
    *   Help structure, avoid doing work for it.
        *   Instead of `реши задачу` Offer `найди главные цифры`.
*   **Attention resource:** Productive concentration on a difficult task-10-15 minutes. After this, a short break is required to restore efficiency.

#### School environment

*   **The wording of tasks:**
    *   The instructions should be short, clumsy, consistent. Duplicate them in writing (board, card).
    *   Use direct verbs: `Подчеркни`, `Обведи`, `Сосчитай`.
*   **Lesson structure and material presentation:**
    *   At the beginning of the lesson, voice and write down the plan.
    *   Use visual supports: schemes, intelligence cards, highlighting keywords with color.
    *   Place the child in the first or second desk in the center, minimizing distracting factors.
*   **Individual educational route (IOM):**
    *   Discuss with the school psychologist and teacher adaptation: reducing the amount of written work, additional time to control.
    *   Allow the use of a line for reading or clean sheet for focusing on part of the page.

### 4. Recommended training

The following exercises develop targeted cognitive functions. Daily game practice is recommended for 10-15 minutes.

*   **Development of attention and self -control:**
    *   **Game `Find 10 differences`:** He trains concentration and visual attention.
    *   **Correct samples:** In the text, delete certain letters (for example, `А`) or circle others (`О`).
    *   **Game `Yes, and there is no talk`:** Develops control of impulsive answers.
*   **Development of coordination and interhemispheric interaction:**
    *   **Drawing with two hands:** On the sheet, simultaneously draw symmetrical figures (circles, squares).
    *   **Cross -steps:** Standing, slowly touch the right elbow of the left knee, then vice versa. 1-2 minutes.
    *   **Exercise `Kulak-Robro-Ladon`:** Change the position of the brush on the table sequentially. One, then with two hands at the same time.
*   **Development of consistent memory:**
    *   **Game `chain of words`:** Each player repeats the previous words and adds a new one (`дом` → `дом, дерево` etc.).
    *   **Drawing up instructions:** Ask the child to step by step to explain simple actions (brew tea, remove toys).
    *   **Rhythmic drawings:** Reproduce a simple rhythm (claps, stomping) and ask to repeat. Gradually complicate.
