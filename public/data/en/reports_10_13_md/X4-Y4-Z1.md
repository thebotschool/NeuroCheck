### **2. The guide for the success of your student**

#### **Peculiarities**

*   **Pace:** Uneven. The initial impulse is replaced by a decrease in concentration and deceleration. Support for maintaining stable activity is required.
*   **Perception:** Optimally perceives information **Visually** (schemes, illustrations) and in **step -by -step format**. Long oral instructions are less effective.
*   **Attention:** Dynamically. External factors or internal thoughts can easily distract. Maximum concentration on one task: 10-15 minutes. Deep immersion is possible with great interest and a calm environment.

#### **Support**

*   **Visual supports:** Cards, schemes, color selection.
*   **Step -by -step instructions:** Missing the task into micro-shags. Example: "1. Read. 2. Emphasize. 3. Write down."
*   **Timing:** Visual timers (sand clocks) for short intervals help focusing.
*   **Intervals:** The system "15 minutes of work, 5 minutes of break" (movement, water) is more productive.
*   **Predictability:** A clear daily routine and classes creates comfort and stability.

#### **Obstacles**

*   **Multitasking:** Simultaneous listening, recording and observation is ineffective. One task at a time.
*   **Long instructions:** More than two successive commands exceed the volume of working memory.
*   **Noise and vanity:** Distracting factors (conversations, movements, background noise) reduce attention.
*   **Pressure:** A rush or comparison ("faster", "everyone has already done") is alarm and worsen the result.

### **3. Recommendations**

#### **At home**

1.  **Organization of work:**
    *   Savior tasks into parts. For example: "Only the first two examples."
    *   Use a tiemer for 15/5 intervals.
    *   Start with the most difficult tasks while there is enough energy.
    *   Provide a permanent, quiet, clean workplace without unnecessary items.

2.  **Motivation and support:**
    *   Mark **efforts and process**, not just the result. "I see how you tried!" More important than just "excellent."
    *   Avoid criticism for slowness or inattention. Ask: "What prevented? How can you try differently?"
    *   Create success situations through interesting and completed tasks.

#### **At school**

1.  **Educational process:**
    *   **Location:** The first-second desk, in the center, far from distracting factors (windows, doors).
    *   **Tasks:** Formulate clearly, briefly, step by step. Always duplicate the oral instruction visually (board, card).
    *   **Lesson structure:** Sound the lesson plan: "Today we will repeat, then dictation, at the end of the game."

2.  **Individual approach:**
    *   **Additional supports:** Allow the use of a line for reading or algorithm card for tasks.
    *   **Checking understanding:** After the instructions, quietly ask: "What needs to be done now?"
    *   **Time:** If possible, provide a little more time for control and independent work.

### **4. Practical exercises**

These simple exercises, performed regularly in a playful way, contribute to the development of the necessary functions.

*   **For attention:**
    *   **Graphic dictations:** Develop auditory attention and spatial thinking.
    *   **"Find the differences", labyrinths:** They train the concentration and stability of visual attention.
    *   **Correct samples:** For example, deleting a certain letter in the text.

*   **For memory and sequence:**
    *   **"Memory":** Develops visual working memory.
    *   **"I put in a bag ...":** Playing the development of auditory memory and sequence.
    *   **Stories from the pictures:** He teaches to build logical and temporary ties.

*   **For bodily control and coordination (self -regulation):**
    *   **Games with the ball:** Bowing, throwing in rhythm.
    *   **"FROME-OUTOMI":** Teaches a quick reaction and body control.
    *   **RIGHTS RIGHTS:** Repetition of rhythmic patterns (pops, tapping). Develops a sense of rhythm and sequence.
