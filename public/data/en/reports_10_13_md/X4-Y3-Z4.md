## Cognitive profile report: CPT + Go/No-Go + Visual Working Memory (10-13 years)

### **1.**

*   **Sustainability of attention (CPT):** Difficulties with prolonged attention. There is rapid fatigue and distraction, especially with monotonous tasks.
*   **Self-control (Go/No-Go):** Some asymmetry of interhemispheric interaction has been identified. Difficulties with coordination and tasks requiring synchronous work of motor skills and thinking are possible (for example, a letter).
*   **Visual working memory:** Difficulties with operational processing and memorization of information. Effective strategies for streamlining data in memory are not used.

<!-- page-break -->

### **2. The profile of the student**

#### ### Features of perception

*   **Pace:** Uneven. Own rhythm is required. Hashing causes exhaustion. The calm start of work is important.
*   **Perception format:** Visual. It better assimilates information through the schemes, lists, step -by -step instructions. Oral explanations without clarity are less effective.
*   **Focus of attention:** Short, unstable. We distract with external stimuli. It is difficult to take into account several conditions of the task.

#### ### Supporting Environment

*   Step-by-step instructions and checklists.
*   Visual tips: schemes, timers, color selection, memos.
*   Rhythmic, predictable environment. The alternation of work (15–20 min) and recreation (3-5 min).
*   Focus on one information channel. Lack of distracting factors during work.
*   Preliminary familiarization with the plan of action. Reduces anxiety, increases concentration.

#### ### Distracting factors

*   Multitasking.
*   A quick change in tasks without a pause for switching (required 1-2 minutes).
*   Noise and visual disorder.
*   Multi -step oral instructions without visual support.

<!-- page-break -->

### **3. Recommendations**

#### ### for home

*   **Homework organization:**
    *   Separation of tasks into blocks (15–20 minutes). The optimal concentration time.
    *   The use of a timer for structuring work and rest (for example, 15 minutes of work, 5 minutes break).
    *   Start work with the most complex objects.
    *   Drawing up a simple written plan for the evening.
*   **Support and encouragement:**
    *   Promotion for the process and zeal, and not just for the result.
    *   Organization of a quiet workplace without visual abstracts.

#### ### for the school

*   **The wording and structure of tasks:**
    *   Simple and step -by -step formulation of tasks.
    *   Visual schedule on the board with marks of progress.
    *   The use of supporting circuits, intelligence cards, brief abstracts for complex topics.
    *   Checking the understanding of the assignment before starting work.
*   **Wednesday and presentation style:**
    *   The optimal location: the first-second desk in the center, away from distracting factors (window, door).
    *   Calm, friendly tone without pressure.
    *   Consideration of the elements of an individual educational route (IOOM): visual timer, additional time for control, subscription cards.

<!-- page-break -->

### **4. Recommended training**

Short game training (10-15 minutes per day) for the development of the necessary functions.

#### ### for attention

*   Corrective tests: deleting a given letter in the text.
*   Board games: “Volnet”, “Drum”, “High -speed caps”.

#### ### for coordination

*   Neurohymnastics: cross movements (for example, the right hand to the left knee).
*   Drawing with two hands: simultaneous creation of symmetrical figures.

#### ### for memory

*   The game “What has changed?”: Memorizing the location of objects with the subsequent identification of changes.
*   The game "Snow Com": sequential adding elements to the phrase or list.

#### ### for rhythm

*   The game "Rhythmic Echo": the exact repetition of a given rhythm.
