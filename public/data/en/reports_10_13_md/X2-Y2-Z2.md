## Cognitive profile. Age 10-13 years

This report was created for a better understanding of the strengths of the student and areas that require support. The recommendations presented are designed to promote the creation of an optimal environment for teaching and developing both at home and at school.

---

### 1. Test results

*   **Attention (TCP):** Difficulties with long -term focus holding on the same tasks. Distraction in case of loss of interest.
*   **Self-control (Go/No-Go):** Teenage to impulsive actions. It takes time to think before the reaction.
*   **Visual memory (CPT):** Remoning is effective, but sensitive to external distraction. Distortion or adding parts are possible.

### 2. The guide for the success of your student

This leadership covers the individual characteristics of the student to create an effective and comfortable learning process.

#### Individual profile

*   **The pace of work:** The tendency to a quick, impulsive pace. The most productive in measured rhythm with short pauses. Haster leads to errors.
*   **Information perception:** It is best absorbed by visual, structured materials: diagrams, pictures, short videos. Long monotonous text or oral instructions are less effective.
*   **Concentration:** Sustainable on short, interesting tasks (up to 10-15 minutes). It is quickly exhausted from monotony, external support (for example, an adult question) is required to return to the task.

#### Support: What is effective

*   **Step -by -step instructions:** One task is one clear step.
*   **Visual supports:** Tiemers, checklists, highlighting keywords, short memory schemes.
*   **Rhythm:** Speaking the rules out loud, taping the rhythm to keep the pace, the use of counters.
*   **Short tasks:** Tasks performed in 10-15 minutes, with an immediate visible result.
*   **Stop rule:** Learning a pause before the answer or action (short inhale-output, account up to three).

#### Obstacles: what to avoid

*   **Multitasking:** Simultaneous implementation of several cases or long, multi -component instructions.
*   **Monotone:** Long -term monotonous work without breaks and changing activities.
*   **Lack of structure:** An unclean plan of action or blurry criteria for implementation.
*   **Sensory overload:** Noise, disorder on the table, an excess of visual stimuli.
*   **Pressure and rush:** Teams like “faster”, “Come on more” enhance impulsiveness.

### 3. Recommendations

#### Home environment

*   **Homework:**
    *   Use the timer: 15 minutes of work, 5 minutes of active break.
    *   Start with the most complex or unloved objects while the energy level is high.
    *   Savior big tasks into small steps. Write them down on the sticker: “1. Solve 3 examples. 2. Check. 3. Emphasize the answers. "
*   **Motivation and support:**
    *   Instead of "Did you do your homework?" Ask: “What is your step now? What needs to be done next? ” This directs.
    *   Praise not only for the result, but also for the process: “It's great that you noticed a mistake and corrected it!”, “I see how you tried not to be distracted.”
*   **Working space:**
    *   Organize the table so that it is only necessary for the current task. Take out extra objects.

#### At school

*   **Tasks and instructions:**
    *   The instructions should be clumsy, short, step -by -step. At the beginning of the lesson, say a plan.
    *   Duplicate oral instructions visually (recording on the board).
*   **Lesson organization:**
    *   Alternate activities every 15–20 minutes (letter, oral answer, work with cards, physical education).
    *   The optimal place is the first-second desk that minimizes distracting factors and provides direct contact with the teacher.
*   **Individual approach:**
    *   For testing, offer an algorithm memo: “1. Read the task. 2. Emphasize the main thing. 3. Complete. 4. Check yourself. "
    *   Allow short “motor” breaks (to flood a pencil, take a test) for “rebooting” attention.
    *   Give small instructions that require concentration and responsibility.

### 4. Recommended training

These exercises will help to develop key skills in a fascinating form.

*   **Attention:**
    *   **"Corrective test":** In the text (newspaper, magazine) to disrupt all letters “a” or circle “o”. Start with 2-3 minutes.
    *   **"Find 10 differences":** Classic pictures for training concentration.
*   **Memory and strategy:**
    *   **"Memory":** Board game for searching for paired cards.
    *   **"Photographer":** Lay out 5-7 items. Give 30 seconds to "photograph" with the eyes. Cover and ask to name from memory.
*   **Self -control and motility:**
    *   **"Delay!":** Dance to the music. Stop in a pose when you turn off music.
    *   **“No, don't say”:** Answer questions, avoiding prohibited words. Develops speech control.
*   **Rhythm and coordination:**
    *   **"Rhythmic echo":** The adult claps the rhythm, the child exactly repeats.
    *   **Games with the ball:** Beat off the ball from the floor or walls in a given rhythm, maintaining the pace.
