# School of cognitive development of a student

This report provides a comprehensive view of the cognitive characteristics of your child. It contains an analysis of the strengths, development zones and practical recommendations for effective education at school and at home.

---

## 1. Review of tests

*   **Conservative memory test (CPT):** Excellent memorization of information. Support for logical and temporary sequences is required.
*   **Self-control and braking test (Go/No-Go):** Good control of impulses. The development of coordination of both hands is observed, which indicates the formation of interhemispheric connections.
*   **Test for stability of attention (TCP):** Outstanding result. A high, stable concentration on tasks is a key strong side for training.

## 2. Profile of learning

### Individual characteristics

*   **Pace:** Thoughtful, changing. Quick and accurate with concentration tasks. It can slow down if necessary to build a sequence or perform complex written work.
*   **Perception:** Optimally assimilates visual and structural information. Schemes, tables, step -by -step lists, mental cards - effective tools. Oral instructions without a visual support can be less effective.
*   **Attention:** High concentration is a strong side. It is capable of deeply immersed in tasks if he understands their structure and order of execution.

### Success factors

*   **Step -by -step instructions:** Tasks divided into 2-3 clear steps.
*   **Visual support:** Algorithms, schemes, timline.
*   **Predictable rhythm:** The clear structure of lessons and homework reduces the cognitive load.
*   **Preparation time:** A short period is needed to comprehend the task and planning of actions.

### Difficulties in training

*   **Multicomponent oral instructions:** They can overload the memory to the sequence.
*   **Quick change of types of tasks:** Requires interhemispheric coordination, which is in the process of formation.
*   **Work on speed (written tasks):** Time pressure can cause errors associated with motor awkwardness.
*   **Multitasking:** Simultaneous listening and abstracting of complex material requires significant efforts.

## 3. Recommendations

### At home

*   **Organization of homework:**
    *   Create a joint checklist of tasks for the evening. The mark of executed points contributes to the feeling of control and completeness.
    *   Start with the most difficult or written tasks while the level of energy and concentration is optimal.
    *   Miss large tasks into 20–25-minute intervals with short (3-5 minutes) breaks for physical activity.
*   **Development support:**
    *   Help build sequences. Instead of a general question about the day, specify the events in order: "What happened in the first lesson? What after him? What is interesting before lunch?"
    *   Plan everyday business (going to the store, cleaning) using lists or schemes.
    *   Mark and encourage not only the correct answers, but also efforts in the concentration and completion of complex tasks.
*   **Duration of concentration:** On an interesting and understandable task - up to 30-40 minutes. If it is necessary to constant switching or memorizing order, attention can be exhausted faster.

### At school

*   **The wording of tasks:**
    *   Use numbered or marked lists for instructions.
    *   Duplicate oral instructions on the board or cards.
    *   Instead of the general “Retell Text”, offer a supporting plan: “1. The main character? 2. What happened at the beginning? 3. How did it end?”.
*   **Structuring the material:**
    *   Provide supporting schemes, algorithms and templates for standard tasks (for example, solving equations, morphological analysis).
    *   Visually highlight the key steps or stages of new material (color, underline).
*   **Place in the class:** A desk with a minimum number of distracting factors is recommended to maximize the strongest side - concentration.
*   **Individual support:**
    *   The provision of additional time for control and independent work, especially written.
    *   Permission to use a draft or personal plan-scheme when writing presentations and works.

## 4. Recommended training

The proposed exercises and games help strengthen the neural connections responsible for coordination and consistent processing of information.

### Development of attention and consistent memory

*   **The game "I put in a bag ...":** He trains the auditory consistent memory, developing the ability to repeat and add elements in a row.
*   **Table strategies:** Games requiring the planning of moves and memorizing the order of action.
*   **Pictures Stories:** Creating a story based on 4-5 plot images located in the correct sequence.

### Development of coordination and interhemispheric ties

*   **Drawing with two hands:** Simultaneous drawing of symmetrical figures (circles, squares) on a sheet of paper.
*   **Exercise "Fist-Robric Ladon":** A sequential change of three positions of the hand on the table, first with one hand, then with the other, then two at the same time.
*   **Juggling:** Start with two light objects. Effectively develops visual-motor coordination and interhemispheric relations.
*   **Dancing or aerobics:** Activities requiring a repetition of a sequence of movements behind the instructor.
