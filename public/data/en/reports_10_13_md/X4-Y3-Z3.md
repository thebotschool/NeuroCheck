# Cognitive profile: Your child

This report is a guide for understanding the unique cognitive features of your student. He offers the way to disclose potential through targeted support for the house and in the educational environment.

---

# 1. Review: Cognitive functions

### Attention and working memory

Difficulties were noted with the simultaneous retention of several facts or consistent steps. There is a tendency to forget the initial actions and the transition to chaotic manifestations.

### Self -control

The test revealed the ongoing development of interhemispheric coordination. This can affect the accuracy of movements, including when writing, and the consistent performance of tasks.

### Impulsiveness

A tendency to quick reactions was discovered ahead of complete instructions. The development of internal control and self -regulation skills is a priority.

# 2. The guide for the success of your student

### Individual characteristics

*   **Pace:** It is characterized by a quick start, but variable stability. There may be sharp switching, requiring a return to the task.
*   **Perception:** Optimally when submitting information through one sensory channel. Visual and concise data are preferred.
*   **Focus of attention:** Dynamic. The concentration on the task is effective up to 5-7 minutes, after which a short break or a change in activity is recommended.

### Supports for development

*   **Visual:** Short lists (up to 3 points), diagrams, color selection of keywords.
*   **Steps:** Instructions broken into one action. *Example: “Open the notebook. Take the pen. Write down the number "*.
*   **Rhythm:** Predictable daily routine, voiceening of plans, the use of a timer for periods of activity and recreation.
*   **Activity:** Brief physical breaks to or during complex tasks.

### Factors that make it difficult to concentrate

*   **Multitasking:** Simultaneous requests reduce execution efficiency.
*   **Long instructions:** Hard to keep all the information. Brief formulations are preferred.
*   **Competitions:** The emphasis on speed can increase the impulsiveness and number of errors.
*   **Noise and chaos:** Distracting factors, such as loud sound or mess, negatively affect attention.

# 3. Way to growth: Practical recommendations

### Home environment

*   **Organization of work:**
    *   **Sessions:** 10-15 minutes of classes, then 5 minutes of active break.
    *   **Timer:** Visual timer (sand clock, smartphone) helps to track time.
    *   **Order:** On the desktop, only the necessary objects for the current task.
*   **Support and encouragement:**
    *   **Praise:** Mark efforts and process, and not just the result. *Example: "I did a wonderful job!"*.
    *   **Instructions:** Break big tasks into small steps. *Example: “Let's start with mathematics. Read the first task "*.
*   **Communication:**
    *   **Contact:** Install visual and physical contact. The instructions should be brief and clear.
    *   **Understanding:** Ask the child to repeat the task in your own words.

### School environment

*   **The wording of tasks:**
    *   **Tasks:** Divide into 2-3 clear, consistent steps.
    *   **Instructions:** Start with the verbs of action. *Example: “Emphasize. Count. Write down "*.
*   **Structure and organization:**
    *   **Place:** The location of the first or second desks, far from distracting factors (window, door).
    *   **Plan:** A short plan of the lesson on the board (1. Dictation. 2. Task. 3. Game).
    *   **Signals:** Clear signals of the beginning and end of the tasks (for example, a call).
*   **Style style and IOOM:**
    *   **Time:** Provide extra time for writing.
    *   **Tools:** Allow the use of auxiliary products (a reading line, an examples card).
    *   **Grade:** Priority of accuracy, not speed. Encourage attentiveness.

# 4. Training: game development

The proposed exercises contribute to the development of key skills. It is recommended to engage in 10-15 minutes daily using the game format.

### For attention

*   **Correct test:** Refusing certain letters in the text.
*   **Find the differences:** Search for differences on paired images.
*   **Board games:** UNO, Vybl, Memo - develop concentration and following the rules.

### For coordination and interaction

*   **Drawing with two hands:** Simultaneous creation of symmetrical figures (circles, butterflies).
*   **Kulak-Robe Ladon:** A consistent change in the position of the hand on the surface, first one, then both.
*   **Crossroads:** Touching the left knee with the right hand, with the left hand of the right.

### For memory and self -control

*   **I put in a bag ...:** Alternate repetition and adding objects to the chain.
*   **Forbidden movement:** Repetition of all movements except one specified.
*   **Cooling rhythm:** The exact reproduction of a given rhythm with gradual complication.
