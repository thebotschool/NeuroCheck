# Cognitive assessment
## Test results: CPT, Go/No-Go, TCP (10-13 years)

This report reveals the unique features of your child. He distinguishes strengths and areas for development, offering practical steps to comfortable and effective training.

---

## 1. Key indicators

*   **Working memory (CPT):** Effective memorization of information. The development of the skills of the sequence of actions and events is required.
*   **Braking (Go/No-Go):** The difference is noted in performing the tasks of the leading and unknown hand. Intergribute coordination is a area for development that affects the letter and reaction rate.
*   **Sustainability of attention (TCP):** There is a tendency to distract, especially in monotonous tasks. The concentration improves in the presence of external support and a clear structure.

## 2. The guide for the success of your student

### Features of perception

*   **Pace:** Uneven. The child quickly grasps the essence, but can slow down when performing sequential steps.
*   **Format:** Better perceive structured and visual information. Long oral explanations can be less effective.
*   **Focus:** Unstable, quickly exhausted from monotonous work. Attention is activated on short, dynamic tasks.

### Which contributes

*   **Visual supports:** Schemes, checklists, color coding, intelligence codes help to structure information.
*   **Step -by -step instructions:** Clear algorithms of actions simplify the performance of tasks.
*   **Short cycles:** Work with breaks (for example, 15 minutes of work, 5 minutes of rest) is more productive.
*   **Rhythm and movement:** The inclusion of rhythmic exercises and tasks for both hands helps synchronize the brain and improve concentration.

### What makes it difficult

*   **Multitasking:** The simultaneous implementation of several tasks (listen, record, remember) leads to overload.
*   **Distracting factors:** An extraneous noise, a mess at the workplace, background television reduce productivity.
*   **Long monotonous tasks:** Long classes of the same type quickly cause fatigue and loss of motivation.
*   **Sharp transitions:** A pause is needed to switch between tasks. A quick change in activity can disorient.

## 3. Practical recommendations

### Home environment

*   **Organization of work:**
    *   Miss large tasks into 2-3 small parts. Short breaks (2-3 minutes) are provided.
    *   Use a timer to visualize work segments.
    *   Create a simple plan before starting: 1. Mathematics. 2. Reading. 3. Drawing. Cross out the executed points.
*   **Support:**
    *   Praise for the process and effort, and not just for the result.
    *   Focused attention on a complex task is 10-15 minutes. Do not demand more.
*   **Workplace:**
    *   Maintain cleanliness and order, minimize distracting objects.
    *   Turn off the background noise during class.

### School environment

*   **The wording of tasks:**
    *   The instructions should be short, even and step -by -step. Ideally - in the form of a numbered list.
    *   Example: "1. Open p. 25. 2. Find exercise 5. 3. Read the task. 4. Follow it in a notebook."
*   **Lesson structure:**
    *   Place the student in such a way as to minimize distracting factors (for example, closer to the teacher).
    *   Actively use visual materials: schemes, tables, presentations.
    *   Alternate work formats: letter (5-7 minutes), oral answer, work with cards.
*   **Adaptation:**
    *   Adaptation of the volume of written tasks is recommended.
    *   Providing additional time for testing.
    *   Permission to use visual algorithm templates to solve problems.

## 4. Recommended training

Practical classes: 5-10 minutes daily. Game format.

*   **Attention and self -control:**
    *   **"Find 10 differences":** Develops concentration and observation.
    *   **"Corrective tests":** Crossing certain letters in the text. Start from 1-2 minutes.
    *   **The game "Yes and there is no talk":** He trains the braking of impulsive answers.

*   **Memory and sequence:**
    *   **"Snowball":** The consistent repetition and adding of elements to the chain ("I go on a trip and take ...").
    *   **"Repeat the pattern":** Reproduction of a sequence of objects (pencils, cubes). Gradually increase the length.
    *   **Retelling of the day:** Evening presentation of the events of the day in order. Help with leading questions.

*   **Coordination and interhemispheric interaction:**
    *   **"Drawing with two hands":** Simultaneous symmetrical drawing with both hands.
    *   **"Crossroads":** Alternated touches of highly knees with hands.
    *   **"Fist-Robric Ladon":** The sequential execution of movements with each hand, then both.
