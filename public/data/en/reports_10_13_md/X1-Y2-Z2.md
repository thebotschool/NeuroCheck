# Cognitive profile: report

This report is designed to reveal the unique cognitive profile of your student. It contains key test results and practical recommendations aimed at maximum disclosure of potential in study and everyday life.

## 1. Summary of tests

*   **Visual working memory:** Excellently developed. The ability to effectively memorize, hold and operate complex visual information. High skills for building logical connections.
*   **Brake control (Go/No-Go):** Requires development. There is a tendency to impulsive reactions. Making decisions often occurs without a sufficient pause for thinking.
*   **Sustainability of attention (CPT):** Reduced. Attention is unstable, especially when performing the same type or long -term tasks. There is a slight distractiveness and rapid fatigue from monotonous work.

## 2. The guide for the success of your student

Understanding individual characteristics helps to create an optimal environment for training and development.

### Key features

*   **Pace:** Ragged. It is characterized by a rapid start and high energy at the beginning of the task, but by a rapid decrease in motivation when monotony occurs. The impulsive approach prevails.
*   **Perception format:** Expressed visual. The most effectively assimilates and remembers information presented clearly: schemes, images, infographics, video, structured lists.
*   **Focus of attention:** Selective. It is easily concentrated on tasks that seem interesting, bright or dynamic. It is quickly distracted by routine or too long tasks. High memory efficiency directly depends on the level of attention involvement.

### Support and optimization

*   **Visual supports:** Check lists, Mayland cards, visual schemes, highlighting keywords with a marker. Everything that can be seen helps to hold the focus.
*   **Step -by -step instructions:** Decomposition of volumetric tasks for a series of small, specific steps. For example: "1. Think about three ideas. 2. Choose the best. 3. Write a plan of four points."
*   **Clear signals "Start/Stop":** Using a timer for structuring working intervals. Verbal teams (“Started!”, “Time has come out, pause!”) Help training the skill of self -control.
*   **Change of activity:** Short, but intensive periods of work (15–20 minutes) alternate with small physical breaks (warm -up, water balance).

### Factors that reduce efficiency

*   **Multitasking:** The simultaneous performance of several actions (listen, write down, think) leads to a rapid depletion of attention resources and a decrease in productivity.
*   **Volumetric oral instructions:** Long speech instructions are perceived fragmented. The beginning and end of the information can be learned, but the middle is often lost.
*   **Monotony and routine:** Long -term lectures, the same type of exercise or the need to maintain immobility reduce involvement.
*   **Lack of a clear structure:** Blurred or abstract tasks ("think over the project") and the chaotic organization of the workspace negatively affect concentration.

## 3. Recommendations

### Home environment

*   **Organization of classes:**
    *   **Method "Prydor":** Use intervals 20 minutes of work / 5 minutes break. Apply a timer.
    *   **Prioritization:** Start homework with the most complex items while the energy level is optimal.
    *   **Visual plan:** Together, create a visual plan of evening affairs. Mark the completed points to create a sense of completeness.
*   **Support and encouragement:**
    *   **Focus on efforts:** Celebrate efforts and process, and not just the result. Example: "I appreciate your efforts in the concentration!"
    *   **Pause before the answer:** Accustom to think about it. Before the answer to the question, recommend making a short pause.
*   **Limited concentration:** The maximum period of continuous concentration on an uninteresting task is 15–20 minutes. Plan regular breaks.

### School environment

*   **The wording of tasks:**
    *   **Clear instructions:** Use short, specific instructions containing the verbs of action (for example: “Write out three terms”, “Compare two pictures”, “Solve examples 1, 2, 5”).
    *   **Visual support:** Duplicate oral tasks written on a board or cards.
*   **Lesson structure:**
    *   **Lesson plan:** At the beginning of the lesson, briefly voiced its structure. Example: "Today we will do three things: 1. We will repeat ... 2. We will study ... 3. We will fix it ..."
    *   **Optimal place:** Put the child closer to the board and the teacher, away from distracting factors (window, door).
*   **Material feed style:**
    *   **Interactivity:** Apply questions to the class, work in pairs, short discussions.
    *   **Visualization:** Use presentations with a minimum amount of text and maximum visibility.
*   **Elements of an individual educational route (IOM):**
    *   **Individual tools:** Allow the use of a timer or card with a plan of assignment on a desk.
    *   **Adjustable breaks:** Give the possibility of short "legal" breaks (for example, sharpen a pencil) if there is a decrease in attention.

## 4. Recommended training

Develop key skills through game forms and exercises.

### Attention and control of impulses

*   **The game "Frozhi-Otomory":** Develops a rapid reaction to the STOP signal and trains bodily control.
*   **Correct samples:** Search and crossing out the specified characters in the text (for example, all the letters "O" on the newspaper page), with a gradual complication (for example, delete "O", circle "K").
*   **Board games:** "Dobbl", "high -speed caps", "drum" develop the speed of reaction, selective attention and brake control.

### Rhythm and coordination

*   **Cooling rhythms:** Repetition of simple rhythmic patterns with gradual complication.
*   **Games with the ball:** Catching the ball by signal (for example, after cotton) to train the reaction and self -control.

### Development of memory

*   **The game "Memory" ("Find a couple"):** Effectively involves the strong visual memory of the child.
*   **The game "What has changed?":** Removing a group of objects with the subsequent definition of changes (removal, movement, addition).
