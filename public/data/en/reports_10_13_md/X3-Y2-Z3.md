# Cognitive assessment: report

This report provides an in -depth look at the cognitive features of your student. It contains test results and practical recommendations for creating an optimal development environment.

# Analysis of the results

There are key conclusions for each test.

### Memory (CPT)
Information is absorbed effectively. However, consistent reproduction causes difficulties. The schoolboy takes the initiative in the search for his own strategies.

### Self-control (Go/No-Go)
The difficulty in suppressing impulsive reactions is noted, especially with quick decision -making or the need to filter incentives.

### Attention (TCP)
There is a tendency to haste. The schoolboy can answer or complete the tasks without waiting for the full instruction, which leads to errors due to inattention.

# Success Guide

Understanding individual characteristics helps to reveal the potential of the student.

### Key features
*   **Pace:** Fast, impulsive. The tendency to speed to the detriment of accuracy. Focus on immediate execution.
*   **Perception:** The best assimilation of information through clear, step -by -step instructions. It needs external supports: diagrams, algorithms, checklists.
*   **Focus:** Easily holds attention to interesting. The routine and the need for self -control cause rapid fatigue. The impulsiveness prevents the filtering of distraction.

### What supports
*   Visual supports: action schemes, checklists, timline.
*   Step -by -step instructions: Tasks where each stage is revealed sequentially.
*   A pause before action: the “stop-thought” rule, a conscious delay before the answer.
*   Rhythm and predictability: a clear daily routine, rhythmic exercises for concentration.
*   Praise for the process: assessment of thoughtfulness and accuracy, and not just speeds.

### What makes it difficult
*   Multivativity: several instructions simultaneously lead to omissions.
*   Competitive format: Tasks "for speed" cause a rush and errors.
*   Abstract instructions: General formulations without a clear plan of action.
*   Distracting factors: noise or visual overload complicate the concentration.

# Practical recommendations

Optimization of the environment for effective training and development.

### Home environment
#### Organization
*   Break the tasks into blocks for 15–20 minutes. Provide short active breaks.
*   Use checklist checks to track executed subtasks. Visual output motivates.
*   Provide a minimalist workspace. Remove all distracting objects.

#### Support
*   Mark follow the plan and shown thoughtfulness: "Excellent, you did everything according to the list!"
*   Instead of assessments like "Think better!" Ask leading questions: "What is our first step? And the next one?"

#### Focus of attention
*   Realistic concentration time on one task: 15–20 minutes. After that, a break is needed.

### School environment
#### The formulation of tasks
*   Let's get one instruction at a time. Divide complex indications of simple steps.
*   Use the verbs of the action: “Emphasize”, “Cover”, “Mercise”.

#### The structure of the lesson
*   Recommended place: the first desk, the center of the class, away from distracting factors (windows, doors).
*   The presence of a visual lesson plan/assignment on a board or desk.
*   Before starting the task: pause and visual contact to confirm understanding.

#### Individual adaptations
*   Provide tasks on cards with a step -by -step algorithm.
*   Allow the use of a timer for self -control of the time.
*   Apply a "quiet signal" (for example, a light touch) to return attention.

# Developing exercises

Games and activity to support cognitive development.

### Attention and self -control
*   **"Fast!" / "The sea is worried once":** Develops a rapid reaction to the stop signal and body control.
*   **"Edible-universal":** He trains the reaction speed and impulsion of impulses.
*   **Board games (for example, UNO, jenga):** They teach to comply with the rules, wait for your turn and control impulsiveness.

### Memory and planning
*   **"Repeat me":** Repetition of the sequence of movements. Gradually increase the complexity.
*   **Cooking by recipe:** Joint cooking, following the exact sequence of steps.
*   **Stories from the pictures:** Drawing up a story based on a series of images, building a logical chain.

### Rhythm and coordination
*   **"Rhythmic echo":** Repetition of a given rhythm (cotton, tapping). Improves synchronization.
*   **Graphic dictations:** Develop auditory attention, retention of instructions and accuracy of movements.
