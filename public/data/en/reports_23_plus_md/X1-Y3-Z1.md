# 1. Analysis of the results

*   **Continous Performance Test (CPT):** The ability to long and high -quality concentration has been identified. This confirms the high level of self -control and attention to details, which is a key advantage when working with complex tasks.

# 2. Instruction to a person as a student/specialist

## Individual characteristics

*   **Pace:** The optimal pace is measured. When working with familiar tasks, the pace can be high. Switching of speeds or quick adaptation to unpredictable situations can cause difficulties.
*   **Perception:** Structural visual information is most effectively absorbed: schemes, tables, step -by -step instructions. You have the ability to "vision" the structure of the task.
*   **Focus of attention:** A deep and stable concentration on one task is characteristic. External stimuli minimally affect the focus when immersed in the process.

## Which contributes to success

*   **Visual supports:** Check lists, schemes, color coding of key points. The presence of a plan of action before my eyes facilitates the work.
*   **Step -by -step instructions:** Clear algorithms and understanding of the general structure of the task provide complete involvement in the process.
*   **Predictability:** The structured environment with clear and stable rules contributes to maximum performance.
*   **Monosadality:** The best results are achieved with a complete concentration on one task, despite the ability to multitasking.

## Which can reduce efficiency

*   **Chaotic multitasking:** The need for simultaneous response to diverse and unrelated incentives causes significant exhaustion.
*   **Unexpected introductory:** Tasks requiring a quick symmetric reaction or sudden change of leading action without preparation can lead to short -term confusion.
*   **Information noise:** The presentation of information without clear logic and structure prevents the formation of an internal strategy for solving the problem.
*   **Pressure and rush:** The adoption of important decisions in conditions of limited time, especially with new tasks, reduces productivity.

# 3. Recommendations

## For personal development

*   **Organization of work:** Starting to difficult tasks (studying new, personal projects), always start with planning or drawing up an intelligence card. Separate large tasks into consistent, controlled stages.
*   **Balance support:** To enhance the interaction of the cerebral hemispheres, engage in activities requiring coordination of both hands (musical instruments, juggling, assembly of complex models). This will improve the reaction rate in non -standard situations.
*   **Mercing of attention:** Effective work on one task is possible up to 60–90 minutes. After that, a short break (5-10 minutes) is recommended, preferably with moderate physical activity.

## For the educational environment

*   **Task wording:** Tasks should be formulated as clearly as possible, indicating specific success criteria. The optimal format is checklists or step-by-step algorithms.
*   **Information structure:** Presentations, instructions and training materials are more effective if they include schemes, graphs and tables, minimizing continuous text. Submit information with blocks: from a common goal to specific steps.
*   **Communication style:** The calm, logical style of communication, without emotional pressure and artificial haste is preferred. Give the time to "dive" in a new task to achieve the optimal result.
*   **Individual plan:** As part of the development, consider the preparation of an individual plan (IPR), including training on the development of interhemispheric relationships and making decisions in stressful situations.

# 4. Recommended training

These exercises contribute to the balancing of brain activity and the development of key skills.

## Attention and strategy

*   **Board games:** Chess, go, checkers. Develop prolonged concentration and strategic planning.
*   **Exercise "Find 10 differences":** Effectively trains attention to details.

## Coordination and balance

*   **Juggling:** Start with two balls, gradually increasing the complexity. One of the best methods for improving interhemispheric interaction.
*   **Symmetric drawing:** Simultaneous drawing of symmetrical figures (circles, squares, spirals) with both hands.
*   **Game on a musical instrument:** Especially piano, guitar or drums where coordinated work of both hands is required.

## Memory and rhythm

*   **Memory game:** Search for paired cards. Perfectly develops visual working memory.
*   **Rhythmic exercises:** Removing simple rhythms alternately or at the same time with both hands. Promotes synchronization of brain activity.
