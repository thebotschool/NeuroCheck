### **2. The guide for the success of your student**

Understanding the features of thinking and the attention of the student.

#### Individual approach

*   **Pace.** The optimal start is optimal. Haster increases the number of errors. Preferably calm immersion in the task.
*   **Perception.** Visual, requires simplicity. Information is better absorbed with a visual, structured performance without unnecessary details. Excessive text or "noisy" pages overload.
*   **Focus.** Throbbing. The effective concentration lasts 15–20 minutes, then a reboot is required. Attention is easily shifted by external (noise) and internal (thoughts) stimuli.

#### Support

*   **Visual landmarks.** Emphasizing, highlighting, using color for structuring information.
*   **A step -by -step approach.** Decomposition of complex tasks on 3-4 sequential steps. Check lines are effective.
*   **The rhythm of work.** Alternation of activity and rest (for example, 20/5 minutes). Simple rhythmic actions contribute to concentrations.
*   **The principle of "stop-think-make."** Practice of a short pause before action. It is possible to use gestures.

#### Obstacles

*   **Multitasking.** Parallel performance of tasks (for example, listen and write down) reduces effectiveness.
*   **Quick pace of speech.** Long, continuous oral instructions without visual support are perceived poorly.
*   **Unstructured material.** Continuous text without formatting complicates the release of key information.
*   **Speed ​​tasks.** The emphasis on speed, not quality, causes stress and provokes impulsive errors.

### **3. Actions to support**

#### Home environment

*   **Organization of DZ.** Divide the tasks into blocks, alternating items with breaks. Use a simple planner (2-3 tasks for the evening). Before starting, make sure of understanding the task: ask to retell.
*   **Promotion.** Mark efforts and process, not only the result. With impulsive errors, keep calm, offer a strategy: “Hurry, missed. Let's try to read twice next time. ”
*   **Working space.** Minimalistic. Only the necessary items. Remove distracting factors (phone, toys). The maximum concentration is 15-20 minutes. Plan short active breaks.

#### Educational environment

*   **Instructions.** Short, clear. Duplicate oral instructions in writing (board, card). Divide complex tasks into steps: “Read the paragraph 1. Find the keyword. Read the paragraph 2 ... ”
*   **Materials.** Provide templates (tables, schemes, plans). Use lists, markers, bold font to select key information.
*   **Place in the classroom.** The first-second desk, the center, far from distracting zones (window, door). The pace of the teacher’s speech: measured. Maintain eye contact for involvement.
*   **Iom.** It is allowed to use the ruler when reading. Give additional time on control. Assess not only the result, but also compliance with the steps.

### **4. Development of skills**

Games and exercises for the development of key cognitive functions. Daily practice is recommended for 10-15 minutes in a playful way.

#### Attention and accuracy

*   **Corrective tests.** Search and highlighting the specified characters in the text (for example, cross out everything “A” in the newspaper).
*   **"Find the differences."** Classical tasks for the development of targeted search and comparison.
*   **Graphic dictations.** Drawing on cells according to oral instructions (for example, “one to the right, two up”).

#### Self -control

*   **"Crushing-Sreedy."** A game of quick decision -making (catch/beat off the ball).
*   **"The sea is worried once."** Exercise for a signal stop, body control.
*   **Rhythmic games.** Repetition of a given rhythm (clap, steps) with gradual complication.

#### Coordination

*   **Walking along the line.** Movement in a straight line without gathering (straight, back forward).
*   **Balance.** Standing on one leg (with eyes closed), the use of a balancing board.
*   **Games with the ball.** Throwing the ball while performing a cognitive task (for example, call words to the letter).
