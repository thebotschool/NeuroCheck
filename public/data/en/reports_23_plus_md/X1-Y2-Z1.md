## **Cognitive profile. Grade.**

Memory (high). Control (low). Attention (high).
Age: 23+ years.

This report reveals the strengths and development zones. He offers strategies to support training and self -development.

## 1. A brief report for each test

### Visual working memory

Perfectly developed. Effective memorization and reproduction of information. High resistance to distracting factors.

### Impulse control (Go/No-Go Test)

The tendency to hasty, impulsive reactions. Difficulty with timely braking, which can lead to errors in situations of quick choice.

### Continuous Performance Test)

High level of sustainable attention. The ability to concentrate on one task for a long and productively, maintaining accuracy.

### General profile

Interesting profile: powerful cognitive "engines" (attention, memory) with insufficient "control" (impulsiveness). Deep immersion in work, but a tendency to haste without sufficient thought.

## 2. Success Guide

### Features of the profile

*   **Pace.** Fast, at times gusty. The tendency to action before analysis. The speed is useful in creativity, but requires accuracy in other tasks.
*   **Perception.** Better assimilates structured, visually organized information. Schemes, tables, intelligence cards and clear algorithms are preferable to solid text or oral instructions.
*   **Focus of attention.** Deep and stable. When immersed in a task, it is difficult to distract. Impulsiveness is manifested when switching tasks or the need for a quick response.

### Efficiency support

*   **The rule "Stop - Think - do."** Form a pause before the decision or delivery of work. For example: re -read letters before sending, count up to three before the answer.
*   **Visual plans and checklists.** Separation of large tasks into small, visible steps. This reduces a rush and gives a sense of control.
*   **Calm, predictable environment.** The minimum of chaos and surprises allows you to use deep attention.
*   **Clear evaluation criteria.** A clear understanding of the requirements helps to direct attention and avoid impulsive errors.

### Calls

*   **Fast multitasking.** For example, answers to messages during important work. Provokes impulsive reactions.
*   **Faring instructions.** Blurred formulations lead to a stupor or hasty, non -optimal solutions.
*   **Situations of instant reaction.** Blitz-pages, tasks with a rigid time limit.
*   **Psychological pressure.** Phrases like "come on faster" reduce the quality of work, pushing to rash actions.

## 3. Recommendations

### For independent work

*   **Planning tasks.** Before starting work, make a short plan (3-5 steps). This structures thoughts and reduces impulsiveness. Use digital planners or diary.
*   **Self -test.** Develop a habit of self -testing. Put the finished work for 15-30 minutes, then re -read a fresh look. This helps to notice the mistakes made in a hurry.
*   **Support.** Instead of criticism, use the supporting approach: "Great work! Let's see if you are in the place before delivery." Form the skill of self -control without stress.
*   **Attention management.** Productive work on one task is 45-60 minutes. Make short (5-10 minutes) breaks for "rebooting" and preventing automatic actions.

### For mentors and teachers

*   **The clarity of tasks.** Formulate the tasks specifically, if possible, dividing into subparagraphs. Provide the evaluation criteria in advance.
*   **Phased work.** Projects are better to break into stages with intermediate terms. This disciplines and prevents work at the last moment.
*   **Visualization of information.** A visual and logical format is preferred: presentations with diagrams, infographics, step -by -step instructions. With an oral explanation, duplicate the key points.
*   **Constructive feedback.** Let's feedback not only by the result, but also in the process. Example: "The idea is excellent, but in the calculations errors due to rush. Next time, release time for checking."

## 4. Recommended training

These exercises develop self -control and coordination, balancing the cognitive sides.

*   **Attention and control.**
    *   **Board games requiring strategies and patience.** Chess, Go, Economic Strategies. They teach to plan in advance, restrain impulsive moves.
*   **Coordination and rhythm.**
    *   **Playing musical instruments.** Especially shock or guitar. He trains the coordination "I see/hear - I control the movement." Dancing with clear choreography is also effective.
*   **Memory and reaction (controlled conditions).**
    *   **Brain training applications (lumosity, elevate).** Choose games where speed requires accuracy, and the error leads to a loss. Develops a balance of speed and accuracy.
*   **Awareness and control.**
    *   **Consciousness practices (MindFulness) and breathing exercises.** For example, "square breathing" (inhalation of 4, a delay of 4, exhale by 4, a delay of 4) before difficult work. It forms a pause between the stimulus and the reaction.
