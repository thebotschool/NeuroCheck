# Your cognitive profile

### 2. The guide for the success of your student

Understanding the individual features of information processing is the key to the disclosure of potential. This section describes the optimal conditions for learning.

*   **Individual characteristics**
    *   **Pace:** Capable of a flat, methodical pace, with deep immersion in tasks. In conditions of mental fatigue, the pace slows down, becomes intermittent and requires frequent pauses.
    *   **Perception format:** The greatest effectiveness shows when working with structured and visually organized information (diagrams, tables, lists, algorithms). The processing of several information flows is simultaneously difficult.
    *   **Focus of attention:** In a resource state, it demonstrates the ability to deep and prolonged concentration. At the moment, the focus of attention is weakened, easily distracted. This is a consequence of overload, not a lack of motivation.

*   **Which helps**
    *   **Clear structure:** Division of big tasks into understandable steps. The presence of a plan or checklist reduces the cognitive load.
    *   **Visual supports:** The use of Mind Cart, highlighting key thoughts, creating tables to compare information.
    *   **One task at a time:** Complete immersion in one process without attempts to parallel actions.
    *   **Predictability:** The ability to familiarize yourself with the material or plan of the lesson in advance and reduces stress and improves the mood.

*   **What interferes**
    *   **Multitasking:** The requirement of rapid switching between different types of activities depleted resources.
    *   **Rush and pressure:** Situations that require an instant reaction or decision -making in conditions of time deficit aggravate asymmetry and can lead to errors.
    *   **Information chaos:** Unstracting material, an abundance of background noise, tasks without clear criteria.
    *   **Ignoring fatigue:** Attempts to work through force lead to even greater depletion and decrease in productivity.

### 3. Recommendations

*   **For the house**
    *   **Organization of homework**
        *   **Block approach:** Perform tasks with blocks for 30–40 minutes, then a mandatory 10-minute break (without gadgets).
        *   **Priorities:** Start with the most difficult task, while the energy level is maximum.
        *   **Workplace:** Organize a quiet, ordered space. Everything you need should be at hand to minimize abstracts.
    *   **Support and promotion**
        *   **Dialogue:** Instead of the question "Have you done lessons?" Ask: “Where are you planning to start? Need help in decomposition of the task? "
        *   **Promotion:** Praise not for the assessment, but for the process. Example: "Your concentration and hour of work is excellent!"
        *   **Rest:** To emphasize that rest is not idleness, but an important element of brain restoration and productivity.

*   **For school (university)**
    *   **The formulation of tasks**
        *   **Clear tasks:** Provide written tasks with clear evaluation criteria and step -by -step execution plan.
        *   **Specifics:** Avoid general formulations. Example: instead of “analyze the text” use “1. Highlight three key ideas. 2. Give examples. 3. Formulate the conclusion. ”
    *   **Lesson structure/lecture**
        *   **Lesson plan:** To voice the plan at the beginning of the lesson, to summarize at the end.
        *   **Visualization:** Use presentations, patterns on the board, supporting abstracts for clarity.
    *   **Feed and control style**
        *   **Time for the answer:** Provide extra time to think about oral answers, do not require an immediate reaction.
        *   **Control format:** Preference for written works, projects or pre -prepared reports. Avoid spontaneous oral surveys or stress exams.
    *   **Elements of an individual educational route (IOM)**
        *   **Additional time:** Provide the possibility of increasing time on control work and exams.
        *   **Support materials:** Allow the use of sketches or plans for oral answers.

### 4. Recommended training

These exercises are aimed at removing current overload, developing coordination and strengthening attention. They should be performed in a playful way, without pressure.

*   **For attention and reduction of overload**
    *   **Breath "Square":** Inhale-dash-dash-carrying 4 seconds. Repeat 2-3 minutes to quickly relieve stress.
    *   **Correct test:** Take any printed text and cross all the specified letters (for example, “A” or “O” for 5 minutes. Gradually, you can complicate by crossing two different letters.

*   **For coordination and interhemispheric ties**
    *   **Juggling:** Start with the transfer of one ball from the hand in the hand in a high arc, focusing on smoothness. Then try with two. Effectively synchronizes the work of the hemispheres.
    *   **Asymmetric gymnastics:** At the same time, with one hand, draw a circle in the air, and with the other a square. Or stroking yourself on the head with one hand, and the other to pat on the stomach.

*   **For rhythm and bodily control**
    *   **Rhythmic drawings:** Simple rhythms (for example, “Ta-Ta-Ta-TA-TAM”). First with one hand, then the other, then two together.
    *   **Balance:** Stand on one leg for 30 seconds, walk around the curb or a line drawn on the floor. Improves the connection between the brain and the body, increasing the general tone.
