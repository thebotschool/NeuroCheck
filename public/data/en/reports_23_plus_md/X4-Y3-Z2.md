# Cognitive development report
### CPT+Go/No-Go+TCP (23+ years) rating

This report offers a detailed analysis of cognitive functions, identifying the strengths and areas for growth. Recommendations are designed to create an optimal environment for training and self -realization.

---

## 1. Test results

*   **Attention and memory (CPT):** Difficulties with prolonged concentration and memorization of sequences were revealed, especially with fatigue or stress.
*   **Reaction and braking (Go/No-Go):** Confident and quick action in the usual conditions. Slow adaptation to new tasks requiring non -automatic reactions is noted.
*   **Visual memory and attention (TCP):** The instability of attention, fatigue and focus loss were confirmed, especially in the presence of distracting factors.

## 2. The guide for the success of your student

### Individual characteristics
*   **Pace:** It takes time to adapt to the task. Fast exhaustion in monotonous work.
*   **Perception:** It more efficiently assimilates information in short, visually supported blocks (diagrams, lists, discharge). Audial perception without visual support is less effective.
*   **Focus of attention:** Attention “pulsates”: periods of concentration (15–20 minutes) are replaced by fatigue. It is difficult to hold the focus in a noisy or multitasking environment.

### Supporting factors
*   **Clear instructions:** Step-by-step guides and checklists for major tasks.
*   **Visualization:** Color markers, stickers, schemes, intelligence cards.
*   **Quiet environment:** Work in a calm atmosphere or with noise reduction.
*   **Rhythm and mode:** The alternation of work and rest (for example, the technique of "Mondor": 20–25 minutes of work, 5 minutes of break).
*   **One task:** Complete concentration on one task without switching.

### Interviewing factors
*   **Multitasking:** Simultaneous performance of several actions (for example, listening and abstract).
*   **Long oral instructions:** Long monologues without visual support.
*   **Fast change of topics:** Sharp transitions between tasks without logical connection.
*   **Background noise:** Talking, music, working TV.
*   **Pressure and criticism:** Phrases like “Gather”, “Do not be distracted”, “Why so slowly?”, Having increased stress.

## 3. Recommendations

### Home environment
*   **Work planning:**
    *   **Decomposition of tasks:** Separate large tasks into short, controlled blocks with breaks (for example, "20 minutes of mathematics, then a break").
    *   **Joint planning:** Create a clear evening plan (sticker, board): "Mathematics (2 tasks) → break → English (words)".
    *   **Preparation of the workplace:** Provide the clean, free space from distracting objects.
*   **Support and encouragement:**
    *   **The emphasis on the effort:** Mark the efforts and the process, and not just the result: "I see how you focused, this is valuable," "excellent that followed the plan."
    *   **Predictability:** The stable regime of the day (sleep, food, study, rest) reduces the cognitive load and increases efficiency.
*   **Realistic expectations:**
    *   Effective attention to a complex task is preserved **About 15–20 minutes**. Plan work, respectively.

### School environment
*   **The wording of tasks:**
    *   **Clear step -by -step instructions:** Provide the instructions in writing or duplicate oral options (for example, "1. Open p. 45. 2. Read the rule. 3. Follow ex. 5 (a, b)").
*   **Lesson structure:**
    *   **Visual plan:** Start a lesson with the recording of the plan on the board for predictability.
    *   **Visibility:** Use presentations, diagrams, videos to activate the visual channel of perception.
*   **Organization of space:**
    *   **Optimal place:** Place the student at the first or second desk in the center, away from windows and doors.
*   **Style style:**
    *   **Calm pace with pauses:** Give time to assimilate information after each block. Ask clarifying questions.
*   **Individual route (IOM):**
    *   **Additional time:** For control and independent work.
    *   **Blackian plan:** Allow the use for an essay or complex tasks.
    *   **"Sensory" breaks:** The possibility of short pauses (for example, go to the corridor for a minute).

## 4. Practical training

These exercises will help to develop key skills in a playful way. Regularity is more important than the duration (5-10 minutes are enough daily).

### Development of attention and memory
*   **"Corrective test."** In the printed text (magazine, book), offer to delete one letter (for example, “a”) within 5 minutes. Strengthens the stability of attention.
*   **Memorization of lists.** Start with 4-5 words (shopping list). Ask to remember and play in a minute. Gradually increase the amount.

### Coordination and interhemispheric interaction
*   **Kinesiological exercises.** Daily 2-3 minutes:
    1.  **"Kulak-Robe-Ladon"**: Alternately changing the position of the hand on the table.
    2.  **"Ear-too"**: Left hand on the tip of the nose, right on the left ear. Cotton, change of positions.
*   **Juggling.** Start with two small items. Attempts to coordinate both hands actively stimulate the brain.

### Body control and rhythm
*   **Rhythm tap.** You set a simple rhythmic pattern, the child repeats. Gradually complicate. Helps to configure internal rhythms and improves concentration.
*   **Balance exercises.** Walking along the line, stand on one leg (starting with 15 seconds). Develops the connection “brain-Telo” and self-control.
