# The cognitive profile of the student

### Understanding and development of abilities (23+ years)

This report offers a detailed view of the strengths and growth zones of your child, focused on consistent memory, inhibitory control, attention and impulsiveness. Recommendations will help to make training more effective and comfortable.

# 1. Overview of the results

**Consistent memory.** Excellent memorization of individual facts. Difficulties with building logical sequence and holistic perception of information.

**Brake control.** Easy to perform the usual actions. Difficulties when switching to new or non -standard tasks. Practice is required for better coordination of the hemispheres.

**Attention and impulsiveness.** A tendency to quick, impulsive actions in situations requiring patience. The nervous system reacts to a complete analysis of information.

# 2. The guide for the success of your student

## Features of training

**Pace.** A calm rhythm with short pauses minimizes impulsive “jerks”.

**Perception.** Optimally assimilates visual and step -by -step information: diagrams, tables, lists. Long oral text without support can cause confusion.

**Focus of attention.** Unstable when changing activities. Deep immersion in one task. Expensation efforts are required.

## Which contributes to success

*   **Visual support.** Schemes, numbered lists, infographics.
*   **Step -by -step instructions.** A clear algorithm of actions.
*   **A pause for thinking.** The habit of a short pause before action or answer.
*   **Rhythm.** Rhythm tapping, cross -line movements for synchronization of the hemispheres.

## What complicates training

*   **Multitasking.** Simultaneous performance of several actions.
*   **Fast change of tasks.** Transition without a break or logical bridge.
*   **Long oral instructions.** Lack of visual support.
*   **Pressure and rush.** Phrases of the type “Come on faster” provoke stress.

# 3. Recommendations

## At home

### Homework

*   Start large tasks by drawing up a plan: scheme, list.
*   Savior large tasks by 3-4 subtasibles. After each - a short break (2-3 minutes).
*   Use color markers to highlight key points.

### Support

*   Instead of criticism of errors, ask questions: “What step was missed?”, “What should you do first?”
*   Help the child to structure your thoughts, asking: “What is your plan?”, “Why do you start?”

### Promotion

*   Praise not only for the result, but also for the process: “You planned everything perfectly!”, “I see, you tried to follow the order.”

### Holding attention

*   The optimal concentration time on one type of educational activity: 15–20 minutes. Then you need a change in activity or a physical break.

## At school

### Setting tasks

*   Instructions let's in the form of a clear, numbered algorithm.
*   **Example:** Instead of “Write an essay on the book read”, say: “Write a plan from 3 points: 1. The main characters. 2. The most interesting point. 3. What this book taught me. Then write one paragraph at each item. "

### The structure of information

*   On the board and in handouts, use diagrams, tables, timeage.

### Location

*   The optimal place in the class: the first-second desk, closer to the center. This minimizes distraction.

### Presentation style

*   Calm, structured, with pauses for understanding.
*   Before the class question: "I give you 10 seconds to think."

### Individual approach

*   Providing a brief visual lesson plan.
*   Permission to use a draft for response/solution planning.
*   An additional minute to think about an answer in an oral survey.

# 4. Developing training

Simple exercises for the development of key skills.

## Attention and control

*   **"Forbidden movement."** The host shows movements, one of which cannot be repeated. The task is to restrain himself.
*   **"Graphic dictation."** Drawing under dictation (for example, “one cage to the right, two cells up”). Develops auditory attention and step -by -step actions.

## Coordination and integration

*   **"Crossroads."** Standing in place, alternately touch the right elbow of the left knee and the left elbow of the right knee. Perform 1-2 minutes before homework.
*   **Finger gymnastics.** The simultaneous execution of different movements with both hands (for example, one hand shows the “class”, the other - “okay”, then change).

## Memory and sequence

*   **Compilation of comics.** After reading the story/viewing of the cartoon, draw 4-6 key personnel in the correct order.
*   **"Recipe from memory."** Lay out cards with the stages of cooking a simple dish in the right sequence.

## Rhythm and bodily control

*   **"Echo".** Take a simple rhythm, the child’s task is to repeat it exactly. Gradually complicate the rhythmic pattern.
*   **Passing the labyrinths.** On paper or “floor” from objects. Develops planning and control of movements.
