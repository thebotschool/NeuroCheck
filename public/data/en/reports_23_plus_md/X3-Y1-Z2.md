### 2. Success Guide

Your schoolboy demonstrates a high -quality, but concentrated focus. He successfully copes with complex tasks, but may experience difficulties with routine work.

#### Individual profile

*   **Temp: Sprint.**
    Effective in short, intense intervals (15–20 minutes). It quickly gets tired of long monotonous work.
*   **Perception format: architect.**
    It better assimilates information as an integral system (diagrams, maps, infographics). It is important to see a complete picture, not individual elements.
*   **Focus of attention: spotlight.**
    Quickly activated on interesting, new, complex tasks. Quickly loses interest in boring or obscure tasks.

#### Effective methods

*   **Work in short intervals.** Method Methods: 20 minutes of focused work, 5 minutes of outdoor activities.
*   **Visual supports.** Schemes, mental cards, timline, checklists. Help to see the structure entirely.
*   **Element of the game and call.** Tasks requiring decision making, searching for errors, choosing a strategy, competition.
*   **Clear, step -by -step instructions.** For routine tasks, decorated in the form of lists.

#### Which reduces productivity

*   **Monotonous, long lectures.** Without breaks and interactive.
*   **Multitasking.** Simultaneous retention of unrelated processes.
*   **Oral instructions.** Without visual support. Information is quickly lost.
*   **Long data sequences.** Remoning without understanding internal logic.

### 3. Practical recommendations

#### For the home environment

*   **Organization of homework:**
    *   Miss tasks into short blocks for 15–25 minutes. Turn on mandatory breaks. Use the timer.
    *   Start with the most complex or uninteresting objects.
    *   Make a plan for execution: for example, "3 examples in mathematics, then a break, then 2 pages of history."
*   **Support methods:**
    *   Help structure the information. Create schemes, plans, main thoughts.
    *   Turn memorization into a game: use cards, come up with associations.
*   **Forms of encouragement:**
    *   Praise for resourcefulness and result, and not for the duration of efforts. Extract his self -control and strategic thinking.

#### For the educational environment

*   **Formulation of tasks:**
    *   Offer tasks as problems, quests or cases for a clear purpose.
    *   For routine exercises: provide a clear and short algorithm or checklist.
*   **Lesson structure:**
    *   Serve the material with blocks. Plan of the lesson on the board. A brief resume at the end.
    *   Use interactive style: short discussions, questions, work in pairs. This updates attention.
*   **Placing and feeding of the material:**
    *   The optimal place is closer to the board, to minimize distracting factors.
    *   Presentations: schemes, graphs, keywords. Avoid continuous text.
*   **Individual educational route (IOM):**
    *   Allow the use of a timer on a desk for self -control of the time.
    *   Provide tasks in the form of printed checklists.
    *   When evaluating projects: take into account the originality of the solution strategy.

### 4. Developing exercises

These games and exercises will help to develop supporting skills in a fascinating form.

*   **For stability of attention:**
    *   **Board games:** Requiring the expectation of the course and tracking the actions of others (for example, “jackal”, “karcasson”, chess).
    *   **Correct test:** Ringing certain letters or bypassing prepositions in the text for a while.
*   **For structural memory and sequence:**
    *   **The game "Remember and Draw":** Reproduction of a scheme of 4-5 geometric shapes after 10 seconds of viewing.
    *   **Drawing up stories:** Binding 5 random words in one logical story.
*   **For bodily control and rhythm:**
    *   **Juggling:** Starting with two balls. Develops coordination, concentration and the ability to distribute attention.
    *   **Rhythm fighting games:** Repetition with claps or on the table of a given rhythm, gradually complicating.
