# Cognitive assessment: Path to potential (23+ years)

This report reveals the unique cognitive profiles of your student. Our recommendations based on accurate testing data are designed to create optimal conditions for its development and training.

# 1. Assessment of key skills

### Test: Attention (TCP)
Strong side: deep and prolonged concentration on one task in the absence of distracting factors. Potential for thoughtful work.

### Test: Braking (Go/No-Go)
Growth zone: Difficulties with the operational containment of impulses and hasty actions. It is especially manifested with fatigue or quick change in reactions.

### Test: Visual Working Memory (CPT)
Removing information (images, facts) is effective. Difficulties are possible with the reproduction of the sequence of events or steps.

# 2. The guide for the success of your student

Understanding the unique features of your student is the key to effective training and reducing stress.

### Individual profile

*   **Pace:** Uneven. The period of "swing" was noted, followed by a quick, but impulsive pace of work. Own rhythm is preferred, without haste.
*   **Perception:** Visual-structural. The best assimilation of information through visual, step-by-step formats: schemes, checklists, infographics. Long oral instructions are less effective.
*   **Focus:** "Tunnel". The ability to deep immersion in one task. Difficulties with switching. External irritants or multitasking can disorient.

### Optimal strategies

*   **Visual supports:** Check lists, schemes of action, highlighting key points.
*   **Step -by -step instructions:** Separation of large tasks into small, controlled stages.
*   **Predictable rhythm:** The use of a timer for work and breaks (for example, the method of a prying).
*   **Pause before action:** The rule "count up to three" to control impulsivity.
*   **One -time task:** Focusing in one case without parallel processes.

### Factors slowing progress

*   **Multitasking:** Simultaneous requirements lead to errors or failure.
*   **Fast change of tasks:** The lack of a short pause for the "reboot" between the activities.
*   **Long oral instructions:** Partial perception and violation of the order of action.
*   **Noise and distracting factors:** They deplete the resource of self -control, reduce concentration.

# 3. Practical recommendations

### For the home environment

*   **Organization of work:**
    *   Joint preparation of a simple plan (2-4 points) before starting homework.
    *   Using a timer for fixing work intervals (20-25 minutes) and active breaks.
    *   Minimalistic workplace: only the necessary items for the current task.
*   **Support and motivation:**
    *   Encouraging attentiveness and following the plan, and not just speed. Example: "Great, what I checked before the surrender!"
    *   Assistance in structuring oral answers through leading questions.
*   **Attention management:**
    *   Productive work on one task is up to 25-30 minutes. Short breaks are necessary for "restarting" attention.

### For the school environment

*   **The wording of tasks:**
    *   Step -by -step instructions provided in stages, or in writing.
    *   Separation of complex instructions into separate, easily absorbed commands.
*   **Lesson structure:**
    *   The optimal location: the first-second desk, far from distracting factors (windows, doors).
    *   The use of visual elements on the board: color, emphasis, simple schemes.
*   **Teaching style:**
    *   Calm, predictable, structured approach.
    *   Providing time to think on an answer; Avoiding the requirement of instant reaction.
*   **Elements of an individual route:**
    *   Permission to use sheets-planes or checklists to control progress.
    *   Additional time during control work, an emphasis on verification to reduce errors caused by impulsiveness.

# 4. Training of skills

The proposed exercises and games are easy to integrate into everyday life for the development of key abilities.

### Attention and control of impulses

*   **Reaction games:** "Edible, unauthorized" or "no, do not say"-develop the ability to timely inhibition.
*   **Graphic dictations:** Strengthen the skill of following instructions and maintaining attention.
*   **Correct samples:** Daily short exercises (for example, deleting the letter "A" in the text) to increase the resistance of attention.

### Memory for a sequence

*   **Board games:** Games with complex rules, requiring memorizing the sequence of moves ("jackal", "caackasson").
*   **Rhythmic repetitions:** Reproduction of rhythms with claps or a knock with gradual complication.
*   **"Build from memory":** Reproduction of short Lego designs after quick viewing, observing the exact order.

### Bodily control and coordination

*   **"FROME-OUTOMI":** Classic game for instant stopping by signal.
*   **Games with the ball:** The commands "Catch!", "Don’t catch!", "Find off!" Training the speed of reaction and braking.
*   **Motor sequences:** Removing and repetition of simple dance ligaments or morning charging complexes.
