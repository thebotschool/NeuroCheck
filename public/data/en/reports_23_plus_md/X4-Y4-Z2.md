# Cognitive development report

### Key aspects and recommendations for disclosing the potential of the student.
Age: 23+ years

---

## 1. Evaluation. Key results.

### Test for stability of attention and memory (CPT)
Difficulties with maintaining attention and memorization of the sequence were noted. The resource of attention is quickly exhausted.

### Braking and control test (Go/No-Go)
Difficulties in containing impulsive answers were revealed, especially with fatigue. The development of self -control is required.

### Visual Working Memory Test (TCP)
Attention is quickly exhausted, which complicates a long -term concentration and can lead to forgetfulness.

### General conclusion
The main area for development is **attention management and self -control**. Fast fatigue from monotonous or complex tasks is noted, which leads to impulsiveness and focus loss. This indicates the features of the nervous system that can be taken into account for effective development.

## 2. The guide for the success of your student.

### Features of training

*   **Pace.** It’s more comfortable for the child to work at a calm, measured pace with regular short breaks. Hurry increases the number of errors and reduces concentration.
*   **Perception.** Information is better absorbed at **visual presentation**: schemes, pictures, short lists, algorithms. Long oral explanations are less effective.
*   **Focus.** Attention is unstable. The child is easily distracted, especially if the task seems complex or boring.

### Support

*   **Step -by -step instructions.** Separation of volumetric tasks into 2-3 small, understandable steps.
*   **Visual tips.** Cards with rules, checklists, highlighting important in the text.
*   **Predictability.** A clear daily routine, the use of timers for work and rest.
*   **"One task."** At the workplace, only necessary for the current task.
*   **Tactile stimulation.** The ability to use anti-stress toys or short physical exercises in breaks.

### Obstacles

*   **Multitasking.** Fulfillment of several cases at the same time.
*   **Sharp shift.** Switching between tasks without micropauses.
*   **Distracting factors.** Noise, disorder, background stimuli.
*   **Long instructions.** Oral tasks containing more than two steps.

## 3. Recommendations.

### Home environment

*   **Homework.** Divide into blocks. Optimal mode: **15–20 minutes of work, 5-7 minutes break**. In the break - activity without gadgets.
*   **Timer.** Helps to indicate the final working hours.
*   **Priority.** Start with more complex objects while attention is concentrated.
*   **Plan.** Discuss the sequence of tasks.
*   **Promotion.** Praise for **Diligence and process**, not just for the result.
*   **Workplace.** Provide a quiet, permanent place for classes without visual noise.
*   **Attention.** Active attention on a difficult task - before **15 minutes**. Do not demand more without a break.

### School environment

*   **Instructions.** Formulate briefly, clearly, step by step. Duplicate oral instructions **Visually**.
    *   _Example: Divide a complex team into simple steps with pauses._
*   **The structure of the lesson.** Use visual plans. Warn about a change of activity.
*   **Placement.** Optimal - **First-second desk**, in the center, far from distracting zones (windows, doors).
*   **Interaction.** Use non -verbal signals to return attention.
*   **Individual approach.**
    *   Additional time in control work.
    *   Permission to use a draft or a line for reading/writing.

## 4. Practical exercises.

These games and exercises will help develop skills that now require support. Regularity is important, not duration (5-10 minutes daily).

### Attention

*   **Graphic dictations.** Develop auditory attention and following instructions.
*   **Corrective tests.** Crossing or bypassing certain letters in the text.
*   **"Find the differences."** Improve concentration and visual attention.

### Coordination and control

*   **"FROME-OOMI."** Exercise for body control and timely stop.
*   **Games with the ball.** Develop coordination and reaction with additional conditions.

### Memory and logic

*   **"What has changed?"** Develops visual memory and attentiveness (change in location or absence of objects).
*   **Rhythmic repetitions.** The exact reproduction of the specified rhythms. Gradual complication.

### Rhythm and planning

*   **Visual schedules.** Joint preparation of plans for the day (drawings, stickers). Develops planning and self -control.
