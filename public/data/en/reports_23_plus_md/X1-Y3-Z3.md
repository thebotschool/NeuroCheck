# Cognitive assessment report

**Profile: (23+ years)**

This report was created to help you, mentors and teachers to better understand your strengths and areas for development. The purpose of the recommendations is to make training and self -development more effective.

# 1. Review of tests

### Visual working memory
Excellent concentration and memorization of visual information. The memory is resistant to distraction, which indicates a strong cognitive function.

### GO/No-go test (reaction braking)
The dominant hand reacts faster and more precisely. This may indicate the difficulties in synchronization of the cerebral hemispheres with quick actions.

### Continous Performance Test (attention and impulsiveness)
A tendency to quick decisions, sometimes before receiving complete information. This indicates determination, but also about the risk of hasty mistakes.

# 2. Your learning style

## Individual characteristics

### Pace
Your pace is dual. You are capable of deep concentration, but are also prone to quick, impulsive solutions.

### Perception
You perfectly learn structured visual information: diagrams, tables, logical chains, algorithms.

### Focus of attention
Able to keep attention for a long time on one task. Impulsiveness can interrupt this condition, pushing to quick, but not always thought -out actions.

## Which helps

### Inner pause
Before the answer or decision, make a short pause. This gives the brain time to fully process information.

### Visual supports
Use intelligence cards, lists, checklists, step-by-step plans. Visualization reduces the risk of errors.

### Coordination of hands
Classes requiring the work of both hands (music, juggling) help synchronize the work of the cerebral hemispheres.

## What interferes

### Multitasking
The simultaneous performance of several tasks increases the risk of impulsive errors. Work sequentially.

### Time pressure
Strong deadlins provoke hasty and non -optimal solutions.

### Lack of structure
Chaotic information or fuzzy tasks make it difficult to use your logical thinking.

# 3. Recommendations

## For home and study

### Preparation for tasks
Start large tasks with a detailed plan. Divide them into small steps. After each step, make a mini-check.

### Self -support
Do not criticize yourself for hasty mistakes. Analyze them: "What went wrong? What was missing?" The error becomes experience.

### Organization of attention
Effectively work for 30–45 minutes. Then do 5-10 minutes of a break for "rebooting" and reducing impulsiveness.

## For study and careers

### The wording of the tasks
The tasks with clumsy step -by -step instructions and known evaluation criteria are ideal. For example, an essay with a given structure.

### The structure of information
Use presentations, graphs, tables, diagrams. Abstract explanations are less effective.

### Communication style
A calm, logical style without emotional pressure suits you. Give yourself time to ponder the answer, avoid instant reactions.

### Individual path
Develop "flexible skills": critical thinking, time management, decision -making techniques.

# 4. Training

Simple exercises for the balance of cognitive functions and potential disclosure.

## Attention and self -control

### Chess or th
Develop the ability to calculate the consequences and avoid impulsive actions.

### Thinking the consequences
Before an important choice, ask yourself: "What will happen if I do it? And if B?"

### Respiratory practices
Determine the "square breath" (4 breaths, 4 delays, 4 exhalations, 4 delays). Apply in stressful situations or before making decisions.

## Coordination and interhemispheric interaction

### Juggling
Start with two goals. Great exercise for synchronization of the hemispheres.

### Symmetric drawing
Draw at the same time with two hands mirror patterns (circles, waves, butterflies).

### Musical instruments
The piano or guitar require complex coordinated work of both hands.

## Memorial maintenance

### Game "Find a couple" (Memory)
Classical game for training visual memory. Available in digital and desktop versions.

### Memory Palace method
Study this mnemonics. It is ideal for memorizing lists, facts, structure of reports.
