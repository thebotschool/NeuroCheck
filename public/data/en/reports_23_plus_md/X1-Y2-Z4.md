# Cognitive profile report

This report is an analysis of the cognitive profile of the student. Its goal is to identify strengths and areas for development, contributing to the creation of an optimal environment for training and growth.

## 1. Overview of cognitive areas

*   **Visual working memory:** Great ability to memorize information and strategic thinking. This is a strong side and a valuable resource.
*   **Inhibitor control (test Go/No-Go):** A tendency to impulsive actions and a hurry is noted, which can lead to errors due to insufficient exposure.
*   **Attention (CPT):** Unshabited unstable and quickly. This probably indicates a state of mental fatigue or cognitive overload.

**Bottom line:** The student has high potential for strategic thinking, but is now experiencing overwork. Impulsiveness is probably caused by the exhaustion of resources, and is not a constant feature. This leads to attempts to “slip” the tasks, reducing the quality.

## 2. The guide for the success of your student

### Individual characteristics

*   **Pace:** Productive in the presence of strength. In conditions of fatigue, pressure or multitasking, prone to rush and errors.
*   **Perception:** Effectively assimilates visually structured information: diagrams, tables, checklists, intelligence cards. It is preferable to solid text or oral explanations.
*   **Focus of attention:** Unstable and easily depleted. Concentration is possible in interesting tasks, but short intervals (20–25 minutes). Monotony and frequent switching cause rapid fatigue.

### Which helps

*   **Visual supports:** Check lists, schemes, intelligence cards.
*   **Clear structure:** Step -by -step, understandable tasks.
*   **The rule of one task:** Complete concentration without abstracts.
*   **Planning:** Preliminary drawing up a brief plan of action. Promotes a strategic approach.
*   **Mode:** Frequent, short breaks (for example, the technique of prying: 25/5 minutes).

### What interferes

*   **Multitasking:** Simultaneous performance of several tasks or quick switching.
*   **Abstract instructions:** Fongy formulations of tasks.
*   **Rush and pressure:** Phrases that cause stress and impulsive errors.
*   **Information noise:** Distracting factors (sounds, open tabs) quickly deplete attention.

## 3. Recommendations

### For the house

#### Organization of tasks

*   Make a work plan with the priority of complex tasks at the beginning.
*   Miss large projects into parts, distributing them by days.
*   Use a timer for rhythm and control (for example, 25/5 minutes).

#### Support and motivation

*   Reduce pressure and anxiety. Emphasize that fatigue is normal, not a sign of laziness.
*   Encourage the process, not just the result.
*   Provide a good rest: walks, hobbies, time without gadgets.

#### Optimal concentration

Productive attention: 20–25 minutes for a difficult task, then a short break (5-10 minutes).

### For school

#### The formulation of tasks

*   Provide clear, step -by -step instructions with criteria.
*   Use written instructions or checks for complex tasks.

#### The structure of the lesson

*   Place the student away from distracting factors.
*   Serve complex topics visually: diagrams, tables, diagrams.

#### Communication style

*   Use a calm, supporting tone. Pause before questions.
*   Avoid questions that require an instant answer, especially on difficult topics.

#### Individual approach

*   Additional time in control and independent work.
*   Phased delivery of large feedback projects.

## 4. Recommended training

Training for the development of self -control, attention and resource management.

### For attention and self -control

*   **The game "Delay":** Instant fixation of the posture by signal.
*   **Board games:** Chess, GO, Jenga. Develop a strategy and control of impulsivity.

### For memory and strategy

*   **Assembly of complex puzzles/designers (Lego Technic):** He trains the sequence and attention to the details.
*   **Graphic dictations:** Drawing on cells under dictation.

### For rhythm and bodily control

*   **Juggling:** From two items. Develops coordination and concentration.
*   **RIGHT RIGHTS:** Repeating the specified rhythms. Develops a sense of rhythm.
