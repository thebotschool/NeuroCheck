### **2. Features of the educational process**

This section will help you optimize the learning process, taking into account the individual style of your child.

#### **Individual characteristics**

*   **Pace:** Fast, gusty. The tendency to action before thinking. Slow down reduces the number of errors.
*   **Perception:** Preference of clearly structured information. Small blocks and visual accents (emphasizing, color) improve absorption. Overloads the continuous text.
*   **Attention:** Active, but unstable. Quick switching with surface analysis. Difficulties with a long focus on monotonous tasks.

#### **Effective approaches**

*   Step -by -step instructions: Savior tasks into 2-3 simple steps. Speak or write them down.
*   Visual support: Use a ruler when reading. Circle the key elements in the tasks.
*   The principle of "Stop. Think. Do.": Teach a short pause before the answer or action (for example, the score is up to three).
*   Rhythm: rhythmic actions (tapping, quiet pronunciation) help to slow down the pace and synchronize the brain.
*   Two -handed work: Tasks requiring the participation of both hands (modeling, playing instrument, designers) improve interhemispheric ties.

#### **Barriers in training**

*   Multivatism: simultaneous listening and recording, or execution of two cases, leads to errors.
*   Oral instructions: Fast instructions without visual support are often ineffective.
*   Speed ​​tasks: stimulate impulsiveness, increasing the number of errors. Quality priority over speed.
*   Distracting factors: mess, loud sounds, background music overload attention.

### **3. Practical recommendations**

#### **At home**

*   **Organization of tasks:**
    *   Order: Eliminate distracting objects from the table. Principle: "One task is one textbook."
    *   Plan: Agree on the sequence and time (for example, "Mathematics 15 minutes, a break of 5 minutes, Russian 15 minutes"). Use the timer.
    *   Check: focus on the process. Instead of "did it?" Ask: "Let's check if all the conditions are taken into account?" Help me find missing.
*   **Support:**
    *   Encourage the process: "Your efforts and self -test is excellent!" More important than the assessment.
    *   Avoid criticism: replace "Be careful!" On "back to this step" or "How do you understand the task?"
*   **Focus of attention:** Effective concentration lasts 15–20 minutes. Then a short (5-7 minutes) physical break is needed.

#### **At school**

*   **Instructions:**
    *   Cellular: Let's short, clear instructions. Record them on the board.
    *   Accents: Select keywords (`НЕ`, `КРОМЕ`) with color and emphasizing.
*   **Lesson structure:**
    *   Accommodation: Plant the child closer to the teacher and the board.
    *   Contact: Establish eye contact before the task. Ask to repeat the instructions.
    *   Change of activity: Alternate activities every 15–20 minutes (letter, oral work, visual materials, physical education).
*   **Individual support:**
    *   Time: provide extra time for control and independent work.
    *   Tools: allow to use a ruler or a clean sheet for focusing on a line of text.
    *   Broken: divide long tasks into small parts. Allow to take the work in stages.

### **4. Developing activity**

These games and exercises will help to develop the necessary skills in everyday life.

*   **Development of attention and control:**
    *   "Edible-universal": a classic game for training reaction inhibition.
    *   "FRIEND-OOMI": develops bodily control and a quick reaction to the signal.
    *   Corrective tests: on the old page of the magazine, replace certain letters (for example, "a") or circle prefects. Start with 5 minutes.

*   **Coordination and interhemispheric interaction:**
    *   Synchronous drawing: simultaneous drawing of symmetrical patterns (circles, waves) with both hands.
    *   Juggling: Start with two light objects. Develops the coordination of the "eye-hand" and synchronizes the brain.
    *   "Fist-Robric Ladon": a consistent change of three positions of the hand on the table. Perform slowly, alternately with each hand, then two at the same time.

*   **Visual memory training:**
    *   The game "Memory": develops memorizing the location and content of images.
    *   "What has changed?": Lay out 5-7 items. The child remembers, turns away. You change one item. The task is to determine the change.
