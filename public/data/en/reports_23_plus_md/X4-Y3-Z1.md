# Cognitive development report: CPT+Go/No-GO+TCP (23+ years)

Welcome. This report reveals the potential of your child. It was created on the basis of cognitive tests and offers practical recommendations for home and school.

## 1. Evaluation of the results

*   **Long -term attention (CPT):** Difficulties with maintaining concentration on monotonous tasks and memorizing the sequence have been identified. The energy of attention is reduced with monotonous actions.
*   **Braking and reaction (Go/No-Go):** Asymmetry is noted in the speed and confidence of the actions between the hands. The development of coordination and interhemispheric ties is required.
*   **Selective attention (TCP):** High concentration, accuracy and composure when performing interesting, complex or requiring tasks. This is a key force.

**Conclusion:** The attention of the child is selective. He demonstrates a high focus with passion, but quickly loses concentration from routine.

## 2. Approach to learning

### Key features

*   **Pace:** It works effectively short intervals. Frequent breaks are required to maintain a long -distance productivity.
*   **Perception:** He prefers visual and step-by-step information (diagrams, checklists). Perception by ear or continuous text is less effective.
*   **Focus:** The concentration is activated by interest, challenge or game element. Monotonous tasks quickly exhaust attention.

### Effective methods

*   **Visual tips:** The use of stickers, diagrams, color selection.
*   **Step -by -step instructions:** Clear checklists for completing tasks reduce the load as a memory.
*   **Time management:** The use of techniques (for example, 20/5) to maintain energy and concentration.
*   **Game elements:** The transformation of routine tasks into fascinating challenges.

### Obstacles

*   **Multitasking:** Simultaneous listening and abstract reduces the assimilation of information.
*   **Monotonous tasks:** Long -term similar exercises lead to loss of concentration and an increase in errors.
*   **Oral instructions:** Lack of visual or structural support with long oral explanations.
*   **Unstructured environment:** Background noise, mess, frequent change of requirements without warning.
<!-- page-break -->

## 3. Recommendations

### Home practices

*   **Performing tasks:**
    *   Separate homework into blocks **15–20 minutes**. Short breaks are required.
    *   Start with the most difficult or least interesting tasks.
    *   Use a timer to visualize time and self -control.
    *   Make a joint plan for the evening in the form of a checklist.
*   **Support and motivation:**
    *   Ask about the difficulties: “What was the most difficult today? How can this be simplified? ”
    *   Praise for the efforts made, not only for the result: “You tried not to be distracted - this is valuable!”

### Support at school

*   **The wording of tasks:**
    *   Tasks should be clumsy, brief, divided into 2-3 steps.
*   **Lesson structure:**
    *   Use schemes, numbered lists, color selection.
    *   Give the opportunity to move (for example, distribute materials) for "restarting" attention.
*   **Accommodation and feed:**
    *   Optimal place: 1-2 desks in the central row to minimize abstracts.
    *   Put into the lesson with direct questions and short practical tasks.
*   **Elements of an individual educational route (IOM):**
    *   Allow the use of a simple timer for self -control during independent work.
    *   Provide written instructions for complex oral tasks.

## 4. Developing exercises

The presented exercises will help to develop key skills.

### Attention and memory

*   **Correct samples:** Ringing or bypassing certain characters in the text (from 3-5 minutes).
*   **Graphic dictations:** Drawing on cells under dictation.
*   **"Find the differences":** Passing training on details.

### Coordination and interhemispheric interaction

*   **Symmetric drawing:** Simultaneous drawing of simple figures with two hands.
*   **Juggling:** Renting one or two objects from hand to hand.
*   **"Fist-Robric Ladon":** A consistent change in the positions of the hands on the table, including synchronously.

### Bodily control and rhythm

*   **Balance games:** Walking along the line, stand on one leg, "classics."
*   **Rhythm playing:** Repetition and creation of rhythmic drawings (pops, stomping).
