# Cognitive assessment report
### PROTELLENT OF ATTENTION, Control and Memory (23+ years)

This report will help you better understand the cognitive features of the student. The test results indicate high potential, but the current state of exhaustion can temporarily affect its manifestation.

---

### 1. Review of indicators

*   **Attention (CPT):** The concentration is unstable, "floats". Indicates cognitive overload.
*   **Self-control (Go/No-Go):** Difficulties with braking of impulsive reactions have been identified. Probably a consequence of fatigue and a decrease in the resource.
*   **Visual memory:** Great ability to memorize and structure visual information. This is a strong side.

**Key conclusion:** The schoolboy has high cognitive potential. However, current exhaustion affects concentration and self -control. Recommendations are aimed at restoring the resource and adaptation of the environment.

<!-- page-break -->

### 2. Features of training

#### Individual profile

*   **Pace:** Uneven, slowed down. He is quickly depleted, cannot work for a long time without a break. The speed requirement causes stress.
*   **Perception:** Effectively perceives structured visual information: diagrams, tables, Mayind cards. Continuous text or long speech is tiring.
*   **Focus of attention:** Short -term. Deep concentration is possible up to 15–20 minutes. A short "reboot" is required.

#### Which helps

*   Visual support: schemes, color markers, brief notes.
*   Step -by -step instructions: decomposition of tasks for small, specific steps.
*   The “one task at a time” mode: the exclusion of multitasking, the lack of distracting factors.
*   Predictable rhythm: a clear alternation of short work and rest intervals.

#### What interferes

*   Multivativity: simultaneous performance of lessons and the use of gadgets.
*   Hurrying and pressure: phrases like “come on faster”, hard deadlines.
*   Large volumes of information: long texts without paragraphs, lectures without pauses.
*   Unstructured environment: noise, disorder, constant distraction.

<!-- page-break -->

### 3. Recommendations

#### For the house

*   **Organization of work:**
    *   **Modor technique:** 20 minutes of focused work, then 5 minutes of complete rest (without gadgets).
    *   Start with the most difficult or unpleasant task while the resource is high.
    *   Brief written plan: what, how, how much. Distribution of points for a sensation of control.
*   **Support and encouragement:**
    *   Focus on the process, not on the assessment: ask “what did you find out?”, “What was difficult?”, “How did you deal?”.
    *   Without criticism for errors or slowness: this is a symptom of fatigue. Decomposition of the tasks: "Let's start with the first step?"
    *   A full sleep (at least 8–9 hours) and a balanced diet. Temporarily reduce the extracurricular load.

#### For school

*   **The wording of tasks:**
    *   Clear, step -by -step, written tasks (on a board or card). For example: “1. Indicate 3 reasons. 2. Give examples. 3. Make a conclusion. ”
*   **Lesson structure:**
    *   Short pauses (1-2 minutes) every 15–20 minutes during the explanation of the new material.
    *   Using visual materials: presentations, schemes, infographics. Providing access to materials after the lesson.
*   **Class placement:**
    *   Optimal: the first or second desk, far from distracting factors (windows, doors).
*   **Individual route:**
    *   Temporarily reduce homework or provide more time for their implementation (without reducing the assessment). Purpose: to relieve stress, focus on quality.

<!-- page-break -->

### 4. Practical exercises

The main task is to give the brain rest and recover. Exercises should be short, game and without pressure.

*   **For attention and self -control:**
    *   Coloring on numbers or mandalas: a meditative lesson that develops concentration without stress.
    *   Respiratory exercises (for example, “square breath”): 2-3 minutes to calm and concentration.
*   **For memory (with support to the stronger side):**
    *   The game "Memory": Training of visual memory in a light format.
    *   Memory Palace Method: memorization of lists using the imaginary space.
*   **For bodily control and coordination:**
    *   Balance exercises: standing on one leg, walking along the line.
    *   Simple rhythmic exercises: thawing rhythms, repeating for audio. Structures the brain.
