## Report on the cognitive development of a student

This report offers a detailed view of the cognitive profile of your student. Our recommendations are designed to create a supporting environment that contributes to harmonious development and disclosure of full potential. Age group: 23+ years.

### 1. Report on testing results

The test results reveal key features of the student’s cognitive profile.

#### Attention and memory (CPT)

The task required a long maintenance of attention and memorization of the sequence. Fatual fatigue of attention is noted, especially when performing monotonous or complex tasks.

#### Impulse control (Go/No-Go)

There is a tendency to quick, impulsive actions, often without fully pondering the instructions. This can lead to errors caused by inattention.

#### Visual working memory

The results confirm the characteristic feature: making decisions until all information. This is not a problem, but an individual data processing style.

#### Key conclusions

The student demonstrates activity and fast pace. At the current stage of development, he needs support in managing attention and restraining impulsive reactions. These features are part of the work of the nervous system and are subject to development.

### 2. The guide for the success of your student

Understanding individual characteristics helps optimize the learning process.

#### Individual profile

*   **Pace:** Fast, gusty. The periods of high activity replacing fatigue are characteristic. Maintaining an even working pace is a growth zone.
*   **Perception:** Short, clear and visual information (images, diagrams, brief videos) is more effective. Long oral explanations can overload the working memory.
*   **Focus of attention:** Unstable, selective. It is easily concentrated on interesting, but is quickly distracted from monotonous tasks by external stimuli or its own thoughts.

#### What supports

*   **Visual supports:** Check lists, timers (visual), schemes, highlighting keywords.
*   **Step -by -step instructions:** Separation of large tasks into 2-3 small steps. Phased issuance of instructions.
*   **Clear rhythm:** The principle of "15 minutes of work / 5 minutes of outdoor rest".
*   **Predictability:** The stable daily routine and the lesson plan reduce anxiety, releasing resources for training.
*   **Right to error:** The atmosphere, where errors are part of the training, reduces fear and impulsiveness.

#### What makes it difficult

*   **Multitasking:** Simultaneous requirements (listen, write, remember) lead to overload.
*   **Long oral instructions:** The schoolboy can only remember the beginning or end of the message.
*   **Noise and distracting factors:** Any external stimuli instantly switch attention.
*   **Rush:** The pressure of time enhances impulsiveness and increases the number of errors.
*   **Sharp change in activity:** It takes time to complete the current task and transition to a new one.

### 3. Practical recommendations

These recommendations will help create an optimal environment for training and development.

#### Home environment

*   **Organization of work:**
    *   **Place:** The working area should be free from extra items. Leave only the necessary for the current task.
    *   **Plan:** Make a short written plan of homework. For example: "1. Mathematics (2 example). 2. Break. 3. Russian (5 lines)." Cross out the executed points.
    *   **Time:** Use the timer. The effective duration of continuous concentration on a complex task is 10-15 minutes.
*   **Support:**
    *   Praise for effort and process, and not just for the result. For example: "I see how you tried to concentrate!"
    *   Help with leading questions: “Where will we start?”, “What is the first step?”, “Let's re -read the instructions together.”
*   **Communication:** Use short and clear instructions. Instead of “Get in the room” say: “Put books on the shelf. Now collect the designer in the box.”

#### School environment

*   **The wording of tasks:** Use simple, short sentences with verbs of action. Write the tasks on the board or card: "1. Read the task. 2. Emphasize the question. 3. Write down the solution."
*   **Lesson structure:** Alternate activities every 15–20 minutes (letter, discussion, work at the board, physical education).
*   **Class placement:** Optimally - the first or second desk in the central row, to minimize distracting factors.
*   **Style style:** The most interactive. Encourage different channels of perception: “Look at the scheme”, “Touch the subject”, “Let's clap the rhythm”.
*   **Individual approach:**
    *   Allow me to use a visual timer or a ruler for concentration on a line when reading.
    *   Provide extra time to check the work.
    *   Offer the possibility of an oral response if the letter is causing difficulties.

### 4. Developing activity

Regular game activity contributes to the development of key skills. It is recommended for 10-15 minutes a day.

#### Attention

*   **"Corrective tests":** Ringing certain letters on the text page.
*   **Board games:** "Dobble", "Find the differences", "Labyrinths".

#### Coordination and self -control

*   **"The sea is worried once":** Exercise to stop the movement at the signal.
*   **"Fist-Robric Ladon":** Kinesiological exercise for coordination and switching.
*   **Games with the ball:** Bowing the ball from the floor or wall in a given rhythm.

#### Memory

*   **"Memory":** A board game for training visual working memory.
*   **"I put in the basket ...":** Game of auditory memory with the sequential addition of objects.
*   **"Repeat me":** Repetition of a sequence of 3-4 movements or cotton.

#### Rhythm

*   Removing or cooling simple rhythms.
*   Learning and recitation of short poems or tongue twisters.
*   Walking to music with a change in pace.
