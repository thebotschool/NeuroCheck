# Personal cognitive profile

This report reveals the cognitive features of your student based on complex testing. Its goal is to provide a deep understanding for the effective development and create optimal conditions for learning.

---

# 1. Key conclusions

### Memory: structure and sequence

Good assimilation of information (facts, images). There is a tendency to confusion in the sequence of events or steps. It remembers "what", more difficult with "as in order."

### Control: Focus and reaction

There is a tendency to quick, hasty solutions. The reaction may be ahead of thinking, leading to errors in the presence of true knowledge.

### Attention: the depth of immersion

High concentration ability. Sustainable, prolonged attention to one task without abstracts.

# 2. Success Guide

### Individual characteristics

*   **Pace:** Dual character. High attention allows you to work long, but impulsiveness provokes hasty answers, especially under pressure or with apparent simplicity.
*   **Perception:** The priority of the structure. Better assimilates data in the form of schemes, plans, step -by -step lists. Continuous text requires additional processing.
*   **Focus:** Depth and accuracy. The stronger side is a deep immersion in the task. It is important to build a plan in advance in order not to miss the overall picture.

### Supporting factors

*   **Visualization:** Check lists, mental cards, schemes. Everything that structures the information visually.
*   **Pause before action:** Practice "Consider up to three" or a brief pronouncing a plan for self -control.
*   **Step -by -step instructions:** Detailing tasks increases effectiveness. Example: instead of "Write an essay" - "1. Make a plan. 2. Write an introduction."
*   **Structured rhythm:** A clear daily routine and predictability reduce the cognitive load.

### Barriers for progress

*   **Multitasking:** Switching between diverse tasks destroys the focus and provokes errors.
*   **Fast change of tasks:** Oral tasks without time to fix the plan, for example, express surveys.
*   **Faring instructions:** Oral, general formulations, such as "analyze the text."
*   **Time deficiency:** The need for an immediate response enhances impulsiveness, leading to inaccuracies.

# 3. Practical recommendations

### Home environment

#### Organization of the educational process

*   **Preliminary planning:** Create a short plan before starting work (on paper, sticker).
*   **Focus on one task:** Remove distracting items from the table.
*   **Using a draft:** A tool for control of impulsiveness. First fast notes, then structuring.

#### Support system

*   **Code word:** Agree on the signal ("Stop", "Steps") for a soft reminder when rushing.
*   **Effective encouragement:** Appreciate accuracy, following the plan, thoughtfulness. Avoid praise for speed.

#### Holding attention

*   Up to 40-50 minutes on one interesting task. Silence and lack of distracting factors are important.

### School environment

#### Making tasks

*   **Written format:** On the board or individual card.
*   **Detailing:** Miss large tasks into numbered points.
*   **Specific verbs:** "Emphasize", "Write", "Compare", "Make a diagram."

#### Structuring information

*   **Visual methods:** Use schemes, tables, timline for consistent "packaging" of information.
*   **Brief result:** Summarize the lesson in the form of a plan ("Today we learned: 1 ... 2 ...").

#### Optimal placement

*   **Place:** Far from distracting factors (window, door) for maximum concentration.

#### The style of interaction

*   **Thinking time:** Do not rush. Allow the draft for planning in control work, this will reduce errors.

# 4. Developing practices

These practices contribute to the development of key skills and strengthening strengths.

### Development of attention (strong side)

*   **Games:** "Find the differences", complex puzzles, scanwords, assembly of models according to the instructions.

### Memory and sequence (growth zone)

*   **The game "History in the chain":** Repetition and addition of phrases in turn.
*   **Creation of comics:** Visualization of key points in the correct sequence after reading/viewing.
*   **Recipes retelling:** Reproduction of a step -free instruction.

### Control and reduction of impulsiveness (growth zone)

*   **The game "Sea is worried once":** Holding a complex pose.
*   **Balance exercises:** Walking along the line, a stand on one leg, a balancing.
*   **Rhythmic exercises:** Repetition of rhythms (pops, knock) with gradual complication. He trains the coordination "He heard-control-production.
