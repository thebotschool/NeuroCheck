# Cognitive assessment report

This report offers a detailed view of the unique cognitive profile of your student, based on the results of complex testing. Our conclusions and recommendations are designed to support its development and disclose full potential.

### 1. A brief report for each test

*   **Test for attention (CPT):** Difficulties with maintaining consistent information and concentration have been identified. The need for special strategies for multi -stage tasks is possible.
*   **Self-control test (Go/No-Go):** Excellent ability to control impulses. Demonstrates mature self -regulation and the ability to act carelessly.
*   **Test for stability of attention (TCP):** A high level of concentration and attentiveness when performing clear tasks requiring an accurate reaction. This is a strong side.

### 2. The guide for the success of your student

This section reveals the individual style of the brain of your student. Understanding these features helps to use its strengths as much as possible.

#### Features of perception

*   **The pace of work:** Effective when performing short, understandable tasks. Needs to slow down and additional time for multi -step tasks requiring consistent memorization.
*   **Information format:** The best perception is achieved with visual and step -by -step feed. Long oral instructions can be less effective.
*   **Focus of attention:** Capable of deep concentration on one object of interest. Difficulties arise if necessary to distribute attention between several sources at the same time.

#### Which helps

*   **Check lists and stages:** Tasks broken into short, clear steps.
*   **Visual materials:** Schemes, memory maps, text highlighting, short videos.
*   **Clear rules:** Clear and predictable instructions contribute to effective self -control.
*   **Structured time:** Short intervals of work (15–20 minutes) with regular breaks (3-5 minutes).
*   **Personal interest:** The tasks related to the hobby activate maximum involvement.

#### What interferes

*   **Multitasking:** Simultaneous listening and recording can lead to errors.
*   **Longing lectures:** Attention is reduced after 5-7 minutes of continuous oral presentation.
*   **A sharp change in topics:** It takes time to complete the thought process and transition to a new task.
*   **Abstract formulations:** Preference is given to specific examples and tasks instead of general instructions.

### 3. Recommendations

Applied strategies to support the development of your student.

#### Home environment

*   **Planning:**
    *   Make joint plans for the evening in the form of a list of affairs. Distribution of performed points enhances the feeling of control.
    *   Start with the most difficult tasks (requiring memory and concentration) while the energy level is high.
    *   Use the timer: 20 minutes of work, 5 minutes of rest (you can bring the interval to 25-30 minutes).
    *   Discuss the instructions for the tasks together, ask the student to retell them in your own words to test understanding.
*   **Support:**
    *   **Encourage the process:** Mark efforts and following the plan, not just the result. Example: "I see how you tried and followed the plan!"
    *   **Use self -control:** Agree on the rules and encouragement, relying on his strong side.

#### School adaptation

*   **Clear instructions:**
    *   Provide instructions 1-2 steps at a time.
    *   Duplicate the key stages in writing (on a board or individual card).
    *   Reformulate general tasks. Instead of "Retell the Paragraph", use "read the paragraph and answer these 3 questions (in writing)."
*   **Lesson structure:**
    *   Sound the lesson plan at the beginning: "Today we will first check the homework, then we will study the new topic and at the end we will perform the exercise."
    *   Accommodation: Plant the student closer to the board and the teacher, away from distracting factors (window, door).
    *   Visual manuals: use presentations with schemes, infographics, teaching videos.
*   **Individual approach:**
    *   Provide patterns for written works (for example, an essay plan with points: entry, argument 1, argument 2, conclusion).
    *   Allow me to use drafts to fix thoughts so as not to hold everything in my head.

### 4. Recommended training

Practical exercises for the development of key skills.

#### Attention and memory

*   **The game "Memory":** He trains visual memory and concentration.
*   **Correct samples:** Give a page from the old book/newspaper. Ask for 2 minutes to cross out all the letters "A". Complication: delete "A", and "O" Circle.
*   **"Repeat me":** Lay out a pattern of 4-5 matches/coins. The student looks 5 seconds, then repeats from memory. Gradually increase the number of elements.

#### Coordination and rhythm

*   **Rhythmic exercises:** Remove the simple rhythm with your palms, the student must repeat it exactly.
*   **Sport:** Table tennis, badminton. Develop a ligament of the "eye-hand", the concentration and speed of the reaction.

#### Self -control and body

*   **Control games:** "The sea is worried once", "freeze." A funny way to train inhibition and control over the body.
*   **Board games:** Cubbles with a cube. They teach to observe the rules, wait for your turn and control emotions, increasing self -confidence.
