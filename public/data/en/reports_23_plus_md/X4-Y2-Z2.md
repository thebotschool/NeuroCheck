# Cognitive development report
### Age: 23+ years
This report reveals the strengths and growth zones of your child, based on the results of cognitive tests. Here you will find practical recommendations for home and school.

---

# 1. Test results
### Analysis of cognitive indicators

*   **Attention and memory (CPT):** Difficulties with keeping attention and consistent memorization of information were noted. Perhaps new strategies or support for fatigue are required.
*   **Self-control (Go/No-Go):** There is a tendency to impulsive answers. This can lead to errors by carelessness.
*   **Sustainability of attention (TCP):** Attention is quickly depleted, especially when performing long or monotonous tasks.

# 2. The guide for the success of your student
### How your child learns

### Individual characteristics
*   **Pace:** Fast, impulsive. It is inclined to start completing the assignment without fully acquaintance with the instructions.
*   **Perception:** Effectively assimilates visual, brief, step -by -step information. He perceives long oral explanations poorly.
*   **Focus of attention:** Unstable. The effective concentration on one task lasts up to 10-15 minutes, after which a pause or a change in activity is required.

### Which helps
*   **Visual supports:** Short memos, numb steps, highlighting keywords.
*   **Clear rhythm:** Work on the timer (for example, 15 minutes work, 5 minutes rest). In the break - short physical activity.
*   **The rule "Stop-think-make":** The practice of a short pause and comprehension before the start of action.
*   **One task at a time:** Short, clear instructions for every step.
*   **Tactile stimulation:** It is permissible to use a small item (anti-stress sock, smooth stone) to "ground" excess activity.

### What interferes
*   **Multitasking:** Simultaneous requests or several instructions.
*   **Long oral instructions:** Perceived partially or incompletely.
*   **Background noise:** Any distracting sounds reduce concentration.
*   **Rush and pressure:** Phrases of the type "Let's faster" increase anxiety and impulsiveness, lead to errors.

# 3. Recommendations
### Support for training

### For the house
*   **Homework:** Separate short blocks for 15 minutes. Between blocks-a mandatory 3-5 minute break with movement.
*   **Workplace:** Organize a quiet, permanent place without distracting factors. All materials should be at hand.
*   **Support and encouragement:** Praise for the process and efforts, and not just for the result: "You tried to concentrate perfectly!", "It's great that you checked yourself."
*   **Predictability:** Create a clear daily routine for study, rest and games. This reduces anxiety and helps concentration.

### For school
*   **The wording of tasks:** Tasks should be short, unambiguous, toned. One task is one sentence.
*   **Lesson structure:** Give the instructions step by step. Make sure one step before giving the next one.
*   **Class placement:** Optimally-the first-second desk in the center, away from the window and door.
*   **Style style:** Actively use visual materials: schemes, posters, cards. Allow me to use the ruler when reading.
*   **Individual approach:** Provide extra time on control for self -testing. Allow me to use a draft for initial ideas.

# 4. Recommended training
### Developing games to support skills

*   **For attention:**
    *   **Graphic dictations:** They train auditory attention and follow the instructions.
    *   **Correct samples:** Search and dispensing certain letters in the text for a while.
*   **For coordination and bodily control:**
    *   **Games with the ball:** Develop visual-motor coordination and concentration.
    *   **The game "Frozhi-Otomory":** Teaches a quick reaction to the signal and control of movements.
*   **For memory:**
    *   **The game "What is gone?":** Removing and reproducing a number of objects.
    *   **Removing the rows:** Repetition of 3-5 words or numbers in a given order.
*   **For rhythm and self -regulation:**
    *   **Cooling rhythms:** Repetition of a given rhythm with palms. Develops a sense of pace and helps to slow down.
