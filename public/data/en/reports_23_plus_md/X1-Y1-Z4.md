## 2. Success Guide

### Individual characteristics

**Pace:**
Your natural pace is high, productive. The overload slows it, requiring additional efforts.

**Perception format:**
The effectiveness of assimilation increases when working with structured, visual information (diagrams, tables, intelligence cards). This activates your strategic memory.

**Focus of attention:**
Natural ability to deep concentration. In the current state of fatigue, attention can be reduced and quickly depleted.

### Which helps

**Visual supports:**
Check lists, schemes, project plans. Visualization of information.

**Steck:**
Decomposition of tasks. Break the target into specific, consistent steps.

**The mode "One task at a time":**
Focus on one task. Use short breaks (for example, Pomodoro method).

**Predictability and silence:**
A quiet, organized atmosphere. A minimum of distracting factors.

### What interferes

**Multitasking:**
The simultaneous implementation of several tasks reduces effectiveness and promotes depletion.

**Work in the Avarala mode:**
Compressed terms, night work, excess pressure negatively affect your condition.

**Chaos and noise:**
Unstructured information, noise, instability.

**Faring instructions:**
The lack of clear goals and criteria in tasks causes frustration.

## 3. Practical recommendations

### Organization of study and life

**How to do homework:**
Plan a school time. Start with complicated. A break for 5-10 minutes after 30–40 minutes of work.

**How to support:**
Support is the creation of conditions for recovery, not a desire for records. Priority: sleep (at least 8 hours), balanced nutrition, moderate physical activity.

**How much attention can be kept:**
Effective concentration lasts **25-30 minutes**. Then a short break is required without gadgets (for example, stretch, drink water).

**How to encourage:**
Encourage the process and efforts, and not just the result.

### For teachers and educational part

**How to formulate tasks:**
Formulate the tasks clearly. Indicate the criteria for success and examples. One big task with understandable stages is preferred.

**How to structure material:**
Structure the material: subtitles, lists, schemes, tables. Visual feed supports strategic thinking.

**Where to place/feed style:**
The optimal work in a quiet environment is optimal. Group work in noise is ineffective. Style style: calm, logical, without pressure.

**Elements of an individual educational route (IOM):**
Consider a temporary reduction in the training load or flexible deadlines. This is a condition for the restoration of cognitive resources and future productivity.

## 4. Restorative practices

These practices are aimed at restoring the nervous system and removing stress, and not at enhanced training of cognitive functions.

**Attention:**
Meditative practices (Calm, Headspace) or 5 minutes of concentration on breathing. Refusal of high -speed tests.

**Coordination and bodily control:**
Balance exercises (for example, a stand on one leg), unhurried walks. They help to "ground" and switch from thoughts to sensations of the body.

**Memory:**
Railing games instead of memorizing. For example, "memorials", simple puzzles, search for differences.

**Rhythm:**
Calm instrumental music. Rhythm is taped to synchronize the work of the hemispheres and calm down.
