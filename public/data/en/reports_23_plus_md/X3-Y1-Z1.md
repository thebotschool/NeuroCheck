### 2. The guide for the success of your student

Understanding an individual style of learning and thinking will help to maximize the potential of your student.

### Individual characteristics

*   **Pace:** Thoughtful, leisurely. Priority - analysis and accuracy, not speed.
*   **Perception:** Best assimilates information through **integral schemes, structures, visual images**. Long step -by -step instructions without logical connection are more difficult.
*   **Focus:** Sustainable, deep attention. Capable of prolonged concentration (up to 30-40 minutes) with a clear understanding of the purpose and structure of the task.

### Which contributes to training

*   **Visualization:** Convert sequences into diagrams, checklists, infographics.
*   **Algorithms:** Clear, numbered steps available for review.
*   **Goal -setting:** The importance of understanding the common goal of the task before starting.
*   **Structure and rhythm:** A clear daily routine and structured environment help self -organization.

### Which prevents training

*   **Long oral instructions:** More than three actions by ear can be perceived incorrectly.
*   **Multitasking without a plan:** Causes a stupor, reduces productivity.
*   **Abstract requirements:** Ineffective. The specifics are needed: “Check according to the list”, “Use the diagram”.
*   **Rush:** Temporary pressure negatively affects thoughtfulness and accuracy.

### 3. Applied recommendations

### For the house

#### Organization of homework

*   **Visual plan:** Make an evening plan together on a board or in a notebook. Example: 1. Mathematics (3 tasks), 2. Rest (15 min), 3. History (paragraph + 3 dates).
*   **Check lists:** Use for complex tasks. Example for mathematics: 1. Read the condition. 2. Write down "Dano." 3. Choose a formula. 4. Follow the number. 5. Check the answer.
*   **Breaks:** Attention is effective up to 30-40 minutes. Plan short breaks (5-10 minutes) after that.

#### Support and promotion

*   **Focus for accuracy:** Praise for accuracy and following the plan. Example: "You checked each item, excellent!"
*   **Correction:** In case of errors or forgetfulness, calmly suggest: "Let's get back to the plan and see what is missed."

### For school

#### The formulation of tasks

*   **General context:** Start with a common goal. Example: “Today we study equations with two unknown. Three steps to the solution - on the board. "
*   **Visual duplication:** Duplicate oral instructions on the board, card, slide.

#### Structuring the material

*   **Story:** Use time tapes.
*   **Language:** Apply structural schemes or tables for rules.
*   **Biology:** Present the processes in the form of infographics with arrows.

#### Individual approach

*   **Helping cards:** Allow me to use algorithms or answers on the desk.
*   **Control work:** Offer tasks with partially registered steps, where you only need to insert the answer.
*   **Using strengths:** Tasks for searching for errors or systematization of information (drawing up tables/diagrams) will be especially effective.

### 4. Recommended practices

These exercises contribute to the development of consistent thinking in a playful way.

### For attention and memory

*   **"History by pictures":** Cut the comic book into shots, ask to restore order and tell the story.
*   **Graphic dictations:** Exercises such as “two cells to the right, one up” develop auditory perception and sequence of actions.

### For coordination and rhythm

*   **Rhythmic repetitions:** Take the simple rhythms. The child repeats. Gradually complicate.
*   **Dance games:** Games like Just Dance train memorization and reproduction of movements.

### For bodily control and planning

*   **"Obstacle Street":** Create a scheme of actions (for example, climb under the table, go around a chair). The child performs, checking with the plan.
*   **Assembly of models:** Designers (LEGO and analogues) perfectly train following step -by -step instructions to achieve the result.
