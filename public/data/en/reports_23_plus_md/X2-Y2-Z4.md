## Cognitive assessment

This report offers a deep understanding of the strengths of your student and areas for development. Recommendations are based on the results of cognitive tests designed to create an optimal environment for training and growth.

---

### 1. Review of tests

*   **Test for attention (CPT):** The child has difficulties with distinguishing similar details and filtering excess visual information. This does not indicate knowledge gaps, but of selectivity.
*   **Braking Test (Go/No-Go):** There is a tendency to impulsive reactions, when the action is ahead of thinking. This can lead to hasty errors.
*   **Test of visual working memory:** When performing the same or complex tasks, the student’s attention is quickly tired. This reduces concentration and productivity.

<!-- page-break -->

### 2. The guide for the success of your student

### Peculiarities

*   **Pace:** Fast, gusty. The tendency to speed to the detriment of quality. Needs to help develop measured rhythm.
*   **Perception:** It better assimilates clear, structured and visually simple information. Excess details creates "visual noise".
*   **Focus of attention:** Sustainable for short periods (15–20 minutes). Frequent, short breaks are required.

### Which helps

*   **Step -by -step instructions:** Miss large tasks into 2-3 sequential steps. For example: "1. Read. 2. Emphasize. 3. Write down."
*   **Visual supports:** Check lists, simple schemes, line-ups, highlighting keywords, visual timers.
*   **Rhythm and pauses:** The optimal mode: 15 minutes of work, 5 minutes of rest (movement, water). This is more effective than prolonged concentration.
*   **Speaking:** The verbalization of actions out loud slows down the pace and reduces the number of impulsive errors.

### What interferes

*   **Multitasking:** The simultaneous implementation of several cases leads to a decrease in efficiency.
*   **Rush and pressure:** The phrases that adjust the schoolchildren enhance the alarm and provoke errors.
*   **Distracting factors:** An uncleaned table, noise, overloaded educational material disperses attention.
*   **Long instructions:** Only the beginning and end are remembered. The instructions should be brief and capacious.

<!-- page-break -->

### 3. Practical recommendations

### At home

*   **Working space:** Remove all distracting items from the table before the start of classes.
*   **Planning:** Use the principle of "first-point". Divide voluminous tasks into parts, taking breaks between objects.
*   **Promotion:** Mark efforts and process, and not just the result. Example: "Great that you checked the work!" With an error, help find a solution without criticizing.
*   **Realism:** Expect a concentration of no more than 15–20 minutes of continuous work.

### At school

*   **Instructions:** Formulate briefly, clearly, unequivocal. Similar oral instructions by written.
*   **Lesson:** Imagine complex material at the beginning of the lesson. Turn on short breaks or physical education every 15-20 minutes.
*   **Accommodation:** The first or second desk to minimize abstracts is recommended. Use non -verbal signals (for example, "stop") to focus attention before the task.
*   **Support:**
    *   Provide tasks on clean, unloaded sheets.
    *   Allow the use of a ruler or clean sheet for focusing on a line.
    *   Select additional time for testing for self -testing.

### 4. Training

These games and exercises effectively develop key skills.

### Attention and self -control

*   **"Find 10 differences":** Develops concentration on details.
*   **Correct samples:** Discarding certain letters in the text, gradually complicating the task.
*   **Graphic dictations:** Drawing on cells under dictation. Develops auditory attention and following instructions.

### Braking and bodily control

*   **"The sea is worried once":** He trains the ability to freeze by signal.
*   **"You go quieter - you will continue":** Develops a slowdown and control of movements.
*   **"Edible-universal":** It requires a quick reaction and restrained skill.

### Memory and rhythm

*   **"Memory" / "Find a couple":** Improves visual memory.
*   **Rhythmic games:** Repetition of the clawed rhythm. Sets up the internal pace and coordination.
*   **"What has changed?":** Removing the location of objects and searching for changes.
