### Assessment of cognitive functions

This report is intended for parents and teachers. Its goal is to identify the strengths of the student and development zones. The proposed recommendations are based on test results and are aimed at creating an optimal environment for training.

---

### 1. Key results

*   **Test for attention (CPT):** High concentration. The ability to confuse similar details when overloading information or fatigue.
*   **Self-control test (Go/No-Go):** Outstanding ability to inhibition. The ability to make balanced decisions under clear conditions.
*   **Decision -making test (TCP):** Teenage to quick conclusions. Decisiveness, requiring attention to details to prevent inaccuracies.

### 2. Features of training

Unique profile: a combination of high self -control and the desire for quick results. This is not a contradiction, but a feature of thinking. The ability to self -regulation is present, but priority can be given the speed of the task.

#### Profile: Key aspects

*   **Pace:** Duality. Fast and decisive tasks. It is important to consider that speed can affect the accuracy of parts.
*   **Perception:** Optimal for clear, structured information. Schemes, lists and highlighted keywords are more effective than continuous text.
*   **Focus:** Developed internal control. In complex tasks, “tunnel vision” is possible, leading to ignoring part of the conditions.

#### Support: What is strengthening

*   **Visual accents:** The allocation of keywords in the tasks ("not", "besides").
*   **Check lists:** Separation of tasks into understandable stages.
*   **Double check principle:** The habit of re -read conditions and work done.
*   **The rhythm of work:** The alternation of intensive concentration (15–20 minutes) with short pauses (1-2 minutes).

#### Calls: What limits

*   **Multitasking:** The simultaneous performance of several actions reduces accuracy.
*   **Visual noise:** Overloaded pages or materials distract attention.
*   **Time pressure:** It stimulates quick, but potentially erroneous solutions.
*   **Oral instructions:** Long instructions without visual support are less effective.

<!-- page-break -->

### 3. Practical recommendations

#### Home environment

*   **Organization of tasks**
    *   Break the tasks into blocks (15–20 minutes) with short breaks.
    *   Before starting work: ask to say a plan from 2-3 steps. This increases awareness.
    *   Workplace: clean, without distracting factors.
*   **Support and motivation**
    *   Encourage attentiveness and accuracy, and not just the speed of execution. Mark specific details: “You checked every word”, “It is great that you noticed this detail.”
*   **Key aspect**
    *   Effective holding of attention: 15–20 minutes. Long -term concentration without breaks is ineffective.

#### School environment

*   **Formulation of tasks**
    *   Clear formulations, by points. Highlight important details (**Not**, **except**).
    *   Offer tasks with an explanation of the course of thoughts. This contributes to the slowdown and analysis of the process.
*   **The structure of information**
    *   Use lists, schemes, tables. Avoid continuous text.
    *   Place at the first or second desk to minimize distracting factors.
*   **Method of submission**
    *   Combine oral instructions with the record of key points on the board.
*   **Individual approach (IOM)**
    *   Check lists are recommended for self-testing: “Did you check the conditions?”, “Are all the calculations are correct?”
    *   On control: additional time for verification, and not just for solution.

### 4. Recommended training

The proposed exercises contribute to the development of key skills in interactive form.

#### Development of attention and accuracy

*   **"Find the differences":** Classic for visual scanning.
*   **Correct samples:** Refusing or bypassing characters in the text.
*   **Puzzles:** Assembly of elements with small details and similar colors.

#### Mastering strategic thinking

*   **Board games:** Chess, checkers, go, "set". Games requiring thinking.
*   **Labyrinths:** Passing complex labyrinths with a pencil, observing accuracy.

#### Improving coordination and rhythm

*   **Balance exercises:** Standing on one leg (with open/closed eyes).
*   **Rhythmic games:** Reproduction of rhythms (cotton, on the table).
