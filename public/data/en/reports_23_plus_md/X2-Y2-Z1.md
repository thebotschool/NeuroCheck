### Report on the development of cognitive skills

This report was created to understand the strengths and zones of the development of the student. Recommendations are based on testing data and are aimed at supporting its training.

---

### 1. Test results

*   **Attention and memory (CPT):** Understanding the general idea is high. However, there is a tendency to inattention to details, especially with similar visual elements (for example, "B" and "D"). This indicates visual inaccuracy, and not about memory problems.
*   **Braking and control (Go/No-Go):** The tendency to quick, impulsive response is expressed. The schoolboy can give an answer or start a task until a complete understanding of the rules or question.
*   **General performance (TCP):** High potential for self -regulation and concentration. With clear rules and the absence of distracting factors, the student is able to perform complex tasks accurately and long.

**Conclusion:** The student demonstrates the profile "quickly, but not always for sure." The brain works effectively at high speeds, but haste sometimes leads to a passage of important details. The goal is to teach a short pause for self -testing, without slowing down the overall pace.

### 2. Understanding the student

#### Individual characteristics

*   **Pace:** Naturally tall, gusty. First action, then comprehension. This is not laziness, but a desire for a quick result.
*   **Perception:** Optimally perceives clear, structured, visually simple information. Overloaded text or images cause "visual noise" and lead to errors.
*   **Focus:** Strong, but "tunnel". The schoolboy is deeply immersed in one task. It is difficult to switch or hold several conditions at the same time.

#### Which helps

*   **Step -by -step instructions:** Miss tasks into simple, consistent actions.
*   **Visual anchors:** Use color, emphasizing, framework for highlighting key information.
*   **Rhythm and rituals:** Short warm-ups, tapping rhythm, the rule "Stop-think-make."
*   **Singlety:** One textbook, one task on the table.
*   **Timer:** Work in short intervals (15–20 minutes) with mandatory breaks.

#### What interferes

*   **Multitasking:** The simultaneous implementation of several cases almost always leads to errors.
*   **Rush:** Phrases like "Come on faster!" enhance impulsiveness and reduce accuracy.
*   **Long oral instructions:** More than two steps on ear may not be retained. Visual support is necessary.
*   **Visual noise:** Training materials with many distracting elements, overloaded presentations.

### 3. Recommendations

#### For the house

*   **Organization of work:**
    *   **The rule of one subject:** On the table only what is necessary for the current task.
    *   **Check lists:** Make a simple plan for day/evening.
    *   **"Work on mistakes":** Turn errors in the game. Praise for a self -test, and not just for the lack of errors.
*   **Support:**
    *   **Encourage the process:** "I see your efforts!" Or "it's good that you checked yourself."
    *   **Time restriction:** Effective concentration lasts 15–20 minutes. Then a short physical break is needed.

#### For school

*   **The wording of tasks:**
    *   Instructions: short, clear, one step.
    *   Key words: highlight the marker on a board or cards.
    *   Verbs of action: “Emphasize”, “Cut”, “Count”.
*   **Lesson structure:**
    *   **Location:** The first-second desk, closer to the center, minimizing distracting factors.
    *   **Visual cleanliness:** The minimum of the text on slides, the maximum structure (lists, diagrams, large font).
    *   **Pause:** After the question, do 3-5 seconds pauses before receiving an answer. This forms the skill of braking.
*   **Individual route:**
    *   Cards: step -by -step algorithms for performing tasks.
    *   Reading: permission to use a line to hold the line.
    *   Control work: official allocation of additional time for self -test.

### 4. Recommended training

These games and exercises develop growth zones. Perform them regularly, 5-10 minutes a day, in a playful way.

*   **For attention and accuracy:**
    *   **Graphic dictations:** "Two cells to the right, one up ..." trains the ligament "I hear-vision-I will."
    *   **Correct samples:** Refusing certain letters in the text. Gradual complication.
    *   **"Find 10 differences":** Develops visual attentiveness.
*   **To control impulsiveness:**
    *   **"The sea is worried once":** The game that requires freezing at the signal trains the skill of braking.
    *   **"Edible-universal":** Develops a rapid reaction and its cancellation.
    *   **Walking along the line:** Passing along the rope on the floor, first straight, then backwards.
*   **For visual memory:**
    *   **"Memory" ("Find a couple"):** Classical game for memorizing steam.
    *   **"What has changed?":** Memorization of objects, then detecting changes.
*   **For the development of rhythm:**
    *   **RIGHTS RIGHTS:** Repetition of a given rhythm with a hand.
    *   **Games with the ball:** Bowing the ball in a given rhythm.
