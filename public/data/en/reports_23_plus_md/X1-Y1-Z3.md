### **1. Potential assessment**

*   **Visual memory:** The exceptional ability to memorize and strategic retention of visual information. High efficiency of thinking allows you to recall data even when distracting.
*   **Self -regulation:** Great control over reactions. The ability to act quickly and stops in a timely manner demonstrates strong self -regulation even in difficult situations.
*   **Impulsiveness:** With expressed strengths, there is a tendency to hasty decisions. Actions can be ahead of the analysis, which leads to errors due to insufficient reflection.

**Key conclusion:** The student has a powerful cognitive apparatus (memory, strategy) and developed self -control. However, there is a tendency to too fast start. The main goal is to develop a short pause skill: "Stop - think - do it."

### **2. The guide for the success of your student**

#### **Features of perception**

*   **Pace:** A combination of thoughtfulness and natural tendency to speed. Preference of quick performance of quality.
*   **Perception format:** The optimal assimilation of information through visual channels: schemes, tables, intelligence cards, highlighted keywords, short videos. Oral perception is less effective, especially for long instructions.
*   **Focus of attention:** The ability to deep concentration on interesting tasks. The focus may be violated by an impulsive desire to give an answer or quickly complete. Requires an “anchor” for a specific, understandable goal.

#### **Effective approaches**

*   **Visual supports:** Clear plans, schemes, checklists.
*   **Step -by -step instructions:** Separation of large tasks into 3-4 consecutive stages.
*   **The principle "first listen":** Learning the algorithm for complete perception of information before action. Perhaps the introduction of a conditional signal.
*   **Rhythm and structure:** The predictable schedule, a clear start and completion of classes, rhythmic exercises.

#### **Obstacles in learning**

*   **Multitasking:** The simultaneous performance of several actions leads to errors.
*   **Long oral instructions:** The instructions of more than two steps, given only by the voice, often lead to incomplete execution.
*   **Time pressure:** Phrases that stimulate speed ("who is faster?", "A only minute") provoke impulsiveness.
*   **Unstructured environment:** Noise, chaos, a sharp change in activity without warning.

### **3. Actions for growth**

#### **Home environment**

*   **Performing tasks:**
    *   Form a simple plan: reading, draft, check, cleanliness.
    *   Use the timer: 20-25 minutes of work, 5 minutes of break. This reduces haste.
    *   Introduce the "Feature rule": the student independently finds places for re -checking before delivery.
*   **Support:**
    *   Mark attentiveness and following the plan, not speed. "Great, what I checked on the list!"
    *   In case of mistakes due to haste, analyze calmly: "What did not notice? Which part of the task was missed?"

#### **School environment**

*   **The wording of tasks:**
    *   Provide key instructions in writing (board, card). Select verbs-action: read, emphasize, write it out.
    *   Place important information (page/task number) in the upper left corner of the board as "anchor".
*   **Lesson structure:**
    *   Sound and write down a short plan of the lesson: "Today we: 1. We repeat ... 2. We will find out ... 3. We will train ...".
    *   Use the strategic thinking of the student: give him roles that require planning (for example, the "captain" of the group).
*   **Personalized approach:**
    *   Allow the use of checklists for self-testing on control.
    *   Provide an additional minute at the end of the test exclusively for checking.
    *   The optimal place for landing: the first or second desk in the center, to minimize abstracts and visual contact.

### **4. Development of skills**

These games and exercises balance the reaction speed with thoughtfulness.

*   **Focus and control:**
    *   **Graphic dictations:** Develop attentiveness to instructions.
    *   **Strategic board games:** Chess, checkers, “Go” are taught to calculate the consequences.
    *   **The game "Delay!":** He trains the ability to stop by signal in time.
*   **Coordination and rhythm:**
    *   **Juggling:** Improves the coordination of the "eye-handed" and concentration.
    *   **Game of musical instruments:** Develops accuracy and rhythm without haste.
    *   **Balanceage exercises:** Increase self -awareness and control of impulses.
*   **Memory development:**
    *   **The game "Memory":** He trains visual memory. Complication: call a couple before the opening.
    *   **"Repeat the pattern/sequence":** Reproduction of the overall sequences of objects. Gradual complication.
