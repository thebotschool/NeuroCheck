## Neurocheck: Your cognitive profile (23+ years)

This report offers a detailed overview of cognitive abilities. We provide personalized recommendations designed to support training and development, helping to reveal the potential as much as possible.

---

### **1. Analysis of tests**

*   **Visual working memory:** Effective memorization and structuring of information, even in the presence of distracting factors. Developed strategic thinking is distinguished.
*   **Go/No-Go Test (reaction and braking):** Minor asymmetry in the speed of the reaction between the hands. In new or stressful conditions, it may take a little more time to process information and making decisions.
*   **Attention for stability (CPT):** Difficulty with a long concentration on one task. The resource of attention is quickly depleted with monotonous work.

### **2. The guide for the success of your student**

#### **Key features**

*   **Pace:** You are a sprinter, not a marathon. High productivity at short distances. On long, monotonous tasks, the pace is reduced due to fatigue.
*   **Perception:** You better learn structured, visually organized information. Schemes, lists, tables and intelligence cards are most effective.
*   **Focus of attention:** Your attention is "bright but short." Easily and deeply immerse yourself in interesting tasks, but quickly lose interest in routine or prolonged work.

#### **Efficiency support**

*   **Visual supports:** Use checklists, highlight the key color, create schemes.
*   **Step -by -step instructions:** Divide big tasks into small, understandable stages. Each completed step gives motivation.
*   **Pomodoro method:** 25 minutes of intensive work, then a 5-minute break. Supports concentration and prevents exhaustion.
*   **A clear rhythm:** A stable sleep and wakefulness regime directly affects concentration.

#### **Factors that reduce efficiency**

*   **Multitasking:** The simultaneous fulfillment of several cases exhausts the resource of attention and reduces the quality.
*   **Long monotonous tasks:** Attention begins to decline after 25-30 minutes of continuous work of the same type.
*   **Information noise:** Open tabs, background music or TV strongly interfere with concentration.
*   **Rush and pressure:** Under such conditions, the decision -making speed may decrease, and the risk of errors can grow.

<!-- page-break -->

### **3. Recommendations**

#### **Home environment**

*   **Performing tasks:** Work short sessions for 25-30 minutes. Take breaks for 5-10 minutes (get up, walk, distract). Start with the most difficult tasks while the energy level is high.
*   **Support:** Instead of "did you do lessons?" Ask: "What is your plan for today? How to break the task into parts?" Encourage not only the result, but also the ability to make pauses, correctly distributing strength.
*   **Mercing of attention:** On a difficult task - up to 30 minutes. After that, a short reboot or change of activity is required.

#### **Training in an educational institution**

*   **The wording of tasks:** Ideally-in the form of a clear checklist or algorithm. Get large projects with breakdown into stages and intermediate terms.
*   **Structuring information:** Alternate the theory with practice or discussion every 20–25 minutes. A lecture plan or key theses in a prominent place will help keep the focus.
*   **Style style:** Visual. Presentations with schemes, infographics, short videos are perceived better than long texts or monotonous lectures.
*   **Individual route:** The provision of lecture materials in advance and the ability to take large projects in parts can significantly increase performance.

<!-- page-break -->

### **4. Recommended training**

These simple exercises will strengthen the weaknesses and strengthen the existing abilities.

*   **For attention:**
    *   **The game "Corrective test":** Print the text. Timer for 2 minutes. Cross out all the letters "o". Complicate: look and cross out two letters (for example, “a” and “B”) or certain syllables (“but”).
*   **For coordination and interhemispheric ties:**
    *   **Juggling:** Start with two balls, throwing them from hand to hand. This synchronizes the work of the cerebral hemispheres.
    *   **Exercise "Fist-Robric Ladon":** Change the three positions of the brush on the table sequentially. Perform both hands at the same time, from a slow pace to a quick one.
*   **For memory and strategic thinking:**
    *   **Board strategic games:** Chess, Go, Reversi. They train planning a few moves in advance and holding a complex picture in the mind.
*   **For rhythm and bodily control:**
    *   **Dancing or fitness:** Any activity with repetition of movements behind the coach (zumba, aerobics, dances) perfectly develops coordination, attention and relieves mental stress.
