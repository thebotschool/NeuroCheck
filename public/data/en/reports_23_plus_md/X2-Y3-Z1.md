### 2. The guide for the success of your student

#### Features of training

*   **Pace:** The best, measured rhythm of work is optimal. Hurry often leads to errors due to visual confusion.
*   **Perception:** The step -by -step and auditory format of information is preferred. Excess visual elements (diagrams, images, text) on one page can disorient.
*   **Focus:** High concentration on one task. Attention is most stable in the absence of visual interference and with a clear understanding of the goal.

#### Supporting environment

*   **Step -by -step instructions:** Miss complex tasks into simple, consistent stages. For example: "1. Read. 2. Write out. 3. Resolve."
*   **Visual accents:** Use textbooks or color stickers for key information, facilitating navigation.
*   **Rhythm:** Rhythmic actions, such as tapping or pronouncing aloud, contribute to synchronization of the brain.
*   **Clear algorithms:** A clear sequence of actions activates strong self -control.

#### Factors that make the process difficult

*   **Visual noise:** Overloaded pages of textbooks, small font or close tasks create discomfort.
*   **Multitasking:** Simultaneous listening and recording reduce effectiveness and cause stress.
*   **Fast change of tasks:** It takes time for the mental completion of one task and a smooth transition to the next.
*   **Time pressure:** Installation on the speed of work often leads to errors due to inattention.

### 3. Recommendations

#### For the home environment

*   **Organization of work:**
    *   **The principle of "one task":** On the desktop there is only material in the current item.
    *   **Tomato method:** Use a focusing timer (20–25 minutes) with a subsequent short break (5 minutes) for physical activity.
    *   **Skipping tasks:** Divide major tasks into controlled stages.
*   **Support and encouragement:**
    *   Mark **accuracy and effort**, not speed. For example: "I appreciate your accuracy."
    *   Support his strong side - self -control. For example: "Your ability to concentrate is impressive."
*   **Mercing of attention:** Effective concentration on a complex task lasts 15–25 minutes. After that, a short rest is needed.

#### For the school environment

*   **The wording of tasks:** The instructions should be brief, clear and consistent, preferably orally with duplication on the board.
*   **Structuring the material:**
    *   Use numb lists and checklists.
    *   Leave enough free space between tasks on the work sheets.
    *   Select key information, avoiding excess accents.
*   **Class placement:** The optimal place is in the first or second desks in the central row to minimize distracting factors.
*   **Style style:** Use a combination of an oral explanation with a minimalist visual support (1-2 key examples on the board).
*   **Individual approach:**
    *   Give additional time to perform tests.
    *   Allow the use of the line to keep the focus when reading.

### 4. Recommended training

Regular short classes (5-10 minutes a day) contribute to the development of the necessary skills.

#### For attention and visual perception

*   **Correct samples:** Crossing certain letters in the text.
*   **"Find the differences":** Exercise for the development of skills in comparison and filtering of visual information.
*   **Labyrinths:** The passage of the labyrinths (first with a finger, then with the eyes) improves concentration.

#### For coordination and interhemispheric interaction

*   **"Fist-Robric Ladon":** The rhythmic execution of the sequence of movements with each hand, then both at the same time.
*   **Juggling:** Work with two light objects for synchronizing the movements of the hands and hemispheres.
*   **Tool game:** The piano or synthesizer is an effective simulator for two -handed coordination.

#### For memory and rhythm

*   **"Memory":** Search for paired cards.
*   **Tapping rhythms:** Repetition and creation of rhythmic sequences with palms or pencils.
