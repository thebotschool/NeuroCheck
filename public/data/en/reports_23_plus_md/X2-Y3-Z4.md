# Cognitive profile: attention, control, memory
Age: 23+ years

This report reveals the cognitive profile of the student, offering the path to effective training and development. The test results are not a diagnosis, but a practical guide.

---

# 1. A brief test report

*   **Test for attention and working memory (CPT):** With an abundance of information, there are difficulties with filtering visual noise. It is more difficult for the child to focus on key details and hold them in working memory.
*   **Braking and control test (Go/No-Go):** It takes more time to coordinate thought processes and actions, especially in new or stressful conditions. This can slow down the reaction and decision making.
*   **Cognitive load level (TCP):** The state of significant mental fatigue is recorded. Attention is scattered, self-control is temporarily reduced due to the depletion of cognitive resources.

# 2. Guide for the student

## Individual characteristics

*   **Pace:** Prefers a leisurely pace. A rush or the need for quick solution can cause errors and confusion.
*   **Perception format:** Effectively perceives clear, structured information. Simultaneous processing of several stimuli is difficult.
*   **Focus of attention:** The focus of attention is unstable and quickly exhausted. Long -term holding of attention on one task requires regular breaks.

## Which helps

*   **Visual supports:** Check lists, lists, color coding, recovery stickers.
*   **Step -by -step instructions:** Decomposition of complex tasks, consistent implementation.
*   **Rhythm and movement:** Short physical activity, rhythmic coordination exercises.
*   **Predictability:** A clear schedule organized working space.

## What interferes

*   **Multitasking:** The simultaneous performance of several tasks causes overload.
*   **Fast change of tasks:** The absence of pauses between activities makes it difficult to switch.
*   **Visual noise:** Overloaded pages, disorder in the workplace.
*   **Background noise:** Distulating sounds critically reduce concentration.

# 3. Recommendations

## For the house

### Organization of tasks

*   Break work into intervals (15–20 minutes) with short breaks (5 minutes).
*   Start difficult tasks at the beginning using the maximum energy.
*   Optimize the workspace: only the necessary items on the table.

### Support and motivation

*   Use supportive vocabulary: “Let's analyze the first step”, “I appreciate your efforts.”
*   Mark efforts, and not just the result: “You lasted a great 20 minutes!”, “Well broke a difficult task.”

### Reducing the load

*   Priority is a good rest. Provide a sufficient sleep, time in the fresh air and a free game.

## For school

### The wording of the tasks

*   Give the instructions one at a time, as simple as possible. Example: instead of a comprehensive instruction, divide it into:
    1.  Read the first paragraph.
    2.  Find the main idea.
    3.  Write down.

### Material design

*   Use lists, schemes, tables. Structure large texts with paragraphs and subtitles.
*   Recommend the use of a ruler or leaf for focusing when reading.

### Optimization of space and feed

*   The optimal place: the first or second desk, far from distracting factors (window, door).
*   Use a calm tone. Before the task, establish eye contact. Ask to repeat the instructions.

### Individual educational route (IOM)

*   Additional time for tests.
*   Flexibility in the surrender time during overload.

# 4. Recommended training

Simple exercises and games for the development of cognitive functions. Key - regularity: 5-10 minutes daily.

## Attention and control of errors

*   **"Corrective test":** In the printed text, cross out everything “a” in 1 minute. Complication: delete “a”, circle “o”.
*   **Board games:** “Dobble”, “Find a couple”, games for reaction speed and visual search.

## Coordination and interhemispheric ties

*   **"Lazy eights":** Drawing an infinity sign in the air/on paper: alternately with each hand, then both.
*   **Juggling:** Start with two light objects. Throwing synchronizes the brain.

## Rhythm and bodily control

*   **"Rhythmic echo":** You set a simple rhythm, the child repeats. Gradually complicate.

## Visual memory

*   **"Photographer":** Remember 3-5 items in 10 seconds. Cover them and name them. Gradually increase the amount.
