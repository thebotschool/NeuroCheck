## Report on the cognitive development of a student

This report contains key conclusions based on the results of cognitive testing and practical recommendations for effective development.

# 1. Assessment of tests

*   **Attention and working memory (CPT):** Difficulties with prolonged attention and memorization of information in the sequence. Fatual fatigue or lack of effective strategies is possible.
*   **Self-control (Go/No-Go):** Teenage to impulsive actions. This can lead to errors by carelessness, despite the knowledge of the correct answer.
*   **Visual working memory:** Difficulties with the simultaneous retention of several visual details. This is especially true for their exact location or order.

<!-- page-break -->

# 2. Features of training

## Individual characteristics

*   **Pace.** He seeks to work quickly, which sometimes leads to errors. Natural pace is intermittent: short periods of concentration (10-15 minutes) are replaced by distraction and fatigue.
*   **Perception.** It is best absorbed by short, clear, visual information. Long text or oral instructions overload working memory.
*   **Focus of attention.** Unrestrained, easily captured by interesting incentives, but is also easily lost. It is difficult to filter external stimuli and concentrate on monotonous tasks.

## Which helps

*   **Visual support.** Check lists, schemes, pictures, recovery stickers, visual timers.
*   **Step -by -step actions.** Break big tasks into small, understandable steps.
*   **Rhythm predictability.** A clear, pre -known structure of the day and classes.
*   **Short breaks.** The ability to move or perform a simple exercise for the "restart" of the brain.

## What interferes

*   **Multitasking.** The simultaneous performance of several actions causes overload.
*   **Fast change of tasks.** It takes time for mental switching. Sharp transitions lead to errors.
*   **Sensory overload.** Noise, visual mess, a large number of people quickly deplete the resource of attention.
*   **Long oral instructions.** Monologists or long -term explanations lose information content after several sentences.

# 3. Recommendations

## At home

### Homework
*   **Intervals:** 15–20 minutes of intensive work, then 5-10 minutes of break.
*   **Priority:** Start with complex objects while the resource of attention is maximum.
*   **Timer:** Use a timer to visualize the time of work and rest.

### Support and promotion
*   **Efforts:** Praise for the process and effort, and not just for the result.
*   **Errors:** Avoid criticism for impulsive errors. Offer a joint check.

### Organization of space
*   **Desktop:** During classes, it should be free from distracting objects.

## At school

### Instructions
*   **Wording:** Give short, clear, step -by -step instructions.
*   **Duplication:** Duplicate oral instructions in writing (on the board, on the card).

### The structure of the lesson
*   **Step -by -step approach:** Use a step -by -step approach to tasks, giving them one at a time.

### Place in the classroom
*   **Location:** Optimally-the first-second desk, away from windows and doors.

### Elements of an individual route
*   **Timer:** Allow me to use the visual timer on the desk.
*   **Time:** Provide extra time in the test work to verify and correct errors.
*   **Breaks:** Allow short, "legal" breaks for rebooting.

# 4. Recommended training

These games and exercises develop the necessary skills, easily embedding into everyday life.

## For attention

*   **Corrective tests.** Crossing certain letters in the text.
*   **Find 10 differences.** Classic pictures for training concentration.
*   **Labyrinths.** Passing the labyrinths with a pencil.

## For memory and sequence

*   **The game "Memory".** Training of visual working memory.
*   **The game "I put in a bag ...".** Oral game to memorize chains.
*   **Cooking by recipe.** Step -by -step execution of actions according to the instructions.

## For control and impulsiveness

*   **The game "Delay!".** Instant hardening by signal.
*   **The game is "edible-universal."** A game with a ball requiring a quick reaction and inhibition.

## For rhythm and self -regulation

*   **Rhythmic echo.** Repetition of a given rhythm with claps and topot.
