# Cognitive assessment report (23+ years)

This report reveals the strengths and growth zones of your child according to the results of cognitive diagnostics. It contains practical conclusions and recommendations for creating a supporting and developing environment.

---

# 1. Test results

*   **Attention (TCP):** Mental fatigue is observed. Attention is unstable, the concentration on one task is difficult.
*   **Braking (Go/No-Go):** Fatigue prevents the impulses. Impulsive reactions prevail, especially with fatigue.
*   **Working memory (CPT):** The memorization of individual facts is successful, but their construction in a sequence causes difficulties.

**Key conclusion:** The main difficulty is **Cognitive overload**. This is not a lack of desire to learn, but a sign of exhaustion of the nervous system. Support is required and competent loading.

# 2. The guide for the success of your student

## Features of perception

*   **Pace:** A slow, measured pace is preferred. A rush and frequent change of tasks lead to errors and fatigue. It takes time to assimilate instructions.
*   **Format:** The most effectively perceives the information clearly, structurally and step -by -step. Long oral explanations quickly overload.
*   **Focus of attention:** Preserving attention on one task is limited **10-15 minutes**. After this, a short pause or a change in activity is needed.

## Which contributes to progress

*   Clear, step -by -step instructions. Principle: One task is one step.
*   Visual supports: checklists, diagrams, mental cards, highlighting keywords. Everything that structures information.
*   Predictability and rhythm: a clear daily routine, alternation of study and rest.
*   Separation of large tasks into small, controlled parts. The goal should be short -term and achievable.
*   Tactile stimulation: the use of an expander or anti-stress toy during oral explanations to keep the focus.

## Which prevents training

*   Multivativity: simultaneous requirement to listen, record and think.
*   High pace of the lesson and unpredictable change of topics.
*   Background noise: conversations, music, external distracting factors.
*   Tasks for the time and unprepared oral answers. Create stress that blocks thinking.
*   Unstructured information: continuous text without paragraphs, subtitles and discharge.

# 3. Recommendations

## At home

*   **Organization of work:**
    *   Working sessions: 15-20 minutes of work, 5 minutes of break.
    *   Breaks: activity, water, look at the window. Avoid gadgets.
    *   Use a timer (for example, sand clock) for clarity.
    *   Start with the most difficult tasks while the resources are high.
    *   Make a simple plan before work: "Mathematics (task 1) → Rest → Russian (exercise 5)."

*   **Support and encouragement:**
    *   Avoid reproaches for errors in carelessness. Use the phrases: "You get tired. Let's check together."
    *   Praise not the result, but **process and effort**: “Great, you could concentrate for 15 minutes!”, “Well broken the task into steps.”

## At school

*   **The wording of tasks:**
    *   The instructions should be chat, brief, step -by -step. Example: instead of “Read the paragraph and answer the questions“ Use ”1. Read the first paragraph 2. Find the three main names. 3. Write them down.”
    *   Make sure the assignment understands: ask the child to repeat it.

*   **Lesson structure and space:**
    *   **Place:** The first desk, the center of the class, away from the window and doors to minimize abstracts.
    *   **Visualization:** Use schemes, lists, color discharge on the board. This helps to absorb the structure.
    *   **Individual approach (IOM):**
        *   Allow the cards-recalling with a plan or an algorithm.
        *   Provide materials with already highlighted key thoughts.
        *   Increase time for tests or reduce their volume.

# 4. Recommended training

These exercises will strengthen attention, self -control and memory. Do 10-15 minutes a day, in a positive atmosphere.

*   **Attention and braking:**
    *   **The game "Delay!":** Movement to the music, instant stopping on its interruption. He trains quick braking of actions.
    *   **Graphic dictations:** For example, "one cage to the right, two cells up ...". Develops concentration and following the instructions.

*   **Working memory and sequence:**
    *   **The game "I put in a bag ...":** Repetition and adding objects to the chain. He trains auditory memory and sequence.
    *   **Pictures Stories:** Restoring a sequence of 3-5 plot pictures and compilation of a story.

*   **Body control and rhythm:**
    *   **Neurohymnastics "Kulak-Robe-Ladon":** Alternate change of hand positions. Improves interhemispheric interaction and cognitive control.
    *   **Games with the ball:** Bowing the ball from the floor or wall in a given rhythm. Develops rhythm and endurance.
