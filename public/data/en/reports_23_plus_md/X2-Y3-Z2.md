# Cognitive development report
## Deep analysis for personalized growth (23+ years)

This report provides a comprehensive overview of the cognitive features of your student. You will receive a clear understanding of its strengths and areas for development, as well as specific recommendations for effective support both at home and in the educational environment.

---

## 1. Evaluation: Key aspects

*   **Visual attention: focusing**  
    There is a tendency to mix similar visual details, especially at a high speed of information. This does not indicate vision problems, but the specifics of processing visual data, where the brain can finish or pass small elements.

*   **Motor control: coordination**  
    Asymmetry is observed in the speed and confidence of the reaction between the hands. This indicates the developing interaction between the hemispheres of the brain, which requires further harmonization for tasks, where a quick reaction and self -control are needed.

*   **Working memory: Focus retention**  
    The concentration of attention has a tendency to reduce over long tasks. The ability to hold the focus is quickly exhausted, which leads to distraction, loss of thoughts or forgetting the current action.

## 2. The guide for the success of your student

### Individual characteristics

*   **Pace**  
    A calm, measured pace is preferred. Hurry and excessive pressure can provoke errors and visual confusion. Short, but frequent breaks help to restore attention.

*   **Perception format**  
    Visual information is most effectively absorbed, subject to its simplicity, clearness and lack of overload details. Principle: one idea on a slide, one exercise on a sheet.

*   **Focus of attention**  
    Attention is intense, but not tile and short -lived. The child is easily distracted by external stimums (noise, movement) and can hardly return to an interrupted task.

### Which helps

*   **Step -by -step instructions**  
    Divide large tasks into 2-3 small, clear steps. Visual checklists (for example: 1. Read. 2. Emphasize. 3. Write out) contribute to successful execution.

*   **Visual anchors**  
    Use color markers to highlight keywords, stickers for reminders, simple schemes and drawings to explain complex concepts.

*   **Rhythm and movement**  
    Integrate rhythmic actions (clamping, steps). Short physical warm -ups (2-3 minutes) between the tasks activate both hemispheres and improve concentration.

*   **Visual timer**  
    Use a sand clock or a kitchen timer to refer to working intervals (for example: 15 minutes of work, 5 minutes of rest). This makes time tangible and helps to maintain focus.

### What interferes

*   **Multitasking**  
    Simultaneous listening, observation and recording is extremely difficult. Effectively performing only one action at a time.

*   **Visual noise**  
    Overloaded pages of textbooks (many images, different fonts, footnotes) or cluttered desktop become a serious obstacle.

*   **Long oral instructions**  
    From a long verbal instruction, only the first or last word will be perceived. The instructions should be as short and specific as possible.

*   **Fast change of activity**  
    A sharp, unprepared switching between tasks can cause “freezing” and thread loss.

## 3. Practical recommendations

### Home environment

*   **Organization of homework**
    *   Perform lessons with short sessions for 15–20 minutes, with mandatory 5-minute breaks for physical activity.
    *   Start with the most complex objects when the level of attention is maximum.
    *   Before starting the assignment, read it together and ask the child to retell the essence in your own words. Highlight the keywords.

*   **Support and promotion**
    *   Praise not for the result, but for the efforts made: "I noticed how you tried not to be distracted by these 15 minutes, great work!"
    *   Organize a zone on the desktop free from distracting items: only a textbook, notebook and pen.

*   **Important:** The productive holding of attention on the educational task is about 15 minutes, after which the effectiveness is significantly reduced.

### Training space

*   **Organization of the educational process**
    *   An ideal place in the class: the first rows, in the center, away from windows and doors to minimize distraction.
    *   Formulate the tasks sequentially: "Make a, then show me. Now make B." Give only one instruction at a time.
    *   On the board and in handouts, use a minimum of visual noise: a large font, enough free space, a clear structure.

*   **Material feed style**
    *   Duplicate oral instructions with visual (brief records on the board, cards with a task on the desk).
    *   Before starting the explanation, establish visual contact and make sure that the child’s readiness to listen. Ask to nod in understanding.

*   **Elements of an individual route**
    *   Give the possibility of short "motor pauses" (for example, distribute notebooks, wipe the board).
    *   Allow me to use the line for tracking the lines when reading or a card to close the completed part of the task. This helps to focus vision.

## 4. Developing practices

The proposed games are aimed at developing key skills. It is recommended to engage in 10-15 minutes daily in a playful way.

### Development of attention and accuracy

(Help in the fight against visual confusion)

*   **"Find 10 differences":** Classic exercise for training attentiveness for details.
*   **Graphic dictations:** "One cell to the right, two cells up ..." develops auditory attention and spatial perception.
*   **Correct samples:** Invite the child to cross out all the letters "A" or circle all the letters "O" in a small text.

### Coordination and integration

(Help in the fight against asymmetry of the reaction)

*   **Mirror drawing:** Draw with both hands symmetrical patterns (circles, waves, butterflies) on a sheet with a central line.
*   **Games with the ball:** Throw the ball from one hand to another, beat off the wall alternately with your right and left hand.
*   **Exercise "Fist-Robric Ladon":** Alternately change the three positions of the hand on the table, starting slowly, then accelerating. Perform each hand individually, then both at the same time.

### Control and self -regulation

(Assistance in impulsive management)

*   **The game "Sea is worried once":** He teaches instantly "freezing" at the signal, training control over the body.
*   **The game is "edible, unauthorized":** Develops the speed of the reaction and the ability to timely inhibition of the action.
*   **Cooling rhythms:** You set a simple rhythm, and the child exactly repeats it. This develops auditory attention and a sense of rhythm, closely related to self -control.
