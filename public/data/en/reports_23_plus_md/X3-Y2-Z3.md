# Cognitive assessment
## Analysis and recommendations (23+ years)

This report provides key data on the cognitive features of your student, offering targeted strategies to support his development. The report was created for parents and teachers who seek to maximize the student’s potential.

---

### 1. Brief review of the results

*   **Work memory test (CPT):** The ability to effective memorization of individual facts and images has been identified, but difficulties with their consistent organization are possible. This indicates a strong instant memory if it is necessary to develop information structuring skills.
*   **Braking Test (Go/No-Go):** The tendency to impulsive actions was noted - making decisions until complete comprehension. This can lead to errors in tasks requiring exposure and accuracy.
*   **Attention test (TCP):** In situations requiring high concentration, the student is inclined to quick decisions, without waiting for complete information. This confirms the revealed impulsiveness, which can intensify with fatigue or stress.

### 2. The guide for the success of your student

This leadership describes how the student learns, what contributes to his effectiveness and what can create obstacles.

### Individual characteristics

*   **Pace:** A quick, impulsive pace of work is characteristic. Efficiency increases significantly with conscious slowdown and inclusion of pauses for thinking.
*   **Perception:** Optimally assimilates information through bright images, diagrams, infographics. A holistic vision of the structure of the material is priority before linear text or long oral instructions.
*   **Attention:** The focus can be strong, but short -term. There is a tendency to quickly choose the first idea without a deep analysis of alternatives.

### Which contributes to training

*   **Visualization:** Mental cards, diagrams, timelines help streamline and remember information.
*   **Step -by -step instructions:** Separation of volumetric tasks into small, specific steps reduces anxiety and contributes to execution.
*   **Rule "Stop-think-make":** The introduction of a conscious pause before the answer or action. You can use the physical "anchor", for example, a deep breath.
*   **Clear rhythm:** Work in short intervals with mandatory breaks (for example, a method of prying: 25 minutes of work, 5 minutes of rest).

### What can interfere

*   **Multitasking:** Attempts to fulfill several cases simultaneously provoke hasty decisions and disorganization.
*   **Continuous text:** Long text blocks or oral instructions without visual support make it difficult to perception and memorization.
*   **Time limitation:** The pressure pressure enhances stress and impulsiveness, leading to an increase in the number of errors.
*   **Criticism for errors by carelessness:** Unconstructive criticism causes frustration and does not contribute to the development of self -control.

### 3. Recommendations

### For the house

*   **Organization of homework:**
    *   Divide homework into blocks for 20-30 minutes with 5-minute breaks.
    *   Before starting, make a simple, recorded or drawn plan: “1. Mathematics. 2. Break. 3. History ".
    *   The optimal concentration time on one complex task is 20–25 minutes, after which the productivity is reduced.
*   **Support and encouragement:**
    *   Avoid criticism for impulsive errors. Calmly analyze the situation: "What could you do otherwise?"
    *   Encourage the process, not just the result: following the plan, conscious pauses, self -test. This forms stable skills.

### For school

*   **The wording of tasks:**
    *   Tasks should be clumsy, short, contain one call to action.
    *   Instead: "Read the paragraph and answer the questions."
    *   Better: “1. Read the paragraph until page 25. 2. Highlight 3 main thoughts. 3. Verily answer question No. 1. ”
*   **Lesson structure:**
    *   Use on the board or in handouts of the scheme, lists, color selection. This helps the student “grab” the structure and sequence of the topic.
    *   Give the time to think about the answer, for example: "Take your time, think for a minute."
*   **Class placement:**
    *   The optimal place is closer to the teacher, far from distracting factors (window, door) to minimize external stimuli.
*   **Elements of an individual route:**
    *   Permission to use a draft plan before a final response to the control.
    *   Providing checklists for self-testing of work performed.
    *   If possible, the provision of additional time for the performance of tests and control.

### 4. Recommended training

These exercises will help to develop skills detected during testing as a growth zone.

*   **Attention and self -control (impulsive control):**
    *   **The game "Delay":** Movement to music with an instant stop when interrupted. He trains brake control perfectly.
    *   **Board games:** Chess, checkers, “jenga” - contribute to the development of patience, strategic thinking and restraining impulses.
*   **Memory and sequence (memory and logic):**
    *   **Exercise "Tell me in order":** Retelling the plot of the film, the head of the book or the past day in compliance with the sequence of events. Use leading questions: “What happened before? And then?"
    *   **Mental maps (Mind Maps):** On any topic (from the school paragraph to plans for the weekend). Develops the ability to see connections and structure.
*   **Rhythm and coordination (connection "brain-body"):**
    *   **Rhythmic exercises:** Removing the specified rhythm with hands or legs (from simple to complex).
    *   **Juggling:** Even two balls. Effectively trains concentration, visual-motor coordination and forecasting of actions.
