# Cognitive assessment

This report offers a deep analysis of the cognitive features of your student. Our recommendations will help to reveal its potential and create an optimal environment for training.

## 1. Test results

*   **Attention (TCP):** Difficulties with prolonged holding of attention are observed. Distribution of external stimuli or internal thoughts.
*   **Self-control (Go/No-Go):** Difficulties with the suppression of the first impulse. The tendency to hasty actions, especially with fatigue.
*   **Working memory and sequence (CPT):** Good memorization of individual elements, but difficulties with their order or following multi -step instructions are possible.

**General conclusion:** The student needs an external structure for successful education and self -regulation. It achieves the best results in a predictable and organized environment, experiencing difficulties in chaos or multitasking conditions.

## 2. Student support guide

### Features of perception

*   **Pace:** A measured pace is required. Hurry or excessive pressure can increase errors and impulsiveness.
*   **Information format:** He perceives information more effectively, divided into short, clear steps supplemented by visual materials. Long oral instructions can be missed.
*   **Focus of attention:** Better concentrated on one task. Switching between several cases can cause difficulties.

### Contributing factors

*   **Step -by -step instructions:** Decomposition of complex tasks for simple stages (for example, "1. Collect toys. 2. Take the bed.").
*   **Visual support:** The use of checklists, timers (including visual), schemes, intelligence cards.
*   **Predictable rhythm:** Clear mode of the day, alternation of activity and rest (15-20 minutes of work, 5-7 minutes of break).
*   **Physical breaks:** Short active pauses for "restarting" attention and control.

### Factors that reduce efficiency

*   **Multitasking:** Simultaneous requirements, for example, listen and record, or perform several intellectual tasks.
*   **Long -term oral instructions:** Long monologues without pauses or visual support.
*   **External distracting factors:** Noise, conversations, disorder at the workplace, reducing concentration.
*   **Sharp change in activity:** Unexpected switching between tasks can cause stress and disorientation.

## 3. Practical recommendations

### For the house

*   **Homework:**
    *   Divide large tasks into 2-3 short approaches (15–20 minutes each).
    *   Use checklist checks to feel completeness and control.
    *   Start with the most complex or unloved items while the concentration is maximum.
*   **Support and motivation:**
    *   Praise efforts and process, and not just the result. Focus on following the plan or correct errors.
    *   Organize the workplace by removing all unnecessary for the current task.
*   **Attention management:** Remember that an effective concentration on a complex task is limited to 15-20 minutes. After that, a short break is needed.

### For school

*   **The wording of tasks:**
    *   The instructions should be short, cherished, step -by -step. Ideal - one instruction at a time.
    *   Duplicate oral tasks on the board or cards.
*   **Lesson structure:**
    *   At the beginning of the lesson, voice and write down a brief plan.
    *   Place the student closer to the teacher, away from distracting factors.
*   **Teaching style:**
    *   Use visual materials: diagrams, tables, videos, real items.
    *   Enter the active action: demonstration on the board, distribution of materials, short questions.
*   **Elements of individualization (IOM):**
    *   Provide extra time for tests.
    *   Allow the use of a line for reading or a sheet of paper for highlighting the lines in tasks.

## 4. Developing activity

The presented games and exercises are easily integrated into everyday life, contributing to the development of key skills.

*   **Development of attention:**
    *   **"Find the differences":** Classic concentration exercise.
    *   **Graphic dictations:** Improve auditory attention and follow the instructions.
    *   **Correct samples:** Fraightening certain letters in the text for training concentration.
*   **Self -control and coordination:**
    *   **"The sea is worried once"** or **"FROME-OOMILE"**: Games for instant signal stop, training brake functions.
    *   **Board games with rules:** Contribute to the development of patience and compliance with priority.
*   **Memory and sequence:**
    *   **"The story on the pictures"**: Compilation of a coherent narrative in a series of images.
    *   **The game "I put in a bag ..."**: Exercise for memorizing and reproducing the sequence of elements.
*   **Rhythm and movement:**
    *   **"Repeat the rhythm"**: Reproduction of rhythmic patterns with cotton and stomp. Helps to structure activities.
