# Cognitive profile
## Impulsiveness and focus (23+ years)

This report provides an individual view of the developmental features of the student. Its goal is to help create an effective and supportive environment for training and growth.

---

# 1. Review of tests

*   **Test for attention (CPT).** There is a tendency to confusion of similar parts, especially with high information load. The student is inclined to speculate, and not accurately recognize elements.
*   **Reactions braking test (Go/No-Go).** High impulsiveness has been revealed. There is a haste in actions and answers, without a complete expectation of the signal.
*   **Machine decision test (TCP).** Decisions are often made without a complete collection of information. Principle: Action precedes thinking.

**General conclusion.** The schoolboy has a quick, energetic mind. The rapid grasping of the essence is combined with high impulsivity and difficulties of filtering visual data. This can lead to errors by carelessness. These features are not spaces in knowledge, but the features of the nervous system that require development.

# 2. Individual learning profile

## Individual characteristics

*   **Pace.** Fast, gusty. The priority of speed over quality.
*   **Perception.** Short, structured information is most effectively perceived. Long instructions cause visual overload and loss of parts.
*   **Attention.** It is easily involved in the new one, but experiences difficulties with holding concentration on monotonous tasks. The tendency to start the action until the instructions are fully obtained.

## Supporting factors

*   **Step -by -step instructions.** Separate the tasks into 2-3 simple steps.
*   **Visual anchors.** Use markers, stickers, simple checklists to highlight an important.
*   **The rule "Stop-think-make."** Enter micropauzes before action. For example, the ritual: put the handle, take inhale-output, then begin.
*   **Rhythm.** Rhythmic actions (tapping, account) before the task help reduce impulsiveness and tune.

## Interviewing factors

*   **Multitasking.** Many instructions simultaneously guarantee errors. One task is one instruction.
*   **Quick pace and noise.** Fast speech, background noise, clutter space overload attention.
*   **Criticism for errors.** Contemporary for inaccuracies demotivates. Errors are caused by impulsiveness, not ignorance.

# 3. Recommendations

## For the house

*   **Homework organization:**
    *   **Time.** Effective concentration lasts 10-15 minutes. After that, a short physical break is needed.
    *   **Space.** The workplace should be clean. On the table is only necessary for the current task.
    *   **Examination.** Enter the game "Find your mistake." Use a line for a line check. Create a checklist of 2-3 points.
*   **Support and encouragement:**
    *   **Promotion.** Praise for effort and strategy, and not for the assessment: “I see how you tried and checked every word!”, “It is great that you paused before the answer!”
    *   **Constructive feedback.** Instead of censure, use the phrases: “It seems that something has hidden here. Let's find together? ”

## For school

*   **The wording of tasks:**
    *   **The clarity of tasks.** Tasks should be short, unambiguous. Important information is duplicated on the board with the selection.
    *   **Placement.** Tasks to place tasks in the upper left of the sheet or boards for better attention.
*   **Lesson structure:**
    *   **Individual route.** Elements may include: placement at the first or second desk, additional time for checking, and permission to use the ruler.
    *   **The rule "Railed hand".** Enter the rule: "Raise your hand, mentally count to three, then answer." It trains endurance.
*   **Style style:**
    *   **The pace of speech.** Calm, measured. Duplicate the oral instructions with a record or diagram on the board.

# 4. Practical exercises

These exercises develop key functions. Do 5-10 minutes daily in a playful way.

## Attention and visual perception

*   "Corrective test." Ask to cross out specific letters in the text or circle them.
*   Board games. “Find a couple”, “Dobble”, labyrinths, searching for differences.

## Control and reduction of impulsiveness

*   "Agrie-Otomory." Movement to the music, instant stop when it is stopped.
*   "The sea is worried once." Classical game to keep a static posture.
*   "Yes, and no, do not say." Speech control exercise.

## Coordination and rhythm

*   Neurohymnastics. Exercise "Fist-Robric Ladon" is a consistent change in the positions of the hand on the table, from slow to fast.
*   Games with the ball. Bowing the ball from the floor or wall in a given rhythm.
*   Cooling rhythms. You set the rhythm, the student repeats.
