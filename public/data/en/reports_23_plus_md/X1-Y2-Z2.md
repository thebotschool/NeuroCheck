# Cognitive assessment report: profile AB-123 (age: 23+ years)

This report offers a deep look at the unique cognitive characteristics of the student. The goal is to identify the strengths and areas for development, to create optimal conditions for training and growth.

---

## 1. A brief report for each test

### Visual Working Memorial Test
The ability to retain a large amount of visual information is perfectly developed. The student effectively uses memorization strategies. This is a strong side that helps in solving complex, structured problems.

### Go/No-Go Test (impulsive control)
A tendency to quick, hasty reactions has been revealed. The student can act without a complete analysis of the situation, which leads to errors due to inattention.

### Continous Performance Test.
The concentration on monotonous tasks for a long time is difficult. Attention is most productive at short distances, it is scattered with a long monotonous work.

## 2. Instructions for the child as a student

### Individual characteristics

*   **Pace:** The natural pace is fast. The greatest productivity is achieved in measured mode, with an “internal pause” before action.
*   **Perception format:** Prefers structured, visually organized information (diagrams, tables, step -by -step plans). Oral instructions without a visual support are absorbed worse.
*   **Focus of attention:** Intensive, but short -term. A deep immersion in an interesting task is possible, but the concentration resource is depleted after 20–25 minutes, requiring a short break or a change in activity.

### Which helps

*   **Visual supports:** Check lines, highlighting keywords, patterns on the board.
*   **Step -by -step instructions:** Separation of large tasks into 3-4 understandable steps.
*   **Rule "Stop-think-make":** Conscious pause before starting work.
*   **Timer:** Using a timer to organize work and rest.
*   **Predictability:** A clear schedule and structure of a lesson or day.

### What interferes

*   **Multitasking:** Simultaneous listening, recording, thinking.
*   **Rush and pressure:** Phrases like “Come on faster”, “Little time” provoke impulsive errors.
*   **Long oral instructions:** Overload attention.
*   **Monotone:** Long -term completion of the same type of tasks.
*   **Background noise:** Extraneous conversations, music, TV reduce productivity.

## 3. Recommendations

### For the house

#### Organization of homework
*   Divide volumetric tasks into blocks for 20–25 minutes with 5-minute breaks.
*   Before starting work together, read the task, make a short plan (2-3 steps). Ask questions: “Why do you start? What is the second step? ”
*   Organize a quiet, ordered workplace where there are no extra items.

#### Support and motivation
*   Praise for attentiveness and accuracy, and not for speed. Mark the moments when he did not hurry and double -checked himself.
*   With errors, avoid criticism. Use the questions: “What could go wrong here? Perhaps we missed the word in the condition? ”
*   Discuss the importance of sleep and rest. Fatigue directly affects impulsiveness and attention.

### For school

#### Formulation and presentation of tasks
*   Provide the tasks in writing, even if they are duplicated verbally. Ideally-a printed checklist or point on the board.
*   Structure control: clearly separate the tasks, leave a place to solve.
*   Use visual materials (presentations, diagrams, video) to use strong visual memory.

#### Organization of work in the classroom
*   Optimal place: one of the front desks to minimize distracting factors.
*   Paise before the call to the answer, giving time to formulate thoughts.
*   For long tasks, provide for “intermediate control points”.

#### Elements of an individual educational route
*   Allow the use of a timer for self -control.
*   Give the opportunity to make a short “brain break” (1-2 minutes) during a long lesson or control.

## 4. Recommended training

### For attention
*   **"Corrective test":** Refusing certain letters in the text. Start with 5 minutes, gradually increasing time.
*   **Board games:** “Cortex”, “high -speed caps”, “Seth” - develop a quick assessment of the situation and following the rules.

### To control impulsiveness and bodily control
*   **The game "Yes, and not say":** He trains speech control and inhibition of automatic answers.
*   **Balance exercises:** Standing on one leg, walking along the line, Balansboard. Develops the connection of body control with impulses.
*   **Rhythmic games:** Rhythm tapping, instruments play. He teaches you to listen, wait and act at a given pace.

### To use strong memory
*   **The game "Memory" ("Find a couple"):** Harding strategies for memorization.
*   **Creation of intelligence cards (Mind Maps):** Transfer of paragraphs into schemes is an effective training of strengths of memory.
