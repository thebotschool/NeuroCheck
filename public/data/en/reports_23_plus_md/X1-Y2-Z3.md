### Cognitive profile of a student (23+ years old)

This report reveals the cognitive features of your student. It was created to help reveal its potential through targeted recommendations based on testing results.

#### 1. Review of tests

*   **Test of visual working memory:** High ability to memorize, hold and structure complex information. Effective strategies, stable concentration.
*   **Reactions braking test (Go/No-Go):** The tendency to fast, hasty actions. This can cause errors due to a lack of reflection. There is high impulsiveness.
*   **Test for attention and self -control:** Decisions are often made on the basis of the first impulse, without analyzing all the information. Fatigue and stress enhance this trend.

**Key conclusion:** High intellectual potential and excellent memory. However, impulsive actions can interfere with the complete disclosure of these strengths. Purpose: to develop the ability to deliberate actions.

#### 2. Features of your student

*   **Pace:** A tendency to a fast pace. Maximum efficiency is achieved with conscious slowdown and planning.
*   **Perception:** It works most effective with structured, visual information. Schemes, tables, checklists, mental cards-key tools.
*   **Focus of attention:** Capable of deep concentration. A impulsive desire to act can quickly knock down a focus.

##### Which helps

*   **The rule "Stop. Think. Do it. "** A short pause before action or answer.
*   **Visual supports.** Selection of keywords, step-by-step plans, checklists for self-testing.
*   **Skipping tasks.** Division of tasks into small stages. Check after everyone: "Is everything done? The next step?"
*   **Rhythm and timers.** Using a timer (for example, Pomodoro: 25 min. Work, 5 min. Rest) for structuring time and pauses.

##### What interferes

*   **Multitasking.** Quick switching depleted self -control, provokes errors.
*   **Hurry and pressure.** The need for an instant response or action "for speed" leads to impulsive errors.
*   **Chaos and non -settings.** Lack of plan, distracting environment, obscure instructions.
*   **Fatigue.** Overwork and stress enhance impulsiveness.
<!-- page-break -->
#### 3. Recommendations

##### Home environment

*   **Homework organization:**
    *   **Planning:** 2 minutes to draw up a plan before starting work.
    *   **Detailing:** For complex tasks-a mini-plan.
    *   **Wednesday:** The workplace should be clean, without abstracts.
*   **Support and encouragement:**
    *   **Focus on thoughtfulness:** Praise for thoroughness, not speed.
    *   **Questions:** Instead of "made lessons?" - "What is the plan? Need help?"
*   **Mercing of attention:** Effective work on one task is 30-40 minutes. Then a short (5 min.) A break to change activities and restore self -control.

##### School environment

*   **The wording of tasks:**
    *   **Written form:** Tasks with clockwork steps.
    *   **Selection:** Keywords in tests are highlighted with fat font.
*   **Lesson and work structure:**
    *   **Introduction:** 2 minutes for reading and planning before the control/independent.
    *   **Visualization:** Brief lesson plan or algorithm on the board/handout.
*   **Style style:** Priority - reasoning aloud, analysis, planning. Not fast answers.
*   **Individual route:**
    *   **Time:** Additional time on tests to reduce haste.
    *   **Tools:** Allow personal checks for self-testing.

#### 4. Recommended training

These classes develop self -control, attention and coordination in a playful way.

*   **Attention. Strategy.**
    *   **Board games:** Chess, GO, Jackal, Colonialists. Games requiring planning several moves forward.
*   **Memory. Concentration.**
    *   **Exercise "Remember and draw":** Show a complex pattern/diagram for 15 seconds, then play from memory.
*   **Rhythm. Control.**
    *   **Musical instruments:** Especially drums or percussion training the following rhythm.
    *   **Juggling:** Develops the coordination of the “eye-hand” and control over impulses with multitasking.
*   **Awareness.**
    *   **Breath "Square":** Before work or under stress. 4 sec. Inhale - 4 seconds. delay - 4 seconds. Exhale - 4 seconds. delay. Helps to concentrate and activate self -control.
