## The cognitive profile of the student

This report provides an analysis of the cognitive functions of the student. It is intended for parents and teachers to determine the strengths and areas for development, contributing to the creation of an optimal environment for training and growth.

### 1. Evaluation results

*   **Long -term attention (CPT):** Difficulties with maintaining concentration on one task and the implementation of multi -step instructions are noted. This may be due to general fatigue.
*   **Self-control (Go/No-Go):** The tendency to impulsive reactions has been revealed, which is often a sign of overwork.
*   **Visual working memory (TCP):** The indicators indicate cognitive overload. The instability of attention and reactions indicates the exhaustion of resources.

**Key observation:** The data indicate significant cognitive overload and stress. The observed difficulties with attention and impulsiveness are, rather, a consequence of the depletion of resources, and not by constant characteristics. Priority - optimization of load and restoration of forces.

### 2. The guide for the success of your student

#### Individual characteristics

*   **The pace of assimilation:** The current pace is fast and intermittent, which contributes to errors. More effective - a slow and conscious approach with pauses.
*   **Optimal format:** Visual and step -by -step information is most effectively perceived. Long text or oral materials are more complicated.
*   **Duration of concentration:** The focus of attention is currently short -term and unstable. Effective concentration is possible within 15–20 minutes.

#### Supporting methods

*   **Unified task:** Concentration on one task.
*   **Visual funds:** The use of checklists, diagrams, memory cards.
*   **A clear plan:** Tasks with a clear structure: beginning, middle, end.
*   **Modor technique:** Work in short intervals (for example, 20 minutes of work / 5 minutes of rest).
*   **Preliminary pronunciation:** Sounding the plan of action to slow down impulsiveness.
*   **Short physical pauses:** 2-3 minutes of activity for the "reboot" of the brain.

#### Factors that reduce productivity

*   **Multitasking:** Simultaneous performance of several actions.
*   **Ugly instructions:** General tasks without specifics.
*   **Rush and pressure:** Harsh terms without the possibility of planning.
*   **Touch noise:** Distracting sounds, notifications.

### 3. Recommendations

#### Home environment

**Homework**
*   Divide homework into blocks in subjects, with mandatory breaks for 5-10 minutes.
*   Use a timer to control working intervals (15–20 minutes).
*   Start with the most difficult object while the energy level is high.
*   Provide a calm workspace free from distracting factors (especially gadgets).

**Communication and support**
*   Formulate questions about the school, focusing on the interest and lightness of the tasks.
*   Mark efforts and process, and not just the final result.
*   Recognize fatigue by offering a pause to restore strength.

#### School environment

**The formulation of tasks**
*   Provide the instructions step by step: for example, "Step 1: Open the textbook ...", "Step 2: Find the exercise ...".
*   Duplicate oral tasks in writing (on the board, card).

**The structure of the lesson**
*   Design and fix the lesson plan at the beginning to create predictability.
*   Use visual materials: diagrams, tables, short videos.
*   Consider the placement of the student in place with the minimum number of distracting factors (closer to the teacher).

**Individual route (IOM)**
*   Provide additional time to perform control and independent work.
*   Allow the delivery of volumetric projects in parts to reduce anxiety.
*   Consider the possibility of using headphones with white noise to reduce distracting factors (applicable according to the situation).

### 4. Developing practices

The presented practices contribute to the development of self -control, attention and a decrease in overall stress. It is recommended to perform them in a playful way, without coercion.

**Attention and concentration**
*   **Board games:** "Seth", "Volga", "corridor" - develop a quick concentration and retention of the rules.
*   **Schultte tables:** Search for numbers in order - trains peripheral vision and speed of attention.

**Self -control and coordination**
*   **Juggling:** Starting with two objects-synchronizes the work of the hemispheres, develops the coordination of the "eye-hand".
*   **Balance exercises:** Standing on one leg, walking along the line, Balanscher - contribute to the awareness of the body and "grounding".

**Memory and sequence**
*   **The game "Memory":** Search for paired cards.
*   **Rhythmic exercises:** Creating or repeating rhythmic patterns (claps, on tools) - structures and soothes the nervous system.

**Stress removal**
*   **Breathing "on the square":** Inhale, delay, exhale, delay - 4 accounts. 2-3 minutes of practice contribute to rapid calm.
