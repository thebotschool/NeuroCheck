## Cognitive profile. Analysis.

This report provides key data on the cognitive profile of the student. It was created for parents and teachers to reveal the strengths and optimize the environment for development. The results confirm the high level of cognitive abilities - the foundation for future achievements.

## 1. Evaluation. Key data.

*   **Attention (CPT):** Demonstrates a stable concentration and accuracy in performing tasks that require prolonged attention. The ability to hold the focus and notice the details.
*   **Self-control (Go/No-Go):** Great control over impulsive reactions. The ability to deliberately make decisions, analyze the situation before action.
*   **Visual working memory:** A great ability to hold and effectively organize a large amount of visual information. Using a strategic approach to memorization.

## 2. Profile. Potential.

### Individual characteristics.

*   **Pace:** It works effectively at a fast pace, but reaches a maximum if there is time for strategic planning. He appreciates methodological, does not accept fuss without a goal.
*   **Perception:** The best assimilates structured and visual information. Schemes, tables, graphs, intelligence cards-its/its element. He sees logic and system in the data.
*   **Focus:** Attention is deep and stable. Able/to completely immerse himself in a difficult task, not being distracted by the background. Effective/B at high cognitive load.

### Optimal conditions.

*   **Complex tasks:** Tasks requiring analysis, comparison, search for patterns, non -standard solutions.
*   **Autonomous planning:** The ability to independently build an action plan and determine the stages of work on the project.
*   **Visualization:** The use of diagrams, diagrams, color coding and discharge in educational materials.
*   **Project activity:** Tasks where a systematic approach, process coordination and responsibility for the result are important.

### Obstacles.

*   **Monotone:** Simple, monotonous tasks quickly cause boredom and reduce motivation. An intellectual challenge is required.
*   **Microenage:** Tough, step -by -step control limits the initiative. Blocks the strong side - strategic thinking.
*   **Dispersed:** Material without a clear structure or logical connections. It is important to see how new information fits into the overall picture.

## 3. Guide. Support.

### Home environment.

*   **Homework:** Provide autonomy. Focus on planning: "What is your plan? Do you need help in search?" He/A capable/on distribute the load.
*   **Motivation:** Support for the search for complex tasks. Encourage non -standard solutions and an elegant approach, and not just evaluations.
*   **Organization of time:** Effectively concentrates to 45-60 minutes. After that, a short break (5-10 minutes) for rebooting is recommended.
*   **Development:** Discuss complex topics, watch scientific films, solve logical puzzles. The mind needs a qualitative stimulus.

### School environment.

*   **The wording of tasks:** Present tasks as problems or cases. For example: "Based on paragraph 5, make a diagram of causes and consequences ..."
*   **Lesson structure:** At the beginning of the topic, offer a "road map" - a general study scheme. This helps to build connections.
*   **Role in the class:** It can become a mentor or leader of group projects. His/its ability to structure information is useful for the group.
*   **Communication style:** The partnership approach is effective: "Let's think together", "What are your hypotheses?" Avoid excessively directive instructions.
*   **Individual route:** Consider the deepening knowledge through Olympiads, scientific conferences, research projects. This will apply analytical abilities.

Cognitive functions are highly developed, but it is important to support them.

*   **Attention. Strategy:**
