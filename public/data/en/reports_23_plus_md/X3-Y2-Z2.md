# Report: Cognitive profile

This report provides a deep understanding of the individual cognitive characteristics of the student. It is designed to help parents and teachers in creating an optimal environment for training and development, revealing strengths and pointing to growth zones.

---

# 1. Testing analysis

*   **Working memory (CPT):** Effective memorization of individual elements, but difficulties with their logical ordering. Support for structuring information for holistic perception is required.

*   **Reactions inhibition (Go/No-Go):** There is a tendency to impulsive actions ahead of understanding. This can cause errors that require attentiveness and control.

*   **Sustainability of attention (TCP):** The difficulty in long -term attention is noted on one task. Concentration can decrease under the influence of fatigue or external stimuli.

# 2. Features of training

## Individual profile

*   **Pace:** The tendency to quick, sometimes impetuous fulfillment of tasks, which can affect the accuracy and quality of work.
*   **Perception:** It more effectively assimilates information presented in structured and visually designed form (diagrams, lists, short paragraphs). Long oral instructions or continuous text are more complicated.
*   **Attention:** Attention is unstable, easily distracted by external stimuli, such as noise or movement.

## Success factors

*   **Visualization:** Schemes, intelligence cards, checklists and stickers help to structure tasks and information.
*   **Step -by -step instructions:** Skipping large tasks into small, understandable stages. For example: read the condition, highlight the key, write down the solution, check.
*   **Interval approach:** Short periods of concentration (15–20 minutes) with subsequent small breaks (3-5 minutes) increase productivity.
*   **Structure and rhythm:** The predictable daily routine and clear rules minimize the cognitive load, preserving the resource for study.

## Obstacles in learning

*   **Multitasking:** The simultaneous performance of several tasks or quick switching between them leads to errors and frustration.
*   **Time pressure:** Acceleration or indication of a short time enhances impulsiveness, interfering with a qualitative check of work.
*   **Distracting factors:** Noise, disorder at the workplace, background conversations significantly reduce concentration.
*   **Long explanations:** Long -term oral monologues without pauses and visual support are partially absorbed.

# 3. Recommendations

## Home environment

### Organization of the educational process

*   **Interval work:** Separate homework into short blocks (15–20 minutes) with 5-minute breaks. Use the timer.
*   **Planning:** Make a simple work plan before starting classes. For example: mathematics (20 min), break, Russian (20 min).
*   **Check lists:** Use checklist checks to track completed tasks. This develops self -control and creates a feeling of completeness.

### Support and motivation

*   **Focus on efforts:** Mark efforts and process, and not just the result. Example: "Excellent, you could concentrate!" Or "You checked your work - this is a mature approach."
*   **Error analysis:** Instead of criticism, offer a joint analysis: "Let's see where the thought went differently?" This forms analytical thinking and reduces the fear of errors.

### Organization of space

*   **Workplace:** Provide a constant, quiet and neat workspace. Remove all the extra before starting classes.
*   **Silence:** Agree on the "quiet hour" during homework, excluding a background noise.

## School environment

### The formulation of tasks

*   **Clarity and sequence:** The instructions should be short, clear and step -by -step. Key steps can be duplicated on the board.
*   **Checking understanding:** Ask the student to repeat the instructions in your own words to confirm a complete understanding.

### The structure of the lesson

*   **Visual support:** Structure the material using diagrams, tables and lists. Use color markers to select key information.
*   **Change of activity:** Alternate the stages of the lesson: explanation (10-15 minutes), short practical task, physical education or oral survey.

### Accommodation and support

*   **Optimal place:** It is recommended to place at the first or second desk, closer to the center, away from distracting factors (window, door).
*   **Individual approach:**
    *   Providing additional time in control work.
    *   Permission to use a draft plan before the answer.
    *   Tasks in cards with a step -by -step algorithm.

# 4. Developing practices

These simple exercises and games are easy to integrate into everyday life for the development of key cognitive skills.

*   **For the development of attention:**
    *   **Correct test:** The task is to quickly find and cross out certain characters (for example, all letters "A") on the text page.
    *   **Search for differences:** Classic tasks with the search for differences between images for training concentration.
    *   **Board games:** Games like "Jengi", "Uno" or "Dobble" contribute to the development of concentration and reaction speed.

*   **To improve memory and sequence:**
    *   **Plot sequence:** Invite the child to make a coherent story based on several plot pictures located in the correct order.
    *   **What has changed?** Lay out several items, give time to remember. Then change their location or remove one. The task is to determine the changes.
    *   **Learning verses:** Practice for memorizing poems. Start with an understanding of meaning, then proceed to consistent memorization of the lines.

*   **To control impulsiveness and bodily coordination:**
    *   **"FROME-OUTOMI":** A simple game, training ability to control movements and reactions.
    *   **Repetition of rhythms:** Remove the rhythmic patterns, and the student must accurately reproduce them. Gradually complicate the rhythm.
    *   **Respiratory exercises:** The technique of "square breathing" (inhalation, delay, exhalation, delay - each stage in 4 accounts) helps to calm down and focus on complex tasks.
