### **Cognitive profile assessment report**
Sustainability of attention + impulse control + self -regulation | Age: 23+ years

This report offers a detailed analysis of the cognitive characteristics of the student. It is developed for parents and teachers, providing clear recommendations based on objective data. The results reflect the unique style of brain functioning, and not evaluative characteristics.

---

### **1. Analysis of cognitive profile**

*   **Sustainability of attention (CPT):** Maintaining concentration in monotonous tasks can be difficult, especially with or without fatigue.
*   **Fast response (Go/No-Go):** There is a tendency to quick, sometimes impulsive reactions, which can lead to errors in carelessness.
*   **Attention in structured conditions (TCP):** A highly organized environment demonstrates high concentration, accuracy and attention to details.

**Key feature:** Profile "Sprinter". The student is able to show outstanding results on short, focused tasks. Longer efforts require targeted support. The strengths are deep concentration. The area for development is impulsiveness and fatigue from routine.

### **2. The guide for the success of your student**

#### Individual characteristics

*   **Pace:** The natural pace is high and dynamic. The optimal productive pace is measured, with mandatory pauses for self -testing. A rush can be a source of errors.
*   **Perception format:** The best perception is visual and clearly structured information. Long oral instructions or unstructured text are less effective.
*   **Focus of attention:** Intensive, but short -term. Deep concentration is possible for 15–20 minutes, after which a change in activity or “reboot” is required.

#### Support strategies

*   **Step -by -step instructions:** Providing clear, consistent steps, preferably in the form of a checklist.
*   **Visual materials:** The use of schemes, tables, intelligence cards, highlighting keywords.
*   **Division of tasks:** Separation of volumetric work into short segments (15–20 minutes).
*   **Timer:** The use of a timer for each stage of work helps to control time and maintain focus.
*   **Short breaks:** Active 3-5 minute breaks after each stage of work for recovery.

#### What prevents

*   **Multitasking:** The simultaneous performance of several actions reduces the quality of each of them.
*   **Monotone:** Long lectures or the same exercises cause rapid fatigue and a decrease in concentration.
*   **Pressure:** The phrases that stimulate a rush provoke impulsive errors.
*   **Abstract instructions:** Tasks without a specific algorithm of actions are ineffective.

### **3. Application of recommendations**

#### Home environment

*   **Work planning:**
    *   The principle of 20/5: 20 minutes of focused work, then 5 minutes of the break.
    *   A clear, recorded plan of action before starting homework.
    *   Start with the most difficult tasks while the energy level is high.
*   **Support and interaction:**
    *   Focus on a joint analysis of errors, offering to become a “corrector”, not criticize.
    *   To encourage the use of the right strategies, and not just the final result.
*   **Attention regulation:**
    *   Realistic time of effective concentration is 15–20 minutes. Plan activities taking into account this.

#### School environment

*   **Setting tasks:**
    *   The maximum specificity, the use of the verbs of action: “emphasize”, “compare”, “write down”.
    *   Providing ready -made templates or plans for volumetric tasks.
*   **Lesson structure:**
    *   Application of visual materials: presentations, diagrams, tables, handout.
    *   The alternation of activities every 15–20 minutes: survey, independent work, discussion.
*   **Personalized approach:**
    *   Placing a student at a desk with a minimum number of distracting factors.
    *   Providing additional time to verify the work performed.
    *   Permission to use a sheet of paper for focusing on one line or example.

### **4. Developing practices**

The presented exercises contribute to the development of key skills. Daily practice is recommended within 10-15 minutes to achieve the best results.

*   **Attention and memory:**
    *   **Board games:** “Cortex”, “Dobbl”, “drum” - develop the speed of reaction and attentiveness.
    *   **"Corrective test":** Discarding certain letters in any text (for example, all “a” and bypassing all “o”).
    *   **"Memory":** Playing the development of visual working memory.
*   **Impulse control and rhythm:**
    *   **"FROM ATOMISE":** Teaches to control the body by signal.
    *   **Rhythmic exercises:** The exact repetition of the specified rhythms after a pause (gradual complication).
*   **Coordination and bodily control:**
    *   **Juggling:** Start with two items. Develops the coordination of the "eye-hand" and a sense of time.
    *   **Games with the ball:** Table tennis, badminton, “edible-lunch”-require a quick assessment of the situation and accurate reaction.
