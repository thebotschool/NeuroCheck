## Cognitive test report

This report is your guide for understanding the potential of the student. Our recommendations are created to help create a supporting environment for effective and comfortable training.

---

### 1. Test results

*   **Sustainability of attention (CPT):** Long -term holding of attention on one task is difficult. Stress and stress affect.
*   **Braking and control (Go/No-Go):** The difference in the work of the hemispheres of the brain, manifested in the asymmetry of the speed and accuracy of the movements of the hands, has been noted. This can slow down the reaction in new situations.
*   **Visual working memory:** The results indicate cognitive overload. This reduces the stability of attention and control over impulsive reactions.

### 2. The guide for the success of your student

#### Key features

*   **Pace:** It takes time to comprehend information and the beginning of action. A quick change in tasks or high speech speed can cause overload.
*   **Perception:** Preference of visual and kinesthetic formats. Visual materials (diagrams, images) and practical actions are better absorbed. Long oral instructions are perceived with difficulty.
*   **Focus of attention:** Unstable. Effective concentration lasts **10-15 minutes**, after which a short break is needed. Easily distracted by external stimuli.

#### Which helps

*   **Step -by -step instructions:** Skipping a complex task into 3-4 simple steps. Optimal-in a checklist format.
*   **Visual support:** The use of schemes, tables, highlighted text, lesson plan. Everything that helps to see the structure of information.
*   **Predictability:** A clear schedule of work and rest (for example, 15 minutes of study, 3 minutes of activity).
*   **Wednesday:** Organized workplace and minimum background noise.

#### What interferes

*   **Multitasking:** Simultaneous listening, recording, visual focus and answers. The brain currently does not process several information flows effectively.
*   **Rush and pressure:** Phrases that cause stress ("faster", "easy") block cognitive processes.
*   **Long oral instructions:** Instructions from three or more consistent actions without visual support can be made incomplete or inaccurately.
*   **Distracting environment:** Infant, movements, loud conversations.

### 3. Recommendations

#### Home environment

*   **Organization of work:**
    *   Take short sessions (15–20 minutes) with breaks (3-5 minutes). Use a break for physical activity, not for gadgets.
    *   Apply a timer to see the student to see the remaining time.
    *   Make a simple work plan: "Mathematics (back. 1, 2) → break → History (paragraph)".
    *   Start with the most complex or unloved objects while the energy level is high.
*   **Support and encouragement:**
    *   Instead of "did you do lessons?" Ask: "What is the next step according to the plan?"
    *   Praise not for the result, but for efforts and the process: “You followed the plan!”, “I appreciate how you tried to concentrate.”

#### School environment

*   **The wording of tasks:**
    *   Let's clearly, one at a time. Divide complex indications of simple commands.
    *   Duplicate oral instructions visually (for example, write the page number on the board).
*   **Lesson structure:**
    *   Place the student on the first or second desk of the central row to minimize distraction.
    *   Use visual materials: schemes, tables, presentations.
    *   At the beginning of the lesson, voice and write down a short plan: "Today we: 1. We repeat ... 2. We will find out ... 3. We will train ...".
*   **Individual approach:**
    *   Provide extra time for control and independent work.
    *   Allow the use of a draft plan for essays or complex tasks.
    *   Check the understanding of the instructions individually: "Repeat, what needs to be done now?"

### 4. Recommended training

These exercises will help to develop key skills. Do regularly for 5-10 minutes a day, in a playful way.

*   **Development of attention and memory:**
    *   **Correct test:** In the printed text in 2-3 minutes, cross out all the letters "A" or circle "O".
    *   **Remember and name:** Lay out 4-5 items. The student looks 10 seconds, then closes his eyes. Remove one item and ask what has disappeared. Gradually increase the number of objects.
*   **Coordination and interaction of the hemispheres:**
    *   **Neurohymnastics:** Exercise "Fist-Robric Ladon" (a sequential change in the positions of the hand on the table), "Ear-too" (with the right hand by the tip of the nose, left over the right ear, then a change in cotton).
    *   **Drawing with two hands:** At the same time, draw symmetrical figures (circles, squares) on the sheet.
    *   **Juggling:** Start with two small balls or bags.
*   **Body control and rhythm:**
    *   **Walking along the line:** Draw the line on the floor and walk along it straight, then backwards.
    *   **Repeat the rhythm:** Remove a simple rhythmic pattern, the student repeats. Gradually complicate.
    *   **Balance exercises:** Star on one leg with open, then with eyes closed.
