## Report: Assessment of cognitive abilities (23+ years)

This report offers a detailed understanding of the individual characteristics of your student. It contains practical recommendations to increase the effectiveness of learning in any environment.

---

### 1. Assessment review

*   **Particular attention (CPT):** The student demonstrates good results. However, with an abundance of visual information, difficulties are possible with distinguishing similar details or adding extra elements.
*   **Self-control (Go/No-Go):** Difficulties are possible with the suppression of impulsive reactions, especially with fatigue or stress.
*   **Sustainability of attention (TCP):** The ability to concentrate is preserved. Disclosures may become more frequent after 15–20 minutes of continuous work, which indicates an exhaustion of attention.

### 2. Profile of learning

#### Peculiarities

*   **Pace:** The average or slow pace is optimal, allowing short pauses for self -testing. Hurry often leads to impulsive errors.
*   **Perception:** Preferably a clear, structured and unsaturated visual representation of information. The overload of the details tires and prevents the highlighting the main thing.
*   **Focus:** The greatest concentration is achieved in the first 15–20 minutes of work on the task. Further, productivity can decrease, requiring a short break.

#### Supporting factors

*   **Step -by -step instructions:** Skipping complex tasks into small, consistent steps. The principle of "one step at a time" prevents a rush and rash answers.
*   **Visual tips:** Check lists, schemes, highlighting keywords with color. This helps to systematize information and avoid confusion.
*   **Work on the timer:** Adapted "Plaud" technique: 15–20 minutes of focused work, then 3-5 minutes of a break.
*   **The rule "Stop-think-make":** Encouraging a brief pause before action or an answer to verify an understanding of the problem.

#### Distracting factors

*   **Multitasking:** The simultaneous implementation of several cases or work in conditions of noise and abstracts.
*   **Visual noise:** Overloaded training materials, slides with small elements, a mess at the workplace.
*   **Hurry and high -speed oral tasks:** They provoke impulsive answers, not giving time to process information.
*   **Long -term monotonous work:** Tasks requiring more than 20–25 minutes of continuous concentration lead to depletion and growth of errors.

### 3. Practical recommendations

#### At home

*   **Homework:**
    *   Divide the work into blocks for 15–20 minutes with short breaks.
    *   Together, make a simple plan before starting: for example, "first mathematics, then a break, then Russian."
    *   Organize the workplace: only the necessary materials on the table.
*   **Support and encouragement:**
    *   Mark attentiveness, accuracy and self -test, not speed. For example: "You checked everything, that's great!"
    *   Help recognize fatigue. With signs of distraction, offer a pause, instead of reproaches.
*   **Attention:** Keep in mind that effective continuous attention is 15–20 minutes. An additional requirement is unproductive.

#### At school

*   **Instructions:**
    *   Give the tasks briefly, clearly and in stages. Avoid long combined formulations.
    *   Ask to repeat the task in your own words to confirm understanding.
*   **Materials and structure:**
    *   Use simple schemes, lists, highlighting key moments in color on the board and in handouts.
    *   Allow the use of a draft to plan answers and emphasize keywords in tasks.
*   **Organization:**
    *   The optimal place: the first-second desk, the center of the class, away from distracting factors (window, door).
    *   Use a calm, measured pace of speech with pauses to assimilate information.
*   **Individual plan:**
    *   Consider the possibility of providing additional time for tests.
    *   Permission to take large tasks in parts.
    *   Short "sensory" breaks (drink water, rinsen).

### 4. Developing exercises

These exercises are easily integrated into everyday life for the development of key skills.

*   **Attention and accuracy:**
    *   **"Corrective test":** Ask to cross out all the letters "A" or circle all the prepositions "B" on the newspaper page in 2-3 minutes.
    *   **Board games:** "Dobbl", "set", "find a couple" (Memory) - develop the speed of reaction and visual attention.
*   **Self -control and rhythm:**
    *   **"Delay!":** Turn on the music, offer to dance. When the music stops, the student freezes in the current position.
    *   **Graphic dictations:** Drawing patterns on cells under dictation. Develops auditory attention and following instructions.
*   **Visual memory:**
    *   **"What has changed?":** Put 5-7 items. Ask to remember, then remove or change one item in places. The task is to determine the change.
    *   **Assembly of designers (LEGO, etc.) according to the instructions:** He trains attention to the details, following the plan and visual memory.
*   **Bodily control:**
    *   **Games with the ball:** To beat the ball from the wall, to catch, trying to withstand a certain rhythm.
    *   **Balance exercises:** Walking along the line, stand on one leg. Improves body sense and is associated with impulsive control.
