# The cognitive profile of the student

This report illuminates the cognitive characteristics of the student, offering strategies for its development and create an optimal education environment.

## 1. Review of tests

*   **Working memory (CPT):** There are difficulties with holding and reproducing multi -step instructions. Probably, support in strategies for memorization is required.
*   **Self-control (Go/No-Go):** Expressed development. The ability to stop and follow the rules in time.
*   **Sustainability of attention (TCP):** The concentration decreases during prolonged work. The focus can be lost.

**Bottom line:** Strong self -control is combined with the need to support working memory and attention. Help in organizing tasks and cognitive energy management.

## 2. Success Guide

### Peculiarities

*   **Pace:** Effective in short intervals. Long -term monotonous work causes exhaustion. Crushing of tasks is recommended: 3 sets of 15 minutes instead of 60 minutes without a break.
*   **Perception:** Preference to visual formats: schemes, images, brief lists. Long oral instructions are worse absorbed.
*   **Focus:** Strong, but short -term. Effective in tasks with a quick reaction. Difficulties arise in long -term projects requiring independent planning.

### Support

*   **Step -by -step instructions:** Miss tasks into small, specific actions. Example: instead of "Write an essay" - "1. Ideas. 2. Choice. 3. Plan."
*   **Visual supports:** Check lists, schemes, intelligence cards, keywords on stickers.
*   **Timing:** Use timers (for example, 20 minutes of work / 5 minutes of a break) to maintain concentration.
*   **The only task:** One call to action, one task at a time.
*   **Rhythm and structure:** The predictable daily routine reduces the cognitive load.

### Calls

*   **Multitasking:** Simultaneous tasks lead to inefficiency.
*   **Oral instructions:** More than two actions can be missed or made incorrectly.
*   **Monotone:** Reducing attention after 15–20 minutes of monotonous work.
*   **Distracting factors:** Excess of visual or audio stimuli.

## 3. Recommendations

### At home

*   **Homework:**
    *   Divide the tasks into small steps. Record them.
    *   Apply timer: 20 minutes of work, 5 minutes of break.
    *   Short breaks after each step (get up, stretch).
    *   Optimize the workspace: only necessary for the current task.
*   **Support and encouragement:**
    *   Mark the process, not just the result. Example: "Great, that followed the plan!"
    *   Emphasize the manifestations of self -control.
    *   Use the question: "What is the lesson plan?" Instead of "lessons made?"
*   **Effective time:** The focus of attention on a difficult task remains 15–20 minutes. A short break is required.

### At school

*   **The wording of tasks:**
    *   Instructions: One step at a time.
    *   Duplicate oral tasks in writing (board, card).
    *   Use clear verbs: “emphasize”, “write down”, “count”.
*   **Lesson structure:**
    *   Start with a brief plan of the lesson.
    *   Alternate activity every 15–20 minutes: letter, oral answers, group work, video.
*   **Organization of space:**
    *   Accommodation: first-second desk, minimization of abstracts.
    *   Involvement: short interactive tasks that require a quick reaction (signal cards). Use strong self -control.
*   **IOM:** Consider the official consolidation of the use of the timer, visual schedules and written instructions.

## 4. Training

The proposed exercises are integrated into everyday life, contributing to the development of skills.

*   **Attention:**
    *   **Correct test:** Crossing out a given letter in the text (start with 1-2 paragraphs).
    *   **Find the differences:** Classic tasks with the search for differences in pictures.
    *   **Labyrinths:** Passing the labyrinths (first with a finger, then with the eyes).

*   **Working memory:**
    *   **"I put in a bag ...":** Oral game of repetition and adding elements.
    *   **"Remember and draw":** Reproduction of a figure of 3-4 elements after 10-second viewing.
    *   **Shopping List:** Search for 3-4 products in the store according to the list.

*   **Rhythm and control (the connection of the brain and body):**
    *   **"Rhythmic echo":** The exact repetition of a given rhythm.
    *   **Games with the ball:** Throwing the ball while performing verbal tasks.
    *   **"Delay!":** Instant stopping at the signal, training of conscious braking.
