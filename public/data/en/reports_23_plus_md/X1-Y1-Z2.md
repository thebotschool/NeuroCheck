# Personal report: cognitive insights

This report reveals unique cognitive features. We analyze the strengths and areas for development in order to create an optimal environment for training and realization of potential.

---

### 1. Results: Key skills

*   **Working and strategy test (CPT):** The ability to hold information, plan and save focus. Mature and organized thinking was noted.
*   **Braking and self-control test (Go/No-Go):** High self -control. The ability to quickly and accurately make decisions, maintaining calm in dynamic situations.
*   **Test for stability of attention (TCP):** Difficulties with a long focus on monotonous tasks. Attention can be quickly exhausted, reducing concentration.

**Features of the profile:** A unique combination is observed. Strong "quick" functions - analysis, decision -making, strategy. But there is a vulnerability to monotony. This is not a drawback, but an important feature.

### 2. The guide for the success of your student

#### Your unique style

*   **Pace:** "Sprint". Maximum productivity in short, intense sessions. Several short approaches are more effective than one long.
*   **Perception:** Optimally assimilates structured and logical information. Visual formats are effective: diagrams, tables, mental cards, checklists.
*   **Focus:** Selective and intensive. High concentration on complex, dynamic tasks (cases, debate). Fast focus loss with monotony or routine.

#### Effective approaches

*   **Interval work:** The Method Methods (25/5) is perfect.
*   **Call motivation:** Clear terms, competition, problem-oriented tasks.
*   **Visualization:** Glider, boards of tasks (Trello, notion), stickers for progress.
*   **Change of activity:** Alternating tasks (reading, practice, planning).

#### Obstacles for the focus

*   **Monotone:** Long lectures, unstructured tasks.
*   **Duration:** Long sessions reduce efficiency and cause fatigue.
*   **Obscurity:** The absence of a goal and feedback reduces motivation.
*   **Lack of structure:** Continuous text without formatting.

### 3. Practical recommendations

#### Home environment

##### Work on tasks

*   **Separation:** Large tasks (exchange rate, exam) are divided into measurable steps for 25-30 minutes.
*   **Start:** Start with the most interesting part for quick inclusion.

##### Support

*   **Structure:** Help plan the work and break it into stages. This is the basis for independent implementation.
*   **Breaks:** Provide short active pauses (walk, exercises, water).

##### Promotion

*   **Confession:** Mark the result, speed and creativity, not perseverance.

##### Expectations

*   **Foc-limit:** A productive focus on the task is about 25 minutes. Plan breaks.

#### Education and career

##### Setting tasks

*   **Format:** Apply the formats "cases", "projects", "problems". Shift the focus with simple playback for analysis and solution.
*   Tasks should stimulate analysis, comparison and decision -making.

##### Material feed

*   **Structure:** Use information blocks, clear conclusions, presentations, diagrams, infographics.
*   **Landmarks:** In the beginning, designate the plan and the purpose of the lesson. This will keep attention.

##### Interaction

*   **Role:** Effective in discussions, brainstorming, group projects. Strengths: generation of ideas, fast analytics.
*   Give him leadership or analytical roles in the team.

##### Personalization

*   **Choice:** Offer the options for the format of work (presentation instead of an essay).
*   **Projects:** Introduce project activities where he can manage the process and be responsible for the result.

### 4. Cognitive skill training

These exercises develop the stability of attention and strengthen the strengths.

#### Focus

*   **Help+:** Start with 15-20 minutes. Gradually increase the interval by 1-2 minutes weekly, up to 30 minutes. This strengthens a long focus.

#### Coordination

*   **Juggling:** (2-3 goals) develops concentration, peripheral vision, and the brain-tel. Removes stress.

#### Strategy

*   **Games:** Chess, go, complex board games. They activate planning, analysis and flexibility of thinking without monotony.

#### Rhythm and stress relief

*   **Drinking tools:** Develop rhythm, coordination. A great way to relieve voltage, improving concentration.
