### **Cognitive assessment report**
*Your path to revealing potential.*

This report is an analysis of the cognitive functions of the student (23+ years) based on complex testing, including an assessment of visual working memory, braking function (GO/No-go) and stability of attention (TCP). The goal is to identify strengths and provide practical recommendations for optimal development and effective learning.

---

### **1. Key results**

*   **Visual working memory:** The ability to effectively hold and process complex information. High level of strategic thinking and forecasting.
*   **Impulse control (Go/No-Go):** Current data indicate difficulties in containing instant reactions. This may be due to overwork, stress or high load.
*   **Sustainability of attention (TCP):** High concentration on the details and accuracy when performing clear and interesting tasks.

**General conclusion:** The student demonstrates outstanding abilities in the field of memory and attention. However, under the influence of external factors (overload, stress), manifestations of impulsivity are possible. The brain is capable of intensive "acceleration", but requires the development of "braking" mechanisms.

### **2. Profile of a student**

#### **Features of training**

*   **Pace:** Effective and quick work. Risk of errors due to haste. Priority is the depth of study, not speed.
*   **Perception:** Optimally assimilates structured, logically constructed information. Visual schemes, plans, tables are preferred.
*   **Attention:** Capable of deep immersion in interest in tasks. However, fatigue or stress can cause impulsive actions and errors.

#### **Efficiency support**

*   **Check lists:** Step -by -step instructions contribute to deceleration and self -control.
*   **Pause for understanding:** A short pause (for example, deep breaths) before the start of action helps to reduce impulsiveness.
*   **Clear rules:** Environmental prediction facilitates self -regulation.
*   **Mini-shifts:** Short pauses between tasks restore the resource of self -control.

#### **Obstacles**

*   **Multitasking:** The implementation of several cases at the same time almost always leads to a decrease in quality.
*   **Pressure:** Emotional stress and criticism of errors exacerbate impulsivity.
*   **Speed ​​work:** Tasks with a rigid time limit can cause frustration and errors.
*   **Labor lack:** The deficiency of sleep and a good rest directly reduces the ability to self -control.

<!-- page-break -->

### **3. Practical recommendations**

#### **Home environment**

*   **Homework:**
    *   Divide large tasks into 2-3 small blocks. After each block - 5-10 minutes a break.
    *   Form a simple plan: "1. Read. 2. Reply. 3. Check."
    *   Agree to allocate the last 5 minutes to verify the completed. It trains self -control.
*   **Support:**
    *   Focus on efforts, and not just on errors. Question: "What can be done in order to not rush next time?"
    *   Encourage the process of self -testing: "It is great that you checked yourself before completing."
*   **Attention:**
    *   Effective work on a difficult task is up to 30–40 minutes. Then a break is needed to restore the control resource.

#### **School process**

*   **The wording of tasks:**
    *   Tasks should be clumsy, with clear criteria. Priority is accuracy and depth, not speed.
    *   Include tasks requiring planning (for example, an essay according to plan, multi -stage tasks).
*   **The structure of the lesson and control:**
    *   On control, allocate 5-10 minutes for self -testing, reminding this.
    *   Use visual supports (lesson plan, terms, diagrams) on the board.
*   **Accommodation:**
    *   Optimal: the first rows, away from windows and doors to minimize distracting factors. This will direct strong attention to the teacher.
*   **Material feed:**
    *   Calm, structured style, emphasis on logical connections. Avoid the competitive pace and chaotic change of activity.
*   **Individual route:**
    *   Offer project activities where you can show strategic thinking and deep concentration.

### **4. Developing exercises**

These activity contribute to the development of self -control and a decrease in stress.

*   **Focus and control:**
    *   **The game "Yes/no, don't say ...":** He trains awareness and control of automatic reactions.
    *   **Listen to the rhythm:** Testing rhythm with a stop at a signal. Develops a braking reaction.
*   **Coordination and rhythm:**
    *   **Juggling:** Improves visual-motor coordination and control of movements.
    *   **Musical instruments:** The game on drums or guitar develops a sense of rhythm and synchronization.
*   **Thinking and strategy:**
    *   **Chess, go, board games:** They teach to plan actions before performing them, and realize the consequences of hasty decisions.
*   **State regulation:**
    *   **Respiratory exercises:** Mastering "square breathing" (inhale, delay, exhale, delay of 4 seconds). Effective for stress relief.
    *   **Static exercises:** A bar or “chair” near the wall is taught to withstand the tension and control the body.
