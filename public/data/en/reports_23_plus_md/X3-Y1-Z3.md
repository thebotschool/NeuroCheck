## 2. Individual learning profile

### Peculiarities

*   **Pace.** A clear understanding leads to accuracy. Confusion or overload - to speed. The average pace with complex tasks is rare.
*   **Perception.** Effectively assimilates step -by -step instructions. Extensive, unstructured blocks of information can be overload.
*   **Focus.** Deep concentration on one task. The retention of several unrelated elements simultaneously reduces the focus.

### Optimal conditions

*   Step -by -step algorithms of action.
*   Visual means: checklists, schemes, numbered lists.
*   Prioritization of planning before execution.
*   Predictability: a clear rhythm and structure.

### Factors that reduce efficiency

*   Multitasking.
*   Long oral instructions.
*   Hurrying or excessive pressure.
*   Unstructured material.

## 3. Recommendations

### Home environment

*   **Separation of tasks.** Divide large tasks into small stages.
*   **Time management.** Use the timer: 20-25 minutes of work, 5-7 minutes break.
*   **Check lists.** Fix the completed cases.
*   **Focus praise.** Mark accuracy and following the plan, not speed.
*   **Pace regulation.** When hurry, offer a pause and a double -check.
*   **Optimal interval.** High-quality concentration up to 25-30 minutes on an understandable task. Further, a change of activity is needed.

### School environment

*   **Crushing instructions.** Formulate tasks as numbered lists:
    1.  Read the text on paragraphs.
    2.  Emphasize the key thought.
    3.  Answer the questions.
*   **Visualization.** Use lists, schemes, arrows to demonstrate a sequence.
*   **Order.** Make information logically (from left to right, from top to bottom).
*   **Location.** Recommended place: first or second desk in the center. This minimizes distraction and provides contact with the teacher.
*   **Personalization.** Offer written plans for lessons and checklists for projects.
*   **Concentration tools.** Allow me to use a line for tracking a line when reading.
*   **Extra time.** In control work, provide time for verification and reconciliation with a plan, and not just for solving.

## 4. Recommended training

These exercises contribute to the development of key skills in interactive form.

### Attention and self -control

*   **"Delay!".** Develops a quick reaction and bodily control.
*   **Board games.** Checkers, chess, karcasson. Training patience and strategy.

### Coordination and rhythm

*   **Rhythmic exercises.** The beat of the rhythm, gradually complicating.
*   **Graphic dictations.** Exercises such as "two cells to the right, one up." Develop attention to the sequence.

### Memory and sequence

*   **"Find a couple."** Training of visual memory.
*   **Retelling along the chain.** Reproduction of short stories while maintaining the order of events.
*   **Creation of instructions.** Development of instructions for simple actions (for example, brew tea).

### Bodily control

*   **Respiratory exercises.** Technique "Breathing on a square" before complex tasks. Promotes slowdown and concentration.
