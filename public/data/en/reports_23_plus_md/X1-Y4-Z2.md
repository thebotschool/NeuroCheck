# Cognitive profile

This report is an analysis of the cognitive functions of the student. It is designed to identify strengths and designate zones for development, offering practical recommendations for creating an optimal environment of training and growth.

## 1. Test results

*   **Visual working memory:** The exceptional ability to retain and process visual information. It manifests itself in strategic thinking and effective work with large volumes of data, even with distraction.
*   **Impulse control (Go/No-Go):** Difficulties with inhibitor control have been noted. In conditions of fatigue or stress, impulsive reactions are possible, leading to errors.
*   **Sustainability of attention (CPT):** The concentration of attention demonstrates instability and rapid exhaustion. Especially expressed in monotonous or long -term tasks, which can lead to focus loss and missing parts.

## 2. The guide for the success of your student

### Individual characteristics

*   **Pace:** Intensive, but short -term periods of high intellectual activity are characteristic. Monotonous or prolonged work leads to rapid depletion. Rhythm and regular pauses are necessary.
*   **Perception format:** The optimal assimilation of information occurs through visual and structural formats: diagrams, tables, intelligence cards, step-by-step instructions.
*   **Focus of attention:** Attention is intense, but short. Deep immersion in the task is possible for 15–20 minutes, after which the productivity is reduced. This is a feature of cognitive endurance.

### Supporting factors

*   **Visual supports:** Check lists, schemes, highlighting key points, brief video instructions.
*   **Separation of tasks:** Decomposition of large tasks on small, controlled steps.
*   **Timer and rhythm:** The use of a timer (for example, the technique of prying) to regulate the focus and prevent burnout.
*   **Movement:** Short physical pauses for "rebooting" attention.

### Obstacles

*   **Multitasking:** Simultaneous processing of information (listen, record, think) quickly depleted the cognitive resource.
*   **Monotone:** Long -term lectures, monotonous exercises or continuous reading cause a loss of concentration.
*   **Rush and noise:** The pressure of time and background distraction provoke impulsive answers and errors.
*   **Faring instructions:** Abstract or volumetric tasks require additional efforts to understand the essence.

## 3. Recommendations

### Home environment

*   **Organization of the educational process:**
    *   Plan classes in short intervals (20–25 minutes) with mandatory 5-minute breaks.
    *   Use breaks for physical activity, not for gadgets.
    *   Start with the most difficult tasks during the maximum energy.
*   **Support and motivation:**
    *   Focus on efforts and process, and not just on the result. Example: "I see how you try to focus - this is excellent progress."
    *   Recognize fatigue. Avoid a pause if the resource is depleted.
*   **Working space:**
    *   Provide the minimalistic environment: on the table only necessary for the current task. Remove distracting objects.
    *   Use visual tools: boards, glider for tracking progress.

### School environment

*   **Instructions and tasks:**
    *   Formulate the tasks as clearly as possible, step by step. Duplicate oral instructions in writing.
    *   Break large work into logical sections.
*   **Lesson structure:**
    *   Alternate formats: short lectures (5-7 minutes), practical tasks, paired discussions.
    *   Place the student closer to the board, minimizing distracting factors.
*   **Material feed:**
    *   Actively use visual materials: schemes, tables, infographics, intelligence cards for explanation.
*   **Individual approach:**
    *   Allow the use of a timer for self -control of the time.
    *   Give the opportunity for short "restarts" (for example, to go drink water).
    *   Select the time (2-3 minutes) at the end of the work to check for impulsive errors (typos, passes).

## 4. Practical training

The following exercises and games contribute to the development of attention and self -control.

*   **For attention and concentration:**
    *   **Schultte tables:** Classic exercise to improve focus and peripheral vision.
    *   **Board games:** "Cortex", "vibble", "set" - develop a quick reaction and concentration.
*   **For coordination and control:**
    *   **Juggling:** Start with two goals. Develops visual-motor coordination and multitasking control.
    *   **Balance exercises:** Standing on one leg, walking along the line. Combines with closed eyes.
*   **For memory and rhythm:**
    *   **Rhythmic echo:** Reproduction of rhythmic sequences. He trains sequence and auditory attention.
*   **To control impulsiveness:**
    *   **"Yes, and there is no talk":** Develops conscious control over speech and suppressing automatic answers.
