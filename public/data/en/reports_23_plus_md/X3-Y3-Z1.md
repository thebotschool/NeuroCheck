**Cognitive profile: report**

This report analyzes the results of cognitive testing, offering a deep understanding of the strengths and growth zones, as well as practical approaches to support and develop a student’s potential.

---

### **1. Key results**

*   **Test for attention (CPT/TCP):** Great concentration and self -control. The ability to long, productive work without abstracts.
*   **Braking Test (Go/No-Go):** Minor asymmetry in the speed of the reaction between the hands. This can affect the rapid adoption of non -standard decisions in conditions of stress.
*   **Test of visual working memory:** Memory for images is well developed. Feature: Difficulties with accurate memorization of the sequence. Information is absorbed as a set of facts, and not as related logic.

<!-- page-break -->

### **2. The guide for the success of your student**

#### Individual profile

*   **Pace:** The most productive in a stable, predictable regime. Hurry and sharp changes reduce accuracy.
*   **Perception:** The preference of visual information (scheme, graphics), if it is clearly structured.
*   **Focus of attention:** Very high. Deep immersion in tasks, effective work during information load.

#### Optimal approaches

*   **Step -by -step instructions:** Clear algorithms and checklists compensate for the features of memorizing sequences.
*   **Visual supports:** Mental cards, timline, diagrams and tables streamline information.
*   **Predictability:** Famous rules and calm environment maximize effectiveness.
*   **Rhythm:** Rhythmic, repeated actions (for example, under the metronome) improve coordination and reaction.

#### Zones of attention

*   **Multivature in real time:** Simultaneous processing of complex information (lecture and abstract) is difficult. Focusing on one is recommended.
*   **Unstracted stream:** Large volumes of text or oral lectures without a clear plan and visual support are absorbed fragmented.
*   **Time deficiency and pressure:** The need for instant solutions can lead to errors due to the asymmetry of the reaction speed.

<!-- page-break -->

### **3. Recommendations**

#### Home support

*   **Work on tasks:**
    *   Any volumetric task begins with the creation of a plan or "road map".
    *   For new material, use markers, stickers, schemes for a visual organization.
    *   Apply the Pomodoro technique (25 min. Work, 5 minutes of rest) to maintain concentration.
*   **Motivation and environment:**
    *   Encourage for methodology and following the plan, not speed.
    *   Provide a calm, organized educational environment.
*   **Attention management:**
    *   It is able to hold the focus until 45-60 minutes. After that, a short break is recommended (5-10 minutes).

#### In the educational process

*   **Clarity of tasks:**
    *   Provide tasks in writing, with clear division into points.
    *   Substinate the evaluation criteria in advance.
*   **Material feed:**
    *   Lectures and lessons to accompany presentations with key theses and schemes.
    *   Give a lecture plan or its brief theses before class.
*   **Individual route:**
    *   For design work, recommend planning tools (Trello, Notion, checklists).
    *   In case of oral answers, give time to think, avoiding questions requiring an instant reaction.

<!-- page-break -->

### **4. Recommended training**

These practices will strengthen the strengths and develop growth zones.

*   **Memory and structure:**
    *   **"History by the pictures":** Make a logical story for 5-7 unrelated images.
    *   **Personal of the plot:** After watching/reading the film/Books, make a short plot plan and retell it, preserving the sequence.
*   **Coordination and control:**
    *   **Juggling:** Start with two balls, gradually increasing the complexity. Synchronizes the work of the hemispheres.
    *   **Tool game:** Especially the piano or guitar, where both hands perform different, but coordinated actions.
    *   **Balansboard or yoga:** Balance exercises improve bodily control and reaction.
*   **Rhythm and focus:**
    *   **Dancing:** Removing and reproduction of sequences of movements (salsa, bachata).
    *   **Board strategic games:** Chess, go - develop concentration and strategic thinking.
