### Cognitive Assessment: Report

This report is an analysis of the cognitive functions of the student based on the results of CPT, Go/No-Go tests and the assessment of visual working memory. It is designed to help parents and teachers to form an optimal environment for training and development.

#### 1. Detailed test analysis

**Continous Performance Test (CPT)**
The test indicates difficulties with prolonged attention and memorization of consistent actions. This is often associated with fatigue or stress, and not with the lack of abilities.

**Go/No-Go Test**
The results indicate difficulties with self -control and impulsivity. This is a consequence of the depletion of cognitive resources.

**Visual Working Memorial Test**
The test confirms the overall picture of the overload. Attention is unstable, which prevents the effective processing and retention of visual information.

**General conclusion**
All tests indicate **cognitive overload and exhaustion**. The main task now is to restore mental forces. The student needs rest and a competent recovery strategy.

#### 2. Individual approach to training

This section helps to understand how the student perceives the information best and what contributes to his effectiveness.

##### Features of perception

*   **Pace:** A slow, measured pace is required. Fast supply of information reduces the quality of perception.
*   **Format:** Information is better absorbed if it is brief, structured and visual. Long texts or monologues are less effective.
*   **Attention:** The period of productive concentration is 15–20 minutes. After that, a break is needed.

##### Supporting factors

*   **Step -by -step instructions:** Check lines and detailed steps simplify the performance of tasks.
*   **Visual supports:** Schemes, Mayind cards, color allocation and short lists help to unload the working memory.
*   **Short breaks:** Effective technique is 20 minutes of work / 5 minutes of rest (for example, a prying). The break should be active, without gadgets.
*   **Calm Wednesday:** Minimization of distracting factors improves concentration.

##### Factors that impede training

*   **Multitasking:** The simultaneous performance of several actions reduces productivity.
*   **Time pressure:** The compressed terms and speed requirements create stress and block mental processes.
*   **Unstructured information:** Large volumes of text or lectures without pauses are perceived as a background noise.
*   **Uncertainty:** Faring or general tasks require unnecessary mental efforts at the initial stage.

#### 3. Recommendations to support

Effective support helps to restore resources and improve performance.

##### For the house

**Organization of the work process**
*   **The principle of "one subject":** One table is one textbook, one notebook. Remove all distracting objects.
*   **Crushing of tasks:** Divide big tasks into small stages. Between the stages, take short breaks.
*   **Fixed time:** Use the timer. Several short approaches are 20 minutes more effective than long work without a break.

**Support and motivation**
*   **Reduced pressure:** Priority - restoration and well -being, not assessment.
*   **Praise for efforts:** Mark the zeal, planning and attempts to solve complex problems. This strengthens stability.
*   **Full rest:** Provide 8-9 hours of sleep, a balanced diet and time without gadgets, especially an hour before bedtime.

##### For school

**The formulation of tasks**
*   **Definition:** Use the verbs of action. Example: "1. Read paragraph 2. Write 3 key dates. 3. Answer the question No. 5."
*   **Duplication:** Important information (for example, homework) should be voiced and recorded (on the board, in an electronic diary).

**Organization of the educational process**
*   **Place in the class:** Put the student at the first or second desk, away from distracting factors.
*   **Lesson visual plan:** Start a lesson with a brief plan on the board. This creates predictability.

**Elements of an individual route**
*   **Reduced load:** If possible, reduce the number of homework of the same type.
*   **Additional time:** Give 10-15 minutes of additional time in control and independent work.
*   **Micropauzes:** Allow short breaks (1-2 minutes to leave the class or close your eyes) if necessary.

#### 4. Practical training

The purpose of the exercises is to restore resources and improve self -regulation through game activity.

##### Development of attention and self -control

*   **Board games:** Games as “Dobble”, “Seth”, “Uno” develop a reaction in a relaxed form.
*   **Graphic dictations:** Improve hearing and action, train inhibition and program.

##### Memory training and sequence

*   **The game "Memory":** Classical game for training visual memory.
*   **Removing lists:** Start with simple lists (3-5 items), gradually increasing the number and intervals of repetition.

##### Coordination and bodily control

*   **Juggling:** Start with two goals. Perfectly synchronizes the hemispheres of the brain and relieves mental stress.
*   **Balance:** Exercises on one leg or walking along the line help to "ground" and improve bodily control.

##### Rhythm to stabilize the nervous system

*   **Tapping rhythms:** Reproduction of the specified rhythms develops the internal "clock" of the body.
*   **Rhythmic gymnastics:** Any activity to music with a clear rhythm promotes harmonization.
