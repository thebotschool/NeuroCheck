# Cognitive profile

This report offers a detailed analysis of the cognitive characteristics of the student, covering attention, control and memory. The goal is to provide parents and teachers with tools for creating an optimal training environment.

---

# 1. Profile assessment

### Sustainability of attention (CPT)
The accuracy of processing similar visual data. Visual attention is prone to fatigue, which leads to confusion of parts.

### Self-control (Go/No-Go)
Exceptional control of impulses. The ability to follow the rules and timely regulate actions is a sign of mature self -regulation.

### Visual working memory
The concentration decreases over time. A strong initial focus is exhausted in monotonous tasks.

# 2. Success Guide

The schoolboy demonstrates the profile of the “sprinter”: the ability to powerful and accurate jerks. Energy is consumed quickly at long -term distances.

### Key features

*   **Pace:** Optimal in short, intense intervals (15–25 minutes). Reboot is required. Monotonous work causes exhaustion.
*   **Perception format:** Preference is a clear, structured, visually “pure” information. Avoid overloaded formats that cause “cognitive noise”.
*   **Focus of attention:** Attention as a resource: a full charge at the beginning of the task provides self -control. Exposure without interruption leads to errors from fatigue.

### Supporting environment

*   **Step -by -step instructions:** One task, one instruction. Effective checks and algorithms are effective.
*   **Visual anchors:** Selection of keywords, headlines, lists, infographics. Help focus on the main one.
*   **Method Methods:** Short work sessions (for example, 25 minutes of work, 5 minutes of rest).
*   **Clear rules and criteria:** A clear definition of “can” and “cannot” maximizes self -control.

### Obstacles to effectiveness

*   **Multitasking:** The simultaneous performance of tasks leads to errors.
*   **Long oral instructions:** Only the beginning and end are absorbed.
*   **Visual noise:** Overloaded working materials (small font, lack of fields, abundance of images).
*   **Long expectation:** Passive activity tires a control system.

# 3. Recommendations

### For the house

#### Homework
*   Miss large tasks into specific, small steps. Example: “Solve three problems”, a break, “Solve the next two”.
*   Use a timer to control work intervals. This reduces internal stress.
*   Start with the most difficult tasks while attention is as active as possible.

#### Support and promotion
*   Mark efforts and process, and not just the result. Example: "You have focused perfectly", "You followed the plan."
*   Avoid censure for errors from fatigue. Offer the rest: “You're tired. Let's get back later. "

#### Organization of space
*   Provide a minimalist workspace. Remove distracting objects and gadgets.

### For school

#### The formulation of tasks
*   Use the imperative mood: “Read. Highlight. Write down ". Avoid complex designs.
*   Provide the tasks in writing (board, cards), supplementing oral instructions.

#### The structure of the lesson
*   Alternate the formats of the lesson: short lectures (5-7 minutes), discussions, practical tasks, theoretical blocks.
*   Recommended place: first-second desk to minimize distraction.

#### Individual route
*   Consider the possibility of short "micro -barrels" (for example, get out of the class for a minute).
*   Offer structured notes or intelligence cards. This reduces the visual load.

# 4. Training exercises

Exercises strengthen the weaknesses and enhance the advantages. Regularity and game approach are important.

### For stability of attention

#### Corrective tests
Crossing the target letters in the text during the time. Complication: delete “a”, circle “o”.

#### Find the differences
Develops visual accuracy and memory of the reference image.

### For visual memory and load reduction

#### Mnemonics
The "Palace of Memory" method to remember lists. It turns abstract information into visual images, facilitating processing.

#### Puzzles and models
Complex puzzles and models (Lego Technic). They require long -term attention, motivate a visible result.

### For rhythm and control

#### Musical instruments
Drinking instruments develop rhythm, coordination, and control over time.

#### Juggling
Synchronizes the hemispheres, develops concentration and peripheral vision.

#### Game sports
Table tennis, badminton: fast reaction, switching attention, accurate control control. Use strengths (Go/No-Go).
