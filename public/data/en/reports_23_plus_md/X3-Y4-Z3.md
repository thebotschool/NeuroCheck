### Cognitive profile of a student: a detailed report (23+ years)

This report is based on the results of cognitive testing of your student. It is designed to ensure a clear understanding of individual characteristics, strengths and areas for development, offering specific support strategies in the educational process and everyday life.

<!-- page-break -->

### 1. Key test indicators

*   **Test for attention (CPT):** There is a tendency towards a quick, gusty reaction. Making decisions can occur until all the necessary information is obtained, which indicates high impulsiveness.
*   **Self-control test (Go/No-Go):** Difficulties with inhibiting undesirable actions have been noted. The control function can be depleted, especially with fatigue or performing monotonous tasks.
*   **Test of visual working memory:** High ability to memorize visual images and facts. However, confusion may occur in their sequence, which indicates the priority of "what" above "in what order."

<!-- page-break -->

### 2. Profile of educational activity

#### Individual characteristics

*   **Pace:** Fast, gusty. The "sprint" mode is characteristic: a quick start, but the risk of errors due to haste or quick fatigue.
*   **Perception:** Mostly visual. The best assimilation of information through vivid, specific images. Difficulties with understanding sequences and linear instructions.
*   **Attention:** Unstable. Attention is easily attracted, but it is difficult to keep on one task for more than 15–20 minutes. Sensitivity to external distracting factors.

#### Effective approaches

*   **Visual supports:** The use of checklists, step-by-step plans with images, diagrams, intelligence cards. Everything that can be seen, and not just hear.
*   **Clear rhythm:** Work in short intervals with mandatory breaks (for example, 20 minutes of work / 5 minutes of outdoor activities). Timer as an auxiliary tool.
*   **Predictability:** Clear rules and a pre -voiced plan of the lesson or day. Knowledge of subsequent steps reduces anxiety and increases control.
*   **Focus on one task:** A clear setting of the goal: "Now we only write. After that we will draw."

#### Factors that impede training

*   **Multitasking:** Attempts to fulfill several things at the same time (“Listen, write down and look”) lead to disorganization and errors.
*   **Long oral instructions:** Overload of the audio channel. Only the beginning and end of the instruction is remembered, the main part is lost.
*   **Time pressure:** Phrases like "Come on faster!", "Everyone has already finished!" enhance impulsiveness and provoke errors.
*   **Visual and audial noise:** Loud sounds, a mess on the table, active movement in the field of view distracting and exhausting the resource of attention.

<!-- page-break -->

### 3. Practical recommendations

#### For the home environment

*   **Organization of work:**
    *   Miss large tasks into 2-3 small subtasibles. Use stickers or planes.
    *   Use a timer for working intervals (15-20 minutes). After the signal-a mandatory 5-minute break (active rest).
    *   Before starting work, pronounce the plan together: "What are we doing first? And then what?"
*   **Support and motivation:**
    *   Praise not for speed, but for **Compliance with the plan and attentiveness**. Example: "Great, I see, you checked yourself before the surrender!"
    *   With errors caused by a rush, do not criticize. Analyze the situation together: "Why do you think it happened? Next time, we will try to read the task completely first."
*   **Development of self -regulation:**
    *   Use pause games in everyday life: "Frozen!", "The sea is worried once."
    *   Ask to retell a short instruction before the execution: "So, what needs to be done now?"

#### For the school environment

*   **The clarity of instructions:**
    *   Let's briefly, clearly and step by step. It is recommended to duplicate the oral instructions on the board or individual card.
    *   Example: instead of "Open textbooks on page 45, read the rule in the frame, and then perform exercises 5 and 6" - "Step 1: Open p. 45. Step 2: Read the rule in the frame. Step 3: Complete Except 5."
*   **Organization of space:**
    *   Place the student behind a desk in the center of the class, closer to the board, away from windows and doors.
    *   Use the visual schedule on the board (cards with stages).
    *   Give the opportunity to use a draft for planning an answer, especially in written works.
*   **Individual settings:**
    *   Providing additional time for the performance of control and independent work.
    *   Permission to use a ruler or finger to track the line when reading.
    *   The use of helpers with algorithms (for example, "a plan to analyze a word in composition").

### 4. The proposed exercises

*   **Attention and control:**
    *   **Graphic dictations:** "One cage to the right, two up ...". He trains the retention of instructions and step -by -step execution.
    *   **The game "edible is inedible":** Develops a rapid reaction and inhibit.
    *   **Correct samples:** Exercises for crossing one letter (for example, "a") and bypassing another (for example, "o") in tables.
*   **Memory and sequence:**
    *   **Game "Find a couple" (Memory):** Improves visual memory.
    *   **Compilation of stories by a series of pictures:** Lay out 3-4 pictures and ask to make a coherent story. Develops logical and temporary ties.
    *   **Board games-"Walking":** They train the score, following the rules and denigration.
*   **Motor control and rhythm:**
    *   **Balance exercises:** Walking along the line, a stand on one leg, a balancing board. It improves the connection between the brain and the body, indirectly helping with impulsiveness.
    *   **Removing or exhausting rhythms:** You set a simple rhythm, the student repeats. Develops a sense of rhythm and the ability to serial organization of movements.
