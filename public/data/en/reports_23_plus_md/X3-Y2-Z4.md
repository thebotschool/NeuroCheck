**Cognitive assessment: review and recommendations**
*Age group: 23+ years old*

---

### 1. Overview of cognitive functions

*   **Working memory (CPT):** The ability to memorize individual elements is expressed. Difficulties in building information in a consistent structure are noted.
*   **Impulsive control (Go/No-Go):** The tendency to impulsive actions has been revealed. The development of the ability to preliminary comprehension of tasks is required.
*   **Sustainability of attention (TCP):** There is a state of overload. Attention is unstable, quickly exhausted.

<!-- page-break -->

### 2. Personal approach to training

#### Features of the profile

*   **Pace:** The natural pace is fast, impulsive. Efficiency improves with measured rhythm and pauses for understanding.
*   **Perception:** The most effectively perceives visually structured information. Preference is given to clear formats, short texts.
*   **Attention:** Unstable, quickly depleted. The duration of the concentration on the task is 15–20 minutes. This indicates cognitive overload.

#### Learning support

*   **Step -by -step instructions:** Check lists, algorithms, numbered lists reduce anxiety and increase productivity.
*   **Visual supports:** Schemes, intelligence cards, marker allocation, short videos contribute to better assimilation and maintenance of information.
*   **Rule "Stop-think-make":** The development of this technique helps to make deliberate decisions and avoid mistakes.
*   **Short sessions:** The use of the “Modor” method (15–20 minutes of work, 5 minutes of break) significantly increases efficiency.
*   **Predictability:** A clear daily routine and rules reduce the cognitive load, freeing resources for training.

#### Factors that impede training

*   **Multitasking:** The simultaneous performance of several tasks reduces effectiveness and promotes overwork.
*   **Oral instructions:** Long oral instructions without visual support are perceived fragmented.
*   **Rush and temporary pressure:** Enhance impulsiveness and lead to an increase in errors.
*   **Distracting factors:** Noise and external stimuli significantly interfere with concentration due to the state of overload.

<!-- page-break -->

### 3. Practical recommendations

#### Home environment

*   **Task planning:** Break large tasks by 3-4 subtasks. Use a timer for work intervals (20 minutes of work / 5 minutes of rest). Speak the implementation plan before starting.
*   **Support:** Focus on efforts and process, and not just on the result. Create a calm atmosphere. This will allow the nervous system to recover.
*   **Concentration:** The optimal concentration time on a difficult task is 15–20 minutes. After that, a short break is needed.

#### School environment

*   **Instructions:** Present the tasks step by step, one action at a time. Duplicate oral instructions in writing (board, card).
*   **Structure:** Offer templates for work. Surrender a clear plan of the lesson at the beginning.
*   **Accommodation:** The first-second desk, the central location for minimizing distracting factors is recommended.
*   **IOM:** Additional time on control, use of plans for an answer, evaluation of the process.

<!-- page-break -->

### 4. Developing activity

#### Concentration and self -control

*   **The game "Delay!":** Instant command stop while driving.
*   **Board games:** “Jenga”, “Uno”, chess, checkers - develop patience and strategic thinking.
*   **Cooking:** Accurate following the recipe trains control and sequence.

#### Memory and sequence

*   **Compilation of comics:** Draw 3-4 key frames according to the plot - teaches to highlight the main thing and build logic.
*   **The game "I put in a bag ...":** Develops a consistent auditory memory.
*   **Retelling of the day:** The discussion of the day in chronological order trains recalling events.

#### Movement and rhythm (to reduce impulsiveness)

*   **Balance:** Standing on one leg, walking along the line - improves physical and cognitive control.
*   **Rhythms:** The exact repetition of simple rhythms (pops, knocking) develops coordination and attention.
