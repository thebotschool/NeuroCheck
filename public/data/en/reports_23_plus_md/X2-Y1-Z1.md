# Cognitive assessment report

This report is designed to reveal the student’s potential, offering targeted support strategies for home and school. The goal is to create a comfortable and effective environment of training.

## 1. The main test results

### Test for attention and memory (CPT)
High concentration. With an abundance of similar visual elements (letters, symbols), confusion is possible. This indicates a tendency to **visual overload**.

### Self-control test (Go/No-Go)
Excellent results. The student demonstrates a high level of self -control, is able to restrain impulsive reactions and follow the rules. This is a strong side.

### Visual Working Memorial Test
High stability of attention is confirmed. The exact completion of tasks, even with increasing complexity. The ability to long -term productive work.

### General conclusion
The schoolboy has high potential, excellent self -control and the ability to concentrate. The key feature is the sensitivity to the visual "noise", which can reduce the accuracy with an excess of similar parts.

## 2. The profile of the student

### Key features
*   **The pace of work**: Calm, measured. Prone to thoughtful performance of tasks without haste. It works comfortably.
*   **Perception of information**: Better assimilates clearly structured information with an emphasis on the main one. Effectively perceives oral instructions. Requires "pure" visual material.
*   **Features of attention**: Deep and stable. The focus can be disrupted by visual overload or the presence of many of the same type, but of different elements.

### Effective strategies
*   **Visual accents**: Highlighting key information with color, frame or emphasizing. Helps to filter important.
*   **Step -by -step instructions**: Dividing large tasks into 2-3 small, clear steps.
*   **Templates and checklists**: Ready -made structures to reduce cognitive load and prevent errors in details.
*   **Rhythm and predictability**: A clear mode of work and rest (for example, 25 minutes of work, 5 minutes of a break).

### Potential barriers
*   **Visual noise**: Chaotic location of the text, images, diagrams on the pages.
*   **Multitasking with visual support**: Simultaneous tracking of several sources of information (for example, debiting from a board and reconciliation with a textbook).
*   **Copying tasks**: Rewriting large volumes of text or redrawing complex schemes, where it is easy to make an error due to the substitution of similar elements.
*   **Fast change of tasks**: A sharp transition between objects without a short pause for "rebooting".

## 3. Practical recommendations

### Home environment
*   **Organization of training**:
    *   Divide volumetric tasks into parts. After performing the block - a short break.
    *   Increase the habit of "first check, then hand over." Use the line to track the line when reading or checking.
    *   When performing tasks in mathematics or Russian, ask to pronounce actions aloud to increase accuracy.
*   **Support and motivation**:
    *   Praise for **Attentiveness and accuracy**, not for speed. Mark when the student finds and corrects his own mistakes.
    *   Projective attention time: **up to 30-35 minutes**. After that, a 5-minute change of activity is required (for example, a warm-up).
*   **Working space**:
    *   Remove from the table all unnecessary items. Only the materials necessary for the current task should have on the table.

### School environment
*   **Instructions for tasks**:
    *   Provide clear, unambiguous, step -by -step instructions.
    *   In control work, highlight keywords in conditions.
*   **Presentation of the material**:
    *   On the board and in handouts, use lists, tables, diagrams. Avoid continuous text.
    *   Leave more free space between the information blocks.
    *   If necessary, provide work sheets with a drawn structure for answers.
*   **Positioning in the class**:
    *   The optimal place is at the first or second desk, minimizing distracting visual factors.
*   **Individual approach**:
    *   Give the opportunity to double -check the work before delivery.
    *   For tasks for rewriting or copying, offer to use sheets-blocks with large and clear characters.

## 4. Developing activity

These exercises contribute to the development of attention, accuracy, memory and coordination.

### For attention and accuracy
*   **"Corrective test"**: Find and cross out certain letters on the text page for a given time. Gradually complicate the task.
*   **"Find the differences"**: Start with simple images, gradually increasing the number of differences. Focus on a methodical search.

### For visual memory
*   **"Memory"**: Classic game for training to memorize the location and details of the images.
*   **"Photographer"**: Memorization of 5-7 items in 30 seconds, then listing them with details.

### For bodily control and coordination
*   **Neurohymnastics**: Exercises with the simultaneous performance of different actions with two hands (for example, “Fist-Robric Ladon”). Develops interhemispheric ties and improves control.

### For rhythm
*   **"Rhythmic echo"**: Reproduction of rhythm, squandered by another person. Develops a sense of sequence.
