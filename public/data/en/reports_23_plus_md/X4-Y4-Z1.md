# Cognitive profile

This report offers a detailed view of the cognitive features of your student. Our recommendations based on testing results will help create the most favorable environment for its development and training.

# 1. Key conclusions

*   **Preservation (CPT):** With prolonged, monotonous tasks, the concentration of attention is reduced. Effectiveness falls if the trick is necessary.
*   **Self-control (Go/No-Go):** The tendency to impulsive reactions is noted. Holding the first impulse requires additional effort, especially with fatigue or severe enthusiasm.
*   **Visual attention (TCP):** High accuracy and concentration when performing short tasks requiring a search on the sample. This demonstrates the strengths in purposeful visual perception.

<!-- page-break -->

# 2. The guide for the success of your student

## Peculiarities

*   **Pace:** "Sprinter". The student demonstrates high performance at short distances, but quickly gets tired of monotonous or long -term activities.
*   **Perception:** "Visual." Information is better absorbed and remembered through visual materials: diagrams, images, color selection. Long oral explanations can be less effective.
*   **Focus:** Selective. Deep immersion in interesting tasks, but an easy distraction to external stimuli (noise, movement).

## What supports

*   **Visual assistants:** Check lines of tasks, timers, highlighting key points.
*   **Step -by -step instructions:** Separation of large tasks into small, specific stages.
*   **Activity:** The possibility of short physical pauses, the use of anti-stress objects for concentration.
*   **Clarity of the goal:** One is a clearly formulated task at every moment in time.

## What makes it difficult

*   **Multitasking:** The simultaneous performance of several actions or the presence of background noise.
*   **Long -term oral instructions:** Long explanations without visual support.
*   **Monotone:** The same type of work without breaks is longer than 10-15 minutes.
*   **Pressure:** Hurry or criticism for impulsiveness enhance the bustle and number of errors.

<!-- page-break -->

# 3. Practical recommendations

## For the house

*   **Homework:**
    *   Divide the work into 15-minute blocks. Between the blocks-5-minute breaks with movement.
    *   Use the timer: "15 minutes of work, then rest." This makes the task visible.
    *   Start with the most complex or less beloved objects while the energy level is high.
*   **Support:**
    *   Mark the effort, not just the result. Appreciate the moments when the schoolboy has shown excerpt or concentration.
    *   Instead of criticism, offer the decision: “Let's try to fix it. Do it a little slower. "
*   **Working space:**
    *   On the table there should be only objects necessary for the current task. Take out all the extra.

## For school

*   **The wording of tasks:**
    *   The instructions should be short, clear, step -by -step. It is advisable to duplicate oral instructions on the board or card.
    *   Example: Instead of a general task, break it into “1. Open the notebooks. 2. Write down the number. 3. Find exercise 5. 4. Read the first task. ”
*   **Lesson structure:**
    *   Alternate activities every 15–20 minutes (work at the board, independent work, physical education, work in pairs).
*   **Class placement:**
    *   The optimal place is the first two desks, closer to the teacher, away from the window or door to minimize distracting factors.
*   **Individual route:**
    *   Allow me to use a line for reading on the lines.
    *   Offer tasks on cards with clearly divided blocks.
    *   To reduce impulsiveness, suggest “Time to think”: “Do not answer right away, read to three.”

# 4. Development through the game

These exercises and games contribute to the development of key skills.

## Attention and perseverance

*   **Graphic dictations:** “One cage to the right, two upwards ...” - develop following instructions and concentration.
*   **"Find 10 differences":** Improves detail and visual attention.
*   **Correct samples:** Discarding a certain letter in the text - trains selective attention.

## Self -control and coordination

*   **"The sea is worried once":** He teaches to freeze by signal, training the inhibition of reactions.
*   **"Edible-Sreedy":** Develops a rapid reaction and its timely cancellation.
*   **Balance exercises:** Walking along the line, a stand on one leg - improve coordination.

## Memory and sequence

*   **"I put in a bag ...":** Repetition and adding elements to the chain - develops auditory memory and sequence.
*   **Rhythm playing:** The exact repetition of the rhythm heard - trains audio memory and attention.
*   **"Remember and draw":** Memory and reproduction of 3-4 figures from memory - develops visual memory and sequence.
