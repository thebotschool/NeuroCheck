# Report on evaluating cognitive abilities

Assessment of abilities in categories: memory, self -control, attention. Age group: 23+ years.

This report allocates strengths and areas for development. Recommendations are designed to improve the learning process and everyday life.

## 1. Overview of the results

*   **Attention (stability):** Mental fatigue is noted. The concentration on one task is unstable.
*   **Self -control (braking):** High ability to restrain impulses. The actions are deliberate in the presence of a resource.
*   **Visual memory (structuring):** Effective memorization of details. Difficulties with building sequences.

<!-- page-break -->

## 2. The profile of the student

### Key features

*   **Pace:** Uneven. Fast exhaustion after intense start. A measured rhythm with short breaks is required.
*   **Perception:** Optimally assimilates visual and structured information. Long oral explanations are ineffective.
*   **Attention:** An unstable focus. Deep concentration is possible for 15–20 minutes, then a change of activity is required.

### Effective approaches

*   Step -by -step, numbered instructions.
*   Visual aids: schemes, intelligence cards, checklists. Help to structure the task.
*   Interval work (15–20 minutes of work, 5 minutes of rest).
*   Propuring an action plan aloud.
*   The predictable environment reduces the cognitive load.

### What makes it difficult

*   Multivativity: leads to exhaustion, errors.
*   Long oral instructions: often lead to errors or failure.
*   Monotonous work without breaks: quickly exhausts the resource of attention.
*   Noise and distraction: they can be removed from the working condition during overload.

<!-- page-break -->

## 3. Practical recommendations

### At home

*   **Organization of tasks**
    *   Apply the rule 15/5: 15 minutes of work, 5 minutes of rest (window, water, movement).
    *   Make a simple written plan. Cross out the executed points for a sensation of control.
    *   Start with difficult tasks. Leave the lungs for later.
*   **Support and motivation**
    *   Praise for the process, not only for the result: “you followed the plan”, “well used the checklist”.
    *   Question: “What is according to the plan? Where will we start? ” Help break the tasks.
*   **Realistic expectations:** Productive concentration on a difficult task is 15-20 minutes. Avoid the requirements of more continuous work.

### At school

*   **The formulation of tasks**
    *   Clear tasks, divided into 1-2 steps.
    *   Duplicate the key stages in writing (board, card).
*   **Structuring the material**
    *   Use schemes, timline, tables, intelligence cards to explain new topics.
    *   Use numbered lists instead of continuous text.
*   **Organization of space**
    *   Place closer to the board/teacher, away from abstracts.
    *   Provide micro-pauses for switching (give out notebooks, wipe the board).
    *   Use self -control for tasks that require accuracy (check, design).

## 4. Developing classes

These exercises will help strengthen the necessary skills. The main thing is regularity and playing form.

### For attention (stability)

*   “Find differences”: develops concentration on the details.
*   Schultte tables: improve the field of view and the speed of information processing. Start with 3x3.
*   "Corrective tests": deleting a given letter in the text. Start from 1 minute, increasing time.

### For memory (sequence)

*   Graphic dictations: Train adherence to step -by -step instructions.
*   “Remember and repeat”: reproduction of the sequence of objects. Gradually increase their number.
*   Retelling from pictures: develops a logical and temporary sequence.

### For bodily control and rhythm (relief of overload)

*   Neurohymnastics: Coordination exercises (for example, “Fist-Robric Ladon”, “Ring”).
*   Games with the ball: a combination of movement with the name of sequences (days of the week, months).
*   Cooling rhythms: develops a sense of rhythm associated with structuring time and actions.
