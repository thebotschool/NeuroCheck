### **Cognitive profile: report**

This report reveals the cognitive profile of your student. Recommendations are designed to optimize training and home support.

---

#### **1. Overview of the profile**

*   **CPT. Attention. Memory.**
    Excellent stability and use of strategies for memorization. The need for focus with a large amount of similar visual details.
*   **Go/No-Go. Self -control.**
    High level of self -regulation. Fast and accurate decision -making.
*   **TCP. Sustainability of attention.**
    Outstanding ability to long and stable concentration. Key skill for academic success.

#### **2. Success Guide**

This description will help to understand how the student interacts with information and which environment is most productive.

**Individual characteristics**

*   **Pace.** Confident and stable. The speed of work is reduced when processing a large volume of small visual parts.
*   **Perception.** It best perceives clear, structured and logical information. The stronger side is an understanding of algorithms. Growth zone - memorization of unrelated visual facts.
*   **Focus of attention.** Very stable and control with understandable tasks. We vulnerable, if necessary, remember many similar elements.

**Supporting environment**

*   **Visual anchors.** Schemes, mental cards, infographics. They structure the information, helping it to absorb.
*   **Check lists.** Step -by -step instructions support a natural tendency to algorithmic thinking.
*   **Mnemonics.** Associations, acronics, stories. Turn abstract data into memorable images.
*   **Strategic approach.** The discussion of the plan before starting work stimulates strategic thinking.

**Limiting factors**

*   **Visual noise.** Excess small text, unstructured notes, chaotic images.
*   **The same type of crammer.** Mechanical memorization without logical ties tires and reduces efficiency.
*   **Oral instructions.** Complex information submitted only by ear complicates the retention and processing.
*   **Multitasking.** Simultaneous listening, recording and tracking the presentation reduces the quality of memorization.

<!-- page-break -->

#### **3. Recommendations**

**Home environment**

*   **Organization of work.**
    *   **Savior tasks.** Divide large tasks into small steps. Use checklists.
    *   **Start with complicated.** Perform difficult tasks in the first 30–40 minutes, when the concentration is maximum.
    *   **Workplace.** A minimum of distracting items. Pure space helps to focus.
*   **Support.**
    *   **Encourage the strategy.** Praise for the approach, and not just for the result. For example: "Great scheme!" Or "well planned time!".
    *   **Use mnemonics.** Turn the memorization of formulas or dates into an exciting game.
*   **Breaks.** Effective work without a break is 30-40 minutes. Short breaks (5-10 minutes) help to "restart" the visual system.

**School environment**

*   **The formulation of tasks.**
    *   Tasks should be chatty. With understandable evaluation criteria.
    *   Formats that require analysis, planning: projects, debate, reports are suitable.
*   **Structuring the material.**
    *   **Visual cleanliness.** Key information on the board/slide must be highlighted and have "air" around.
    *   **From general to particular.** First, the general scheme, then detailing.
    *   **Supports.** Use logical blocks, tables, graphs, intelligence cards.
*   **Style and approach.**
    *   The most effective partnership style of communication. Based on the independence and responsibility of the student.
    *   A special educational route is not required. A high level of self -regulation allows you to recommend participation in olympiads, projects.

#### **4. Development: practical exercises**

These exercises will strengthen visual memory and attention, making the process fascinating.

*   **Attention and visual memory**
    *   **"Memory" or "Find a couple."** Start with 12-16 cards, gradually increasing the number.
    *   **"Corrective test".** Look and cross out certain letters in the text. He trains concentration on the details.
    *   **"Palaces of memory."** Mentally "lay out" the facts in the rooms of the familiar house. Develops associative memory.
*   **Coordination and self -control**
    *   **Neurohymnastics.** Simple exercises for the coordination of hands (for example, "fist-rib-lady"). Activate interhemispheric interaction.
    *   **Juggling.** He trains attention, coordination and peripheral vision.
*   **Rhythm and strategic thinking**
    *   **Board strategic games.** Chess, go, "jackal". Develop planning, concentration.
    *   **Rhythm.** Exercise with a metronome synchronizes the inner rhythm and increases stability.
