# Cognitive assessment: report

The report analyzes the cognitive characteristics of the student. The goal is to reveal its potential.

<!-- page-break -->

## 1. Test results

*   **Attention (CPT):** High concentration. Sustainable attention. Readiness for serious loads.
*   **Self-control (Go/No-Go):** High impulse control. Fast decision -making. Effective brain function.
*   **Visual memory:** Excellent. Strategic memorization. Effective work with information.

<!-- page-break -->

## 2. Guide. Student

Understanding the cognitive characteristics of the student.

### Peculiarities

*   **Pace:** Confident. Often above average. Prefers his own rhythm.
*   **Perception:** Flexible. Oral, textual, visual formats. Complex information processing.
*   **Attention:** High concentration. Sustainable. Capable of deep immersion.

### Which helps

*   Complex, multi -stage tasks: research, projects, debate.
*   Independence, responsibility: Search for solutions.
*   Academic challenge: Tasks with the Star, Olympiad.
*   Reflection: thinking, analysis of work, conclusions.

### What interferes

*   Simple, monotonous tasks: reduce motivation.
*   Microenage, step -by -step control: suppress strategic thinking.
*   Frequent change in simple activities: interferes with deep immersion.
*   Lack of goal: it is important to understand "why."

<!-- page-break -->

## 3. Recommendations

### At home

*   **Homework:** Focus on planning, not control. Trust confidence.
*   **Support:** Mount interest in complex topics (programming, history, science).
*   **Promotion:** Mark the process. Solutions, perseverance, ideas.
*   **Workplace:** 45-60 minutes of concentration. Provide silence and comfort.

### At school

*   **Tasks:** Formulate as research issues, problems.
*   **Lesson:** Tasks with Open ending. Debats, group projects, research.
*   **Place in the class:** A minimum of abstracts. The possibility of interaction in projects.
*   **Teacher style:** Mentor, not a controller. Consult, direct.
*   **Individual route (IOM):** In -depth study, Olympiads, projects, conferences.

<!-- page-break -->

## 4. Recommended training

The basic functions are developed. Training complicates them, use them in new contexts.

*   **Attention and strategy**
    *   Board games: Go, chess, "Citadels". Develop a strategy, analysis, memory.
    *   Debats, performances: structure of thought, reaction, attention control.

*   **Memory and flexibility**
    *   New languages ​​(programming): memorization, logic, flexible application of rules.
    *   Mind Mapping: Visualization, Structuring of topics (history, biology).

*   **Coordination and control**
    *   Sports: fencing, climbing, table tennis. High concentration, reaction, coordination.
    *   Team sports (basketball, football): spatial thinking, teamwork, quick solutions.
