## 2. Features of training

This is a description of the student’s work style. It will help to choose the most effective approaches to learning.

### Individual characteristics

*   **Pace:** Unstable. A quick start is replaced by a slowdown in tasks requiring analysis and synthesis. The tendency to haste and impulsiveness.
*   **Perception:** It better assimilates finished, structured information. Strong memory allows you to operate with large volumes of data. Independent organization of information is a growth zone.
*   **Focus of attention:** Attention is acute, but short -term. Due to impulsiveness, it is easily distracted, which prevents the completion of tasks without an external structure.

### Supporting factors

*   **Step-by-step instructions, checklists:** They direct actions, help to avoid failures due to impulsiveness.
*   **Visual supports:** Ready -made schemes, maps, graphs and tables simplify work with information.
*   **Clear rhythm, predictability:** The method of “Prydor” (25/5), understandable rules and routine reduce internal chaos.
*   **Short physical pauses:** A small warm -up helps to “restart” the brain.

### Distraction factors

*   **Multitasking:** The implementation of several cases simultaneously leads to errors and loss of concentration.
*   **Blurry, voluminous tasks:** Instructions without a clear plan cause a stupor or surface execution.
*   **Rush, pressure:** Weaken the control, enhancing impulsiveness.
*   **Visual, audio noise:** Excessive items, loud conversations, inappropriate music exhaust the resource of attention.

## 3. Recommendations

### For the house

*   **Organization of homework:**
    *   Miss large tasks into small steps. For example: “Read the paragraph” → “Write out dates”, “solve problems”.
    *   Use a planner or a sheet of paper to track progress. This gives a feeling of control.
    *   The optimal concentration time is 25-30 minutes. Then a short break (5-10 minutes) with physical activity is needed.
*   **Motivation and support:**
    *   Focus efforts, not just the result. Phrases like “I see how you tried to concentrate” is more valuable than the assessment.
    *   Encourage moments when the student managed to restrain the impulse or complete the case according to the plan.

### For school

*   **The wording of tasks:**
    *   As specifically as possible, with clear criteria. For example: "Essay of 5 paragraphs: introduction, 3 arguments with examples, conclusion."
    *   Use the verbs of action: “Write out”, “Compare”, “group”, “Make a list”.
*   **Information structure:**
    *   Provide ready -made templates or schemes (for abstracts, laboratory work). This simplifies information processing.
    *   At the beginning of the lesson, voice a clear plan: “Today we: 1. We repeat ... 2. We will study ... 3. We will train ...”.
*   **Organization of space and feed:**
    *   The optimal place in the classroom: 1-2 desks, away from the window and door, to minimize abstracts.
    *   Presentation style: calm, structured, predictable.
*   **Individual route:**
    *   Provide the opportunity to take volumetric work (projects, reports) in parts.
    *   Allow the use of checklist checks for self-testing before handling work.

## 4. Recommended training

These games and exercises are aimed at developing growth zones: attention, self -control and coordination.

### Attention and self -control

*   **Strategy board games:** Chess, GO, Jackal. They require planning several moves ahead and restraining impulsive actions.
*   **Mindfulness practices:** Daily exercise 3-5 minutes. Focus on your breath, marking it. This teaches you to notice impulses and not succumb to it.

### Coordination and interhemispheric interaction

*   **Juggling:** Start with two balls. Perfectly develops the coordination of the “hands-eye” and the connection between the hemispheres.
*   **Cross exercises:** Alternately, touch the left knee with your right hand, and with your left hand - the right. Can be performed to the music.
*   **Drawing with two hands:** Try to simultaneously draw symmetrical figures (circles, squares, butterflies) on one sheet.

### Bodily control and rhythm

*   **Game of musical instruments:** Especially shock or guitar, where a clear rhythm and coordination of movements are required.
*   **Dancing:** Any types of dances where you need to repeat and remember movements. They help to "ground" impulsiveness and improve the sensation of the body.
