**Cognitive development report: (age 14–18 years)**

This report offers an understanding of the individual characteristics of the development of your student. The goal is to provide practical recommendations for creating an optimal training environment.

---

### **1. Cognitive profile: test review**

*   **Attention and memory (CPT)**
    Difficulties with retention of prolonged attention and sequence of actions are noted. Perhaps rapid fatigue with high concentration requirements.

*   **Switching and control (Go/No-Go)**
    There is a decrease in coordination between the hemispheres. This can slow down the integration of heterogeneous information (for example, concepts and their application).

*   **Visual memory**
    Difficulties in holding the focus on visual details have been identified. It is possible to pass significant elements in volumetric visual materials.

### **2. Guide for Success: how a student is studying**

Understanding the individual learning style of your student is the key to effective development.

#### **Features of training**

*   **Pace**: It needs time to include in the task. A rush leads to a decrease in concentration and errors.
*   **Perception**: Priority of the visual format. It more efficiently assimilates information through diagrams, images, lists, algorithms. Long oral explanations are less effective.
*   **Focus of attention**: Single -to -date, easily distracted. Maximum efficiency is achieved when working on one specific task.

#### **Supporting factors**

*   Clear step -by -step instructions.
*   Visual support: checks, schemes, intelligence cards, color coding.
*   Short, focused tasks.
*   Regular short breaks (15–20 minutes).
*   Predictable daily routine.

#### **Factors that reduce efficiency**

*   Multitasking.
*   Long -term oral explanations.
*   Distracting factors: noise, disorder.
*   Abstract instructions without a clear algorithm.

<!-- page-break -->

### **3. Recommendations**

#### **Home environment**

1.  **Organization of training**
    *   **Separate the tasks**: Crush large tasks for short intervals (for example, 15 minutes of work, 5 minutes of break). Use the timer.
    *   **Use checklists**: Compose the tasks of the tasks. Mark the executed points for a sensation of progress.
    *   **The optimal attention interval**: 15–20 minutes. Plan short pauses after this time.

2.  **Support and motivation**
    *   **Praise efforts, not just the result**. Focus on following the plan and the efforts.
    *   **Develop planning**. Ask leading questions about the sequence of actions.

#### **School environment**

1.  **Setting tasks**
    *   **Maximum specifics**: Formulate the tasks clearly (for example, 'Read the item, write down the terms, answer the question').
    *   **Visual supports**: Use the lesson plan, keywords, diagrams, algorithms.
    *   **Optimal placement**: The first-second desk in the central row to minimize abstracts.

2.  **Structure and control**
    *   **One channel of information**: Avoid the simultaneous provision of verbal and visual information.
    *   **Individual approach**: Allow the block execution of tasks, provide extra time, allow the use of pre -drawn up plans.

### **4. Recommended training**

Practical exercises for the development of key skills.

*   **Attention**
    *   “Find the differences”, the search for objects in detailed images.
    *   Schultte tables (5 minutes, 1-2 times a day).
    *   Graphic dictations.

*   **Inter -recliped coordination**
    *   Cross-line movements (for example, “elbow-kolon”, 1-2 minutes).
    *   Juggling, ball games.
    *   Simultaneous drawing of symmetrical patterns with both hands.

*   **Memory and self -control**
    *   The game "Memory".
    *   Removing sequences of objects.
    *   Board games that require compliance with the rules and expectation of the course.
