# Cognitive assessment report
### Memory. Control. Attention. (14–18 years)

This report reveals the unique cognitive features of your teenager. It offers practical recommendations for optimizing training and creating a supporting environment at home.

---

# 1. A brief report for each test

### Working memory
Strong side. Great ability to retain a large amount of information. The focus is preserved even during distraction. The foundation for successful training.

### Brake control
The area for development. The difficulty to restrain the first impulse. The tendency to impulsive actions with quick decision -making.

### General attention and behavior
Influence on solutions. The tendency to hasty reactions reduces control of behavior in stressful or emotional situations.

# 2. The guide for the success of your student

Understanding these features is critical for effective learning.

### Features of training

#### Pace
High speed potential. A conscious slowdown in quality is required. Hurry leads to errors in carelessness.

#### Perception of information
Preference of structure and logic. Visual schemes, step -by -step plans, algorithms - the key to assimilation.

#### Focus of attention
The ability to deep concentration is interesting. Impulsiveness can interrupt the focus until the completion of the task.

### Support for training

#### Which helps
*   **Visual supports:** Check lists, schemes, intelligent cards, marker isolation.
*   **Step -by -step instructions:** Separation of tasks into simple stages. Example: "Read. Emphasize. Write down. Follow. Check."
*   **"Inner pause":** A short break (inhale-output) before the answer or action.
*   **Rhythm:** The use of timers (for example, Pomodoro) to organize work and rest.

#### What interferes
*   **Multitasking:** The simultaneous implementation of several tasks depletes self -control.
*   **Oral instructions without restraint:** Long explanations without visual support are lost.
*   **Competitive stress:** The need to act "to speed" provokes errors.
*   **Noise and distracting factors:** The chaotic environment enhances impulsive reactions, making it difficult to filter stimuli.

# 3. Recommendations

### Home environment

#### Organization of work
*   **Planning:** Make a task plan together, write it down. Mark the completion of each item.
*   **The rule "read twice":** Teach the task twice before starting.
*   **Breaks:** The optimal concentration is 30-40 minutes. Next-5-10 minutes of outdoor activities.

#### Support and motivation
*   **Praise for thought:** Mark self -control, not speed. Example: "You took a pause, it's great!"
*   **Analysis of situations:** Calmly discuss hasty actions. Focus on finding a solution: "What will help not rush next time?"

### School environment

#### Instructions and tasks
*   **Written instructions:** Supplement oral instructions with written (on the board, card).
*   **Clear formulations:** Use short verbs of action: “Write out”, “Compare”, “justify”.

#### Structure and control
*   **Separation of tasks:** Divide large tasks (works, projects) into semantic blocks.
*   **Drafts:** Offer to use a draft on control for planning and reducing impulsivity.

#### Environment and feed
*   **Optimal place:** Behind a desk with a minimum amount of distraction, but with contact with the teacher.
*   **Style style:** Calm, structured. Logical pauses for the assimilation of information.

#### Individual route
*   **Additional time:** On tests and control for thinking and checking answers.
*   **Emotional intelligence:** The inclusion in the development programs of self -regulation and emotional intelligence.

# 4. Recommended training

These exercises develop self -control and concentration.

### Attention and control
*   **Board games:** Chess, checkers, go, strategic games that require thought of passages and expectations.
*   **"Yes, and there is no talk":** Speech reaction control training.
*   **Graphic dictations:** Tasks for continuing patterns or repeating the drawings. Develop attention and control of movements.

### Memory and strategy
*   **The game "Memory":** With complicated rules (for example, the search for three cards).
*   **Puzzles and quests:** Deriving many conditions in memory.

### Rhythm and body
*   **Musical instruments:** Especially drums or percussion, where rhythm and coordination are important.
*   **Juggling:** Develops the coordination of the "eye-hand" and the focus on several objects.
*   **Dancing:** With a clear sequence of movements (hip-hop, break-dance).

### Awareness and relaxation
*   **Respiratory exercises:** "Square breathing" (4-4-4-4) before difficult work.
*   **Consciousness practices:** "Body scan", focus on sounds (2-3 minutes) for "internal observer".
