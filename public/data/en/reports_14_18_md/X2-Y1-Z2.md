# Cognitive profile: revealing potential
A comprehensive analysis for the purposeful development and success of your student.
This report provides key data on its cognitive abilities, offering strategies for effective training and training.

# 1. Analysis of abilities
A detailed view of the results of cognitive testing.
*   **Self-control (Go/No-Go):** Outstanding skills of self -control and behavior management. The ability to quickly make decisions, suppression of impulses and efficient switching of tasks is a key strength.
*   **Sustainability of attention (TCP):** The tendency to the loss of focus during prolonged monotonous work. This can affect the pace of training, especially with fatigue.
*   **Visual memory (CPT):** When memorizing a large volume of visual information (diagrams, graphs), missing details are possible, especially when distracting. However, the strategic approach helps to compensate for this feature.

# 2. The guide for the success of your student
Features of interaction in the educational environment.

## Individual profile
*   **Rhythm:** Nonlinear. Effective at short distances. Requires regular micro-strokes to restore concentration.
*   **Perception:** Visual support. Information is better absorbed in the presence of visual materials: diagrams, lists, keywords.
*   **Concentration:** Deep, but short. Complete immersion in an interesting task is limited to 20-25 minutes. After a change in activity or a short rest is required.

## Optimal conditions
*   **Check lists:** Step -by -step instructions and structured plans help maintain focus.
*   **Visual accents:** The use of color, stickers, Mind Cart contributes to the organization of information.
*   **Timing:** Techniques like “prying” (25/5 minutes) create a predictable rhythm and retain energy.
*   **Independent planning:** Involvement in the development of tasks plans is used by strong self -control to increase responsibility.

## Factors that reduce efficiency
*   **Multitasking:** The simultaneous performance of several actions quickly depleted the resource of attention.
*   **Monotone:** Oral lectures without a visual support make it difficult to hold the focus.
*   **Fast transitions:** A short pause is needed (10-15 seconds) for mental switching between tasks.
*   **Excess visual information:** Overloaded materials (slides, pages) interfere with the allocation of the main.

# 3. Recommendations for use
Practical steps to support the development of a student.

## In the home environment
### Organization of the educational process
*   Separate large tasks into short (20-25 minutes) blocks. Use a spacing timer.
*   Turn on short (5 minutes) physical breaks between blocks for the "reboot" of the brain.
*   Make a joint plan of evening classes. Celebrate the control and progress made to feel the control.

### Motivation and support
*   Focus on the process, and not just on the result: "I did a great job of these 20 minutes!" Or "a good preparation plan!".
*   With errors on inattention, offer strategies for self -testing, for example: "Check only the punctuation marks before surrender."

### Important note about attention
The effective concentration lasts about **20–25 minutes**. An increase in this time can be counterproductive.

## In the training space
### Making tasks
*   Provide tasks in the format of clear algorithms or checklists, especially for complex projects.
*   Select the key elements of instructions or questions with bold font/color.

### The structure of the material
*   Duplicate oral information on the board (key terms, plan).
*   Use minimalistic presentations: one idea is one slide.
*   Place important visual materials (formulas, rules) in prominent places.

### Individual approach
*   Consider the possibility of using noise -handing headphones during independent work.
*   In preparation for exams (OGE/exam), focus on strategies of self -testing using strong self -control.
*   Give a choice of response format (for example, scheme/presentation instead of oral presentation).

# 4. Developing exercises
Game methods for strengthening cognitive functions.
*   **For attention and visual memory:**
    *   **Schultte tables:** Classic to expand the field of view and improve concentration. Start with simple ones, gradually complicating.
    *   **"Find 10 differences":** Choose images with non -obvious, small differences.
    *   **"Memory":** Use sets with a large number of cards and similar images to enhance the call.
*   **For coordination and bodily control:** (Increases attentiveness)
    *   **Juggling:** Start with two balls. Synchronizes the brain, develops concentration.
    *   **Balance Bord:** He trains focus and bodily control through maintaining balance.
*   **For rhythm:** (Stabilizes the pace)
    *   **Tapping rhythms:** Offer to repeat the specified rhythmic pattern (for example, short-short-bed) alternately with each hand, then both. Develops an inner sense of rhythm and pace.
