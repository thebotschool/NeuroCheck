## Assessment of cognitive skills

---
#### 1. Test results
### Working memory (CPT)
The ability to memorize individual facts is high. Building a sequence or order of events requires additional attention.

### Braking (Go/No-Go)
There are difficulties with quickly stopping the action and control of impulses, especially in conditions of speed or stress.

### Attention (TCP)
The impulsiveness is manifested. It takes more time to think about the answer before acting.

---
#### 2. The guide for the success of the student
### Individual characteristics
- **Pace:** An unhurried one is preferable. A quick change in tasks or instant reaction can cause stress.
- **Perception:** Better assimilates visually structured information: schemes, lists, plans. Abstract text is less effective.
- **Attention:** Unstable. Easily distracted. Long -term concentration requires a clear structure and support.

### Which helps
- Step-by-step instructions, checklists.
- Visual supports: diagrams, maps, timline.
- Clear rules. The predictability of the environment.
- Using templates, samples.
- A rhythmic, understandable environment.

### What interferes
- Multitasking, frequent switching.
- Hurry, temporary pressure.
- Noise, chaos, distracting factors.
- Only oral instructions.
- Tasks without a clear plan or example.
<!-- page-break -->

---
#### 3. Recommendations
### At home
#### Organization of work
- Divide large tasks into 2-3 small steps. Write down the list.
- Use the timer: 20 minutes work, 5-7 minutes break (physical activity).
- The workplace should be clean, without distracting items.

#### Support
- Praise the process, not only the result: "How did you try to follow the plan!"
- Discuss plans for the day, make them together. Train logic.

#### Attention
- Productive concentration: 15-20 minutes for a difficult task. After - a break.

### At school
#### The formulation of tasks
- Tasks should be short, specific, classmate. Example: "Make a plan", not "write an essay."

#### Submitting information
- Provide samples, templates, algorithms (formulas, diagrams).
- Duplicate oral instructions in writing (board, printout).
- Communicate calmly, kindly. Let's think about the time.

#### Wednesday
- Recommended place: first-second desk, a class center. Away from distracting factors.

#### Individual route
- Consider the additional time for testing.
- Allow me to use checklists, support circuits for answers.
- A consultation of a psychologist/tutor for self -control and planning programs is recommended.

#### 4. Recommended training
Games and exercises for the development of skills.

### Attention and self -control
- The game is "edible, unauthorized" or "sea is worried once": they train a stop.
- Board games: waiting for the course, following the rules (Jenga, Uno, checkers).

### Memory and planning
- Compilation of stories according to pictures/comics: development of logical sequence.
- Assembly of models according to the instructions (LEGO, designers): following a step -by -step plan.
- The game "I put in a bag ...": training of consistent memory.

### Coordination and bodily control
- Jugling (two goals/scarf): improvement of the coordination of the "eye-hand", concentration.
- Balance exercises: stand on one leg, walking along the line, balance board.
- Rhythmic exercises: taping the rhythm (music, metronome). Development of a sense of pace.
