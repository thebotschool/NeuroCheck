## Individual report: cognitive profile (14–18 years)

This report is designed to understand unique cognitive features. It offers strategies for the development of potential and optimizing the learning process.

---

### 1. Key insights

*   **CPT: Attention and memory**  
    There is a difficulty in memorizing accurate visual parts, especially when distracting. There is a systematic approach to the beginning of tasks.
*   **Go/No-Go: Reaction and Control**  
    High -speed and attentive tasks are performed more efficiently with one hand. This indicates the need for synchronizing the work of the hemispheres for a comprehensive analysis.
*   **TCP: Self -regulation**  
    The tendency to impulsive actions has been revealed. Making decisions is often ahead of the analysis, which affects the control of behavior.

### 2. The guide for the success of your student

#### Features of perception

*   **Pace**  
    The fast but intermittent pace is characteristic. High inclusion is replaced by loss of concentration due to impulsiveness or parts.
*   **Perception format**  
    The most effectively perceives information in one, clearly structured format (text or diagram). The combination of formats (text + graph) makes processing difficult.
*   **Focus of attention**  
    The general canvas of the task is held, but the focus easily shifts to the details, which leads to errors. Attention is unstable, depending on the environment.

#### Support for progress

*   **Step-by-step instructions and checklists.**  
    Savior of large tasks into small, understandable steps. The use of checklists.
*   **Visual supports.**  
    Visual means: schemes, mental cards, color coding, short lists. Help to structure information.
*   **Rhythm.**  
    Rhythmization of work: pronouncing rules, using a timer (for example, according to the method of ironing). Promises to maintain pace and synchronization.
*   **"One task at a time."**  
    The principle 'one task is at a time.' Focusing on the current action without switching.

#### Obstacles for learning

*   **Multitasking.**  
    Simultaneous listening and recording, reading and compiling tables leads to errors and frustration.
*   **Visual and audial noise.**  
    External noise (visual, audial): a mess on the table, background sounds. Instantly violates concentration.
*   **Complex, composite instructions.**  
    Long oral instructions without visual support. They complicate understanding.
*   **Hurry and pressure.**  
    Enhance impulsiveness and increase the number of errors.

### 3. Development strategies

#### Home environment

*   **Homework organization:**
    *   Divide the tasks into short blocks (20–25 minutes) with 5-minute breaks.
    *   Make a simple work plan: “1. Reading. 2. Answers to questions. 3. Solution of examples. "
    *   Provide a quiet workplace free from gadgets and visual disorder.
*   **Support and encouragement:**
    *   Focus on the process, and not on the result: “You follow the plan!”, “I see your zeal.”
    *   Before complex tasks, offer 3 deep breath-sinking for concentration and slowdown.
    *   In case of errors due to inattention, indicate: "Check this fragment, perhaps here is inaccuracy."

#### School interaction

*   **The wording of tasks:**
    *   Formulate the instructions step by step: instead of generalized tasks, offer “1. Read the text. 2. Highlight 3 ideas. 3. Write at the proposal to each. "
    *   Place key information (lesson plan, homework) on the board in the form of numbered lists or schemes.
*   **Subject style and lesson structure:**
    *   Apply visual coding: colors for those symbols, simple patterns.
    *   Give the time to think about it. Avoid the requirements of an instant answer. Example: "Think, then answer."
    *   The optimal location: the first-second desk minimizing distraction.
*   **Elements of an individual educational route (IOM):**
    *   It is recommended to use checklist or prepared plans during testing.
    *   In preparation for exams, it is advisable to interact with the tutor for the development of metacognitive strategies: planning, self-test, time management.

### 4. Practical training

These exercises and games are aimed at developing key skills. A regular, positive and game form is recommended.

*   **Attention and memory:**
    *   **Corrective test.** Crossing out certain letters/words in the text. Start from 5 minutes.
    *   **Board games.** “Find a pair”, “Dobbl”, “Code names: Pictures”. Develop visual attention and memory for details.
*   **Coordination and interhemispheric interaction:**
    *   **Juggling.** Starting from two items. Stimulates interhemispheric ties.
    *   **Mirror drawing.** Simultaneous drawing of symmetrical figures with both hands.
    *   **Exercise "Fist-Robric Ladon".** A consistent change in hand positions, first one, then both.
*   **Bodily control and reduction of impulsiveness:**
    *   **Balance games.** Walking along the line, a stand on one leg, a balance border. Develop bodily control.
    *   **"Square breath."** Inhale-track-outbreak (4 accounts). Repeat 5-7 times. Effective for reducing anxiety and concentration.
