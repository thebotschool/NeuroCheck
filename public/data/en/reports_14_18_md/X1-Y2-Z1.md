## Cognitive profile report

### Understanding the potential of the student.

This report is based on these three cognitive tests. He offers a clear idea of ​​the processes of processing information, attention and self -control. The goal is to provide practical recommendations for support in study and everyday life.

### 1. A brief report for each test

*   **Attention (Continous Performance Test):** High and mature concentration. Sustainable holding of attention on one task.
*   **Reactions control (Go/No-Go):** Difficulties with the suppression of impulsive actions. Especially if necessary, a quick reaction or the availability of a ban.
*   **Working memory (visual):** An excellent ability to memorize and maintain complex information, such as sequences or multi -level conditions.

**Key understanding:** Profile "Fast mind, but hasty actions." Strengths: concentration and memory. The main growth zone: impulse control.

### 2. The guide for the success of your student

#### Features: individual profile

*   **Pace:** An optimal thoughtful, unhurried approach. Haste increases the risk of errors. Time for thinking activates strong working memory.
*   **Perception:** Effectively visual and structured. The information that can be seen, read, checked by the list is better absorbed.
*   **Focus of attention:** Sustainable, but prone to "tunnel vision". A deep immersion can lead to a passage of “stop” signals or additional conditions if they are not distinguished visually.

#### Support: success factors

*   **Pause before action:** The introduction of a conscious pause. For example, the internal account "one-two-three" before the answer or recording.
*   **Check lists and instructions:** Separation of large tasks into small steps. Refusing the made reduces impulsiveness, gives control.
*   **Visual tips:** Markers, stickers with reminders ("Check!", "Do not rush!"), Schemes, plans on paper.
*   **Predictable environment:** A calm atmosphere with a minimum of distracting factors contributes to better control of impulses.

#### Calls: obstacles

*   **Multitasking:** The requirement to fulfill several cases at the same time often leads to errors and bustle.
*   **Fast change of tasks:** It takes time for the mental completion of one task and switching to the next. Sharp transitions are knocked down.
*   **Oral instructions without support:** Especially long or containing prohibitions ("do not do", "except"). The particle "not" can be missed.
*   **Time deficiency and stress:** Pressure (for example, on control) provokes a rush and impulsive, rash answers.

### 3. Recommendations

#### Houses: Support

*   **Homework:**
    *   Together, make a brief work plan: "Mathematics (ex. 5) - a break - English (words)."
    *   A draft is safe. To perform, then after a pause (5-10 minutes) to jointly check and rewrite. He trains a self -test.
    *   Encourage not for speed, but for accuracy and following the plan. Mark: "You checked yourself, it's great!"
*   **Communication:**
    *   Avoid phrases like "Hurry up again!" Ask: "What will help you pause before the answer?"
    *   After 30–40 minutes of concentration on an interesting task, a short (5-10 minutes) active break for "rebooting" is needed.

#### At school: practical advice

*   **The wording of tasks:**
    *   The instructions should be talked, according to the points. It is preferably supported by a board or handout.
    *   Key restraints ("besides", "not", "only") are highlighted in voice, color or emphasizing.
*   **Lesson structuring:**
    *   Give time to think about an answer. Format: "You have a minute for thinking and formulation."
    *   In control work: first read all the tasks, make a plan, then start.
*   **Organization:**
    *   The optimal place is the first-second desk. This minimizes visual distracting factors.
    *   Consider the elements of an individual educational route. For example, additional time in exams (GIA/exam). This is not a benefit, but the creation of equal conditions that allow compensating for impulsiveness and showing thoughtfulness.

### 4. Recommended training

These exercises develop self -control and coordination in an interesting format.

*   **Attention and braking:**
    *   **The game "Yes and no."** Teaches to control automatic speech reactions.
    *   **The game "Stop frame".** Instant stop at a signal. He trains the interruption skill.
*   **Coordination and bodily control:**
    *   **Juggling.** Requires concentration, coordination of the "eye-hand", control of movements.
    *   **Balansboard / Slaelain.** Develops balance and general self -regulation.
*   **Memory and strategic thinking:**
    *   **Table strategies.** Chess, GO, Carcasson, Colonialists. They use working memory, teach to plan moves, suppress impulsive solutions.
*   **Rhythm and self -regulation:**
    *   **Musical instruments.** Drums, percussion, bitbox. Develop a sense of rhythm and internal timing-the basis of the impulse control. You can just beat the rhythm.
