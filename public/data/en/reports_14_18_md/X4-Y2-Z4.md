### **Cognitive assessment: report**

This report is designed to help parents and teachers understand the strengths of the student and determine the areas for development. Recommendations are aimed at creating a more effective and comfortable learning process, as well as the formation of key life skills.

---

### **1. Test results**

*   **Attention and working memory (CPT):** The student experiences difficulties with long -term retention and memorization of consistent actions. This can lead to errors and slowdown tasks.
*   **Reaction inhibition (Go/No-Go):** A tendency to impulsive actions is noted. It is difficult to restrain the first impulse, which can lead to hasty decisions.
*   **General rating (TCP):** Tests indicate possible cognitive overload. The student is experiencing difficulties with the simultaneous concentration, planning and control of reactions.

<!-- page-break -->

### **2. The guide for the success of your student**

#### **Features of perception**

*   **Pace:** It takes time to "include" in the task. After the start, haste and impulsiveness can show.
*   **Information format:** Better perceives short, clear steps. Visual supports are effective: schemes, lists, highlighted words.
*   **Focus of attention:** Attention is unstable. It is easily distracted, especially when working with long or multicomponent tasks.

#### **Supporting factors**

*   **Clear step -by -step instructions:** Separation of the task into "A → B → C" increases efficiency.
*   **Visual supports:** The plan on the sticker, the scheme, the allocation of keywords help to keep the focus.
*   **Pause before action:** It is recommended to teach a schoolboy a short pause (inhale-output) and mental planning before the answer or start of work.
*   **Structured environment:** An understandable routine, rules and organized workplace reduce the cognitive load.

#### **Interviewing factors**

*   **Multitasking:** Several simultaneous commands lead to overload and errors.
*   **High pace:** Fast speech or changing tasks without pauses make it difficult to process information.
*   **Distracting factors:** Noise and visual chaos exhaust the resource of attention.
*   **Fonger instructions:** General phrases ("be more attentive") are ineffective and can cause frustration.

<!-- page-break -->

### **3. Practical recommendations**

#### **Home environment**

*   **Organization of work:**
    *   Divide the tasks into blocks for 15–20 minutes. Between them are short (3-5 minutes) active breaks.
    *   Create a simple written plan before starting work. For example: "1. Solve the problem No. 5. 2. Read 2 pages of history. 3. Take a break."
    *   The desktop should contain only necessary for the current task.
*   **Support and encouragement:**
    *   Praise for efforts, and not for the result: "You tried to follow the plan," "it's good that you checked yourself."
    *   Help realize impulsive impulses. Coldly offer: "We will pause and re -read the condition."
*   **Mercing of attention:**
    *   Be realistic: stable attention on a difficult task - about 15–20 minutes. Exceeding this limit is counterproductive.

#### **Educational environment**

*   **The wording of tasks:**
    *   Give the tasks one at a time. Make sure of the previous step.
    *   Use the verbs of the action: “Emphasize”, “Write”, “Make a list”.
*   **Lesson structure:**
    *   Duplicate oral instructions visually: a plan on the board, cards with an algorithm, scheme.
    *   Make semantic pauses. Ask clarifying questions: “What are we going to do now?” To return attention and test understanding.
*   **Accommodation:**
    *   Optimal-at the first or second desk in the central row. This reduces distracting factors and provides contact with the teacher.
*   **Individual route:**
    *   It is recommended to develop an IOM with an accent for self -regulation and attention.
    *   An additional time in control work and permission to use a draft plan can be useful.

### **4. Developing training**

These exercises will help to develop the necessary skills in a playful way. Regularity is important: 10-15 minutes daily.

#### **Attention**

*   **Graphic dictations:** They train to keep the instructions in memory.
*   **Correct samples:** Search and dispensing certain letters in the text for a while.
*   **Board games:** "Find 10 differences", "Dobbl", labyrinths.

#### **Memory and planning**

*   **The game "Memory":** Develops visual working memory.
*   **Retelling according to the plan:** Drawing up a plan (3-4 points) and retelling of content (video, text) on it.
*   **Assembly according to the instructions:** Designers, models, assembly according to schemes - tracing the algorithm.

#### **Control and impulsiveness**

*   **The game "Delay!":** Movement to the music with a stop when turning it off. Teaches a quick change in the states of the "action".
*   **Rhythmic exercises:** Cooling or tapping rhythms according to the model (from simple to complex).
*   **Balance exercises:** Walking along the line, stand on one leg. Improve the connection of the brain and body, develop self -control.
