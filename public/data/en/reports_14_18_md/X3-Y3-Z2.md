### Cognitive assessment report

This report offers a detailed view of the strengths and growth zones of your student. Information is based on the results of cognitive testing and translated into the form of practical recommendations to support the development of home and at school.

---

#### 1. Review of tests

*   **Memory and planning (CPT):** The student demonstrates excellent memorization of individual facts and details. However, the organization and ordering of this information in the correct sequence require additional support.
*   **Control and braking (Go/No-Go):** The results indicate the need to develop the coordinated work of the cerebral hemispheres. This can be manifested in difficulties in performing tasks requiring simultaneous involvement of logical and figurative thinking.
*   **Sustainability of attention (TCP):** Maintaining a long concentration on one task can be complicated. Attention is prone to short -term "falling out", especially with fatigue or monotonous work.

#### 2. The profile of the student

##### Features of perception

*   **Pace:** Productivity is achieved in individual rhythm. Hurrying or excessive pressure can lead to errors and rapid fatigue. Short pauses contribute to rebooting.
*   **Format:** Information is better absorbed at **visual and structured** feed. Oral or abstract instructions without a visual support are less effective.
*   **Focus:** Attention works "impulses". A deep concentration on interesting tasks is possible for a short time, but long or monotonous periods cause distraction.

##### Effective approaches

*   **Step -by -step instructions:** Separation of large tasks into specific, measurable steps simplifies their implementation.
*   **Visual support:** Schemes, mental cards, tables, text highlighting - everything that can be seen helps to structure thoughts.
*   **Short intervals:** The “Prydor” technique (25 minutes of work, 5 minutes of rest) supports the rhythm and prevents overwork.
*   **Clear boundaries:** A certain beginning and end of the task create a sense of control and reduce anxiety.

##### Obstacles in learning

*   **Multitasking:** The simultaneous implementation of several cases (for example, lessons and messenger) reduces effectiveness. Focus on one task is required.
*   **Long oral instructions:** More than 2-3 consecutive teams are difficult to hold by ear. Important information should be recorded.
*   **Unstructured tasks:** The absence of a clear frame (for example, "write an essay on an summer" without a plan) can cause a stupor.
*   **Monotony and noise:** Long monologues without interactive or distracting background noise quickly deplete the resource of attention.

<!-- page-break -->

#### 3. Recommendations for support

##### In the home environment

*   **Organization of work:**
    *   **Decomposition:** Separate large tasks (project, preparation) into small daily steps. Use a planner or stickers.
    *   **Timer:** Consider working intervals (for example, 25/5 minutes). This creates the structure and provides rest.
    *   **Preliminary planning:** Teach a brief plan of 2-3 steps before starting work. This trains the skill of structuring.
*   **Support and encouragement:**
    *   **Process rating:** Praise not for the result, but for the efforts made: “Great plan!”, “I noticed how you worked concentrated 20 minutes, it's great!”
    *   **Realistic expectations:** Keep in mind that a continuous concentration on an uninteresting task can be 15-25 minutes.
*   **Environment:** Organize the workplace by excluding distracting factors (telephone, TV).

##### In the school environment

*   **The wording of tasks:**
    *   **Specifics:** Use the exact instructions: "Read paragraph 5, write down 3 dates and 2 terms" Instead of "Analyze the chapter."
    *   **Templates:** Provide support schemes for essays, laboratory work (for example, essay structure: introduction, argument 1+example, argument 2+example, conclusion).
*   **Lesson structure:**
    *   **Visualization:** Duplicate the key points of the lesson on the board or in the presentation.
    *   **Location:** Place the student closer to the board, away from distracting factors (windows, doors).
    *   **Interactivity:** Use dialogue and short questions to maintain involvement.
*   **Individual route:**
    *   **Check lists:** Allow the use of checklists for self-testing on control (for example: “Checking the punctuation marks?”, “Completed all steps?”).
    *   **Additional time:** If possible, provide extra time on exams to check and structuring answers.

#### 4. Developing classes

These simple exercises and games contribute to the development of skills in unobtrusive form.

*   **Attention and control:**
    *   **Correct samples:** Crossing certain letters in the text. Gradually complicate (for example, delete "a", circle "O").
    *   **Board games:** "Dobble", "uno", "drum" develop a reaction and attentiveness.
*   **Coordination and interaction:**
    *   **Juggling:** Start with two balls. Perfectly synchronizes the hemisphere.
    *   **Crossroads:** Exercise "Lokot-Kolon" (right elbow to the left knee and vice versa) at a calm pace.
    *   **Tool game:** Musical instruments requiring separate work (guitar, piano).
*   **Memory and planning:**
    *   **Cooking by recipe:** Ideal training in the execution of the algorithm with a clear result.
    *   **Assembly of models:** Designers (LEGO) according to the instructions.
    *   **Table strategies:** Games requiring the planning of the moves in advance ("Chess", "Carcasson", "Jackal").
    *   **Retelling:** Talking of the plots of films or comics with an emphasis on the correct sequence of events.
