### **Cognitive profile report: success and development**

This report offers a deep understanding of the cognitive characteristics of the student. We allocated the strengths and offered the ways to develop to support it in study and everyday life.

---

### **1. Test results**

*   **Consistent memory (CPT):** Great ability to memorize information. Support may be required to assimilate order or sequence of actions. Best of all, perceives structured data.
*   **Self-control (Go/No-Go):** High level of self -control. Effectively controls actions, which contributes to the successful implementation of educational and complex tasks.
*   **Sustainability of attention (TCP):** Confirmed ability to long -term concentration. The student is deeply immersed in tasks without being distracted. This is a key skill for design work and preparation for important tests.

<!-- page-break -->

### **2. Profile of abilities**

#### **Key features**

*   **Pace:** Thoughtful approach. It takes time for analysis and planning, especially for new or voluminous tasks. After the preparation, he is able to work productively and without breaks.
*   **Perception:** Visual-structural. Information is better absorbed in the form of diagrams, lists, tables, intelligence cards or step-by-step algorithms. Continuous text or audio feed can be less effective.
*   **Attention:** Deep and stable. It is able to completely focus on an interesting task, effectively filtering out external distracting factors. This is a valuable quality for research.

#### **Which contributes to success**

*   **Visual supports.** Schemes, timline, checklists, infographics. Everything that allows you to see the overall structure.
*   **Step -by -step instructions.** Clear algorithms remove the load on the planning and direct the resource to execution.
*   **Ready -made samples.** Examples of work or templates help to master logic and requirements.
*   **Planning time.** The ability to pre -draw up a plan of action significantly increases efficiency.

#### **What can make difficult**

*   **Multitasking.** Simultaneous retention of several diverse tasks.
*   **Oral instructions.** Long explanations without visual support.
*   **Hurry and frequent change of tasks.** The lack of time for the internal organization.
*   **Unstracted stream of information.** A large amount of data without a clear logical connection.

<!-- page-break -->

### **3. Support strategies**

#### **Home environment**

*   **Preparation for tasks:**
    *   Start large tasks by drawing up a plan. Use stickers, boards or applications.
    *   Miss large tasks into 3-4 small, completed steps for every evening.
    *   When studying complex subjects, create connections, timline or intelligent cards to structuring information.
*   **Motivation and encouragement:**
    *   Mark not only the result, but also concentration, perseverance, the ability to bring the matter to the end. Extract these qualities.
    *   If the student experiences difficulties, help with the question: “What is the first step here? And the next? " Offer to draw a scheme.
*   **Effectiveness of attention:** The student is working effectively on one task for 45-60 minutes. After that, a short break (5-10 minutes) is desirable.

#### **School environment**

*   **The wording of tasks:**
    *   Provide written, numbered instructions.
    *   For complex tasks, offer a clear sample or evaluation criteria.
*   **Lesson structure:**
    *   At the beginning of the lesson, voice and write down the plan on the board.
    *   Present the new material using visual supports: schemes, tables, structured presentations.
    *   When explaining the algorithms (for example, in physics or grammar), duplicate steps on the board.
*   **Individual approach:**
    *   High self -control and sustainable attention makes the student an ideal candidate for long -term projects, research and olympiads.
    *   It can be a valuable participant in group work, especially in the role of the one who monitors time and structure, if there is a finished plan.
    *   Consider a short course on intellect-cards or methods for structuring information (Cornell method). This will give tools for an independent organization.

### **4. Development of skills**

These exercises will help strengthen the weaknesses and support the strong in a game and applied form.

*   **For consistent memory and planning:**
    *   **Board games:** “Progors”, “Robogem” or other games for algorithms.
    *   **Creating stories:** Coming comics or rebukes according to short stories.
    *   **Cooking:** Accurate following recipes for preparing new dishes.
    *   **Assembly of models:** Lego Technic designers, models of ships or aircraft requiring compliance with instructions.

*   **For attention and self -control (strengths):**
    *   **Strategic Games:** Chess, GO, desktop strategies (“sickle”, “Carcasson”).
    *   **Juggling:** Develops coordination, concentration and interaction of the hemispheres of the brain.
    *   **Balance Bord:** Training of bodily control and ability to hold the focus.
