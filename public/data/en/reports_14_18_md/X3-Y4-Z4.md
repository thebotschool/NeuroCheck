# Your potential: cognitive test report

This account reveals the unique cognitive features of your student. Our recommendations are designed to help create optimal conditions for its successful training and development.

---

## 1. Key results

*   **Consistent memory (CPT):** Confident memorization of individual elements. Difficulties arise when they are ordered. There is an active search for your own strategies, which is a positive sign.
*   **Self-control (Go/No-Go):** Difficulties with inhibitor control and quick reaction were noted. This is a systemic feature that requires targeted support.
*   **Sustainability of attention (TCP):** The general overload of the attention system has been revealed. Holding concentration and avoiding errors is difficult. This may be due to fatigue, anxiety or educational load.

## 2. Success Guide: Understanding your student

### Individual profile

*   **Pace:** A reduced, individual pace of work is needed. The student shows thoroughness: he needs time to process information and planning. A rush leads to errors and frustration.
*   **Perception format:** The optimal perception of information is achieved through visual and structural formats: diagrams, lists, step -by -step algorithms. Long or abstract oral explanations are less effective.
*   **Focus of attention:** Attention is unstable and quickly depleted. The schoolboy hardly filters out external (noise, movement) and internal distracting factors. Productive concentration on one task is no more than **15–20 minutes**, after which a short break is required.

### Support: The key to progress

*   **Step -by -step instructions:** Break big tasks into 3-5 specific steps.
*   **Visual supports:** Use checks, recall stickers, highlighting keywords, finished templates.
*   **A clear rhythm:** Use short work intervals with planned breaks (for example, 20 minutes of work, 5 minutes of rest).
*   **Predictability:** Report in advance about the lesson or homework plan. This reduces anxiety and releases the resource for study.
*   **One focus:** The rule "one table is one task." Remove all the excess that does not apply to the current lesson.

### Calls: what should be taken into account

*   **Multitasking:** The requirement to fulfill several cases at the same time or quickly switch between them almost always leads to a decrease in efficiency.
*   **Long oral instructions:** Phrases like “listen, remember and do” are ineffective. Information is not absorbed.
*   **Noise and chaos:** Loud sounds, active movement, visually overloaded space reduce the resource of attention.
*   **Abstract requirements:** Tasks such as “Analyze the Text” or “Be careful” do not work. We need specifics: “Find 3 main arguments”, “Check your work on this list”.

## 3. Optimization of the environment: practical recommendations

### Home environment

*   **Organization of work:**
    *   Together draw up a brief plan: “First mathematics (tasks 1, 2), then a break, then a story (read a paragraph, answer questions).”
    *   Use the timer for 20 minutes. After the signal-a mandatory 5-minute break (get up, drink water, look out the window).
    *   For tasks for memorizing a sequence (history, biology), create schemes, time tapes or comics.
*   **Motivation and support:**
    *   Praise for the process and efforts, and not just for the result: “It's great that you followed the plan!”, “I see how you tried not to be distracted.”
    *   Create a calm, predictable and accepting the atmosphere. In case of error or distraction, calmly discuss the causes and methods of support next time.
*   **Attention management:**
    *   Take 15–20 minutes as a working maximum. Do not demand more. Three approaches of 20 minutes are more effective than an hour of barren efforts.

### Educational environment

*   **Making tasks:**
    *   The instructions should be brief, clear, step -by -step. Use the verbs of action: “Emphasize”, “Write”, “Draw up a scheme of 3 points”.
    *   Duplicate oral tasks in writing (on a board or on a card).
*   **Learning structure:**
    *   At the beginning of the lesson, voice the plan. Use visual materials: presentations with schemes, supporting abstracts, intelligence cards.
    *   Provide templates to complete complex tasks (for example, an essay plan, an algorithm for solving a problem).
*   **Organization of space:**
    *   The optimal place is at the first or second desk, away from the window and door, to minimize distracting factors.
    *   Provide a little more time for control and independent work.
*   **Individual route:**
    *   It is advisable to officially fix special conditions: the possibility of using checklists, templates, additional time.
    *   It is recommended to attract a school psychologist or tutor to help in planning and organizing educational activities.

## 4. Development of skills: training

These exercises will help to develop the necessary skills in a game and affordable form. Regularity is important, not a duration.

### Attention and self -control

*   **Correct samples:** Print a text or table with letters/symbols. The task is to find and cross out all the letters “A” in 5 minutes and circle all the letters “K” into the circle.
*   **Graphic dictations:** “Two cells to the right, one upward, one to the left ...” - effectively trains the holding of the instructions and attention.
*   **The game "Forbidden movement":** Agree on the movement that cannot be repeated for the presenter. This is trained by impulse braking.

### Memory and planning

*   **Board games-strategies:** Games requiring the planning of the moves in advance (“Chess”, “Checkers”, “Carcasson”, “Train ticket”).
*   **Compilation of comics:** Draw a simple story in the correct sequence using 4-6 empty squares (for example, “How I cook a sandwich”).
*   **Retelling along the chain:** Ask to talk about the events of the day, strictly in order. Help with leading questions: “What happened *Immediately after* Breakfast? "

### Rhythm and coordination

*   **Repeat the rhythm:** Cut or check the simple rhythm (for example: two cotton - pause - one cotton). The task is to repeat exactly. Gradually complicate.
*   **Juggling:** Training in juggling two, and then three balls develops concentration, coordination and a sense of rhythm.
*   **Dancing or playing musical instruments (especially drums):** Develops a sense of rhythm, synchronizes movements and hearing, trains bodily control.
