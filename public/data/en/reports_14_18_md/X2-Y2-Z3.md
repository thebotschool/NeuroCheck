# Cognitive profile

This report offers a deep understanding of individual cognitive features. The goal is to create an optimal environment for training and development, using strengths and supporting growth zones.

***

### 1. Test results

*   **Sustainability of attention (CPT):** Difficulties with prolonged concentration and control of impulsive reactions have been identified. Disclosures are characteristic of monotonous tasks.
*   **Self-control (Go/No-Go):** A tendency to quick, rash actions is noted. There is a tendency to an immediate reaction without preliminary thinking.
*   **Visual memory:** General information is absorbed effectively. However, individual visual parts can be lost or confused, especially with external distraction.

### 2. Individual training guide

#### Features of training

*   **Pace:** Tall, impulsive. The tendency to make quick decisions without a sufficient pause for analysis.
*   **Perception:** Visual and structured information (diagrams, lists) is most effectively absorbed. Long oral or continuous text instructions can cause difficulties.
*   **Focus:** Attention is unstable, retained up to 15-20 minutes. Returning to the task after distraction requires effort.

#### Support

*   **Clear structure:** Step -by -step instructions, consistent presentation.
*   **Visual support:** Schemes, intelligence cards, markers, short lists.
*   **Pause:** Short, planned breaks (2-3 minutes) for restarting attention.
*   **Predictability:** Knowledge of the sequence of actions (for example, "first 5 tasks, then a break").
*   **Verbalization:** Sounding the action plan helps focusing.

#### Calls

*   **Multitasking:** Simultaneous performance of several tasks or quick switching.
*   **Time pressure:** The need for a quick response without time to think.
*   **Demonstrations:** Noise, disorder in the workplace.
*   **Lack of structure:** Long, monotonous text or lecture without pauses.

<!-- page-break -->

### 3. Recommendations

#### At home

*   **Organization:**
    *   **Separation of tasks:** Divide the work into blocks for 15-20 minutes with a timer. Turn on short physical breaks.
    *   **Planning:** Make a simple plan of 2-3 steps before starting work.
    *   **Workplace:** Limit distracting objects. Gadgets outside the visibility zone.
*   **Support:**
    *   **Focus on the process:** Praise for attentiveness and deliberation, not speed.
    *   **Involvement:** Replace "Have you done lessons?" on "What is according to plan? Need help first?"

#### At school

*   **Tasks:**
    *   **Step -by -step instructions:** Give the instructions sequentially, confirming the understanding of each step.
    *   **Visual duplication:** Duplicate oral tasks on a board or card.
*   **Material feed:**
    *   **Location:** Planting closer to the board, away from distracting factors.
    *   **Interactive:** Turn on short discussions, work with cards, quick polls to maintain attention.
    *   **Preparation for the answer:** Warn about the upcoming issue, giving time for concentration.
*   **Individual approach:**
    *   **Time:** Additional time for control and independent work.
    *   **Planning:** Allow me to use a draft or a plan for the answer.
    *   **Reboot:** Short outputs from class to reduce overwork.

### 4. Recommended training

These exercises are aimed at developing key functions. Apply them in a playful way.

*   **Focus:**
    *   **Correct test:** Crossing/underlining letters in the text (for example, 'a' and 'n') within 3-5 minutes. Gradually complicate.
*   **Coordination:**
    *   **Balance:** Standing on one leg, walking along the line (develops control).
    *   **Juggling:** Start with two goals (develops the 'eye-hand ”coordination and concentration).
*   **Memorization:**
    *   **What has changed?:** Remember 7-10 items in 30 seconds, then determine the change (removed/moved).
    *   **Palace of memory:** Visualization of information (for example, shopping list) in the familiar space (rooms of the house).
*   **Rhythm and control:**
    *   **Rhythm:** The exact repetition of the torn rhythm (develops a sense of time and pause).
