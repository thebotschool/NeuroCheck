## Cognitive assessment: teenager (14–18 years old)

This report reveals the teenage cognitive profile. It offers practical recommendations for creating an effective educational environment and supporting home atmosphere.

## 1. Review of testing

*   **Consistent memory (CPT).** It remembers the facts well. The development of the skills of organizing information into a logical sequence is required.
*   **Inhibitory control (Go/No-Go).** There is a tendency to impulsive actions and hasty answers.
*   **Attention and self -control (TCP).** Impulsiveness affects the decision -making and control of reactions in difficult situations.
<!-- page-break -->

## 2. Individual approach to training

### Peculiarities

*   **Pace.** The tendency to a quick, impulsive pace. Conscious slowdown and external structuring is required to prevent errors in inattention.
*   **Perception.** It more efficiently assimilates the information visually and step by step. Difficulties with long text and oral instructions.
*   **Attention.** Unstable, especially with monotonous or complex tasks. It is difficult to filter distracting factors.

### Supporting factors

*   **Visual supports.** Schemes, memory cards, checklists, infographics.
*   **Clear algorithms.** Step -by -step instructions simplify the performance of tasks.
*   **Short pauses.** Breaks (3-5 minutes after 15–20 minutes of work) help restore attention.
*   **Predictability.** Clear rules and a pre -known plan reduce anxiety.

### Calls

*   **Multitasking.** The performance of several tasks simultaneously leads to errors.
*   **Time deficiency.** Fast answers without preparation provoke impulsive reactions.
*   **Unstructured information.** Long texts, lectures without visual support, blurry formulations.
*   **Sensory overload.** Noise, activity, accumulation of people exhaust attention.
<!-- page-break -->

## 3. Practical recommendations

### For the house

*   **Homework:**
    *   **Planning.** Create a brief written plan before starting work.
    *   **Crushing of tasks.** Separate large tasks into small, controlled steps.
    *   **Visualization.** Use stickers, markers, gliders for clarity.
*   **Support and encouragement:**
    *   Mark **process**, not just the result. Example: "Great plan!", "You thought before answering."
    *   Teach technique **"Stop-think-make"**. Before the impulsive action, offer to stop, count to three.
*   **Environment organization:**
    *   The workplace should be minimalistic.
    *   Exclude background noise (TV, music) during classes.

### For school

*   **The wording of tasks:**
    *   Provide the instructions step by step.
    *   Savior large tasks for subtasks with clumsy criteria.
*   **Structuring the material:**
    *   Use checks, templates, support circuits.
    *   Select key thoughts (markers, frames, lists).
*   **Accommodation and feed:**
    *   Recommended place: in front, away from distracting factors.
    *   Style style: calm, structured, with logical pauses.
*   **Individual educational route (IOM):**
    *   The possibility of providing **support materials** (schemes, plans) on control.
    *   Recommendation **additional time** to perform tests to reduce impulsiveness.
<!-- page-break -->

## 4. Development of skills

The proposed exercises will help strengthen key cognitive functions.

*   **Attention and inhibitory control:**
    *   **Board games.** Chess, checkers, go and other strategic games requiring expectation.
    *   **Graphic dictations.** Developing instructions and sequence.
    *   **The game "Yes and no, do not say."** He trains control over speech reactions.
*   **Consistent memory and planning:**
    *   **Assembly of models.** Lego Technic, prefabricated models, complex puzzles according to the instructions.
    *   **Creating stories.** Comics, storytellers on books or events of the day, developing a logical chain.
    *   **Cooking on recipes.** Strict adherence to step -by -step instructions.
*   **Body control and rhythm:**
    *   **Balance exercises.** Standing on one leg (with eyes closed), walking along the line.
    *   **Juggling.** Develops coordination and concentration.
    *   **Conscious breathing.** 2-3 minutes a day for training the "internal pause" and reducing impulsiveness.
