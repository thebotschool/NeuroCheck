**The cognitive profile of the student**

*   High concentration
*   The need for structure
*   The development of coordination
*   (14–18 years)

This report reveals the strengths and growth zones of the student. Recommendations will help to make training more efficiently, more comfortable - at home and at school.

---

### 1. Test results

*   **Visual working memory.** Excellent memorization of individual facts and images. The need for logic and structure, and not for mechanical memorization.
*   **Inhibition and coordination.** Inter -reciprocal interaction requires development. This can affect tasks requiring simultaneous thinking and action.
*   **Sustainability of attention.** High ability to long -term concentration and self -control. The key stronger party for successful studies and projects.

<!-- page-break -->

### 2. The guide for the success of the student

#### Peculiarities

*   **Pace.** Able to work thoughtfully and for a long time. Need an internal action plan. He does not like a rush or a quick change in tasks.
*   **Perception.** Better assimilating clearly structured information. Visual supports (diagrams, lists) are more effective than continuous text or lectures.
*   **Focus of attention.** Sustainable and deep concentration. Productive if he understands the structure and purpose of the task.

#### Effective approaches

*   **Visual tips.** Mental cards, diagrams, timline, checklists.
*   **Step -by -step instructions.** Separation of complex tasks into simple and understandable steps.
*   **Algorithms and templates.** A clear plan or sample (for example, an essay structure) reduces anxiety and directs energy.
*   **Rhythm "Work-Route".** The alternation of concentration periods and short pauses (for example, the method of sores).

#### Obstacles

*   **Multitasking.** Tasks without fixing or recordings can lead to confusion.
*   **Continuous flow of information.** Long texts without paragraphs, lectures without visual support greatly complicate perception.
*   **Fast change of tasks.** The requirement to quickly switch between different types of activity depletes the planning resource.
*   **Tasks for "synchronization".** Difficulties with simultaneous listening, understanding and recording (for example, lecture abstract with graphs).

### 3. Recommendations

#### At home

*   **Organization of work.**
    *   **Plan.** Make a list of tasks in order. Refusing gives a feeling of control and progress.
    *   **One task.** On the table only materials for the current item. Minimize visual noise.
    *   **Concentration.** Effective work 30–45 minutes. Then a short (5-10 minutes) break with physical activity.
*   **Support.**
    *   **Praise the process.** Mark the ability to plan and follow the plan. Ask: "How do you plan to do this?"
    *   **Tiemers.** The visual timer helps to independently control the time.

#### At school

*   **Tasks.**
    *   Miss large projects into stages with clockwork and dates.
    *   Duplicate oral instructions in writing or on a board/presentation.
*   **Material.**
    *   Actively use visibility: diagrams, tables, diagrams. They bind a logical and figurative perception.
    *   Provide templates to perform complex work (analysis plan, report structure).
*   **Feed and place.**
    *   The style that combines an oral explanation with a visual support is effective.
    *   A good overview of the board and demonstration materials is important.
*   **Individual route.**
    *   Consider the extra time not to the decision, but on **planning** Tasks in control.
    *   Allow me to use pre-drawn up plans or checklists for answers/exams.

### 4. Training

These exercises and games develop planning skills and improve interhemispheric coordination.

*   **Attention and planning.**
    *   **Strategic board games:** Chess, go, “jackal”, “karcasson” - everything that requires the passage of the moves.
    *   **Creating instructions:** Writing step -by -step guides (how to prepare a dish, go through the level in the game).
*   **Coordination and interaction.**
    *   **Juggling:** Start with two balls. Synchronizes the brain and body.
    *   **Cross -steps:** Treat the left knee with the right hand, the left - the right. 1-2 minutes.
    *   **Game of musical instruments:** A guitar or a synthesizer requires the coordinated work of both hands.
*   **Consistent memory.**
    *   **Game "Find 10 differences":** He trains a system search.
    *   **Assembly according to the instructions:** Lego Technic, prefabricated models - develop the ability to follow the plan.
    *   **Creation of comics:** Retelling of the film, books or events in 3-5 consecutive pictures.
*   **Control and rhythm.**
    *   **Balance exercises:** Standing on one leg (you can with closed eyes), a balance book.
    *   **Dancing or martial arts:** Activity that requires coordination of the movements of the whole body in the rhythm.
