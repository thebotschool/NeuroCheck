### Cognitive profile. Development assessment.

This report provides an in -depth analysis of the cognitive features of your student. It is designed to help in revealing its potential, offering strategies for effective training and development.

---

### 1. Key results

*   **Working memory:** Great ability to memorize and maintain information. This is a significant resource for academic successes.
*   **Brake control:** The tendency to impulsive, hasty reactions was noted. Difficulties are observed with the formation of an internal pause before action.
*   **Sustainability of attention:** Significant difficulties with concentration and self -control have been identified. Perhaps this indicates the current overload of the nervous system, which affects the focus.

### 2. The guide for the success of your student

Your schoolboy has a unique combination of qualities: a quick mind and excellent memory. In this case, the development of control of reactions and stability of attention is required.

#### Individual profile

*   **Pace:** Fast but uneven. It assimilates the information promptly, but impulsiveness and distractiveness can lead to errors in carelessness.
*   **Information perception:** Optimally assimilates structured and visually presented information (diagrams, lists). Long oral explanations are perceived more complicated.
*   **Focus and concentration:** He is able to immerse himself deeply in the task of interest, but is easily distracted by external incentives and can hardly return to work.

#### Which contributes to training

*   **Step -by -step instructions:** Separation of volumetric tasks into short, understandable stages (3-4 steps).
*   **Visual supports:** The use of checklists, diagrams, highlighting keywords, short notes.
*   **The "One task at a time" mode:** Elimination of multitasking. Working space should be free from distracting items.
*   **Predictability and rhythm:** A clear schedule that determines the beginning, end and content of the work.
*   **Rule "Stop-thought-action":** Teaching a conscious pause before the answer or the performance of the task.

#### What complicates training

*   **Multitasking:** Attempts to perform several cases simultaneously reduce effectiveness.
*   **Fast change of tasks:** It takes time to transition between different activities.
*   **Long oral instructions:** Information of more than two proposals in a row can be lost due to difficulties with concentration.
*   **Noise and distracting factors:** Extraneous sounds, conversations, open tabs negatively affect productivity.
*   **Pressure and rush:** Phrases pushing to speed enhance impulsiveness and anxiety, which leads to errors.

### 3. Recommendations for development

#### Home environment

*   **Organization of at home:**
    *   Divide homework into short blocks (15–20 minutes) with 5-minute breaks for movement or rest.
    *   Use a timer to visualize working intervals.
    *   Start with the most complex or least favorite items, while the level of attention is high.
    *   Provide the "clean" workplace: only the necessary accessories. Take distracting gadgets into another room.
*   **Motivation and support:**
    *   Encourage efforts, and not just the result: "I see how you tried to concentrate," "great that you checked your work."
    *   Instead of direct criticism of impulsiveness, help analyze errors: "Let's think about where you could pause and test yourself?"
    *   Together, plan the day, recording the tasks in the form of a list. It trains self -control.

#### School environment

*   **Task wording:**
    *   The instructions should be short, clumsy, step -by -step (preferably in the form of a numbered list).
    *   Give up tasks one at a time.
    *   Select the key points in tasks (bold, emphasizing).
*   **Material feed and lesson structure:**
    *   It is recommended to place the student in the first or second desk, in the center of the class, away from windows and doors to minimize distracting factors.
    *   Use visual materials: presentations with schemes, cards, mental cards.
    *   Before the question, pause and contact a student by name for focusing.
    *   Encourage his strong working memory: offer tasks for planning, structuring information, highlighting the main.
*   **Individual educational route:**
    *   The results of tests can serve as the basis for organizing psychological and pedagogical support.
    *   It is advisable to temporarily reduce the volume of tasks requiring a long -term monotonous concentration.
    *   Provide additional time to perform control and independent work.
    *   Classes with a psychologist are recommended aimed at developing self -regulation and attention.

### 4. Practical exercises

The proposed exercises develop key skills in an interactive and affordable form.

*   **Attention and self -control:**
    *   **Correct samples:** Ask for a certain letter (for example, “a”) in a newspaper text. Start from 5 minutes, gradually increasing time.
    *   **Board strategic games:** Chess, checkers, go - develop concentration, planning and control of impulses.
    *   **Labyrinths:** The passage of complex labyrinths on paper develops stability of attention.

*   **Brake control and rhythm:**
    *   **The game "Frozhi-Otomory":** He trains the skill of quick stopping at the signal.
    *   **Graphic dictations:** Drawing according to the instructions ("Two cells to the right, one up") requires attentiveness and lack of rush.
    *   **RIGHT RIGHTS:** You set the rhythm (for example, two short blows, a pause, one long), the student repeats it exactly, withstanding pauses.

*   **Body control and coordination:**
    *   **Balance exercises:** Standing on one leg (with open, then with eyes closed), walking along the line.
    *   **Juggling:** Training in juggling with two balls develops the coordination of the "eye-hand" and concentration.
