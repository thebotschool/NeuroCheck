### **Report: Cognitive profile**

The goal of the report is to reveal the potential of your student, offering individual strategies for learning and development.

### 1. Cognitive possibilities

*   **Working memory:** The ability to remember and process information is at a high level. The student effectively builds strategies and plans actions.
*   **Self -control (braking):** High level of pulse control. Acts according to the rules, avoids hasty errors, carries out an effective self -test.
*   **Sustainability of attention:** Concentration on one task is difficult, especially with monotony or passive hearing.

<!-- page-break -->

### 2. The guide for the success of your student

Understanding these features opens the way to effective interaction and training.

#### Individual characteristics

*   **Pace:** Throbbing. High productivity with interest and understanding. Reducing the focus and pace with monotony.
*   **Perception:** Visual-structural. Effectively assimilates schemes, lists, algorithms. Oral explanations without visual support are less effective.
*   **Focus of attention:** Narrow and deep. At concentration, almost inevitable, capable of deep analysis. The task is to activate and maintain focus.

#### Supporting factors

*   **Step -by -step instructions:** A clear plan of action, divided into stages.
*   **Visual funds:** Schemes, tables, intelligence cards, marker isolation.
*   **Short sessions:** Work of intervals (25-30 min) with short breaks (5 min).
*   **Clear rhythm:** Predictable change of activity.
*   **Element of novelty:** Minor changes in the presentation of the material support interest.

#### Distracting factors

*   **Monotone:** Long lectures or the same type of exercises (more than 30 minutes).
*   **Multitasking:** Chaotic switching of tasks exhausts attention.
*   **Obstimate plan:** Tasks without a clear plan cause procrastination.
*   **Auditory overload:** Noise, conversations, music with words prevent concentrations.

<!-- page-break -->

### 3. Recommendations

Practical recommendations to support the student at home and at school.

#### Home environment

*   **Organization of homework**
    *   **Modor technique:** Working cycles 25 minutes, breaks 5 minutes. After 3-4 cycles-a long break (15-20 minutes).
    *   **Planning:** Make a list of tasks for the evening. Focus on the structure, not the control of the process. A clear plan contributes to the implementation.
    *   **One task:** On the table - only the necessary materials. Remove the phone from the access zone during operation.
*   **Support and promotion**
    *   **Praise the process:** Mark efforts and strategy: “You planned it perfectly,” “perfectly withstood the intervals.”
    *   **Discuss strategies:** Analyze successful solutions and obstacles. This develops reflection and self -control.

#### School environment

*   **The formulation of tasks**
    *   **Written instructions:** Provide the instructions in writing (board, card, chat). This provides clarity.
    *   **Decomposition of projects:** Separate large tasks into stages with intermediate terms. This prevents the emergency.
    *   **Verbs of action:** Formulate the tasks actively: “Analyze”, “Compare”, “Make” instead of “Study”.
*   **Lesson structure and workplace**
    *   **Place in the class:** The first-second desk is a minimization of visual abstracts.
    *   **Involvement:** Short questions or instructions (for example, recording on the board) activate attention.
    *   **Group work:** The role of the planner or controller is perfect.
*   **Elements of an individual educational route (IOM)**
    *   **Check lists:** Allow the use of checklists for self-testing in control work.
    *   **Condition plan:** Give a brief lecture plan in advance. This helps to monitor the course of thought and maintain focus.

### 4. Recommended training

Training for potential development, based on strengths.

#### For attention

*   **Schultte tables:** Search for numbers in order by time. Develops concentration and field of view.
*   **Search for differences:** Tasks “Find 10 differences” or searching for objects on a complex background.

#### For coordination and bodily control

*   **Juggling:** Start with two balls. Synchronizes the hemisphere, requires concentration.
*   **Balance Bord:** Exercises for the development of balance.

#### To memory and rhythm

*   **Memory game:** Develops visual memory and strategic thinking.
*   **Rhythmic exercises:** Rinse rhythms (hands, percussion). Stabilizes the system, sets the internal pace.
