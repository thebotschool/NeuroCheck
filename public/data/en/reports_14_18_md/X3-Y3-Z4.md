# Report. Cognitive assessment

This report reveals the cognitive features of the student, offering practical strategies to support in study and at home. The information is presented clearly and accessible, without complex terms.

# 1. Analysis. Cognitive abilities

## Memory and planning (CPT)
The student remembers the details well, but experiences difficulties with building them in the sequence. For effective memorization, a clear structure and logic is necessary.

## Self-control (Go/No-Go)
The insufficient consistency of the work of the cerebral hemispheres was noted. This can complicate the fulfillment of tasks requiring simultaneous use of logical and figurative thinking.

## Sustainability of attention
Significant difficulties with concentration and retention of attention have been identified. This indicates a possible mental or emotional overload, requiring attention and, if necessary, consulting a specialist.

# 2. Understanding. Training profile

This leadership will help to understand how the schoolboy assimilates the information and which educational environment is most suitable for him.

## Features of training

### Pace
The student prefers a leisurely, measured pace of work. Hurrying and harsh deadlines cause stress and reduce quality.

### Perception of information
It is best absorbed by information through visual schemes, step -by -step lists, algorithms, tables. Long oral explanations or continuous text are perceived with difficulty.

### Focus of attention
Attention is unstable. The student is easily distracted, quickly gets tired of the mental stress, can “fall out” from the process with volumetric or uninteresting tasks.

## Which helps

*   Step -by -step instructions: dividing large tasks into 2-3 small stages.
*   Visual supports: diagrams, mental cards, checklists, recovery stickers.
*   A clear algorithm: a ready -made plan or a sample of the task.
*   Structured environment: order in the workplace, a minimum of distracting factors.
*   Predictable rhythm: alternation of work (15–20 minutes) and short breaks (5 minutes).

## What interferes

*   Multivativity: simultaneously performing several tasks reduces productivity.
*   Fast change of tasks: sharp switching between topics make it difficult to concentrate.
*   Long oral explanations: such information is not kept in memory due to the complexity of building logic.
*   Noise and irritants: distracting sounds and notifications reduce concentration.
*   Abstract tasks: tasks without a clear plan or example cause confusion.

# 3. Action. Recommendations

## For the house

### Homework
*   Make a plan for the evening: no more than 1-2 items.
*   Savior tasks into stages: read the paragraph, write out dates, answer the question.
*   Use the timer: 20 minutes of work, 5 minutes of break.
*   Organize the workplace: only a textbook, notebook, pen. The phone is in another room.

### Support and motivation
*   Praise for the process and efforts, and not just for the result.
*   Help find personal memorization strategies: drawing comics, schemes.
*   Consider the resource of attention: no more than 15–20 minutes for a difficult task.

## For school

### The formulation of tasks
*   Duplicate oral instructions in writing (board, chat).
*   Use clear verbs: “Write out”, “Emphasize”, “Make a table”.
*   Take a sample or plan (3-5 points) to volumetric tasks.

### Organization of the lesson
*   Optimal place: the first desk, away from distracting factors.
*   Enjoy short direct questions for the return of the focus.
*   Use visual elements: diagrams, graphs, short videos, color selection.

### Individual route
*   Indicators require the development of a psychological and pedagogical support plan.
*   It is possible to temporarily reduce homework.
*   Additional time is recommended for control work.
*   The help of a tutor or psychologist in the planning of educational activities will be effective.

# 4. Development. Recommended training

These exercises will help to develop key cognitive functions. It is recommended to engage in 10-15 minutes daily, in a playful way.

## Attention and self -control

*   Corrective tests: removal of certain letters or words in the text.
*   Schultte tables: Search for numbers in order by time.
*   Find the differences: develops concentration on the details.

## Memory and planning

*   Redections: Illustration of the events of the day or plot of the film in the correct sequence (4-6 pictures).
*   Retelling of the day back: reproduction of the events of the day from the current moment to the morning.
*   Strategic games: board games requiring the planning of moves (for example, Jackal, Ticket to Ride).

## Coordination and motility

*   Crossroads: touching the left knee with the right hand, with the left hand - the right. Perform slowly.
*   Juggling: Start with two balls. Synchronizes the work of the hemispheres.
*   Rhythmics: tapping or cooling the given rhythm, from simple to complex.
