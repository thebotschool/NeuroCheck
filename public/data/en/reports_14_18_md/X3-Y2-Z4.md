### **Cognitive profile: a report for parents and teachers**

This report provides a deep understanding of the individual cognitive characteristics of the student 14-18 years. Its goal is to propose practical recommendations for creating an optimal environment of training and development based on the results of cognitive diagnostics.

### 1. Test results
*   **Memory (CPT).** The assimilation of individual facts is effective, but consistent reproduction causes difficulties. Support is required in the structuring of information.
*   **Self-control (Go/No-Go).** The tendency to impulsive reactions is noted. The skill of thinking before action requires development, especially in dynamic situations.
*   **Attention (TCP).** Difficulties with concentration and stability of attention have been identified. Overload or anxiety affecting concentration is likely. This is a key aspect that requires attention and, possibly, consulting a specialist.
<!-- page-break -->

### 2. The guide for the success of your student
#### Individual characteristics
*   **Pace.** The tendency to impulsiveness. The calm, predictable rhythm without hard pressure is optimal. A quick pace reduces the accuracy of the task.
*   **Perception.** The visually structured format is preferred. Long texts and oral instructions without schemes are difficult to assimilate.
*   **Focus.** Attention is unstable and easily exhausted. Long -term concentration on monotonous or complex tasks is difficult.

#### Which contributes to success
*   **Step -by -step instructions.** Clear, numbered algorithms of action.
*   **Visual support.** Schemes, intelligence cards, infographics for clarity.
*   **Control lists.** Check lines for tracking progress reduce the load as a memory.
*   **Time management.** Short working intervals (15–20 minutes) with regular breaks (3-5 minutes).
*   **Speaking.** The voicing of the action plan helps to slow down and structure thoughts.

#### What creates barriers
*   **Multitasking.** The simultaneous performance of several tasks or frequent switching leads to errors.
*   **Unstructured information.** Long text blocks, oral lectures without visual supports.
*   **Abstract instructions.** Tasks without specific examples or samples.
*   **Distracting factors.** Noise and external activity significantly reduce productivity.
<!-- page-break -->

### 3. Recommendations
#### Home environment
*   **Organization of work.**
    *   Joint planning of the evening in a checklist format.
    *   Priority for complex tasks at the beginning.
    *   Miss large tasks into small steps. For example, the "Project on History" is transformed in: "Find 3 sources. Write 5 dates. Write an introduction."
    *   Use a timer for working sessions (for example, 20 minutes of work, 5 minutes of rest).
*   **Support and motivation.**
    *   Praise for efforts and the chosen strategy, and not just for the result.
    *   Develop the skill of "pause": "Stop. Let's think about what the next step."
    *   The optimal duration of focused attention is 15–20 minutes, after which a short break is needed.
*   **Reducing the load.**
    *   Provide enough time for relaxation and sleep. Fatigue enhances cognitive difficulties.

#### School environment
*   **Setting tasks.**
    *   The instructions must be written and step -by -step.
    *   Use clear verbs of action: “emphasize”, “write down”, “compare”.
    *   Provide samples of tasks.
*   **The structure of the lesson.**
    *   Duplicate the key ideas of the lesson on the board (diagrams, lists).
    *   Make logical pauses between information blocks.
    *   Check the understanding of the task: "Repeat, please, what needs to be done first?"
*   **Workplace.**
    *   Optimally-the first-second desk, away from distracting factors (window, door).
*   **Individual educational route (IOM).**
    *   Based on the results, consider the development of IOM.
    *   Possible elements: additional time on tests, the use of a pre -prepared plan, phased surrender of tasks.
    *   The involvement of a school psychologist and interdisciplinary discussion for a single support strategy are recommended.

### 4. Developing practices
These exercises and activity contribute to the development of key cognitive skills. Regularity and positive approach are important.
#### For attention and self -control
*   **Board games.** Games requiring the expectation of the course and control of impulses: "jenga", chess, checkers.
*   **Exercise "Pause".** Before starting an action or an answer - a slow breath and exhale for thinking.
*   **Games to the reaction.** For example, "edible, unique" for training a quick reaction and its inhibition.

#### For sequence and planning
*   **Design.** The assembly of LEGO or models according to the instructions is the perfect practice of following the algorithm.
*   **Cooking.** Fulfillment of recipes as a way of mastering step -by -step tasks.
*   **Visual narrative.** Creation of comics or recitroaches for the retelling of the plot or the events of the day in the correct sequence.

#### For coordination and bodily control
*   **Juggling.** Even two balls - develops concentration, coordination and a sense of rhythm.
*   **Balance exercises.** Walking in a straight line, a stand on one leg - improve the awareness and control of the body.
