# Cognitive assessment report: CPT + Go/No-Go + TCP (Age: 14–18 years)

### 1. Analysis of tests

*   **CPT.** Difficulties with keeping attention and working memory.
*   **Go/No-Go (braking and control test):** Difficulties with braking and control of impulsive reactions.
*   **TCP (test for complex attention):** Confirms the general difficulties with attention and self -control, enhanced by multitasking.

### 2. The guide for the success of your student

This guide will help to build effective interaction with the child in the learning process.

#### Individual profile

*   **Pace:** A measured pace of work is required. Hashing causes errors and frustration.
*   **Perception:** The best assimilation of information is visually and step by step. Abstract oral instructions are ineffective without a visual support.
*   **Attention:** Unstable, quickly exhausted. The concentration on one task is effective up to 15-20 minutes, then the overload occurs.

#### Effective methods

*   **Visual supports:** Cards with rules, diagrams, intelligence cards, highlighting keywords.
*   **Step -by -step instructions:** Clear algorithms of actions (1. Do it. 2. Do that. 3. Check yourself). Check lists.
*   **Predictability and rhythm:** A clear order of action reduces anxiety, maintains attention resources. It is important to designate the beginning and end of the task.
*   **Singlety:** One task at one point in time.
*   **Silence:** Minimization of distracting factors (sound, visual noise).

#### Potential obstacles

*   **Multitasking:** The requirement of the simultaneous performance of several actions leads to overload.
*   **Fast change of activity:** Switching without a pause does not give time to perestroika.
*   **Oral instructions:** Long monologues are absorbed fragmented.
*   **Time pressure:** The installation of hard time frames causes stress and blocks thinking.
*   **Incompture:** The lack of a plan makes you spend efforts on finding an algorithm instead of solving the problem.

### 3. Personal recommendations

#### For the house

*   **Organization of work:**
    *   Miss large tasks into small, understandable steps. Example: instead of “Make mathematics” - “Solve problem No. 1, rest, then solve examples.”
    *   Use the timer: 15-20 minutes of work, 5-10 minutes an active break.
    *   Create a “clean zone” on the desktop: remove extra objects.
    *   Make a plan-bank list before starting work. Mark the executed points.
*   **Support and encouragement:**
    *   Encourage the process and effort, not just the result. Example: "I see, you tried to concentrate!" Or "excellent, you followed the plan."
    *   Instead of criticism "Why is a mistake again?" Use the approach: "Let's see what went wrong, and we’ll figure it out together."
*   **Attention:** Effective concentration on the task is up to 15-20 minutes. Then a short break without gadgets is needed.

#### For school

*   **The wording of tasks:**
    *   The instructions should be brief, clumsy, consisting of 1-2 steps.
    *   Use the imperative mood: “Read the text. Emphasize the names of the heroes. Write down in a notebook. "
    *   Duplicate oral tasks in writing (board, card).
*   **Lesson structure and feed:**
    *   Optimal place: the first desk in the central row to minimize abstracts.
    *   At the beginning of the lesson, voice and write down a brief plan.
    *   Use visual supports: diagrams, tables, presentations with a minimum of text.
*   **IOOM elements (individual educational route):**
    *   Additional time for control and independent work.
    *   Allocate assistants with algorithms/rules.
    *   It is recommended that the support of the specialist (psychologist, tutor) and appeal to the PMPK to formalize the conditions of training are recommended.

### 4. Practical training

Exercises for the development of key functions. It is recommended to practice 10-15 minutes daily.

#### For attention and memory

*   **Graphic dictations:** They train auditory attention and follow the instructions.
*   **The game "What has changed?":** Remember 5-7 items. Remove or change in places alone. Task: determine the change.
*   **Correct samples:** Cross out certain letters in the text, circle others.

#### For control and reaction

*   **The game "edible is inedible":** Develops a quick reaction and inhibition.
*   **The game "Yes and there is no talk":** He trains the control of speech reactions.
*   **RIGHT RIGHTS:** The exact repetition of the rhythm (including pauses).

#### For coordination and balance

*   **Balance exercises:** Walking along the line, stand on one leg (with open/closed eyes).
*   **The game "Sea is worried once":** Holding a complex pose of 10-15 seconds.
*   **Simultaneous multidirectional movements:** (for example, stroking your head and knocking on your stomach).
