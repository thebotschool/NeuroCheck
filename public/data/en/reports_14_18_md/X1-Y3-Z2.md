# Cognitive profile: ABV-123

This report provides an in -depth look at the cognitive features of your student. The goal is to identify strengths and growth zones to create a more effective and comfortable educational environment. Recommendations are based on the results of complex testing.

## 1. Test results

*   **Working memory.** An excellent ability to memorize information, planning and concentration. This is a key strong side, the basis for successful training and problem solving.

*   **Inhibition and coordination.** The insufficient consistency of the work of the cerebral hemispheres has been revealed. This can complicate the simultaneous processing of different -type information.

*   **Sustainability of attention.** Attention is unstable. Difficulties with a long concentration are possible, especially on monotonous or oral information. Fast fatigue can lead to a decrease in involvement.

## 2. Profile of learning

### Key features

*   **Pace.** Work rhythm: "Sprinter, not a marathon." High productivity in short intervals, rapid exhaustion at a monotonous load.
*   **Perception format.** Optimally perceives clear, structured and visual information (diagrams, lists). Difficulties with simultaneous processing of text, numbers and images without external support.
*   **Focus of attention.** Attention is "impulsive." Deep immersion in interesting tasks, but is easily distracted by noise, multitasking or fatigue. An external structure is needed to maintain concentration.

### Supporting factors

*   **Step -by -step instructions.** Clear checks and algorithms.
*   **Visual supports.** Mind cards, schemes, infographics, highlighting keywords. Help systematize information.
*   **Work on the timer.** Short intervals (25/5 minutes) to maintain productivity.
*   **Silence and order.** The minimum number of distracting factors at the workplace.

### Distracting factors

*   **Multitasking.** The implementation of several tasks simultaneously reduces the quality of work.
*   **Long oral instructions.** A large amount of information on ear overloads attention.
*   **Unstructured tasks.** The lack of a clear plan and criteria causes difficulties.
*   **Background noise.** Talks, music, TV significantly reduce concentration.

<!-- page-break -->

## 3. Recommendations

### For the house

*   **Organization of work.**
    *   Use a timer (for example, a prying): 25 minutes of work, 5 minutes of a break.
    *   Start with the most difficult tasks when the resource of attention is maximum.
    *   Make a written plan for the evening. Mark the executed points for control and motivation.
*   **Support and promotion.**
    *   Praise the process, not just the result: “How well you planned everything!”, “Great scheme turned out!”
    *   Instead of a direct question about the lessons, ask: "What was difficult today? How can you break this task?"
*   **The regime of the day.**
    *   A stable sleep, nutrition and physical activity mode is important. Fatigue directly affects attention.

### For school

*   **The formulation of tasks.**
    *   Provide written tasks, divided into 2-3 specific steps.
    *   Start the points with the verbs: "Read", "Write out", "Compare", "draw a conclusion."
*   **The structure of the lesson and material.**
    *   Sound the lesson plan at the beginning: "Today we will do: 1, 2, 3".
    *   Use visual supports (diagrams, tables, keywords) on the board or in the presentation.
    *   Place the student closer to the board, away from distracting factors (windows, doors).
*   **IOM elements.**
    *   Allow the use of checklists and plans for control work.
    *   Let me rely on a short plan or a diagram in an oral answer.

## 4. Training for development

### Attention and self -control

*   **Schultte tables.** Develop concentration and peripheral vision.
*   **Respiratory exercises.** Techniques for quick reassurance and focusing (for example, "square breath").

### Inter -recliped coordination

*   **Cross -steps.** Alternate touching the left knee with the right hand, with the left hand - the right. 1-2 minutes.
*   **Juggling.** Develops the coordination of the "eye-hand" and the connection between the hemispheres.
*   **Mirror drawing.** Simultaneous drawing of symmetrical figures with both hands.

### Bodily control and rhythm

*   **Game on a musical instrument.** Especially the piano or guitar, requiring the coordinated work of both hands.
*   **Balance brigade or balancing pillow.** Equilibrium exercises activate the cerebellum associated with the functions of attention.
