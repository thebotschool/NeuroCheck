# Cognitive profile of the student: report

This report provides an analysis of the strengths and areas for the development of a student, based on the results of cognitive testing. Recommendations are designed to support the learning process and everyday life.

<!-- page-break -->

## 1. Analysis of cognitive indicators

### Assessment by tests

*   **Attention for stability (CPT):** Paying attention can be unstable, especially with fatigue or monotonous tasks. This affects the pace and concentration.
*   **Braking and coordination test (Go/No-Go):** Asymmetry was noted in the coordination between the left and right hemispheres. This can complicate the fulfillment of tasks requiring comprehensive integration of logic and creativity.
*   **Test of visual working memory:** Visual memory is well developed. However, difficulties may arise with memorizing small details when overloading information or distraction. At the same time, effective personal memorization strategies are used.

<!-- page-break -->

## 2. Individual learning profile

### Features of training

*   **Pace:** The pace of work is changed. Productivity is reduced with fatigue or the same tasks. Effective rhythm "Work-Remado".
*   **Perception:** Preferably structured visual presentation of information. Long texts or chaotic schemes can cause overload. Information should be divided into logical blocks.
*   **Attention:** The focus of attention is unstable. It is easily distracted by external and internal stimuli. It is difficult to maintain prolonged concentration and filter the excess.

### Support factors

*   Clear, step -by -step instructions.
*   Visual supports: checklists, diagrams, intelligence cards, highlighting keywords.
*   Focusing on one task.
*   Short breaks (for example, in the technique of prying).
*   Support for the conscious use of personal memorization strategies.

### Barriers in training

*   Multitasking.
*   Long oral explanations without visual support.
*   Visual noise: a mess on the desktop, overloaded slides.
*   High pace and haste when switching tasks.

<!-- page-break -->

## 3. Development recommendations

### Home environment

*   **Homework organization:**
    *   Break large tasks into parts. Create a plan for the evening (for example, "15 minutes of mathematics, 5 minutes break, 20 minutes English").
    *   Use a timer to control time.
    *   Provide a clean workplace where there is nothing superfluous.
*   **Support and encouragement:**
    *   Praise for effort and process, and not just for the result. ("I see you tried very hard to concentrate", "A great plan for writing!").
    *   Discuss effective strategies for memorization.
*   **Mercing of attention:** The expected period of focused work is 20–25 minutes. After that, a short (3-5 minutes) break with motor activity is needed.

### School adaptation

*   **The wording of tasks:**
    *   Instructions to provide in writing or duplicate on the board (numbered/marked lists).
    *   Clearly highlight the main thing in tasks. ("It is important to pay attention to the dates").
*   **Structuring the material:**
    *   Use schemes, tables, diagrams when explaining new material.
    *   Offer templates for abstracts or intelligence cards to prepare for control.
*   **Working space:** The optimal place is the front desk, away from distracting factors (window, door).
*   **Individual route:**
    *   Consider the possibility of providing additional time in control work.
    *   Allow the use of checklist checks for self-testing.

## 4. A set of training

These exercises are aimed at developing key cognitive functions. It is recommended to perform them regularly, in a playful way, without coercion.

### Development of attention

*   **Correct samples:** Crossing certain letters or words in the text (start from 5 minutes).
*   **Schultte tables:** Search for numbers in order by time. He trains concentration and peripheral vision.

### Coordination and integration of hemispheres

*   **Cross -steps:** Alternate touching with the right hand of the left knee and the left hand of the right. Perform slowly, rhythmically.
*   **Simultaneous drawing:** Drawing symmetrical or different figures with both hands at the same time.
*   **Juggling:** Start with two items. Promotes synchronization of the hemispheres.

### Visual memory and detail

*   **Game "Find 10 differences":** Classic exercise for attention to details.
*   **The game "Memory" ("Find a couple"):** Search for paired cards. He trains memory.
*   **Mnemics:** Creation of visual associations or short stories to remember information (for example, a list of purchases presented in a funny scene).

### Bodily control and rhythm

*   **Tapping rhythms:** Reproduction of a given rhythm. Complicate gradually.
*   **Table tennis, badminton:** Games requiring a quick reaction, coordination of the "eye-hand" and holding attention.
