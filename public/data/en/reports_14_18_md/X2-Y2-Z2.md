# Cognitive development report
## Analysis of the ability of a student (age: 14–18 years)

*Understanding the strengths and growth zones. Personalized recommendations for parents and teachers.*

---

# 1. Review of tests

*   **Visual memory (CPT):** Effectively assimilates general information. It requires attention to the details, especially with fatigue or distraction.
*   **Impulse control (Go/No-Go):** A tendency to a quick reaction. The development of brake control will enhance the deliberation of actions.
*   **Sustainability of attention (TCP):** The concentration weakens with a long or monotonous load. Attention requires support.

# 2. The guide for the success of your student

## Features of training
*   **Pace:** Quick start, but decreased productivity with monotony. The optimal "sprints" with pauses are optimal.
*   **Perception:** The best assimilation in a combined format (visual + audial). Avoid overloading with text or only oral lectures.
*   **Focus of attention:** Easily distracted by external stimuli. It requires a clear structure and dynamics in tasks.

## Effective strategies
*   **Planning:** Check lists, step-by-step instructions help to keep the focus and details.
*   **Visualization:** Schemes, intelligence cards, discharge, short videos consolidate information.
*   **Pause:** The rule "Stop-think-make" for self-testing before action.
*   **Time management:** The Pomodoro technique (25/5 minutes) maintains a pace and prevents fatigue.
*   **Organization:** Minimalism at the workplace, the lack of distracting objects.

## Obstacles in learning
*   **Multitasking:** Reduces speed and quality of work.
*   **Long oral instructions:** It does not completely perceive information without visual support.
*   **Rush:** The pressure of time provokes impulsive errors.
*   **Monotone:** Classes for more than 30–40 minutes turn off attention without a break.
*   **Noise and distraction:** Open tabs, notifications, conversations interfere with concentration.

# 3. Recommendations

## Home environment
*   **Homework:**
    *   Break into blocks for 25-30 minutes with 5-minute breaks.
    *   Make a simple plan before starting work.
    *   After completing, offer a 10-15 minute pause for a “fresh look” and self-testing.
*   **Support:**
    *   Mark the process and strategies, and not just the result.
    *   Organize the workplace: remove the excess, phone in another room.
*   **Concentration:** The maximum deep concentration time is 25-30 minutes. Follow the mode.

## School environment
*   **Setting tasks:**
    *   Formulate the tasks briefly, step by step. Duplicate oral instructions in writing.
*   **Lesson structure:**
    *   Alternate activities every 15–20 minutes.
    *   Report important information at the beginning of the lesson.
*   **Accommodation and feed:**
    *   Place the student closer to the teacher, away from distracting factors.
    *   Use visual supports (diagrams, tables) with brief comments.
*   **Individual approach:**
    *   Give additional time (5-10 minutes) for the final check on the control.
    *   Allow the use of checklist or plans for design work.

# 4. Training for development
Developing practices and games.

## Attention and perception
*   Board games for concentration and search for details: **"Dobble"**, **"Set"**, **"Corridor"**, **"Find a couple" (Memory)**.

## Impulse management
*   Games: **"FROM ATOMENT"**, **"The sea is worried once"**.
*   Rhythmic exercises: Rhythm taping with a conscious pass of every third/fourth blow for training brake function.

## Memory and organization of information
*   **Mnemics:** Creation of stories, abbreviations, visual associations for memorization.
*   **Intellect-cards (Mind Maps):** Practice of compilation in texts or films.

## Coordination and bodily control
*   Balance exercises (stand on one leg, walking along the line), juggling. Improve the connection of the "eye-hand" and self-control.
