# Cognitive test report
### Results for the student 14-18 years old

This report is intended for parents and teachers. He offers an understanding of the strengths of the student, identifies areas for development and recommends steps to support learning. The information is presented simply and clearly.

---

# 1. Test results

*   **CPT (attention and memory).** Systemic assimilation of information. The tendency to confuse similar details with fatigue or distraction.
*   **Go/No-Go (self-control).** Difficulties with the suppression of impulsive reactions. Hasty errors that are aware of post -facts are possible.
*   **TCP (stability of attention).** Quick exhaustion of attention. The risk of "falling out" from monotonous or oral work, the passage of important information.

# 2. The guide for the success of the student

## Features of training

*   **Pace.** He begins confidently, but quickly tires. This leads to haste and impulsiveness. Time pressure reduces quality.
*   **Perception format.** It better assimilates visual, structured information: schemes, tables, checklists. Oral instructions without visual support are remembered worse.
*   **Focus of attention.** Short, unstable. It is easily distracted by external events or own thoughts. Included to lose the thread in long explanations.

## Which contributes to progress

*   Step -by -step instructions. Division of large tasks into 3-4 small steps.
*   Visual supports. Cards with rules, text highlighting, a brief lesson plan.
*   A clear rhythm. Work in short intervals (for example, 20 minutes of focus, 5 minutes of rest).
*   Predictability, structure. A well -known plan organized workplace.
*   One task at a time. Focus on one action without switching.

## What prevents

*   Multitasking. Simultaneous listening, recording, tracking the presentation.
*   Hass, pressure of time. The main reason for errors in inattention.
*   Long oral explanations. More than 5-7 minutes without interactive or visual support.
*   Noise, mess. Distracting factors (telephone, conversations, chaos) reduce productivity.

# 3. Recommendations

## At home

*   **Homework.**
    *   Savior tasks into parts. Example: "First 3 examples, then 5 minutes of rest, then the next 2".
    *   Use a timer (for example, a method of prying) to visualize periods of work and rest.
    *   Organize a quiet, permanent workplace. Remove the phone from the access zone.
*   **Support, motivation.**
    *   Praise for the process: "I see, you followed the plan," "excellent that you noticed and corrected the error."
    *   Help with planning: in the evening, make a list of affairs for tomorrow, check in the morning.
*   **Holding attention.**
    *   Realistic concentration on a difficult task: **15–20 minutes**. Next is a short break (get up, stretch).

## At school

*   **The formulation of tasks.**
    *   Let's clear the instructions, step by step. Duplicate oral speech with entries on the board or in a printout.
    *   At the beginning of the lesson, voice a short plan: "Today we will check your homework, analyze a new topic, we will complete the exercise."
*   **Structure, presentation of material.**
    *   Use visual supports: diagrams, intelligence cards, tables, color release.
    *   Place the student at the first or second desk. This minimizes distraction and provides visual contact.
    *   Apply interactive methods: short questions to verify understanding ("What is the next step? Repeat").
*   **Elements of an individual educational route (IOM).**
    *   On control work, provide **extra time** for execution and verification.
    *   Allow use **Draft-plan** Or a checklist for self-testing.
    *   Crusting volumetric control to parts, if possible.

# 4. Recommended training

These exercises develop growth zones. The key is regularity: it is better than 10 minutes daily than an hour once a week.

*   **For attention, self -control.**
    *   Schultte tables. Search for numbers in order. Develops concentration, expands the field of view.
    *   Graphic dictations. Exercises "left-right-up-down" under dictation. They train auditory attention, action control.
    *   Board games. They require concentration and following the rules: "corridor", "set", "vibble".
*   **For coordination, bodily control.**
    *   Juggling. Start with two goals. Improves the coordination of the "hands-eye", a sense of rhythm.
    *   Balance exercises. Standing on one leg: first with open, then with eyes closed.
    *   Rhythmic exercises. Tapping or cooling the specified rhythm according to the model.
*   **For visual memory.**
    *   The game "Memory". Classical game to memorize the location of the pictures.
    *   Exercise "Photographer". Remember 5-7 items, turn away and list. Gradually increase the quantity.
    *   Creation of intelligence cards. Helps to visually structure and remember the material.
