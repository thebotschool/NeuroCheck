# Cognitive profile report: 14–18 years old

This report is an individual cognitive profile. It will help to reveal the potential and create optimal conditions for training and development.

---

## Analysis of abilities

### Memory: Consistent thinking (CPT)
Successful assimilation of facts and details. The development of the skills of structuring information and its consistent reproduction is required. Attempts to use strategies for memorization are noted, which is a positive sign.

### Self-control: reaction and thinking (Go/No-Go)
The tendency to quick, impulsive reactions. It is important to develop the ability to a short pause before action, especially in conditions of stress or, if necessary, a quick solution.

### Attention: Focus and stability (TCP)
The instability of concentration and the pace of work is noted. Fast fatigue with monotonous or long -term tasks is possible. This can affect the involvement in the educational process.

## Success Guide

### Individual characteristics
*   **Rhythm:** Uneven pace of work. A quick start is possible with subsequent slowdown, especially with monotonous tasks.
*   **Perception:** The preference for structured and visual information (diagrams, lists, maps). Long oral explanations without clarity are more complicated.
*   **Focus:** Attention can be unstable. Distraction with monotony or prolonged concentration. High involvement in the field of interest.

### Success factors
*   **Visualization:** Schemes, timline, infographics, comics. Any means helping to see the structure of information.
*   **Step -by -step approach:** Separation of large tasks into small, understandable steps. The use of checklists to track progress.
*   **The principle of "internal pause":** Mastering a short delay ("3 seconds") before the reaction or action. This reduces impulsiveness.
*   **Interval work:** Short work sessions with mandatory breaks (for example, 25/5 minutes according to the method of prying).

### Risk factors
*   **Multitasking:** The simultaneous implementation of several cases or frequent switching between them leads to errors.
*   **Monotone:** Long -term oral lectures without visual support quickly reduce attention.
*   **Pressure:** Phrases like "faster" or "is just" provoke impulsiveness and stress.
*   **Lack of structure:** Infusal information (texts, slides) makes it difficult to understand the priorities.

## Support recommendations

### Home environment
*   **Organization of work:**
    *   Break homework into blocks for 20–25 minutes. Turn on 5-minute breaks with physical activity.
    *   Together draw up a work plan: "Read, perform, check."
    *   Use checklists or stickers to visualize the tasks performed.
*   **Support and dialogue:**
    *   In case of errors, instead of criticism, ask the questions: "What do you think, why did it happen? Let's analyze it together."
    *   Encourage for the sequence, accuracy and self -test, and not for speed.
    *   Teach the planning: "What is the main thing in the essay? Let's put out the plan."
*   **Realistic expectations:** Effective concentration on one task is 20–25 minutes. Exceeding this limit can be ineffective.

### School environment
*   **Task wording:**
    *   Provide instructions in the form of algorithms or numbered lists.
    *   Give the time to think before the answer or the control. Encourage drafts.
*   **Lesson structure:**
    *   Use visual supports (lesson plan, diagrams) on the board or slides.
    *   Structure tests and control, dividing them into blocks with clear formulations.
    *   Recommended place: Closer to the teacher, away from distracting factors (window, door).
*   **Individualization:**
    *   Consider the possibility of increasing time for testing for thorough self -testing.
    *   Allow the use of templates, diagrams or checklists for volumetric written works.

## Developing practices

### Attention and memory
*   **"Find a couple" (Memory):** Develops visual memory and concentration.
*   **Intellect-cards (Mind Maps):** Teaches to structure data and see the relationship, not individual facts.
*   **Retelling through a comic:** Visualization of the key scenes of the film or book in sequential order.

### Self -control and rhythm
*   **The game "Delay!":** Moving to music with an instant stop when turning it off. He trains the braking reaction.
*   **Juggling:** Starting with two goals. Improves coordination, a sense of rhythm and concentration.
*   **Strategic Games:** Chess, checkers, go. Develop planning and prevent impulsive solutions.

### Bodily administration
*   **Exercise "conductor":** Repetition of movement with a delay. Tracks switching and braking.
*   **Balance Bord / Walking along the line:** Develops balance and improves bodily control.
