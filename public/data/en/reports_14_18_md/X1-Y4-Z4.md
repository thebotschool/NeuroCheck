## Cognitive assessment report

### Attention, self -control and working memory (14–18 years)

This report is based on the results of three cognitive tests that evaluate the key brain functions that are important for training and behavior. Its goal is not a diagnosis, but the provision of practical tools to support the schoolboy at home and at school.

An important feature has been revealed: high potential (excellent working memory, strategic thinking) is combined with difficulties in concentration and impulse control. This means that the student is capable of deep analysis, but requires a special organization to realize his potential.

### 1. Review of tests

*   **Attention:** Difficulties with a long concentration, holding attention and maintaining mental performance.
*   **Self -control:** Significant difficulties with inhibition of reactions and the prevention of impulsive actions.
*   **Visual working memory:** The test is perfect. High potential for memorization, analysis and planning in the presence of a focus.

<!-- page-break -->

### 2. The guide for the success of your student

#### Features of training

*   **Pace:** Uneven. An external incentive is required to start work. At the same time, on an interesting task, the student is able to work quickly. A quick change in tasks depletes.
*   **Perception:** The visual channel prevails. The best assimilation of information occurs through schemes, tables, intelligence cards, highlighted keywords, short videos. Long texts or oral monologues are difficult to absorb.
*   **Focus of attention:** Unrestrained and exhausted. It is easily distracted by external noises, movements and inner thoughts.

#### Support: what works

*   **Step -by -step instructions.** Detailed steps, checklists.
*   **Visual supports.** Tiemers, algorithms, painters-recalling.
*   **Rhythm and predictability.** The alternation of short working periods (15–25 minutes) with physical breaks (3-5 minutes).
*   **The only task.** A clearly defined and only goal in focus.

#### Obstacles: what is interfering

*   **Multitasking.** Parallel performance of several actions leads to failures.
*   **Long oral instructions.** More than two consistent actions are not remembered by ear.
*   **Monotone.** A long work of the same type causes fatigue and error.
*   **Sensory overload.** Noise and distracting background factors reduce the resource of attention.

<!-- page-break -->

### 3. Recommendations

#### House

1.  **Organization of work**
    *   **Crushing the tasks.** Divide large tasks into specific, measurable steps. Make a plan together.
    *   **Use the timer.** Method Methods: 25 minutes of concentrated work, then 5 minutes of break.
    *   **Start with complicated.** Perform difficult tasks while the resource of attention is maximum.
    *   **Prepare a place.** Take out the excess. Put the phone into another room at the time of the task.

2.  **Support and promotion**
    *   **Praise for the process.** Mark efforts, not just the result.
    *   **Be predictable.** Keep calm. Criticism enhances the alarm and control problems.
    *   **Expectations.** A realistic period of continuous concentration on a complex educational task: 15–25 minutes.

#### School

1.  **Setting and structure of tasks**
    *   **Clear step -by -step instructions.** Give the tasks in the format "do once, do two." Duplicate oral instructions written.
    *   **Structuring the material.** Use schemes, intelligence cards, presentations with a minimum of text. Select key concepts.
    *   **Accommodation in the classroom.** Optimal: the first-second desk in the central row, to minimize distracting factors.

2.  **Style style and individual approach**
    *   **Interactivity.** Enjoy the student in dialogue, ask short questions to keep attention.
    *   **Elements of an individual route:**
        *   An additional time to perform control and independent work.
        *   The ability to do work in a more calm environment.
        *   The use of pre-prepared templates and checklists for design and creative work.

### 4. Development of skills

These exercises in a playful way will help to develop key functions. Regularity is more important than the duration.

*   **Attention and self -control:**
    *   **Board games:** “Dobble”, “high -speed caps”, “Cow 006”. They train a quick reaction and impulse inhibition.
    *   **Graphic dictations / Corrective tests.** Exercises for crossing or stroke of symbols in the text according to a certain rule.

*   **Coordination:**
    *   **Balansboard / Balance pillow.** 5-10 minutes a day of standing on an unstable surface, a cerebellum associated with the control of movements is developed.
    *   **Juggling.** Start with two goals. Develops the coordination of the "eye-hand" and concentration.

*   **Memory and rhythm:**
    *   **Game on drums / cachon.** Removing and repetition of rhythms develop a sense of rhythm and self -regulation.
    *   **The game "Memory" ("Find a couple").** Uses visual memory, requires attention and concentration for success.
