# Cognitive assessment: revealing potential
Visual memory, self -control, attention. Age: 14-18 years.

This report is your guide on the unique abilities of the student. It reveals the strengths and denotes growth areas, offering practical recommendations for harmonious development in study and life.

## 1.

*   **Visual working memory (CPT):** The strategy of memorization is developed. The accuracy of details and resistance to distraction are growth zones.
*   **Self-control (Go/No-Go):** Difficulties with braking impulsive reactions and maintaining attention according to the instructions were noted.
*   **General attention (TCP):** Signs of overload of the nervous system. Concentration and self-control requires effort, possibly due to stress or load.

## 2. Success Guide

Understanding the unique features of your student is the key to his development.

### Features of perception

*   **The rhythm of work.** Effective on clear tasks. Slow down with complexity, volume or frequent switching.
*   **Perception format.** Optimal for structured and visual information (diagrams, tables, lists). Sensitive to information noise.
*   **Focus of attention.** Prone to external and internal distraction. Concentration with high accuracy or impulsive answers requires effort.

### Which maintains effectiveness

*   **A step -by -step approach.** Division of tasks into controlled stages, the use of checklists.
*   **Visual support.** Schemes, intelligence cards, color coding, icons.
*   **The optimal rhythm.** Work on intervals (for example, 20-25 minutes of focus, 5 minutes of break).
*   **Mnemonics.** Reception techniques through associations, stories, visual images.
*   **Strategic planning.** Discussion of the action plan before the start. The question "Why will you start?" Activates its own strategy.

### Which reduces effectiveness

*   **Multitasking.** Simultaneous performance of several tasks or quick switching.
*   **Unstructured information.** Long oral explanations or voluminous text without pauses and visual separation.
*   **High pace and haste.** Create a load on the self -control system.
*   **Distracting noise.** Infusal, loud sounds or background music reduce concentration.

## 3. Practical recommendations

### Home environment

*   **Organization.** Separate major tasks. Use the timer (20-25 minutes of work, break). Provide a minimalist workspace by removing distracting items.
*   **Support.** Focus on efforts and process, and not just on the result. Supply for help in planning: "What do you have today?"
*   **Optimal load.** An effective focus on a difficult task is 15-20 minutes. Then a short break is needed.

### Training space

*   **The clarity of tasks.** Provide clear, step -by -step instructions. Duplicate oral instructions in writing. Make sure of understanding by asking the student to repeat the task.
*   **Organization of space.** Place the student in a zone with minimal distraction. Offer checklists for self-testing when completing tasks.
*   **Visualization of the material.** Use schemes, tables, infographics. Select the key. Make short pauses to assimilate information.
*   **Individual approach.** Consider: additional time on control, the use of reference schemes or plans for answers.

## 4. Training for development

These training are aimed at developing key skills. Regularity is more important than duration.

*   **Focus and accuracy.**
    *   “Corrective test”: crossing the letters in the text (3-5 minutes a day).
    *   Games: “Find differences”, labyrinths. Develop concentration on the details.
*   **Coordination (brain-body connection).**
    *   Neurohymnastics: exercises for coordination of hands ("fist-rib-ladon", simultaneous drawing).
    *   Jugling: develops concentration, rhythm, self -control.
*   **Memory.**
    *   The game "Memory": improves visual memory.
    *   Exercise "Photographer": memorizing objects in the room/on the table in 15 seconds.
*   **Rhythm and self -regulation.**
    *   RIGHT RITMES: Repetition of simple and complex rhythmic patterns.
    *   Musical instruments (drums, percussion) or dancing.
