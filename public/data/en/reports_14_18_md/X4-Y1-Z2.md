# Cognitive assessment report
### Student profile (14–18 years old)

This report is intended for parents and teachers. He offers an understanding of the cognitive characteristics of the student, highlighting the strengths and areas for development. Recommendations are aimed at creating an optimal environment for training and growth.

---

# 1. Evaluation results

*   **Sustainability of attention and working memory (CPT):** The difficulty in long -term attention is noted on monotonous tasks and memorizing consistent information. Fast fatigue and focus loss is possible.
*   **Self-control and braking (Go/No-Go):** Outstanding results. High ability to control impulses, quickly respond and avoid hasty decisions. This is a key strong side.
*   **Visual attention:** The need for concentration development is confirmed. The student can lose the focus with a quick presentation of information, especially the audio one.

**Key conclusion:** Developed self -control with a limited resource of attention. The student is not impulsive, but is prone to loss of concentration during overloads.

# 2. The student profile

## Key features
*   **Pace:** The most productive at a measured pace. Hurrying or pressure reduce concentration. It takes time to immerse and plan a task.
*   **Perception:** Optimally assimilates visually and structurally presented information. Long oral monologues or continuous text quickly tire.
*   **Concentration:** Effective concentration lasts 20–25 minutes. After this, a short break for recovery is required. High self -control allows you not to interfere with others, but the development of the material ceases.

## Effective approaches
*   **Step -by -step instructions:** Broken of big tasks into small, understandable steps. Ideal in a checklist format.
*   **Visual supports:** Schemes, tables, intelligence cards, highlighting keywords. Visualization helps in keeping information.
*   **Clear structure:** A pre -known lesson plan or an algorithm of action. This reduces the cognitive load.
*   **Silence:** The ability to work in a calm environment or use noise -disposable headphones for independent work.

## Interviewing factors
*   **Multitasking:** The simultaneous fulfillment of several tasks (listen, write, monitor) almost always leads to a loss of information.
*   **Long explanations:** Loss of concentration after 10-15 minutes of an oral monologue.
*   **Unstructured information:** Abstract tasks. Specifies are required: for example, "Write 3 dates and 2 reasons."
*   **Fast change of activity:** Frequent, demausal switching between different tasks exhaust the resource of attention.

# 3. Recommendations

## Home environment
*   **Homework:**
    *   Use **Tomato method**: 25 minutes of work, 5 minutes of break.
    *   Compose **Daily checklist** tasks for visualization of progress.
    *   Start working with the most complex objects while the level of attention is maximum.
*   **Support and motivation:**
    *   Break large projects into stages: for example, “Search for Information”, “Planning”, “Writing of Introduction”.
    *   **Assess the process, not just the result:** Mark zeal and diligence, and not just marks.
    *   Discuss difficulties and offer joint decisions: for example, “I know, this is not easy. Maybe we will pause? ”

## School environment
*   **The wording of tasks:**
    *   Duplicate oral instructions **in writing** (on the board, in the chat).
    *   Start instructions with **specific verbs**: “Write down”, “compare”, “emphasize”.
    *   Provide **Samples of execution** Or clear algorithms for new types of tasks.
*   **Lesson organization:**
    *   Optimal place: **First-second desk** To minimize abstracts.
    *   Allow use **noise -disposable headphones** during independent work.
    *   Before issuing the assignment, make sure of his attention. Ask a clarifying question: “What needs to be done first?”
*   **Elements of an individual route (IOM):**
    *   Provision **additional time** In control and independent work.
    *   The possibility of receiving **Copies of the presentation** Or summary of the lesson.
    *   Usage **planners and checklists** as official tools in the lesson.

# 4. Targeted training

The presented exercises will help improve cognitive skills in an accessible form.

*   **Attention and working memory:**
    *   **Schultte tables:** Search for numbers in order. Develops concentration and peripheral vision. Start with 3x3 format.
    *   **"Find the differences":** He trains focused attention to the details.
    *   **Retelling:** Reading a short paragraph (5–7 sentences) followed by a retelling. Gradually increase the volume of text.
*   **Planning and self -regulation:**
    *   **Board strategic games:** Chess, checkers, settlers (Catan). Develop the ability to predict and strategic thinking.
    *   **Assembly according to the instructions:** Lego Technic, prefabricated furniture, preparation of dishes by recipe. Strengthens the skill of following the algorithm.
*   **Coordination and bodily control (interhemispheric interaction):**
    *   **Juggling:** Start with one ball, then two. Synchronizes the brain and body.
    *   **Rhythmic exercises:** Testing or cooling rhythms according to the model, playing on drums. Structures activities and attention.
