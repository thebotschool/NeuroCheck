# Cognitive profile

This report provides key insights about the cognitive features of your student. The goal is to optimize training and support the development of the house.

## 1. Cognitive insights

*   **Attention and memory (CPT):** Difficulties with the retention of information and following the instructions are noted. A decrease in concentration is observed with long or complex tasks.
*   **Self-control (Go/No-Go):** The tendency to impulsive reactions has been identified. It is difficult to think about actions before their implementation, which can lead to errors.
*   **Behavioral control (TCP):** The effect of impulsiveness on the decision -making process is confirmed. It is manifested in difficulties with the management of primary reactions.

## 2. The guide for the success of your student

### Individual characteristics

*   **Pace:** Effective work requires unhurried, measured rhythm. A rush leads to stress and errors.
*   **Perception:** Optimally assimilates visual and step -by -step information. Abstract or prolonged oral explanations are less effective.
*   **Focus of attention:** Attention is unstable. Avoid large volumes of information or multitasking. One clear target at a time.

### Supporting factors

*   **Visual tips:** Schemes, tables, intelligence cards, highlighting keywords.
*   **Step -by -step instructions:** Check lists, algorithms, numbered lists.
*   **Measured rhythm:** Work of intervals for 15–20 minutes, with 2-3-minute pauses.
*   **One task at a time:** A clear, specific goal. For example: "Decide the first three examples."
*   **Preliminary planning:** Pronouncing the order of actions before starting.

### Distracting factors

*   **Multitasking:** Simultaneous tasks or quick switching causes an overload.
*   **Quick pace:** Accelerated speech, frequent change of tasks, limited time.
*   **Noise and distracting factors:** Extraneous conversations, visual mess, noisy atmosphere.
*   **Oral instructions:** Long explanations without visual or written support.
*   **Pressure:** The need for an instant reaction without time to think.

## 3. Recommendations

### For the house

*   **Organization of work:** Break the tasks into blocks for 15–20 minutes. Take short breaks (2-5 minutes). Use checklist checks to track progress and a feeling of completeness.
*   **Support and encouragement:** Mark efforts and process, not only the result. Example: "I see, you tried to concentrate!" Avoid criticism for inattention. Instead, analyze: "What prevented? How to improve?"
*   **Working space:** Provide a quiet, minimalist place. On the table only necessary for the current task.

### For school

*   **The wording of tasks:** Tasks should be clumsy, short, one at a time. Visual instructions (board, cards) are more effective than oral.
*   **Lesson structure:** Place at the first desks. Use a calm tone, pause for information processing. Give the time to think about the answer before the question.
*   **Individual route:** It is recommended to create an individual support plan. Turn on visual algorithms, assistant cards. Consider the issue of a question for a school PMPK for a general support strategy.

## 4. Recommended training

The proposed training is integrated into everyday life, developing key skills in a playful way.

### For attention

*   **"Corrector":** Cross out certain letters (for example, “a”) or words (for example, “B”) in the text. Start with one paragraph.
*   **Board games:** "Find the differences", labyrinths, puzzles.

### For memory

*   **"Memory":** Search for paired images.
*   **"What has changed?":** Place 5-7 items. After memorization, remove or change one item. The task is to determine the change.

### For self -control and rhythm

*   **"Stop-signal":** Perform a rhythmic effect (pops, steps). On the command "Stop!" freeze instantly. He trains brake reactions.
*   **Respiratory exercises:** Slow inhalation (4 accounts), delay (4 accounts), slow exhalation (6–8 accounts). Promotes concentration and tranquility.

### For coordination and balance

*   **Balance:** Stand on one leg, first with open, then with your eyes closed.
*   **Juggling:** Start with one or two goals. Develops visual-motor coordination and concentration.
