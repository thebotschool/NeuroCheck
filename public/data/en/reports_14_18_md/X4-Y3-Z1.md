# Cognitive assessment. Report.

This report is designed to reveal the individual characteristics of the student and offer strategies to create a supporting environment at home and at school. The information is clearly presented and contains practical recommendations.

# 1.

### Attention and concentration

*   **Preservation (CPT):** Difficulties with a long concentration on the same tasks and the retention of the sequence of actions were noted. It is possible to lose the plan with boring or voluminous tasks.
*   **Braking and switching (Go/No-Go):** Coordination of the hemispheres can be inconsistent. This affects tasks that require simultaneous logical and spatial thinking (for example, analysis of schemes).
*   **Arbitrary attention (TCP):** High concentration and independence are manifested in interesting, purposeful tasks. This is a key strong side.

# 2. The guide for the success of your student

### Individual approach

*   **Pace:** It takes time to adapt. Acceleration at the beginning of the task reduces the focus and leads to errors.
*   **Perception:** Optimally assimilates visual and structured information. Oral instructions without support are less effective.
*   **Focus:** Attention is activated by interesting, understandable and almost significant tasks. Decreases with monotony or abstraction.

### Which contributes to training

*   **Visualization:** Check lists, schemes, intelligent cards, marker isolation.
*   **Step -by -step instructions:** Separation of large tasks into specific stages.
*   **Rhythm and timers:** Use of the "Prydor" method (20-25 minutes of work, 5 minutes of rest).
*   **Clarity of the goal:** Determination of the first step and visualization of the final result.

### Which prevents training

*   **Multitasking:** The implementation of several cases simultaneously reduces productivity.
*   **Frequent switching:** A quick change in tasks depletes the resource of attention.
*   **Noise and distraction:** Difficulties with suppressing unnecessary incentives in a noisy environment.
*   **Abstract instructions:** Unknown or oral instructions without specifics.

# 3. Recommendations

### For the house

*   **Organization of work:**
    *   Provide a quiet, permanent place for classes.
    *   Make short plans (2-4 points) before starting work.
    *   Use the timer: 25 minutes of work, a short break.
*   **Support and motivation:**
    *   Praise for the process, and not just for the result: "Great work according to the plan!"
    *   Remember: effective concentration up to 25 minutes. Longer sessions without a break are ineffective.
*   **Performing tasks:**
    *   Start with a simple task to create success.
    *   Use drafts for schemes and connections when working with complex objects.

### For school

*   **Making tasks:**
    *   Use oral and written instructions (board, card).
    *   Apply the imperative verbs: "Write out", "Emphasize", "Make".
    *   Place the student closer to the board, away from distracting factors (window, door).
*   **Lesson structure:**
    *   Adhere to a clear structure, link the theory with practice.
    *   Offer finished schemes or tables to fill out in front of complex topics.
*   **Individual route:**
    *   Give additional time in the test work.
    *   Allow the use of a draft for planning answers.
    *   Involve in design activities, where the depth of immersion in interesting topics will appear.

# 4. Recommended training

Regular exercises contribute to the development of skills. The sequence is important, not the duration.

### Skills and exercises

*   **Attention and self -control:**
    *   **Correct samples:** Crossing out a given letter in the text (from 3-4 minutes).
    *   **Strategic Games:** Chess, GO, Carcasson - develop planning in advance.
*   **Coordination and interaction of the hemispheres:**
    *   **Juggling:** Start with two goals - trains the coordination of the eyes, hands, hemispheres.
    *   **Musical instruments:** Piano, guitar - require coordinated work of hands, develop the brain.
    *   **Symmetric drawing:** Drawing patterns with two hands at the same time.
*   **Working memory:**
    *   **The game "Memory":** Develops visual working memory.
    *   **Retelling:** A brief retelling of a film or chapters with the allocation of key events.
*   **Body control and rhythm:**
    *   **Cross -steps:** Slow walking on the spot, touching the opposite knee with the hand - stimulates interhemispheric ties.
