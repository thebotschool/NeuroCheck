### **Cognitive assessment report: CPT + Go/No-Go + TCP (Age: 14–18 years)**

This report is designed to help parents and teachers understand the strengths of the student and the region for development. Recommendations are aimed at creating an optimal environment for training.

---

### **1. Analysis of tests**

*   **Working memory and planning (CPT):** The test revealed difficulties in the simultaneous retention of several elements of information and following multi -stage plans. The student can "be lost" in the process of performing a complex task.
*   **Self-control (Go/No-Go):** Great self -control. The ability to stop impulsive reactions in time. High coordination of actions.
*   **Sustainability of attention (TCP):** High concentration on one task. The student is able to hold a focus for a long time without abstracts. This is a strong base for effective training.

<!-- page-break -->

### **2. The guide for the success of your student**

The student has a high ability to concentrate, but needs a clear structure for complex tasks.

**Key features:**

*   **Pace:** Capable of prolonged and high -quality work on one task. Difficulties arise when switching or complicating the instructions.
*   **Perception:** It better assimilates information, broken into short, consistent steps. Visual supports (diagrams, lists, algorithms) are critical.
*   **Focus of attention:** Attention "Tunnel" is deeply concentrated on one object. The distribution of attention between several tasks is difficult.

**Effective approaches:**

*   **Step -by -step instructions:** The next step is given only after the previous one.
*   **Visual anchors:** Recorded plan, highlighting keywords, short schemes.
*   **Clear beginning and end:** Clear boundaries of the assignment.
*   **One task at a time:** Allows you to use the strong side - deep concentration.
*   **Predictable structure:** The rhythmic format of lessons or homework.

**Barriers for training:**

*   **Multitasking:** Simultaneous requirements (listen, write, remember) lead to overload.
*   **Long oral instructions:** Without visual support. Working memory does not cope with volume.
*   **Fast change of activity:** Without completing the previous task.
*   **Self -building strategy:** For new, difficult tasks.

<!-- page-break -->

### **3. Recommendations**

**At home:**

*   **Homework:** Break into short blocks. Example: "First we solve the first three problems, then a break."
*   **Planning:** Help make simple checklists. Example: "1. Read. 2. Write out dates. 3. Answer the question." Distribution of points gives a feeling of control.
*   **Organization of space:** The workplace should be free from distracting items. It is better to remove the phone.
*   **Promotion:** Praise for following the plan, concentration and efforts on self -organization, and not just for the final result.

**At school:**

*   **The wording of tasks:** Specific, linear instructions. Example: "1. Read the first paragraph 2. Find three terms. 3. Formulate the main idea."
*   **Lesson structure:** Provide ready -made algorithms or templates (essay plan, solution scheme). This will reduce the load on the working memory.
*   **Class placement:** The first desks. The minimum number of distracting factors. The opportunity to the teacher invisiblely check the implementation.
*   **Style style:** "Instructor" style: clearly, by steps, with an intermediate check. Long lectures without supports are ineffective.
*   **Individual route:** It is recommended to develop an IOM with an emphasis on the development of executive functions. Consultation with a school psychologist or neuropsychologist for correctional exercises.

<!-- page-break -->

### **4. Recommended training**

These exercises develop work memory and planning using strengths - concentration and self -control.

**For attention and working memory:**

*   **"Remember and draw":** Show a simple scheme (4-5 figures) for 10 seconds. Reproduce from memory.
*   **"Auditory dictations":** Dictate the sequence of actions. Example: "Take a red pencil, draw a circle, put a point inside it." Complicate the chain.
*   **Board games:** "Corridor", "Seth", "Dobble". They require quick scanning and holding the rules.

**For planning and self -regulation:**

*   **Strategic Games:** Checkers, simple chess, UNO. Develop the ability to think through moves.
*   **Cooking on recipes:** Preparation of dishes by step -by -step recipe. Planning training, sequence, self -control.
*   **Visual gliders:** Applications (Trello, Todoist) or magnetic boards for a list of affairs and tracking execution.

**For coordination and bodily control:**

*   **Juggling:** Start with two goals. Develops coordination of the "eye-hand" and interhemispheric interaction.
*   **Balance exercises:** Walking along the line, stand on one leg (with open, then closed eyes).
*   **Rhythmic exercises:** Testing or cooling rhythmic patterns on the sample. Improves the sense of rhythm and serial organization of movements.
