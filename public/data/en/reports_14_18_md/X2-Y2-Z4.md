# Cognitive report
### CPT+Go/No-GO+TCP (14–18 years old)

This report offers an understanding of the cognitive characteristics of the student and recommendations to support his development.

## Review of the results

### Attention and memory (CPT)
The difficulty in keeping accurate visual details may confuse similar objects, especially with fatigue or distraction. It shows a strategic approach to memorizing information.

### Self-control (Go/No-Go)
Difficulties with the suppression of impulsive actions. This can manifest itself both in oral answers and in written works.

### Performance and attention (TCP)
General results indicate a significant cognitive load. Concentration and self-control can be difficult due to overwork, stress or learning difficulties.

## Success's success guide

### Peculiarities
*   **Pace.** Works at its own pace. Needs short pauses for correction. A quick pace from the outside leads to errors.
*   **Perception.** It better assimilates visual, structured information: schemes, lists, keywords. Visual "noise" interferes, confuses similar elements.
*   **Attention.** Unstable, quickly exhausted. Can be distracted. External supports are required to return the focus.

### Support
*   **Visual supports.** Clear schemes, Mayind cards, cards with rules or algorithms.
*   **Step -by -step instructions.** Separation of large tasks into small, understandable steps. Principle: "One step is one action."
*   **Built -in pauses.** Short, planned breaks or moments for self -testing.
*   **Check lists.** Lists of affairs or steps to complete tasks. Reduce the load as a memory, organize work.
*   **A quiet environment.** A workplace with a minimum of distracting factors.

### Obstacles
*   **Multitasking.** The requirement to listen to, write, monitor the presentation simultaneously.
*   **Fast change of tasks.** Sharp transitions between topics without logic and pauses.
*   **Noise and chaos.** Loud conversations, accumulation of people, cluttering space.
*   **Long oral instructions.** Information is given verbally without visual support.
*   **Time pressure.** Situations requiring an instant reaction.

## Recommendations

### For the house
*   **Homework.** Divide into short blocks (15–20 minutes) with 5-minute breaks. Use a break for movement, drinking water.
*   **Organization of space.** Create a constant, quiet workplace. On the table is only necessary for the current task.
*   **Support.** Enjoy efforts, not only the result. Example: "Let's see together what could go wrong."
*   **Planning.** Use checks, glider checks. Make a simple plan for tomorrow. This develops self -organization.

### For school
*   **Tasks.** The instructions should be clumsy, short, step -by -step. Instead of one big task - several small ones.
*   **The structure of the lesson.** Support and write down the lesson plan. Use visual anchors (schemes, keywords) to maintain attention.
*   **Placement.** The first and second desks in the central row. This minimizes distraction, maintains eye contact.
*   **Style style.** Calm, measured voice. Before the question, please call by name to attract attention and give time to prepare.
*   **Individual route.**
    *   Provide extra time for control.
    *   Allow me to use helping cards (formulas, algorithms).
    *   Consider the possibility of writing oral themes.
    *   The connection of a school psychologist for psychological and pedagogical support is recommended.

## Recommended training

These exercises will help to develop skills that require support.

### Attention and visual memory
*   **Corrective test.** In the newspaper text, cross out the letters "A", circle "o". Start from 5 minutes, gradually increase time.
*   **Board games.** “Find 10 differences”, “Memory”, puzzles.

### Self -control
*   **The game "Delay".** Move to the music, freeze when it is stopped. Complication: freeze at the expense of "three" after stopping.
*   **Rhythmic exercises.** Take the rhythm with your palms. Then pass every third cotton. He trains the skill of braking.

### Coordination and bodily control
*   **Balance.** Stand on one leg (with your eyes open, then closed), walk along the line. Balanceboard is an effective simulator.
*   **Juggling.** Start with two balls. Develops the concentration and coordination of the “eye-hand”.
