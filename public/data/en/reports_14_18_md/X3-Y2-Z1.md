### **Cognitive profile report**
Memory. Self -control. Attention.
This report reveals the unique cognitive features of your student. It contains practical recommendations to support the development and increase the effectiveness of learning.

---

### **1. Key indicators**
*   **Memory and planning (CPT):** Effective memorization of facts and details. The task is to build them in the correct order.
*   **Self-control (Go/No-Go):** Tender to hasty decisions. The development of the internal "pause" before the reaction is required.
*   **Concentration (TCP):** High ability to deep and prolonged concentration. It is activated in the presence of a clear goal and interest.

<!-- page-break -->

### **2. Profile of learning**
#### Peculiarities
*   **Working pace:** Fast, impulsive. The need for a conscious slowdown to prevent errors in carelessness.
*   **Information perception:** Optimally assimilates structured data. Schemes, lists, algorithms and clear steps are preferred.
*   **Focus of attention:** Capable of deep, sustainable attention. It requires a clear goal and plan for maximum efficiency.

#### Optimal conditions
*   **Visualization:** Schemes, memory cards, timline, checklists.
*   **Step -by -step algorithms:** Clear, consistent instructions.
*   **Pause before action:** The practice of a conscious slowdown in the reaction.
*   **Structural approach:** Start with understandable elements.
*   **One focus:** Work on one task at one point in time.

#### Factors that reduce efficiency
*   **Multitasking:** Reduces productivity.
*   **Time deficiency:** Provokes impulsive errors.
*   **Faring instructions:** Call frustration and stupor.
*   **Distracting factors:** Prevent deep concentration.

<!-- page-break -->

### **3. Practical recommendations**
#### In the home environment
*   **Organization of work:**
    *   **Planning:** Make a short work plan. Example: "1. We decide the algebra. 2. We write 3 paragraphs. 3. We read the paragraph."
    *   **One focus:** Eliminate distracting factors. Perform one task at a time.
    *   **Timing:** Use the timer ("tomato" method). 25-30 minutes of work, 5 minutes break. It is possible to increase the intervals to 40-50 minutes with a high interest.
*   **Support and encouragement:**
    *   **The process is more important:** Mark efforts and strategy, not just the result. Example: "I see, you have thought over the answer before writing down."
    *   **Structural assistance:** Help build an algorithm. Ask questions: "Why would you start? What is the next step?"

#### In the educational environment
*   **Setting tasks:**
    *   **Clear instructions:** Use numb lists.
    *   **Samples:** Provide examples of execution or evaluation criteria for complex tasks.
    *   **Example:** Instead of “Write an essay on the reforms of Peter I”, use: “1. Describe 3 key reforms. 2. Indicate goals. 3. Make a conclusion about their influence.”
*   **Lesson organization:**
    *   **Visual plan:** Place the key thesis of the lesson on the board.
    *   **Thinking time:** Give the time to think about the answer. The possibility of a conventional sign for reflection alarms.
    *   **Answer planning:** Encourage the use of drafts to structuring answers.
*   **Individual route:**
    *   **Project activity:** Put in projects where its ability to concentrate is as useful as possible.
    *   **Escort:** In case of complex projects, provide initial support for drawing up a plan and schedule.

### **4. Developing practices**
These games and exercises will help strengthen planning and self -control skills.

*   **Attention and strategy:**
    *   **Board games:** Chess, GO, complex economic strategies. Develop long -term planning and use the ability to concentrate.
*   **Memory and logic:**
    *   **Comics without words:** Creation of stories according to the series of images. He trains the construction of logical sequences.
    *   **Retelling with a plan:** Drawing up a brief plan (3-5 points) before retelling the text or film.

*   **Self -control and coordination:**
    *   **"Stop-signal":** Instant stop and fixation of the posture on a conditional signal. Develops the "internal brake".
    *   **Rhythmic exercises:** Playing musical instruments (especially drums), dancing, picking rhythm. Teach movements and pauses over time.
