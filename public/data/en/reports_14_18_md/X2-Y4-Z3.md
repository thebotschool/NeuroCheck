# Cognitive Profile: Your path to potential

This report offers a detailed view of the cognitive features of your student. It is designed to help in understanding the strengths and areas for development, as well as offer practical steps to create an optimal training environment at home and at school.

---

### 1. Key conclusions

A short overview of the results of cognitive testing.

*   **Attention and memory (CPT):** The ability to a systematic approach and the development of strategies for memorization has been shown. There is a difficulty with maintaining small visual details, especially in the presence of distracting factors.
*   **Reactions control (Go/No-Go):** Difficulties with timely suppression of impulsive reactions in thoughts and actions have been revealed, which can lead to hasty decisions.
*   **Impulsiveness (TCP):** The test confirms the tendency to impulsiveness. Difficulties with behavior control and preliminary pondering of actions are observed, especially in situations requiring exposure.

### 2. The guide for the success of your student

Understanding the individual characteristics of the student is the key to the disclosure of his potential. This leadership offers practical ideas for creating an optimal education environment.

#### Individual characteristics

*   **Pace:** Fast but uneven. The tendency to impulsiveness can lead to a missing of details and hasty conclusions.
*   **Perception:** The preference of a clear, structured visual information (diagrams, checklists). Excess details reduces concentration.
*   **Focus of attention:** Attention is easily attracted, but also quickly dissipated by external factors. Long -term concentration on monotonous or complex tasks is difficult.

#### Which contributes

*   **Step -by -step instructions:** A clear plan of action helps to reduce impulsiveness.
*   **Visual supports:** Cards, diagrams, highlighting keywords help to fix information.
*   **Clear rhythm of work and rest:** Application of the method, for example, 20 minutes of work / 5 minutes of break.
*   **The principle of "one task at a time":** Elimination of distracting factors.
*   **Plan verbalization:** Propying of actions aloud for self -regulation.

#### What prevents

*   **Multitasking:** The simultaneous performance of several tasks reduces effectiveness.
*   **High pace of feed:** Fast speech without pauses complicates assimilation.
*   **Noise and chaos:** Distracting factors reduce concentration.
*   **Oral instructions without visual support:** Information can be missed.

### 3. Recommendations

Practical steps to support and develop a student.

#### For the house

*   **Homework:**
    *   Miss large tasks into short blocks (15–20 minutes) with mini-over (2-5 minutes).
    *   Make a clear work plan, use checklists.
    *   Organize a quiet, ordered workplace without unnecessary items.
*   **Support and motivation:**
    *   Focus on the process, and not just on the result: "I see how you tried and followed the plan!"
    *   Offer breaks instead of reproaches: "It seems you are tired. Let's pause?"
    *   Keep in mind that the effective concentration on a complex task lasts about 15–20 minutes.
*   **Self -control development:**
    *   Discuss the consequences of impulsive actions calmly, after a decline in emotions.
    *   Agree on a simple stop signal (word or gesture) to realize impulsiveness in the moment.

#### For school

*   **The wording of tasks:**
    *   The most clear and step -by -step instructions, preferably in the form of a numbered list on a board or in a printout.
    *   Visually select keywords and important details in tasks.
*   **Lesson structure:**
    *   Alternate activities: 10 minutes of the hearing, 5 minutes of the letter, 10 minutes of discussion.
    *   Sound the lesson plan at the beginning: "Today we will repeat at first ..., then we will study ... and at the end we will fulfill ..."
*   **Organization and feed:**
    *   The optimal place is closer to the teacher, away from distracting factors (windows, doors).
    *   Use a calm, flat tone. Visualize key points on the board/slide. Pauses before the question of questions, giving time to think.
*   **Elements of an individual educational route (IOM):**
    *   Providing additional time for the performance of tests.
    *   The ability to use submarine cards with algorithms or formulas.
    *   Permission to use a draft to plan an answer before recording.

### 4. Recommended training

These practices are aimed at strengthening cognitive functions identified as growth zones.

*   **For attention and memory:**
    *   **Games:** "Find the differences", "copyer" (reproduction of the pattern from memory).
    *   **Strategic board games:** "Carcasson", "jackal", chess, Sudoku.
    *   **Mnemics:** Mastering methods of memorization (associations, abbreviations).
*   **For coordination and self -control:**
    *   **Exercise "Stop-signal":** Instant rhythmic stop at the signal.
    *   **Juggling:** Develops the coordination of the "eye-hand" and concentration.
    *   **Balance:** Equilibrium exercises (stand on one leg, walking along the line, balance book).
    *   **Movement:** Dancing or martial arts requiring accurate reproduction of movements.
