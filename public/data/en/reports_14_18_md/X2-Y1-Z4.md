# Cognitive profile

This report provides key data on the cognitive abilities of the student. The goal is to help parents and teachers create an optimal environment for development and training, using strengths and working on growth zones.

<!-- page-break -->

## 1. Analysis of indicators

*   **Visual working memory (CPT):** There is the ability to develop strategies for memorization. There is an opportunity to confuse similar visual details, which may indicate distracting or difficulties with accurate coding of information.
*   **Self-control (Go/No-Go):** An outstanding result, demonstrating a high level of self -control, quick decision -making and effective braking of impulsive reactions. This is a key strong side.
*   **Sustainability of attention (TCP):** There are significant difficulties with maintaining concentration for a long time. This is probably due to fatigue, anxiety or general overload.

<!-- page-break -->

## 2. Profile of learning

We have a unique profile: the student has high potential for self -control, but his attention resource is limited under load. He effectively copes with short, clockwork, but experiences difficulties in prolonged and monotonous work.

### Individual characteristics

*   **Pace:** "Sprinter". Capable of brief periods of high concentration, but is quickly depleted from long -term, monotonous activity. The rhythm of work with regular pauses is required.
*   **Perception:** Optimally perceives clear, structured and visually divided information. The continuous text or the abundance of similar elements on the page can cause errors and visual overload.
*   **Focus of attention:** High -quality, but short -term. Deep immersion in the task is possible for 15–20 minutes, after which the productivity is sharply reduced, a break is required.

### Supporting factors

*   **Step -by -step instructions:** Tasks divided into 2-3 simple steps.
*   **Visual anchors:** Markers, stickers, diagrams, Mayland cards for structuring information.
*   **Timer:** The use of a timer (for example, in the technique of prying: 20 minutes of work, 5 minutes of rest) helps legalize the need for breaks and save the resource.
*   **Clear rules:** An accurate understanding of the requirements and evaluation criteria activates self -control.

### Obstacles

*   **Multitasking:** The requirement to fulfill several cases at the same time or quickly switch between tasks without a pause leads to errors and frustration.
*   **Long instructions:** The monotonous presentation of information without pauses and interactive quickly depleted the resource of attention.
*   **Visual and audial noise:** Distracting factors (open tabs, mess, loud sounds) critically reduce productivity.
*   **Pressure and rush:** The coercion or requirement of the immediate execution of a volumetric assignment can lead to a “disconnection” of the attention system as a protective reaction.

<!-- page-break -->

## 3. Recommendations

The main goal is not to “fix” attention, but to teach a student and his environment to effectively manage the existing cognitive resource.

### For the house

*   **Homework organization:**
    *   Miss large tasks into small parts. Use checklists.
    *   Apply the “20/5” rule: 20 minutes of focused work, then a 5-minute break.
    *   Provide a calm, clean workplace without unnecessary items.
*   **Support and encouragement:**
    *   Praise for the process, and not just for the result: "You perfectly used the strategy for separating the task."
    *   Reduce the level of anxiety around your studies. Emphasize that mistaken is normal.
    *   With fatigue, offer a short physical warm -up or breathing exercise.

### For school

*   **The wording of tasks:**
    *   Provide the instructions step by step, use numbered and marked lists.
    *   Example: “Open page 45. Read the first paragraph. Find three key dates and write them out. ”
*   **The structure of the lesson and materials:**
    *   Place the student closer to the teacher, away from distracting factors (window, door).
    *   Use visual materials: schemes, tables, laconic presentations.
    *   Include short "rebooting" in the lesson: a moment of silence, a simple physical exercise.
*   **Elements of an individual educational route (IOM):**
    *   Consider the possibility of providing additional time on control and examination work (taking into account the TCP test).
    *   Attract a school psychologist to develop strategies for reducing anxiety and teaching self -regulation.
    *   It is possible to temporarily reduce the volume of homework with an emphasis on quality.

## 4. Training

These exercises and games will help to develop the necessary skills in an informal and exciting form.

### Attention

*   **Search for differences:** Games “Find 10 differences” or “Wimmelbuch” develop visual attention to details and concentration.
*   **Puzzles:** Sudoku, Japanese crosswords train logic and require a long, structured concentration.
*   **Thamers application:** For example, Forest helps to remain focused using game mechanics.

### Memory

*   **Memory game:** Classical game for training visual working memory.
*   **Mnemics:** Creation of associations or stories for memorizing rules, formulas, dates.
*   **Copying on the model:** Redistering complex patterns or assembly of the designer according to the scheme.

### Bodily control

*   **Juggling:** Develops coordination, concentration and relieves stress.
*   **Rhythmic games:** Testing rhythms, dancing.
*   **Balance exercises:** Standing on one leg, the use of a balance board. Help improve the connection between the brain and the body.
