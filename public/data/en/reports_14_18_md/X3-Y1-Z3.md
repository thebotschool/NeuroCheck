This report is your guide to understand the potential of the student. He reveals the strengths and offers strategies for development, making training effective and comfortable.

### **1. Analysis: Key indicators**

*   **Memory (CPT):** The ability to memorize individual facts and images is strong. However, confusion may occur in a sequence or logical connection. This indicates the need to develop a structural organization of memory.

*   **Self-control (Go/No-Go):** A high level of self -control and concentration is demonstrated in the presence of clearly defined tasks. This is a reliable basis for the exact follow of the rules.

*   **Attention and impulsiveness (TCP):** In situations of uncertainty or emotional stress, there is a tendency to impulsive actions. Support for the development of the ability to make balanced decisions and control behavior in unclear conditions is required.

### **2. Profile of learning**

Your schoolboy is a unique combination of qualities: from deep focus to instant impulsiveness. The key to maximum efficiency - **clear structure**.

#### **Features of training:**

*   **Pace:** Moderate. For optimal results, it takes time to plan before starting work. Attempts to accelerate can lead to randomness.
*   **Perception:** Visual-logical. It is best assimilating information presented in the form of schemes, lists or step -by -step instructions. Oral explanations are more effective with visual support.
*   **Focus:** Able to immerse himself in one specific task. The multitasking or unclear instructions reduce concentration.

#### **What supports:**

*   **Visual tips:** Check lists, Mayland cards, diagrams, highlighting keywords.
*   **Step -by -step instructions:** Missing large tasks into 3-5 small, understandable stages.
*   **Clear rules:** Clear evaluation criteria and algorithms.
*   **Rhythm and pauses:** Short breaks between tasks for rebooting; Small physical activity.

#### **Which complicates:**

*   **Multitasking:** The simultaneous performance of several tasks causes overload.
*   **Fast change of tasks:** A sharp transition from one topic to another without a pause and preparation.
*   **Oral instructions:** Long monologues without visual or written support.
*   **Pressure and rush:** Phrases such as “faster” or “this is easy”, as well as the conditions of time pressure, provoke impulsive errors.

### **3. Recommendations**

#### **For home:**

*   **Organization of work:**
    *   **Planning:** Together draw up a work plan. Fix tasks, order and time of execution.
    *   **Decomposition:** Separate large tasks into small stages, distributing them for several days.
    *   **Working space:** Provide a clean, free place from distracting factors.
*   **Support and encouragement:**
    *   **Confession:** Mark following the plan, accuracy and the ability to notice the error in time, and not just speed.
    *   **Pause:** With signs of impulsiveness or irritation, offer a short break (2-3 minutes) for rebooting.
    *   **Reflection:** Discuss the process of work - which was difficult, which helped to cope. This develops introspection.
*   **Mercing of attention:**
    *   **Focus:** The optimal time of effective work on one task is 30–40 minutes, then a short break (5-7 minutes) is needed before switching.

#### **For school:**

*   **The wording of tasks:**
    *   **Definition:** Duplicate oral instructions in writing or use numbered/marked lists.
    *   **Examples:** Provide samples or templates for difficult work.
    *   **Detailing:** Miss volumetric tasks into specific steps. For example, instead of “analyze the text” - “1. Read the text. 2. Highlight 3 key ideas. 3. Put your position for each of them. ”
*   **Lesson structure:**
    *   **Plan:** Start a lesson by voicing its structure and goals.
    *   **Location:** The first desks reduce distraction and improve the visibility of the board.
*   **Individual route:**
    *   **Projects:** The student is perfect for design and research activities, provided that it is supported in the initial planning.
    *   **Scheduler:** Introduction of a training planner or diary for fixing tasks and stages. Cupporting with a class teacher or tutor.

### **4. Recommended training**

These simple exercises will help strengthen the strengths and develop areas that require attention.

*   **For attention and logical thinking:**
    *   **Assembly according to the instructions:** Lego Technic, modeling, complex puzzles. Develops following the algorithm and retention of the sequence of actions.
    *   **Visualization of stories:** Creation of comics on short stories (3-5 key scenes). Develops the selection of the main and chronological thinking.
    *   **Strategic Games:** Board games (for example, "Carcasson", chess). He teaches planning several moves ahead and control of impulsive decisions.

*   **For bodily control and removal of impulsiveness:**
    *   **Juggling:** Starting from two items. Develops synchronization of the work of the hemispheres, concentration and a sense of rhythm.
    *   **Balance exercises:** Standing on one leg, walking along the line, Balansboard. Improves the connection between the body and the brain, develops control.
    *   **Rhythmic activity:** Fighting the rhythm with palms or legs, repetition of dance movements (for example, from games like just dance). Promotes "grounding" and reduction of internal stress.
