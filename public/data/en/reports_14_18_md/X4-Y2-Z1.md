## Cognitive profile
Your path to understanding.
This report reveals individual characteristics of training and offers practical recommendations to support development.

---

### 1. Individual profile

*   **Working memory (CPT):** Tasks with several stages or complex instructions can cause difficulties. Support for planning and sequence of actions is necessary.
*   **Impulsive control (Go/No-Go):** Sometimes it is difficult to restrain the first impulse, which can lead to hasty actions and errors in carelessness, especially in conditions of quick decision -making.
*   **Sustainability of attention (TCP):** High ability to long, deep concentration. After immersing in the task, attention becomes extremely stable.

### 2. The guide for the success of your student

Unique profile: powerful potential requires a special start. The beginning can be unhurried, but then confident and prolonged progress follows.

#### Key features:

*   **Pace.** Uneven start. It takes time to "include" and the organization. Then - productive, long work. Rush at the start is counterproductive; Own rhythm is important.
*   **Perception.** Clear, structured, visual instructions are most effective. Ideal: step-by-step plans, schemes, checklists.
*   **Focus.** Difficulties in starting and switching tasks. However, with deep immersion in one clear task, attention becomes extremely stable.

#### What supports:

*   **Visual landmarks.** The plan of the lesson on the board, the printed algorithm, highlighted keywords.
*   **The principle of the first step.** Sometimes it is enough to help only with the first step, the rest will be performed independently.
*   **Singlety.** The maximum efficiency in the mode of performing one task at a time.
*   **Meaningful pauses.** Practice “Think before the answer” or “Check yourself to change”. Helps to fight impulsiveness.
*   **Predictability and rhythm.** A clear schedule and structure reduce the alarm, accelerate “inclusion”.

#### What makes it difficult:

*   **Multitasking.** Requests that require simultaneous listening, recordings and memorization cause overload.
*   **Obscure instructions.** Tasks without specific steps (for example, “analyze the text”) cause a stupor.
*   **Frequent switching.** A quick change in activity does not allow to enter the productive regime.
*   **Distracting factors.** The noise is especially critical at the start of work, when the maximum concentration is needed.

### 3. Practical recommendations

#### For home:

*   **Organization of work.**
    *   Make a written plan of tasks (for example: 1. Mathematics: Experitation 5, 6; 2. Break; 3. History: paragraph, questions).
    *   Check lists. A mark of the points of the points gives a sense of control and completeness.
    *   It is difficult tasks after 10-15 minutes of “warming up” on simple tasks.
*   **Support and motivation.**
    *   The emphasis on following the plan and self -test. Example: "You checked yourself perfectly!" More important than the speed of execution.
    *   Concentration: up to 40-50 minutes on the task. It takes 5-10 minutes to “turn on”. Plan breaks every 45 minutes.
*   **Impulsive control.**
    *   Rule 10 seconds: before the answer or action, mentally count up to ten.

#### For school:

*   **The wording of the tasks.**
    *   Miss large tasks into numbered steps.
    *   Use written instructions or visual schemes, even for oral tasks.
    *   Example: instead of “write an essay”, offer: “1. Make a plan (3 points). 2. Write the introduction. 3. Develop the main part (2 arguments). 4. Make a conclusion. 5. Check the text. "
*   **Organization of the lesson.**
    *   Place closer to the board to minimize distraction.
    *   Sound and write down the lesson plan on the board.
    *   Paise before the answer, encouraging deliberation, and not impulsiveness.
*   **Individual route.**
    *   The planning and self -control development program is recommended.
    *   Consider the accompaniment of the tutor or psychologist. Discuss the need to consult a PMPK.

### 4. Training for development

Games and exercises for the exciting development of key skills.

*   **Attention and planning (CPT).**
    *   Table strategies: chess, checkers, cacasson, jackal. Develop long -term planning.
    *   Assembly according to the instructions: Lego Technic, Models, Origami. Teach to follow the algorithm.
    *   Graphic dictations, labyrinths. Develop preliminary planning of actions.

*   **Control and braking (Go/No-Go).**
    *   Games "Delay", "The sea is worried." Training bodily control.
    *   Rhythmic exercises: taping the rhythm with a pass or weakening of each fourth blow. He trains cognitive braking.
    *   Board games: Dobble, drum. They require a quick reaction with pulse control.

*   **Working memory (CPT).**
    *   The game "Find a couple" (Memory). Classical training of visual working memory.
    *   Repetition of sequences: repeat 3-4 movements (cotton, top, sit down). Gradually complicate.
