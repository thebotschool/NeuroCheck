# Cognitive profile
## Analysis and potential
Age: 14-18 years

This report provides key data on the cognitive functions of the student. It is designed to help in revealing individual potential, offering effective strategies to support and development.

---

## 1. Analysis of indicators

### Sustainability of attention (CPT)
There are difficulties with prolonged focus retention and reproduction of the sequence of actions. This can affect the performance of multi -step tasks and cause rapid fatigue.

### Braking and control (Go/No-Go)
Features in the interaction of the hemispheres have been identified. This can be manifested in tasks requiring synchronous coordination and analysis.

### Visual Working Memory (TCP)
The need for external support with a high mental stress has been noted. A clear plan of action helps the student to complete the tasks.

## 2. Success Guide

### Features of training

**Pace.** A slow, measured pace is preferable. A rush leads to errors and frustration.

**Perception.** Information is better absorbed through visual images: diagrams, pictures, short lists, color selection. Oral explanations are more complicated.

**Focus of attention.** Attention is unstable. The productive concentration is 10-15 minutes, after which a short break or a change of activity is needed.

### Effective approaches

*   **Step -by -step instructions.** Separation of large tasks into small, understandable steps (checklists).
*   **Visual supports.** Schemes, mental cards, reminders, algorithm cards.
*   **Visual timer.** Helps manage time and distribute efforts.
*   **A clear designation of borders.** The clear start and completion of the task.
*   **Rhythm.** Rhythmic actions (account aloud, tapping) improve concentration.

### Factors that reduce productivity

*   **Multitasking.** Attempts to perform several cases simultaneously reduce effectiveness.
*   **Long oral instructions.** Long explanations without pauses or visual supports overload the memory.
*   **Noise and distraction.** Distracting factors, visual disorder reduce concentration.
*   **Unstructured tasks.** The lack of a clear plan and criteria causes confusion.

## 3. Recommendations

### Home environment

**Organization of work.**
*   Divide tasks into blocks: 15 minutes of work, 5 minutes break.
*   Use checklists for evening tasks. Refusing the executed gives a sense of control.
*   Start with the most difficult tasks while there is energy.

**Support and promotion.**
*   Praise the process, not just the result: "I see your efforts!"
*   Avoid criticism for slowness. Ask: "What is bothering? Let's figure it out."

**Working space.**
*   On the table there should be only objects necessary for the current task.

### School strategies

**The formulation of tasks.**
*   Let's short, step -by -step instructions (for example: "1. Open page 42. 2. Read the paragraph 1. 3. Find the answer to question 1.").
*   Use the verbs of the action: “Emphasize”, “Close”, “Connect”, “Smnerme”.

**Lesson structure and material.**
*   Visualize the information: the plan of the lesson on the board, diagrams, color release.
*   Provide supports: algorithms, templates, lists of terms.

**Accommodation in the classroom.**
*   Optimally-the first-second desk in the center. This reduces distraction and promotes contact with the teacher.

**Individual route.**
*   Consider the development of an individual educational route (IOM) with a psychologist and speech therapist.
*   Include the development of attention, memory, interhemispheric interaction in IOM.

## 4. Practical exercises

These exercises support the development of the necessary skills. Regularity is important: 10-15 minutes daily.

### For attention
*   **Corrective tests.** Crossing out a given letter in the text. Complication: delete one, circle the other.
*   **"Find the differences."** A classic game for the development of concentration and visual attention.

### For coordination and interhemispheric interaction
*   **"Fist-Robric Ladon."** A consistent change in the position of the hand on the table: one, then the other, then two at the same time.
*   **Cross -steps.** Walking on the spot touched the opposite knee.
*   **Mirror drawing.** Simultaneous drawing of symmetrical figures with both hands.

### For memory
*   **The game "Memory".** Search for paired cards is an effective simulator of visual working memory.
*   **"What has changed?"** Remember the location of objects, then determine the changes after moving them or removal.

### For rhythm and bodily control
*   **Tapping rhythms.** Repetition of a given rhythm.
*   **Balance exercises.** Standing on one leg, walking along the line.
