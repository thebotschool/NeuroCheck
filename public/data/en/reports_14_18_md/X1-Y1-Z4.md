# Cognitive assessment

## 1. Review of tests

Test results reveal high potential. Current features can be associated with external or internal factors.

*   **Work memory test (CPT):** High working memory. The ability to concentrate and strategic thinking. The potential for the development of complex material.
*   **Self-control test (Go/No-Go):** Effective self -control. Accuracy and quick switching between tasks. The basis for independent work.
*   **Test for stability of attention (TCP):** Difficulties with prolonged attention. Probably indicates overload, stress or fatigue.

**The core of potential:** A strong planner and concentrator.
**Task:** Optimize endurance of attention.

## 2. The profile of the student

### Features of perception

*   **Pace:** Effective in short, intense sprints. Long monotonous loads lead to exhaustion and errors.
*   **Perception format:** Flexibility in working with information: text, formulas, visual schemes. Analytical mindset.
*   **Focus of attention:** The ability to deep immersion in one task. Attempts by multitasking or prolonged fixation lead to fatigue.

### Which optimizes

*   **A clear plan.** A detailed task plan. Breaking into small steps.
*   **Visual supports.** Schemes, intelligence cards, lists. Reduce the load as a memory, serve as points of return.
*   **Rhythm "Work-Route".** Short, intensive periods (25–35 min) with mandatory breaks (5-10 min). The “Prydor” approach is perfect.
*   **One task.** Maximum productivity for focus in one business.

### What slows down

*   **Multitasking.** The simultaneous performance of several tasks reduces effectiveness, causes exhaustion.
*   **Long -term monotonous work.** Reducing productivity in continuous work (more than 40 minutes).
*   **Pressure and rush.** Anxiety and deadlines worsen planning and self -control.
*   **Sensory overload.** Noise, a large number of irritants, the mess at the workplace is exhausted.

## 3. Recommendations

### Home environment

*   **Homework:**
    *   Planning of the evening: Set up tasks into blocks for 25-30 minutes.
    *   Use the timer. Mandatory 5-minute breaks (physical activity, without gadgets).
    *   Organization of the workplace: only the necessary items for the current task.
*   **Support:**
    *   Priority: Reducing anxiety. Praise for efforts, compliance with the plan, timely breaks.
    *   Open dialogue about the condition: “I see you are tired”, “Where will we start?”
    *   Ensure enough sleep and non -academic rest (walks, hobbies).
*   **Attention:** Effective concentration on a difficult task is up to 35 minutes. After - a mandatory break.

### Educational environment

*   **The wording of tasks:**
    *   The most clear, step -by -step instructions (preferably, in writing).
    *   For volumetric work: set 2-3 intermediate periods for planning.
*   **Lesson structure:**
    *   One clear instruction at a time.
    *   Alternation of formats: lecture, independent work, discussion.
*   **Accommodation:** The first-second desk, in the center. A minimum of visual and auditory abstracts.
*   **IOM (individual educational route):** Temporarily applicable:
    *   Additional time on control and independent.
    *   The possibility of phased delivery of large works.
    *   Permission to use headphones (without words/noise reduction) for concentration.

## 4. Training

The proposed exercises will strengthen the endurance of attention and improve self -regulation.

### Focus and self -control

*   **Chess, go, shogi.** Develop strategic thinking and prolonged concentration.
*   **Corrective test.** Crossing out symbols in the text (e.g., “a” and “o” in 5 minutes). He trains the stability of attention.

### Coordination and balance

*   **Juggling.** Start with two goals. Develops interhemispheric interaction, visual-motor coordination and concentration.
*   **Balance exercises.** Standing on one leg (with eyes closed), walking along the line, a balanceballboard. They contribute to "grounding" and bodily awareness, reducing anxiety.

### Memory and rhythm

*   **Rhythmic exercises.** Tapping or cooling the specified rhythm (with metronome). Develops an inner sense of time and pace.
*   **The game "Memory".** The use of sets with a large number of cards to train visual memory and strategies for memorization.
