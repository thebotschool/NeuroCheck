### 2. The guide for the success of your student

#### Individual profile

*   **Pace:** Flexible. Capable of long, thoughtful work. When changing tasks or simple stimuli, impulsiveness is manifested.
*   **Perception:** Effectively assimilates structured, logical information. Avoid continuous text and excessive visual parts.
*   **Concentration:** Deep immersion in interesting topics. Disclosures can lead to impulsive errors or loss of parts.

#### Which helps

*   **Step -by -step instructions:** Clear, numbered algorithms of action.
*   **Visual supports:** Schemes, intelligence cards, tables, color coding. They help to structure and avoid confusion.
*   **Pause rule:** A short break before the answer or a new task. Reduces impulsiveness.
*   **Project work:** Ideal for deep immersion, develops strengths.

#### What prevents

*   **Multitasking:** Leads to errors.
*   **Fast change of tasks:** Expands self -control without pauses.
*   **Noise:** Distracting factors (visual, audial) violate concentration.
*   **Time pressure:** Stimulates impulsive, rash answers.

<!-- page-break -->

### 3. Recommendations

#### Home environment

*   **Working space:**
    *   **Place:** Quiet, secluded, without unnecessary items.
    *   **Structure:** Divide big tasks. Use a timer (for example, a prying) for rhythm, not for speed.
    *   **Examination:** Enter a mandatory stage to identify hasty errors. He trains self -control.
*   **Motivation and support:**
    *   Mark thoughtfulness, accuracy, concentration. Not speed.
    *   Make checks for complex tasks. This reduces the load.
    *   **Attention:** Concentration up to 30-45 minutes. Then a short break for rebooting is needed.

#### School environment

*   **Setting tasks:**
    *   Use clear, step -by -step instructions. Example: "Read. Highlight. Write."
    *   On control: encourage drafts and time for the final check.
*   **Material feed:**
    *   Structure information: diagrams, lists, tables. This contributes to memorization.
    *   **Location:** In the center of the class, away from distracting factors (window, door).
*   **Training approach:**
    *   Let's think about the time. Do not rush.
    *   IOOM: Turn on the support schemes, allow the use of color markers.
    *   Enjoy the design, research work. This uses the ability to deeply concentrate as much as possible.

### 4. Recommended training

Develop skills with these exercises. Interesting and effective.

*   **Attention and memory:**
    *   **Game "Find a couple" (Memory):** Develops accurate visual memorization.
    *   **Exercise "What has changed?":** Items (7-10), 30 seconds. Take/change one, ask to find a difference.
*   **Self -control and coordination:**
    *   **Juggling:** Improves visual-motor coordination and concentration, reducing impulsiveness.
    *   **Balance games:** Develop bodily and behavioral control.
*   **Rhythm and pause:**
    *   **Musical instruments (drums, percussion):** They train a sense of rhythm and timeliness of actions.
    *   **Dancing (for example, Just Dance):** Develop attention, bodily control, a sense of rhythm.
