### Cognitive potential report
Understanding abilities: focus, control, memory.

This report reveals a unique cognitive profile of a student aged 14-18 years, revealed during testing. Its goal is to help parents and teachers use strengths and support development.

---

### 1. Test results

*   **Sustainability of attention (CPT):** Outstanding ability to long and deep concentration on one task. This is a key force for the development of complex objects and design work.
*   **Reactions inhibition (Go/No-Go):** Difficulties with rapid control of impulsive actions and reactions. This can be manifested in hasty answers, even when knowing the right decision.
*   **Visual working memory:** Great visual working memory. The ability to hold and effectively use complex visual information in the mind: schemes, images, sequences.

<!-- page-break -->

### 2. The guide for the success of your student

Your schoolboy is a “powerful processor” with a deep analytical potential, but with the need for the development of the “Stop signal” function. It is capable of prolonged immersion in the task, but impulsiveness can manifest itself in critical moments.

**Features of the approach:**

*   **Pace:** Productive in deep, long tasks (for example, writing an essay). It is difficult with a frequent change in simple actions or the need for fast, monosyllabic answers.
*   **Perception:** Optimally assimilates complex, structured information presented visually. Schemes, intelligence cards, tables, graphs-effective tools.
*   **Focus:** Capable of complete immersion in the process. However, impulsiveness can lead to hasty actions or answers, and not to distracting.

**Support and development:**

*   **Clear instructions:** Consistent checks and algorithms reduce impulsiveness.
    *   Example: "Read, emphasize, make a plan, write."
*   **Visual support:** Using color, diagrams, templates organizes thoughts and slows down the reaction.
*   **Conscious pause:** Learning the rule “Inhale-out of the answer” forms the necessary skill before action.
*   **Time for verification:** Providing 5-10 minutes for self -testing after work allows you to fix hasty errors.

**Avoid factors that interfere:**

*   **Multitasking:** Fast switching between short tasks.
*   **Oral instructions:** The lack of visual support can lead to a missing of parts.
*   **Time pressure:** The requirement of an immediate response provokes impulsiveness.
*   **Criticism for errors:** Phrases like “you know” demotivate, since the problem is not in knowledge, but in control.

<!-- page-break -->

### 3. Recommendations

**Home environment:**

*   **Organization of work:**
    *   Use checklist checks to complete tasks.
    *   Start with the most difficult tasks while the concentration level is maximum. Leave monotonous tasks for later.
    *   The optimal focus time: up to 40-50 minutes for complex tasks; 15-20 minutes for tasks requiring control, followed by a short break.
*   **Support and encouragement:**
    *   Focus praise on thoughtfulness and self -testing, and not at speed. Example: "It's great that you were in no hurry and found a mistake yourself!"
    *   Instead of criticism for impulsive errors, look for solutions together. Example: "How can you do the next time to take a hurry?"

**School environment:**

*   **The wording of tasks:**
    *   Tasks should be clumsy, with clear criteria. Projects, research, essays are most revealed by analytical abilities and memory.
    *   On control: allow drafts and emphasizing keywords to reduce impulsive errors.
*   **Lesson structure and feed:**
    *   Use a lesson plan, diagrams, tables and presentations.
    *   In case of oral answer, pause (5-10 seconds) for thinking. Perhaps a conditional sign of readiness.
*   **Accommodation and individualization:**
    *   The optimal place in the class: not the first desk (due to distracting factors of the teacher), but a more calm place for deep immersion.
    *   **Individual route:** Consider the possibility of additional time for self -testing of written works. Instead of blitz-questioning, propose preparing a short message on the topic.

### 4. Recommended training

The goal is to develop the skill of “braking” and strengthen the connection between thought and action.

**Development of control and attention:**

*   **The game "Delay!":** Move to the music, freeze by signal. Complication: freeze on one leg. Direct braking training.
*   **Graphic dictations:** Clear movements of movement (for example, “two cells to the right, feet”). He trains the bunch of "he heard - he did - stopped."

**Coordination and rhythm:**

*   **Table tennis, badminton:** These games require constant switching between attack and protection, as well as accurate control of the impact force.
*   **Fighting rhythm:** Repetition of a given rhythm with accurate observance of pauses.

**Strategic thinking and memory:**

*   **Strategic board games:** Chess, Go, Economic Strategies. They encourage deliberate decisions and punish impulsive moves.

**Bodily control:**

*   **Balance exercises:** Standing on one leg, walking along the line, Balansboard. Improving the feeling of body is directly related to behavioral control.
