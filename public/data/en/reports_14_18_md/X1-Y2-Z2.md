### 1. Test results

### 2. Features of training

Profile: 'capable, but scattered.' Powerful memory. Growth zones: stability of attention, pulse control.

**Individual characteristics**

*   **Pace:** Unstable. Effective work is possible in short intervals. The tendency to a hurry leading to errors in carelessness.
*   **Information perception:** The preference for structured and visual information. Oral instructions without visual support can be less effective.
*   **Focus of attention:** High concentration on tasks requiring active thinking and strategy. Fast focus loss during passive listening or monotonous work.

**Which helps**

*   **Step -by -step instructions:** Clear, short, consistent actions.
*   **Visual supports:** Check lists, diagrams, intelligent cards, highlighting keywords.
*   **Predictable rhythm:** Alternation of short periods of intensive work (15–25 minutes) with interruptions (3-5 minutes).
*   **"Stop rule":** Conscious pause before the answer or action.

**What interferes**

*   **Multitasking:** The simultaneous performance of several tasks reduces effectiveness.
*   **Long oral lectures:** The focus of attention is lost in 10-15 minutes.
*   **Hurry and time Tale:** The pressure of time enhances impulsiveness.
*   **Distracting factors:** Noise, disorder, digital distraction.

---

### 3. Recommendations

**For the house**

*   **Organization of work at home:**
    *   Start with complex or least interesting tasks when the energy is maximum.
    *   Use the timer (for example, the method of prying): 25 minutes of work, 5 minutes of rest. During the break, change activity.
    *   Make a plan for the evening in the form of a checklist. Crossing out the completed tasks motivates.
*   **Support and motivation:**
    *   Praise for efforts and following the plan, and not just for the result.
    *   When an error, analyze: "Where could I pause?"
*   **Attention:** The optimal concentration time on the educational task is 20–25 minutes. Then a short reboot is required.

**For school**

*   **Instructions:**
    *   Provide instructions both verbally and in writing (on the board, in handouts).
    *   Use the verbs of action: “emphasize”, “write down”, “compare”.
    *   Break large projects into small, understandable stages with intermediate terms.
*   **Lesson format:**
    *   Change the activities more often: explanation, work with cards, discussion.
    *   Recommended place in the class: the first or second desk, away from distracting factors.
*   **Individual approach:**
    *   Provide a checklist for self-testing during testing.
    *   Allow the use of headphones with neutral background music without words (if it helps concentration).

### 4. Training

The development of skills: regularity is more important than duration.

*   **Attention and control:**
    *   **Schultte tables:** Search for numbers in order. 1-2 tables daily.
    *   **Graphic dictations / copying of patterns:** The development of follow -up and attention to details.
    *   **"Find 10 differences":** Concentration training.

*   **Coordination and impulsiveness:**
    *   **Juggling:** Coordination of "Hands-Glaze", control of actions.
    *   **Balance exercises:** Standing on one leg, walking along the line. The development of the connection of the brain and body.
    *   **Strategic board games:** Chess, go. Develop planning and restraint of impulsiveness.

*   **Feeling of rhythm:**
    *   **Game on a musical instrument:** Especially shock or guitar.
    *   **Exercises with metronome:** Shooting rhythm, reading or completing tasks under the metronome to stabilize the internal pace.
