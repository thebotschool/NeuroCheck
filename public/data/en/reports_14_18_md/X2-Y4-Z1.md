### 2. Features of training

#### Individual approach

*   **Pace:** The most effective in a calm, measured rhythm. Hurry increases the number of errors.
*   **Perception:** Better assimilates the information visually and step by step: schemes, checklists, instructions with illustrations. Oral or continuous text perception is less effective.
*   **Focus of attention:** It has “laser” attention, deeply immersed in interesting tasks. Switching between tasks or their simultaneous execution causes confusion.

#### Which helps

*   **Visual supports:** Check lists, marker allocation, short video instructions.
*   **Structure and predictability:** A clear schedule, an understandable plan of the lesson or assignment. The wording: "First, then then."
*   **Decomposition of tasks:** Separation of large tasks into small, understandable stages.
*   **Rhythm and pauses:** Short physical breaks for rebooting the nervous system.
*   **One information channel:** Exclude oral instructions while the student is busy.

#### What interferes

*   **Multitasking:** Simultaneous performance of several actions or listening to instructions.
*   **Fast change of tasks:** The sharp transition between objects without a pause reduces productivity.
*   **Sensory overload:** Noise, conversations, visual disorder deplete attention.
*   **Abstract instructions:** General phrases such as “be more attentive” are ineffective.

<!-- page-break -->

### 3. Recommendations

#### For the house

*   **Organization of work:**
    *   **Place:** Clean desktop, without distracting items.
    *   **Process:** One task at a time. They completed one thing - they removed it, paused, proceeded to the next.
    *   **Planning:** Use a simple glider or a board to mark the tasks performed.
*   **Support and encouragement:**
    *   Praise for effort and process: “You followed the plan!”, “Perfectly corrected the error.”
    *   Attention is held for 20–25 minutes. Then - a short break (5-7 minutes) with physical activity.
*   **Communication:**
    *   Warn in advance about changing activities for mental training.

#### For school

*   **The wording of tasks:**
    *   Instructions one step.
    *   Duplicate important tasks in writing.
*   **Lesson structure:**
    *   Sound a short lesson plan.
    *   Place the student away from distracting factors (doors, windows).
*   **Material feed:**
    *   Use visual materials: schemes, tables, infographics.
    *   Speak in a calm, measured voice.
*   **Individual route:**
    *   Additional time for tests.
    *   Permission to use headphones with music without words to block noise.
    *   The use of templates and checklists for design work.

### 4. Recommended training

Games and exercises for the development of the necessary skills.

*   **Attention and braking:**
    *   **"Delay!":** Movement to the music, instant stop when turning it off.
    *   **"Edible-universal":** Classic game with a ball, developing a reaction and inhibition.
    *   **Board games:** "Jenga", "Uno" - train concentration, control of movements, switching attention.

*   **Memory and accuracy of perception:**
    *   **"Find 10 differences":** Develops attention to details.
    *   **"Memory":** He trains visual working memory.
    *   **"Graphic dictations":** Drawing in cells-develops auditory-motor coordination and attention.

*   **Body control and rhythm:**
    *   **Balance exercises:** Walking along the line, stand on one leg.
    *   **RIGHTS RIGHTS:** Repetition of rhythmic drawings is the connection of auditory perception and motor skills.
