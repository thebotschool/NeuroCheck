### Cognitive assessment report

This report provides key data on the cognitive profile of the student. It was created to help parents and teachers identify the strengths and areas for development, supporting success in study and life.

---

#### 1. Profile of abilities

*   **Working memory:** Effective assimilation of complex information. The strength of strategic thinking and the development of new material.
*   **Impulsive control (Test Go/No-Go):** Growth area. Difficulties with the control of fast, impulsive reactions. The skill requires development.
*   **Sustainability of attention (CPT test):** High but short -term concentration. Long and monotonous tasks cause fatigue.

<!-- page-break -->

#### 2. The guide for the success of your student

Schoolchild profile: "Fast mind, impulsiveness." It is capable of a deep understanding, but fatigue and impulsiveness can lead to errors.

**Features of training:**

*   **Pace:** Impulsive. Quick start, but fatigue at long -term distances. Does not accept monotony.
*   **Perception:** The preference of visual structured information: scheme, tables, checklists. More effective than continuous text or oral lectures.
*   **Focus of attention:** The ability to deep concentration (20–25 minutes). Then attention is reduced, distractability is growing.

**Success factors:**

*   Step -by -step instructions.
*   Visual supports.
*   Time management (for example, the technique of prying).
*   Game format or challenge in tasks.
*   Time for self -testing with clumsy criteria.

**Risk factors:**

*   Multitasking.
*   Long oral instructions without visual support.
*   Pressure, rush.
*   Unstructured, open tasks.

<!-- page-break -->

#### 3. Recommendations

##### Home environment:

*   **Organization of work:**
    *   Working intervals: 25 minutes of focusing, 5-7 minutes of a break (without gadgets).
    *   Check list of tasks for the evening.
    *   Clean workplace. The phone is in another room for the time of class.
*   **Support and encouragement:**
    *   Praise for the process (concentration, zeal), not only for the result. This develops self -control.
    *   Question-help: "What is your plan? Where will you start?" Instead of "did you make lessons?" Help structure.
*   **Attention:** The maximum concentration of the student is 20–25 minutes. Longer periods provoke errors and fatigue.

##### Educational process:

*   **The wording of tasks:**
    *   Clear algorithms or checklists.
    *   The allocation of keywords in the conditions of tasks (for example, "not", "besides", "most").
*   **Lesson structure and material presentation:**
    *   Optimal place: 1-2 desks, minimization of distracting factors.
    *   Visual materials: lesson plan, presentation, schemes. Oral information is duplicated in writing.
    *   Change of activity every 20–25 minutes (lecture, work with text, discussion, independent work).
*   **Individual route (IOM):**
    *   Allow the use of a glider or checklist to fix tasks.
    *   Provide extra time on control - for self -testing, not for speed.
    *   Templates with stages and terms for volumetric projects.

#### 4. Targeted training

Game and affordable exercises for the development of self -control, attention and coordination.

*   **Attention and self -control:**
    *   **Board games:** "Jenga" (pulse control), "code names" or "dixit" (concentration), "drum" (reaction rate).
    *   **The game "Yes and no, do not say":** Control of speech reactions.
*   **Coordination and body:**
    *   **Juggling:** Develops the coordination of the "hands-eye" and concentration.
    *   **Balance Bord:** He trains balance, bodily control, focus.
    *   **Dancing or martial arts:** Develop the connection of the brain and body through the sequence of movements.
*   **Rhythm and self -regulation:**
    *   **Game of musical instruments:** Especially shock or guitar for the development of rhythm and coordination.
    *   **Conscious breathing techniques:** For example, "square breath" for calm and focusing.
*   **Memory and strategy (using the stronger side):**
    *   **Chess or th:** They are taught to plan, forecasting, control of impulsive moves.
    *   **Appendix "Simon Says":** Memorization and reproduction of sequences.
