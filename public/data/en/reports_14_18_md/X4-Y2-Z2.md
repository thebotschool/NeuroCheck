# Neurocheck: Cognitive Profile Assessment

This report is intended for parents and teachers. It helps to identify the strengths of the student and areas that require support. Our recommendations contribute to the creation of an optimal environment for revealing potential and effective learning.

## 1. A brief test report

*   **Working memory and planning (CPT):** Difficulties with the simultaneous retention of information and following the plan. The loss of a sequence or the general structure of the task is possible.
*   **Self-control (Go/No-Go):** The tendency to hasty, impulsive actions. Difficulties with a pause before making a decision can lead to errors.
*   **Sustainability of attention (TCP):** Difficulties with a long concentration. Attention is reduced with monotonous or oral work, which leads to fatigue.

## 2. Instructions for the child as a student

### Key features

*   **Pace:** An unhurried, measured pace is preferred. A rush or a quick change in tasks lead to errors and frustration.
*   **Perception:** Optimal assimilation of visual information presented by small, consistent steps: schemes, checklists, brief instructions. Long oral explanations are perceived with difficulty.
*   **Attention:** Unrestrained and exhausted. It is distracted by external stimuli, especially with monotonous work. The active concentration remains for 15–20 minutes, after which a short break is needed.

### Which helps

*   **Visual supports:** Schemes, Mayind cards, color selection, short videos.
*   **Step -by -step instructions:** Clear plan of action; Each step opens after performing the previous one. Perfectly-checklists.
*   **Regular pauses:** Short breaks (3-5 minutes) every 15–20 minutes to restore attention.
*   **Speaking out loud:** Repeating instructions or action plans aloud improves memorization.
*   **Predictability:** A clear daily routine and the structure of the lesson reduce anxiety, preserving the attention resource.

### What interferes

*   **Multitasking:** The requirement of the simultaneous performance of several actions (for example, listen, write down, watch) causes overload.
*   **Rush:** Time limited in time provoke impulsive errors and block thinking.
*   **Long oral instructions:** More than two sentences in the instructions are lost. Only the beginning or end is remembered.
*   **Distracting factors:** Extraneous sounds, visual disorder reduce productivity.

## 3. Recommendations

### In the home environment

*   **Organization of work:**
    *   Break large tasks into blocks for 15–20 minutes. Use the timer.
    *   Between the blocks, take short breaks for physical activity.
    *   Make a simple plan of 2-3 steps before starting work. Record it.
    *   The workplace should be quiet, minimalistic, without distracting items.
*   **Support and encouragement:**
    *   Praise for the efforts made, not only for the result. For example: "Excellent, you could concentrate for 15 minutes!" Or "I see how you tried to test yourself."
    *   Help to master a “pause for thinking”. Offer: "Take a hurry, let's think together."
    *   Discuss success and difficulties, look for reasons (for example: “Was it noisy?” Or “The task seemed too big?”).

### In the educational process

*   **The wording and structure of tasks:**
    *   Instructions provide one step.
    *   Present a written or visual plan of the lesson/task (on the board, card).
    *   For testing, provide extra time or division into parts.
    *   Use checklists for self-testing.
*   **Style style and space:**
    *   Place the student closer to the teacher, away from distracting factors (window, door, noisy classmates).
    *   Combine an oral presentation with visual (keywords, schemes, slides).
    *   Before the task, establish eye contact; Ask the student to briefly repeat the task.
*   **Elements of an individual route (IOM):**
    *   Consider the creation of an IOM with a focus on the development of control functions (planning, self -control).
    *   It is possible to use adapted control materials: smaller volume, more time, clear wording.
    *   It is recommended to accompany a school psychologist or tutor to help the organization of educational activities.

## 4. Recommended training

These exercises contribute to the development of identified skills. The key role is regularity (5-10 minutes daily), not a duration.

*   **For attention:**
    *   **Correct samples:** Search and dispensing of one letter in the text (for example, all “o”).
    *   **Games:** “Find 10 differences” or board games for attentiveness (Dobble, Spot it!).
*   **For memory and planning:**
    *   **Memory game:** Training of visual working memory.
    *   **Exercise "Buying":** Remembering a list of 5-7 items without recording.
*   **For bodily control and reduction of impulsiveness:**
    *   **The game is "edible-universal" with the ball:** Develops a rapid reaction and inhibition of action.
    *   **Balance exercises:** Standing on one leg, walking along the line. Improves the connection of the brain and body, enhancing self -control.
*   **For rhythm and pace:**
    *   **RIGHT RIGHTS:** The exact repetition of a simple rhythm, gradually complicating.
    *   **Juggling:** (even two balls) develops coordination, a sense of rhythm and concentration.
