# Cognitive profile
## Assessment of the ability of the student

This report is designed to help parents and teachers to identify the strengths and areas of growth of the student. Recommendations contribute to the creation of an optimal environment for training and development.

---

# 1. Review of tests

*   **Visual memory test (CPT):** There is a systematic approach to memorization. There is a tendency to confusion of similar visual details, especially with fatigue or distraction.
*   **Self-control test (Go/No-Go):** A high level of self -control and concentration is demonstrated in clear and consistent tasks.
*   **Attention test (TCP):** In situations of uncertainty or requiring quick solution, impulsiveness is manifested. There is a tendency to hasty actions without complete thinking.

# 2. The guide for the success of your student

This leadership describes the conditions in which the student reaches maximum efficiency.

## Features of training

*   **Pace:** The work is characterized by a quick, impulsive pace. The greatest productivity and the minimum number of errors are achieved with measured, step -by -step work with sufficient time for thinking.
*   **Perception format:** Clearly structured visual information is optimally perceived: schemes, lists, algorithms. An excess of small details, text or images on one page leads to overload and errors.
*   **Focus of attention:** Attention is dual. High concentration is observed on understandable, organized tasks. With a chaotic presentation of material, multitasking or oral explanations without visual support, attention is scattered.

## Supporting factors

*   **Step-by-step instructions and checklists:** Separation of large tasks into small, understandable steps.
*   **Visual supports:** Schemes, Mayind-cards, highlighting keywords. Help focus on the main one.
*   **The principle of "Think - write - do":** An additional planning step reduces impulsiveness.
*   **A clear rhythm of work and rest:** For example, the Pomodoro method (25 minutes of work, 5 minutes of rest) to maintain concentration.
*   **One task at a time:** Focusing on the completion of one task is more effective.

## Factors that reduce efficiency

*   **Multitasking:** The simultaneous performance of several actions (for example, listening and recording without visual support) almost always leads to errors.
*   **Visual or audial "noise":** Unstructured information or background distracting sounds significantly reduce productivity.
*   **Oral instructions without visual support:** Information can be perceived inaccurately or forgotten.
*   **Time deficiency:** Situations that require quick solutions without the possibility of thinking provoke impulsive, incorrect answers.

# 3. Recommendations

## For the home environment

*   **Homework organization:**
    *   Together, make a simple plan before starting: for example, “Mathematics (tasks 1, 2), 15 minutes break, then history”.
    *   Miss large projects into pieces, distributing them for several days.
    *   The workplace should be ordered, without distracting items (it is recommended to remove the phone).
*   **Support and encouragement:**
    *   Praise for attentiveness, accuracy and following the plan, and not for speed. Mark the successes in restraining haste and self -testing.
    *   For verification, use a “fresh look”: errors are not checked immediately, but 15–20 minutes after execution. This helps to notice the missed.
*   **Mercing of attention:**
    *   The effective concentration on a complex task lasts about **20–25 minutes**. After this, a short (5-minute) break for physical activity or rest is needed.

## For the school environment

*   **The wording of tasks:**
    *   Tasks should be as clear, unambiguous and linear as possible. The optimal format is a numbered list.
    *   Provide instructions both verbally and in writing (on the board, in handouts, on slides).
*   **Structure and presentation of material:**
    *   When explaining new topics, use diagrams, tables, diagrams. Select key concepts and rules visually (color, frame).
    *   Important information (for example, a lesson plan or homework) should be placed in the same, predictable place.
*   **Elements of an individual educational route (IOM):**
    *   For design or research work, provide a ready-made checklist with stages. This will reduce anxiety and prevent impulsive solutions.
    *   During testing, it is permissible to use a simple timer for independent distribution of time and avoiding haste.
    *   It is recommended to involve the student in group work, where he can play a role that requires planning and structuring (for example, drawing up a performance plan).

# 4. Recommended training

Effective exercises for the development of skills in an accessible form.

## Attention and accuracy

*   **Game "Find 10 differences":** Perfectly develops visual attentiveness and ability to notice details.
*   **Schultte tables:** The search for numbers in order trains concentration and expands the field of view.
*   **Exercise "Corrective test":** Regular deletion of certain letters in the text temporarily improves attention stability.

## Memory and structuring

*   **Creating Mind Cart (Intellect-Cart):** It helps to visually organize information on any topic (from educational material to personal plans).
*   **Mnemics:** Inventing associations, phrases or stories for memorizing rules, formulas, dates.

## Control and impulsiveness

*   **Juggling:** Start with two items. The exercise develops the coordination of the "hand-eye" and promotes concentration.
*   **Balance exercises:** Standing on one leg (with open and closed eyes), the use of a balance board. They teach control over the body and, as a result, over impulses.
*   **Rhythmic exercises:** Cooling or tapping the specified rhythm according to the model. Develops a sense of rhythm and self -regulation skills.
