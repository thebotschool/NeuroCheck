## 2. Guide for success: student profile

This section reveals the unique profile of your student, offering a deep understanding of his potential.

### Individual approach

*   **Pace:** He quickly absorbs complex material and is deeply immersed in the topic. At the same time, he is prone to hasty answers. The profile "Fast mind, hasty hands" is characteristic.
*   **Perception:** Most effectively assimilates information structured and submitted step by step. We need a clear plan to systematize knowledge, despite the ability to work with large amounts of data.
*   **Focus:** Capable of deep concentration on interesting, complex tasks (projects, research). It is easily distracted and acts impulsively in routine situations or where mechanical self -control is required (for example, tests).

### Supporting factors

*   **Visual supports:** Check lists, schemes, intelligent cards, algorithms help structure thoughts and hold details.
*   **Step -by -step instructions:** Separation of large tasks into small, understandable steps. This reduces the load and helps control impulsiveness.
*   **Rhythm and pauses:** Short, regular breaks for “rebooting” (for example, 25 minutes of work, 5 minutes of rest). The ability to move helps to relieve motor stress.
*   **Clear rules:** Clearly certain rules and evaluation criteria. This facilitates self -control.
*   **Drafts:** The opportunity to first state ideas, and then calmly edit them, eliminating hasty mistakes.

### Calls

*   **Multitasking:** Quick switching between different activities exhausts the resource of self -control.
*   **Abstract instructions:** Tasks without a clear plan of action (for example, “think about it”) may be difficult.
*   **Time pressure:** Tough time frames on tasks requiring attentiveness and inhibition, provoke errors.
*   **Chaos and noise:** A disorganized environment with many distracting factors enhances impulsiveness.

## 3. Way to Development: Recommendations

### Home environment

*   **Organization of work:**
    *   Divide large tasks into blocks for 20–25 minutes. After each block-a 5-minute break for activity.
    *   Use a timer (visual or sound) to establish a temporary framework.
    *   Together, draw up a plan to fulfill the tasks: “Where will we start?”, “What next?”, “What is needed to complete?”. This develops strategic thinking.
*   **Support and encouragement:**
    *   Praise not for the result, but for the process: “I see, you tried very hard and not distracted” or “An excellent plan, you thought out everything”. This shifts the focus on self -control.
    *   Discuss errors calmly as part of training. Analyze the reasons: “Have you hurried? Let's try to check the next time. ”
*   **Reduced load:**
    *   Before going to bed and after school, offer physical activity or hobbies without gadgets for “unloading” the nervous system.

### School environment

*   **The wording of tasks:**
    *   Step -by -step instructions are preferred, for example: “1. Make a plan from 3 theses. 2. Write the introduction. 3. Expand every thesis ... ".
    *   Provide templates or checklists for self-test before handling work.
*   **Lesson structure and feed:**
    *   The optimal place is closer to the teacher, further from distracting factors (window, door).
    *   Use his strong memory: offer research and project tasks where he can prove himself, possibly as a leader in the group.
    *   Warn in advance about the change of activity: "After 5 minutes we finish it and start the test."
*   **Individual route:**
    *   Consider the possibility of additional time in control work and exams (GIA, exam).
    *   If possible, offer alternative forms of control: oral answer, project or creative task instead of tests.

## 4. Practice: Effective training

These exercises will help to develop skills in growth zones.

*   **Attention and braking:**
    *   **The game "Delay":** During movement or dance by signal, you must instantly freeze. Develops the connection “I hear, I am making” and the skill of braking.
    *   **Computer simulators (type Go/No-Go):** Games where you need to quickly respond to one type of incentives and ignore others.
*   **Coordination and bodily control:**
    *   **Juggling:** Starting with two goals. He trains the coordination of the “eye-hand” and concentration.
    *   **Balance exercises:** Standing on one leg, walking along the line, a balancing board. It teaches you better feel and control the body.
*   **Memory and strategy:**
    *   **Board strategic games:** Chess, GO, Monopoly, Jackal. They require planning several moves ahead and containing impulsive decisions.
*   **Rhythm:**
    *   **Working with metronome:** Removing the specified rhythm (palm, foot), starting slowly, then faster. Develops an inner sense of pace and control.
*   **Mindfulness practices:**
    *   **Conscious breathing:** 3-5 minutes a day, calmly focus on breaths and exhalations. A powerful tool for “slowing down” and training prefrontal cortex responsible for self -control.
