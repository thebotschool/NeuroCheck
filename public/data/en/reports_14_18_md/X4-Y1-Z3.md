### Cognitive assessment. Report.

This report offers a detailed analysis of the results of cognitive testing, revealing individual characteristics and offering strategies for effective training.

---

### **1. Key skills**

*   **Attention and memory (CPT):** There are difficulties with holding the sequence of actions and concentration without a pre -set plan.
*   **Self-control (Go/No-Go):** High ability to control actions with simple and understandable rules. This indicates the significant potential of self -regulation.
*   **Reaction and choice (TCP):** There is a tendency to quick decisions, especially in situations requiring an operational reaction without enough time to think.

**The main conclusion:** The child has a developed self -control skill, but does not always manage to apply it in new or complex tasks, where independent planning is needed. The goal is to teach the conscious use of this skill.

<!-- page-break -->

### **2. The guide for the success of your student**

#### Features of training

*   **Pace:** It takes time for planning and comprehension. In the presence of a clear plan, an average or fast pace of work is possible. Hurrying at the beginning of the work can lead to errors.
*   **Perception:** Better assimilates the visually submitted information and step -by -step instructions. Volumetric or abstract instructions can cause overload.
*   **Focus:** Sustainable attention when performing short, structured tasks. It is easily distracted with multitasking, noise, or when the task seems too voluminous.

#### Which contributes

*   **Step -by -step instructions:** Breaking large tasks into 3-4 small, specific steps.
*   **Visual supports:** Check lists, schemes, highlighting key points, timers.
*   **Clear rhythm:** The work of short, focused intervals (for example, 20 minutes of work, 5 minutes of break).
*   **Predictable environment:** A calm workplace, a minimum of distracting factors.
*   **Operational feedback:** A clear and immediate confirmation of the correctness of actions.

#### What makes it difficult

*   **Multitasking:** Simultaneous requests (for example, listen and write down).
*   **Unstructured tasks:** Open questions without a clear action plan.
*   **Rush and pressure:** The requirement of an immediate response.
*   **Sensory overload:** Noise, a large accumulation of people, a quick change in activity.

### **3. Practical recommendations**

#### Home environment

*   **Tasks at home:**
    *   **Planning:** Make a simple, written plan of 2-3 points. Example: Read the rule. Do exercise 5. Check.
    *   **Schedule:** The optimal duration of work on the task is 15–20 minutes. Then - a short break (water, movement).
    *   **Working space:** The table should contain only necessary for the current task.
*   **Motivation and support:**
    *   Encourage not the result, but the process and effort: "You followed the plan!", "It is wonderful that you noticed and corrected the error." This shifts the focus from an impulsive achievement to conscious actions.

#### School environment

*   **The clarity of tasks:**
    *   Use specific verbs: do not "study the paragraph", but "read the paragraph and write out 3 terms."
    *   Give the instructions one at a time, not a list.
*   **Support and structure:**
    *   Provide templates: for tasks, for retelling, checks for checking.
    *   Place important information (lesson plan, homework) on the board in a permanent place.
*   **Interaction:**
    *   Establish eye contact before the instructions, make sure that attention is attracted.
    *   Let's “think for the time”, do not demand an immediate answer.
*   **Personalization:**
    *   Consider the possibility of providing additional time for control and independent work.
    *   It is recommended to place at the first or second desk to minimize abstracts.

### **4. Developing activity**

These games and exercises will support the development of the necessary skills. It is recommended to engage in 10-15 minutes daily in a playful way.

*   **Attention and memory:**
    *   **Correct samples:** Crossing out a given letter in the text. Complication: cross one, circle the other.
    *   **Graphic dictations:** "One cage to the right, two cells up ..." - develops the adherence to the instructions and keeping it in memory.
    *   **"Memory":** Search for paired cards.

*   **Self -control and balance:**
    *   **Game "Delay":** Movement to the music, instant stop when it is stopped.
    *   **Balance exercises:** Walking along the line, stand on one leg (with open/closed eyes).
    *   **Board games:** Any games with the sequence of strokes and the rules ("checkers", "walkers").

*   **Rhythm and organization:**
    *   **Tapping rhythms:** Repetition of a rhythmic pattern. Gradual complication. Improves the ability of the brain to temporary organization of activity.
