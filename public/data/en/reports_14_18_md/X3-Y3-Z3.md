This report reveals the strengths and areas for the development of your student. It contains practical recommendations to support learning and growth.

### 1. Test results

**Consistent memory**
The ability to remember the details is noted. The development of the skills of structuring information is required. The initial sequences strategies are already being formed.

**Coordination and braking**
There is a need to improve interhemispheric interaction. This affects the performance of tasks requiring complex thinking.

**Attention**
The tendency to impulsive actions has been revealed. This may affect the adoption of balanced decisions and self -control in difficult situations.

### 2. Approach to learning

**Individual characteristics**

*   **Pace**
    It takes time to plan before starting the task. A rush can lead to impulsive actions and passing steps. A short pause is needed before the start.

*   **Perception format**
    Preference is given to structured and visual information. Continuous text or long oral explanations are more complicated. Effective: checklists, diagrams, tables.

*   **Focus of attention**
    Attention is stable if there is a clear plan. The lack of a plan can lead to distraction and errors in the sequence of actions.

**Which helps**

*   Visualization: schemes, infographics, lists, step -by -step instructions.
*   Preliminary planning: Learning a brief plan.
*   Regular physical pauses: short breaks with active movements.
*   Clear and predictable rules: contribute to self -control.

**What interferes**

*   Multivativity: leads to errors and reducing efficiency.
*   Abstract tasks without specific examples or criteria.
*   A sharp change in activity without a pause for switching.
*   External distracting factors: noise, extraneous actions.

<!-- page-break -->

### 3. Recommendations for support

**At home**

*   **Homework**
    Miss large tasks into small steps. Use visual tools (stickers, board) to track progress.
    Before starting work, discuss the plan: "Where will we start?" Help in compilation without fulfilling the task for the student.
    Implement the interval approach: 25 minutes of work, 5 minutes of a break to change activity. This helps maintain concentration.

*   **Support and promotion**
    Mark efforts, not just the result. Examples of praise: "Great plan!", "It is noticeable how you thought your answer."
    Discuss emotions. Help recognize impulsiveness and find constructive methods of its expression (sports, creativity).

**At school**

*   **The formulation of tasks**
    Use step -by -step instructions (a, then b, then c). Example: "First write out the dates, then place on the scale, describe the events."
    Use specific verbs of action: emphasize, make a list, draw a diagram.

*   **Structuring the material**
    For complex tasks (essays, projects), provide templates, samples or checklists.
    Place the student in a place with a minimum number of distracting factors (far from the window, closer to the board).

*   **Style style**
    Combine oral explanations with visual support (board, presentation).
    In group work, assign a specific, structured role (for example, "you are responsible for the project plan").

*   **IOM elements**
    Consider the short -term tutor support for the development of planning and self -control.

### 4. Practical training

These exercises will help to develop key skills. Perform them in a playful way, 10-15 minutes daily.

**Attention and control**

*   **Games "Sea Battle" or "Sudoku":** Develop logic, planning, stability of attention.
*   **Respiratory practices:** Exercise "Squarey breathing" (inha than 4 accounts diverting) helps to reduce impulsiveness.

**Coordination and interaction**

*   **Juggling:** (starting with two balls) improves the coordination of the "hand-eye" and interhemispheric interaction.
*   **Cross -steps:** Truck by the right elbow of the left knee and vice versa. Perform at a calm pace.
*   **Game on a musical instrument:** (guitar, piano) develops coordinated work of hands.

**Memory and planning**

*   **Comics assembly:** Restore the sequence of the plot from the mixed fragments.
*   **Table strategies:** ("Jackal", "Carcasson", chess) develop multi -way planning.
*   **Preparation of food by recipe:** Training to the algorithm and sequence control.
