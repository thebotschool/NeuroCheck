### 2. Success Guide

#### Features of teaching

*   **Pace:** Confident, methodical. High productivity in concentration on one task without frequent abstracts.
*   **Perception:** Effective assimilation of structured, consistent information. The simultaneous perception of several data flows (audio, visual, recording) can be difficult.
*   **Focus:** Deep, long -term immersion in the topic. The risk of omission of peripheral, but significant details.

#### Efficiency support

*   **Step-by-step instructions, checklists:** Separation of volumetric tasks for subtasibility simplifies execution and minimizes errors.
*   **Visual anchors:** Selection of key elements, the use of schemes and simple illustrations.
*   **Mental cards:** A tool for the connection of logic and images (texts analysis, preparation of reports).
*   **Rhythmic actions:** The tapping of the rhythm during memorization (dates, words) contributes to synchronization.
*   **Continuous immersion:** The ability to focus on one task without abstracts maximizes productivity.

#### Factors that reduce efficiency

*   **Multitasking:** Parallel performance of several actions (listen, watch, notes) reduces assimilation.
*   **Visual noise:** Excess information, distracting elements or mess interferes with concentrations on the details.
*   **Frequent change of tasks:** A quick transition between diverse actions (mathematics, essays) causes increased fatigue.

### 3. Recommendations

#### Home environment

*   **Homework:**
    *   Miss large tasks into parts. Example: "Today is the search for information, tomorrow plan, the day after tomorrow slides."
    *   A minute for planning before starting: "Where do you start? What are the steps?" This activates strategic thinking.
    *   The workplace should be ordered: only necessary for the current task.
*   **Support and motivation:**
    *   Mark attentiveness to details, not just speed. For example: "It is wonderful that you found this typo."
    *   Effective concentration lasts up to 40-50 minutes. Then a short (5-7 minutes) physical break (stretch, look out the window, squats) is recommended. This contributes to the rebooting of the brain better than gadgets.

#### School environment

*   **The wording of tasks:**
    *   The instructions should be consistent, not simultaneous. "First read the paragraph. Then answer the questions. After that, fill out the table."
    *   Attract to tasks requiring long research and deep analysis - here its potential will be revealed as much as possible.
*   **The structure of the material:**
    *   On the board and in presentations, use a clear structure, minimizing distracting elements. Select key points with color or frame.
    *   When explaining where the connection of text and graphics (physics, geography, geometry) is important, use schemes and mental cards that can be supplemented independently.
*   **Individual route:**
    *   Optimal for long -term design and research work. This will use its high concentration and strategic thinking.
    *   Consider participation in debates or scientific conferences, where deep preparation on one topic is required.

### 4. Developing training

The presented exercises are aimed at harmonizing the brain and the development of key skills.

*   **Attention to details:**
    *   Games "Find the differences", assembly of complex puzzles or models.
    *   Coloring on numbers with small elements.
    *   Mnemics "Palace of Memory" for visual memorization of sequences.

*   **Coordination and synchronism:**
    *   **Juggling:** Start with two items. Perfectly synchronizes the brain.
    *   **Crossroads:** Alternated touches with the right hand of the left knee, the left - the right. Possible pace options to music are possible.
    *   **Tool game:** Tools requiring the participation of both hands (piano, guitar).

*   **Rhythm and control control:**
    *   Dancing with repeating ligaments of movements.
    *   Removing or cutting rhythms (can be used by metronome).
    *   Pronouncing the tongue twisters while performing a simple rhythmic effect (for example, squeeze and unclench the fists to the beat).
